       The Veterans Memorial Amphitheater, a key component of our Measure J

       functionality of the Black Box Theater. This door is essential for maintaining
       secure operations when the theater is not in use and for the dual
       functionality of the space, facilitating comfortable temperatures and secure
       settings for events.

       During the February 2025 Board Meeting, approval of the proposed Change
       Order was withheld pending further clarification on several points related to
       the unavailability of specified materials and the timing of orders for
       necessary replacements. Attached to this Board Report is the Board Report
       and additional information that was presented at the February 2025 Board
       Meeting. (Attachment A)

       Construction Management and FORPD’s Attorney will be present to provide

          simultaneously.

          panels were unavailable.
          Financial Responsibility: Clarification of the parties responsible for
          covering the additional costs incurred from these changes.

           Approve the Change Order for the Amphitheater Hangar Door in the amount
           of $77,454.17, with detailed justifications regarding the financial
           responsibility and necessity due to obsolete materials.
    II.    Background
           The Veterans Memorial Amphitheater, a crucial element of our Measure J
           project, incorporates a substantial hangar door that is vital for both security
           and the facility’s versatility. This door supports secure operations when not
           in use and enhances the dual functionality of the space, which includes the
           Black Box Theater. It is vital for maintaining comfortable temperatures and
           security for the facilities and events that are held.
           During the January Board Meeting, concerns were raised regarding the
           unforeseen increase in costs due to the specified 1.5”-thick panels being
           unavailable at time of order requiring a replacement 1.5” panel product
           specification and necessitating additional modifications such as new framing
           and weather stripping.
           The Board requested a detailed explanation of why FORPD is financially
           responsible for these additional costs. This change was necessitated by the
           discontinuation of the originally specified panels.
    III. Problem /Situation/ Request
        1. Contractual Provisions and Adjustments: The project's contract with
           Bobo Construction outlines provisions for adjustments in contract amounts
           through change orders in response to unforeseen changes like material
           discontinuations. The specific clause from the contract reads:
               • "Section 4 - CONTRACT AMOUNT. District agrees to pay and
                   Contractor agrees to accept, for the full and complete performance
                 of this Agreement in full payment for the Work performed the sum
                 of Twenty-One Million Seven Hundred-Fifty Thousand DOLLARS
                 ($21,750,000.00), subject to adjustment as provided in the Contract
                 Documents."
             • This section supports the process for implementing change orders
                 when necessary modifications arise during the project's execution,
                 which impact the original contract terms or costs.
             • If the responsibility for dealing with obsolete materials or unforeseen
                 substitutions was assigned to the General Contractor, the initial bid
                 amount might have been significantly higher. Contractors often
                 increase their bids to mitigate potential risks associated with material
                 availability and price volatility, thereby protecting themselves from
                 unforeseen expenses. Consequently, our contract amount of
                 $21,750,000 could likely have been higher to cover these risks.
      2. Risks of Pre-Ordering Materials: Pre-ordering construction materials
         such as the panels for the hangar door carries several risks that can affect
         project cost and timeline:
         • Increased Costs: Storing large or specialized items like hangar door
             panels can incur significant costs related to warehousing, security, and
             insurance. These costs can escalate if the storage period extends
             unexpectedly due to construction delays or other factors.
         • Damage and Loss: Stored materials are susceptible to damage from
             environmental factors, vandalism, or accidents. This risk requires
             additional financial outlays for protective measures and potential
             replacements, increasing overall project expenses.
         • Warranty Issues: Materials purchased well in advance of their
             installation can have warranties that expire before the materials are
             even used. This leaves the project exposed to risks if defects appear
             after the warranty period.
         • Inflexibility to Design Changes: Pre-ordering locks the project into
             specific materials and technologies early on. If later design changes or
             technological improvements occur, the project cannot adapt without
             incurring costs related to swapping out already purchased materials.
         • Potential for Non-Returnable Items: Customized or specially
             ordered materials often cannot be returned if not used, or if project
             specifications change. This can lead to wastage and financial losses if
             the materials are not suitable when the time comes for installation.
      3. Project Design and Timeline Constraints: The design of the Village
         Park project was completed and approved well before the actual material
         procurement started, a common scenario in large construction projects. The
         significant time gap between design approval and construction initiation
         increased the risk of material discontinuation.

                    Bannister Park Boundary Survey and Next Steps

Prepared By:        Sean Ventura, Parks & Facilities Manager

 I.   Recommendation
      It is the staff’s recommendation that an informal course design is created
      and that community outreach meetings be scheduled to gauge community
      interest in the installation of a disc golf course at Bannister Park.

II.   Background
      Due to ongoing safety concerns at Miller Park Disc Golf Course, the Board
      of Directors directed staff to identify other potential locations for a disc
      golf course within the District.

      Staff returned to the Board of Directors with a presentation outlining the
      pros and cons of each of the District’s managed properties. From this
      presentation and staff’s recommendations, Bannister Park was identified
      as the most likely candidate for the relocation of the Miller Park Disc Golf
      Course; however, the full extent of the property boundaries at Bannister
      Park was unknown to current FORPD staff. As such, staff were directed to
      engage the services of an engineering firm to identify property lines at the
      site.

      Staff contracted Burrell Consulting Group, Inc., a firm which has
      historically completed surveys for Bannister Park and which staff felt
      would have the greatest success in identifying property markers. In
      February of 2025, Burrell Consulting Group, Inc. completed their
      investigations at Bannister Park and provided documentation to the
       District highlighting the property markers present at the site (Attachment
       A).

III.   Problem /Situation/ Request
       The identified boundary markers do confirm the overall space available at
       Bannister Park to be just under ten acres; the property extends to the
       east from the current multipurpose field to a greater extent than
       previously thought. Much of the terrain sits upon two seasonal streams
       with significant elevation changes throughout. The area is wooded and in
       need of extensive maintenance to clear out downed and dead limbs which
       could pose a potential fire hazard during summer months. Much of the
       undeveloped property is overgrown with both native and invasive species
       of plant life.

       Neighboring this property are several other property owners who have,
       due to a lack of markers from the District, utilized this space as their own.
       The adjacent property owners include at least one neighbor who appears
       to have erected a boundary fence which looks to be encroaching upon
       District property. One property owner has created a pedestrian corridor
       through both their property and the District’s in order to eliminate the
       need for Waldorf School children to walk on a busy section of Fair Oaks
       Blvd. on their way to school. The Waldorf School is located on the
       southern edge of Bannister Park.

       The space is conducive to an interesting and interactive disc golf course
       with no regular use otherwise identified. There are no playgrounds at the
       park. The bulk of the location’s use are visitors who utilize our free
       parking to visit Sacramento County Regional Parks along the American
       River. These visitors walk from the park, along the American River Bike
       Trail, and out of the park’s boundaries; staff do not believe that these
       users would interface with any possible disc golf configurations at this
       location (excepting potential verbal/visual interactions which regularly
       occur along a trail). During this process, staff did witness some use of
       Bannister Park’s outdoor exercise equipment; staff do not believe that
       visitors using this equipment would interact with any possible disc golf
       configurations at the location. Finally, staff witnessed several visitors to
       the park, who had walked in from nearby neighborhoods, to run dogs off
       leash on the multipurpose field; this user group could potentially interact
       with several layouts of a disc golf course on site, though their actions (off-
       leash dogs) are prohibited in the park by Sacramento County Code.

       Staff propose that several informal course layouts be designed by staff
       and the local disc golf community in preparation for public outreach prior
       to beginning any construction at the site. Utilizing these layouts as a visual
       aid for the local community, staff would present the possibility of a disc
       golf course to local residents and stakeholders in order to ascertain any
       concerns there may be. Staff also recommend that the informal designs
       produced for any potential public outreach express more than simply
       adding a disc golf course to the existing site. Instead, the outreach should
       cover further site-wide modifications to the location, including expanded
       parking and enhanced ADA access. Further site upgrades may be
       proposed by the public as part of the public outreach efforts.

IV.    Financial Analysis
       The financial impact of a project such as this depends entirely on which
       direction the District chooses to head. If only public outreach meetings are
       considered at this point then the costs to the District would be minimal.

       If, after public outreach, the District decides that a course at Bannister
       Park is desirable, then the costs would be considerably higher. The full
       costs of such a project have not yet been fully explored and depend on
       various factors such as: professional or in-house design, scope of project,
       permitting requirements, and contracted or in-house construction.

Meeting Date:       March 19th, 2025
To:                 Board of Directors
From:               Michael J. Aho, District Administrator
Subject:            Discussion and Possible Action Regarding
                    Community Request to Return Disc Golf Baskets to
                    Miller Park Disc Golf Course

Prepared By:        Sean Ventura, Parks & Facilities Manager

 I.   Recommendation
      Staff recommends returning disc golf baskets to Miller Park Disc Golf
      Course only during periods of extended closures at Earl Legette
      Elementary School (e.g. Winter and Summer breaks, etc.).

II.   Background
      Fair Oaks Recreation & Park District was approached by a group of
      concerned residents regarding the safety of Miller Park Disc Golf Course
      and how the course and its users were interfacing with non-participants at
      Miller Park.

      FORPD staff contracted the services of Whirlwind Disc Golf, an
      organization which specializes in professional course design and safety
      analysis, to examine the course to identify safety concerns and propose
      modifications to the course to reduce hazardous conditions at the site.

      FORPD staff also held a community outreach meeting for concerned
      residents to express where they felt hazardous conditions existed at the
      site.

      Using the above information, staff followed recommendations of both
      residents and Whirlwind Disc Golf to enact mitigation measures on the
      course and were planning to complete further mitigation measures as
      outlined in the documents provided by Whirlwind Disc Golf; however, due
      to ongoing issues identified at Miller Park Disc Golf Course, staff were
       directed to remove from Miller Park all baskets that still had the potential
       to interface with non-disc golf visitors to the park.

       In February of 2025, members of the disc golf community approached the
       District with a request to return baskets to the course during periods at
       which school was not in session, more specifically during weekends and
       school breaks. At this point, the Board of Directors requested staff bring a
       recommendation and an actionable item to the Board of Directors at their
       March 2025 meeting.

III.   Problem /Situation/ Request
       The local disc golf community has requested that baskets be returned to
       the course on Friday evenings after school has been let out for the
       weekend and subsequently removed again on Sunday evenings prior to
       school returning to session.

       Staff do not support the idea due to limited resources as a District. The
       task would require multiple staff members to be present (minimum two)
       and multiple trips from the Maintenance Offices at Fair Oaks Park, where
       the baskets are currently stored, due to the size of the baskets and the
       space available in District vehicles. On Friday evenings there are not
       enough staff members scheduled to complete such a task while
       simultaneously completing other necessary tasks required of their
       positions. Likewise on Sunday evenings the same staffing restrictions are
       in effect. The District would therefore be required to change scheduling to
       have enough staff on hand to complete this singular task; staff do not feel
       that this would be a sufficient enough benefit to the community to
       warrant this specialized position on the schedule.

       Staff were approached by the local disc golf community with a proposal
       which may alleviate the above concerns, though the proposal does not
       alleviate all of staff’s concerns. The proposal is as follows: several new pin
       locations (the collars anchored in the ground which hold disc golf baskets
       in place out in the field) would be installed in an out-of-the-way location
       at the site in which the removed baskets would be placed; these locations
       would not be playable and would be considered “temporary storage
       positions;” from these locations, disc golf community members would
       move the baskets back into their play positions on the weekends and
       return them to their temporary storage positions at the end of the
       weekend. The costs of installing the new temporary storage locations
       would be borne by the local disc golf community.

       This proposal eliminates the need for District staff to be rescheduled in
       order to install/remove the baskets on a weekly basis and therefore
       eliminates much of the concerns staff have with such a plan; however,
       staff still believes there could be issues with community buy-in and
       availability to be present every weekend throughout the year to
       consistently install/remove the baskets. In addition, there will be little
      District oversight during this process, which would enable the community
      to place baskets in the pin locations they held prior to the safety survey
      (these pin locations were not fully removed in the interest of maintaining
      a layout for disc golf tournaments in the future). This opens the District to
      some risk as these locations have been deemed relatively unsafe by
      Whirlwind Disc Golf.

      If there is no guaranteed consistency in basket installation and removal
      then the community may well become frustrated with the District due to
      changing course schedules which may seem to be at whim. If there are no
      guarantees that the baskets will be placed in approved locations, then the
      community may be placed unnecessarily at risk.

      It should also be noted that non-disc-golf residents may see this as a
      return to, what they consider to be, a lack of care for the safety of other
      park visitors despite the District taking significant measures to mitigate
      risk at the park.

      With all of these factors in mind, staff recommend only returning baskets
      to their play positions during long school breaks when staff can be present
      to place the baskets in appropriate locations and will consistently and
      reliably have the baskets placed and removed as appropriate to manage
      the school break schedule. At the same time, staff will be continuing to
      explore options related to the relocation of the disc golf course.

IV.   Financial Analysis
      There are no costs associated if the District does not return baskets to the
      course.

      If the District decides to have staff return baskets to their play positions
      on a weekly basis, it would at minimum cost the District roughly $13,000
      in staff time annually. This cost is assuming two part-time staff members
      are scheduled on a weekly basis to accomplish this task. The cost is hard
      to quantify overall due to the interconnected nature of the requirements
      of staff time, as the reallocation of staffing may have broader affects upon
      the services rendered to other parks and user groups throughout the
      District. In addition, if fluctuations occur with the amount of part-time
      staff available, then full-time staff would be required to shift into these
      schedules which would likely result in an increase in CTO pay, as parks
      management is unable to remove any of the more pressing duties
      assigned to staff on a weekly basis in order to schedule them for eight
      hours each weekend.

      If staff were directed to install “temporary storage positions” for baskets
      within the park and work with the disc golf community on installation and
      removal of baskets on a weekly basis, then there would likewise be little
      to no cost to the District, as the proposal from the community includes
      funding of the temporary positions.
       If staff were directed to return baskets to their play positions only during
       long school break periods, the cost would be subsumed by the regular
       schedules of parks staff and would likely not increase costs for the
       District. Baskets would be returned to play positions on the first District
       work day aligning with the school break periods and removed once more
       prior to school breaks ending, though this may mean some weekends on
       the inside/outside edge of break periods do not see the baskets yet
       returned to play.

 I.     Recommendation
        The Board nominate a candidate for consideration for the Special District
        Representative for the Sacramento County Treasury Oversight Committee if
        desired.

II.     Background
        On February 27, 1996, the Sacramento County Board of Supervisors established
        the Treasury Oversight Committee by Resolution #96-0163, as required by
        Government Code Section 27131. The committee consists of ten members, one
        of whom represents special districts with funds in the County Treasury as
        outlined in Attachment A.

        The duties of the Committee are established under Government Code Section
        27132. Responsibilities include review of the Investment Policy of the Pooled
        Investment Fund, a quarterly review of investments, and causing an annual
        audit of the Pooled Investment Fund portfolio. The Committee is advisory and
        does not direct individual investment decisions, select investment advisors,
        brokers, or dealers, or impinge on the day-to-day operations of the County
        Treasury.

III.    Problem /Situation/ Request
        A majority of public members must have expertise or an academic background
        in public finance. The other public members should be economically diverse and
      bipartisan in political registration. The term of appointment is normally three
      (3) years. Appointees must also comply with the following restrictions:

                •   A member may not be employed by any entity that has either
                    contributed to a campaign of a candidate for the office of local
                    Treasurer or contributed to the campaign of a candidate to be a
                    member of a legislative body of any agency that has money
                    deposited in the County Treasury in the previous three (3) years
                    or during the period that the employee is a member of the
                    Committee;
                •   A member may neither directly nor indirectly raise money for a
                    candidate for either local Treasurer or a member of the governing
                    board of any local agency that has money deposited in the County
                    Treasury;
                •   A member may neither secure employment with nor be employed
                    by bond underwriters, bond counsel, security brokerages or
                    dealers, or financial services firms, with whom the Treasurer is
                    doing business as a member of the Committee or for one year
                    after leaving the Committee;
                •   A member must complete and file a Statement of Economic
                    Interests (Form 700) within 30 days of assuming office and file
                    annually thereafter.

      The Sacramento County Department of Finance requests that the Board provide
      a nomination by April 14, 2025. Individuals nominated must meet the criteria
      listed above and be a current member or employee of the District. On April 21,
      2025, a ballot will be mailed listing all nominations. This ballot must be returned
      to the Department of Finance no later than May 30, 2024. Ballots will be
      tabulated and notifications of election results will be sent by mail on June 6,
      2025. A run-off election will be held in the event of a tie. The Department of
      Finance expects the newly elected Special District Representative to the
      Treasury Oversight Committee to be ratified by the Board of Supervisors on July
      22, 2025.

      Meetings are scheduled for the third Friday of the second month following the
      end of each quarter. Meetings are held at the County of Sacramento
      Administration Building, 700 H Street, Hearing Room One, Sacramento, CA
      95814.

      Please note that this is a request from the Sacramento County Department of
      Finance, not a requirement. The FORPD Board of Directors may submit the
      name of an existing Board member consistent with the qualifications noted
      above.

IV.   Financial Analysis
      There is no financial impact associated with the staff recommendation.
