                                PROCLAMATION

                  OF THE GOVERNING BOARD OF
          THE FAIR OAKS RECREATION AND PARK DISTRICT

                  County of Sacramento, State of California


                                 HONORING
                          Fair Oaks Theatre Festival

 RECOGNIZING THE FAIR OAKS THEATRE FESTIVAL FOR OVER
FOUR DECADES OF DEDICATED SERVICE TO THE COMMUNITY OF
                      FAIR OAKS

WHEREAS, the Fair Oaks Theatre Festival was founded over 40 years ago by a
visionary group of local business leaders and community members who saw promise
in the underutilized Veterans Memorial Amphitheater; and

WHEREAS, under the leadership of founders Wendell and Lois Frazier, and
continued by dedicated leaders such as Tony and Barbara Sestito, the Fair Oaks
Theatre Festival provided a platform for Shakespearean productions, community
performances, educational programs, and mentorship for emerging artists; and

WHEREAS, Fair Oaks Theatre Festival became a respected cultural institution in the
Sacramento region, offering affordable, quality theatrical performances and enriching
the lives of thousands of residents and visitors alike; and

WHEREAS, the success and longevity of Fair Oaks Theatre Festival was made
possible through the tireless efforts of passionate volunteers, directors, designers,
actors, technicians, board members, and community supporters; and

WHEREAS, countless individuals, families, and organizations have supported and
contributed to the Fair Oaks Theatre Festival’s mission, including American River
College, the Fair Oaks Rotary Club, Fair Oaks Chamber of Commerce, and the Fair
Oaks Recreation and Park District itself; and

WHEREAS, the Fair Oaks Theatre Festival has officially closed its curtain for the final
time, marking the end of a beloved and influential era in Fair Oaks history;

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Board of Directors of the Fair Oaks
Recreation and Park District does hereby extend its deepest appreciation and
heartfelt recognition to the Fair Oaks Theatre Festival, its founders, leaders,
supporters, and artists for their outstanding contributions to the cultural and
community life of Fair Oaks.

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the legacy of the Fair Oaks Theatre Festival shall
be forever remembered as a cherished part of the community’s identity and cultural
history.

Subject:            Approval of Draft Sidewalk Vending Policy for
                    FORPD Parks and Facilities
I.    Recommendation
      Review and approve the Draft Sidewalk Vending Policy (Attachment A) and
      authorize staff to forward the policy to legal counsel for review. Following
      legal approval or edits, the policy will be routed for signatures and
      implemented as an official District policy.

II.   Background
      In 2018, the California Legislature passed the Safe Sidewalk Vending Act
      (SB 946), which legalized sidewalk vending statewide. This law restricts
      local governments from prohibiting vending but allows them to impose
      reasonable time, place, and manner regulations if directly tied to public
      health, safety, or welfare.

      On February 4, 2025, Sacramento County adopted Chapter 4.55 of the
      County Code requiring sidewalk vendors to obtain a Sacramento County
      business license, comply with public health regulations, and observe specific
      operational standards. Sacramento County Regional Parks also limits
      vending to designated spaces and times to maintain safety, ADA access,
      and the natural character of parks.

      As a special district within unincorporated Sacramento County, the Fair Oaks
      Recreation & Park District (FORPD) must align with these regulations. To
      address vending on District-owned parks and facilities, staff developed a
      draft policy that defines allowable vending locations, safety requirements,
      and event restrictions.
      The Policy Committee reviewed this draft at their October 7, 2025, meeting
      and recommended forwarding it to the full Board of Directors for
      consideration at the October 15, 2025, meeting. Final adoption and
       implementation will occur following legal counsel review and signature
       routing.

III.   Problem /Situation/ Request
       Currently, FORPD has no adopted policy governing sidewalk vending.
       Without such a policy:

              •   Vendors may establish in unsafe or inappropriate areas of
                  District parks.
              •   Conflicts may occur between sidewalk vendors and District-
                  sponsored events, concession agreements, or private facility
                  rentals.
              •   Staff lack clear enforcement authority for managing vending on
                  District property.

          The draft policy provides the necessary framework by:

              •   Requiring vendors to maintain County licenses, health permits,
                  and insurance.
              •   Designating vending areas that preserve pedestrian flow and
                  ADA accessibility.
              •   Prohibiting vending in or around District events, facility rentals,
                  or reservable picnic areas.
              •   Defining clear enforcement procedures and appeal processes.

IV.    Financial Analysis
       The cost to the District is unknown at this time. If legal counsel review of
       the draft policy is required, that would be the only anticipated cost to the
       District.

 consistent with California law and Sacramento County regulations, in order to protect
 public health, safety, access, and enjoyment of District parks and facilities.

 DEFINITIONS
 District Property: Parks, trails, plazas, parking lots, and building grounds owned or
 operated by FORPD.

 Sidewalk Vendor: A person who sells food or merchandise from a pushcart, stand,
 display, pedal-driven cart, wagon, showcase, rack, or similar non-motorized conveyance,
 or from their person, upon a public sidewalk or pedestrian path.

 Stationary Vendor: A sidewalk vendor who vends from a fixed location.

 Roaming Vendor: A sidewalk vendor who moves from place to place.

 REQUIRED LICENSES AND PERMITS
 All vendors must maintain and display:

    1.   A valid Sacramento County Sidewalk Vendor Business License.
    2.   A valid Environmental Management Department health permit for food/beverage
    3.   vending. A California Department of Tax and Fee Administration seller’s permit, if
    4.   applicable.
    5.   Any required FORPD-issued event or concession permit.

 TIME, PLACE, AND MANNER REGULATIONS
 Location:

    1. Vending is permitted only in areas designated by FORPD that maintain safe
       pedestrian and ADA access.
    2. A minimum 8-foot clear pedestrian/ADA pathway must be maintained at all times.
    3. No vending within 25 feet of park entrances/exits, playgrounds, restrooms, trash
       enclosures, athletic fields, courts, stages, bleachers, or fenced areas unless
       authorized by permit.
    4. Sidewalk vendors are not permitted to operate in, at, or within 25 feet of District-
       run events, event spaces, reservable picnic areas during active reservations, or
       facility rentals.
Hours:

   1. Vending is permitted only during posted park open hours, unless otherwise
      authorized.
   2. Set-up and tear-down must occur within park open hours.

Operations and Equipment:

   1. Equipment must be self-contained and maintained in good condition.
   2. Vendors must provide trash, recycling, and organics receptacles, and keep the
      area free of debris.
   3. No amplified sound without authorization.
   4. Cooking/heating equipment must meet all safety and fire code requirements.
   5. No open flames in posted fire-restricted areas.

Queueing and Crowding:

Customer lines must not block sidewalks, entrances, or drive aisles.

Concessions and Events:

   1. Sidewalk vendors may be limited, relocated, or prohibited if they conflict with
      exclusive concessions agreements, scheduled District events, rentals, or
      permitted activities.
   2. District staff may temporarily relocate or suspend vending if crowding or safety
      issues arise.

INSURANCE AND INDEMNIFICATION
   1. Vendors participating in FORPD-permitted events must provide proof of liability
      insurance naming FORPD as additional insured.
   2. Insurance may be waived for low-risk non-food vendors at District events, at staff
      discretion.
   3. Food vendors must carry insurance unless covered under an existing District-
      approved agreement (e.g., contracted food truck partners).
   4. All vendors shall indemnify and hold harmless FORPD for claims arising from
      vending activities.

ENFORCEMENT
   1. Violations may result in relocation, suspension of vending privileges, or referral to
      Sacramento County for administrative penalties.
   2. Repeat violations may result in loss of vending privileges on District Property.

APPEALS
Appeals of District decisions may be submitted in writing to the District Administrator
within 10 calendar days. The Administrator’s decision is final.

TRESPASS POLICY FOR PARKS AND RECREATION FACILITIES

PURPOSE
The purpose of this policy is to provide clear guidelines for preventing,
identifying, and responding to trespass on District-owned or operated parks,
open spaces, trails, recreation facilities, and administrative buildings. The policy
is intended to protect the safety of the public, staff, and District property while
ensuring compliance with applicable California Penal Code sections and
Sacramento County ordinances.

DEFINITIONS
District Property: All land, buildings, structures, facilities, open spaces, and
amenities owned, leased, or operated by the Fair Oaks Recreation and Park
District.
Trespass: Entering District property during posted closed hours, entering
restricted areas without written permission, or refusing to leave when directed by
authorized personnel.
Authorized Personnel: District staff, law enforcement officers, or contracted
security authorized to enforce park rules.
Notice: Verbal or written direction to vacate property, or visible signage in
compliance with California Penal Code § 602 requirements.

PROHIBITED CONDUCT
A person shall not:
   1. Enter or remain on District property outside of posted hours of operation.
   2. Enter closed facilities, fenced areas, or sections of property under maintenance
       or construction.
   3. Refuse to vacate when instructed by authorized personnel.
   4. Camp, lodge, or remain overnight without a valid permit issued by the District.
   5. Interfere with park operations, events, or maintenance activities after being
       directed to leave.
   6. Damage, deface, or otherwise misuse District property.
   7. Individuals who, after being informed by District staff of a violation of Sacramento
       County Codes, refuse to comply in a manner that creates a disruption, interferes
       with park operations, or poses a hazard to other members of the public or
       Authorized Personnel will be considered in violation of this policy. Such refusal
       undermines the safe and orderly use of District facilities and may result in
       enforcement action, including removal from District property or referral to law
       enforcement.
ENFORCEMENT PROCEDURES
  1. Initial Contact – District staff should first approach the individual in a
     professional and non-confrontational manner, identify themselves, and
     explain the violation.
  2. Request to Comply – Provide a clear verbal request to leave or correct the
     behavior.
  3. Law Enforcement Involvement – If the individual refuses to comply or
     poses a safety risk, staff shall contact the Sacramento County Sheriff’s
     Office for enforcement under Penal Code § 602 or Sacramento County
     Code § 9.80.
  4. Documentation – Staff should document the incident using Sacramento
     County’s Notice of Trespass on Public Property Official Notice form.
  5. Letter of Agency – The District will maintain a current Letter of Agency on
     file with the Sacramento County Sheriff’s Office to authorize deputies to
     enforce trespass laws on District property.

PENALTIES
  • Infraction (PC § 602.8): $75 first offense; $250 subsequent offenses within
    the same year.
  • Misdemeanor (PC § 602, SCC § 9.80): Up to $1,000 fine and/or six (6)
    months in county jail.
  • Aggravated Trespass (PC § 601): Misdemeanor or felony, punishable by
    up to three (3) years in county jail and/or $10,000 fine.

EXCEPTIONS
This policy does not apply to:
   • Activities expressly permitted by the District (e.g., special event
      reservations).
   • Individuals exercising lawful rights under state or federal law, including
      free speech activities in designated public forums, lawful picketing, or
      ADA-required access.

NOTE ON APPLICABILITY
This policy is intended to supplement, not replace or supersede, all applicable
provisions of the California Penal Code (including but not limited to Penal Code §
601 – Aggravated Trespass; Penal Code § 602 – Trespass; and Penal Code §
602.8 – Unauthorized Entry of Land) and the Sacramento County Code
(including but not limited to Chapter 9.80 – Trespass on Private Property). All
relevant state and county codes remain in full effect and are enforceable at all
times within District-managed parks and facilities. The Fair Oaks Recreation and
Park District affirms its commitment to support and abide by those laws, and this
policy is adopted to reflect the District’s role in ensuring safe, lawful, and
consistent use of its properties in alignment with those codes.

IMPLEMENTATION AND REVIEW
   • Signage – District will post operational hours and restricted areas at all
     park entrances.
   • Training – All relevant staff will receive annual training on trespass
     enforcement procedures and de-escalation techniques.
   • Coordination – District will maintain communication protocols with the
     Sacramento County Sheriff’s Office for timely response.
   • Annual Review – Policy will be reviewed annually and updated to reflect
     changes in state law, county ordinances, or District needs.

Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish guidelines for the effective and efficient
management of accounts payable, ensuring accuracy, transparency, and compliance
with applicable laws and regulations, including those specific to parks and recreation
districts. This policy aims to provide a clear process for handling payments to vendors
and contractors, maintaining financial accountability, and safeguarding district resources.

Scope

This policy applies to all departments and staff responsible for the accounts payable
function within the district, including management, accounting staff, and any other
personnel involved in the processing, approval, and payment of invoices.

Policy Statements

Vendor Selection and Contract Compliance:

All vendors providing goods or services to the District must complete and submit all
forms required by Sacramento County before payment can be issued. This includes, but
is not limited to, the Payee Data Record form.

Each contract or purchase order (PO) must be reviewed and approved by the
department manager before any invoice is processed.

Invoice Processing:

Invoices shall be reviewed for accuracy, including the verification of pricing, quantities,
services provided, and compliance with the contract or purchase order.

The accounts payable team will ensure that all necessary documentation, such as
receipts, contract terms, and approvals, are attached to the invoice before processing.

All invoices will be reviewed by the department head or designee for approval prior to
payment.

Approval Process:

Invoices must be approved by the designated manager or supervisor. The approval
process should ensure that the goods or services have been received satisfactorily, and
any discrepancies are resolved before payment is made.

Payments will only be made upon receipt of appropriate documentation, including the
invoice, proof of receipt of goods/services, and applicable approval signatures.

Documentation and Recordkeeping:

All accounts payable records, including invoices, contracts, purchase orders, and
payment documentation, must be retained in accordance with the district’s document
retention policy.

A detailed ledger of accounts payable transactions will be maintained and reconciled
monthly to ensure that the district’s financial records are accurate and up to date.

Checks and Balances:

A segregation of duties will be maintained to ensure that no one person is responsible
for all aspects of the accounts payable process (i.e., receiving, approval, and payment).
This will reduce the risk of fraud and error.

The accounts payable process will be subject to regular audits, both internal and
external, to ensure compliance with policies and standards.

Fraud Prevention and Internal Controls:

All accounts payable transactions will be subject to internal controls to prevent fraud.
These include verifying that invoices are legitimate, checking for duplicate payments,
and ensuring that all payments are authorized.

Payment Methods:

Payments may be made via check, wire transfer, or other electronic methods in
accordance with and subject to Sacramento County policy.

When making electronic payments, the accounts payable team must ensure that proper
authorization is obtained and documented.

Accounts payable transactions must be in line with the district’s budget including
maintaining sufficient reserves to cover outstanding liabilities and ensuring that funds are
available for operational needs.

Audit and Review:

The accounts payable process will be regularly reviewed and audited by internal and
external auditors to ensure compliance with the district’s financial policies and applicable
laws, including those specific to parks and recreation districts.

Any irregularities or areas for improvement identified during audits will be addressed
promptly to maintain the integrity of the district’s financial operations.

and ensures adherence to the district’s budget and financial goals.

By adhering to this Accounts Payable Policy, the district will maintain financial integrity,

regulations governing parks and recreation district operations.

   V.      Cash Handling Policy

Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to establish consistent procedures for receiving, recording,
verifying, and securely storing cash prior to bank deposit, in order to safeguard District
assets and ensure accountability.

Policy Statement

All cash received by the District must be properly recorded, verified, secured, and stored
in accordance with this policy. Exceptions may only be granted by the Administrative
Manager and the Accounting Supervisor when operational limitations prevent full
compliance.

Recording Cash Receipts:

All cash received must be immediately recorded in one of the District’s approved
payment systems.

Each cash receipt must be tied to the corresponding purchase, program, or transaction.

Verification at Time of Receipt:

The staff member who accepts cash is responsible for counting it in the presence of the
payer to ensure the amount is correct.

The amount received must match what is recorded in the payment system.

Daily Balancing:

At the end of each business day or staff shift, cash received must be balanced against
the payment system reports.

The Accounting Supervisor or Administrative Manager may waive this balancing
requirement in limited cases where staffing, timing, or resources do not allow. Any
waivers must be documented.

Securing Cash During the Day:

At the end of the day or shift, all cash must be totaled, placed under lock and key, and
safeguarded until transfer to the District safe.

Access to locked storage areas shall be restricted to authorized personnel only.

Safe Storage:




              Fair Oaks Recreation & Park District – Financial Policies Manual | Page 13
Before placing cash in the safe, the funds must be counted and verified by two staff
members.

The cash shall be placed in a sealed envelope, with both staff members’ initials on the
envelope to confirm the count.

Each envelope must include the payment system report or other documentation showing
the amount of cash recorded for that day/shift.

Sealed envelopes must be placed in the District safe and remain there until they are
prepared in accordance with the District’s separate Deposit Policy.

Responsibilities

Staff Members – Responsible for accurately recording, counting, and securing cash in
compliance with this policy.

Supervisors/Managers – Responsible for ensuring staff follow cash handling procedures
and for addressing any discrepancies.

Administrative Manager – Provides oversight, approves exceptions, and ensures proper
compliance with this policy.

Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to establish guidelines for the issuance, management, and
reporting of debt by the Fair Oaks Recreation & Park District (“the District”). The policy
ensures compliance with California law, Sacramento County requirements for
independent special districts, and Governmental Accounting Standards Board (GASB)
rules, while maintaining fiscal responsibility and transparency.

Policy Statements

The District may issue debt, including General Obligation Bonds and other permitted
financing instruments, for the purpose of financing capital improvements in compliance
with California law and voter authorization.

Debt shall not be issued for operational expenses.

Proceeds shall be used solely for voter-approved or Board-authorized projects.

The District will maintain repayment schedules and disclose obligations annually in
audited financial statements, consistent with GASB requirements.

Legal and Regulatory Compliance:

California Law – Debt issuance will comply with provisions of the California Government
Code applicable to independent special districts.

Sacramento County Requirements – The District will follow any County reporting and
oversight requirements related to debt issuance.

GASB Standards – Debt obligations will be recorded and disclosed in accordance with
GASB Statements, including:

GASB 34: Government-wide financial reporting.

GASB 88: Notes disclosure for direct borrowings and placements.

GASB 87: Leases, where applicable.

Permitted Uses of Debt:

Voter-approved General Obligation Bonds.

Lease revenue bonds, certificates of participation, or other Board-authorized debt
instruments.

Refunding obligations, provided there is a demonstrable benefit such as cost savings or
improved structuring.

Short-term borrowing for cash flow management, if approved by the Board.

Debt Limitations:

Debt may only be issued when supported by reliable revenue streams sufficient to cover
repayment.

The District will comply with constitutional and statutory debt limits, including Proposition
13 limits on ad valorem property tax–backed bonds.

Long-term debt shall not exceed the useful life of the asset financed.

Debt Management and Accounting:

The Administrative Services Department will:

   •   Maintain complete debt service schedules.
   •   Ensure timely payment of principal and interest.
   •   Record and disclose debt obligations in the District’s audited financial statements
       per GASB.

assist with market disclosure, continuing disclosure filings, rating agency
communication, and other specialized compliance activities.

Oversight and Approval

All debt issuance must be authorized by the Board of Directors.

Voter authorization is required for General Obligation Bonds.

The Administrative Services Department will provide the Board with updates on
outstanding debt, repayment status, and future debt capacity.

   X.        Capital Assets Policy

Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to establish uniform guidelines for the acquisition,
management, depreciation, and disposal of capital assets. This ensures compliance with
Governmental Accounting Standards Board (GASB) requirements, promotes
accountability, and provides accurate financial reporting.

Definitions (per GASB)

   •    Capital Assets – Land, land improvements, buildings, infrastructure, machinery,
        equipment, and intangible assets with a useful life greater than one year and an
        initial cost of $5,000 or more .
   •    Construction in Progress (CIP) – Capital outlays for construction or development
        of assets not yet completed or ready for service. CIP is reported separately until
        the project is substantially complete, at which point costs are reclassified to the
        appropriate category .
   •    Infrastructure Assets – Long-lived, stationary assets such as roads, drainage,
        lighting, or pathways.
   •    Intangible Assets – Assets lacking physical substance but providing value (e.g.,
        easements, land use rights, or internally developed software).
   •    Non-Depreciable Assets – Land, CIP, and certain intangible assets with indefinite
        useful lives.

Policy Statements

Capitalization Threshold:

Capital assets must have an initial individual cost of $5,000 or more and an estimated
useful life of greater than one year .

Items under the threshold will be expensed in the year of purchase.

Valuation:

Capital assets are recorded at historical cost or, if donated, at estimated acquisition
value on the date received .

Depreciation:

Depreciation is calculated using the straight-line method over estimated useful lives with
no residual value unless otherwise required.

Useful lives follow GASB guidance and District practice:



               Fair Oaks Recreation & Park District – Financial Policies Manual | Page 22
   •   Buildings: 50 years
   •   Building Improvements: 20 years
   •   Improvements other than buildings: 35 years
   •   Infrastructure: 50 years
   •   Equipment and Machinery: 5–20 years
   •   Land, CIP, and intangible assets with indefinite lives are not depreciated.

Additions and Improvements:

Expenditures that significantly extend the useful life or increase the value of an asset
shall be capitalized.

Routine repairs and maintenance are expensed as incurred.

Deletions and Disposals:

When assets are sold, traded, or retired:

The asset’s cost and accumulated depreciation will be removed from the accounts.

Any gain or loss will be recognized in the financial statements.

Oversight:

The Administrative Services Department maintains the Fixed Asset Register, updates
balances annually, and ensures compliance with GASB standards.

The Board of Directors must approve all additions and deletions of capital assets.

   XIV. Investment Policy

Purpose

To establish guidelines for the investment of District funds, ensuring the preservation of
principal and compliance with the Sacramento County Pooled Investment Fund's
(SCPIF) policies and applicable law.

Scope

This policy applies to all financial assets of the District that are available for investment.

Policy Statements

Delegation of Investment Authority:

The District delegates its investment authority to the County of Sacramento, which
manages the District's funds within the Sacramento County Pooled Investment Fund
(SCPIF). The County's Director of Finance, serving as the Trustee of the Pooled
Investment Fund, is responsible for all investment decisions, adhering to the California
Government Code and the County's Annual Investment Policy.

Investment Objectives

The primary objectives, in order of priority, are:

   1. Safety of Principal – Preservation of capital by avoiding losses from default or
      market fluctuations.
   2. Liquidity – Maintaining sufficient liquidity to meet the District's operational
      requirements.
   3. Return on Investment – Achieving a reasonable return while maintaining safety
      and liquidity.

Authorized Investments:

All investments shall conform to the instruments permitted by the Sacramento County
Annual Investment Policy and the California Government Code.

The SCPIF primarily invests in high-quality, short- to medium-term fixed-income
securities that ensure preservation of principal.

Diversification:

Because District funds are invested through the SCPIF, diversification is managed at the
County level. The County’s Investment Policy outlines diversification strategies to
mitigate risks.



              Fair Oaks Recreation & Park District – Financial Policies Manual | Page 31
Investing Outside the County Pool:

The District’s funds are generally invested through the SCPIF; however, under California
Government Code Section 53684 and the Sacramento County Pooled Investment Fund
Policy, local agencies may request to withdraw funds for the purpose of investing or
depositing them outside of the County pool.

Any such investment requires:

Board of Directors approval by formal action.

   •   A written request submitted to the Sacramento County Treasurer.
   •   Review and evaluation by the Treasurer to ensure that the withdrawal will not
       adversely impact the interests of other pool participants.

No investments outside the SCPIF may be made without compliance with these
requirements.

Reporting:

The County of Sacramento provides regular reports on the SCPIF’s performance. The
District shall review these reports to ensure funds are being managed in accordance
with this policy.

Policy Review

This policy shall be reviewed annually and updated as necessary to remain aligned with
the County’s Investment Policy, California Government Code, and the District’s financial
objectives.

References

California Government Code Sections governing the investment of public funds

      Staff recommends approving the purchase of a paint striping machine for

      Parking lots throughout the District are in immediate need of re-striping

      addition, Recreation programs regularly require lines to be painted for

      2024/2025 fiscal year at which time it ceased functioning.

      overall.

      example, current quotes for parking lot striping at Phoenix Park alone
      would cost the District roughly $20,000. Utilizing a paint striping machine

        currently being used for spray cans of field marking paint.

        was removed from the parks department’s operational budget. Staff still
        feel that the machine would be an important asset to the District, and is
        now requesting that the purchase be capitalized and made utilizing Fair
        Oaks Landscape & Lighting funds.

        Though the environmental impact of an electric striper is much less, the

II.   Background
      LAFCos are state-mandated quasi-judicial countywide Commissions whose
      purview is to oversee boundary changes of cities and special districts, the
      formation of new agencies, including the incorporation of new cities and
      districts, and the consolidation or reorganization of special districts and or
      cities. LAFCo has the power to approve, modify or deny proposals for
      Spheres of Influence (SOI), Annexations/Detachments, Incorporation,
      Formation of Special Districts, and Consolidations.

      The broad goals of the Commission's directive are to ensure the orderly
      formation of local governmental agencies, to preserve agricultural and open
      space lands, and to discourage urban sprawl. Commissions must, by law,
      create Municipal Service Reviews and Update Spheres of Influence for each
      independent local governmental jurisdiction within their countywide
      jurisdiction.

         Applicant Statement for the Alternate Special District LAFCO Representative

I moved from Glassboro New Jersey to Rio Linda Elverta in 1981, along with my 3 year old
daughter and my husband.

Almost immediately I became involved with Sacramento County Service Area #3 which was
the forerunner of our current independent Parks and Recreation District serving the Rio Linda
Elverta Communities. This involvement was the result of reading a statement in the local pa-
per, indicating that the Parks Advisory Board had determined that there were sufficient eques-
trian trails in the area. I was very happy to advocate for additional trails and in the process
begin my education in local governance processes.

Over the next several decades I was appointed to numerous County and Local advisory
boards, steering committees and ad hoc committees. My interest expanded to include growth
issues, flood issues and agriculture/suburban/urban issues.

I learned a great deal about how government works and how Special Districts fit into the pro-
cess. I became a strong advocate for local governance after going through 3 incorporation
attempts in the Rio Linda Elverta communities and in the education process I switched from
anti-incorporation to pro-incorporation for unincorporated communities.

In 2002 I successfully ran for the Rio Linda Elverta Recreation and Parks District. I truly en-
joyed and have greatly benefited from the experience of becoming an elected board member.
I ran again in 2016 and 2020 am currently serving as Chair for the RLE Recreation and Parks
District. I have taken numerous SDLA courses and Board Training seminars and I continue to
learn about the process of governance.

I anticipate taking CALAFCO U Webinar 1 July 17, 2025 Commissioner Best Practices in order
to continue educating myself about LAFCO. I have participated in several LAFCO decisions
as an alternate and I hope to continue to do so in the future.

As Chair of RLEPD I requested investigating the very opaque process of County funding for
Special Districts and into the ERAF funds that are taken from Special Districts by the State ev-
ery year. These investigations are prompted by what I precieve as very unfair and unfanthom-
able processes and procedures for funding Special Districts. I believe that all Special Districts
statewide will need to be included in any actions regarding this funding.

I believe that my history in the community and experience as an elected public official will be
an asset for all Special Districts as a LAFCO Special District Alternate Board member.

Thank you for your consideration,

Charlea R. Moore
           STAFF REPORT

Meeting Date:        October 15, 2025

To:                  Fair Oaks Recreation Foundation Board of Directors

From:                Michael J. Aho, District Administrator

Subject:             Approval of Foundation Name Change


I.     Recommendation
       Staff recommends that the Board of Directors review and approve a new
       name for the Foundation, to be formally incorporated into the amended and
       restated Bylaws.

II.    Background
       The current Bylaws of the Foundation identify the corporation as the Fair
       Oaks Recreation Foundation Over the past several months, the District has
       been working with counsel to update and revise the Foundation’s Bylaws to
       better reflect its evolving mission and role in supporting arts, culture,
       recreation, and community services in Fair Oaks.

       As part of this process, it has been determined that a new name for the
       Foundation would provide clarity, visibility, and a stronger connection to the
       broader community identity. Staff has developed a short list of potential
       names to serve as a starting point for discussion, attached as Attachment
       A.

III.   Problem /Situation/ Request
       In order to submit final documents to the California Secretary of State, the
       Foundation must select a new legal name to incorporate into the Bylaws.
       Staff is requesting that the Board of Directors:
          • Review the attached list of potential names.
          • Select one of the suggested names, or propose an alternative name
               of their own choosing.
          • Approve a final name to be included in the revised Bylaws.
       The revised Bylaws are scheduled to be presented to the Foundation and
       District Board of Directors at the November meeting, along with the selected
       name.

IV.    Financial Analysis
       There is no direct financial cost associated with adopting a new Foundation
       name.


Respectfully Submitted,



Michael J. Aho
District Administrator
