                     Announcement for Recruitment Process

I. Recommendation
Staff recommends that the Board of Directors approve the District Administrator
Job Announcement as presented, authorizing its use in the upcoming recruitment
process to fill the position following the retirement of the current District
Administrator effective June 1, 2026.

II. Background
The Fair Oaks Recreation and Park District (FORPD) is initiating the recruitment
process for the position of District Administrator in anticipation of the retirement
of the current Administrator, Michael J. Aho, effective June 1, 2026. The District
will prepared a comprehensive recruitment brochure outlining the role,
responsibilities, and qualifications for the position. This brochure reflects the
District’s strategic priorities, organizational culture, and community values, and is
intended to attract highly qualified candidates to lead FORPD into its next phase
of growth and development.

III. Problem /Situation/ Request
With the current District Administrator scheduled to retire effective June 1, 2026,
the Fair Oaks Recreation and Park District must begin the recruitment process to
ensure a smooth leadership transition. The District is developing a
comprehensive job announcement and recruitment brochure that outlines the
position’s responsibilities, qualifications, and strategic priorities. Board approval
of this announcement is required prior to its public release and use in the
recruitment process.

IV. Financial Analysis
At this time, there are no direct financial impacts associated with the approval of
the District Administrator Job Announcement. However, staff is currently
evaluating potential recruitment firms to assist with the hiring process. A
separate request for Board approval of a recruitment firm, along with associated
costs, is anticipated to be presented at the December 2025 Board meeting.

V. Summary of Key Changes
The District Administrator Job Announcement has been updated to reflect the
District’s current strategic priorities, including long-term planning, sustainability,
and community partnerships. The brochure highlights recent capital
improvements, outlines new organizational goals, and emphasizes leadership
qualities aligned with FORPD’s evolving mission. These updates are intended to
attract candidates with the vision and experience to guide the District into its
next chapter.

THE OPPORTUNITY

The Fair Oaks Recreation and Park District (FORPD) invites applications for the position of
District Administrator — a unique opportunity to lead a dynamic and community-centered
organization entering its next era of growth.

Following nearly a decade of transformation, FORPD has emerged as a regional model for
recreation, arts, and cultural engagement. Through the successful management of a $26.9
million Measure J bond program, the District has delivered award-winning public facilities
including the Fair Oaks Village Park campus, the Veterans Memorial Amphitheater, the
Black Box Theater, the Community Clubhouse, and the Arts & Crafts Building.

The new District Administrator will build on this momentum, guiding the District through
operational expansion, long-term master planning, and a growing emphasis on
sustainability, partnerships, and innovation.

THE ORGANIZATION

Founded in 1945, the Fair Oaks Recreation and Park District is an independent special
district serving approximately 32,000 residents in northern Sacramento County. The
District encompasses over 11.25 square miles, offering a blend of urban convenience and
small-town character.

FORPD operates and maintains twelve community parks and open spaces that blend
modern amenities with the natural character of Fair Oaks. In addition, the District manages
key community facilities—including the Macmillan Center, the Fair Oaks Preschool, the
former library facility, and the District Office—which together support a broad range of
public programs and services. The District also oversees the newly redeveloped cultural and
recreation campus at Village Park, home to the 480-seat Veterans Memorial Amphitheater,
the Black Box Theater, community meeting rooms, and multiple versatile event venues that
anchor the community’s arts, culture, and recreation offerings.


The District offers a diverse portfolio of recreation and enrichment programs, including
youth and adult sports, senior activities, arts and cultural programs, and signature
community events such as the Concerts in the Park series and seasonal festivals.

The District is governed by a five-member elected Board of Directors, who set policy and
provide strategic direction. The District Administrator serves as the chief executive officer,
responsible for carrying out Board policies, overseeing all operations, and ensuring fiscal
and organizational excellence.

THE COMMUNITY

Nestled along the American River Parkway between the cities of Citrus Heights and Rancho
Cordova, Fair Oaks is known for its small-town spirit, cultural vitality, and strong civic
engagement.

Old Fair Oaks Village serves as the heart of the community—a gathering place for events,
markets, performances, and festivals that celebrate local heritage and creativity. Residents
enjoy access to miles of trails, regional parks, and outdoor recreation while remaining just
20 minutes from downtown Sacramento.

The District’s partnerships with the Fair Oaks Chamber of Commerce, Fair Oaks Recreation
and Arts Foundation, Rotary Club, and local schools foster a deeply connected network of
community support and collaboration.

THE POSITION

Reporting directly to the Board of Directors, the District Administrator provides leadership
and management for all aspects of the District’s operations, capital projects, financial
management, and community services.

Key responsibilities include:

Implementing Board policies and guiding long-term strategic planning.

Overseeing annual budgets, capital improvement programs, and funding strategies.

Directing the operation and maintenance of all District parks, facilities, and programs.

Fostering community partnerships and intergovernmental relationships.
Providing leadership and mentorship to the management team and staff.

Serving as the District’s primary representative to the public, media, and partner agencies.


The District Administrator directly supervises: Administrative Services Manager, Parks &
Facilities Manager, Recreation Manager, and Arts & Entertainment Manager.

DISTRICT PRIORITIES

As the District transitions from a decade of construction to long-term operation and
sustainability, the incoming District Administrator will lead initiatives that define FORPD’s
future:

Comprehensive Planning and Facility Development

Lead the update of the District Master Plan (last revised in 2010) to align with community
needs and demographics.

Oversee design, funding, and community outreach for the renovation of La Vista Elementary
School Park fields, with a primary focus on developing facilities for girls’ softball while also
supporting additional recreational opportunities for community use.

Advance Phoenix Park improvements, including expanded parking, modernization, and
development of underutilized parcels.

Complete Fair Oaks Park renovations, with enhancements to the skate park and other high-
use areas.

Implement long-term planning for the Village Park campus, ensuring sustainable operations
and community access.

Asset Management and Infrastructure Renewal

Establish a Facility Repair and Replacement Plan to guide ongoing capital investment.

Develop a Vehicle, Equipment, and Technology Replacement Program to improve efficiency
and safety.

Prioritize ADA accessibility, energy efficiency, and sustainability in all capital projects.

Financial Sustainability and Funding Strategy

Expand pursuit of grants, partnerships, and sponsorships to diversify revenue sources.

Strengthen long-term financial planning and cost recovery systems.

Ensure fiscal accountability while supporting innovation and program growth.
Support funding of scholarship programs, unfunded liabilities, and community benefit
initiatives, while demonstrating knowledge of assessment and revenue-generating district
mechanisms—such as benefit, entertainment, or parking assessments—to expand long-
term funding opportunities.

Organizational Development and Culture

Promote collaboration, transparency, and professional growth across all departments.

Strengthen leadership capacity through mentorship and succession planning.

Modernize policies, procedures, and operational standards to ensure consistency and
efficiency.

Community Engagement and Partnerships

Maintain strong relationships with residents, businesses, and civic organizations to support
community initiatives.

Enhance visibility of District programs and facilities through effective marketing and
outreach.

Continue building community goodwill and support for future investments in parks, arts,
and recreation.

Support and strengthen the Fair Oaks Recreation and Arts Foundation as a key partner in
advancing the District’s mission.

The ideal candidate will be a visionary, collaborative, and strategic leader with a strong
commitment to public service and community engagement. This individual will possess:

Proven success in parks, recreation, or public agency management.

Experience in arts and cultural programming and operations.

Strong financial acumen and experience with capital project oversight.

Exceptional communication skills and the ability to build trust across diverse stakeholder
groups.

Political and organizational awareness to navigate complex partnerships and governance
structures.

A leadership style that empowers staff, fosters innovation, and models accountability.

offers a comprehensive benefits package including:

- Medical, dental, vision, and life insurance coverage.
- Vacation, sick leave, and management leave allowances.
- Professional development support and longevity benefits.

Meeting Date:     November 19, 2025

To:               Fair Oaks Recreation Foundation Board of Directors

From:             Michael J. Aho, District Administrator

Subject:          Revision of Foundation Bylaws


I.    Recommendation
      It is recommended that the Board of Directors approve the
      revised bylaws of the Fair Oaks Recreation and Arts Foundation
      to reflect the updated organizational name, structure, and
      governance provisions.

II.   Background
      The Fair Oaks Recreation Foundation has undergone a strategic
      review of its mission and organizational structure to better align
      with its evolving role in supporting arts, culture, recreation, and
      community services in Fair Oaks. Over the past several months,
      the District has worked closely with legal counsel to update and
      revise the Foundation’s Bylaws.

      At the October 15, 2025 Board meeting, the Foundation
      approved a new organizational name — Fair Oaks Recreation
      and Arts Foundation — to better reflect its expanded mission and
      community identity. The revised Bylaws incorporate this name
      change and include updates to governance provisions,
      membership classifications, officer responsibilities, and
      compliance with applicable laws such as the Ralph M. Brown Act.
      These changes are intended to enhance the Foundation’s clarity,
      visibility, and connection to the broader community, while
      ensuring continued support for the Fair Oaks Recreation and Park
      District and local nonprofit partners.

III. Problem /Situation/ Request
     In order to finalize the legal transition of the Foundation’s name
     and governance structure, the Board must formally adopt the
     revised Bylaws of the Fair Oaks Recreation and Arts Foundation.
     These Bylaws incorporate the new name approved at the
     October 15, 2025 Board meeting and reflect updates to the
     Foundation’s mission, officer roles, membership classifications,
     and compliance with California nonprofit law and the Ralph M.
     Brown Act.
     Staff requests that the Board of Directors:
        • Review the proposed revised Bylaws;
        • Approve the final version for adoption;
        • Authorize submission of the amended Articles of
            Incorporation to the California Secretary of State to reflect
            the name change and updated governance provisions.

   Summary of Key Amendments The updated Bylaws of the Fair
   Oaks Recreation and Arts Foundation reflect these principal
   modifications:
     • Organizational Name: Changed from Fair Oaks Recreation
        Foundation to Fair Oaks Recreation and Arts Foundation.
     • Purpose and Mission: Expanded to include support for arts,
        entertainment, and human needs services.
     • Membership Structure: Eliminated voting members;
        introduced General Members and Community Partner
        Members for engagement.
     • Board Composition and Governance:
           o 5 to 7 Directors, with no more than two appointed by
               the FORPD Board.
           o Two-year staggered terms.
           o District Administrator serves as non-voting CEO.
     • Officers     and Responsibilities: Clarified roles for
        Chairperson, Vice-Chairperson, Secretary, Treasurer, and
        CEO.
     • Compliance and Transparency: Required adherence to
        the Ralph M. Brown Act, annual audits, and public financial
        disclosures.
      •   Indemnification and Insurance: Expanded coverage and
          authority to purchase liability insurance.
      •   Amendment Procedures: Certain sections require
          unanimous Board approval for changes.

IV.   Financial Analysis
      There is no direct financial cost associated with the revision and
      adoption of the Foundation’s Bylaws or the legal name change
      to the Fair Oaks Recreation and Arts Foundation. The filing of
      the Certificate of Amendment with the California Secretary of
      State incurs a standard fee of $30, with an optional $5 fee for a
      certified copy. These minor administrative costs are covered
      within the District’s existing operational budget and do not
      require additional Board appropriation.

      Legal review and document preparation were conducted in
      coordination with District counsel and are part of ongoing
      administrative support for the Foundation.

                                           ARTICLE I
                                            NAME

1.01. Name. The name of this Corporation shall be Fair Oaks Recreation and Arts Foundation, a
California nonprofit public benefit corporation (herein referred to as the "Corporation" or the
“Foundation”).

                                           ARTICLE II
                                            OFFICE

2.01. Principal Office. The principal office for the transaction of business of the Foundation
(“Principal Executive Office") is located at: 4150 Temescal Street, Fair Oaks, CA 95628.

2.02. Change of Address. The Directors may change the principal office from one location to
another. The Secretary of the Foundation shall note any change of this location.

                                         ARTICLE III
                                   OBJECTIVE AND PURPOSES

3.01. General Purpose. The Foundation is a nonprofit public benefit corporation and is not
organized for the private gain of any person. It is organized under the Nonprofit Public Benefit
Corporation Law for public charitable purposes.

3.02. Specific Purpose. The specific purpose of the Foundation is:

       (1) To support, assist, and augment the Fair Oaks Recreation and Park District (“FORPD”
           or “District") and other nonprofit organizations serving the Fair Oaks community by
           advancing parks and recreation, arts and entertainment, and human needs services
           consistent with FORPD’s mission;

       (2) To build the Fair Oaks community through partnerships that mutually benefit the
           nonprofit groups of the Fair Oaks area, FORPD, and the members of the community in
           general;

       (3) To assist and carry out the above-mentioned goals and objectives by receiving,
           managing and disbursing funds and property, including real property, exclusively for
           the benefit of the FORPD and nonprofit organizations in the Fair Oaks area. Such
           assistance may include distributions to government organizations with similar
           objectives and/or nonprofit organizations that have qualified for tax-exempt status under
           the Internal Revenue Code;
                                                                                         8452.001-8924778.4
       (4) To promote the Fair Oaks community in the attainment of its visions, goals and
           objectives; and

       (5) To be recognized as a public charity so that all contributions to the Foundation shall be
           treated as tax deductible under Section 170 of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986, as
           amended.

3.03. Limitations. The Foundation shall not, except to an insubstantial degree, engage in any
activities or exercise any powers that are not in furtherance of the purposes of the Foundation, and
the Foundation shall not carry on any other activities not permitted to be carried on (i) by a
corporation exempt from Federal income tax under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code
of 1986, as amended, or the corresponding section of any future Federal tax code, or (ii) by a
corporation, contributions to which are deductible under Section 170(c)(2) of the Internal Revenue
Code of 1986, as amended, or the corresponding provisions of any future Federal tax code.

                                           ARTICLE IV
                                      DEDICATION OF ASSETS

4.01. Assets. All corporate assets are irrevocably dedicated to the purposes set forth above. No
part of the net earnings of the Foundation shall inure to the benefit of any of its Directors, trustees,
officers, or to individuals.
                                             ARTICLE V
                                            MEMBERSHIP

5.01. Members Prohibited. The Foundation shall not have any members.

5.02. Effect of Prohibition. Any action, which would otherwise require approval by a majority of
all members or approval by the members, shall require only approval of the Foundation Board of
Directors. All rights, which would otherwise vest under the Nonprofit Corporation Law in the
members, shall vest in the Directors.

5.03. Other Persons Associated with Foundation. The Foundation may refer to other persons or
entities associated with it as "members" or "associates" even though those persons or entities are
not voting members under these Bylaws, but no such reference shall constitute anyone a member
within the meaning of Section 5056 of the California Corporation Code. By amendment of its
Articles of Incorporation or these Bylaws, the Foundation may grant some rights to members or
associates, but no such person or entity shall be a member within the meaning of Section 5056 of
the California Corporation Code.

5.04. Classes of Membership. For the purpose of building community support for the Foundation,
the Foundation Board may create the following classes of membership:

           •   General membership (open to any person, company, or nonprofit entity); and
           •   Community Partner membership (open to any nonprofit group that has a
               partnership agreement with FORPD).

                                             ARTICLE VI
                                             DIRECTORS

6.01. Number of Authorized Directors. The Foundation shall have no fewer than five (5) and no
more than seven (7) Directors. The exact number of Directors may be modified from time to time
by amendment of these Bylaws, duly adopted by the Board of Directors, consistent with the
qualifications and structure outlined herein.

6.02.   Qualifications and Restrictions.

        (1) No more than two current elected Board members of the FORPD, appointed by the
            District Board of Directors;

        (2) Individuals from the community-at-large or community partners, elected by the
            Foundation Board of Directors;

        (3) Other members elected by vote of the Foundation Board of Directors; and

        (4) The District Administrator of the FORPD, or their designated appointee, shall serve as
            Chief Executive Officer of the Foundation in an ex-officio, nonvoting capacity.

6.03.   Term of Office.

        (1) Each Director appointed by the District Board of Directors shall serve a term of two (2)
            years. These individuals may be reappointed by the District Board for successive two-
            year terms without term limits, provided they continue to serve on the District Board.

        (2) All other Directors elected by the Foundation Board shall also serve two-year terms and
            may be re-elected to successive terms by vote of the Foundation Board of Directors.
            There shall be no limit on the number of terms a Director may serve, provided they are
            duly re-elected.

        (3) To ensure continuity of governance, terms shall be staggered so that approximately one-
            half of the Director positions expire each year.

        (4) In all cases, a Director shall continue to serve until a successor is duly appointed or
            elected, unless the Director resigns, is removed, or becomes otherwise ineligible to
            serve.

6.04. Vacancies. Any Director may resign at any time by giving written notice to the Chairperson,

                                              MEETINGS

7.01. Brown Act. To the extent required by law, the Foundation shall comply with the provisions
of the Ralph M. Brown Act, Chapter 9 of Part 1 of Division 2 of Title 5 of the California
Government Code (commencing with section 54950) (the "Brown Act").




                                                    4
7.02. Annual Meeting. The annual meeting of the Foundation shall be held on the fourth Monday
of June for the purpose of electing the Board of Directors, as necessary, the officers of the
Foundation and conducting any other business or transactions as shall come before the meeting.
Notice of the annual meeting shall be given in writing not less than ten (10) days prior to said
meeting date.

7.03. Regular Meetings. Regular meetings of the Board of Directors shall be held at such time
and place as the Board may determine. Notice of the regular meetings shall be given ten (10) days
prior to the meeting.

7.04. Special Meeting Notice. Special meetings of the Board of Directors shall be held whenever
called by the Chairperson of the Board or by two or more of the Directors. Notice of each such
special meeting shall be mailed, via first-class mail, to each Director, addressed to the Director at
his or her residence or usual place of business, at least ten (10) days before the day on which the
meeting is to be held, or to be delivered personally or by telephone, no later than ten (10) days
before the day on which the meeting is to be held, except in the event of an emergency within the
meaning of the Brown Act. Each such notice shall state the time and place of the meeting but need
not state the purposes thereof except as otherwise herein expressly provided. Any meeting of the
Board shall be a legal meeting without any notice thereof having been given, if all the Directors
then in office shall be present thereat or waive such notice in writing before, at or after such meeting.

7.05. Waiver of Notice. The transactions of any meeting of the Board of Directors, however
called and noticed or wherever held, shall be as valid as though taken at a meeting duly held after
regular call and notice if a quorum is present and, either before or after the meeting, each of the
Directors either signs a written waiver of notice, a consent to holding the meeting, or an approval
of the minutes. The waiver of notice or consent need not specify the purpose of the meeting. All
waiver, consents, and approvals shall be filed with the corporate records or made a part of the
minutes of the meeting.

7.06. Quorum. A majority of the authorized number of Directors shall constitute a quorum for
the transaction of business. Every act or decision done or made by the majority of the Directors
present at a meeting duly held at which a quorum is present shall be regarded as the act of the Board
of Directors, subject to the provisions of the California Nonprofit Corporation Law, especially those
provisions relating to (i) approval of contracts or transactions in which a Director has a direct or
indirect material financial interest, (ii) appointment of committees, and (iii) indemnification of
Directors. A meeting at which a quorum is initially present may continue to transact business,
notwithstanding the withdrawal of Directors, if any action taken is approved by at least a majority
of the required quorum for that meeting.

7.07. Conduct of Meeting. The Chairperson of the Board, or in his or her absence, any Director
selected by the Directors present, shall preside at the meeting of the Board of Directors. The
Secretary of the Foundation, or in the Secretary's absence, any person appointed by the presiding
officer shall act as a Secretary of the Board.

7.08. Place of Meetings. Meetings shall be held at the Principal Executive Office of the
Foundation unless otherwise provided by the Board of Directors or at such place within or outside
the State of California, which has been designated from time to time in the notice of meeting or by
resolution of the Board of Directors. Any meeting, regular or special, may be held by conference
telephone or similar communications equipment, so long as the Brown Act is complied with and
all Directors participating in such meeting can hear one another.

employment of any officer of the Foundation. Except on notice to the California Attorney

Chief Executive Officer, such vacancy shall be filled temporarily by appointment by the
Chairperson of the Board or the Chief Executive Officer until such time as the vacancy can be filled
in the manner prescribed in these Bylaws for regular appointment to that office.

8.06.   Responsibilities of Officers.

        (1) Chairperson of the Board:
           The Chairperson of the Board (the “Chairperson”) shall preside at all meetings of the
           Board of Directors and shall be the principal representative of the Board in overseeing
           the affairs of the Foundation. The Chairperson shall ensure that all orders and resolutions
           of the Board are implemented, work in close consultation with the Chief Executive
           Officer, appoint committee members (subject to Board approval), and perform such
           other duties as may be prescribed by the Board of Directors or these Bylaws. The
           Chairperson shall also serve as the spokesperson for the Foundation, unless otherwise
           delegated.

       (2) Vice-Chairperson of the Board:
          The Vice-Chairperson of the Board (the “Vice-Chair”) shall act in place of the
          Chairperson in the event of the Chairperson’s absence, inability, or refusal to act. In
          such instances, the Vice-Chair shall perform all the duties of the Chairperson and shall
          have all the powers of and be subject to the same restrictions as the Chairperson. The
          Vice-Chair shall also perform such other duties as may be assigned by the Board of

          Board of Directors.

          Foundation, to execute contracts, financial instruments, and other official documents on
          behalf of the Foundation. The CEO shall ensure proper maintenance of financial and
          operational records, oversee the deposit of all funds, and regularly report on the
          Foundation’s financial and organizational status to the Foundation Board.

          The CEO shall perform such additional duties as may be assigned by the Chairperson
          or the Foundation Board of Directors.

          The Secretary shall be responsible for ensuring that accurate minutes of all Board
          meetings are taken and maintained, including records of votes, motions, and actions.

          Bylaws and applicable laws. The Secretary shall maintain the corporate records of the
          Foundation, including Articles of Incorporation, Bylaws, Board policies, and official
          correspondence. The Secretary may delegate minute-taking duties to a staff person or
          designee, but remains responsible for their accuracy and official recordkeeping.

          The Treasurer shall oversee the financial matters of the Foundation and shall ensure
          accurate books of account are maintained, including records of income, disbursements,
          assets, and liabilities. The Treasurer shall provide regular financial reports to the Board,
          assist in the preparation and presentation of the annual budget, and ensure compliance
          with all financial and tax reporting requirements. The Treasurer shall serve as Chair of
          the Finance Committee (if such a committee is established) and shall perform all other
          duties incident to the office of Treasurer or as assigned by the Board.

Directors in any number that the Directors may deem necessary. The Advisors shall have no vote

                                   ARTICLE X
         PERMITTED USES OF PRINCIPAL AND INCOME AND RELATED MATTERS

10.01. Permitted Uses. It shall be the policy of this Foundation to use its funds for one or more of
the purposes for which the Foundation is organized, including administrative expenses and amounts
paid to acquire an asset used (or held for use) in carrying out one or more of its purposes, in amounts

in the carrying out of its mission, goals and objectives.

10.02. No Self-Dealing. It shall be the policy of the Foundation not to engage in any act, which
would constitute "self-dealing" as defined in Section 4941 (d) of the Internal Revenue Code of
1986, as amended.

10.03. No Jeopardy Investments. It shall be the policy of the Foundation to assure that no funds,
whether title thereto is vested in the Foundation or is vested in trust for the benefit of the Foundation,
are knowingly invested or reinvested in such a manner as to jeopardize the carrying out of any of
the purposes for which the Foundation is organized.


10.04. Compensation and Reimbursement. No Director shall receive directly or indirectly any
compensation for his or her services as Director. No benefit shall inure to the Directors as a result
of their participation on the Board of the Foundation. Directors may receive reimbursement of
expenses incurred in services to the Board as the Board may determine to be just and reasonable.

                               ARTICLE XI
   INDEMNIFICATION OF DIRECTORS, OFFICERS, EMPLOYEES, AND OTHER AGENTS

11.01. Definition. For purpose of this Article:

       (1) Agent. The term "agent" means any person who is or was a Director, officer, employee,
           or other agent of the Foundation, or is or was serving at the request of the Foundation
           as a Director, officer, employee, or agent of another foreign or domestic foundation,
           partnership, joint venture, trust, or other enterprise;

       (2) Proceeding. The term "proceeding" means any threatened, pending, or completed
           action or proceeding, whether civil, criminal, administrative, or investigative; and

       (3) Expenses. The term "expenses" includes, without limitation, all attorneys' fees, costs,
           and any other expenses incurred in the defense of any claims or proceedings against an
           agent by reason of his or her position or relationship as agent and all attorneys' fees,
           costs, and other expenses incurred in establishing a right to indemnification under this
           Article.


11.02. Successful Defense by Agent. To the extent that an agent of the Foundation has been
successful on the merits in the defense of any proceeding referred to in this Article, or in the defense
of any claim, issue, or matter therein, the agent shall be indemnified against expenses actually and
reasonably incurred by the agent in connection with the claim. If an agent either settles any such
claim or sustains a judgment rendered against him or her, then the provisions of Sections 11.03
through 11.05 shall determine whether the agent is entitled to indemnification.

11.03. Actions Brought by Persons other than the Foundation. Subject to the required findings to
be made pursuant to Section 11.05, the Foundation shall indemnify any person who has or is a
party, or is threatened to be made a party, to any proceeding other than an action brought by, or on
behalf of, the Foundation, or by an officer, Director or person granted related status by the Attorney
General, or by the Attorney General on the ground that the defendant Director was or is engaging
in self-dealing within the meaning of California Corporations Code Section 5233, or by the
Attorney General or a person granted related status by the Attorney General for any breach of duty
relating to assets held in charitable trust, by reason of the fact that such person is or was an agent
of the Foundation, for all expenses, judgments, fines, settlements, and other amount actually and
reasonably incurred in connection with the proceeding.

11.04. Action Brought by or on Behalf of the Foundation.

       (1) Claims Settled out of Court. If any agent settles or otherwise disposes of a threatened
           or pending action brought by or on behalf of the Foundation, with or without court
           approval, the agent shall receive no indemnification for either amounts paid pursuant to
           the terms of the settlement or other disposition or for any expenses incurred in defending
           against the proceeding, unless it is settled with the approval of the Attorney General.




                                                     9
       (2) Claims and Suits Awarded against Agent. The Foundation shall indemnify any person
          who was or is a party or is threatened to by a made party to any threatened, pending, or
          completed action brought by or on behalf of the Foundation by reason of the fact that
          the person is or was an agent of the Foundation, for all expenses actually and reasonably
          incurred in connection with the defense of that action, provided that both of the
          following are met:

          A. The determination of good faith conduct required by Section 11.05, below, must be
                made in the manner provided for in that Section; and

          B. Upon application, the court in which the action was brought must determine that, in
              view of all of the circumstances of the case, the agent should be entitled to
              indemnity for the expenses incurred. If the agent is found to be so entitled, the court
              shall determine the appropriate amount of expenses to be reimbursed.

11.05. Determination of Agent's Good Faith Conduct. The indemnification granted to an agent in
Sections 11.03 and 11.04, above, is conditioned on the following:

       (1) Required Standard of Conduct. The agent seeking reimbursement must be found, in the
          manner provided below, to have acted in good faith, in a manner he or she believed to
          be in the best interest of the Foundation, and with such care, including reasonable
          inquiry, as an ordinarily prudent person in a like position would use in similar
          circumstances. The termination of any proceeding by judgment, order, settlement,
          conviction, or on a plea of nolo contendre or its equivalent shall not, of itself, create a
          presumption that the person did not act in good faith or in a manner which he or she
          reasonably believed to be in the best interest of the Foundation or that he or she had
          reasonable cause to believe that his or her conduct was unlawful. In the case of a criminal
          proceeding, the person must have had no reasonable cause to believe that his or her
          conduct was unlawful.

       (2) Manner of Determination of Good Faith Conduct. The determination that the agent did
          act in a manner complying with Section 11.05(1), above, shall be made by:

          A. The Board of Directors by a majority vote of a quorum consisting of Directors who
             are not parties to the proceeding; or

          B. The court in which the proceeding is or was pending. Such determination may be
             made on application brought by the Foundation, the agent, or the attorney or other
             person rendering a defense to the agent, whether or not the Foundation opposes the
             application by the agent, attorney, or other person.

11.06. Limitations. No indemnification or advance shall be made under this Article, except as
provided in Sections 11.02 or 11.05(2)B under any circumstances when it appears:

       (1) Inconsistent Indemnification. The indemnification or advice would be inconsistent with
          a provision of the Articles of Incorporation, a resolution of the Board, or an agreement
          in effect at the time of the accrual of the alleged cause of action asserted in the
          proceeding in which the expenses were incurred or other amounts were paid, which
          prohibits or otherwise limits indemnification; or



                                                  10
       (2) Court Condition. That the indemnification would be inconsistent with any condition
           expressly imposed by a court in approving a settlement.

11.07. Advance of Expenses. Expenses incurred in defending any proceeding may be advanced by
the Foundation before the final disposition of the proceeding on receipt of an undertaking by or on
behalf of the agent to repay the amount of the advance unless it is determined ultimately that the
agent is entitled to be indemnified as authorized in this Article.

11.08. Contractual Rights of Non-directors and Non-officers. Nothing contained in this Article
shall affect any right to indemnification to which persons other than Directors and officers of the
Foundation, or any subsidiary hereof, may be entitled by contract or otherwise.

11.09. Insurance. The Board of Directors may adopt a resolution authorizing the purchase and
maintenance of insurance on behalf of any agent of the Foundation against any liability other than
for violating provisions against self-dealing asserted against or incurred by the agent in such
capacity or arising out of the agent's status as such, whether or not the Foundation would have the
power to indemnify the agent against that liability under the provisions of this Section.

settles any such claim or sustains a judgment rendered against him or her, then the provisions of
Sections 11.03 through 11.05 shall determine whether the agent is entitled to indemnification.

duty relating to assets held in charitable trust, by reason of the fact that such person is or was an

and reasonably incurred in connection with the proceeding.

       (1) Claims Settled out of Court. If any agent settles or otherwise disposes of a threatened
           or pending action brought by or on behalf of the Foundation, with or without court
           approval, the agent shall receive no indemnification for either amounts paid pursuant
                                                    9                                      8452.001-8924778.3
          to the terms of the settlement or other disposition or for any expenses incurred in

           STAFF REPORT

Meeting Date:        October 15, 2025

To:                  Fair Oaks Recreation Foundation Board of Directors

From:                Michael J. Aho, District Administrator

Subject:             Approval of Foundation Name Change


I.     Recommendation
       Staff recommends that the Board of Directors review and approve a new
       name for the Foundation, to be formally incorporated into the amended
       and restated Bylaws.

II.    Background
       The current Bylaws of the Foundation identify the corporation as the Fair
       Oaks Recreation Foundation Over the past several months, the District has
       been working with counsel to update and revise the Foundation’s Bylaws to
       better reflect its evolving mission and role in supporting arts, culture,
       recreation, and community services in Fair Oaks.
       As part of this process, it has been determined that a new name for the
       Foundation would provide clarity, visibility, and a stronger connection to the
       broader community identity. Staff has developed a short list of potential
       names to serve as a starting point for discussion, attached as Attachment A.

III.   Problem /Situation/ Request
       In order to submit final documents to the California Secretary of State, the
       Foundation must select a new legal name to incorporate into the Bylaws.
       Staff is requesting that the Board of Directors:
           • Review the attached list of potential names.
           • Select one of the suggested names, or propose an alternative name
               of their own choosing.
           • Approve a final name to be included in the revised Bylaws.
       The revised Bylaws are scheduled to be presented to the Foundation and
       District Board of Directors at the November meeting, along with the
       selected name.

IV.    Financial Analysis
       There is no direct financial cost associated with adopting a new Foundation
       name.


Respectfully Submitted,



Michael J. Aho
District Administrator
