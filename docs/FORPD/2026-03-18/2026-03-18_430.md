###### I. Recommendation

Staff recommend approving the use of funds totaling $2,288.77 from Gum Ranch Landscape & Lighting account 343B to cover needed repairs to the Gum Ranch Park splash pad surfacing by adoption of Resolution No. 031826-01:

• Amendment Of The Fiscal Year 2025-2026 Budget Of The General

Fund (341A) Fund Center (9349341) To Reflect An Increase In The Other Operating Expenses - Supplies (20289800) For Costs Related To Splash Pad Repair At Gum Ranch Park

###### II. Background

The Gum Ranch Park splash pad is one of the most oft-used amenities throughout the entire Gum Ranch Park site. From the opening of the splash pad in early May to its closure in early October, dozens of families utilize the amenity on a daily basis.

Surfacing on the splash pad at Gum Ranch Park, due to non-stop seasonal usage and exposure to the elements, has deteriorated away from one of the splash pad features. Further damage has been caused to this surfacing by way of inquisitive splash pad users pulling at the edges of the original damage. Overall, roughly a square foot of poured-in-place, rubberized surfacing has been peeled back from the underlying concrete.

Staff do not believe that this currently represents a major safety concern, but they do believe that repairs must be made to the surfacing prior to the damage becoming any worse.

###### III. Problem /Situation/ Request

Staff have approached the original installer of the surfacing to inquire about repairs; however, the company does not perform patch repairs and advised staff that a complete replacement would be necessary. The overall quote for the work totaled $39,290 for the installation of new materials. This did not include site demolition or any preparations ahead of the new installation; these two tasks would also not be performed by the original installer. This would require a secondary and potentially tertiary contractor whose services are estimated to bring the total closer to the $50,000 mark.

Staff reached out to other organizations who provided similar services but were again met with estimates in the $34,000 range, not including the potential for secondary contract work.

Unfortunately, none of these companies sold a product with which staff could utilize in-house labor to perform the work without need for outside contracting.

After receiving several quotations, staff began researching other avenues towards repair. CAPRI, the District’s risk managers, were consulted through which a connection was made to several park districts within California who operated splash pads without poured-in-place, rubberized surfacing on their splash pads.

Working with these other park districts, staff identified several other potential products which could work for the repairs until finally settling on a polymer-modified cementitious coating utilized by industry professionals on ponds, water play features, beach access points and similar structure. The product is similar to what might be seen surrounding pools throughout many park districts throughout the nation.

Utilizing this product (see Attachment A), staff will perform the work inhouse and enact repairs prior to the splash pad’s opening day in early May.

###### IV. Financial Analysis

A quote for materials to perform the repairs to the splash pad at Gum Ranch Park totals $2,288.77. This project, and its requisite materials, meet all criteria for utilizing Landscape & Lighting funds for Gum Ranch Park, which currently has a balance of funds of approximately $180,000.
