1. Update and discussion on the FOWD Field Services Center Project (verbal – no written staff report)  
   - Information only.  
   - General Manager Gray stated that the Board of Directors decided it was time to provide a public update on the Field Services Center Project. The project has been ongoing for a number of years. It is still with the County of Sacramento Planning Department, seeking final permitting approval. The approval process is twofold; there is a Planning Department for the building development and there is Site Improvement and Permit Section (SIPS) for all the public frontage improvements. He received a notification today, that on June 3rd, the civil improvement plans were accepted for review. The FOWD Board of Directors does not want to be involved in construction of any type, even demolition, at the same time that the Fair Oaks Recreation & Park District Project is ongoing-this was prior direction from the Board. President Sarkovich is working directly with the consultant. FOWD also received notice from SMUD that they are ready to start construction on some improvements related to the project, and that includes setting the transformer. In this case, the Board has a decision to make: direct staff to contact SMUD and inform them that FOWD is putting them on hold until FOWD gets an approved plan set, or direct General Manager Gray to contact SMUD and start work.  
   - President Sarkovich was under the impression that SMUD simply wanted to start the process. The process calls for an upfront fee of $60,000. He believes he misunderstood. It’s a tricky question given that FOWD does not have a building permit, so FOWD will be sticking its neck out by paying in advance and maybe by the time the FOWD’s building permit is approved the fees might be higher than $60,000. SMUD has set the fee of $60,000.  
   - General Manager Gray stated that this is just the initial fee set by SMUD, it could be more.  
   - President Sarkovich stated he is leaning towards paying the fee now. This ship sailed many years ago, FOWD just has to go through it. Worst case scenario, if the building permit is not granted or something happens, he’s pretty sure SMUD would refund the $60,000. How would they put the transformer on our site when the site is not even ready?

2. Update and discussion on the FOWD Northridge Well Project (verbal – no written staff report)
- General Manager Gray stated that FOWD Management decided to indicate when a verbal update will be given on an agenda item to avoid confusion from the public. The agenda item will indicate whether the update is verbal or if there is a written staff report included in the Board Packet. Making the public aware of a verbal update will encourage people to attend the Board Meetings. This will also provide an added level of transparency to the agenda.
- General Manager Gray noted that construction has started. The well was destroyed and then filled with concrete. This was a fun experience for FOWD’s young engineers.
- Director Petersen inquired if the well has been cemented up.
- General Manager Gray responded yes. FOWD is getting ready to move equipment in for the drilling. The Northridge Well and New York Well Projects are our last dance for some major players in the well drilling, equipping and discovery of groundwater in the Sacramento region. Jack Scroggs owned KASL Engineering, and is retired all but for, committing to finish FOWD’s well projects. One of the most respected hydrogeologists, Dale Bugenig, is incredible in finding water and designing wells. It’s a testament to the FOWD that they want to finish working with us on completing these projects. This is a committed project of about $3 million.

3. Update and discussion on the FOWD Transmission Main Replacement Project – Phase I (verbal – no written staff report)
- General Manager Gray stated that outreach has been completed on this project and construction has started. Outreach was done to the neighboring area, the same took place for Northridge. Potholing, among other things have been completed.
- Operations Manager Huckaby mentioned FOWD started cutting the streets.

- General Manager Gray responded that it starts with going to all the utilities inquiring about infrastructure in the street and asking for their as-builts for a civil project. As part of good construction, FOWD confirms the infrastructure is located in the place and depth that the as-built states before construction starts by verifying this in the field via “potholing”.
- Director Petersen stated that there was some potholing taking place in his neighborhood last week. He was wondering if that was FOWD.

4. Update and discussion on the Water Forum Agreement 2050 (verbal – no written staff report)
- General Manager Gray noted the Board received a copy of the draft agreement in last month’s Board Packet. Since then, he has attended a meeting regarding the Purveyors Specific Agreements (PSA). FOWD has a PSA jointly with the San Juan Member Agencies. There was some discussion and there is a meeting scheduled this Thursday just with the San Juan family GMs to agree upon content of the common agreement. In addition, FOWD will pay its own portion of the Water Forum cost. For years, FOWD has done it through the San Juan Family of Agencies, so San Juan Water District (SJWD) would pay for all agencies even though FOWD is an individual signatory to the agreement. The City of Sacramento and Sacramento County funded the majority of the Water Forum Agreement, and now Sacramento County has backed down and other agencies will have to pay more. Executive leadership and the executive director of the Water Forum requested Fair Oaks pay individually. Citrus Heights Water District (CHWD) is also paying individually. Orangevale is staying with San Juan paying for them.
- General Manager Gray noted that the Board has already authorized him to make the payment directly and that he will do so when it comes time. Will execute the agreement and bring it back. The payment will be around $55,000 to $60,000. He reminded the Board that the Water Forum Agreement has been leveraged for all the grant funding the region has received. It’s been very advantageous for FOWD.

4. Discussion and possible action on FOWD Policy No. 6030 - “Cross-Connection & Backflow Prevention”  
   - General Manager Gray presented his recommendation for the Board to approve the updated Policy No. 6030.  
   - General Manager Gray stated that in accordance with California code, FOWD has to have this program. It sets the requirements for public water systems to comply with cross-connection control, keeping our public water system from being contaminated. FOWD is required to develop a plan to comply with the regulation and it goes hand in hand with the FOWD policy update.

- General Manager Gray noted that other public water districts paid to have this done by a consultant. This was all done in-house by FOWD staff.
- President Sarkovich inquired about the cost of backflow devices.
- General Manager Gray responded that it depends on the size. A residential backflow device is around $700, not including installation.
- President Sarkovich inquired if the customer needs to hire their own contractor to have the backflow device installed. He also inquired how many customers would need such device.
- General Manager Gray responded yes and the customer must hire their own contractor.
- Water Supply Superintendent Siebensohn stated that there are currently 1,247 backflow devices in the FOWD water system. Anytime there is new residential development or a remodel it goes to the County planning department review and the fire department review – there has to be a fire sprinkler system put in and that requires a backflow.
- President Sarkovich noted that it’s less than 10%.
- General Manager Gray stated that in accordance with this new law, FOWD has to survey every property for a potential cross-connection issue.
- Director Babcock shared his experience with backflow installation when he started the landscaping for his yard. This was about ten years ago, and it cost him around $250-$300.
- General Manager Gray responded that it was good design because Director Babcock connected irrigation into his portable water and it has the same contamination potential.
- General Manager Gray noted that all dedicated irrigation services have a backflow requirement.

President Sarkovich moved to adapt the updated Policy No. 6030 – “Cross-Connection & Backflow Prevention,” as presented.

Director Dolby seconded the motion.

Motion carried with the following votes: Babcock – aye, Dolby – aye Marx – aye, Petersen – aye, and Sarkovich – aye.
