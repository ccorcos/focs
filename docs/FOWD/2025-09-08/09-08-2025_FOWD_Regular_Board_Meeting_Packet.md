The Board of Directors of the Fair Oaks Water District holds its Regular Board Meetings on the third Monday of each month at 6:30 p.m. The meetings are held at the District Offices located at 10326 Fair Oaks Blvd., Fair Oaks, CA 95628. The Board may discuss any item on the agenda and may act on any of those items.

The Board of Directors welcomes public participation in its meetings. Public comments relating to matters within jurisdiction of the District, and not included on the posted agenda, may be addressed under “public comment,” both at the beginning and at the end of the meeting, subject to reasonable time limitations for each speaker. Please note that State law prevents the Board from discussing or acting on items not listed on the agenda. Public comments relating to matters listed on the agenda may be provided at the time when that agenda item is heard.

Agenda items are numbered for identification purposes only and will not necessarily be considered in the indicated order. Items appearing on the Consent Calendar are considered routine and may be acted upon by the Board by one motion, without discussion; however, any item may be considered separately at the request of any Board member or any member of the public.

In compliance with the American with Disabilities Act, if you have a disability and need a disability-related modification or accommodation to participate in this meeting, please contact the Human Resource Administrator at (916) 967-5723. Requests must be made as early as possible, and at least one full business day before the start of the meeting.

## 2. Discussion on FOWD Emergency Water Supply
- **Information item only.**
- General Manager Gray noted that one of the goals set for the FOWD when he was selected as General Manager in 2004 was to develop a reliable groundwater supply to meet emergency water demand for the entire FOWD system upon ultimate build out. By the end of 2026, the FOWD will have met this goal. The 10,700 gallons per minute listed in the report was developed with brand new wells between 2007 and 2026. FOWD worked with SGA to receive grant funding for every single one of those wells. It’s a wonderful team accomplishment. The Board, staff and RWA were a big part of FOWD being able to do this and keep rates low.
- General Manager Gray stated that if FOWD were to take the largest producing well out of service, FOWD would have 8,400 gallons per minute and the largest single day demand is 7,500 gallons per minute. It’s a great story to tell.
- General Manager Gray stated that no municipality in the State of California would ever give up reliable access to “water right water.” It would be financially irresponsible of this Board to try to produce groundwater to meet peak demand. If you want to do that, you do it by developing water storage infrastructure.
- By the end of 2026 FOWD will own four more properties for water supply infrastructure - FOWD has planned well.
- Director Sarkovich replied I would say so and thanked the management team.
- Director Petersen noted that recently, Citrus Heights Water District (CHWD) had an unveiling of two of their wells and a politician attended. Maybe FOWD should consider doing something like this when the wells come online. It could create some engagement in the community. It could be an educational opportunity.

**REGULAR Board Meeting on September 8, 2025**

**To:** Board of Directors  
**From:** Tom R. Gray  
**Date:** September 3, 2025  
**Subject:** Update and discussion on the impact of the FOWD Transmission Main Replacement Project – Phase I on the cost of power to supply water to the FOWD Upper Pressure Zone

**Recommendation:**  
Information item only.

**Discussion:**  
In accordance with Fair Oaks Water District (FOWD) Board direction, FOWD is implementing Phase I of the FOWD Transmission Main Replacement Project (T-Main project). 

This project being completed during a high-water demand period has caused a financial impact on the FOWD due to increased groundwater pumping. FOWD staff anticipate that the cost of power for water supply will require additional funding. Once the actual impact is determined FOWD staff will report back to the Board.

The time frame for implementing Phase I was impacted by the scheduled Sacramento County Overlay Project on Madison Avenue. The section of the new FOWD T-Main to be located on Madison Avenue needed to be completed before the Sacramento County Overlay Project could begin. The overlay project was originally scheduled for June 2025 but the County agreed to delay their project so that the new paving is not disturbed.

**Note:** As reported in the April 2025 FOWD Board meeting, by completing Phase I prior to the Sacramento County Overlay Project, FOWD will see significant savings in paving costs.

To allow the T-Main replacement project to proceed, the section that is being replaced was taken off-line, drained, and abandoned, while the newly installed T-Main is tested and connected to the FOWD distribution system. To accommodate the lack of flow that would normally be fed into FOWD’s distribution system from the San Juan Water District wholesale connection, FOWD needed to operate FOWD wells and tank’s booster pumps to meet summer water demands.

To accommodate the T-Main being offline, groundwater pumping has increased from approximately 8% of our total water supply to 24% for the month of August. The associated costs with increased groundwater pumping include increased power demand and increased chlorine needed for disinfection. It is estimated that the section of T-Main that is being replaced will be off four (4) weeks. FOWD staff plan on having this project completed by mid-September 2025.
