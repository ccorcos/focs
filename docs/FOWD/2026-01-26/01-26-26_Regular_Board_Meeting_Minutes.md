3. Discussion and possible action on FOWD Resolution No. 26-01: “Water Forum
   2050 Agreement”
   • General Manager Gray provided a staff recommendation to approve the FOWD
      Resolution No. 26-01 and read the resolution for the Board.
   • Director Babcock expressed appreciation for the work done on the agreement. He
      stated that he is concerned that the agreement states it is co-equal with water
      districts and other entities because not all are equal. He stated that water districts
      should be more protected. If there is a drought period and to ensure water for our
      customers, there are no protections for that because one of the entities in the
      agreement could sue FOWD. The agreement is not legally binding, and it concerns
      him that it does not mention that people come first.
   • General Manager Gray responded that water districts being sued by the
      environmental caucus and businesses in 2002 is what brought the Water Forum
      Agreement forward. He stated that it brought entities together, while not legally
      binding, to prevent litigation.
   • General Manager Gray stated that the agreement is implemented through mutual
      commitments and if there are multiple years of drought, FOWD will provide water
      to customers first.
   • Director Petersen provided comments on the history of the Water Forum
      Agreement.
   • Director Babcock stated that he is nervous about the agreement not stating that other
      groups will not necessarily acquiesce to the others.
   • General Manager Gray responded that it is an agreement with a level of trust
      between the caucuses. He stated that if others do not follow the intention of the
      agreement, the water caucus will push back.
   • Visitor Joseph stated that he absolutely agrees with General Manager Gray. It is a
      matter of trust built up over decades that goes a long way with state and federal
      funding. They are pleased to hear that there are no disputes or litigation/verge of
      litigation. He stated that RWA continually points to the Water Forum Agreement
      that has enabled them to be successful and advance projects.

   Director Sarkovich moved to approve the FOWD Resolution No. 26-01 as presented.
   President Marx seconded the motion.

   Motion carried with the following votes: Babcock – aye, Dolby – aye, Marx – aye,
   Petersen – aye, and Sarkovich – aye.
