V. DISCUSSION AND ACTION ITEMS: OLD BUSINESS
1. Discussion and possible action on FOWD Policy No. 5110 – “Procurement and Payment Policy”
   - President Sarkovich inquired if the proposed FOWD Policy No. 5110 – “Procurement and Payment Policy” reflected FOWD’s legal counsel opinion.
   - General Manager Gray confirmed that Policy 5110 reflects FOWD’s legal counsel opinion.
   - Director Petersen stated he would like to remove the word “may” under 5.13.1.
   - General Manager Gray suggested changing “may” to “will.”
   - General Manager Gray suggested, and the Board agreed, to remove “in lieu of the bid process.”
   - President Sarkovich stated staff needs to add clarifying language for Board members to be ultimate authority for contracts with a firm.
   - General Manager Gray suggested revising the policy to include that the Board authorize staff to execute the agreement in substantial conformance with the firm selected by the Board. Should negotiations fail, staff will come back to the Board with recommendations.
   - Vice President Marx recommended adding “Project Understanding” to sections 5.13.1.5 and 5.13.1.2.
   - President Sarkovich directed staff to revise FOWD Policy No. 5110 – “Procurement and Payment Policy” to reflect the Board’s comments and place it on the April Board meeting.

2. Update and discussion on FOWD owned property in Phoenix Park
   - President Sarkovich and General Manager Gray attended the meeting with the Fair Oaks Recreation and Park District (FORPD).
   - General Manager Gray provided an overview and update. The FORPD owes FOWD another well site. It will not necessarily be located in Phoenix Park. FOWD will be looking at different properties around Phoenix Park and will inform FORPD which location they would like to provide. This is something positive for FOWD. FOWD will be working on a proposed site and an agreement.

3. Discussion and possible action on awarding the Northridge Well Phase I Project construction contract
   - General Manager Gray recommended the Board enter into an agreement with Nor-Cal Pump & Well Drilling, for a total cost of $1,176,776 and approve a ten percent contingency of $117,678 for the project budget.
   - General Manager Gray clarified this is a firm price for the generic well design – drilling and well construction. This allows all firms to bid on the same thing. The actual construction will be based on the test hole and the hydrology that is found.

- SJWD General Manager Helliker inquired if this project is being funded by the Artesian Grant and mentioned that all the expenses need to be incurred by December 2025.
- General Manager Gray stated that FOWD has it worked out. In accordance with FOWD policy, one of the components in this RFQ was the ability to meet schedule.
- Discussion between Director Babcock and General Manager Gray on price differences if the well does not need to be drilled as deep as currently planned.
- General Manager Gray stated that FOWD has a standard bidding process. There is a line-item bid sheet for every component with its unit price.
- A brief discussion took place regarding the New York Water Main Replacement Project. Operations Manager Huckaby will post an update on the FOWD website and will modify the sign at the site updating the date of completion.

President Sarkovich moved to approve awarding the Northridge Well Phase I Project to Nor-Cal Pump & Well Drilling as presented.

Vice President Marx seconded the motion.

Motion carried with the following votes: Babcock – aye, Dolby – aye, Marx – aye, Petersen – aye, and Sarkovich – aye.

4. Discussion on drilling a “test hole” for the possible development of a water production facility at Phoenix Park
- General Manager Gray provided a brief summary. The cost for a “test hole” is $180,000.
- The Board discussed options to obtain quotes for smaller rake drilling equipment, to bring costs down. The $180,000 estimate is for a large reverse rotary rake that is extremely expensive.
- General Manager Gray stated he likes the idea of test-drilling the site.
- Discussion about the benefits of drilling other wells took place.
- General Manager Gray stated that FOWD was given the opportunity to obtain two additional well sites at no cost. FOWD would like to ensure the sites have good water supply and the only way to do this is to test drill. As per the agreement with the FORPD, FOWD gets to test drill twice to ensure there is water at the site. If neither site is productive or has bad water quality, the sites can be eliminated and FOWD has the option to go to a known site that has good production and good water quality.
- The Board agreed that test drilling will be discussed further at a future meeting.

3. Sacramento Water Forum  
- None.  
- General Manager Gray stated that he was contacted by the Sacramento Water Forum members asking FOWD to consider self-funding its portion of the Water Forum. SJWD took it upon themselves to totally fund it as a wholesale cost, though Citrus Heights Water District (CHWD) and FOWD are both signatories in the Water Forum. As a Water Forum member, FOWD will pay a certain amount; a renegotiated amount. The county used to pay around $800,000 annually and they are dialing it down. This amount needs to be split up by Forum members and it will be paid by the San Juan wholesale agencies one way or another. Instead of SJWD paying for it through wholesale, CHWD and FOWD agreed to pay its own share since we are signatories to the agreement. By doing so, it gives CHWD and FOWD more voice in the Sacramento Water Forum.  
- General Manager Gray provided a summary of how and why the Water Forum was created. It’s money well spent by FOWD, as the net result is more than what’s put in.
