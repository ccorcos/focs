2. Discussion and possible action on completing Phase I of the New York Main Replacement Project  
   - General Manager Gray provided an overview and update.  
   - General Manager Gray stated that staff obtained an estimate from Central Valley Engineering & Asphalt, Inc. (CVE) to complete the paving portion of the project at a lower cost than the initial vendor, saving the District a total of $51,357.  
   - President Sarkovich inquired if FOWD currently has a contract with CVE.  
   - General Manager Gray responded that FOWD has a contract with CVE. He explained that there are two types of contracts. One is a specific contract for a given project and an annual contract to perform routine work throughout the year at a fixed rate with the latter work being authorized via task orders. Both types of contracts go through a bidding process.  

President Sarkovich moved to approve General Manager Gray to execute the agreement with Central Valley Engineering & Asphalt, Inc., at a cost of $209,975.  
Director Babcock seconded the motion.  
Motion carried with the following votes: Babcock – aye, Dolby – aye, Marx – aye, Petersen – aye, and Sarkovich – aye.

4. Discussion possible action on awarding a contract for the construction of Phase I and Phase II of the FOWD Transmission Main Replacement Project
   - General Manager Gray stated there have been changes since the staff report was put together; therefore, the recommendation will be amended.
   - General Manager Gray recommended that the Board enter into an agreement with Flowline Contractors, Inc., for a total cost of $2,882,000 and approve a ten percent contingency of $3,170,000 for the project budget; pending legal review and approval.
   - General Manager Gray provided an overview of the bidding process and reviewed the bid summary chart.
   - Director Petersen inquired about the work schedule for the completion of this project.
   - Engineer Chetcuti stated it will be 120 days for Phase I and 150 days for Phase II. It has to move at a speedy pace due to the County Overlay Project.
   - General Manager Gray noted Engineer Chetcuti’s great work on the internal memo provided as Exhibit A.

   President Sarkovich moved to approve General Manager Gray to enter into agreement with Flowline Contractors, Inc., as amended, pending legal review and approval, at a cost of $2,882,000 and approve a ten percent contingency of $3,170,000 for the project budget.
   Director Petersen seconded the motion.
   Motion carried with the following votes: Babcock – aye, Dolby – aye, Marx – aye, Petersen – aye, and Sarkovich – aye.

3. Discussion on “Why FOWD is not pumping more groundwater in 2025”
   - General Manager Gray provided staff’s recommendation to direct him to minimize groundwater use until presenting a plan to the FOWD Board that provides financial and operational advantages of increasing groundwater production.
   - General Manager Gray noted that over the past few years SJWD has made major changes to the wholesale rate structure used to charge FOWD for surface water.
   - President Sarkovich stated that due to SJWD’s wholesale rate structure changes, it makes sense to switch from groundwater to surface water. He believes in the concept but disputes the Acre Feet (AF) estimate reflected on the chart.
   - Director Dolby stated that he was under the impression that FOWD was already pumping at a minimal volume.
   - General Manager Gray responded that this is a true statement. FOWD would simply continue pumping at a minimal volume. FOWD is currently at 90% surface water and 10% groundwater.
