2. Discussion on FOWD Emergency Water Supply
   - Information item only.
   - General Manager Gray noted that one of the goals set for the FOWD when he was selected as General Manager in 2004 was to develop a reliable groundwater supply to meet emergency water demand for the entire FOWD system upon ultimate build out. By the end of 2026, the FOWD will have met this goal. The 10,700 gallons per minute listed in the report was developed with brand new wells between 2007 and 2026. FOWD worked with SGA to receive grant funding for every single one of those wells. It’s a wonderful team accomplishment. The Board, staff and RWA were a big part of FOWD being able to do this and keep rates low.
   - General Manager Gray stated that if FOWD were to take the largest producing well out of service, FOWD would have 8,400 gallons per minute and the largest single day demand is 7,500 gallons per minute. It’s a great story to tell.
   - General Manager Gray stated that no municipality in the State of California would ever give up reliable access to “water right water.” It would be financially irresponsible of this Board to try to produce groundwater to meet peak demand. If you want to do that, you do it by developing water storage infrastructure.
   - By the end of 2026 FOWD will own four more properties for water supply infrastructure - FOWD has planned well.
   - Director Sarkovich replied I would say so and thanked the management team.
   - Director Petersen noted that recently, Citrus Heights Water District (CHWD) had an unveiling of two of their wells and a politician attended. Maybe FOWD should consider doing something like this when the wells come online. It will create some engagement in the community. It could be an educational opportunity.
