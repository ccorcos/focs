Public Comment

 

Anyone may appear at the Board meeting to provide public comment regarding any item on the agenda or regarding any matter that is within the Board’s subject matter jurisdiction. However, the Board may not take action on any item not on the agenda except as authorized by Government Code section 54954.2.  If possible, notify the Board President or Board Secretary in writing prior to the meeting if you wish to testify.

 

Individuals wishing to speak in person before the Board are requested to complete a public comment form. Speakers will be called in the order in which the cards are received. 

 

Public comment via Zoom teleconference may be made by using the raised hand feature in Zoom during the agenda item to be addressed. The speaker will be identified by their Display Name in Zoom when called upon to speak. Those joining the meeting via Zoom are requested to display their full name so there is no confusion when called upon to speak. Public comments will be received until the public comment period has elapsed.

 

Comments are limited to a maximum of two (2) minutes per speaker per agenda item, and individuals are limited to a single comment per agenda item.

 

Please contact boardcomments@scoe.net before the meeting if you have questions about the public comment process. 

 

Accommodating Individuals with Disabilities

 

The Sacramento County Office of Education encourages those with disabilities to participate fully in the public meeting process. If you need a disability-related modification or accommodation, including auxiliary aids or services, to participate in the public meeting, contact the Superintendent’s Office at (916) 228-2410 at least 48 hours before the scheduled Board meeting so that we may make every reasonable effort to accommodate you. [Government Code § 54953.2; Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990, § 202 (42 U.S.C. § 12132).]

BACKGROUND: 

 

CLASSIFIED 

 

Jessie Heidrich, Staff Secretary with the Special Education Department, was nominated by Area Principal, Randy Olson. Jessie just celebrated her one-year anniversary with SCOE in March 2023. Since joining SCOE, Jessie has volunteered to do as much as she can to support SCOE's Special Education Department and Seeds of Partnership. Jessie provides incredible support to her team by consistently checking in to see about the needs of individuals and the program as a whole. Jessie supports our Foundations school nursing team as well as our pre-k programs. She ensures both teams receive the supplies, trainings, and planning time they need to be effective. Jessie also provides support to other staff secretaries if there is a need or if someone needs coverage for an extended absence. Ryan Robins with Seeds of Partnership highlighted Jessie's willingness to help whenever needed. Jessie Heidrich has been a SCOE employee since March 2022. 

 

CERTIFICATED

 

Eziuche Okemiri, Resource Specialist with the Special Education Department, was nominated by Community Schools Principal Lisa Alcalá. Dr. Eziuche Okemiri is the Resource Specialist for Community Schools. Dr. O, as he is known to students and staff, works with students who receive special education services. He helps them set both long-term and short-term goals, as well as provides scaffolding to help students be successful in the classroom. Dr. O also works with our families, ensuring that parents and guardians are aware of all the tools that are available to them as the family of a special needs child. He communicates clearly and often, helping families navigate a system that is sometimes confusing. As a colleague, Dr. O is always willing to help wherever he is needed. With a bright smile and friendly demeanor, Dr. O helps to create an inclusive community within our schools. Eziuche Okemiri has been a SCOE employee since March 2008.

BACKGROUND:

 

Annually, the funds generated through the United Way Campaign support hundreds of community and health organizations that provide valuable assistance in Sacramento County and the region. Many of our county's students, as well as some of our Sacramento County Office of Education programs, are among those assisted by agencies funded by United Way. Each year, the Sacramento County Board of Education considers and has, numerous times in the past, adopted a Board Resolution in support of the Annual United Way Campaign. 

 

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:

 

The Superintendent recommends the Board adopt Resolution No. 23-03 in support of and designating May 1-31, 2023 as United Way Campaign Month.

BACKGROUND:

 

Per Sacramento County Board of Education Bylaw 9260 (attached), Board members may be paid for any meeting when absent if the Board, by resolution duly adopted and included in its minutes, finds that at the time of the meeting, he or she was performing services outside the meeting on behalf of the Board, he or she was ill, on jury duty, or the absence was due to a hardship deemed acceptable by the Board.
