Public Comment

 

Anyone may appear at the Board meeting to provide public comment regarding any item on the agenda or regarding any matter that is within the Board’s subject matter jurisdiction. However, the Board may not take action on any item not on the agenda except as authorized by Government Code section 54954.2. If possible, notify the Board President or Board Secretary in writing prior to the meeting if you wish to testify.

 

Individuals wishing to speak in person before the Board are requested to complete a public comment form. Speakers will be called in the order in which the cards are received. 

 

Public comment via Zoom teleconference may be made by using the raised hand feature in Zoom during the agenda item to be addressed. The speaker will be identified by their Display Name in Zoom when called upon to speak. Those joining the meeting via Zoom are requested to display their full name so there is no confusion when called upon to speak. Public comments will be received until the public comment period has elapsed.

 

Comments are limited to a maximum of two (2) minutes per speaker per agenda item, and individuals are limited to a single comment per agenda item.

 

Please contact boardcomments@scoe.net before the meeting if you have questions about the public comment process. 

 

Accommodating Individuals with Disabilities

 

The Sacramento County Office of Education encourages those with disabilities to participate fully in the public meeting process. If you need a disability-related modification or accommodation, including auxiliary aids or services, to participate in the public meeting, contact the Superintendent’s Office at (916) 228-2410 at least 48 hours before the scheduled Board meeting so that we may make every reasonable effort to accommodate you. [Government Code § 54953.2; Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990, § 202 (42 U.S.C. § 12132).]

Program Description:

The purpose of the SNPSPD grant is to provide consistent, high-quality professional development for California non-profit, private school teachers, and administrators, as identified through collaboration with the CDE and any necessary advisory groups. Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) staff will ensure equitable access statewide for professional development, integration of technology where appropriate, and implementation of a professional development plan.

  • Education Speakers Network (ESN) in the amount of $1,519,700 to provide workshops on project-based learning, classroom management, student motivation, differentiated instruction, standards-based grading, two virtual multi-topic conferences and two in-person multi-topic conferences, and other trainings provided by ESN staff with expertise in relevant content areas. 
  • Professional Development provided by subcontractors with expertise in relevant content areas in the amount of $60,000.

 

Evaluation Component:

SCOE staff will compile and report on survey feedback obtained from participants in the professional development workshops. Quarterly and end-of-year reports, data files, reports from professional development sessions, website development and maintenance, and ongoing administration of grant deliverables will be provided to the CDE. This project will be measured and evaluated by successful completion of deliverables as described in the contract.

Subject
B. Health Net CalAIM Grant - Shanine Coats
Meeting
Feb 18, 2025 - Sacramento County Board of Education Meeting Agenda - 6:30 p.m.
Category
10. New Business - Authorization to Submit Grant Applications/Service Contracts and Accept Funding if Awarded; and Approval of Contracts, Positions, and Other Expenditures Associated with the Grants as Outlined in the Proposed Budget – David W. Gordon
Type
Action
Recommended Action
Motion to approve.

Department/Cabinet Member: Administration/Shanine Coats

 

Funding Source: Health Net

 

Amount Requested: $100,000

 

Fiscal Year(s): 2024-2025, 2025-2026

 

Program Description: 

SCOE is applying for a CalAIM grant through Health Net for funding to support the Career Pipeline and the objectives set by the Mental Behavioral Health Workforce Advisory Committee, a subcommittee of the Mental Behavioral Health (MBH) Collaborative. Our goals include enhancing Peer-to-Peer mentorship programs, amplifying youth voices, and developing an online tool that connects students and job seekers to educational programs, certifications, and employment opportunities in the MBH field. We will achieve these goals through two projects:

 

  1. A Peer-to-Peer Mentorship Convening: Peer to Peer mentorship is a strategy that is recommended by the Sacramento County Behavioral Youth Advisory Board, as a means to expand the number of trusted peer-to-peer messengers around youth behavioral health and wellbeing. Schools are a key place where peer to peer mentorship could take place. SCOE, along with our partners and youth, will hold a Peer-to-Peer Mentorship Convening to provide a space for LEAs, youth, and community-based organizations to learn about this strategy.
  2. An Online Behavioral Health Workforce Map: One barrier to recruiting and retaining mental behavioral health professionals is the lack of a clear path to the profession. Additionally, aspiring professionals from high school through licensure, are not aware of what academic and employment opportunities are available. The web-based landscape map will clarify what opportunities are available in Sacramento County for aspiring MBH professionals from high school (example existing CTE programs, peer to peer programs), CHW, Wellness Coach and other school-based positions, to graduate degrees and licensure (example employment positions that allow for the supervision of 3,000 associate licensure hours).

 

New Positions: None

 

Subcontracts: None

 

Evaluation Component:

SCOE will submit semiannual progress reports to Health Net of all grant activities and measurable outcomes related to each program.

BACKGROUND:

 

The Local Control and Accountability Plan (LCAP) mid-year update is an annual requirement enacted by Senate Bill 114 in July 2023. Districts, charter schools, and county offices of education are required to present on the annual update to the LCAP on or before February 28 each year at a regularly-scheduled meeting of the governing board.

 

The mid-year report must include the following elements:

  • All available mid-year outcome data related to metrics identified in the current year's LCAP
  • All available mid-year expenditure and implementation data on all actions identified in the current year's LCAP

 

The report provides an overview on progress toward the LCAP goals based on available mid-year data. 

 

The SCOE Schools Data Report will be presented at this time. 

 

This is an informational item and no Board action is required.
