Public Comments\
Public comments may be made via the Zoom Q&A Tool. Once the meeting has
started, click the Q&A button at the bottom of the Zoom window. When the
Q&A window appears, enter your name and the agenda item you wish to
address in the input box, then choose \"Send.\" Be prepared to speak
when prompted by name during the public comments portion of the meeting.
Public Comments will be received until the public comment period has
elapsed. Comments are limited to a maximum of two (2) minutes per
speaker per agenda item, and individuals are limited to a single comment
per agenda item. You may contact boardcomments@scoe.net prior to the
meeting if you have questions about this process.\
\
Anyone may submit public comments to the Board on any item that is
within its subject matter jurisdiction. However, the Board may not take
action on any item not on the agenda except as authorized by Government
Code section 54954.2. Anyone may provide public comments in support of
or in opposition to any item being presented for consideration.\
\
Accommodating Individuals with Disabilities\
The Sacramento County Office of Education encourages those with
disabilities to participate fully in the public meeting process. If you
need a disability-related modification or accommodation, including
auxiliary aids or services, to participate in the public meeting,
contact the Superintendent's Office at (916) 228-2410 at least 48 hours
before the scheduled Board meeting so that we may make every reasonable
effort to accommodate you. \[Government Code § 54953.2; Americans with
Disabilities Act of 1990, § 202 (42 U.S.C. § 12132).\]\

and community;\
• provide educational leadership to the diverse groups we serve;\

• give educators and support staff the training and tools they need for
success.

[[The Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) has submitted a
proposal to the California Department of Heath Care Services (DHCS) as a
partner to support Social Emotional Learning (SEL) in a statewide
Community of Practice (CoP). Cal-HOPE Student Support will enable
California\'s schools to be leaders in supporting prevention and early
interventions for students and families as we collectively respond to
the mental health and wellness needs of students, families, and
educators across California. Through a unique partnership with the
California Department of Health Care Services, California Public
Education System, University of California, Berkeley, and the Orange
County Department of Education, California front-line educators will
support families and children by responding to the trauma and isolation
caused by the COVID-19
Pandemic. ]{style="font-size:14px;"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}

 

[[Our plan leverages each of the 58 county offices of education,
supporting local districts and schools statewide, to prepare educators
to be first-line responders to protect the [psychological wellbeing of
children and youth during the 2021-2022, 2022-2023, and 2023-2024
academic years. Professional development and technical assistance
delivered regionally for educators will: (1) give teachers the mindset
and skills to recover from their own traumas, restoring their capacity
to serve the diverse and substantial needs of children; and (2) give
teachers concrete strategies to work with children experiencing an
ongoing
crisis. ]{style="color:black"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[[The Northern ]{style="color:black"}California hub, led by SCOE, and
the Southern Hub led by the Orange County Department of Education will
ensure funding is quickly distributed to other hubs and CoPs for needed
training and technical assistance. SCOE has identified The Greater Good
Science Center (GGSC) as a lead partner in developing training and
creating content for all partners. The GGSC at UC Berkeley will
re-purpose and refine existing resources, create supplemental content,
and deliver a high-quality, virtual professional development experience
to California educators.
]{style="font-size:14px;"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}

ARCA would like to update its charter so that it may provide TK as an
option for
families.  ]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[Under Education Code section 47605(a)(4), after receiving approval of
its petition, a charter school that proposes to expand operations to
additional grade levels must request a material revision to its charter,
and the chartering authority must consider whether to approve the grade
levels at an open, public meeting. ARCA has submitted the attached
request for a material revision to its charter, which further explains
its plans for offering TK and specifies the areas of the charter that
would be revised if the Board approves its
request.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[While charter schools are not currently required to provide TK, Senate
Bill (SB) 130 calls for the expansion of TK, and will gradually phase in
TK so that it is available to all 4-year-old children by
2025-2026. While families are not required to enroll their children in
TK, making TK available to more students supports the public policy of
California.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}
