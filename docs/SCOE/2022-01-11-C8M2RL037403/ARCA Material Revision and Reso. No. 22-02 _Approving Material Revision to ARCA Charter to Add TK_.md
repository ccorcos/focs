OVERVIEW

Rocklin Academy Family of Schools currently offers transitional kindergarten at our
Rocklin Academy Gateway campus in Placer County. To provide access to full-day
learning the year before kindergarten for families in the Citrus Heights and surrounding
areas, Rocklin Academy Family of Schools seeks to provide transitional kindergarten as
an option for families at American River Collegiate Academy. As a “second year” of
kindergarten, our current transitional kindergarten program was designed in collaboration
with preschool, transitional kindergarten, and kindergarten educators utilizing Core
Knowledge Preschool Standards, Core Knowledge Kindergarten Standards, and
California Preschool Learning Foundations Standards. As with many public schools in the
State, Rocklin Academy Family of Schools is currently preparing for implementation of
Senate Bill (SB) 130 - Transitional Kindergarten Expansion including redesigning
transitional kindergarten standards and curriculum to meet the needs of younger four year
old students. In addition to redesigning the transitional kindergarten program, budgets will
be developed with staffing ratios per SB 130. Knowing that Universal Transitional
Kindergarten is going to cause a great demand on hiring teachers in an environment
where a teacher shortage exists, we continue to focus on recruitment and pipeline
strategies to meet the transitional kindergarten credentialing requirements. We look forward
to partnering with the Sacramento County Office of Education to provide a high-quality, distinct
transitional kindergarten program at American River Collegiate Academy.

SACRAMENTO COUNTY BOARD OF EDUCATION

Resolution Approving Material Revision to the American River Collegiate
Academy Charter to Add Transitional Kindergarten
Resolution No. 22-02

WHEREAS, on February 4, 2020, the Sacramento County Board of Education (Board)
approved the American River Collegiate Academy (ARCA) Charter to serve students in
grades K-12 within the San Juan Unified School District (SJUSD).

WHEREAS, the ARCA charter petition approved by the Board did not contemplate
offering Transitional Kindergarten (TK) at ARCA. In order to provide access to full-day
learning the year before kindergarten for families in the Citrus Heights and surrounding
areas, ARCA is now seeking to update its charter so that it may provide TK as an option
for families.

WHEREAS, under Education Code section 47605(a)(4), after receiving approval of its
petition, a charter school that proposes to expand operations to additional grade levels
must request a material revision to its charter, and the chartering authority
must consider whether to approve the grade levels at an open, public meeting.

WHEREAS, ARCA has submitted the attached request for a material revision to its
charter, which further details its plans for offering TK and specifies the areas and pages
of the charter to be revised so that ARCA may offer TK as part of its approved charter.

WHEREAS, while charter schools are not currently required to provide TK, Senate Bill
(SB) 130 calls for the expansion of TK, and will gradually phase in TK so that it is
available to all 4-year-old children by 2025-2026.

WHEREAS, while families are not required to enroll their children in TK, making TK
available to more students supports the public policy of California.

WHEREAS, a public hearing regarding ARCA’s request for a material revision was held
on January 11, 2022.

IT IS HEREBY RESOLVED that:

    1. The recitals set forth above are true and correct.

    2. The Sacramento County Board of Education hereby approves a material revision
       to the American River Collegiate Academy (ARCA) charter to provide
       Transitional Kindergarten to families, as set forth in ARCA’s attached request for
       a material revision.

    3. The Sacramento County Board of Education hereby authorizes the Sacramento
       County Superintendent of Schools or designee to take any other actions needed
       to effectuate the purposes of this Resolution.
