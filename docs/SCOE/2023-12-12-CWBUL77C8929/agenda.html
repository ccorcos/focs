Public Comment

 

Anyone may appear at the Board meeting to provide public comment regarding any item on the agenda or regarding any matter that is within the Board’s subject matter jurisdiction. However, the Board may not take action on any item not on the agenda except as authorized by Government Code section 54954.2.  If possible, notify the Board President or Board Secretary in writing prior to the meeting if you wish to testify.

 

Individuals wishing to speak in person before the Board are requested to complete a public comment form. Speakers will be called in the order in which the cards are received. 

 

Public comment via Zoom teleconference may be made by using the raised hand feature in Zoom during the agenda item to be addressed. The speaker will be identified by their Display Name in Zoom when called upon to speak. Those joining the meeting via Zoom are requested to display their full name so there is no confusion when called upon to speak. Public comments will be received until the public comment period has elapsed.

 

Comments are limited to a maximum of two (2) minutes per speaker per agenda item, and individuals are limited to a single comment per agenda item.

 

Please contact boardcomments@scoe.net before the meeting if you have questions about the public comment process. 

 

Accommodating Individuals with Disabilities

 

The Sacramento County Office of Education encourages those with disabilities to participate fully in the public meeting process. If you need a disability-related modification or accommodation, including auxiliary aids or services, to participate in the public meeting, contact the Superintendent’s Office at (916) 228-2410 at least 48 hours before the scheduled Board meeting so that we may make every reasonable effort to accommodate you. [Government Code § 54953.2; Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990, § 202 (42 U.S.C. § 12132).]

BACKGROUND: 

 

CLASSIFIED 

 

Marina Miller, Program Analyst, Curriculum and Instruction Department, was nominated by Executive Director, District and School Support, Channa Cook and California Dyslexia Initiative (CDI) Director, Tami Wilson. Marina has a positive, can-do attitude while providing support to many programs and projects. Most of these projects are statewide initiatives that put SCOE in a highly visible environment, and Marina always ensures that we shine. Marina communicates with stakeholders from across the state in the various projects, including teachers, administrators, community members, and other educational agencies. She is clear, positive, and professional. Marina is a team player and always willing to assist with activities that are not part of her main tasks. One example is her support of the new countywide initiative, Sacramento County READS. Sacramento County READS is a highly visible SCOE initiative that is attended by each district in our county, community members, and SCOE Board trustees. Marina has handled the logistics, registration and communication with participants, and been on-site to ensure that the first two community of practice sessions were well-run, professional, high quality events for participants. Marina Miller has been a SCOE employee since September 2019.

 

CERTIFICATED

 

Barbara Fraijo, Alternative Education Teacher, Cordova Lane Senior Extension Program, was nominated by Court & Community Schools Principal Craig Bradford. Barbara has an exceptional ability for connecting with students by taking time to listen and develop a personal stake in their passions. She encourages students to continue their educational journey toward community college or to enter the workforce. Barbara goes above and beyond to ensure every student has a chance to succeed. She has taken a leadership role in the development of our new literacy curriculum to help our students grow as readers. Barbara has also taken the role of Teacher-in-Charge to help guide and mentor our Senior Extension staff. Barbara has worked in most of our SCOE settings and has been a CARE teacher, a Community School teacher, and an El Centro teacher. Barbara Fraijo has been a SCOE employee since January 2020.

BACKGROUND:

 

The Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) has the need for a new warehouse facility to replace our leased facility at 3735 Bradview Drive. Lease costs of our current warehouse space have grown exponentially, and the owner/landlord is increasingly resistant to negotiations. SCOE proposes building a Support Services Warehouse Facility to replace the leased facility. 

 

The proposed Support Services Warehouse Facility will be located on SCOE-owned land and will be part of our Missile Way complex. The project will involve the construction of a 24,000 sq. ft. facility that includes 14,000 sq. ft. of warehouse space and 10,000 sq. ft. of multi-use space, relocation of three portable buildings, parking lot reconfiguration, seal and restripe, security fencing, and lighting upgrades.

 

SCOE has sufficient reserve funds to design and build the new Support Services Warehouse Facility at Missile Way and believes it would be a prudent long-term investment for the agency. Architectural and engineering services will be needed to assist in determining best options for the required upgrades, to develop construction documents, and to ensure construction documents are in adherence with the laws and codes required by federal, state, local, and fire district agencies.

 

This project will be undertaken after using a formal bid process. Upon Board authorization, and completion of architectural drawings and specifications, staff will advertise and seek bids. At a future Board meeting, SCOE will seek Board approval to award the contract for the construction work to the lowest responsible/responsive bidder.

 

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:

 

The Superintendent recommends the Board authorize staff to conduct business as required to obtain bids for the new warehouse facility project, including obtaining all required federal, state, and local approvals, hiring an architect, and seeking public bids for the work.

BACKGROUND:

 

As authorized by Education Code section 1090(a)(2), Sacramento County Board of Education (Board) members who attend all meetings in a month receive compensation of $441.00 per month. On an annual basis, the Board may increase its members' monthly compensation by up to 5 percent. Any increase becomes effective upon approval of the Board, unless rejected by voter referendum. (Ed. Code, § 1090(a)(2),(g); Board Bylaw 9260.) 

 

According to information gathered by Sacramento County Office of Education staff, county board of education members in similarly sized counties receive the following monthly sums as compensation for their services:

 

  • $520.02 - San Bernadino
  • $562.61 - Orange
  • $651.29 - Riverside
  • $562.83 - San Diego
  • $627.40 - Contra Costa
  • $649.00 - Fresno
  • $1,061.28 - Alameda
  • $1,114.40 - Santa Clara

 

In the event the Board would like to increase its monthly compensation by 5 percent (from $441.00 to $463.05 per month) as permitted by law, Resolution No. 23-15 is attached for the Board's consideration. 

 

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:

 

The Superintendent recommends the Board consider and decide whether to approve Resolution No. 23-15, which would increase its monthly compensation by $22.05 per month.
