                                              RESOLUTION NO. 20/2022-23

                             OF .THE CENTER JOINT UNIFIED SCHOOL DISTRICT IN:
                             OPPOSITION TO THE ASPIRE SOLAIRE TERRITORY
                             TRANSFER PETITION AND FURTHER REQUESTING
                             THE    PLACER    AND    SACRAMENTO     COUNTY
                             COMMITTEES ON SCHOOL DISTRICT ORGANIZATION
                             DENY THE PETITION

               WHEREAS, on or about December 16, 2023, Petitioner Melissa Campbell submitted a petition
               to the Placer County Committee on School District Organization ("Placer County Committee")
               to transfer territory currently within the Center Joint Unified School District ("Center") to the
               Roseville Joint Union High School District and Roseville City School District (the "Petition"); -
               and

               WHEREAS, on February 8, 2023 and February 25, 2023, the Placer County Committee and
               Sacramento County Committee on Scho-ol District Organization conducted public hearings at
               which they received presentations from Petitioner, as well as public testimony from residents and
               organizations from each of the affected territories; and

               WHEREAS, the information provided highlighted the shortcomings of the Petition, including,
               but not limited to:

                   1) Loss of $78.9 million ($2023) in current assessed value which represents 2.25% of the
                      Center tax base

                  2) Eventual loss of a projected $119.5 million in future assessed value once the territory is
                     fully developed

                  3) Removal of 25 students originating from the territory and currently attending Center,
                     generating operational funding under the LCFF

                  4) An increase in General Obligation Bond tax rates· which are limited under Prop 39, for
                     Center taxpayers due to loss of assessed value estimated to total $3.59 per $100,000 of
                     assessed valuation

                  5) Lim,itations on future General Obligation Bond issuance plans for the Center Measure N
                     (2008) due to assessed value loss which niay impact future school construction plans

                  6) Removal of territory from which attendance to fill future Center school campuses
                     planned for the areas will be lost

                  7) Changes toJi still developing area before final road and school facilities plans have been
                     enacted

               WHEREAS, owing to these shortcomings, Center desires to publically express its opposition to
               this current Petition and further requests that the Placer County Committee (and Sacramento
               County Comm!ttee should it become involved) summarily deny the Petition.    •
