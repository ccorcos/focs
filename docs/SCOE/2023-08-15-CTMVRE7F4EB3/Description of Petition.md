 DESCRIPTION OF PETITION

 PROPOSED TRANSFER OF TERRITORY FROM CENTER JOINT UNIFIED
 SCHOOL DISTRICT TO ROSEVILLE CITY SCHOOL DISTRICT AND ROSEVILLE
 JOINT UNION HIGH SCHOOL DISTRICT

 Education Code section 35705.5 requires that a county committee on school district
 organization make available to the public and to the governing boards affected by a
 petition proposing to transfer territory between school districts a description of the petition
 and its effect, if approved.

 The petition being considered by the Sacramento County Committee on School District
 Organization (“County Committee”) requests a transfer of the “Aspire Solaire”
 (Westpark area of Roseville) neighborhood from the Center Joint Unified School
 District to the Roseville City School District and the Roseville Joint Union High School
 District (“Petition”). As described in the Petition, the area of the proposed Aspire
 Solaire territory transfer is bounded by Daylight Drive, Solaire Drive, Santucci
 Boulevard, and Pleasant Grove Boulevard in Roseville, CA within Placer County. The
 chief petitioner is Melissa Campbell.

 Following are specific details regarding the Petition, required by section 35705.5,
 relating to school district employees, funding, governance, elections, and division of
 property, obligations, and bonded indebtedness.

     1. The rights of the employees in the affected districts to continued employment.

 Not applicable to the Petition. The rights of the employees to continued employment
 will not be affected by the proposed territory transfer.

     2. The local control funding formula (LCFF) allocation per pupil for each affected
        district and the effect of the petition, if approved, on that allocation.

 None of the affected school districts are community funded (formerly known as “basic
 aid”) districts. Accordingly, funding calculations will continue to be determined on a per-
 pupil basis in accordance with the Local Control Funding Formula (LCFF).

     3. Whether the districts involved will be governed, in part, by provisions of a city
        charter and, if so, in what way.

 The City of Roseville is governed by a City Charter; however, it does not contain
 provisions referencing school districts or boards of education.

     4. Whether the governing boards of any proposed new district will have five or seven
        members.

 Not applicable to the Petition, as it does not propose the creation of any new district(s).

     5. A description of the territory or districts in which the election, if any, will be held.

 The election, if any, will be held within the Center Joint Unified School District. This
 specification is subject to change pending any additional information obtained during
 the public hearings (Ed. Code, § 35705), and decision on the Petition (Ed. Code, §
 35706).

     6. Where the proposal is to create two or more districts, whether the proposal will
        be voted on as a single proposition.

 Not applicable to the Petition, as it does not propose the creation of any new district(s).

     7. Whether the governing board of any new district will have trustee areas and, if so,
        whether the trustees will be elected by only the voters of that trustee area or by
        voters of the entire district.

 Not applicable to the Petition, as it does not propose the creation of any new school
 district(s). The proposed territory transfer will not immediately impact the membership
 or election of governing boards in the affected school districts, except that any population
 changes will need to be considered as part of redistricting after the next decennial
 census.

     8. A description of how the property, obligations, and bonded indebtedness of
        existing districts will be divided.

 The area proposed for transfer under the Petition contains no public school property or
 buildings. The plans and recommendations of the County Committee would stipulate
 the division of any other property, funds or obligations (except bonded indebtedness)
 affected by the proposed transfer. The County Committee may use any equitable
 means to divide the property, funds, and obligations, including assessed valuation,
 average daily attendance (ADA), or value and location of property. (Ed. Code, §§
 35560, 35736.) Absent any provision of the plans and recommendations of the County
 Committee to the contrary, the territory transferred will drop any liability for outstanding
 bonded indebtedness of the district of which it was formerly a part and assume its
 proportionate share of the outstanding bonded indebtedness of the district of which it
 becomes a part. (Ed. Code, § 35575.) Provisions for the exchange of property tax
 revenue are set forth in Revenue and Taxation Code, Section 99(i).

     9. A description of when the first governing board of any new district will be elected
        and how terms of office for each new trustee will be determined.

 Not applicable to the Petition, as it does not propose the creation of any new district(s).
