[[[**BACKGROUND:**]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}]{style="color:#000000;"}

 

[[[In December of 2022, the Sacramento and Placer County Offices of
Education each received a completed petition to transfer territory from
the Center Joint Unified School District (Center District) to the
Roseville City School District and the Roseville Joint Union High School
District (Roseville Districts). The Placer County Superintendent of
Schools determined the petition was legally sufficient and transmitted
the petition to the Placer County Committee on School District
Organization (Placer County Committee) and the State Board of Education
(SBE) on January 13, 2023. The Sacramento County Superintendent of
Schools concurred with this sufficiency determination and transmitted
the petition to the Sacramento County Committee on School District
Organization (County Committee) and SBE on January 24, 2023. 
  ]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}]{style="color:#000000;"}

 

[[[Because the petition involves school districts within the
jurisdictions of the Sacramento County Committee and the Placer County
Committee, both County Committees must consider the petition, make
necessary findings and tentative conclusions, and exchange these
findings and conclusions before the petition may be granted or denied.
(Ed. Code, sections 35520, 35524.) In addition, each County Committee
must consider environmental impacts of the proposed territory transfer
under the California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA). The Placer County
Committee held a public meeting on the CEQA issue on August 7,
2023.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}]{style="color:#000000;"}

 

[[[**PROCEDURAL HISTORY AND
REQUIREMENTS:**]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}]{style="color:#000000;"}

 

[[[[Petition to Transfer
Territory]{.underline}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}]{style="color:#000000;"}

 

[[[The petition requests transfer of the \"Aspire Solaire\" neighborhood
located in the Westpark area of Roseville, California, 95747, bordered
by Daylight Drive, Solaire Drive, Santucci Boulevard and Pleasant Grove
Boulevard within Placer County.
]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}]{style="color:#000000;"}

 

[[[[Environmental
Evaluation]{.underline}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}]{style="color:#000000;"}

 

[[[Before approving a proposal to reorganize school districts, the
County Committee must first determine the application of CEQA to the
reorganization project. If the County Committee finds the project is
exempt from CEQA, no further environmental review is necessary, and in
such cases, the County Committee may file a notice of CEQA exemption, if
it chooses to do so. (Cal. Code Regs., tit. 14, section 15062,
subd.(a).)
]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}]{style="color:#000000;"}

 

[[[If the County Committee determines the project is not exempt, the
County Committee must conduct an initial study to determine whether the
proposed project may have a significant impact on the environment. The
County Committee will then file a negative declaration of environmental
impact or complete an environmental impact report depending on the
findings of the initial study. (Ed. Code, section
35706.)]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}]{style="color:#000000;"}

 

[[[**CALIFORNIA ENVIRONMENTAL QUALITY ACT
ANALYSIS:**]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}]{style="color:#000000;"}

 

[[[[The Proposed Territory Transfer Appears to be Exempt From
CEQA]{.underline}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}]{style="color:#000000;"}

 

[[[Under CEQA regulations, certain types of projects are categorically
exempt from CEQA requirements, including minor additions to schools.
This is defined as, \"minor additions to existing schools within
existing school grounds where the addition does not increase original
student capacity by more than 25% or 10 classrooms, whichever is less.\"
(Cal. Code Regs., tit. 14, section
15314.)]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}]{style="color:#000000;"}

 

[[[Here, staff for the Placer County Committee conducted a preliminary
review of the proposed territory transfer. They learned it would
reportedly involve an estimated 25
students]{style="color:#000000;"}[[[[\[1\]]{.MsoFootnoteReference
style="vertical-align:super"}]{.MsoFootnoteReference
style="vertical-align:super"}]{style="color:#000000;"}](#_ftn1){#_ftnref1}[
residing within Aspire Solaire in the Center District transferring to
five possible receptor
schools]{style="color:#000000;"}[[[[\[2\]]{.MsoFootnoteReference
style="vertical-align:super"}]{.MsoFootnoteReference
style="vertical-align:super"}]{style="color:#000000;"}](#_ftn2){#_ftnref2}[
in the Roseville Districts. All of the impacted Roseville schools are
reportedly operating with sufficient capacity to house the entire
incoming student population without increasing their original student
capacity by 25% or ten (10) classrooms. Though one of the possible
receptor schools is reportedly operating at or close to capacity, even
if all 25 students were to transfer to the one school, the increase in
capacity would be much less than 25% or 10 classrooms. Additionally,
with the possible exception of potentially adding a portable classroom
at this one receptor school, no other classrooms will likely need to be
added and there will reportedly likely be minimal to no physical changes
required at any receptor site to accommodate the potential transferring
students.]{style="color:#000000;"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[[[There Do Not Appear to be Applicable Exceptions to the
Exemption]{.underline}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}]{style="color:#000000;"}

 

[[[CEQA regulations list several exceptions to the CEQA exemption. The
only exception that may possibly apply is the \"Cumulative Impact\"
exception. It states that exemptions are inapplicable \"when the
cumulative impact of successive projects of the same type in the same
place, over time is significant.\"(Cal. Code Regs., tit. 14, section
15300.2, subd.(b).) In this case, there has been just one prior
territory transfer in 2019 that reportedly occasioned the transfer from
the Center District to the Roseville Districts of 27 students. Even if
the total students from the prior transfer and the proposed transfer are
combined for a total of 52 students, the increase in capacity would
still be much less than 25% or 10 classrooms. That remains true even if
all of the potentially impacted students transferred to just one school,
which is not anticipated.
]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}]{style="color:#000000;"}
