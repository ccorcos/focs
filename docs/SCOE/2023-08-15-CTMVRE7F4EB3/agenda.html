Public Comment

 

Anyone may appear at the County Committee meeting to provide public comment regarding any item on the agenda or regarding any matter that is within the Committee's subject matter jurisdiction. However, the Committee may not take action on any item not on the agenda except as authorized by Government Code section 54954.2.  If possible, notify the Committee Chair in writing prior to the meeting if you wish to testify.

 

Individuals wishing to speak in person before the Committee are requested to complete a public comment form. Speakers will be called in the order in which the cards are received. 

 

Public comment via Zoom teleconference may be made by using the raised hand feature in Zoom during the agenda item to be addressed. The speaker will be identified by their Display Name in Zoom when called upon to speak. Those joining the meeting via Zoom are requested to display their full name so there is no confusion when called upon to speak. Public comments will be received until the public comment period has elapsed.

 

Comments are limited to a maximum of two (2) minutes per speaker per agenda item, and individuals are limited to a single comment per agenda item.

 

Please contact boardcomments@scoe.net before the meeting if you have questions about the public comment process. 

 

Accommodating Individuals with Disabilities

 

The Sacramento County Office of Education encourages those with disabilities to participate fully in the public meeting process. If you need a disability-related modification or accommodation, including auxiliary aids or services, to participate in the public meeting, contact the Superintendent’s Office at (916) 228-2410 at least 48 hours before the scheduled Board meeting so that we may make every reasonable effort to accommodate you. [Government Code § 54953.2; Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990, § 202 (42 U.S.C. § 12132).]

BACKGROUND:

 

In December of 2022, the Sacramento and Placer County Offices of Education each received a completed petition to transfer territory from the Center Joint Unified School District (Center District) to the Roseville City School District and the Roseville Joint Union High School District (Roseville Districts). The Placer County Superintendent of Schools determined the petition was legally sufficient and transmitted the petition to the Placer County Committee on School District Organization (Placer County Committee) and the State Board of Education (SBE) on January 13, 2023. The Sacramento County Superintendent of Schools concurred with this sufficiency determination and transmitted the petition to the Sacramento County Committee on School District Organization (County Committee) and SBE on January 24, 2023.    

 

Because the petition involves school districts within the jurisdictions of the Sacramento County Committee and the Placer County Committee, both County Committees must consider the petition, make necessary findings and tentative conclusions, and exchange these findings and conclusions before the petition may be granted or denied. (Ed. Code, sections 35520, 35524.) In addition, each County Committee must consider environmental impacts of the proposed territory transfer under the California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA). The Placer County Committee held a public meeting on the CEQA issue on August 7, 2023.

 

PROCEDURAL HISTORY AND REQUIREMENTS:

 

Petition to Transfer Territory

 

The petition requests transfer of the "Aspire Solaire" neighborhood located in the Westpark area of Roseville, California, 95747, bordered by Daylight Drive, Solaire Drive, Santucci Boulevard and Pleasant Grove Boulevard within Placer County.

 

Environmental Evaluation

 

Before approving a proposal to reorganize school districts, the County Committee must first determine the application of CEQA to the reorganization project. If the County Committee finds the project is exempt from CEQA, no further environmental review is necessary, and in such cases, the County Committee may file a notice of CEQA exemption, if it chooses to do so. (Cal. Code Regs., tit. 14, section 15062, subd.(a).)

 

If the County Committee determines the project is not exempt, the County Committee must conduct an initial study to determine whether the proposed project may have a significant impact on the environment. The County Committee will then file a negative declaration of environmental impact or complete an environmental impact report depending on the findings of the initial study. (Ed. Code, section 35706.)

 

CALIFORNIA ENVIRONMENTAL QUALITY ACT ANALYSIS:

 

The Proposed Territory Transfer Appears to be Exempt From CEQA

 

Under CEQA regulations, certain types of projects are categorically exempt from CEQA requirements, including minor additions to schools. This is defined as, "minor additions to existing schools within existing school grounds where the addition does not increase original student capacity by more than 25% or 10 classrooms, whichever is less." (Cal. Code Regs., tit. 14, section 15314.)

 

Here, staff for the Placer County Committee conducted a preliminary review of the proposed territory transfer. They learned it would reportedly involve an estimated 25 students[1] residing within Aspire Solaire in the Center District transferring to five possible receptor schools[2] in the Roseville Districts. All of the impacted Roseville schools are reportedly operating with sufficient capacity to house the entire incoming student population without increasing their original student capacity by 25% or ten (10) classrooms. Though one of the possible receptor schools is reportedly operating at or close to capacity, even if all 25 students were to transfer to the one school, the increase in capacity would be much less than 25% or 10 classrooms. Additionally, with the possible exception of potentially adding a portable classroom at this one receptor school, no other classrooms will likely need to be added and there will reportedly likely be minimal to no physical changes required at any receptor site to accommodate the potential transferring students.

 

There Do Not Appear to be Applicable Exceptions to the Exemption

 

CEQA regulations list several exceptions to the CEQA exemption. The only exception that may possibly apply is the "Cumulative Impact" exception. It states that exemptions are inapplicable "when the cumulative impact of successive projects of the same type in the same place, over time is significant."(Cal. Code Regs., tit. 14, section 15300.2, subd.(b).) In this case, there has been just one prior territory transfer in 2019 that reportedly occasioned the transfer from the Center District to the Roseville Districts of 27 students. Even if the total students from the prior transfer and the proposed transfer are combined for a total of 52 students, the increase in capacity would still be much less than 25% or 10 classrooms. That remains true even if all of the potentially impacted students transferred to just one school, which is not anticipated.

 

[1] According to Center District data, this includes eight high school students, five middle school students, and 12 elementary school students.

[2] According to the Roseville Districts, these schools include Westbrook Elementary School, Chilton Middle School, Roseville High School, Oakmont High School, or Antelope High School.

 

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:

 

The Superintendent recommends the Sacramento County Committee on School District Organization determine the proposed territory transfer is categorically exempt under CEQA without exception in accordance with Public Resource Code section 21084 and CEQA regulations (Cal. Code Regs., tit. 14, sections 15300.2, 15314).
