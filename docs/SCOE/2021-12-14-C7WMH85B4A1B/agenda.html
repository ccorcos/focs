Program Description:  SCOE will receive $50,000 to provide onsite coaching to Natomas Charter School as they engage in work around developing and refining their Multi-Tiered System of Support (MTSS). Specifically, Natomas Charter School is working toward creating coherence among their five academies. BACKGROUND:

 

Sacramento County Board of Education (Board) seats in Trustee Areas I, II, and III are up for re-election in June 2022. The cost of this Trustee election will be substantially less expensive if it is consolidated with the statewide primary election held on June 7, 2022. To consolidate its Trustee election with the statewide election, the Board must adopt and file a resolution requesting consolidation.

 

Resolution No. 21-16 calls for the election of Board Trustees, and requests that it be consolidated with the primary election scheduled for June 7, 2022 for the following Trustee Areas and terms of office:

 

Trustee Area I

Said Term: 4 years

(07/01/2022 – 06/30/2026)

 

Trustee Area II

Said Term: 4 years

(07/01/2022 – 06/30/2026)

 

Trustee Area III

Said Term: 4 years

(07/01/2022 – 06/30/2026)

 

The Resolution sets forth other guidelines for the election. To reduce election costs, the Resolution asks the County Registrar to combine the required notice of election with other special districts. It also declares that individual candidate(s) shall be responsible for and prepay the publication cost of the voluntary candidate’s statement in the Sample Official Ballot Pamphlet, and it designates the number of words that may be in the candidate’s statement.

 

The Board will need to determine whether the candidate’s statement will be a maximum of either 200 or 400 words. The County Elections Office estimates that a 400 word statement will cost approximately $2,150 - $2,250 per candidate, while a 200 word statement will cost approximately $1,850 - $1,950 per candidate.

 

By law, Sacramento County Board of Education members are elected on the same date as the statewide direct primary election and take office on the first day of July. (Ed. Code § 1007.) In 2022, and in years without a presidential election, the statewide direct primary is held on the first Tuesday after the first Monday in June in each even-numbered year. (Elec. Code, § 316.) In presidential election years, the statewide direct primary is held on the first Tuesday after the first Monday in March.

 

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:

 

The Superintendent recommends the Board determine the maximum number of words for the voluntary candidate’s statement and adopt Resolution No. 21-16 - Calling for Election of Trustees to the Sacramento County Board of Education on June 7, 2022.

BACKGROUND:

 

Recently enacted Assembly Bill 361 amended elements of the Brown Act (Government Code section 54950, et seq.) to permit local agencies to continue holding teleconferenced meetings using simplified procedures during a proclaimed state of emergency.

 

Under Government Code section 54953(e), in order to continue holding teleconferenced board meetings using simplified Brown Act procedures, local agencies, including school districts and county boards of education, must make the following findings by majority vote and readopt such findings every 30 days thereafter:

  1. The board has reconsidered the circumstances of the state of emergency, AND
  2. The state of emergency continues to directly impact the ability of members to meet safely in person, OR state or local officials continue to impose or recommend measures to promote social distancing.

In the event the Board decides to continue to hold teleconferenced Board meetings using abbreviated Brown Act procedures, Resolution No. 21-17 is attached for the Board's consideration.

 

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:

 

The Superintendent recommends that the Board consider and decide whether to adopt Resolution No. 21-17, which would permit it to continue holding teleconferenced Board meetings under abbreviated Brown Act procedures.

BACKGROUND:

 

The Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) has developed and will present a plan for the expenditure of Educator Effectiveness Block Grant funds. These funds can be expended between 2021-2022 and 2025-2026.

 

The intent of the funds is to provide professional learning for teachers, administrators, paraprofessionals who work with pupils, and classified staff that interact with pupils, with a focus on any of the following areas: coaching and mentoring, programs that lead to effective standards-based instruction that improves literacy across all areas, practices and strategies to re-engage pupils and lead to accelerated learning, strategies to implement social-emotional learning, strategies to create a positive school climate, strategies for effective language acquisition for English learners, strategies to incorporate ethnic studies curriculum, and strategies for early childhood educators. This plan will extend the professional learning activities detailed in the Expanded Learning Opportunities Grant (ELOG) and the Elementary and Secondary School Emergency Relief Fund (ESSER) plans and extends the targeted and detailed work towards student achievement. SCOE will align evaluation of the various professional learning activities detailed in the Educator Effectiveness Block Grant funds to the larger programmatic outcome and evaluation currently being developed.

 

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION: 

 

The Superintendent recommends the Board approve the plan for the expenditure of Educator Effectiveness Block Grant funds to be expended between 2021-2022 and 2025-2026.
