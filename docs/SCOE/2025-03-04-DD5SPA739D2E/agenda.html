Public Comment

 

Anyone may appear at the Board meeting to provide public comment regarding any item on the agenda or regarding any matter that is within the Board’s subject matter jurisdiction. However, the Board may not take action on any item not on the agenda except as authorized by Government Code section 54954.2. If possible, notify the Board President or Board Secretary in writing prior to the meeting if you wish to testify.

 

Individuals wishing to speak in person before the Board are requested to complete a public comment form. Speakers will be called in the order in which the cards are received. 

 

Public comment via Zoom teleconference may be made by using the raised hand feature in Zoom during the agenda item to be addressed. The speaker will be identified by their Display Name in Zoom when called upon to speak. Those joining the meeting via Zoom are requested to display their full name so there is no confusion when called upon to speak. Public comments will be received until the public comment period has elapsed.

 

Comments are limited to a maximum of two (2) minutes per speaker per agenda item, and individuals are limited to a single comment per agenda item.

 

Please contact boardcomments@scoe.net before the meeting if you have questions about the public comment process. 

 

Accommodating Individuals with Disabilities

 

The Sacramento County Office of Education encourages those with disabilities to participate fully in the public meeting process. If you need a disability-related modification or accommodation, including auxiliary aids or services, to participate in the public meeting, contact the Superintendent’s Office at (916) 228-2410 at least 48 hours before the scheduled Board meeting so that we may make every reasonable effort to accommodate you. [Government Code § 54953.2; Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990, § 202 (42 U.S.C. § 12132).]

CLASSIFIED 

 

Parul Sheth, an Accounting Technician with the Financial Services Department, was nominated by Associate Superintendent Nick Schweizer and Manager Hong Yamamoto for her outstanding performance and commitment to excellence. Parul consistently pays attention to details and maintains a high standard of accuracy in her work from verifying and processing invoices against purchase orders, reconciling discrepancies, to maintaining and auditing financial documents. Her proactive approach to communication with staff and vendors has increased efficiency, ensuring timely payments to vendors and building professional relationships with the vendors. Parul is a meticulous organizer. She confirms that purchase orders and contracts are properly documented and financial records are filed to ensure consistency, compliance, and traceability. Parul plays a significant role in Accounts Payable, and she is also an exceptional collaborator who is always ready to support the team whenever is is needed. Her contributions are instrumental in enhancing the operations and reputation of the Financial Services Department, making her a key asset to the organization. Parul Sheth has been a SCOE Employee since September 2023. 

 

CERTIFICATED

 

Suzanne White, affectionately known as Fern, is a Teacher at Sly Park Environmental Education Center and was nominated by Director II Jeremy Meyers. Suzanne brings a lifelong love for nature and learning to her role. From leading kayaking adventures in Alaska to sparking curiosity in the classroom, she seamlessly blends outdoor expertise with a passion for education. Her enthusiasm for environmental studies and language makes her a dynamic and engaging educator, inspiring students to explore and appreciate the natural world. Beyond teaching, Suzanne has taken on leadership responsibilities to enhance Sly Park's programs. She plays a key role in maintaining the center's online presence, ensuring it reflects the high-quality education offered. Her dedication extends beyond the classroom into the community, where her involvement has helped Sly Park earn an award of distinction. A dedicated and trusted team member, she continuously seeks ways to elevate the learning experience for students. Suzanne's commitment to learning and environmental stewardship makes her an invaluable asset to Sly Park. Suzanne White has been a SCOE employee since October 2012. 

 

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION: 

 

The Superintendent recommends that the Board approve commendation of the individuals named as Sacramento County Office of Education Classified and Certificated Employees of the Month for April 2025 and that the Board present Certificates of Recognition to these employees.

Department/Cabinet Member:  Educational Services/Dr. Channa Pitt

 

Funding Source:  California Department of Education (CDE) 

 

Amount Requested:  $155,410

 

Fiscal Year(s):  2024-2025, 2025-2026

 

Program Description:  

SCOE was selected in Region 3 as the Regional English Learner EL/Title III Specialist as part of the California Statewide System of Support to which the CDE awarded grants to one county within each of the state's eleven California County Superintendents Educational Services Association regions. 

 

As the Region 3 EL Specialist, SCOE will provide technical assistance for continuous improvement to equip LEAs and COEs with resources and tools to support the development, implementation, and evaluation of strategies to ensure that English learners have the resources they need to succeed. The scope of work includes guidance and support to LEAs and COEs in implementing and improving programs and developing Local Control and Accountability Plan (LCAP) Addenda. In addition, SCOE will organize and provide coaching for systemic improvement in data analysis and data-based decision-making, high-quality instruction, connect resources for specialized programs, and facilitate quarterly Multilingual Education Leadership Network meetings to support LEAs. 

 

New Positions:  None

 

Subcontracts:  English learner services and differentiated regional support with expert entities TBD.

 

Evaluation Component:  SCOE will report on services provided and this qualitative data for annual reports will include survey results based on the support provided to LEAs. Quantitative data is captured on the number of consultants provided to LEAs annually.

Department/Cabinet Member:  Educational Services/Dr. Channa Pitt

 

Funding Source:  California Department of Education 

 

Amount Requested:  $2,700,000

 

Fiscal Year(s):  2024-2025, 2025-2026, 2026-2027

 

Program Description: 

Count Play Explore (CPE, led by Fresno County Superintendent of Schools) and the California Preschool Instructional Network (CPIN, led by SCOE) are partnering to increase the expertise regarding early math throughout the state by leveraging the structure of CPIN and professional learning content developed by WestEd with the support of CPE resources (formerly California Statewide Early Math Initiative). Specific goals are to increase the knowledge and skills of existing CPIN instructors as well as to increase the number of authorized CPIN trainers in the domains of math and science. Ultimately, the target outcome will be to increase the number of high-quality professional learning opportunities offered for early learning and care educators statewide. The concerted efforts to expand knowledge and build strong pedagogy in early math will include a variety of audiences from local education agencies (LEAs) to private/public preschool centers and family child care as well as family, friends, and neighbors (FFNs). 

 

New Positions: None

 

Subcontracts: Based on California Department of Social Services CPIN regional allocations TBD. 

 

Evaluation Component: Data is collected regarding number of participants (teachers/providers) across the state. Survey data will be captured within the CPIN established survey/evaluation.
