Community Engagement

Describe the meaningful efforts made by the LEA to consult with its required community members and the opportunities provided by the school for public input in the development
of the plan.
Based on the excerpt from SCOE’s 2024–25 Local Control and Accountability Plan (LCAP), two groups explicitly listed access to the arts as a priority:

Community-Based Organizations - They emphasized the importance of “engaging students with the arts, hands-on learning” as part of their broader recommendations for
student engagement and culturally relevant education.
Students (via listening sessions and surveys) - Students highlighted a desire for “hands-on arts education” among their top priorities, alongside academic support, mental health
resources, and opportunities for student voice.
These inputs suggest that both external partners and students themselves see arts education as a meaningful and necessary component of their learning experience.


Describe how the development of the plan was influenced by community input.
To amplify student voice and engagement, a Youth Advisory Committee was initiated in 2022-23. At midyear, seven meetings had been held with twelve students participating.
They are developing an overall vision for the Committee with an eye to the future. The students have discussed activities like spirit week, college tours, and field trips. The student
mural project is one example of this committee’s work. It integrates youth voice, art, and leadership. Students noted that even if they may not be at the school in the future to
experience some of the innovations, they know it is the best thing to do for future students.
