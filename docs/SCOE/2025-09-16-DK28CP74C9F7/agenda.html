Public Comment

 

Anyone may appear at the Board meeting to provide public comment regarding any item on the agenda or regarding any matter that is within the Board’s subject matter jurisdiction. However, the Board may not take action on any item not on the agenda except as authorized by Government Code section 54954.2. If possible, notify the Board President or Board Secretary in writing prior to the meeting if you wish to testify.

 

Individuals wishing to speak in person before the Board are requested to complete a public comment form. Speakers will be called in the order in which the cards are received. 

 

Public comment via Zoom teleconference may be made by using the raised hand feature in Zoom during the agenda item to be addressed. The speaker will be identified by their Display Name in Zoom when called upon to speak. Those joining the meeting via Zoom are requested to display their full name so there is no confusion when called upon to speak. Public comments will be received until the public comment period has elapsed.

 

Comments are limited to a maximum of two (2) minutes per speaker per agenda item, and individuals are limited to a single comment per agenda item.

 

Please contact contactscoe@scoe.net before the meeting if you have questions about the public comment process. 

 

Accommodating Individuals with Disabilities

 

The Sacramento County Office of Education encourages those with disabilities to participate fully in the public meeting process. If you need a disability-related modification or accommodation, including auxiliary aids or services, to participate in the public meeting, contact the Superintendent’s Office at (916) 228-2410 at least 48 hours before the scheduled Board meeting so that we may make every reasonable effort to accommodate you. [Government Code § 54953.2; Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990, § 202 (42 U.S.C. § 12132).]

A. ***REVISED***NOTICE OF PUBLIC HEARING and Action on Proposed Material Revisions to Capital College & Career Academy (CCCA) Charter School Petition and CCCA Status Update - Dr. Nancy Herota

BACKGROUND:

 

A public hearing will be held by the Sacramento County Board of Education (County Board) to receive public comment and input from the community regarding proposed material revisions to the Capital College & Career Academy (CCCA) Charter School petition. The Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) will also provide a status update on areas of concern regarding the CCCA Charter School. The public hearing will be held at SCOE located at 10474 Mather Boulevard in Mather, California during the regular meeting of the County Board which is scheduled to commence at 6:30 p.m. on September 16, 2025.

 

REQUEST FOR MATERIAL REVISIONS TO CHARTER PETITION:

 

The County Board's Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) with the Charter School requires CCCA to seek a material revision of its charter petition when there are changes in enrollment that represent an increase or decrease from the annual enrollment originally projected in the charter petition by more than 25% in any grade level or 10% of total enrollment in any given year. The MOU also requires a material revision to the petition when there is a substantial change to the educational program or if there is a substantial change to admission requirements and/or enrollment preferences in the charter petition.

 

CCCA has submitted its request for material revisions to (1) update its current and projected enrollment, (2) update its educational offerings and graduation requirements, and (3) update its enrollment process, and (4) update the charter petition in accordance with current laws. (See Attachments 1, 2)

 

Enrollment:

 

CCCA's actual enrollment has been significantly less than what was projected in its approved charter petition. Therefore, it is proposing to update the current and projected enrollment listed on page 34 of its charter petition as follows:

(See Attachment 1, page 3) Please note that CCCA currently has 126 students enrolled for 2025-2026.

 

Educational Program/Course Offerings:

 

CCCA proposes making changes to the course offerings originally described on pages 44-45 of its petition in Table 1C: Course Offerings. CCCA planned to offer four years of Spanish as the “World Language” but has changed the language courses in the charter petition to “World Language” and has been offering Mandarin as the world language. The Art courses offered in the original petition were related to construction and architecture. CCCA proposes changing the Art courses to Video Game Design and an Arts Media and Entertainment class. (See Attachment 1, pages 3-4, Table 1C)

 

The charter petition describes job shadowing and internships culminating in a final capstone project. However, CCCA has established graduation requirements that now include “Career Experience” prior to the final capstone project, which results in 30 additional units required for graduation. (See Attachment 1, page 4, Table 1D)

 

Admissions Process:

 

The application process described on page 170 of CCCA's charter petition states that applications for enrollment will be accepted for students enrolling in the 9th and 10th grades. CCCA also intends to accept applications for students who will enroll in the 11th grade level. (See Attachment 1, page 5)

 

Updating Charter Petition in Accordance with Current Laws:

 

Pursuant to Education Code section 47607(b), CCCA also proposes to update its charter petition with new legal requirements that have been enacted since approval of the original charter petition. (See Attachment 2)

 

In the event the County Board decides to approve CCCA's request for material revisions, Resolution No. 25-13 is provided for the County Board's consideration. (See Attachment 3)

 

AREAS OF CONCERN:


As shown in the enrollment chart above, since its opening in 2023-2024 to the current school year, CCCA's actual enrollment has been 35–50% lower than projected in its charter petition. These large differences in projected versus actual enrollment have resulted in significant shortfalls in revenues and a heavy reliance on fundraising and one-time grants to cover operational costs. In addition, CCCA has not maintained the required 5% reserve for economic uncertainties required by the MOU in any year of its operation and is at risk of running out of cash in a few months. As CCCA’s lower than expected enrollment directly impacts its financial condition and solvency, CCCA has been asked to present a plan for ensuring that it has sufficient funds to maintain operations throughout the 2025-2026 school year in conjunction with its request to materially revise the enrollment projections in its charter petition.


Throughout the 2024-2025 school year, SCOE provided extensive support to CCCA to address significant concerns with teacher assignments and vacancies in the 2025-2026 school year. SCOE continues to provide extensive technical assistance on teacher credentialing issues, including providing a detailed analysis of educators' credentials/assignments, and working with CCCA to identify steps for pathways to temporary permits and/or clear credentials. CCCA continues to struggle with its teacher credentialing and assignments.  With SCOE’s detailed guidance, CCCA is now on track to having appropriate credentialing and temporary certificates for its educator assignments. CCCA currently has two teacher vacancies (in its English and Entrepreneurship courses), which are being filled by a rotation of substitute teachers.  

 

On September 3, 2025, SCOE sent a written notice of concern regarding CCCA's current fiscal condition and ability to continue to fund its operations through the 2025-2026 school year and its teacher credentialing and assignments.  SCOE’s notice of concern requested that CCCA, by September 10 2025, provide a revised budget and cashflow projections and a detailed plan to decrease expenditures and increase revenues as needed to remain cash solvent and meet its minimum reserve requirement.  CCCA provided its revised budget and financial plan to SCOE after 5 pm on Friday, September 12, 2025.  

 

SCOE has reviewed CCCA’s plan and determined that it does not include adequate documentation to ensure that CCCA can remain financially solvent to continue operating throughout the 2025-2026 school year. CCCA proposes to close its budget deficit with one-time grants and fundraising; however, such additional funding has not been verified. SCOE has asked CCCA to clarify certain items and to present its revised budget and financial plan at the September 16, 2025 Board meeting. SCOE is preparing and will provide a more detailed analysis of CCCA’s revised budget and financial plan at that time.

 

It is recommended that CCCA’s request for a material revision of its actual and projected enrollment not be approved unless and until CCCA provides sufficient documentation that CCCA can remain financially solvent throughout the 2025-2026 school year, notwithstanding its current enrollment, and that CCCA be required to provide this documentation within 30 days.  A proposed Revised Resolution No. 25-13 has been included for the Board’s consideration. (See Revised Attachment 3)

 

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:

 

The Superintendent recommends that the County Board (1) consider the Capital College & Career Academy's request for material revisions to its charter petition, (2) and decide whether to adopt Revised Resolution No. 25-13 approving the material revisions to CCCA’s course offerings, graduation requirements, and admissions process; and withholding approval of the material revisions to CCCAs actual and projected enrollment unless and until CCCA sufficiently documents its ability to remain fiscally solvent notwithstanding its current enrollment, and (2) (3) consider the areas of concern identified by SCOE and determine whether any other next steps or actions are appropriate at this time.

BACKGROUND:

 

The Sacramento County Office of Education’s Arts & Music Annual Report is developed in accordance with statute code 8820 (g)(4). This annual report must be approved by the Board, submitted to the CDE, and posted to the SCOE website. The mandated information for this report includes:

 

Narrative Overview 

This year, SCOE made significant strides in expanding direct arts instruction across its community schools, reaffirming its commitment to equity, creativity, and student voice. Through intentional planning and collaboration, arts education became a more visible and vital part of the student experience at: Nathaniel S. Colley High School, North Area Community School, Elinor Lincoln Hickey Jr./Sr. High School, El Centro Jr./Sr. High School, Leo A. Palmiter Jr./Sr. High School, and Foundations Academy.

 

Our schools received arts and arts-integrated instruction that connected core academic content, social emotional learning, culturally responsive pedagogy and student voice. For example, El Centro Jr./Sr. High School received a particularly robust infusion of arts instruction. These lessons focused on entrepreneurship in visual art and provided students with meaningful opportunities to explore identity, build confidence, and engage in hands-on learning. The Foundation Academy received music instruction in every classroom with every teacher throughout the school year.

 

In partnership with local mural artists, four campuses—Colley, El Centro, Hickey, and Palmiter—now feature vibrant, student-informed murals that reflect the spirit and stories of their school communities. These public artworks have transformed shared spaces and fostered a sense of pride and belonging.

 

Additionally, Colley, Hickey, and Palmiter now host a student art gallery, offering a dedicated space for students to display their work throughout the year. These galleries have become powerful platforms for student voice, showcasing a wide range of artistic media and perspectives.

 

Together, these initiatives represent a bold step forward in SCOE’s vision for inclusive, arts-rich education. By embedding the arts into the fabric of our community schools, we are not only enriching academic outcomes but also nurturing the creative potential of every student.

 

Staffing Supported by AMS Funds: Through AMS Funding SCOE was able to fund a .4 FTE to provide direct arts instruction for its Court/Community Schools.

Funding Utilized: Of the $153,719 allocated to SCOE, $78,744 was used to provide arts instruction.

School Sites Providing Arts Education: 24 school sites including the Foundations and CARE Programs.

Students Served: Approximately 452 students were directly impacted. Through SCOE’s Foundations program, approximately 1,760 students were impacted through our community arts programs.

 

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:


The Superintendent recommends that the Board approve the Sacramento County Office of Education’s Arts & Music Annual Report.

Department/Cabinet Member: Educational Services/Dr. Nancy Herota

 

Funding Source: Walton Investment Foundation

 

Amount Requested: $1,100,000

 

Fiscal Year(s): 2025-2026, 2026-2027, 2027-2028 

 

Program Description:

Phase II of this project aims to lead a countywide initiative to ensure every K-8 student develops the numeracy skills, confidence, and curiosity needed for success in higher -level mathematics. Grounded in the California Mathematics Framework, this work supports districts through a sequenced, cohort-based model of high-quality instructional material (HQIM) adoption, providing tailored support aligned to each system's readiness and context. 

 

Subcontracts:

SCOE will enter into subcontracts, as necessary, to ensure the successful implementation of the grant. The subcontracts will support the completion of tasks outlined in the grant's scope of work and will help fulfill all deliverables in alignment with the expectations and requirements set forth by the grantor.

 

Evaluation Component:

The data collected and evaluation process will focus on assessing the adoption and implementation of Math HQIM, professional learning, and student outcomes. This includes reviewing district benchmarks, CAASPP, and formative assessment data, along with professional learning calendars, funding allocations, and meeting agendas. Key staff interviews, classroom walkthroughs, lesson plan reviews, and observations will provide insights into instructional practices and educator support. Additionally, student pre- and post-assessments, along with empathy interviews, will help evaluate the impact of implementation on learning experiences and outcomes.
