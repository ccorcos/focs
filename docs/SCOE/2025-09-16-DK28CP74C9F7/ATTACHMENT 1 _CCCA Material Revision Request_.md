I. Overview
As outlined in our Memorandum of Understanding with Sacramento County Office of
Education, a material revision to the charter will take place when, changes in enrollment
represent an increase or decrease from the annual enrollment originally projected in the charter
petition by more than 25% in any grade level or 10% of total enrollment in any given year.
Furthermore, while we do not believe any substantive changes to the proposed course outline has
taken place we are using this opportunity to include a revision to our graduation requirements
and proposed course outline to provide further clarification and continuity between our petition
and our current practices.

The purpose of this material revision is to provide enrollment projections that are reflective of
our current enrollment and projected growth, clarify our educational offerings, and update the
petition in accordance with current laws. While enrollment has come in lower than originally
anticipated we continue to exceed the promises outlined in our original charter petition. We are
closing the achievement gap, engaging learners in a broad course of study, and ensuring our
upcoming graduates are truly college accelerated and career prepared. We have a growing and
dedicated group of students, parents, and staff members that all contribute to a one-of-a-kind
learning environment. With all of the positives on campus, we are taking a multi-faceted
approach to ensure our enrollment exceeds these projections.


II. Material Revisions
The proposed material revision on page 34 are as follows:
       • Capital College & Career Academy has updated the enrollment chart on page 34 (Table
       1A) with grade level assumptions reflective of current enrollment. These enrollment
       numbers are not intended to be enrollment caps, but rather a reasonable approximation of
       the level of community interest. Actual grade level ratios may be adjusted based on
       community interest and demand. As stated in our original charter petition we do not plan
       to reach full enrollment until approximately year 7 or 8.
The proposed material revision on pages 44 and 45 are as follows:
       • While the proposed course outline (Table 1C) remains essentially the same, the way in
       which we have actualized our graduation pathway has changed slightly. While we
       originally planned to offer Spanish as our world language, we ended up offering
       Mandarin instead for a range of reasons. In the original petition we believed our art
       courses would be more aligned with architecture and engineering but all of the proposed
       courses are currently offered through our dual enrollment partnership. Reflective of
       student interests and the desire to further strengthen our career pathways this year we are
       offering Video Game Design and an Arts Media and Entertainment class.
       •In the original charter petition we describe our job shadows and internships culminating
       with a final capstone project. While this all remains true still, we have since prior to
       enrolling our first student established the graduation requirements found in Table 1D.
       The additional units are the result of recognizing the career experience work students do
       leading up to the culminating capstone project.
The proposed material revision on page 170 is as follows:
       • Based on enrollment we have enrolled several first semester juniors who can still
       graduate on time and while meeting our graduation requirements.

The Charter School and the County agree to adhere to the requirements related to admission
preferences as set forth in Education Code Section 47605.6(e)(2)(B)(i)-(iv).
The Charter School’s application process is comprised of the following steps:
          1. Applications requesting basic student information will be available at the Charter
             School’s website and at the Charter School’s physical location. Full registration packets
             will be provided to families of admitted students to confirm attendance following the
             lottery.
          2. Applications will be accepted during a publicly advertised open enrollment period,
             which occurs in late November to early February of each year for enrollment in 9th or
             10th grade in the following school year. The charter school may enroll students entering
             the 11th grade through the first semester of their 11th grade year if space permits and
             the pupil is on track to meet all applicable graduation requirements.
          3. Following the open enrollment period each year, applications shall be counted to
             determine if the Charter School has received more applications than availability. If this
             happens, the Charter School will hold a lottery to determine enrollment.
          4. The lottery will be public, transparent, and fair and will take place at the Charter School,
             during after school hours in the month of March. The lottery will be conducted in a
             space large enough to accommodate all interested parties. Families do not need to
             attend to participate or secure a spot.
          5. The executive director or other uninterested designee will conduct the lottery, which is
             open to the public. The recording of lottery results will be conducted by a different,
             unrelated uninterested party. Detailed procedures for the public random drawing are
             provided below. All names will be drawn randomly to determine the students who are
             admitted and those who will be placed on the waitlist.
          6. At the conclusion of the lottery, students who were admitted will be notified by the
             Charter School by phone, email, and/or mail within 1 week and asked to complete a
             registration form and register within two weeks for the upcoming school year, online,
             in person, by email, fax and any other means that would work for the family and school.
             All students who were not granted admission due to capacity shall also be contacted
             and told their number on the waitlist. As openings become available, the Charter School
             will offer enrollment to students in the order as listed on the waitlist through the online
             enrollment system. Students applying for enrollment after the lottery will be added to
             the waitlist in the chronological order in which they applied. In no circumstance will a
             waitlist carry over to the following school year.
          7. Every effort will be made to contact the parents and ensure registration forms are
             submitted in a timely fashion. The Charter School will also help any parents who need
             assistance in completing the lottery and/or registration forms, and the Charter School
             will keep written records of the lottery forms, procedures, accepted lists, and wait lists
             for a period of 1 year.

III. Other Compliance Requirements

In compliance with Education Code Section 47607(b), which provides that “material revisions of
charters are governed by the standards and criteria described in Section 47605, and shall include,
but not be limited to, a reasonably comprehensive description of any new requirement of charter
schools enacted into law after the charter was originally granted or last renewed,” CCCA has
updated its charter, as indicated in the attached, to include such new requirements in Element 1
(program description) on page 45, Element 5 (governance) on page 115, Element 6 (employee
qualifications) on page 122, and Element 7 (Health & Safety) on pages 134-141. CCCA affirms
that it will comply with any and all state, federal, and local laws applicable to CCCA that came
into effect after the time that CCCA’s charter was initially granted.
