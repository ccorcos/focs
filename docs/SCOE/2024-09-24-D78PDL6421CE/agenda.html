Public Comment

 

Anyone may appear at the Board meeting to provide public comment regarding any item on the agenda or regarding any matter that is within the Board’s subject matter jurisdiction. However, the Board may not take action on any item not on the agenda except as authorized by Government Code section 54954.2.  If possible, notify the Board President or Board Secretary in writing prior to the meeting if you wish to testify.

 

Individuals wishing to speak in person before the Board are requested to complete a public comment form. Speakers will be called in the order in which the cards are received. 

 

Public comment via Zoom teleconference may be made by using the raised hand feature in Zoom during the agenda item to be addressed. The speaker will be identified by their Display Name in Zoom when called upon to speak. Those joining the meeting via Zoom are requested to display their full name so there is no confusion when called upon to speak. Public comments will be received until the public comment period has elapsed.

 

Comments are limited to a maximum of two (2) minutes per speaker per agenda item, and individuals are limited to a single comment per agenda item.

 

Please contact boardcomments@scoe.net before the meeting if you have questions about the public comment process. 

 

Accommodating Individuals with Disabilities

 

The Sacramento County Office of Education encourages those with disabilities to participate fully in the public meeting process. If you need a disability-related modification or accommodation, including auxiliary aids or services, to participate in the public meeting, contact the Superintendent’s Office at (916) 228-2410 at least 48 hours before the scheduled Board meeting so that we may make every reasonable effort to accommodate you. [Government Code § 54953.2; Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990, § 202 (42 U.S.C. § 12132).]

BACKGROUND:

 

On November 8, 2022, California voters approved Proposition 28: The Arts and Music in Schools (AMS) Funding Guarantee and Accountability Act. The measure required the state to establish a new, ongoing program supporting arts instruction in schools beginning in 2023–2024. A minimum of 80 percent of AMS funds are to be expended to employ certificated or classified employees to provide arts education program instruction. The remaining funds must be used for training, supplies and materials, and arts educational partnership programs.

 

Local Education Agencies must annually certify that all funds will be used to provide arts education programs and approve an annual spending plan.

 

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:

 

The Superintendent recommends that the Board approve the proposed annual spending plan.

Funding Source: California Commission on Teacher Credentialing

 

Amount Requested: $576,000

 

Fiscal Year(s): 2024-2025, 2025-2026

 

Program Description: 

The Classified Employee Credential Grant is funded through the California Commission on Teacher Credentialing (CTC) to support local educational agencies in the region by offering grants to current classified employees to complete their bachelor’s degree and their teaching credential.

 

The overall goal of the Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) Classified School Employee Teacher Credentialing Program is to create bachelor’s degree-credential pathways. The program offers financial support with tuition for participants. SCOE will serve as the lead Local Education Agency (LEA) for this grant and will partner with UMass Global University, Pacific Oaks College, and Yolo-Solano Center for Teacher Credentialing. SCOE funds for the 2024-2026 grant cycle will include tuition support for participants to offset financial obligations for classified employees.

 

New Positions: None

 

Subcontracts:

  • UMass Global University will offer a Bachelor of Arts in Liberal Studies pathway to participants.
  • Pacific Oaks College will offer a teaching credential for Multiple Subject, Mild/Mod Ed Specialist, or Extensive Needs Ed Specialist pathway to participants.

  • Yolo-Solano Center for Teacher Credentialing (CTC) will provide support in helping future Sacramento County Interns become Intern eligible so they can join the SCOE Intern program.

 

Evaluation Component:

SCOE will provide annual reports to the CTC that include participant information, participant progress, credentials provided, and funding allocation per participant.

BACKGROUND:  

 

The Sacramento County Office of Education’s (SCOE) Personnel Commission administers the Merit System for SCOE classified employees. The Commission is comprised of three members: one recommended by SCOE’s classified collective bargaining unit, one recommended by the Sacramento County Superintendent of Schools (Superintendent), and another jointly recommended by the other two appointees.   

 

Jan Mayer Ashley, Ed.D. was originally appointed in July 2024 to fill the unexpired term of Ms. Sherry Hansen. The current term of Jan Mayer Ashley, Ed.D., the Superintendent’s recommended appointee, will expire on November 30, 2024.

 

A public hearing and an action item on the appointment will be placed on the October 22, 2024, Board Agenda. If re-appointed, Dr. Mayer Ashley will serve on the Personnel Commission from December 1, 2024, to November 30, 2027.
