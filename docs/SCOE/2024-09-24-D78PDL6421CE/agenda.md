[[[The Classified Employee Credential Grant is funded through the

educational agencies in the region by offering grants to current
classified employees to complete their bachelor's degree and their
teaching credential.
]{style="line-height:105%"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[[The overall goal of the Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE)
Classified School Employee Teacher Credentialing Program is to create
bachelor's degree-credential pathways. The program offers financial
support with tuition for participants. SCOE will serve as the lead Local
Education Agency (LEA) for this grant and will partner with UMass Global
University, Pacific Oaks College, and Yolo-Solano Center for Teacher
Credentialing. SCOE funds for the 2024-2026 grant cycle will include
tuition support for participants to offset financial obligations for
classified
employees.]{style="line-height:103%"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

  Studies pathway to
  participants.]{style="line-height:103%"}]{style="line-height:103%"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

- [[[[Pacific Oaks College will offer a teaching credential for Multiple
  Subject, Mild/Mod Ed Specialist, or Extensive Needs Ed Specialist
  pathway to
  participants.]{style="line-height:103%"}]{style="line-height:103%"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}
