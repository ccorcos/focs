BACKGROUND:

 

Heather Scott is the Sacramento County Office of Education 2022 Classified Employee of the Year. Ms. Scott has served as a SCOE employee since 2014. She is an Infant Educator in SCOE’s Special Education Department.  Ms. Scott was inspired to pursue a career in Special Education after her daughter, who was born with Down syndrome, received SCOE special education services. She is a co-founder and former board member for the Down Syndrome Information Alliance (DSIA) which provides support and resources to empower individuals with Down syndrome, and their families. She previously worked with Warmline Family Resource Center as a Family Resource & Support Specialist, and with the Sacramento County Sheriff’s Department as a Deputy Sheriff.  She is credited with creating a more efficient system to track SCOE student progress during COVID while staff has been unable to meet with students and families in-person.

 

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:

 

The Superintendent recommends that the Sacramento County Board of Education approve commendation of Heather Scott as the Sacramento County Office of Education Classified Employee of the Year 2022 and that the Board present her with a plaque commemorating this honor.

BACKGROUND:

 

The Every Student Succeeds Act (ESSA) requires schools receiving Title I funds and operating a school wide program, or schools identified for Comprehensive Support and Improvement (CSI), Targeted Support and Improvement (TSI) or Additional Targeted Support and Improvement (ATSI), to develop a school plan.  Assembly Bill 716 revised California Education Code sections 64001-65001 to streamline and align state and federal planning processes and organized federal school planning requirements in ESSA.

 

The development of the School Plan for Student Achievement (SPSA) is required to include the following:

  • A comprehensive needs assessment (pursuant to ESSA)
    • Analysis of verifiable state data, consistent with state priorities, including state-determined long term goals
    • The plan may include local data
  • An identification of the process for evaluating and monitoring the implementation of the School Plan and progress towards accomplishing the goals

 

The Juvenile Court and Community School programs have prepared their school plans in accordance with School-wide Program stipulations.

 

In accordance with California Education Code Section 52068(a)(4), a county office of education superintendent must review the Local Control and Accountability Plan (LCAP) and School Plans to ensure that the two plans are consistent. An integrated approach to planning ensures a consistent alignment between the LCAP and School Plans. As a result, the goals and relevant metrics outlined in the 2021-22 Juvenile Court and Community School SPSAs are aligned to the 2021-22 Sacramento County Office of Education LCAP.

 

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:

 

The Superintendent recommends that the Board approve each School Plan for Student Achievement in accordance with the requirements under Education Code and federal law.

A. First Reading of New Board Policy 3311.1 - Uniform Public Construction Cost Accounting Procedures - Teresa Stinson
Meeting
May 3, 2022 - Sacramento County Board of Education Meeting Agenda - 6:30 p.m.
Category
10. New Business - Other
Type
Information

BACKGROUND:

 

Attached is the new proposed Board Policy 3311.1 – Uniform Public Construction Cost Accounting Procedures. 

 

SCOE's Business Services Office is recommending that SCOE opt into the California Uniform Construction Cost Account Act (CUPCCAA) to help SCOE timely and efficiently complete small construction projects. CUPCCAA will enable SCOE to use its own work force for small projects and will provide more opportunities to contract with local businesses. Opting into CUPCCAA requires the Board to adopt the new policy and elect by resolution to become subject to the Act. The proposed resolution will be presented when this policy is before the Board on second reading.   

 

What is CUPCCAA? 

CUPCCAA was enacted in 1983 to help promote uniformity in public entity construction projects in California. Almost 1,500 public entities in California are using CUPCCAA, including 691 school districts and 67 community college districts. Public entities that elect to become subject to CUPCCAA must adopt a resolution and policy, file a copy of the resolution with the State Controller’s Office, and comply with the Uniform Public Construction Cost Accounting Procedures. CUPCCAA only governs bidding and cost accounting procedures for construction projects. Other applicable public contracting requirements will still apply, such as paying prevailing wages.

 

Key Provisions of CUPCCAA 

CUPCCAA would allow SCOE to timely execute small construction projects, expedite project delivery, and would provide greater access to local businesses. SCOE could use its own workforce to complete construction projects costing $60,000 or less, or negotiate a contract with a local business or other qualified contractor. SCOE could utilize an informal bid process for projects costing $200,000 or less, and formal bidding would be required for projects costing more than $200,000. Currently, public projects costing $15,000 and more require formal bidding.

 

CUPCCAA would enable SCOE to timely complete small construction projects and would allow for greater flexibility in the scheduling of projects. While the formal bid process takes 3.5-4 months, the contracting process under CUPCCAA would be much shorter for projects less than $200,000 (i.e., within two weeks for projects less than $60,000, and within three or four weeks for projects less than $200,000 using the informal bid process).

 

For the informal bid process, CUPCCAA requires SCOE to establish or update a list of qualified contractors at least once per calendar year by sending out a notice to designated trade journals inviting licensed contractors to submit their names. SCOE would also post the notice with local trade journals and with various groups to attract a diverse pool of contractors. Qualified contractors must be placed on the list and notified by SCOE of opportunities to bid on construction projects. The lowest responsive bidder would be awarded the contract.

 

Creating a list of qualified contractors will provide SCOE with greater access to local businesses. SCOE must currently utilize CMAS and other cooperative agreements for small, time-sensitive construction projects. These lists enable SCOE to timely complete projects while complying with public contracting requirements, but they do not have a large selection of local contractors.

 

In the last six months, SCOE has completed twelve construction projects that were less than $200,000. They encompass both public projects and maintenance, and they include several flooring, roofing, and demolition projects. SCOE has five pending construction projects, including several that require structural and safety-related repairs (e.g., roofing, siding, painting, and fire safety devices). CUPCCAA's process would enable SCOE to complete those projects located at SCOE school facilities during summer break while students are not on campus.

 

The Policy Committee reviewed Board Policy 3311.1 – Uniform Public Construction Cost Accounting Procedures on March 15, 2022 and recommended that the new policy be presented to the Board for First Reading.  

 

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:

 

The Superintendent concurs with the recommendation of the Policy Committee that Board Policy 3311.1 - Uniform Public Construction Cost Accounting Procedures be submitted to the Board of Education for First Reading.

BACKGROUND:

 

Attached is the new proposed Board Bylaw 9150 – Student County Board Members.

 

The new proposed Bylaw follows the statutory requirements and conditions under which students may serve on county boards of education. The Bylaw allows the Board to include one or more student Board members, but does not require it. The Board will be considering different possibilities for engaging student voice, input, and involvement, including whether or not to have student Board members and/or how student(s) would be selected and involved.   

 

The Policy Committee reviewed the Bylaw on March 15, 2022 and recommended that the new Bylaw be presented to the Board for First Reading.
