The Sacramento County Board of Education (Board) provides leadership for
educational programs and services operated by the Sacramento County Office of
Education (SCOE). The primary objectives of the Board are to ensure effective
governance by collaborateing with the Sacramento County Superintendent of
Schools (Superintendent) to carry out the Board’s essential responsibilities:
establishing direction and priorities for SCOE, and to creating a supportive
environment, ensuring accountability, utilizing governance structures, and
provideing leadership necessary for the success of public education.

In fulfilling its objectives, it is the role of the Board to:

1.       Hire, annually evaluate performance, and set terms and conditions of
         employment of the Superintendent.

2.       Adopt the mission, vision and goals of the Board and adopt and update
         policies for the Board’s own governance and for programs under the
         authority of the Board.

3.       Collaborate with the Superintendent to ensure implementation of the
         shared mission, vision, goals, and policies of the Board, Superintendent,
         and SCOE.

4.       Adopt and update policies for the Board’s own governance and for
         programs under the authority of the Board.

5.       Adopt policies and a course of study for the juvenile court school and
         SCOE community schools.

6.       Evaluate schools and programs under the authority of the Board.

7.       Approve the acquisition, purchase, or lease of real property for SCOE
         programs.

8.       Fulfill responsibilities related to the local control funding formula (LCFF),
         including adopting SCOE’s local control and accountability plan (LCAP),
         and the LCFF budget overview for parents and guardians, and other
         funding requiring Board approval.

9.       Adopt Approve the annual budget of the Superintendent, review and
         approve interim reports, and review the annual audit.

10.      Serve as the appeal board for expulsion appeals; interdistrict transfer
         requests; and other matters when required by law.

The Sacramento County Board of Education is the Governing Board of the Sacramento
County Office of Education. Its legal authority is determined by the State Constitution,
the Statutes of the State Legislature, and regulations of the State Board of Education,
and that authority conferred by trustee area elections.

The County Board of Education shall be the general policy making body for the
Sacramento County Office of Education, and with the assistance of the County
Superintendent and his/her staff, the Board shall establish general policies governing
the operation of the County Office of Education. Adoption of these written policies shall
be the principal means by which the Board shall exercise its leadership. The study and
evaluation of reports concerning the execution of its written policies shall constitute the
basic method by which the Board shall determine the effectiveness of the operation of
the County Office of Education.

The County Board of Education is fiscally independent pursuant to Education Code
Section 1080, and duties and responsibilities have been transferred from the Board of
Supervisors to the County Board of Education as enumerated in Board of Supervisors
Resolution No. 67-477 dated 6/26/67.
