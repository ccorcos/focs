The Sacramento County Board of Education (Board) values and encourages parents, guardians,
caregivers and other members of the community, as well as Sacramento County Office of Education
(SCOE) employees to share their time, knowledge and abilities with our pupils.

The Superintendent or designee may authorize the use of volunteers and shall adopt administrative rules
and regulations (ARR) regarding the screening and appropriate use of volunteers. The ARR will address
measures to protect health and safety in accordance with laws related to tuberculosis testing,
fingerprinting, criminal records check, immunizations, and other screening required by law or SCOE
programs. Volunteers shall only be assigned to a SCOE-operated program after meeting the
requirements specified in the ARR, including obtaining official clearance through the fingerprinting
process, a negative tuberculosis test result, and clearance from a criminal records check.

The services performed by volunteers to SCOE programs shall not be used to replace those duties
performed by certificated and/or classified staff in the performance of their duties.

SCOE prohibits the unlawful discrimination or harassment of volunteers on the basis of the person's
actual or perceived race, religious creed, color, national origin, immigration status, ancestry, age,
marital status, pregnancy, physical or mental disability, medical condition, genetic information, military
and veteran status, gender, gender identity, gender expression, sex, sexual orientation, or any other
legally protected class.

The Board encourages the Superintendent or designee to develop a means for recognizing the
contributions of volunteers.
