Public Comment

 

Anyone may appear at the Board meeting to provide public comment regarding any item on the agenda or regarding any matter that is within the Board’s subject matter jurisdiction. However, the Board may not take action on any item not on the agenda except as authorized by Government Code section 54954.2.  If possible, notify the Board President or Board Secretary in writing prior to the meeting if you wish to testify.

 

Individuals wishing to speak in person before the Board are requested to complete a public comment form. Speakers will be called in the order in which the cards are received. 

 

Public comment via Zoom teleconference may be made by using the raised hand feature in Zoom during the agenda item to be addressed. The speaker will be identified by their Display Name in Zoom when called upon to speak. Those joining the meeting via Zoom are requested to display their full name so there is no confusion when called upon to speak. Public comments will be received until the public comment period has elapsed.

 

Comments are limited to a maximum of two (2) minutes per speaker per agenda item, and individuals are limited to a single comment per agenda item.

 

Please contact boardcomments@scoe.net before the meeting if you have questions about the public comment process. 

 

Accommodating Individuals with Disabilities

 

The Sacramento County Office of Education encourages those with disabilities to participate fully in the public meeting process. If you need a disability-related modification or accommodation, including auxiliary aids or services, to participate in the public meeting, contact the Superintendent’s Office at (916) 228-2410 at least 48 hours before the scheduled Board meeting so that we may make every reasonable effort to accommodate you. [Government Code § 54953.2; Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990, § 202 (42 U.S.C. § 12132).]

BACKGROUND: 

 

CLASSIFIED 

 

Tina Engwis, School Secretary, Court and Community Schools, was nominated by Principal Barbara Modlin for her contributions to the Sacramento County Office of Education. Tina does a wonderful job as the El Centro school secretary. Her impact has been profound since joining SCOE in 2022. Diligently fulfilling her job duties, Tina goes above and beyond, elevating each day. Her dedication, compassion, and unwavering support for colleagues and students make her invaluable. Tina's exceptional organizational skills and warm demeanor create an inviting atmosphere for all. She exemplifies the core values of teamwork, reliability, and dedication, making her the ideal recipient of the Classified Employee of the Month award. Her contributions and commitment inspire others, fostering a positive work environment and enhancing our collective success. Tina Engwis has been a SCOE employee since August 2022. 

 

CERTIFICATED

 

Allen Walsh, Teacher, Foundations Academy, was nominated by Area Principal Guy Holman. Allen is the teacher of the Young Adult Transition Program at Galt High School. Allen has developed an exceptional program, which focuses on functional academics, daily living skills, community-based instruction, and vocational training through the Workability program - all with the purpose of helping his students become the best versions of themselves as full participants in their community. Allen has a passion for teaching and is devoted to providing quality activities that reflect his students' interests and abilities. He constantly strives for active student engagement and adjusts his instruction and strategies to meet the specific needs of his students to promote improved outcomes. He is proficient at building positive relationships with families, related service providers, campus faculty, as well as his own instructional team. His coworkers respect him and have shared their gratitude for Allen's consistent leadership in the classroom. Allen's supervisor has expressed being incredibly impressed with Allen's "can do" attitude, his professionalism, flexibility, and patience, but most importantly, his commitment to his students. Allen strives to give his students a voice in their education, respecting the fact that they are adults and want to be in charge of their lives. He is constantly challenging them towards self-advocacy and greater levels on independence, while providing them with genuine care and support for success. It is with great honor and respect that Allen Walsh is recommended as the September Certificated Employee of the Month. Allen Walsh has been a SCOE employee since July 2008.

Department/Director: Educational Services/Brent Malicote

 

Funding Source: California Department of Education

 

Amount Requested: $3,990,114

 

Fiscal Year(s): 2023-2024, 2024-2025

 

Program Description:

The Quality Rating Improvement System (QRIS) Block Grant is designed to raise the quality of state preschool and migrant education programs. The QRIS Block Grant will be used to award funds to California State Preschool Programs (CSPP) and migrant education programs that have achieved a Tier 4 or Tier 5 rating as described in California's Quality Continuum Framework. It is CDE's intent that these funds will help programs maintain their high quality. Funds will also be used to raise the quality of CSPPs and migrant education programs that have not yet achieved Tier 4 or Tier 5 status. Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) staff have designed a plan to improve program quality, strengthen partnership, monitor progress, and assess program quality. 

 

New Positions: None 

 

Subcontracts:

Evaluation Component: 

SCOE will prepare an annual report that summarizes the ratings of participating early learning programs and progression toward higher tier ratings. Tier ratings are based on the Quality Continuum Framework Rating Matrix in the areas of Child Development and School Readiness, Teachers and Teaching, and Program and Evaluation.

Sacramento County Office of Education

BACKGROUND:

 

The Sacramento County Board of Education will hold a public hearing during the August 15, 2023 Regular Board Meeting to determine whether each pupil in the juvenile court schools has sufficient textbooks or instructional materials, or both, in English/language arts, including the English language development component of an adopted program, mathematics, history/social science, and science that are aligned to the state content standards adopted by the State Board of Education (SBE). As part of this determination, the Board also must determine if each pupil who is actually enrolled in a foreign language or health course has sufficient instructional materials that are consistent with the content and cycles of curriculum frameworks adopted by the SBE. In addition, the County Board must determine if pupils enrolled in a laboratory science course have adequate equipment. Detail substantiating that sufficient instructional materials are available to each student will be available for inspection by the County Board and public at the hearing.

 

In accordance with the sufficiency requirements of Education Code Section 60119(c), every pupil in juvenile court schools will have sufficient textbooks or instructional materials, or both, in English/language arts, including the English language development component of an adopted program, mathematics, science and history/social science that are aligned to the state content standards adopted by the SBE. Pupils enrolled in a health course will have sufficient materials. The Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) does not offer foreign language instruction in any grades, nor does SCOE offer science laboratory courses in any of grades 9 through 12. Therefore, SCOE need not maintain science laboratory equipment. Completion of a science laboratory course is not a requirement for high school graduation, though completion of a course in either foreign language or visual or performing arts is a requirement (EC 51225.3). As necessary for completion of this graduation requirement, SCOE students are provided a course in fine arts.

 

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:

 

The Superintendent recommends that the Board adopt Resolution No. 23-10 - Determination of Textbooks and/or Instructional Materials Sufficiency Pursuant to Education Code Section 60119 for Juvenile Court Schools.
