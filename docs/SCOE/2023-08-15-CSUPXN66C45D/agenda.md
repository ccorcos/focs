[[**Allen Walsh**, Teacher, Foundations Academy, was nominated by Area
Principal Guy Holman. Allen is the teacher of the Young Adult Transition
Program at Galt High School. Allen has developed an exceptional program,
which focuses on functional academics, daily living skills,
community-based instruction, and vocational training through the
Workability program - all with the purpose of helping his students
become the best versions of themselves as full participants in their
community. Allen has a passion for teaching and is devoted to providing
quality activities that reflect his students\' interests and abilities.
He constantly strives for active student engagement and adjusts his
instruction and strategies to meet the specific needs of his students to
promote improved outcomes. He is proficient at building positive
relationships with families, related service providers, campus faculty,
as well as his own instructional team. His coworkers respect him and
have shared their gratitude for Allen\'s consistent leadership in the
classroom. Allen\'s supervisor has expressed being incredibly impressed
with Allen\'s \"can do\" attitude, his professionalism, flexibility, and
patience, but most importantly, his commitment to his students. Allen
strives to give his students a voice in their education, respecting the
fact that they are adults and want to be in charge of their lives. He is
constantly challenging them towards self-advocacy and greater levels on
independence, while providing them with genuine care and support for
success. It is with great honor and respect that Allen Walsh is
recommended as the September Certificated Employee of the Month. Allen
Walsh has been a SCOE employee since July
2008.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

**[[BACKGROUND:]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}**

 

[[The Sacramento County Board of Education will hold a public hearing
]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}[[during
the August 15, 2023 Regular Board Meeting to determine whether each
pupil in the juvenile court schools has sufficient textbooks or
instructional materials, or both, in English/language arts, including
the English language development component of an adopted program,
mathematics, history/social science, and science that are aligned to the
state content standards adopted by the State Board of Education (SBE).
As part of this determination, the Board also must determine if each
pupil who is actually enrolled in a foreign language or health course
has sufficient instructional materials that are consistent with the
content and cycles of curriculum frameworks adopted by the SBE. In
addition, the County Board must determine if pupils enrolled in a
laboratory science course have adequate equipment. Detail substantiating
that sufficient instructional materials are available to each student
will be available for inspection by the County Board and public at the
hearing.]{style="font-size:14px;"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}

 

[[In accordance with the sufficiency requirements of Education Code
Section 60119(c), every pupil in juvenile court schools will have
sufficient textbooks or instructional materials, or both, in
English/language arts, including the English language development
component of an adopted program, mathematics, science and history/social
science that are aligned to the state content standards adopted by the
SBE. Pupils enrolled in a health course will have sufficient materials.
The Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) does not offer foreign
language instruction in any grades, nor does SCOE offer science
laboratory courses in any of grades 9 through 12. Therefore, SCOE need
not maintain science laboratory equipment. Completion of a science
laboratory course is not a requirement for high school graduation,
though completion of a course in either foreign language or visual or
performing arts is a requirement (EC 51225.3). As necessary for
completion of this graduation requirement, SCOE students are provided a
course in fine arts.
]{style="font-size:14px;"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}
