Public Comment

 

Anyone may appear at the Board meeting to provide public comment regarding any item on the agenda or regarding any matter that is within the Board’s subject matter jurisdiction. However, the Board may not take action on any item not on the agenda except as authorized by Government Code section 54954.2. If possible, notify the Board President or Board Secretary in writing prior to the meeting if you wish to testify.

 

Individuals wishing to speak in person before the Board are requested to complete a public comment form. Speakers will be called in the order in which the cards are received. 

 

Public comment via Zoom teleconference may be made by using the raised hand feature in Zoom during the agenda item to be addressed. The speaker will be identified by their Display Name in Zoom when called upon to speak. Those joining the meeting via Zoom are requested to display their full name so there is no confusion when called upon to speak. Public comments will be received until the public comment period has elapsed.

 

Comments are limited to a maximum of two (2) minutes per speaker per agenda item, and individuals are limited to a single comment per agenda item.

 

Please contact boardcomments@scoe.net before the meeting if you have questions about the public comment process. 

 

Accommodating Individuals with Disabilities

 

The Sacramento County Office of Education encourages those with disabilities to participate fully in the public meeting process. If you need a disability-related modification or accommodation, including auxiliary aids or services, to participate in the public meeting, contact the Superintendent’s Office at (916) 228-2410 at least 48 hours before the scheduled Board meeting so that we may make every reasonable effort to accommodate you. [Government Code § 54953.2; Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990, § 202 (42 U.S.C. § 12132).]

Department/Director: Educational Services/Channa Pitt

 

Funding Source: California Department of Education

 

Amount Requested: $150,590

 

Fiscal Year(s): 2023-2024, 2024-2025

 

Program Description: The California Department of Education (CDE) has developed a comprehensive plan to provide technical assistance for continuous improvement to Local Education Agencies (LEAs). The Title III County Office of Education Regional English Learner (EL) Specialists Agreement grant is funded through the Elementary and Secondary Education Act (ESEA). As part of the California Statewide System of Support, the CDE awarded grants to one county within each of the state's eleven California County Superintendents service regions. The Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) was selected in Region 3 as the Regional English Learner EL/Title III Specialist.

 

As the Region 3 EL Specialist, SCOE will provide technical assistance for continuous improvement to equip LEAs and COEs with resources and tools to support the development, implementation, and evaluation of strategies to ensure that English learners have the resources they need. The scope of work includes guidance and support to LEAs and COEs in implementing and improving programs and developing Local Control and Accountability Plan (LCAP) Addenda. In addition, the SCOE's Director for English Learner Programs and Accountability will organize and provide coaching for systemic improvement in data analysis and data-based decision-making, high-quality instruction, connect resources for specialized programs, and facilitate quarterly Multilingual Education Leadership Network meetings to support LEAs.

 

New Positions: None

 

Subcontracts: None 

 

Evaluation Component: The Title III County Office of Education Regional English Learner Specialist will report on services provided. Data for these annual reports include qualitative data that includes survey results based on the support provided to LEAs. Quantitative data is captured on the number of consultations provided to LEAs annually.

BACKGROUND:

 

Senator Monique Limón recently introduced Senate Bill (SB 1094) which promotes greater civic learning and engagement opportunities, especially in grades 1-8. The Sacramento County Board of Education (Board) may review and consider expressing its support for this education-related legislation. If the Board decides to support SB 1094, Sacramento County Office of Education staff will prepare a letter of support on behalf of the Board.

 

Senate Bill 1094 (Limón) – Civic Learning in Grades 1-8

 

Under California law, social sciences is a required course of study for grades 1-6 and grades 7-12. Current law requires instruction in social sciences to provide a foundation for understanding, among other things, the history, resources, development, and government of California and the United States of America.

 

If enacted, SB 1094 would require the study of social sciences to also include principles of democracy and the State and Federal Constitutions. It would also require students to actively engage with a local, state, or federal government institution at least once in grades 1-6 and at least once in grades 7-8.  

 

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:

 

The Superintendent recommends that the Board support SB 1094 and direct staff to send a letter of support on behalf of the Board.
