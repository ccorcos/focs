[[**BACKGROUND:**]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[Proposition 30, The Schools and Local Public Safety Protection Act of
2012, approved by voters on November 6, 2012, temporarily increased the
state sales tax rate for all taxpayers and the personal income tax rates
for upper-income taxpayers. Proposition 55, The California Children's
Education and Health Care Protection Act of 2016, approved by voters on
November 8, 2016, extended the increase of the personal income tax rate
for upper-income taxpayers, but allowed the temporary state sales tax
rate increase to expire. A portion of the revenues generated are used to
support school funding and deposited into a state account called the
Education Protection Account (EPA). These funds replace previous
allocations of apportionments which are no longer received by Local
Education Agencies
(LEAs).]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[The language of Proposition 30 states that each LEA "shall have the
sole authority to determine how the monies received from the EPA are
spent in the school or schools within its jurisdiction, provided,
however, that the appropriate governing board or body shall make these
spending determinations in open session of a public meeting of the
governing board or body and shall not use any of the funds from the EPA
for salaries or benefits of administrators or any other administrative
costs."]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[Per California Department of Education estimates, the Sacramento
County Office of Education (SCOE) will receive \$2,188,575 for 2023-2024
and is proposing that the monies be used to augment our Juvenile Court
Schools, Community Schools, and Special Education Programs, excluding
administrators' salaries and benefits or any other administrative costs.
These are not new or increased expenditures for SCOE. The EPA money is
being used to fulfill existing budget expenditure
commitments.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

Subject
:   E. Universal PreKindergarten Mixed Delivery Planning Grant
    Proposal - Brent Malicote
:::

    6:30 p.m. - Closed Session at 4:30 p.m.

<!-- -->

::: {.itembody tabindex="0"}
[[The Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) was allocated
\$609,842 on behalf of the Sacramento County Local Child Care and 
Development Planning Council (LPC) by the California Department of
Education (CDE) for the fiscal year 2022-2023 to convene a diverse 
workgroup of partners with broad representation of the early learning
and care community to advise on the development of a plan to expand
access universally to preschool programs for three- and four-year-old
children across the county through a mixed-delivery system.
Sacramento's  Universal Prekindergarten (UPK) mixed-delivery system
includes Head Start; community-based early learning and care programs;
Local Education Agency (LEA) PreK and Transitional Kindergarten; tribal
child care and preschool; family child care homes; Family, Friend and
Neighbor providers; and expanded learning
programs.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[The UPK Mixed Delivery Planning Grant supports relationship building
between the LPC, SCOE, LEAs, early learning and care programs, and Child
Action, the local Resource & Referral
agency.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[SCOE is required to develop a plan for, but not directly fund, the
provision of mixed-delivery UPK options for three-and four-year-old
children that provides outreach services and enrollment support for
families and increases inclusion of children with exceptional needs.
SCOE must also partner with LEAs to conduct UPK-related activities as
well as align and coordinate plans across the county. In addition, SCOE
must assist existing and aspiring UPK staff in accessing local workforce
pathway programs and partner with tribes to reflect family and tribal
community
needs.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[Funding is intended for assessing parental preferences and needs for
UPK; establishing or strengthening partnerships with early childhood
education service providers; engaging in community-level coordination
and planning; coordinating with special education partners to ensure
access to UPK for children with exceptional needs; and supporting
workforce development and quality improvements. Funds must be expended
by June 30, 2026. If funds are available in fiscal years 2023-2024 and
2024-2025, SCOE may apply for a renewal
grant.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

RECOMMENDATION:**]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[The Superintendent recommends the Board consider the plan for a UPK
mixed-delivery system in Sacramento
County.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}
