[[[**BACKGROUND:**]{style="line-height:normal"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[[Juvenile court and community schools operate year-round and have
highly specialized staffing needs whose costs are relatively fixed.
Court and community school attendance is based on referrals from
probation departments, school districts, and families. Students
frequently attend for a short period of time and ultimately return to a
comprehensive
school.]{style="line-height:normal"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[[Existing funding for court and community schools is both insufficient
and unpredictable. It is based on average daily attendance (ADA) which
is highly variable since students enter and exit the schools frequently
throughout the calendar year. The volatility and insufficiency of
resources creates perennial financial uncertainty for student programs
and other support staff. Furthermore, there has been a statewide decline
in referrals which does not change the baseline need for these important
programs to continue providing an educational safety
net.]{style="line-height:normal"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[[SCOE has also experienced a significant decrease in court and
community school enrollments resulting in reduced revenue for these
schools. SCOE court and community school ADA was almost 250 students in
2017-2018, but in 2021-2022 it had fallen to only 135. While growth in
the Senior Extension and CARE programs have offset the community school
revenue decreases, the court school continues to rely on \$887,000 in
short-term COVID-19 relief grant funding to support its
costs.]{style="line-height:normal"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[[SCOE is working with the California County Superintendents and other
county offices of education on a proposal to provide a standardized
funding level for court and community schools based on countywide
factors that will provide sufficient and predictable resources and
protect our schools from annual funding
disruptions.]{style="line-height:107%"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

RECOMMENDATION:**]{style="line-height:normal"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[[The Superintendent recommends that the Board adopt Resolution No.
