BACKGROUND:

 

After each decennial federal census, county board of education trustee areas must be assessed and potentially adjusted in order to meet legally required population criteria. (Ed. Code, § 1002(b).)  Demographer, Cooperative Strategies, LLC, has been engaged to assist the Sacramento County Board of Education (Board), in its capacity as Sacramento County Committee on School District Organization (County Committee), to analyze 2020 Census data and provide options for ensuring the Board's trustee areas are compliant with state and federal legal requirements. By law, the difference in population between the smallest and largest trustee area must be less than 10%. Based upon assessment of the Board's trustee area population balance using 2020 Census data, the variance between the smallest and largest trustee areas is 8.3%, which is within the 10% threshold. Therefore, the County Committee may choose to maintain the Board's current trustee area boundaries, or make adjustments to further balance population.

 

At the Board's July 13, 2021 meeting, the demographer reviewed the standards and process for adjusting trustee area boundaries. At the October 19, 2021 County Committee meeting, the demographer reviewed redistricting standards and the current trustee area boundaries, provided a potential scenario for further balancing the population among the trustee areas, and answered questions. In response to the County Committee's feedback, the demographer developed three potential trustee area map scenarios that would reduce the variance in the Board's trustee area size.  

 

Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) staff have publicized the trustee area redistricting process and solicited public comment on the Board's current trustee area boundaries and the three alternative trustee area map scenarios through postings on SCOE’s website, social media postings, a news release sent to 23 different television and print media outlets throughout the region and to all our local school districts, a public notice published in the Sacramento Bee, and communications with SCOE’s employee associations (California School Employees Association [CSEA] and Sacramento County Office of Education Teachers Association [SCOETA]).

 

At the November 16, 2021 County Committee meeting, SCOE staff and the demographer provided additional background and history regarding the development of the Board's existing trustee area boundaries after the 2010 census, reviewed the three alternative trustee area map scenarios that would further balance population among the Board's trustee areas, and sought County Committee input and direction. 

 

NEXT STEPS:

 

At the December 14, 2021 meeting, in response to comments and feedback received at the November 16 County Committee meeting, additional information and another potential scenario for the Board's trustee area boundaries will be presented for the County Committee's consideration. Before the meeting, SCOE staff will publicize and seek public comment regarding the potential trustee area map scenarios.  

 

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:

 

The Superintendent recommends that the Sacramento County Committee on School District Organization review and consider the potential scenarios for the Sacramento County Board of Education's trustee areas and take action as it deems appropriate.

 

This agenda item has been revised to include the following document: Resolution No. CC-21-03 - Adopting County Board Redistricting Plan, to be considered in the event the County Committee takes action on December 14, 2021 to adopt a redistricting plan.
