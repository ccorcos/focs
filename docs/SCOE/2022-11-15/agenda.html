Public Comment

 

Anyone may appear at the Board meeting to provide public comment regarding any item on the agenda or regarding any matter that is within the Board’s subject matter jurisdiction. However, the Board may not take action on any item not on the agenda except as authorized by Government Code section 54954.2.  If possible, notify the Board President or Board Secretary in writing prior to the meeting if you wish to testify.

 

Individuals wishing to speak in person before the Board are requested to complete a public comment form. Speakers will be called in the order in which the cards are received. 

 

Public comment via Zoom teleconference may be made by using the raised hand feature in Zoom during the agenda item to be addressed. The speaker will be identified by their Display Name in Zoom when called upon to speak. Those joining the meeting via Zoom are requested to display their full name so there is no confusion when called upon to speak. Public comments will be received until the public comment period has elapsed.

 

Comments are limited to a maximum of two (2) minutes per speaker per agenda item, and individuals are limited to a single comment per agenda item.

 

Please contact boardcomments@scoe.net before the meeting if you have questions about the public comment process. 

 

Accommodating Individuals with Disabilities

 

The Sacramento County Office of Education encourages those with disabilities to participate fully in the public meeting process. If you need a disability-related modification or accommodation, including auxiliary aids or services, to participate in the public meeting, contact the Superintendent’s Office at (916) 228-2410 at least 48 hours before the scheduled Board meeting so that we may make every reasonable effort to accommodate you. [Government Code § 54953.2; Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990, § 202 (42 U.S.C. § 12132).]

CLASSIFIED

 

Joanna Graves, Program Analyst, Early Learning and SELPA was nominated by SELPA Administrator Debbie Morris and Julie Montali, Executive Director for Early Learning. Joanna has quickly become an indispensable member of both the Early Learning and SELPA teams. She brings a positive spirit along with high quality work, professionalism, and ingenuity. Joanna has successfully navigated the very complicated world of SELPA finance and even developed a system to monitor the expenditures and reimbursement process for our Learning Recovery, Dispute Resolution, and Low Incidence funds which exceed 1.5 million dollars and must be carefully monitored. Joanna is relationship-centered, treating everyone with kindness and a warm smile. Joanna Graves has been a SCOE employee since August 2017.

 

CERTIFICATED

 

Danielle Scarson, Nurse, was nominated by Area Principal Jillian Friend for her work in the Foundations Academy programs. As a school nurse, Danielle provides support to students and staff. She collaborates with colleagues and teaching staff to ensure that the best nursing support is being provided in order to meet our students' unique needs. Danielle is a leader amongst SCOE’s nursing staff and is always willing to lend a hand when needed. She played a key role in supporting the Foundations Academy programs with unprecedented COVID practices during the pandemic to ensure student and staff safety. Danielle consistently demonstrates her organizational and technological skills in order to streamline procedures and processes. Danielle Scarson has been a SCOE employee since 2019.

BACKGROUND:

 

Kevin Jordan is the Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) 2023 Teacher of the Year. He is an instructor for the Urban Oasis Project, part of SCOE’s Sustainable Environments Learning Academy at Leo A. Palmiter Jr./Sr. High School and was nominated by Principal Lauren Roth. The program introduces students to the fields of landscape architecture, environmental design, and engineering. Mr. Jordan has been a SCOE employee since 2009.

 

He has a bachelor’s degree in geography from California State University, Sacramento, along with a Career Technical Education teaching credential. Unlike most teachers, dirt is part of his daily life—his classroom is filled with bags of potting soil and gardening tools. When selecting Mr. Jordan as the SCOE 2023 Teacher of the Year, the selection committee noted that he involves his students in community engagement so that they can see the fruits of their labor and become aware there are great careers that relate to science and plants; his classroom is based on building relationships, embracing student cultures, and using the background of his students as a resource to ask questions, make connections and learn from one another; and he is connected to the community in a variety of venues - providing plants and produce as well as assisting with plant production for organizations.

 

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:

 

It is recommended that the Sacramento County Board of Education recognize Kevin Jordan, the Sacramento County Office of Education 2023 Teacher of the Year and that the Board present him with a plaque commemorating this honor.

Program Description:

Title II, Part A of the Federal Elementary and Secondary Education Act requires that private elementary and secondary schools have access to equitable services as provided to teachers, principals, and other educational personnel in public schools. The California Private Schools Professional Development (CAPSPD) program is one part of the statewide services that California provides for private schools within the state. 

 

The SNPSPD contract will assist in providing consistent, high-quality professional development for California non-profit, private school teachers and administrators, as identified through a collaboration with CDE and any necessary groups. SCOE staff will ensure equitable access statewide for professional development, integration of technology where appropriate, and implementation of a professional development plan. 

 

New Positions: None

 

Subcontracts: 

  • Education Speakers Network (ESN) - $1,829,995, to provide workshops on project-based learning, classroom management, student motivation, differentiated instruction, standards-based grading, one virtual multi-topic conference and one in-person multi-topic conference, and other trainings provided by their staff with expertise in relevant content areas. 

 

  • Professional Development - $52,000, provided by subcontractors with expertise in relevant content areas. 

 

Evaluation Component:

SCOE staff will report on survey feedback from participants in the professional development workshops. SCOE will provide CDE with quarterly and end-of-year reports, data files, and reports from professional development, website development and maintenance, and ongoing administration of grant deliverables. This project will be measured and evaluated by successful completion of deliverables as described in the contract.

BACKGROUND:

 

The County Superintendent of Schools is required to submit a report each November to the County Board of Education describing the state of the schools in the county that have been designated as Williams Schools.  (Ed. Code, § 1240.)

 

The enclosed report summarizes the results of Williams reviews conducted during the 2021-2022 school year. For additional details regarding the 2021-2022 Williams reviews, please refer to the Annual Williams reports provided by the County Superintendent in September 2022. Electronic copies are available upon request.

 

The Superintendent of Public Instruction recently compiled a new list of Williams schools using revised criteria. (Ed. Code, § 1240.) The Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) has been visiting schools on the revised list and reviewing the sufficiency of instructional materials and the condition of school facilities in 2022-2023. The results of these school visits will be included in the First Quarterly 2022-2023 Williams Review Reports, which will be completed in December 2022. These results also will be shared with the Board as part of the 2022-2023 Annual Report.

 

This item is provided for information and comment, and no action is required by the Board.
