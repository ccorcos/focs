Public Comment

 

Anyone may appear at the Board meeting to provide public comment regarding any item on the agenda or regarding any matter that is within the Board’s subject matter jurisdiction. However, the Board may not take action on any item not on the agenda except as authorized by Government Code section 54954.2.  If possible, notify the Board President or Board Secretary in writing prior to the meeting if you wish to testify.

 

Individuals wishing to speak in person before the Board are requested to complete a public comment form. Speakers will be called in the order in which the cards are received. 

 

Public comment via Zoom teleconference may be made by using the raised hand feature in Zoom during the agenda item to be addressed. The speaker will be identified by their Display Name in Zoom when called upon to speak. Those joining the meeting via Zoom are requested to display their full name so there is no confusion when called upon to speak. Public comments will be received until the public comment period has elapsed.

 

Comments are limited to a maximum of two (2) minutes per speaker per agenda item, and individuals are limited to a single comment per agenda item.

 

Please contact boardcomments@scoe.net before the meeting if you have questions about the public comment process. 

 

Accommodating Individuals with Disabilities

 

The Sacramento County Office of Education encourages those with disabilities to participate fully in the public meeting process. If you need a disability-related modification or accommodation, including auxiliary aids or services, to participate in the public meeting, contact the Superintendent’s Office at (916) 228-2410 at least 48 hours before the scheduled Board meeting so that we may make every reasonable effort to accommodate you. [Government Code § 54953.2; Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990, § 202 (42 U.S.C. § 12132).]

BACKGROUND:

 

A public hearing will be held by the Board during the October 17, 2023 Board meeting to receive public comment and input from the community in establishing the appropriations limit for each fiscal year.

 

Proposition 4 of 1979 and Proposition 111 of 1990, limit the growth in appropriations made by the State of California, school agencies, and local governments. Under these amendments, the County Board of Education is required to adopt a resolution establishing the appropriations limit for each fiscal year and to provide public notice of the action. BACKGROUND:

 

The Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) has grown extensively since it moved to the Mather location twenty years ago. New legislation, grants, and contracts have resulted in SCOE taking on additional responsibilities locally and statewide, creating many new programs. Consequently, SCOE’s current facilities are not sufficient to house SCOE’s staff, and there is a need to secure additional space. 

 

SCOE has been exploring other facility options and after looking at the cost of leasing a facility, it appears it would be more fiscally responsible to purchase a property if we are able to find one with a suitable location and price.

 

SCOE has learned that the single-story office building adjacent to the Conference Center may be available for purchase. The owners of 10520 Armstrong Avenue have indicated a potential interest in selling their building, and they have quoted a price. SCOE staff will continue to negotiate. 

 

SCOE has sufficient reserve funds to purchase the property and believes it would be a prudent long-term investment for the agency. The building is currently being used as office space by an architecture firm, and it could be configured to meet our current office space needs. Its proximity to SCOE’s other facilities is ideal and would facilitate connecting technology and enable a more efficient use of space as staff could continue to utilize shared conference and meeting rooms. 

 

This project will require SCOE to contract with consultants in the following areas:

1. Appraisers;

2. Architects and a design team; 

3. Experts for specialized studies and reports as needed.

 

For this project, staff requests authority to conduct research regarding potential acquisition of the proposed Armstrong Avenue site and enter negotiations. This grant of authority includes the ability to seek proposals or bids as appropriate, engage all necessary contractors, to conduct research, and negotiate. 

 

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION: 

 

The Superintendent recommends the Board authorize staff to complete the business as required to hire necessary consultants, including an architect and design team, and complete the research and negotiations towards the purchase of the property located at 10520 Armstrong Avenue.
