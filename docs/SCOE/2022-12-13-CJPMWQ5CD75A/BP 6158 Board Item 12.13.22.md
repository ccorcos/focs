The Sacramento County Board of Education (Board) authorizes independent study as an optional alternative
instructional strategy by which students pupils may reach curriculum objectives and fulfill graduation requirements.
Independent study shall offer a means of individualizing the educational plan for students pupils whose needs may
be best met through study outside the regular school program. classroom setting. A
student's A pupil's participation in independent study shall be voluntary, . Students and pupils participating in
independent study shall have the right, at any time, to enter or return to the regular classroom mode of in-
person instruction at any time.

When a request is received for independent study from a parent or guardian (or from a student pupil who is an adult
or emancipated minor), the Superintendent, or designee, shall determine whether the prospective independent
study student pupil understands and is prepared to meet the Sacramento County Office of Education's (SCOE)
requirements for independent study. Independent study may be offered only to students who can achieve in this
program as well or better than they would in the regular classroom. SCOE's independent study program shall
provide participating pupils with standards-aligned content substantially equivalent in quality and quantity
to in-person instruction. For high school pupils, this shall include access to all courses offered by SCOE
for graduation and approved by the University of California or the California State University as creditable
under the A-G admissions criteria.

Independent study for each pupil shall be under the general supervision of a SCOE employee who
possesses a valid certification document pursuant to Education Code 44865 or an emergency credential
pursuant to Education Code 44300. Pupils' independent study shall be coordinated, evaluated, and
documented, as prescribed by law and reflected in the accompanying administrative regulation.

Furthermore, the The Superintendent or designee shall ensure that a written master independent study agreement
and a course-based learning agreement exists for each participating pupil student, and is are maintained in the
student's pupil's file, as prescribed by law.

The written agreement shall specify the length of time in which each independent study assignment must be
completed. Because excessive leniency in the duration of independent study assignments may result in a student
pupil falling behind his/her peers and increase the risk of dropping out of school, the maximum length of time

progress, an evaluation shall be conducted to determine whether it is in the pupil's student ’s best interest to

trigger an evaluation to determine whether the student pupil should remain in independent study.

A pupil-parent-educator conference shall be held as appropriate, including when requested by a parent, as
a reengagement strategy, and prior to enrollment or disenrollment from independent study.

The Board directs the Superintendent to develop Administrative Rules and Regulations to implement this Board
Policy. This policy will apply to independent study offered as of the 2022-2023 school year.
