Public Comment

 

Anyone may appear at the Board meeting to provide public comment regarding any item on the agenda or regarding any matter that is within the Board’s subject matter jurisdiction. However, the Board may not take action on any item not on the agenda except as authorized by Government Code section 54954.2. If possible, notify the Board President or Board Secretary in writing prior to the meeting if you wish to testify.

 

Individuals wishing to speak in person before the Board are requested to complete a public comment form. Speakers will be called in the order in which the cards are received. 

 

Public comment via Zoom teleconference may be made by using the raised hand feature in Zoom during the agenda item to be addressed. The speaker will be identified by their Display Name in Zoom when called upon to speak. Those joining the meeting via Zoom are requested to display their full name so there is no confusion when called upon to speak. Public comments will be received until the public comment period has elapsed.

 

Comments are limited to a maximum of two (2) minutes per speaker per agenda item, and individuals are limited to a single comment per agenda item.

 

Please contact boardcomments@scoe.net before the meeting if you have questions about the public comment process. 

 

Accommodating Individuals with Disabilities

 

The Sacramento County Office of Education encourages those with disabilities to participate fully in the public meeting process. If you need a disability-related modification or accommodation, including auxiliary aids or services, to participate in the public meeting, contact the Superintendent’s Office at (916) 228-2410 at least 48 hours before the scheduled Board meeting so that we may make every reasonable effort to accommodate you. [Government Code § 54953.2; Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990, § 202 (42 U.S.C. § 12132).]

CLASSIFIED 

 

Annemarie Kennedy, Program Analyst with Curriculum and Instruction was nominated by Multilingual Education Director, Graciela Garcia-Torres. Annemarie has gone above and beyond as a program analyst, surpassing expectations and achieving remarkable results when supporting the Curriculum and Instruction team. She demonstrates a can-do attitude in all tasks. Annemarie is quick at lending a hand and taking the initiative to support others. Annemarie's efforts play a crucial role in the success of the customer service we provide in serving multilingual learners. Annemarie is an asset to SCOE for her continued growth mindset, support across departments, providing excellence to our LEAs, and representing SCOE's vision and mission. She is recognized for her extraordinary efforts that go beyond the norm, making a lasting and positive impact across systems. Annemarie Kennedy has been a SCOE employee since May 2022. 

 

CERTIFICATED

 

Shannon Casey, School Nurse at Dry Creek Elementary was nominated by Area Principal, Jillian Friend. Shannon is a SCOE school nurse providing support to students and staff in the Foundations Academy programs. Shannon has been with SCOE for two years and from the beginning has proven to be an extremely hard worker, the epitome of a team player, and demonstrates leadership qualities that are constantly refining our nursing practices. She collaborates with her colleagues and teaching staff to ensure that we are providing the best nursing support to our students and meeting their unique needs. Shannon has an extremely positive and an unhesitating attitude. Shannon has stepped up to support other programs when needed despite having an already full plate and is a leader amongst our nursing staff. She has repeatedly demonstrated her organizational and technological skills to support the nursing team to streamline procedures and processes. Shannon Casey has been a SCOE employee since August 2022.

BACKGROUND:

 

The California School Boards Association (CSBA) has distributed the official ballot for the election of two county representatives to CSBA's Delegate Assembly, Subregion 6-B. Christine Jefferson, a trustee from Twin Rivers Unified School District, is an incumbent delegate. Ken Barnes, a trustee from the Robla School District, is a nominee for this seat. A biographical sketch of each candidate is attached.

 

The Board may vote for up to the number of seats to be filled. There are two seats up for election, so the Board may vote for up to two candidates. However the Board may not cast more than one vote for any one candidate. The ballot must be returned to CSBA on or before Friday, March 15, 2024.
