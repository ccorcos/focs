Public Comment

 

Anyone may submit public comments to the County Committee on any item on the agenda or regarding any matter that is within the County Committee’s subject matter jurisdiction. However, the County Committee may not take action on any item not on the agenda except as authorized by Government Code section 54954.2. 

 

Individuals wishing to speak in person before the County Committee are requested to complete a public comment form and submit it to the County Committee Recording Secretary. Speakers will be called in the order in which the cards are received. 

 

Public comment via Zoom teleconference may be made by using the raised hand feature in Zoom during the agenda item to be addressed. The speaker will be identified by their Display Name in Zoom when called upon to speak. Those joining the meeting via Zoom are requested to display their full name so there is no confusion when called upon to speak.

 

Individual speakers addressing the County Committee will be allowed two (2) minutes to address the County Committee on the agenda item.

 

Public comments may also be submitted in writing through an online form at https://bit.ly/scoe-comments. Public comments received at least 90 minutes before the start of the meeting, will be distributed to County Committee members before the meeting. Comments received after that time will be distributed to County Committee members after the meeting.

Please contact boardcomments@scoe.net before the meeting if you have questions about the public comment process. 

 

Accommodating Individuals with Disabilities

 

The Sacramento County Office of Education encourages those with disabilities to participate fully in the public meeting process. If you need a disability-related modification or accommodation, including auxiliary aids or services, to participate in the public meeting, contact the Superintendent’s Office at (916) 228-2410 at least 48 hours before the scheduled Board meeting so that we may make every reasonable effort to accommodate you. [Government Code § 54953.2; Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990, § 202 (42 U.S.C. § 12132).]

6. NEW BUSINESS - Galt Joint Union High School District
Type
Action

BACKGROUND:

 

The governing board of the Galt Joint Union High School District (GJUHSD) has adopted and submitted a proposal to change the method of electing GJUHSD governing board members. (Ed. Code, § 5019(a), (c).) Currently, the five GJUHSD board members are elected under an "at-large” system, where each trustee is elected by the registered voters of the entire school district. GJUHSD proposes, effective for the November 2022 and 2024 election cycles, to establish trustee areas, change its election method so one board member residing within each trustee area will be elected by the registered voters residing in that trustee area (“by-trustee area” elections), and establish the sequence of elections (proposal).

 

GJUHSD board members are elected in even-numbered years and serve staggered, four-year terms. The next election for three GJUHSD board members is scheduled for November 2022, and the election for the remaining two board members is scheduled for November 2024. GJUHSD proposes to use the same sequence of elections so that three trustee areas are assigned for election in 2022 and two trustee areas are assigned for election in 2024.

 

By law, the Sacramento County Committee on School District Organization (County Committee) must call and conduct a hearing within the district on GJUHSD’s proposal and, at the conclusion of its hearing, approve or disapprove GJUHSD's proposal. (Ed. Code, § 5019(c)(2).)

 

PUBLIC HEARING PROCESS:

 

The GJUHSD’s proposal and public website and other publicly available information indicate the following history, public hearings, outreach, and other events related to GJUHSD’s proposed change of election method:

 

  • April 14, 2022 – GJUHSD received a "Notice of Violation" of the California Voting Rights Act (CVRA) from Southwest Voter Registration Education Project, a Texas non-profit corporation registered to conduct operations in California.

 

  • June 11, 2020 – GJUHSD entered into a conditional settlement agreement with Southwest Voter Registration Education Project in which GJUHSD denied that its at-large election system violated the CVRA, but agreed to change to by-trustee area elections to avoid litigation and ensure compliance with the CVRA. The settlement agreement recognized GJUHSD's desire to draw trustee area boundaries based on the 2020 federal decennial census data.

 

  • June 11, 2020 – GJUHSD adopted Resolution No. 1920-14 initiating the process to transition to a by-trustee area election system to be implemented with the November 2022 and 2024 governing board election cycles.

 

  • November 4, 2021 – GJUHSD presented a timeline for transition to by-trustee area elections.

 

  • December 16, 2021 and January 13, 2022 – GJUHSD held public hearings to consider community input and comment regarding the potential composition of trustee areas prior to draft maps being drawn.

 

  • GJUHSD launched a dedicated webpage and a dedicated email address specific to the transition to by-trustee area elections, both of which were communicated through various platforms.

 

  • January 18, 2022 and February 10, 2022 – GJUHSD published draft trustee area Map Scenarios 1-4 for public review and input and posted on its public website.

 

  • GJUHSD communicated that its draft map scenarios were available for viewing through multiple platforms, including but not limited to: announcements on the main GJUHSD webpage and a dedicated webpage for the transition to by-trustee area elections, email messages to families, flyers and advertisements at its schools, meeting with stakeholders, including City and Schools Together, and placing advertisements in The Galt Herald, Sacramento Bee, and Lodi News Sentinel.

 

  • January 25, 2022 and February 10, 2022 – GJUHSD held public hearings to gather public input on the proposed trustee-area map scenarios and sequence of elections.

 

  • February 10, 2022 – GJUHSD considered Map Scenarios 1-4 and election sequencing schedules and expressed its preference for Map Scenario 4 for final consideration on March 10, 2022.

 

  • March 10, 2022 – GJUHSD held public hearing and adopted Resolution No. 2122-16 proposing to transition to “by-trustee area” elections using the GJUHSD trustee area boundaries map and sequence of elections identified as Map Scenario 4.


Additional information regarding the GJUHSD process, along with copies of the alternative trustee area maps, GJUHSD resolutions, and presentations are included in Attachments 1 and 2.

 

COUNTY COMMITTEE ROLE:

 

The County Committee must call and conduct a hearing and, at its conclusion, either approve or disapprove GJUHSD’s proposal. (Ed. Code, § 5019(c)(2).) GJUHSD Resolution No. 2122-16 (Attachment 1, Exhibit C) requests County Committee approval of GJUHSD's proposal to:

 

  1. 1. Adopt by-trustee area elections, implementing GJUHSD’s trustee area boundaries map identified as Map Scenario 4.
  2. 2. Assign the sequence of elections for GJUHSD’s five trustee areas as follows:
    1. a.    Trustee Areas 1, 2, and 4 scheduled for election in 2022; and
    2. b.    Trustee Areas 3 and 5 scheduled for election in 2024.

 

The relevant statutes provide no guidelines or standards by which the County Committee must review a school district’s proposal. Therefore, the County Committee has discretion to consider GJUHSD’s proposal in light of the local area concerned. Absent specific standards, government entities often give deference to the decisions of other governmental entities with more localized knowledge of the issue at hand, unless the local decision was arbitrary, capricious, or entirely lacking in evidentiary support. Thus, if the record reflects rational reasons for GJUHSD’s decision, the reasons are supported by evidence, and the decision is not in violation of the law, the County Committee may properly approve GJUHSD’s proposal.

 

In the event that the County Committee approves GJUHSD's proposal after the public hearing, a proposed resolution (County Committee Resolution No. CC-22-02) is included for the County Committee’s consideration. (See Attachment 3.) If the County Committee denies GJUHSD’s proposal, a resolution is not needed. 

 

Approval by County Committee

 

As of January 1, 2022, school districts may transition to by-trustee area elections upon approval by the County Committee without voter approval and without a State Board of Education waiver of the election requirement so long as the County Committee's Resolution includes a declaration that the change in election method is in furtherance of the purposes of the California Voting Rights Act of 2001 (CVRA). (Ed. Code, § 5020 (a)(2).) GJUHSD's proposal and supporting materials demonstrate that GJUHSD initiated its change to by-trustee area elections in furtherance of the CVRA. Therefore, if the County Committee approves GJUHSD’s proposal to change to “by-trustee area” elections and adopts Resolution No. CC-22-02, GJUHSD will change to by-trustee area elections beginning with the next GJUHSD governing board election on November 8, 2022.

 

Denial by County Committee

 

If the County Committee denies GJUHSD’s current proposal, GJUHSD could conduct further public hearing(s) to consider and adopt an alternative plan and submit a revised proposal or take no further action and maintain the status quo.

 

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:

 

The Sacramento County Superintendent of Schools recommends that the Sacramento County Committee on School District Organization hold a public hearing and approve or deny GJUHSD’s proposal.
