FINDINGS AND TENTATIVE CONCLUSION                                                RESOLUTION NO. 2023-01

IN THE MATTER OF:

PROPOSED TRANSFER OF TERRITORY FROM
CENTER JOINT UNIFIED SCHOOL DISTRICT
TO ROSEVILLE CITY SCHOOL DISTRICT AND
ROSEVILLE JOINT UNION HIGH SCHOOL
DISTRICT


WHEREAS, in December 2022, the Placer County Superintendent of Schools and the
Sacramento County Superintendent of Schools received a Petition for Transfer of Territory
("Petition") from Melissa Campbell ("Chief Petitioner"); and

WHEREAS, the Petition proposes to transfer the territory known as "Aspire Solaire," bounded
by Daylight Drive, Solaire Drive, Santucci Boulevard, and Pleasant Grove Boulevard and
containing 213 parcels of residential property from the Center Joint Unified School District
("CJUSD") to the Roseville City School District ("RCSD") and Roseville Joint Union High
School District ("RJUHSD"); and

WHEREAS, in January 2023, the Placer County Superintendent of Schools notified the Chief
Petitioner that the Petition was legally sufficient under the Education Code, and the Sacramento
County Superintendent of Schools concurred in this conclusion; and

WHEREAS, on February 8, 2023, the Placer County Committee on School District
Organization ("Placer County Committee") held a hearing on the Petition within the boundaries
of RCSD and RJUHSD; and

WHEREAS, on February 25, 2023, the Sacramento County Committee on School District
Organization ("Sacramento County Committee") held a hearing on the Petition within the
boundaries of CJUSD; and

WHEREAS, on August 7, 2023, the Placer County Committee determined that transfer of
territory pursuant to the Petition, if approved, would be exempt from the requirements of the
California Environmental Quality Act ("CEQA"); and the Sacramento County Committee
concurred in this conclusion on August 15, 2023; and

WHEREAS, a county committee may approve a legally sufficient petition to transfer territory
between two or more school districts after public hearing on the matter and if such petition
substantially meets the criteria set forth in the Education Code, section 35753; and

WHEREAS, the Placer County Superintendent of Schools and Sacramento County
Superintendent of Schools engaged Government Financial Services Joint Powers Authority to
prepare a report on the application of the statutory criteria set forth in the Education Code,

WHEREAS, the members of the Placer County Committee have considered all verbal and
documentary information submitted during the public hearings and have reviewed the Petition,
Report, and all other documents filed with the Placer County Committee related to the Petition;
and

WHEREAS, the members of the Placer County Committee have considered all written and oral
comments submitted in support of and in opposition to the Petition; and

WHEREAS, CJUSD has provided written notice to the Placer County Committee that it is in
opposition to the Petition; and

WHEREAS, pursuant to Education Code, section 35520 where a petition seeks to transfer
territory between school districts located in more than one county, prior to any decision on the
petition, the county committees on school district organization in each county must make and
transmit findings and a tentative conclusion to the county committee on school district
organization in the other county.

NOW, THEREFORE, the Placer County Committee on School District Organization hereby
resolves, determines, and finds the following:


SECTION 1. The recitals set forth above are true and correct.
