                                                       RESOLUTION NO. 2322
    (
             A RESOLUTION OF THE ROSEVILLE JOINT UNION HIGH SCHOOL DISTRICT
            IN OPPOSITION TO THE ASPIRE SOLAIRE TERRITORY TRANSFER PETITION
              AND FURTHER REQUESTING THE PLACER AND SACRAMENTO COUNTY
             COMMITTEES ON SCHOOL DISTRICT ORGANIZATION DENY THE PETITION

        WHEREAS, on or about December 16, 2022, Petitioner Melissa Campbell submitted a
        petition to the Placer County Committee on School District Organization (Placer County
        Committee) to transfer territory currently within the Center Joint Unified School District
        (CJUSD) to the Roseville Joint Union High School District (RJUHSD) and Roseville City
        School District: and

        WHEREAS, the information provided demonstrates the shortcomings of the petition,
        including but not limited to:

             •The boundary between CJUSD and RJUHSD was already addressed, along with
              the developer, in 2019. Further changes could lead to other nearby
              neighborhoods going through the same petition process rather than waiting for
              CJUSD to build its new schools.
(           • CJUSD would lose $78 million in assessed value representing 2.25% of the
              CJUSD tax base.
            • Loss of developer fees for purchased homes in Aspire Solaire which have
              already been allocated to CJUSD.
            • Due to anticipated enrollment projections at West Park High School, students
              residing in Aspire Solaire would be zoned to attend either Roseville, Oakmont or
              Antelope High Schools which are farther than their current school attendance
              zoning of Center High School.
            • Increased cost to RJUHSD transportation of approximately $50,000 annually as
              students will be bussed to Roseville, Oakmont or Antelope High Schools. This
              would also require the need to purchase an additional bus and train and hire an
              additional bus driver.
            • Would create confusion among attendance boundaries because the Aspire
              Solaire zoning would be non-contiguous.

        WHEREAS, RJUHSD desires to publicly express its opposition to this current Petition
        and further requests that the Placer County Committee and Sacramento County
        Committee, should they become involved, summarily deny the Petition.
