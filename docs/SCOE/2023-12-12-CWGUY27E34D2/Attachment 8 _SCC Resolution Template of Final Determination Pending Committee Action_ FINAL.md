SACRAMENTO COUNTY COMMITTEE ON SCHOOL DISTRICT ORGANIZATION

Final Determination Regarding Petition to Transfer Territory From Center Joint
Unified School District to Roseville City School District and Roseville Joint
Union High School District

Resolution No. CC-23-02

WHEREAS, in December 2022, the Sacramento and Placer County Offices of
Education each received a completed petition (Petition) to transfer territory presently
under the jurisdiction of the Center Joint Unified School District (CJUSD) and the
Sacramento County Superintendent of Schools, to the Roseville City School District
(RCSD) and the Roseville Joint Union High School District (RJUHSD) under the
jurisdiction of the Placer County Superintendent of Schools; and

WHEREAS, the territory proposed to be transferred and its 213 parcels of residential
property, known as the “Aspire Solaire” neighborhood, are located in the City of
Roseville, County of Placer, and bounded by Pleasant Grove Boulevard, Solaire Drive,
Santucci Boulevard, and Daylight Drive; and

WHEREAS, in accordance with Education Code section 35521, in January 2023, both
County Superintendents determined the Petition to be legally sufficient and thereafter
transmitted the Petition to the State Board of Education, as well as their respective
County Committees on School District Organization; and

WHEREAS, on February 8, 2023, the Placer County Committee on School District
Organization (Placer County Committee) held a public hearing on the Petition within the
boundaries of RCSD and RJUHSD; and

WHEREAS, on February 25, 2023, the Sacramento County Committee on School
District Organization (Sacramento County Committee) held a public hearing on the
Petition within the boundaries of CJUSD and, at least 10 days before the public hearing,
a description of the petition was provided to the public and the affected governing
boards; and

WHEREAS, on March 15, 2023, the governing board of CJUSD adopted Resolution No.
20/2022-23 declaring CJUSD’s opposition to the Petition and provided a copy of this
resolution to both County Committees; and

WHEREAS, on August 7, 2023, the Placer County Committee determined that the
proposed territory transfer would be exempt from the requirements of the California
Environmental Quality Act (CEQA); and

WHEREAS, following public hearing on August 15, 2023, the Sacramento County
Committee determined that the proposed territory transfer is categorically exempt under
CEQA without exception in accordance with Public Resources Code section 21084 and
Cal. Code Regs., tit. 14, sections 15300.2 and 15314; and

WHEREAS, Education Code section 35710 provides that a county committee may
approve a legally sufficient petition to transfer territory between two or more school
districts after public hearing on the matter and if such petition substantially meets the
criteria set forth in Education Code section 35753; and

WHEREAS, the Sacramento and Placer County Offices of Education engaged
Government Financial Services Joint Powers Authority (GFS-JPA) to analyze the
statutory criteria set forth in Education Code section 35753 in the context of the Petition,
which GFS-JPA then completed, rendering its analytical findings in a written report
(Report) dated October 16, 2023; and

WHEREAS, Education Code section 35524 specifies that when separate hearings are
conducted in each county, a decision to grant or deny the proposed reorganization shall
be made only after findings and tentative conclusions (Findings and Tentative
Conclusions) of the hearings in each county have been transmitted to the officer or
agency which conducted the hearings in each of the other counties; and

WHEREAS, on October 30, 2023, the Placer County Committee held a public hearing
to render its Findings and Tentative Conclusions wherein it (1) failed to either approve
or disapprove the Petition and, (2) found the statutory criteria set forth in Education
Code section 35753 to be substantially met; and

WHEREAS, on November 9, 2023, the governing board of RJUHSD adopted
Resolution No. 2322 declaring RJUHSD’s opposition to the Petition and provided a copy
of this resolution to both County Committees; and

WHEREAS, on November 14, 2023, the Sacramento County Committee held a public
hearing to render its Findings and Tentative Conclusions wherein it considered the
GFS-JPA Report, the Findings and Tentative Conclusions of the Placer County
Committee, and all written and oral comments submitted in support of and in opposition
to the Petition, and thereafter passed Resolution No. CC-23-01 wherein the Sacramento
County Committee (1) disapproved the Petition and, (2) found statutory criteria numbers
2, 4, 6, 8, and 9 set forth in Education Code section 35753 to not be substantially met;
and

WHEREAS, on November 27, 2023, the governing board of RCSD adopted Resolution
No. 2023-24.12 declaring RCSD’s opposition to the Petition and provided a copy of this
resolution to both County Committees; and

WHEREAS, on December 4, the Placer County Committee held a public hearing
wherein it made a final determination (Final Determination) to approve/disapprove
[SELECT ONE] the Petition, and

WHEREAS, the Sacramento County Committee has considered all written and oral
comments submitted in support of and in opposition to the Petition.

THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, that the Sacramento County Committee makes its
Final Determination to approve/disapprove [SELECT ONE] the Petition.
