                                                                                                          ATTACHMENT 9



                                            RESOLUTION NO. 2023-24.12


   A RESOLUTION OF THE ROSEVILLE CITY SCHOOL DISTRICT IN OPPOSITION TO THE
   ASPIRE SOLAIRE TERRITORY TRANSFER PETITION AND FURTHER REQUESTING THE
       PLACER AND SACRAMENTO COUNTY COMMITTEES ON SCHOOL DISTRICT
                        ORGANIZATION DENY THE PETITION



  WHEREAS, on or about December 10, 2022, Petitioner Melissa Campbell submitted a petition
  to the Placer County Committee on School District Organization (Placer County Committee) to
  transfer territory currently within the Center Unified School District (CJUSD) to the Roseville
  Joint'Union High School District (RJUHSD) and Roseville City School District (RCSD); and

  WHEREAS, the information provided demonstrates the shortcomings of the petition, including
  put not limited to:

       •    The boundary between CJUSD and RJUHSD was already addressed, along with the
            developers, in 2019. Further changes could lead to other nearby neighborhoods going
            through the same petition process rather than waiting for CJUSD to build its new
            schools.
       •    CJUSD would lose $78 million in assessed value, representing 2.27% of the CJUSD tax
            base.
       •    Loss of developer fees for purchased homes in Aspire Solaire, which have already been
            allocated to CJUSD.
       •    Due to anticipated enrollment projections at Chilton Middle School, students residing in
            Aspire Solaire may be zoned to attend Eich Middle School, which is farther than their
            current school attendance zoning of Center High School.
       •    Increased cost to RCSD transportation of approximately $50,000 annually as students
            may be bussed to Eich Middle School. This would also require purchasing an additional
            bus and training and hiring an additional bus driver.
       •    Would create confusion among attendance boundaries because the Aspire Solaire
            zoning would be non-contiguous.

  WHEREAS, RCSD de�ires to publicly express its opposition to the current Petition and further
  requests that the Placer County Committee and Sacramento County Committee, should they
  become involved, summarily deny the Petition.

  THEREFORE, LET IT BE RESOLVED the Governing Board of the Roseville City School District
  opposes the current Petition as presently constituted.
                                                                     '
  BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, the Governing Board of the Roseville City School District
  respectfully requests the Placer County Committee and the Sacramento County Committee
  deny the Petition.
