[[**BACKGROUND:**]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[The Every Student Succeeds Act (ESSA) requires schools receiving Title
I funds and operating a school-wide program, or schools identified for
Comprehensive Support and Improvement (CSI), Targeted Support and
Improvement (TSI) or Additional Targeted Support and Improvement (ATSI),
to develop a school plan. Assembly Bill 716 revised California Education
Code sections 64001-65001 to streamline and align state and federal
planning processes and organized federal school planning requirements in
ESSA.]{style="font-size:14px;"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}

 

[[The development of the School Plan for Student Achievement (SPSA) is
required to include the
following:]{style="font-size:14px;"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}

- [[A comprehensive needs assessment (pursuant to
  ESSA)]{style="font-size:14px;"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}
  - [[Analysis of verifiable state data, consistent with state
    priorities, including state-determined long term
    goals]{style="font-size:14px;"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}
  - [[The plan may include local
    data]{style="font-size:14px;"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}
- [[An identification of the process for evaluating and monitoring the
  implementation of the School Plan and progress towards accomplishing
  the
  goals]{style="font-size:14px;"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}

 

[[The Juvenile Court and Community School programs have prepared their
school plans in accordance with school-wide program
stipulations.]{style="font-size:14px;"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}

 

[[In accordance with California Education Code Section 52068(a)(4), a
county office of education superintendent must first review the Local
Control and Accountability Plan (LCAP) and School Plans to ensure that
the two plans are consistent. An integrated approach to planning ensures
a consistent alignment between the LCAP and School Plans. As a result,
the goals and relevant metrics outlined in the 2021-22 Juvenile Court
and Community School SPSAs are aligned to the 2021-22 Sacramento County
Office of Education
LCAP.]{style="font-size:14px;"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}

access for all four-year-old children to enroll in high quality programs
the year before kindergarten (full roll out by 2025-2026). Under
California Education Code (EC) Section 8281.5(c), grant funds are
allocated to county offices of education to support countywide planning
and capacity building around UPK. The funds are intended to support
school districts and charter schools in the creation or expansion of
state preschool programs (CSPP) and transitional kindergarten (TK)
programs, and to establish or strengthen collaboration between and among
