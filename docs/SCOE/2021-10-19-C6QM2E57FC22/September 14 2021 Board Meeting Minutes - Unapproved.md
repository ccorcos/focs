Superintendent Gordon reported on the following:

         LOCAL DISTRICT UPDATES
           • Arcohe: There has been low participation in independent study. The
             district is experiencing a shortage of subs.
           • Center: COVID cases are slowing and processes for tracking are going
             well. Independent study enrollment has doubled. Retirees are coming
             back to assist with staffing shortage.
           • Elk Grove: COVID cases are decreasing. District has increased rate for
             off-track teachers and retirees which has improved availability of staff.
           • Elverta: Some staff are reluctant to be vaccinated.
           • Folsom Cordova: Staff shortages and independent study issues
             creating stress. COVID cases are decreasing after a recent spike.
             There is a need for bus drivers, instructional assistants, and food
             service workers. Sub rates have been increased. Recently received a
             charter petition.
           • Galt Elementary: Hired additional staff for contact tracing. Staffing for
             teachers and subs is adequate. Need classified staff. The number of
             students quarantined has decreased.
           • Galt High: COVID cases are decreasing. 39 students enrolled in
             independent study. Reaching out to retirees for staffing support.
           • River Delta: Hired health assistants to support contact tracing.
           • Robla: Contact tracing is a challenge. Staff shortages in Special Ed
             and subs.
           • Sacramento City: 162 active quarantine cases thus far. Staff shortages
             are a problem. Passed a proposal to increase sub rates.
           • San Juan: COVID cases are decreasing.

         COMMUNITY SCHOOLS
           • We have 19 students enrolled at Colley and 17 enrolled at Hickey.
           • We are excited to report that our Community Based Organizations
             (CBOs) have returned to providing services including Earth Mama
             Healing (mentoring), The Hawk Institute (Mentoring), Milton Bowens
             (visual artist in residence), and 916 Ink (writing). We expect to
             welcome additional CBOs this year.

         SENIOR EXTENSION
           • We are currently serving students at five locations and have over 328
              students enrolled to date. Our Senior Extension Programs have
              expanded access to RSP services. Our Community Based
              Organizations are providing supports to Senior Extension.

EL CENTRO
   • Improving Your Tomorrow (IYT) has started working with students in
     the El Centro Program. This work is part of a pilot project to engage
     students with mentoring services while attending El Centro and provide
     continuing services when they transition to other school settings.
   • Milton Bowens (visual artist) has begun his yearlong residency with the
     girl's unit.

SPECIAL EDUCATION UPDATE
  • Special Education will be collapsing and consolidating classrooms to
     better utilize staff and best accommodate students as we teach our
     students through in-person and Independent Study.
        o Seven classes in total that had low enrollment were combined
            into three in-person classrooms.
  • These new combined classes started Monday, September 13.
  • The other four teachers not teaching in-person will teach independent
     study classes for up to 48 students.
  • This allowed us to put appropriate staffing in all of our classrooms.
     Before this change we had over five paraprofessional vacancies that
     we were unable to fill.
  • All in-person classes are going well. We have had to quarantine
     several students, but we are following all public health protocols and
     students and staff are returning to in-person after their quarantine
     period.

SUMMER INNOVATION HUBS
  • During the months of June and July, 175 students were served in
    SCOE’s Summer Education Innovation Centers at three school sites:
    FC Joyce in Twin Rivers Unified, Rosa Parks in Sacramento City
    Unified, and Thomas Edison in San Juan Unified. The Summer
    Centers were created to support our Sacramento Communities and to
    create safe, welcoming spaces for students, SCOE partnered with
    Community-Based Organizations, local non-profits, and school
    districts. The video that we created showcases highlights of the
    program.

SCHOOLSFIRST BACKPACK DONATIONS
  • Thanks to a generous donation of backpacks filled with school
    supplies, courtesy of SchoolsFirst Federal Credit Union, 100 children in
    Sacramento County will be better prepared for school this year. Our
    Project TEACH homeless services program received the backpacks –
    filled with supplies – on September 1. Many thanks to our friends from
    SchoolsFirst.

CA KIDS FIRE RELIEF
  • On August 25, he took part in a news conference to announce the
      launch of “CA Kids Fire Relief.” This is a statewide effort where
      students and school communities are being urged to provide financial
      and emotional support to students, families, and schools impacted by
      the catastrophic wildfires.
  • Tim Taylor – from the Small School Districts Association – and El
      Dorado County Superintendent Ed Manansala, and Nevada County
      Superintendent Scott Lay joined this event.
  • During this effort, we are asking for donations of gift cards for food,
      clothing, gasoline, and credit card-style gift cards.
  • SCOE is the collection point. The cards which will be distributed to the
      affected schools, students, and families.
  • On September 9, leaders of our organizations – CSEA and SCOETA
      made generous donations to the program.
  • Many thanks to SCOETA President John Liddicoat and CSEA 1st Vice
      President Tara Degg who made the donations on behalf of their
      organizations.

SENATE HEARING TESTIMONY
  • On August 18, he testified – virtually – for the Senate Special
    Committee on Pandemic Emergency Response and Senate Education
    Committee.
  • He talked about the work of the Mental Health Services Oversight and
    Accountability Commission – or MHSOAC – on which he serves.
  • He also spoke about the importance of youth voice in meeting the
    mental health needs of our children, and about SCOE’s work in making
    schools Centers of Wellness in Sacramento County.
  • He also spoke of how we are coordinating $6.8 million from FEMA and
    DHCS to facilitate a statewide Social Emotional Learning Community
    of Practice that started in January 2021 and will continue for the next
    three years.
