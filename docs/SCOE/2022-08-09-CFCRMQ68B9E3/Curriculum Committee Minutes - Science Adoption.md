    I.        Call to Order and Roll Call:
             The meeting was called to order by Trustee Bina Lefkovitz, Chair, at 4:33 p.m.
             followed by a roll call of the members present. Present were: Trustee Bina
             Lefkovitz, Chair, Trustee O. Alfred Brown, Member, Trustee Harold Fong,
             Member, Jackie White, Nancy Herota, Melissa Neuburger and Amy Kraft.
             Recorder: Marty Florey

    II.      Review of New Adoption – Science Curriculum
             Jackie introduced Amy Kraft, Curriculum Specialist for SCOE. Amy presented
             the Science Curriculum Committee update.

             The Science Adoption team consisted of eight teachers from SCOE’s Special
             Education Program and Court and Community Schools. Committee members
             included Tereze Lear, Brendan Moore, Angel Martinez, Julie Wilde, Ben
             Diaz, Ju Park, Robin Bacon and Sterling Alley. Amy Kraft, SCOE’s
             Curriculum Specialist is also a member of the committee.

             Amy Kraft presented an overview of the pros and cons for each of the
             programs the committee reviewed. The Science curriculum adoption started
             in December with STEMscopes and lasted for 2 months. The second
             program, McGraw Hill, began in March and was piloted until the end of April.
             After the pilot program concluded, the adoption committee felt that the
             STEMscopes curriculum was by far the better program and that it met most of
             the needs of the students including providing opportunities for unique, hands-
             on activities. This was not a typical “read and recite” program but included
             much more of a project-based learning approach. Amy stated that the
             teachers are excited and looking forward to having access to materials and
             planning their classes around this new curriculum.

             Jackie White stated that there will be a Summer Professional Learning series
             for all of Court and Community School and Special Education for the first time
             this July. This will include Amy reviewing StemScopes and prepping teachers
             for its use.
