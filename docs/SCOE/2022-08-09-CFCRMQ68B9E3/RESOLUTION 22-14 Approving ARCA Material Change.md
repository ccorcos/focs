SACRAMENTO COUNTY BOARD OF EDUCATION
Resolution Approving Material Revision to American River Collegiate Academy
Charter School
Resolution No. 22-14

WHEREAS, on February 4, 2020, the Sacramento County Board of Education (Board)
approved the American River Collegiate Academy (ARCA) Charter School to serve
students within the San Juan Unified School District.

WHEREAS, ARCA’s approved charter states that ARCA will serve 468 students in
grades TK-6 by the charter school’s fifth year of operation.

WHEREAS, based on actual enrollment during its first two years of operation, ARCA is
now projecting that it will serve 180 students in grades TK-5 by its fifth year of operation.

WHEREAS, the Board's Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) with ARCA requires
ARCA to seek a material revision of its charter petition when there are changes in
enrollment that represent an increase or decrease from the annual enrollment originally
projected in the charter petition by more than 25% in any grade level or 10% of total
enrollment in any given year, and when grade levels to be served are added or deleted
in any given year or as a whole.

WHEREAS, ARCA has submitted the attached request for a material revision to its
charter to update the actual and projected enrollment and grade levels to be served by
ARCA within the first five years of the charter school’s operation, which specifies the
areas and pages of the charter to be revised.

WHEREAS, a properly noticed public hearing regarding ARCA’s request for a material
revision was held on August 9, 2022.

IT IS HEREBY RESOLVED that:

     1. The recitals set forth above are true and correct.
     2. The Sacramento County Board of Education hereby approves the attached
        material revision to the American River Collegiate Academy (ARCA) charter to
        update the actual and projected enrollment and grade levels to be served by
        ARCA within the first five years of the charter school’s operation.
     3. The Sacramento County Board of Education hereby authorizes the Sacramento
        County Superintendent of Schools or designee to take any other actions needed
        to effectuate the purposes of this Resolution.
