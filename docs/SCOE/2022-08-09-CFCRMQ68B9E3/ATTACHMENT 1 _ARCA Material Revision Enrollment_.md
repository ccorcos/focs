I.        Overview
As outlined in our Memorandum of Understanding with Sacramento County Office of
Education, American River Collegiate Academy’s authorizer, a material revision is
required to be completed when enrollment fluctuates by 25% in any grade level or 10%
of total enrollment originally projected in the charter petition.
The purpose of this material revision is to align our actual enrollment with an enrollment
projection that aligns with our currently projected growth and waitlist demand. As a
reminder, we opened our school in the same year that a global health emergency was
declared by the World Health Organization. These declarations were followed by the
State of California declaration of emergency and local declarations to address the global
pandemic.
Although we opened smaller than we had originally projected, we successfully opened,
in the midst of a global pandemic, with a group of dedicated families and students. We
are very grateful for the families and students who have chosen, and continue to
choose, our school, as well as our ongoing partnership with our authorizer. We look
forward to serving additional students within this growing community and know that we
will attain and surpass these revised enrollment projections.

II.       Material Revisions
The proposed material revision on page 18, as follows:
      •    Consistent with the other Rocklin Academy elementary schools, American River
           Collegiate Academy plans to have a student to teacher ratio of 24 to 1 in grades
           K-3, and 30 to 1 in grades 4-12. American River Collegiate Academy’s
           transitional kindergarten will align with SB 130 class size ratios with one adult per
           12 pupils in the 2022-2023 school year and one adult for every 10 pupils in the
           2023-2024 and each year after, or as otherwise indicated in law. Although
           American River Collegiate Academy intends to plan assumptions based on grade
           level ratios indicated above, these ratios are not intended to be enrollment caps,
           but rather a reasonable approximation of the level of community interest. Actual
           grade level ratios may be adjusted based on community interest and demand.
           By the end of the end of the first charter term five years of operation, American
           River Collegiate Academy plans to serve approximately 468 180 students in
           grades TK -6 5. These assumptions are not intended to be enrollment caps, but
           rather a reasonable and conservative approximation of the level of community
           interest that the Academy believe exists.
