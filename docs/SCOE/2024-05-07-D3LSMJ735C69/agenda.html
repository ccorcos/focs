Public Comment

 

Anyone may appear at the Board meeting to provide public comment regarding any item on the agenda or regarding any matter that is within the Board’s subject matter jurisdiction. However, the Board may not take action on any item not on the agenda except as authorized by Government Code section 54954.2.  If possible, notify the Board President or Board Secretary in writing prior to the meeting if you wish to testify.

 

Individuals wishing to speak in person before the Board are requested to complete a public comment form. Speakers will be called in the order in which the cards are received. 

 

Public comment via Zoom teleconference may be made by using the raised hand feature in Zoom during the agenda item to be addressed. The speaker will be identified by their Display Name in Zoom when called upon to speak. Those joining the meeting via Zoom are requested to display their full name so there is no confusion when called upon to speak. Public comments will be received until the public comment period has elapsed.

 

Comments are limited to a maximum of two (2) minutes per speaker per agenda item, and individuals are limited to a single comment per agenda item.

 

Please contact boardcomments@scoe.net before the meeting if you have questions about the public comment process. 

 

Accommodating Individuals with Disabilities

 

The Sacramento County Office of Education encourages those with disabilities to participate fully in the public meeting process. If you need a disability-related modification or accommodation, including auxiliary aids or services, to participate in the public meeting, contact the Superintendent’s Office at (916) 228-2410 at least 48 hours before the scheduled Board meeting so that we may make every reasonable effort to accommodate you. [Government Code § 54953.2; Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990, § 202 (42 U.S.C. § 12132).]

CLASSIFIED 

 

Michael Shell, Transition Specialist with the Re-Entry Department, was nominated by Project Specialist Brandee Brewer, Re-Entry Department. Michael is the only Transition Specialist at our West Sacramento location and supervises a full caseload of clients, which includes both parole and probation participants. Brian Gibbons, Resource Specialist with the Special Education, was nominated by Principal Lisa Alcalá, Community Schools. Since joining the Special Education team as a Resource Specialist, Brian has been an exceptional employee. He creates an environment where students feel comfortable asking questions about their school, which can be really hard for young people. Brian is also incredibly supportive of his peers and is always willing to jump in and help. Additionally, Brian has helped refine protocols and processes for helping our students who receive Special Education services. We appreciate his positive, solutions-based approach to his work. Brian Gibbons has been a SCOE employee since May 2019.

Department/Director: College and Career Readiness/Jacqueline White

 

Funding Source: California Commission on Teacher Credentialing

 

Amount Requested: $250,000

 

Fiscal Year(s): 2024-2025

 

Program Description: 

The School Counselor Residency Capacity Planning Grant will allow SCOE's College and Career Readiness Department to develop a plan to support a collaborative partnership between eligible school districts, charter schools, and California State University, Sacramento’s (CSUS) Pupil Personnel Services program and create a school counselor residency program. If awarded this one-year Planning Grant, the intention is to apply for the Implementation Grant the following year which will fund the further development of a new school counselor residency program in support of local efforts to recruit, develop support systems based on the American School Counselor Association model, and provide outreach and communication strategies to promote diversity and retention of a school counseling workforce that reflects each district's diversity.

 

New Positions: None

 

Subcontracts: 

Sub-contracts will be developed with CSUS for staff hours to develop the program and with the American School Counselor Association (ASCA) for professional development. Participating districts will also be sub-contracted to provide mentor stipends to lead counselors involved in the development of this program.

 

Evaluation Component: 

SCOE will apply for the Implementation Grant which is awarded to agencies who have been successful in completing the objectives of the planning grant.

Department/Director: Curriculum and Instruction/Dr. Channa Pitt

 

Funding Source: California Department of Education 

 

Amount Requested: $300,000

 

Fiscal Year(s): 2023-2024, 2024-2025, 2025-2026

 

Program Description: 

The California Department of Education (CDE) selected the Sacramento County Superintendent of Schools (SCSS) Contractor, Tamara Wilson, to conduct activities related to the development of the California Literacy Roadmap. The Contractor will provide content expertise, thought partnership, and guidance for the development of the California Literacy Roadmap for Prekindergarten through Grade Twelve. The Contractor will also provide leadership and assistance to its own districts and schools, as well as to districts and schools across California in multiple areas of literacy, including but not limited to early literacy and Dyslexia. The SCSS has led the Early Literacy State Block Grant and is currently leading the California Dyslexia Initiative and Literacy Coaches and Reading Specialist Educator Training Grant. The Contractor will enter into agreements with a partner consultant that is a Literacy Lead Partner who is an expert in literacy, a researcher, and a co-author of the English Language Arts/English Language Development Framework (ELA/ELD Framework). 

 

Evaluation Component:

The Contractor will:

  • demonstrate consistent communications with the CDE Contract Monitor, attend meetings via conference call; and provide updates
  • participate in quarterly check-in meetings with the CDE and Lead Partners
  • submit quarterly progress reports and invoices
  • maintain electronic files to ensure successful project completion
  • in coordination with the Lead Partner, develop the Literacy Roadmap and provide literacy models of instruction and guidance for literacy content blocks
  • support the development and delivery of webinars related to the ELA/ELD Framework
  • participate in a virtual planning meeting within the first 15 days of the contract for the development of the Literacy Roadmap guiding documents 
  • participate in a virtual planning meeting with Lead Partners within the first 30 days of the contract for the development of the English-medium literacy content blocks for kindergarten and grade one 
  • participate in a virtual planning meeting to collaborate on findings to develop the Literacy Roadmap documents
