[[Education Code section 17545 and 17546 allow for the disposal of
property no longer needed or that is determined unsuitable for school
use.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[The Computer, Network and Telecommunication Support Department (CNTS)
reviews all technology surplus equipment to ensure that any repairable,
non-obsolete equipment is re-used by Sacramento County Office of
Education (SCOE) programs. In the event the equipment cannot be
repaired, is no longer able to support the latest security patches and
updates (posing a network security risk) or is so obsolete it can no
longer be used for its intended purpose, the equipment is deemed
unsuitable for use and is recommended for
disposal. ]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[The technology equipment listed below has been determined to be
unsuitable for use and of insufficient value to defray the costs of
arranging a sale. It is in the SCOE's best interest to deem these items
obsolete and dispose of them. All storage devices (hard drives, solid
state drives, USB drives, etc.) are completely wiped of data and,
whenever possible, physically shredded to ensure the destruction of all
electronic data before
disposal.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

[[**BACKGROUND:**]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[The Every Student Succeeds Act (ESSA) requires schools receiving Title
I funds and operating a school wide program, or schools identified for
Comprehensive Support and Improvement (CSI), Targeted Support and
Improvement (TSI), or Additional Targeted Support and Improvement
(ATSI), to develop a school
plan.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[Assembly Bill 716 revised California Education Code sections
64001-65001 to streamline and align state and federal planning processes
and organized federal school planning requirements in
ESSA.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[The development of the School Plan for Student Achievement (SPSA) is
required to include the
following:]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

- [[A comprehensive needs assessment (pursuant to
  ESSA)]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}
  - [[Analysis of verifiable state data, consistent with state
    priorities, including state-determined long term
    goals]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}
  - [[The plan may include local
    data]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}
- [[An identification of the process of evaluating and monitoring the
  implementation of the School Plan and progress towards accomplishing
  the
  goals]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[The Juvenile Court, Community School, and Special Education programs
have prepared their school plans in accordance with school-wide program
stipulations.
]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[In accordance with California Education Code Section 52068(a)(4), a
county office of education superintendent must review the Local Control
and Accountability Plan (LCAP) and School Plans to ensure that the two
plans are consistent. An integrated approach to planning ensures a
consistent alignment between the LCAP and School Plans. As  result, the
goals and relevant metrics outlined in the 2022-2023 Juvenile Court,
Community School, and Special Education SPSAs are aligned to the
2022-2023 Sacramento County Office of Education
LCAP.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}
