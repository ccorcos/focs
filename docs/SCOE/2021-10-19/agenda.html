Grant/Contract Proposal Abstract

 

Title of Grant/Contract: American Rescue Plan — Homeless Children and Youth

 

Department/Director: Educational Services/Brent Malicote

 

Funding Source: California Department of Education

 

Amount Requested: $487,274

 

Fiscal Year(s): 2021-2022, 2022-2023, 2023-2024

 

Program Description: 

For the 2021-2022, 2022-2023, and 2023-2024 program years, the Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) will receive one-time American Rescue Plan for Homeless Children and Youth (ARP-HYC) funds of $487,274 from the California Department of Education/Integrated Student Support & Programs Office (CDE/ISSPO). The funds will be used to facilitate equal access to public education by providing technical assistance and support to local education agencies (LEAs) and community partners through SCOE’s Project TEACH program.

 

These funds will allow Project TEACH to increase program capacity to provide services that include: training on the McKinney-Vento Assistance Act for LEA homeless liaisons and community partners; supporting LEA identification and enrollment of students, resolving eligibility and school of origin disputes; facilitating collaboration among LEAs and community partners; representing the needs of homeless youth in the community; and providing outreach to Sacramento County families and youth experiencing homelessness.

 

New Positions:  

  • Project Specialist I

Subcontracts:

  • None

Evaluation Component: 

SCOE will submit a budget, expenditure reports, and a year-end program report annually to CDE/ISSPO.

BACKGROUND:

 

The Sacramento County Board of Education (Board) has held meetings via teleconference from April 2020 through September 2021 in compliance with Governors' Executive Orders, which relaxed certain legal requirements for Board meetings during the COVID-19 pandemic. The legal requirements for public meetings are set forth in Government Code section 54950, et. seq., which is known as the “Brown Act.”  The Executive Orders expired on September 30, 2021. However, recently enacted Assembly Bill 361 amended elements of the Brown Act to permit local agencies to continue holding teleconferenced meetings using simplified procedures during a proclaimed state of emergency.  

Under Government Code section 54953(e), in order to continue holding teleconferenced board meetings using simplified Brown Act procedures, local agencies, including school districts and county boards of education, must make the following findings by majority vote and readopt such findings every 30 days thereafter:

  1. The board has reconsidered the circumstances of the state of emergency, AND
  2. The state of emergency continues to directly impact the ability of members to meet safely in person, OR state or local officials continue to impose or recommend measures to promote social distancing.

In the event the Board decides to continue to hold teleconferenced Board meetings using abbreviated Brown Act procedures, Resolution No. 21-13 is attached for the Board's consideration. 

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:

 

The Superintendent recommends that the Board consider and decide whether to adopt Resolution No. 21-13, which would permit it to continue holding teleconferenced Board meetings under abbreviated Brown Act procedures.

BACKGROUND:

 

Elementary and Secondary School Emergency Relief Fund (ESSER) III funds are part of the American Rescue Plan (ARP) Act for Fiscal Year 2020-2021 and are to be obligated by September 30, 2024.

 

PROGRAM DESCRIPTION:

 

ESSER III funds are part of the ARP Act which was signed into law on March 11, 2021 (Public Law 117-2). ESSER III funds are apportioned to local educational agencies (LEAs) to address the impact of COVID-19 on elementary and secondary schools. SCOE received an allocation of $11,701,506.

 

Per the ESSER requirements, SCOE has developed a plan detailing the intended use of ESSER III funds to address students' academic, social, emotional, and mental health needs, as well as any opportunity gaps that existed before, and were made worse by the COVID-19 pandemic. The ESSER III plan includes an overview, stakeholder input, planned actions, and initial planned progress monitoring. Also included in the plan are proposed actions to support student learning loss. In developing the ESSER III plan, SCOE has included community and staff input and/or actions included in our Local Control and Accountability Plan (LCAP).

 

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:

 

The Superintendent recommends that the Board of Education approve the submission of the ESSER III Expenditure Plan for the 2021-2022 school year. These funds are to be obligated by September 30, 2024.
