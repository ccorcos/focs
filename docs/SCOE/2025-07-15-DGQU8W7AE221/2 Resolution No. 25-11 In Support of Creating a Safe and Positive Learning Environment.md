                         SACRAMENTO COUNTY BOARD OF EDUCATION

     Resolution in Support of Creating a Safe and Positive Learning Environment
                                                Resolution No. 25-11
                                                      July 15, 2025

WHEREAS, The Sacramento County Board of Education (Board) and Sacramento
County Superintendent of Schools (Superintendent) are committed to the success of all
pupils in Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) schools and programs,
regardless of the immigration status of the pupils or their family members, and believe
that every SCOE school and program should be an inclusive, welcoming, safe
environment for pupils and their families;
WHEREAS, All pupils have a right to attend school free of discrimination, harassment,
or intimidation;

WHEREAS, The U.S. Supreme Court has held, in Plyler v. Doe, 457 U.S. 202 (1982),
that children have a right to attend school regardless of their immigration status and
states cannot constitutionally deny pupils a free public education because of the
immigration status of the pupils or their families;

WHEREAS, Public schools are vital for the preservation of our democratic system of
government and are essential for maintaining the fabric of our society. Our community
is strengthened when all students are educated so that they may achieve their highest
potential and contribute to the good of all.

WHEREAS, School officials shall not report undocumented pupils or their families to
U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) because such reporting could
discourage students from enrolling in or attending school, and could effectively deny
them a public education in violation of the Constitution and state and federal civil rights
laws;

WHEREAS, Actual or perceived ICE activities in and around schools and educational
facilities create a severe disruption to the learning environment and educational setting
for pupils;

WHEREAS, Local educational agencies should not collect or maintain documents that
may be related to immigration status, including, but not limited to, passports, visas, and
Social Security numbers, as they are not needed for K-12 educational purposes, may
discourage students from enrolling in school, and may be subject to requests from
federal agents;

WHEREAS, Federal regulations and state law prohibit educational agencies from
disclosing personally identifiable pupil information to anyone, including law enforcement,
without consent of a parent or guardian, a court order or lawful subpoena, or in the case
of a health or safety emergency;

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, The Board and Superintendent reaffirm that
pursuant to federal and state law, every pupil served in SCOE schools and programs
has the right to attend those schools and programs regardless of immigration status;

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That the Board and Superintendent confirm that all
pupils enrolled in SCOE schools and programs, regardless of immigration status, have
the right to receive all educational and support services, and that SCOE personnel shall
not take actions that would deny access to these services based upon the immigration
status of the pupils or their family members;

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, In order to provide a public education, regardless of
immigration status, absent any applicable federal, state, local law or regulation, local
ordinance, or court decision that may lawfully provide otherwise, SCOE shall act
consistent with the following practices:

1. SCOE shall not treat pupils differently on the basis of their actual or perceived
immigration status and shall treat all pupils equitably in the receipt of all school services
for which they are eligible.

2. SCOE shall not enter into any agreement with ICE to enforce federal immigration law,
and shall not voluntarily participate in any ICE or Border Patrol enforcement actions.

3. SCOE shall not inquire about a pupil's or parent’s immigration status or require
documentation of legal status, such as asking for a visa or passport, during enrollment
or at any other time.

4. Consistent with Education Code section 49076.7, SCOE shall not require pupils or
parents to apply for Social Security numbers nor collect or solicit Social Security
numbers, including the last four digits of Social Security numbers, unless otherwise
required by state or federal law.

5. For enrollment and other purposes, SCOE shall not require birth certificates, and
shall also accept the forms of evidence listed in Education Code section 48002,
including baptismal certificates, parent affidavits, or other reliable means of establishing
the pupil’s age.

6. All requests by immigration enforcement agents for personally identifiable information
or documents regarding SCOE pupils shall immediately be sent to the Superintendent,
who in consultation with SCOE legal counsel, shall consider all legally permissible
actions that may be taken to respond to such requests to protect the privacy rights of
pupils and their families.

7. The Superintendent shall provide information to immigrant pupils and families so that
they may continue to attend school and exercise their right to education.

8. The Superintendent shall ensure that SCOE teachers, school administrators, and all
other appropriate SCOE personnel are adequately trained on this Resolution's
directives and on the provisions of SCOE Administrative Rules and Regulations
5145.13.

9. On an ongoing basis, the Superintendent, Board, and SCOE’s legal counsel shall
review changes in federal immigration laws, policies, and practices, and California law
to assess the impact on the educational rights of SCOE students and families.
