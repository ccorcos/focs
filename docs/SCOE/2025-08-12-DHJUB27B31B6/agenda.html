Public Comment

 

Anyone may appear at the Board meeting to provide public comment regarding any item on the agenda or regarding any matter that is within the Board’s subject matter jurisdiction. However, the Board may not take action on any item not on the agenda except as authorized by Government Code section 54954.2.  If possible, notify the Board President or Board Secretary in writing prior to the meeting if you wish to testify.

 

Individuals wishing to speak in person before the Board are requested to complete a public comment form. Speakers will be called in the order in which the cards are received. 

 

Public comment via Zoom teleconference may be made by using the raised hand feature in Zoom during the agenda item to be addressed. The speaker will be identified by their Display Name in Zoom when called upon to speak. Those joining the meeting via Zoom are requested to display their full name so there is no confusion when called upon to speak. Public comments will be received until the public comment period has elapsed.

 

Comments are limited to a maximum of two (2) minutes per speaker per agenda item, and individuals are limited to a single comment per agenda item.

 

Please contact boardcomments@scoe.net before the meeting if you have questions about the public comment process. 

 

Accommodating Individuals with Disabilities

 

The Sacramento County Office of Education encourages those with disabilities to participate fully in the public meeting process. If you need a disability-related modification or accommodation, including auxiliary aids or services, to participate in the public meeting, contact the Superintendent’s Office at (916) 228-2410 at least 48 hours before the scheduled Board meeting so that we may make every reasonable effort to accommodate you. [Government Code § 54953.2; Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990, § 202 (42 U.S.C. § 12132).]

BACKGROUND:

 

The California Department of Education Consolidated Application Part I allows school districts and county offices of education to apply for available federal categorical program funding. This apportionment is made from federal funds provided to the state. This year the Sacramento County Office of Education will apply for four (4) federally funded programs listed on the Consolidated Application for 2025-2026.

 

Federal Programs

  • Title I Part A: Improving Basic Programs Operated by Local Educational Agencies - preliminary award amount is estimated at $1,324,664 for the 2025-2026 school year.
  • Title I Part D, Subpart 2: Prevention and Intervention Programs for Children and Youth Who Are Neglected, Delinquent, or At-Risk - preliminary award amount is estimated at $755,832 for the 2025-2026 school year.
  • Title II Part A: Supporting Effective Instruction of the Elementary and Secondary Education Act of 1965 (ESEA), as amended by Every Student Succeeds Act (ESSA) (Public Law 114-95) preliminary award amount is estimated at $29,104 for the 2025-2026 school year.
  • Title IV Part A: Student and School Support (ESSA Sec.4101) - preliminary award amount is estimated at $99,297 for the 2025-2026 school year.

 

Title I Programs

Title I Part A funds are used to support effective, evidence-based educational strategies that close the achievement gap and enable students to meet the state's challenging academic standards.

 

Title I Part D provides funds to improve educational services for formerly incarcerated youth or for neglected youth so that such youth have the opportunity to meet the same challenging state academic standards that all youth in the state are expected to meet. Additionally, Title ID funds may be used to provide such youth with the services needed to make a successful transition from incarceration to further schooling or employment.

 

Title II Part A Program funds are apportioned to local educational agencies (LEA) to increase student academic achievement through strategies focused on preparing, training, and recruiting high-quality teachers, principals, or other school leaders.

 

Title IV Part A: Student Support and Academic Enrichment: A Federal program to increase the capacity of LEAs to meet the goals of the ESSA by providing all students with access to a well-rounded education, improving school conditions for student learning, and improving use of technology in order to improve the academic achievement and digital literacy of all students. 

 

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:

 

The Superintendent recommends that the Board approve the submission of the Consolidated Application Part I and the acceptance of funds allocated for the 2025-2026 school year.

Department/Cabinet Member: Prevention and Early Intervention/Brent Malicote

 

Funding Source: Sacramento County Department of Health Care Services

 

Amount Requested: $522,500

 

Fiscal Year(s): 2025-2026

 

Program Description: 

The Sacramento County Department of Health Services (DHS), with Mental Health Services Act (MHSA) Prevention and Early Intervention funds, will contract with the SCOE to continue providing school-based bullying prevention and mental health wellness projects. In coordination with DHS, school districts, and the Student Mental Health and Wellness Collaborative, SCOE will:

  • Maintain and supplement bullying and cyberbullying prevention activities at established elementary demonstration schools
  • Conduct training and evaluation activities
  • Serve as liaison for community-based providers
  • Attend the Student Mental Health and Wellness Collaborative meetings
  • Provide Youth Mental Health First Aid (YMHFA) trainings to district and school staff, parents, youth-service law enforcement officers, and community-based organizational staff
  • Provide Question, Persuade, Refer (QPR) for Suicide Prevention trainings
  • Work collaboratively with designated school districts to conduct YMHFA and QPR trainings to district and/or site staff

 

New Positions: None

 

Subcontracts:

  • Agreements with each of the 13 school districts in Sacramento County ranging from $6,000 to $19,900 based on district student enrollment and scope of work from the Bullying Prevention Project.
  • Agreements with three selected school districts in Sacramento County ranging from $5,630 to $22,085 based on the number of trainings to be conducted for the YMHFA program.
  • Agreements with five selected school districts in Sacramento County for $3,000 each to support QPR training efforts.

 

Evaluation Component: 

SCOE’s Prevention & Early Intervention department will coordinate with SCOE’s Planning and Improvement Department, district leads, demonstration sites, YMHFA and QPR trainers to measure the following goals:

 

Bullying Prevention Program 

  • Increase awareness among school staff of the negative effects of bullying
  • Increase ability by school personnel to learn specific techniques to intervene early
  • Increase shared collaboration among school districts, SCOE, Behavioral Health Services (BHS), community partners, and family members
  • Develop best practices and policies regarding bullying and other educational efforts as part of a comprehensive school improvement and safety plan
  • Improve students’ perception of school safety

 

Youth Mental Health First Aid and Question, Persuade, Refer Program

  • Increase participant recognition of the signs that a young person may be dealing with a mental health crisis or addiction challenge or crisis
  • Increase participant ability to reach out to a young person who may be dealing with a mental health crisis or addiction challenge or crisis
  • Increase participant awareness of biased views and feelings about mental health problems and disorders
  • Increase participant knowledge of mental health supports and treatment resources

Funding Source: Sacramento County Department of Health Care Services

 

Amount Requested: $515,582

 

Fiscal Year(s): 2025-2026

 

Program Description: 

The Sacramento County Division of Behavioral Health Services, Alcohol and Drug Services, will renew its contract with SCOE to support the Friday Night Live (FNL), Club Live (CL), and Friday Night Live Mentoring (FNLM) programs. FNL/CL programs and services are available to high school and middle school students throughout Sacramento County. These programs include on-campus chapters supported by FNL/CL staff members, leadership training, and opportunities for teens, school-wide and countywide events, and professional development opportunities and program support for adult school-site advisors.

 

FNL Mentoring is a cross-age mentoring program that matches trained and supervised high school students with middle/junior high school students who are struggling socially and/or academically. Statewide evaluation of the program has demonstrated improved personal, academic, and social outcomes for proteges and mentors.

 

New Positions: None
 

Subcontracts: None
 

Evaluation Component: 

SCOE will utilize the required online ECCO system to submit data, and use the FNL Youth Development Survey developed by SCOE’s Planning and Improvement Department, and the California Healthy Kids Survey tools to measure:

  • Decreased binge drinking among youth ages 12-20 
  • Decreased number of youth who report drinking three or more days in the last 30 days
  • Increased number of participants who report never having tried alcohol
  • Increased number of participants who report the program helps them feel more connected to their community and committed to school
  • Increased number of participants who indicate they have opportunities for leadership within the program
  • Increased number of students who report involvement in the program helped them learn reasons why they should not use alcohol, tobacco, and other drugs
  • Increased knowledge among mentors and proteges of the risk of harm of alcohol, tobacco, and marijuana

Department/Cabinet Member: Educational Services/Dr. Channa Pitt

 

Funding Source: Gates Foundation

 

Amount Requested: $699,960

 

Fiscal Year(s): 2025-2026

 

Program Description: 

This project will support school districts through the comprehensive adoption of high-quality mathematics instructional materials. Grounded in the California Math Framework, SCOE's approach will emphasize alignment with state standards, including mathematical practices and English Language Development standards, while integrating research-based best practices, cultural and linguistic responsiveness, and the unique vision of each Local Education Agency (LEA). Over the course of the year, districts will receive structured guidance that promotes spiraling content, just-in-time learning, and culturally responsive pedagogy.  

 

To meet districts where they are in the adoption process, LEAs may engage in one of two interconnected pathways. The California Math Framework Foundation pathway offers a year-long professional learning experience for lead educators, focused on the Framework's three dimensions of systemic change: asset-based approaches, active engagement through investigation, and cultural and personal relevance. The Ready to Review pathway builds on this foundation, guiding lead educators through piloting and evaluating instructional materials using the same guiding principles, with an emphasis on ensuring alignment with the framework's vision and the diverse needs of all learners. Though each pathway serves a distinct phase of the process, they are intentionally connected to provide coherence, continuity, and a strong foundation for successful implementation. Throughout the project, adoption teams comprised of teachers, instructional leaders, and key decision makers, will collaborate with the SCOE Math Team to analyze the Framework's recommendations and review curriculum options with technical support and thought partnership. By fostering deep mathematical thinking, and creating learning experiences that reflect students' identities and lived experiences, LEAs will be empowered to select materials that not only meet rigorous academic standards, but also support meaningful, student-centered mathematics learning for all. 

 

New Positions:  Certificated: Curriculum Specialist, Mathematics

 

Subcontract:

SCOE will enter into subcontracts, as necessary, to ensure the successful implementation of the grant. The subcontracts will support the completion of tasks outlined in the grant's scope of work and will help fulfill all deliverables in alignment with the expectations and requirements set forth by the grantor. 

 

Evaluation Component:  

The evaluation of this project will focus on district-level progress and the broader insights gathered to inform and enhance future adoption efforts. Success will be measured through three key areas of impact: 1. A development of a district -level mathematics vision statement; 2. The adoption of high-quality instructional materials and aligned professional learning; and 3. Informing broader systems-level understanding of the adoption process. This will include the development of a High-Quality Instructional Materials Adoption Process Resource Hub which will serve as a guide for other districts and agencies seeking to undertake similar work.

Program Description:

SCOE has submitted a grant application to serve as a California Community Schools Partnership (CCSPP) Regional Transformational Assistance Center (R-TAC). In partnership with Placer County Office of Education (PCOE), SCOE will serve as a co-lead for the Capitol Area Community Schools Consortium (CACSC). CACSC consists of twelve county offices of education, including Alpine, Colusa, El Dorado, Nevada, Placer, Sacramento, San Joaquin, Sierra, Solano, Sutter, Yolo, and Yuba. 

 

SCOE and PCOE will continue to lead the consortium's strategic direction to develop and implement a high-quality CCSPP R-TAC to support community schools across the Capitol Area region. The consortium is partnering with three renowned and established non-profit organizations: Community Schools Learning Exchange, Center for Whole-Child Education, and Learning Heroes. CACSC and its key partners will collaborate to reach its goals and expected outcomes, by co-creating the design of the regional technical assistance system and implementation of the strategic plan to sustain and scale community schools. Customized technical assistance and partnerships will be provided via consultation, training, coaching, and regional capacity building to support high-quality systems improvement at the county, district, and school levels.

NOTICE OF PUBLIC HEARING SCHEDULED FOR TUESDAY, AUGUST 12, 2025 AT 6:30 P.M. or thereafter

Sacramento County Office of Education

David P. Meaney Education Center

10474 Mather Boulevard

Mather, CA  95655

In accordance with the requirements of Education Code Section 60119(c), the Sacramento County Board of Education (Board) will hold a public hearing during the August 12, 2025 Regular Board Meeting to determine whether each pupil in the juvenile court schools has sufficient textbooks or instructional materials, or both, in English/language arts, including the English language development component of an adopted program, mathematics, history/social science, and science that are aligned to the state content standards adopted by the State Board of Education (SBE).

As part of this determination, the Board also must determine if each pupil who is enrolled in a foreign language or health course has sufficient instructional materials that are consistent with the content and cycles of curriculum frameworks adopted by the SBE. In addition, the Board must determine if pupils enrolled in a laboratory science course have adequate equipment.

Pupils enrolled in a health course will have sufficient materials. SCOE does not offer foreign language instruction in any grades, nor does SCOE offer science laboratory courses in any of grades 9 through 12. Therefore, SCOE need not maintain science laboratory equipment. Completion of a science laboratory course is not a requirement for high school graduation, though completion of a course in either foreign language or visual or performing arts is a requirement (EC 51225.3). As necessary for completion of this graduation requirement, SCOE students are provided a course in fine arts.

Detail substantiating that sufficient instructional materials are available to each student will be available for inspection by the Board and public at the hearing.

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:

The Superintendent recommends that the Board adopt Resolution No. 25-12 Determination of Textbooks and/or Instructional Materials Sufficiency Pursuant to Education Code Section 60119 for Juvenile Court Schools.
