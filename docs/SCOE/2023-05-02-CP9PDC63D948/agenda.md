[[**Lily Chu**, Program Analyst, College and Career Readiness (C&CR)
Department, was nominated by Jared Amalong, Director, Computer Science
Education and Distance Learning. Lily supports two multi-million-dollar
statewide projects to broaden student participation in Computer Science
(CS). Her attention to detail, paired with her compassion for the
students, educators, and professionals involved in the projects is
inspiring. Lily has a natural ability to build relationships of mutual
respect with her colleagues in the C&CR Department, as well as partners
from UCLA, the Small School District\'s Association, and the CDE
Foundation. Lily is also creative and analytical; recently, she designed
a registration system that features a user-friendly experience that
matches a potential participant with one of over 40 workshops that are
offered through the statewide CS projects. In sum, Lily\'s positive
impact in SCOE and organizations across the state is significant; given
her noted impact, she is being recognized as the SCOE Classified
Employee of the Month. Lily Chu has been a SCOE Employee since May
2020.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

[[**Carrie Tilley**, Teacher for Students with extensive support needs,
Special Education Department, was nominated by Area Principal Jillian
Friend. Carrie is a special education teacher in the Foundations Academy
programs at Rutter Middle School and has worked in this capacity at SCOE
for 20 years. Carrie is a dedicated teacher, advocate, and colleague.
She maintains good communication with families and works hard to support
families as they navigate the sometimes-cumbersome Individualized
Education Plan process. Carrie has demonstrated resilience not only
through the pandemic as an Independent Study teacher but has also been a
trooper this year amid a great staffing shortage. Her dedication to
SCOE, her students and their families is so appreciated! Carrie Tilley
has been a SCOE Employee since August
2002. ]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}
