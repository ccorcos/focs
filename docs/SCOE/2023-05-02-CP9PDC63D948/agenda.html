Public Comment

 

Anyone may appear at the Board meeting to provide public comment regarding any item on the agenda or regarding any matter that is within the Board’s subject matter jurisdiction. However, the Board may not take action on any item not on the agenda except as authorized by Government Code section 54954.2. If possible, notify the Board President or Board Secretary in writing prior to the meeting if you wish to testify.

 

Individuals wishing to speak in person before the Board are requested to complete a public comment form. Speakers will be called in the order in which the cards are received. 

 

Public comment via Zoom teleconference may be made by using the raised hand feature in Zoom during the agenda item to be addressed. The speaker will be identified by their Display Name in Zoom when called upon to speak. Those joining the meeting via Zoom are requested to display their full name so there is no confusion when called upon to speak. Public comments will be received until the public comment period has elapsed.

 

Comments are limited to a maximum of two (2) minutes per speaker per agenda item, and individuals are limited to a single comment per agenda item.

 

Please contact boardcomments@scoe.net before the meeting if you have questions about the public comment process. 

 

Accommodating Individuals with Disabilities

 

The Sacramento County Office of Education encourages those with disabilities to participate fully in the public meeting process. If you need a disability-related modification or accommodation, including auxiliary aids or services, to participate in the public meeting, contact the Superintendent’s Office at (916) 228-2410 at least 48 hours before the scheduled Board meeting so that we may make every reasonable effort to accommodate you. [Government Code § 54953.2; Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990, § 202 (42 U.S.C. § 12132).]

BACKGROUND: 

 

CLASSIFIED 

 

Lily Chu, Program Analyst, College and Career Readiness (C&CR) Department, was nominated by Jared Amalong, Director, Computer Science Education and Distance Learning. Lily supports two multi-million-dollar statewide projects to broaden student participation in Computer Science (CS). Her attention to detail, paired with her compassion for the students, educators, and professionals involved in the projects is inspiring. Lily has a natural ability to build relationships of mutual respect with her colleagues in the C&CR Department, as well as partners from UCLA, the Small School District's Association, and the CDE Foundation. Lily is also creative and analytical; recently, she designed a registration system that features a user-friendly experience that matches a potential participant with one of over 40 workshops that are offered through the statewide CS projects. In sum, Lily's positive impact in SCOE and organizations across the state is significant; given her noted impact, she is being recognized as the SCOE Classified Employee of the Month. Lily Chu has been a SCOE Employee since May 2020.

 

CERTIFICATED

 

Carrie Tilley, Teacher for Students with extensive support needs, Special Education Department, was nominated by Area Principal Jillian Friend. Carrie is a special education teacher in the Foundations Academy programs at Rutter Middle School and has worked in this capacity at SCOE for 20 years. Carrie is a dedicated teacher, advocate, and colleague. She maintains good communication with families and works hard to support families as they navigate the sometimes-cumbersome Individualized Education Plan process. Carrie has demonstrated resilience not only through the pandemic as an Independent Study teacher but has also been a trooper this year amid a great staffing shortage. Her dedication to SCOE, her students and their families is so appreciated! Carrie Tilley has been a SCOE Employee since August 2002.

On January 14, 2020, the Board authorized deferred maintenance and modernization plans for various SCOE sites. ProEx Construction

Amount: $238,000

Funding Source: County Schools Facility Fund

 

With approval from the Board, SCOE will be authorized to contract with additional consultants and retain additional services including, but not limited to, project inspection, materials testing, traffic management, site security, mechanical system commissioning and calibration, and technology installations and connections.

The Superintendent recommends the Board authorize staff to award the construction contract to the selected contractor, enter into such additional contracts as necessary, hire additional consultants, and retain any services required to bring the projects to completion.

Department/Director: College and Career Readiness/Jacqueline White

 

Funding Source: California Department of Education/Educator Excellence and Equity Division (CDE/EEED)

 

Amount Requested: $15,000,000

 

Fiscal Year(s): 2022-2023, 2023-2024, 2024-2025

 

Program Description: 

Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) will receive a grant award from the CDE/EEED to provide targeted support focused on strategies for high-quality Computer Science (CS) instruction and learning experiences aligned to California K12 CS Content Standards (CA K12 CSS). The Computer Science Program will assist in building the capacity of LEAs across the state through professional learning opportunities for teachers, paraprofessionals, school leaders, and counselors that pertains to strategies for high-quality instruction and CS learning experiences.

 

New Positions: None

 

Subcontracts:

  • CDE Foundation, in the amount of $618,438 to provide project leadership, strategic planning, project implementation, and operational supports
  • UCLA, in the amount of $600,000 to plan and deliver a statewide community of practice for participating COE staff
  • San Bernardino County Superintendent of Schools, in the amount of $600,000 to serve as a COE co-lead, and to provide project leadership, strategic planning, project implementation, and operational supports
  • 7 Partner COEs (TBD), in the amount of $5,250,000 to serve as Regional Lead Agencies responsible for organizing and delivering regional professional learning offerings
  • CS Curriculum Providers in the amount of $1,892,400 to design and facilitate the CS professional learning content
  • American Institute for Research, in the amount of $600,000 to provide research and evaluation services

 

Evaluation Component: 

In addition to the quarterly expenditure reports to be submitted to CDE/EEED, SCOE will submit measurable outcomes identified in the application narrative that include:

  • Educator Professional Learning Participation
  • Educator Experiences with Professional Learning
  • Development and Reach of CS Champions (Staff at COEs tasked with supporting CS Implementation)
  • Shifts in Teacher Practice
  • Changes in Student Access to CS Education

BACKGROUND:

 

California Day of the Teacher is a special recognition of the important role that teachers have in the lives of students across the state. Day of the Teacher is modeled after Dia del Maestro, a cherished Latin American tradition that formally recognizes educators. Because teachers are held in the highest esteem, everyone, from the youngest school children to the oldest generation of citizens, is asked to take the time to thank the teachers in their lives.

 

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:
