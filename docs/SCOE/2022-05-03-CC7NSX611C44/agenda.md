[[**BACKGROUND:**]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[The Every Student Succeeds Act (ESSA) requires schools receiving Title
I funds and operating a school wide program, or schools identified for
Comprehensive Support and Improvement (CSI), Targeted Support and
Improvement (TSI) or Additional Targeted Support and Improvement (ATSI),
to develop a school plan.  Assembly Bill 716 revised California
Education Code sections 64001-65001 to streamline and align state and
federal planning processes and organized federal school planning
requirements in
ESSA.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[The development of the School Plan for Student Achievement (SPSA) is
required to include the
following:]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

- [[A comprehensive needs assessment (pursuant to
  ESSA)]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}
  - [[Analysis of verifiable state data, consistent with state
    priorities, including state-determined long term
    goals]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}
  - [[The plan may include local
    data]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}
- [[An identification of the process for evaluating and monitoring the
  implementation of the School Plan and progress towards accomplishing
  the
  goals]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[The Juvenile Court and Community School programs have prepared their
school plans in accordance with School-wide Program
stipulations.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[In accordance with California Education Code Section 52068(a)(4), a
county office of education superintendent must review the Local Control
and Accountability Plan (LCAP) and School Plans to ensure that the two
plans are consistent. An integrated approach to planning ensures a
consistent alignment between the LCAP and School Plans. As a result, the
goals and relevant metrics outlined in the 2021-22 Juvenile Court and
Community School SPSAs are aligned to the 2021-22 Sacramento County
Office of Education
LCAP.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

RECOMMENDATION:**]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[The Superintendent recommends that the Board approve each School Plan

[[Attached is the new proposed Board Policy 3311.1 -- Uniform Public
Construction Cost Accounting Procedures. 
]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[SCOE\'s Business Services Office is recommending that SCOE opt into
the California Uniform Construction Cost Account Act (CUPCCAA) to help
SCOE timely and efficiently complete small construction projects.
CUPCCAA will enable SCOE to use its own work force for small projects
and will provide more opportunities to contract with local businesses.
Opting into CUPCCAA requires the Board to adopt the new policy and elect
by resolution to become subject to the Act. The proposed resolution will
be presented when this policy is before the Board on second reading. 
 ]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[[[[[[[[[[[***[[[[[What is
CUPCCAA?]{style="font-variant-ligatures:none !important"}]{style="-webkit-tap-highlight-color:transparent"}]{style="-webkit-user-drag:none"}]{contrast="auto"
style="user-select:text"
lang="EN-US"}]{.underline}***]{style="-webkit-tap-highlight-color:transparent"}]{style="-webkit-user-drag:none"}]{style="user-select:text"}* *]{style="font-kerning:none"}]{style="white-space:pre-wrap"}]{style="overflow-wrap:break-word"}]{style="-webkit-tap-highlight-color:transparent"}]{style="-webkit-user-drag:none"}]{style="user-select:text"}]{style="vertical-align:baseline"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[CUPCCAA was enacted in 1983 to help promote uniformity
in public entity construction projects in California. Almost 1,500
public entities in California are using CUPCCAA, including 691 school
districts and 67 community college districts. Public entities that elect
to become subject to CUPCCAA must adopt a resolution and policy, file a
copy of the resolution with the State Controller's Office, and comply
with the
]{style="-webkit-tap-highlight-color:transparent"}]{style="-webkit-user-drag:none"}]{style="user-select:text"}]{style="font-variant-ligatures:none !important"}]{style="-webkit-tap-highlight-color:transparent"}]{style="-webkit-user-drag:none"}]{contrast="auto"
style="user-select:text" lang="EN-US"}Uniform Public Construction Cost
Accounting Procedures. [[[[[[[CUPCCAA only governs bidding and cost
accounting procedures for construction projects. Other applicable public
contracting requirements will still apply, such as paying prevailing
wages.
]{style="-webkit-tap-highlight-color:transparent"}]{style="-webkit-user-drag:none"}]{style="user-select:text"}]{style="font-variant-ligatures:none !important"}]{style="-webkit-tap-highlight-color:transparent"}]{style="-webkit-user-drag:none"}]{contrast="auto"

[[[[[[[[[[[[***[[[[[Key Provisions of
CUPCCAA]{style="font-variant-ligatures:none !important"}]{style="-webkit-tap-highlight-color:transparent"}]{style="-webkit-user-drag:none"}]{contrast="auto"
style="user-select:text"
lang="EN-US"}]{.underline}***]{style="-webkit-tap-highlight-color:transparent"}]{style="-webkit-user-drag:none"}]{style="user-select:text"}* *]{style="font-kerning:none"}]{style="white-space:pre-wrap"}]{style="overflow-wrap:break-word"}]{style="-webkit-tap-highlight-color:transparent"}]{style="-webkit-user-drag:none"}]{style="user-select:text"}]{style="vertical-align:baseline"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[CUPCCAA would allow SCOE to timely execute small
construction projects, expedite project delivery, and would provide
greater access to local businesses. SCOE could use its own workforce to
complete construction projects costing \$60,000 or less, or negotiate a
contract with a local business or other qualified contractor. SCOE could
utilize an informal bid process for projects costing \$200,000 or less,
and formal bidding would be required for projects costing more than
\$200,000. Currently, public projects costing \$15,000 and more require
formal
bidding.]{style="-webkit-tap-highlight-color:transparent"}]{style="-webkit-user-drag:none"}]{style="user-select:text"}]{style="font-variant-ligatures:none !important"}]{style="-webkit-tap-highlight-color:transparent"}]{style="-webkit-user-drag:none"}]{contrast="auto"
style="user-select:text"
lang="EN-US"}]{style="font-kerning:none"}]{style="white-space:pre-wrap"}]{style="overflow-wrap:break-word"}]{style="-webkit-tap-highlight-color:transparent"}]{style="-webkit-user-drag:none"}]{style="user-select:text"}]{style="vertical-align:baseline"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[CUPCCAA would enable SCOE to timely complete small
construction projects and would allow for greater flexibility in the
scheduling of projects. While the formal bid process takes 3.5-4 months,
the contracting process under CUPCCAA would be much shorter for projects
less than \$200,000 (i.e., [[[[[[[within two weeks for projects less
than \$60,000, and
]{style="-webkit-tap-highlight-color:transparent"}]{style="-webkit-user-drag:none"}]{style="user-select:text"}]{style="font-variant-ligatures:none !important"}]{style="-webkit-tap-highlight-color:transparent"}]{style="-webkit-user-drag:none"}]{contrast="auto"
style="user-select:text"
lang="EN-US"}]{style="-webkit-tap-highlight-color:transparent"}]{style="-webkit-user-drag:none"}]{style="user-select:text"}]{style="font-variant-ligatures:none !important"}]{style="-webkit-tap-highlight-color:transparent"}]{style="-webkit-user-drag:none"}]{contrast="auto"
style="user-select:text" lang="EN-US"}[within three or four weeks for
projects less than \$200,000 using the informal bid
process).]{style="font-variant-ligatures:none"}]{style="font-kerning:none"}]{style="white-space:pre-wrap"}]{style="overflow-wrap:break-word"}]{style="-webkit-tap-highlight-color:transparent"}]{style="-webkit-user-drag:none"}]{style="user-select:text"}]{style="vertical-align:baseline"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[For the informal bid process, CUPCCAA requires SCOE to
establish or update a list of qualified contractors at least once per
calendar year by sending out a notice to designated trade journals
inviting licensed contractors to submit their names. SCOE would also
post the notice with local trade journals and with various groups to
attract a diverse pool of contractors. Qualified contractors must be
placed on the list and notified by SCOE of opportunities to bid on
construction projects. The lowest responsive bidder would be awarded the
contract.
]{style="-webkit-tap-highlight-color:transparent"}]{style="-webkit-user-drag:none"}]{style="user-select:text"}]{style="font-variant-ligatures:none !important"}]{style="-webkit-tap-highlight-color:transparent"}]{style="-webkit-user-drag:none"}]{contrast="auto"
style="user-select:text"
lang="EN-US"}]{style="font-kerning:none"}]{style="white-space:pre-wrap"}]{style="overflow-wrap:break-word"}]{style="-webkit-tap-highlight-color:transparent"}]{style="-webkit-user-drag:none"}]{style="user-select:text"}]{style="vertical-align:baseline"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[Creating a list of qualified contractors will provide
SCOE with greater access to local businesses. SCOE must currently
utilize CMAS and other cooperative agreements for small, time-sensitive
construction projects. These lists enable SCOE to timely complete
projects while complying with public contracting requirements, but they
do not have a large selection of local
contractors.]{style="-webkit-tap-highlight-color:transparent"}]{style="-webkit-user-drag:none"}]{style="user-select:text"}]{style="font-variant-ligatures:none !important"}]{style="-webkit-tap-highlight-color:transparent"}]{style="-webkit-user-drag:none"}]{contrast="auto"
style="user-select:text"
lang="EN-US"}]{style="font-kerning:none"}]{style="white-space:pre-wrap"}]{style="overflow-wrap:break-word"}]{style="-webkit-tap-highlight-color:transparent"}]{style="-webkit-user-drag:none"}]{style="user-select:text"}]{style="vertical-align:baseline"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[[[[[[[[In the last six months, SCOE has completed twelve construction
projects that were less than \$200,000. They encompass both public
projects and maintenance, and they include several flooring, roofing,
and demolition projects. SCOE has five pending construction projects,
including several that require structural and safety-related repairs
(e.g., roofing, siding, painting, and fire safety devices). CUPCCAA\'s
process would enable SCOE to complete those projects located at SCOE
school facilities during summer break while students are not on campus.
]{style="font-kerning:none"}]{style="white-space:pre-wrap"}]{style="overflow-wrap:break-word"}]{style="-webkit-tap-highlight-color:transparent"}]{style="-webkit-user-drag:none"}]{style="user-select:text"}]{style="vertical-align:baseline"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}
