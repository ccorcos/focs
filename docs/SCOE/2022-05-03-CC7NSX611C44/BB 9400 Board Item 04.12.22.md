Book                        Board Policies and ARRs

Section                     9000 Bylaws of the Board

Title                       Board Self-Evaluation

Code                        9400 BB

Status                      First Reading

Prior Revised Dates         03/15/22 Policy Committee Meeting



The Sacramento County Board of Education (County Board) may shall periodically conduct a self-
evaluation to demonstrate accountability to the community and ensure that its governance effectively
supports student achievement and attainment of the County Board's vision and goals.

The evaluation may address any areas of County Board responsibility, including the roles and
responsibilities outlined in Board Bylaw 9000. The evaluation may also address objectives relating to
County Board meeting operations, community relations, relationships among County Board members
and with the Sacramento County Superintendent of Schools (County Superintendent), understanding
of County Board and County Superintendent roles and responsibilities, or other boardsmanship skills.

The County Board may determine an evaluation method or instrument that measures a reasonable
number of identified performance objectives, and may measure how County Board interactions,
operations, and performance contribute to the mission, vision, and goals of the Sacramento County
Office of Education.

The County Board shall be evaluated as a whole and not as individual members. However, individual
County Board members may also be encouraged to use the evaluation process as an opportunity to
privately assess their own personal performances as a Board member.

Although the County Board President may request County Board members to complete an evaluation
instrument individually, discussion of the County Board's self-evaluation shall be conducted in open
session. A facilitator may be used to assist with the evaluation process. The County Board may also
invite the County Superintendent or others to provide input into the evaluation process.
