Grant/Contract Proposal Abstract

 

Title of Grant/Contract: Project Cal-HOPE Student Support

 

Department/Director: Educational Services/Brent Malicote

 

Funding Source: California Department of Health Care Services (DHCS)

 

Amount Requested: $45,000,000

 

Fiscal Year(s): 2021-2022, 2022-2023, 2023-2024

 

Program Description: 

The Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) has submitted a proposal to the California Department of Heath Care Services (DHCS) as a partner to support Social Emotional Learning (SEL) in a statewide Community of Practice (CoP). Cal-HOPE Student Support will enable California's schools to be leaders in supporting prevention and early interventions for students and families as we collectively respond to the mental health and wellness needs of students, families, and educators across California. Through a unique partnership with the California Department of Health Care Services, California Public Education System, University of California, Berkeley, and the Orange County Department of Education, California front-line educators will support families and children by responding to the trauma and isolation caused by the COVID-19 Pandemic. 

 

Our plan leverages each of the 58 county offices of education, supporting local districts and schools statewide, to prepare educators to be first-line responders to protect the psychological wellbeing of children and youth during the 2021-2022, 2022-2023, and 2023-2024 academic years. Professional development and technical assistance delivered regionally for educators will: (1) give teachers the mindset and skills to recover from their own traumas, restoring their capacity to serve the diverse and substantial needs of children; and (2) give teachers concrete strategies to work with children experiencing an ongoing crisis. 

 

The Northern California hub, led by SCOE, and the Southern Hub led by the Orange County Department of Education will ensure funding is quickly distributed to other hubs and CoPs for needed training and technical assistance. SCOE has identified The Greater Good Science Center (GGSC) as a lead partner in developing training and creating content for all partners. The GGSC at UC Berkeley will re-purpose and refine existing resources, create supplemental content, and deliver a high-quality, virtual professional development experience to California educators.

 

New Positions:  

Coordinator

 

Subcontracts:

  • County Offices of Education will participate in statewide community of practices and create local supports to build capacity in their districts.
  • University of California Berkeley GGSC will develop online curriculum and content to support a state-wide Community of Practice as well as local dissemination of content and strategies to districts and schools. 
  • Orange County Department of Education as the Southern "hub" will ensure funding is disseminated to other hubs in Southern California.
  • The Center for Implementation will support Implementation Science and strategic planning for all 58 COE's as they partner with schools and districts in their county.
  • LEA (TBD) will partner on a youth engagement SEL partnership.
  • Organization (TBD) will assisting in the development of a social emotional learning micro credential/ certificate.

Evaluation Component: 

UC Berkeley will evaluate the impact of this work for continuous quality improvement to contribute to the knowledge base of best practices.

BACKGROUND:

 

Recently enacted Assembly Bill 361 amended elements of the Brown Act (Government Code section 54950, et seq.) to permit local agencies to continue holding teleconferenced meetings using simplified procedures during a proclaimed state of emergency.  Under Government Code section 54953(e), in order to continue holding teleconferenced board meetings using simplified Brown Act procedures, local agencies, including school districts and county boards of education, must make the following findings by majority vote and readopt such findings every 30 days thereafter:

  1. The board has reconsidered the circumstances of the state of emergency, AND
  2. The state of emergency continues to directly impact the ability of members to meet safely in person, OR state or local officials continue to impose or recommend measures to promote social distancing.

In the event the Board decides to continue to hold teleconferenced Board meetings using abbreviated Brown Act procedures, Resolution No. 22-01 is attached for the Board's consideration.

 

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:

 

The Superintendent recommends that the Board consider and decide whether to adopt Resolution No. 22-01 which would permit it to continue holding teleconferenced Board meetings under abbreviated Brown Act procedures.

BACKGROUND:

 

On February 4, 2020, the Sacramento County Board of Education (Board) approved the American River Collegiate Academy (ARCA) Charter to serve students in grades K-12 within the San Juan Unified School District (SJUSD). The ARCA charter petition approved by the Board did not contemplate offering Transitional Kindergarten (TK) at the school. However, to provide access to full-day learning the year before kindergarten for families in the Citrus Heights and surrounding areas, ARCA would like to update its charter so that it may provide TK as an option for families.  

 

Under Education Code section 47605(a)(4), after receiving approval of its petition, a charter school that proposes to expand operations to additional grade levels must request a material revision to its charter, and the chartering authority must consider whether to approve the grade levels at an open, public meeting. ARCA has submitted the attached request for a material revision to its charter, which further explains its plans for offering TK and specifies the areas of the charter that would be revised if the Board approves its request.

 

While charter schools are not currently required to provide TK, Senate Bill (SB) 130 calls for the expansion of TK, and will gradually phase in TK so that it is available to all 4-year-old children by 2025-2026. While families are not required to enroll their children in TK, making TK available to more students supports the public policy of California.

 

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:

 

The Superintendent recommends that the Sacramento County Board of Education approve Resolution No. 22-02 approving the American River Collegiate Academy's request for a material revision to the charter so that it may offer Transitional Kindergarten as an option for families.
