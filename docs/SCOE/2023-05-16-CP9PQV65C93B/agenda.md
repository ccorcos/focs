[[**BACKGROUND: **]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[\"Operation Recognition\" is a program adopted by the Sacramento
County Board of Education and other organizations throughout the United
States to honor individuals whose secondary education was interrupted
due to circumstances of World War II, the Korean War, and the Vietnam
War. During the 1940s, and again in the 1950s and 1960s, thousands of
young men and women left high school to serve in the United States Armed
Forces. After these wars, many veterans were not able to finish high
school but led productive lives and helped build our communities over
their
lifetimes. ]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[Also, during World War II, thousands of Japanese American citizens
were forced to leave their homes to be interned in relocation camps at
various locations in the United States. These citizens were denied the
freedom other Americans enjoyed, and many served with the U.S. Armed
Forces. Some were unable to complete high school, yet they, too, went on
to build productive lives and have greatly contributed to the American
way of
life.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[Through the \"Operation Recognition\" program, the Sacramento County
Office of Education will provide a high school diploma to qualifying
World War II, Korean War, and Vietnam War veterans, and to Japanese
American citizens who were interned in World War II relocation camps.
This is a gesture of our society\'s gratitude for the sacrifices they
made in the name of
freedom. ]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[The Sacramento County Office of Education has received one application
from an individual who qualifies as a recipient of a high school diploma

[[The Capital Adult Education Regional Consortium (CAERC) is a regional
delivery system for adult education programs across the greater
Sacramento Region that provides education and workforce development
services to the adult members of our communities whose level of
education is below high school diploma and/or who need basic and
intermediate skills for local jobs. CAERC supports courses for adults
including short-term career technical education and pre-apprenticeship
programs. Perkins funding would support three consortium adult education
programs aiming to improve career-technical education programs,
integrate academic and career-technical instruction, serve special
populations, and meet gender equity

[[**BACKGROUND:**]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[The Local Child Care and Development Planning Council (LPC) is
requesting that the Board approve the appointment of Janine Cooper,
Child Care Project Manager at the City of Sacramento, to serve on the
LPC for the 2022-2024 Term. Prior to her role as Child Care Project
Manager, Janine worked at Catalyst Kids for 15 years with her most
recent position being a Program Director. She has worked in the field of
early learning and childcare for close to 20 years in both the private
and public sectors, supporting early learning and childcare teachers in
their growth and development. Janine was selected through an interview
process conducted by Emma Baier, Project Specialist in the Early
Learning Department. Janine will be replacing Abra Ruthenbeck-Boyd who
resigned in February
2023. ]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[The LPC assesses child care and development services, advises the
Sacramento County Board of Supervisors (BoS), and the Sacramento County
Board of Education (BoE) on child care needs of families, serves as a
forum to address child needs, develops annual priorities for potential
state funding for child care for infants, toddlers, preschools, and
school-age children, and develops a five-year plan identifying local
needs, gaps, and recommendations. The full LPC meets four times per year
and ad hoc subcommittees meet as needed, typically an additional one to
three times per year. Current members of the LPC are Rachelle Davis,
Lisa Carr, Lindsay Dunckel, Ph.D., and Dr. Tabitha Thompson, Karen
Hamnman, Kelli Newman, Shayla Williams-Barnes, Maria Lopez, and Samantha
Fernandez (BoE appointments), and Deborah Doss (BoS
appointee). ]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

RECOMMENDATION:**]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

Janine Cooper to serve on the LPC for the 2022-2024
term. ]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}\

[[**BACKGROUND:**]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[On April 12, 2022, [the Sacramento County Board of Education (Board)
conditionally approved the Capital College and Career Academy (CCCA)
countywide charter petition. Under Board Resolution No. 22-07
(Attachment 1), before opening and operating the charter school, CCCA is
required to satisfy the following conditions through a Memorandum of
Understanding (MOU) between CCCA, the Board, and SCOE which shall
include, but not be limited to the following
terms:]{arial=""}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

1.  [[[By May 1, 2023, CCCA shall provide verification that the school
    has parent or guardian enrollment commitments for the number of
    students equal to at least 90% of the enrollment projected in its
    budget for the first year of operation \[i.e., 72 of 80 students
    enrolled by May 1,
    2023\].  ]{arial=""}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}
2.  [[[By May 1, 2023, CCCA shall provide verification that it has a
    beginning cash balance of at least \$400,000 comprised of
    unrestricted grants and
    donations.]{arial=""}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}
3.  [[[By May 1, 2023, CCCA shall provide evidence that it has secured
    acceptable facilities for its main school site and sufficient
    learning center classrooms near student internship locations to
    serve students as proposed in its
    petition.]{arial=""}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}
4.  [[[CCCA shall revise its charter petition to describe a high-quality
    ]{arial=""}[[Career and Technical Education
    (]{style="color:black"}]{style="line-height:107%"}[CTE) program for
    students that would qualify CCCA for competitive state and federal
    CTE grants to augment its attendance-based
    apportionment.]{arial=""}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}
5.  [[[[Given the unique countywide program promised by CCCA, before
    opening and operating,
    ]{style="color:black"}]{style="line-height:107%"}[CCCA shall
    demonstrate that it has sufficient certificated staff to provide the
    courses promised in the
    petition.]{arial=""}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}
6.  [[[Before opening and operating, CCCA shall be accepted as a member
    of a Special Education Local Plan
    Area.]{arial=""}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[Sue Stickel was approved by the Board to assist the Sacramento County
Superintendent of Schools and the Board with the oversight and
monitoring of CCCA. Ms. Stickel has met regularly with CCCA to review
its progress in meeting its pre-opening requirements and conditions and
her Pre-Opening Status Update is included as Attachment 2. As further
explained in Ms. Stickel\'s report, CCCA has satisfied or is in the
process of satisfying conditions 2, 3, 4, and 6 (cash balance,
facilities, charter revisions, and SELPA membership) and is working on
satisfying condition 5 (sufficient certificated staffing). However, with
63 students enrolled on May 1, 2023, CCCA has not met condition
1.  ]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[Based upon concerns regarding unexpected borrowing by CCCA related to
its school facility purchase and the resulting impact on CCCA\'s budget
and its ability to successfully operate its charter school program, SCOE
recently requested a revised budget and met with CCCA to discuss these
concerns and potential scenarios for mitigation. SCOE\'s fiscal analysis
of CCCA\'s revised budget is included as Attachment 3. 
]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[Ms. Stickel and Associate Superintendent Nick Schweizer will present
their CCCA status reports at the Board meeting. CCCA representatives
will also provide updates regarding the
charter.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}
