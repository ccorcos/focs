Public Comment

 

Anyone may appear at the Board meeting to provide public comment regarding any item on the agenda or regarding any matter that is within the Board’s subject matter jurisdiction. However, the Board may not take action on any item not on the agenda except as authorized by Government Code section 54954.2.  If possible, notify the Board President or Board Secretary in writing prior to the meeting if you wish to testify.

 

Individuals wishing to speak in person before the Board are requested to complete a public comment form. Speakers will be called in the order in which the cards are received. 

 

Public comment via Zoom teleconference may be made by using the raised hand feature in Zoom during the agenda item to be addressed. The speaker will be identified by their Display Name in Zoom when called upon to speak. Those joining the meeting via Zoom are requested to display their full name so there is no confusion when called upon to speak. Public comments will be received until the public comment period has elapsed.

 

Comments are limited to a maximum of two (2) minutes per speaker per agenda item, and individuals are limited to a single comment per agenda item.

 

Please contact boardcomments@scoe.net before the meeting if you have questions about the public comment process. 

 

Accommodating Individuals with Disabilities

 

The Sacramento County Office of Education encourages those with disabilities to participate fully in the public meeting process. If you need a disability-related modification or accommodation, including auxiliary aids or services, to participate in the public meeting, contact the Superintendent’s Office at (916) 228-2410 at least 48 hours before the scheduled Board meeting so that we may make every reasonable effort to accommodate you. [Government Code § 54953.2; Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990, § 202 (42 U.S.C. § 12132).]

BACKGROUND: 

 

"Operation Recognition" is a program adopted by the Sacramento County Board of Education and other organizations throughout the United States to honor individuals whose secondary education was interrupted due to circumstances of World War II, the Korean War, and the Vietnam War. During the 1940s, and again in the 1950s and 1960s, thousands of young men and women left high school to serve in the United States Armed Forces. After these wars, many veterans were not able to finish high school but led productive lives and helped build our communities over their lifetimes. 

 

Also, during World War II, thousands of Japanese American citizens were forced to leave their homes to be interned in relocation camps at various locations in the United States. These citizens were denied the freedom other Americans enjoyed, and many served with the U.S. Armed Forces. Some were unable to complete high school, yet they, too, went on to build productive lives and have greatly contributed to the American way of life.

 

Through the "Operation Recognition" program, the Sacramento County Office of Education will provide a high school diploma to qualifying World War II, Korean War, and Vietnam War veterans, and to Japanese American citizens who were interned in World War II relocation camps. This is a gesture of our society's gratitude for the sacrifices they made in the name of freedom. 

 

The Sacramento County Office of Education has received one application from an individual who qualifies as a recipient of a high school diploma through the "Operation Recognition" program. The following individual is considered to have met the requirements for high school graduation:

 

Louis Soto (Vietnam War)

 

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION: 

 

The Superintendent recommends awarding a high school diploma to the individual named above.

BACKGROUND:

 

The Local Child Care and Development Planning Council (LPC) is requesting that the Board approve the appointment of Janine Cooper, Child Care Project Manager at the City of Sacramento, to serve on the LPC for the 2022-2024 Term. Prior to her role as Child Care Project Manager, Janine worked at Catalyst Kids for 15 years with her most recent position being a Program Director. She has worked in the field of early learning and childcare for close to 20 years in both the private and public sectors, supporting early learning and childcare teachers in their growth and development. Janine was selected through an interview process conducted by Emma Baier, Project Specialist in the Early Learning Department. Janine will be replacing Abra Ruthenbeck-Boyd who resigned in February 2023. 

 

The LPC assesses child care and development services, advises the Sacramento County Board of Supervisors (BoS), and the Sacramento County Board of Education (BoE) on child care needs of families, serves as a forum to address child needs, develops annual priorities for potential state funding for child care for infants, toddlers, preschools, and school-age children, and develops a five-year plan identifying local needs, gaps, and recommendations. The full LPC meets four times per year and ad hoc subcommittees meet as needed, typically an additional one to three times per year. Current members of the LPC are Rachelle Davis, Lisa Carr, Lindsay Dunckel, Ph.D., and Dr. Tabitha Thompson, Karen Hamnman, Kelli Newman, Shayla Williams-Barnes, Maria Lopez, and Samantha Fernandez (BoE appointments), and Deborah Doss (BoS appointee). 

 

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:

 

The Superintendent recommends the Board approves the appointment of Janine Cooper to serve on the LPC for the 2022-2024 term.

B. Capital College & Career Academy Countywide Charter Pre-Opening Status Update - Teresa Stinson
Meeting
May 16, 2023 - Sacramento County Board of Education Meeting Agenda - 6:30 p.m.
Category
10. New Business - Other
Type
Action, Discussion

BACKGROUND:

 

On April 12, 2022, the Sacramento County Board of Education (Board) conditionally approved the Capital College and Career Academy (CCCA) countywide charter petition. Under Board Resolution No. 22-07 (Attachment 1), before opening and operating the charter school, CCCA is required to satisfy the following conditions through a Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) between CCCA, the Board, and SCOE which shall include, but not be limited to the following terms:

 

  1. By May 1, 2023, CCCA shall provide verification that the school has parent or guardian enrollment commitments for the number of students equal to at least 90% of the enrollment projected in its budget for the first year of operation [i.e., 72 of 80 students enrolled by May 1, 2023].  
  2. By May 1, 2023, CCCA shall provide verification that it has a beginning cash balance of at least $400,000 comprised of unrestricted grants and donations.
  3. By May 1, 2023, CCCA shall provide evidence that it has secured acceptable facilities for its main school site and sufficient learning center classrooms near student internship locations to serve students as proposed in its petition.
  4. CCCA shall revise its charter petition to describe a high-quality Career and Technical Education (CTE) program for students that would qualify CCCA for competitive state and federal CTE grants to augment its attendance-based apportionment.
  5. Given the unique countywide program promised by CCCA, before opening and operating, CCCA shall demonstrate that it has sufficient certificated staff to provide the courses promised in the petition.
  6. Before opening and operating, CCCA shall be accepted as a member of a Special Education Local Plan Area.

 

Sue Stickel was approved by the Board to assist the Sacramento County Superintendent of Schools and the Board with the oversight and monitoring of CCCA. Ms. Stickel has met regularly with CCCA to review its progress in meeting its pre-opening requirements and conditions and her Pre-Opening Status Update is included as Attachment 2. As further explained in Ms. Stickel's report, CCCA has satisfied or is in the process of satisfying conditions 2, 3, 4, and 6 (cash balance, facilities, charter revisions, and SELPA membership) and is working on satisfying condition 5 (sufficient certificated staffing). However, with 63 students enrolled on May 1, 2023, CCCA has not met condition 1.  

 

Based upon concerns regarding unexpected borrowing by CCCA related to its school facility purchase and the resulting impact on CCCA's budget and its ability to successfully operate its charter school program, SCOE recently requested a revised budget and met with CCCA to discuss these concerns and potential scenarios for mitigation. SCOE's fiscal analysis of CCCA's revised budget is included as Attachment 3. 

 

Ms. Stickel and Associate Superintendent Nick Schweizer will present their CCCA status reports at the Board meeting. CCCA representatives will also provide updates regarding the charter.
