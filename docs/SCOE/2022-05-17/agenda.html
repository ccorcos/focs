BACKGROUND:

 

"Operation Recognition" is a program adopted by the Sacramento County Board of Education and other organizations throughout the United States to honor individuals whose secondary education was interrupted due to circumstances of World War II, the Korean War, and the Vietnam War. During the 1940s, and again in the 1950s and 1960s, thousands of young men and women left high school to serve in the United States Armed Forces. After these wars, many veterans were not able to finish high school but led productive lives and helped build our communities over their lifetimes.

 

Also during World War II, thousands of Japanese American citizens were forced to leave their homes to be interned in relocation camps at various locations in the United States. These citizens were denied the freedom other Americans enjoyed, and many served with the U.S. Armed Forces. Some were unable to complete high school, yet they, too, went on to build productive lives and have greatly contributed to the American way of life.

 

Through the "Operation Recognition" program, the Sacramento County Office of Education will provide a high school diploma to qualifying World War II, Korean War, and Vietnam War veterans, and to Japanese American citizens who were interned in World War II relocation camps. This is a gesture of our society's gratitude for the sacrifices they made in the name of freedom.

 

The Sacramento County Office of Education has received applications from individuals who qualify as recipients of high school diplomas through the "Operation Recognition" program. The following individual is considered to have met the requirements for high school graduation:

 

Charles “Chuck” LeDoux (Vietnam War)

 

The diploma program is accompanied by a scholarship essay contest sponsored by SAFE Credit Union with a prize of $1,000. This year’s recipient is Lorenzo Chacon, a student at Foothill High School, Twin Rivers Unified School District. Mr. Chacon will attend the meeting to read his top selected essay. The 2022 scholarship contest theme is “How We Can Thank Our Veterans for Their Sacrifices.”

BACKGROUND:

 

Attached is the new proposed Board Policy 3311.1 – Uniform Public Construction Cost Accounting Procedures and proposed Resolution No. 22-10 for the Board's consideration.   

 

SCOE's Business Services Office is recommending that SCOE opt into the California Uniform Construction Cost Account Act (CUPCCAA) to help SCOE timely and efficiently complete small construction projects. CUPCCAA will enable SCOE to use its own work force for small projects and will provide more opportunities to contract with local businesses. Opting into CUPCCAA requires the Board to adopt the new policy and to adopt a resolution electing to become subject to the Act. 

 

What is CUPCCAA? 

 

CUPCCAA was enacted in 1983 to help promote uniformity in public entity construction projects in California. Almost 1,500 public entities in California are using CUPCCAA, including 691 school districts and 67 community college districts. Public entities that elect to become subject to CUPCCAA must adopt a resolution and policy, file a copy of the resolution with the State Controller’s Office, and comply with the Uniform Public Construction Cost Accounting Procedures. CUPCCAA only governs bidding and cost accounting procedures for construction projects. Other applicable public contracting requirements will still apply, such as paying prevailing wages.

 

Key Provisions of CUPCCAA 

 

CUPCCAA would allow SCOE to timely execute small construction projects, expedite project delivery, and would provide greater access to local businesses. SCOE could use its own workforce to complete construction projects costing $60,000 or less, or negotiate a contract with a local business or other qualified contractor. SCOE could utilize an informal bid process for projects costing $200,000 or less, and formal bidding would be required for projects costing more than $200,000. Currently, public projects costing $15,000 and more require formal bidding.

 

CUPCCAA would enable SCOE to timely complete small construction projects and would allow for greater flexibility in the scheduling of projects. While the formal bid process takes 3.5 to 4 months, the contracting process under CUPCCAA would be much shorter for projects less than $200,000 (i.e., within two weeks for projects less than $60,000, and within three or four weeks for projects less than $200,000 using the informal bid process).

 

For the informal bid process, CUPCCAA requires SCOE to establish or update a list of qualified contractors at least once per calendar year by sending out a notice to designated trade journals inviting licensed contractors to submit their names. SCOE would also post the notice with local trade journals and with various groups to attract a diverse pool of contractors. Qualified contractors must be placed on the list and notified by SCOE of opportunities to bid on construction projects. The lowest responsive bidder would be awarded the contract.

 

Creating a list of qualified contractors will provide SCOE with greater access to local businesses. SCOE must currently utilize CMAS and other cooperative agreements for small, time-sensitive construction projects. These lists enable SCOE to timely complete projects while complying with public contracting requirements, but they do not have a large selection of local contractors.

 

In the last six months, SCOE has completed twelve construction projects that were less than $200,000. They encompass both public projects and maintenance, and they include several flooring, roofing, and demolition projects. SCOE has five pending construction projects, including several that require structural and safety-related repairs (e.g., roofing, siding, painting, and fire safety devices). CUPCCAA's process would enable SCOE to complete those projects located at SCOE school facilities during summer break while students are not on campus.

 

The Policy Committee reviewed the new proposed Policy on March 15, 2022 and recommended that the proposed Policy be presented to the Board for First Reading. The First Reading of the proposed Policy occurred at the May 3, 2022 Board meeting.

 

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:

 

The Superintendent recommends that the Board of Education hear the Second Reading and adopt the new proposed Board Policy 3311.1 - Uniform Public Construction Cost Accounting Procedures, and adopt Resolution No. 22-10.

BACKGROUND:

 

The Kern County Office of Education, Elk Grove Unified School District, Davis Joint Unified School District, Santa Clara Unified School District, Capistrano Unified School District and Schools Insurance Authority are partnering with Government Financial Strategies, Inc to create a Joint Powers Authority (JPA) that is principally dedicated to providing financial services support for school districts in California. Upon formation of the JPA, Government Financial Strategies, which SCOE currently relies on for financial services, will transfer its assets and staff to the JPA. As a result, we are requesting the Sacramento Board of Education adopt a resolution to authorize the SCOE to join the JPA as a founding member.

 

SCOE's Need for Financial Services 

 

SCOE currently contracts with Government Financial Strategies (GFS) for technical assistance and support with debt financing issues. GFS provides financial modeling and analysis of general obligation bonds, lease-purchase financings, lease revenue bonds, State and Federal loan programs, etc. GFS also provides support with fraud prevention and examination, post-bond sale compliance and the required continuing disclosure reports. GFS assisted SCOE with the financing for the Museum of Science and Curiosity (MOSAC) and continues to support SCOE with compliance related to our allocation of federal Qualified Zone Academy Bond tax credits to the project.

SCOE also relies on GFS to assist us with fiscal oversight and support for the school districts in the county. SCOE must review all issuances by school districts of non-voter-approved debt and may comment on a district’s capacity to repay its debt obligations. For districts with qualified or negative interim certification reports, SCOE must approve the issuance of all non-voter-approved debt. GFS provides technical assistance to support our review of district debt issuances. We also use GFS to provide professional development and training to district staff on debt financing and compliance issues and financial analysis.

Joint Powers Authorities 

State law authorizes two or more public agencies to establish a JPA for the purpose of jointly exercising common powers. Forming such entities facilitates creative approaches to the provision of public services and provides public agencies with the means to provide services more efficiently and in a cost-effective manner. Local educational agencies (LEAs) have formed JPAs to provide special education, school transportation, school construction, financial information systems, procurement services and insurance and risk management, among other things.

SCOE created the oldest JPA in California when it formed the Schools Insurance Authority (SIA) in 1974. SIA currently provides property, liability and workers' compensation insurance to 35 LEA and JPA members in California. The JPA structure allows SIA’s members to take advantage of prudent risk pooling techniques, effective prevention services, joint purchasing of services and the development of necessary programs that serve to protect their staff and their assets.

Government Financial Services JPA

The volume of debt issued by school districts in California has grown significantly over the last few years and reached a new high of $14.4 billion last year. As a result, there is  a growing need for specialized expertise to support LEA debt financings that is guided by established best practices and that is transparent, efficient and encourages public trust with respect to taxpayer funds.

The purpose of the Government Financial Services JPA is to meet this need by assisting LEAs with their financial matters including, but not limited to, planning and implementing financings and financial strategies best practices for the fiscal benefit of LEAs and the taxpayers which support them. The JPA will provide SCOE with all the services and support it currently receives from Government Financial Strategies.

SCOE will be a founding member of the JPA with a permanent seat on its Board which will provide policy direction for the JPA, annually adopt a budget, appoint a chief executive officer, approve new members and set the annual membership fee. JPA members will have three year-terms. At the end of each three-year term, SCOE will have the option of discontinuing its membership or renewing it for another three-year term.

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:

The Superintendent recommends that the Board adopt Resolution No. 22-11 - In Support of Adopting and Approving the Joint Powers Authority Agreement Creating Government Financial Services JPA.

BACKGROUND:

 

At its previous meeting, the Sacramento County Board of Education (Board) expressed interest in reviewing and considering whether to express its support for certain education-related legislation. If the Board decides to support either of the Assembly Bills summarized below, Sacramento County Office of Education staff will prepare a letter of support on behalf of the Board.

 

Assembly Bill 2774 (Weber) - Adds Lowest-Performing Pupil Subgroup to Supplemental and Concentration Funds

 

California's public school financing system currently provides funding for county superintendents of schools, school districts, and charter schools (LEAs) as calculated under the Local Control Funding Formula (LCFF). Under the LCFF, LEAs receive additional supplemental and concentration funding based on their percentage of “unduplicated pupils,” which are defined as English learners, pupils eligible for free or reduced-price meals, and foster youth.

 

If enacted, AB 2774 would expand the definition of “unduplicated pupils” to include pupils in the lowest performing subgroup(s) based on the most recently available mathematics or language arts results on the California Assessment of Student Performance and Progress (CAASPP). The bill would require the State Superintendent of Public Instruction to annually identify the lowest performing pupil subgroup(s) based upon the most recently available CAASPP results, and to use 2019 CAASPP results to identify this subgroup for the 2023-2024 school year.

 

The lowest performing subgroup would not include those already receiving LCFF supplemental and concentration funding under current law (English learners, low-income pupils, and foster youth) or already receiving additional per-pupil funding through state or federal resources other than LCFF (pupils with disabilities).

 

Assembly Bill 2617 (Holden) and Governor's Budget Proposal on Dual Enrollment

 

Governor Newsom's budget has proposed $500 million for school districts, charter schools, and county offices of education (LEAs) to strengthen and expand access for high school students to participate in dual enrollment opportunities. Dual enrollment allows high school students to take college courses and earn college credit.

 

Existing law authorizes partnerships between community college districts and LEAs to create dual enrollment opportunities with the goal of developing seamless pathways for career technical education, preparing students to transfer to college and to achieve college and career readiness, and improving high school graduation rates.

 

If enacted, AB 2617 would appropriate $500 million for competitive grants for LEAs to establish opportunities for pupils to obtain college credit while enrolled in high school and provide dual enrollment. An LEA could apply for (1) a one-time grant of up to $500,000 to establish outreach campaigns to encourage pupils to enroll in dual enrollment programs and to couple robust pupil advising and success supports with available dual enrollment and accelerated college credit opportunities; (2) a one-time grant of up to $250,000 to plan for and start up a middle college or early college high school on its campus; and/or (3) a one-time grant of up to $100,000 to establish a College and Career Access Pathways dual enrollment partnership agreement with a community college to enable its pupils to access dual enrollment opportunities.

 

The State Superintendent of Public Instruction would administer the grant program and would be required to give priority for available grant funds to LEAs with (1) enrollment of more than 50% unduplicated pupils; (2) a higher than state average dropout rate; or (3) a higher than state average rate of suspension and expulsion.

 

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:

 

The Superintendent recommends that the Board discuss AB 2774 and AB 2617 and decide whether to send a letter supporting either or both of these pending legislative bills.
