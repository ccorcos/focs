Charter School Legislative Update
On July 10, 2023, the Governor signed Senate Bill 114 to extend the term of charter schools that
expire on or between January 1, 2024, and June 30, 2027, by one additional year. Fortune's
current charter term originally was scheduled to expire on June 30, 2026. However, as per
Education Code section 47604.4 and the California Department of Education (CDE) Charter School
Database, Fortune's charter term was extended and will expire on June 30, 2027.

Highlights of the LCAP

Fortune School is committed to serving disadvantaged students with 62 percent African American
youth and 74 percent socioeconomically disadvantaged students enrolled during the 2023-2024
school year, compared to the countywide averages of 10 percent African American and 62 percent
socioeconomically disadvantaged students. To address the unique needs of their students, Fortune
School focuses their LCAP goals on three key areas; 1) closing the achievement gap, 2) providing
safe, welcoming, and rigorous, standards-aligned learning environments, and 3) providing
nurturing communities that engage and support students.

Fortune School allocated nearly $18 million to fund schoolwide actions targeting students with the
lowest academic achievement. Investments in lower teacher-student ratios, more instructional
aides, small-group instruction, longer instructional days, master teachers, and professional

enrollment. The metrics used to measure progress towards meeting Goal 1 include the California
School Dashboard - Academic Indicators in ELA, math, and science, measures of English Learner

earning college credit.

Summary of Academic Interventions
Fortune School prioritizes the academic achievement of all students and aims to provide students
with the individualized support they need to close achievement gaps. In the 2023-2024 school
year, Fortune School implemented three major initiatives to provide academic interventions.
    1. Fortune Rise - Fortune Rise is an opportunity to accelerate student learning by building 30
       minutes into the regular school day for ELA interventions and 30 minutes for math
       interventions, every day except Wednesday. Teachers provide targeted intervention
       lessons to small student groups to improve skills and close gaps. While the teacher is
       working with a small group, the rest of the class is working on independent skill building
       activities using iReady MyPath personalized lessons. Fortune Rise allows for teacher-led
       intensive intervention and technology-led intervention to help close gaps and accelerate
       learning. Evidence collected by Fortune School during the 2023-2024 school year showed
       that for every 100 minutes students spent mastering lessons in MyPath, they performed
       two scale score points higher on the CAASPP assessments.
    2. Crescendo – Crescendo is preparation for the CAASPP assessments which focuses on a
       gradual increase in the intensity of instruction leading up to the CAASPP assessments.
       Teachers challenge student thinking by providing them with prompt feedback and multiple
       opportunities to practice success.
              a. Error Analysis Reteach Lessons – Students receive targeted reteaching of core
                 standards based on weekly assessments.
              b. Saturday Academy – Students who are at or above grade level are offered more
                 time to practice performance tasks and receive immediate feedback on the most
                 rigorous portions of the CAASPP assessments.
              c. Intersession – Students who are not yet meeting grade level standards with error
                 analysis reteach lessons are offered intensive interventions in a small group setting.
              d. Performance Task Wednesday – Students in grades 3-8 are provided more time to
                 practice performance tasks and receive immediate feedback on the most rigorous
                 portions of the CAASPP assessments.
    3. Summer School – Fortune School partnered with the National Summer School Initiative to
       provide curriculum, training, and support to operate a rigorous summer academic
       program. From June 17 to July 23, 2024, Fortune School offered nine hours of daily

Admissions Practices

Fortune’s admissions process begins with the completion of a student application submitted with
the following required documents; proof of birth date, proof of residency, immunization records,
and physical examination (for TK/K only). Fortune School enrolls all students who submit complete
applications unless the number of applications exceeds the charter school's capacity. According to
Fortune, in accordance with Education Code section 48850(a)(3), unhoused children and youth are
enrolled immediately even if they lack required documentation like residency proof or previous
academic records. In the event the number of students seeking admission to any grade level
exceeds capacity, a lottery is held. During the 2023-2024 and 2024-2025 school years, no lotteries
were held at any Sacramento County campuses.

Fortune School may grant priority in admissions to current students and residents of Sacramento
County, as provided under the law when a lottery is conducted. The school may also grant
admission preference to siblings of existing pupils and children of school faculty, provided students
admitted under any such preference do not constitute more than 10 percent of the school’s total
enrollment. Currently enrolled students do not participate in the random drawing, as they are
automatically reserved a space for the following year. Fortune School reported 1,700 students
enrolled in 2024-2025, a decrease of 2.7 percent students from 2023-2024.

Cycle of Professional Development and Dissemination of Best Practices

Annually, Fortune School follows a strategic cycle of professional development to ensure that
organizational priorities guide the work of all staff members throughout the year. The professional
development cycle begins with a cabinet retreat and a leadership retreat. The cabinet retreat
includes members of the cabinet who work to define their roles and responsibilities in line with
organizational priorities. The leadership retreat includes cabinet members in addition to directors,
principals, assistant principals, and master teachers. At the leadership retreat, leaders work
together to integrate organizational priorities with other schoolwide planning efforts and prepare
for the launch of the new school year.

The retreats lead into a principal institute and master teacher institute, where principals and
master teachers align objectives and tools to help them accomplish their annual goals. The
institutes then lead into the annual summer symposium which every staff member attends. In
preparation for the 2023-2024 school year, the summer symposium was held on August 2-4, 2023.
At the summer symposium, staff were introduced to or reviewed the priorities of the school
culture. The focus in 2023-2024 was on Community Circles. Facilitated by Beyond Us and Them,
staff learned the process for conducting Community Circles to build community on campus.
Following the summer symposium, staff received additional site-level training through a sequence
of professional development led by the principal. In addition to site-level training, teachers and
instructional aids received curriculum training through a process of onboarding led by master
teachers.

Fortune School organizes annual professional learning goals through an “Arc of the Year.” The year
begins with a focus on culture, pivots to academic rigor, and intensifies with crescendo where
students receive additional individualized support based on needs identified through interim
assessments. The final arc of the year is planning. Fortune School’s cycle of professional
development focuses on the improvement of learning and instruction, integrating job-embedded
teacher learning through weekly early-release days. Teachers attend professional development
tailored to their grade and subject expertise. Principals lead an academic strategy meeting each
Wednesday, delivering operational and instructional information to the entire staff to continue
progress in each arc of the year. Every other Wednesday, master teachers facilitate a data meeting,
guiding teachers and instructional aids through the process of data analysis and lesson planning. In
addition, master teachers provide professional development on key instructional routines and
practices.

Master teachers conduct classroom observations once a week and principals observe classrooms
twice a week during the culture arc ending in September, and then once a week during the rigor
arc ending in March, the crescendo arc ending in May, and the planning arc ending on the last day
of school. Master teachers record their feedback including actionable steps to improve culture,

of professional learning and improvement throughout the year.

includes a structured meeting agenda to ensure everyone understands and executes the

Key Board Decisions and Policy Revisions
On February 2, 2024, the Fortune School Education Board of Directors adopted a revised
medication policy. A review of the existing policy revealed the need for a revision of the policy to
allow for student self-administration of EpiPens and asthma inhalers. Students who experience
allergic or asthmatic episodes need to be able to receive their life-saving medication immediately.
Revisions to the policy also included a definition of an employee volunteer who assists with the
administration of medication and a declaration that employees are allowed to either opt in or opt
out of administering medication to students.

Parental Involvement in Governance and School Operations
The goal of Fortune School is to empower parents as educational partners. Parents are actively
involved in the education of students. Their presence on campus and assistance to teachers in the
classroom continues to be of the utmost importance. To ensure significant parent involvement and
strengthen the school community, Fortune School forms parent-based groups that are responsible
for recruiting parents into school-based activities. Parents are members of the School Site Council
and are encouraged to participate in School Site Council meetings. Fortune’s School Site Council
consists of 10 members comprised of the principal/designee, one non-teaching staff, three
teachers, four parents, and one high school student.

Fortune School campuses foster a climate of parent volunteerism. After signing a Commitment to
Excellence Contract, parents are encouraged to complete 40 hours of voluntary service each
academic year. Fortune’s Commitment to Excellence Contract is in accordance with Education Code
section 47605.6(n) which states “a charter school may encourage parental involvement, but shall
notify the parents and guardians of applicant pupils and currently enrolled pupils that parental
involvement is not a requirement for acceptance to, or continued enrollment at, the charter
school.” There is no consequence to families if they do not complete voluntary parent service
hours, however, parent volunteerism is an important part of Fortune School’s approach to closing
the achievement gap. Fortune School calls for parents to accept responsibility for the education of
their children.

  V.     SUMMARY


During the 2023-2024 school year, Fortune School demonstrated progress in creating safe school
environments with positive relationships between students and staff reflected in a decrease in the
suspension rate to 3.9 percent in 2023-2024 compared to 15.6 percent in 2022-2023. Fortune’s
efforts to improve school culture also supported improved environments for student learning,
demonstrated in overall increases in both ELA and math on statewide assessments. The African
American and socioeconomically disadvantaged student groups also demonstrated increases in
both ELA and math. Additionally, African American students and English learner students
outperformed their peers in Sacramento County in both ELA and math on statewide assessments.
However, there were areas of opportunity, as students with disabilities and socioeconomically
disadvantaged students performed lower than their peers in Sacramento County. Fortune’s master
teachers play an important role in student achievement by providing teachers with professional
development on key instructional routines and practices including strategies to individualize
student learning to close achievement gaps. Another Fortune School practice to address the
achievement gap for students with disabilities is a bi-weekly check-in plan between special
education specialists and classroom teachers to ensure everyone understands and executes the
accommodations and modifications for students with disabilities.

Fortune School also reduced chronic absenteeism by 5 percent during the 2023-3024 school year,
though the overall rate remained at 32.6 percent. African American students had lower
absenteeism rates than their peers countywide, while socioeconomically disadvantaged students
experienced higher rates.

The mission of Fortune School is to graduate high-achieving students of good character prepared for
college and citizenship in a democratic society. Aligned with this mission are Fortune School’s
partnerships with California Polytechnic State University, San Luis Obispo (Cal Poly) and Cosumnes
River College. Fortune School’s dual enrollment partnership with Cosumnes River College ensures
