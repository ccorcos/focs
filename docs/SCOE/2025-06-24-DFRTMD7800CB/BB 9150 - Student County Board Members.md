The Sacramento County Board of Education (Board) believes in the importance of promoting civic
engagement and seeking out and considering youth students' ideas, viewpoints and reactions to the
educational program to inform the Board's decision making. As a means of engaging student voice, input,
and involvement empowering youth to improve their schools and communities, the Board may choose to
include one or more youth student Board members, contingent upon finding a qualified applicant. Upon
receiving a qualified student petition under Education Code section 1000(b), the Board shall include one or
more student members.

Student Youth County Board Member Selection

When seeking to fill a student youth Board member position, students currently or formerly enrolled in
a SCOE school or program will be considered first.

In the event that a current or former SCOE student is not available or selected, the Board mayshall
appoint a recent high school or college student or graduate or SCOE fellow. who hasThis individual
shall have lived experience that demonstrates a knowledge and understanding of the issues faced by
SCOE students, schools, or programs. The person appointed pursuant to this paragraph shall have the
same role and responsibilities as a student Board member and shall be considered a student Board
member in all respects.

The Superintendent or designee will work with SCOE schools and community partners to recruit,
identify and screen for qualified potential candidates for the student Youth County Board member role.
The Board shall be informed of the approve the planned recruitment activities. The Board President
may appoint Two or three Board members shall have the opportunity to review a list of qualified
candidates, meet with selected candidates, and recommend a final candidate(s) to the Board. The
Board shall appoint the Youth County Board member at a public meeting. The Superintendent will
coordinate a meeting between the recommended candidates and two or three Board members and
these Board members will recommend a final candidate for selection by the Board.

The term of the Youth County student Board member shall be one year, commencing oin July 1. The
Board may adjust the term if a vacancy occurs or for other good cause.

Role and Responsibilities of Student Youth County Board Members
Student Board members shall be selected in accordance with procedures approved by the Board.

Upon being selected, student Youth County Board members shall receive an orientation on the role and
responsibilities of serving as a student Youth County Board member and shall receive guidance,
training, and mentoring during their term of office. The Board President may assign a Board member
mentor. In fulfilling their role, youth Board members shall be encouraged to seek input from students in
SCOE schools and programs, and to share their observations and/or recommendations with the Board
during Board member reports.

Upon being selected and seated, student Youth County Board members shall have the right to attend all
Board meetings except closed sessions. Student Youth County Board members shall be seated with regular
Board members and be recognized at meetings as full members. They may participate in questioning
presenters witnesses and discussing issues and shall receive all materials presented to Board members
except those related to closed sessions and other confidential materials. The student Youth County Board
member shall not be counted in determining whether a quorum is in attendance at a Board meeting and
shall not be considered a member of the Board for purposes of the Brown Act.

The Youth County Board member's name shall be called when attendance is taken and shall be noted in
the minutes. Student Youth County Board members may cast preferential votes on all matters except those
subject to closed session discussion. A preferential vote means a formal expression of opinion that is cast
before the official Board vote and recorded in the meeting minutes. The preferential vote shall not be included
in determining the vote required to carry any measure before the Board.

The Board may adopt a resolution authorizing student members to make motions that may be acted upon by
the Board., except on matters dealing with employer employee relations pursuant to Government Code
sections 3540, et seq.

Each student Youth County Board member may be appointed to Board subcommittees in the same manner as
other Board members. Student Youth County Board members will be made aware of the time commitment
required to participate in subcommittee meetings and work, and may decline an appointment to a
subcommittee.

The policies and procedures for the selection participation of student Youth County Board members shall
ensure and protect the privacy of each student and parent/guardian involved in appellate proceedings before
the Board.

The Board may appoint a student youth to serve as an alternate student member to fulfill all duties and have
the same rights as a student Youth County Board member if the Board determines the student Youth County
Board member is not fulfilling their duties. If the Board appoints an alternate student Youth member, the Board
shall suspend the prior student's Youth County Board member’s rights and privileges related to service on
Board.

A majority vote of all voting Board members shall be required to approve a motion to eliminate a student Youth
County Board member position. The motion shall be listed as an item on the Board's public meeting agenda
before the motion is voted upon.

Student Youth County Board members may, upon request, be reimbursed for mileage but shall not and
shall receive compensation for attendance at Board meetings as allowed by law. , except that that student
Board member may be awarded elective course credit based on the number of equivalent daily instructional
minutes for student member services provided.

A student Youth County Board member shall not be liable for any acts of the Board.
