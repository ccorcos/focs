     Annual Summary Report
Local Context
The Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) is one of 58 county offices of education in California and serves 13 school
districts: Arcohe (TK-8, 560 students); Center (TK-12, 4,614 students); Elk Grove (TK-12, 63,159); Elverta (TK-8, 218 students); Folsom
Cordova (TK-12, 21,630); Galt Elementary (TK-8, 3,452 students); Galt High (9-12, 1,943 students); Natomas (TK-12, 11,029 students);
River Delta (TK-12, 1,714 students); Robla (TK-6, 1,894 students); Sacramento City (TK-12, 36,423 students); San Juan (TK-12, 39,200
students); and Twin Rivers (TK-12, 22,562 students). Additionally, Sacramento county has 52 charter schools serving 49,022 students.
In total, there are over 250,000 students in Sacramento County and LEAs are both rural and urban. The demographic makeup of our
county consists primarily of Hispanic/Latino (32.7%) and White (26.1%) students with also some Asian (18.8%) and African American
(9.4%). Additionally there are 11,154 homeless students and 749 foster youth. English learners comprise 18% of the students and
14% have some sort of disability.

In the 2024-25 school year, systems across the region continue to grapple with staffing shortages, increased behavior issues, even in
early grades, and student absences, but we are seeing some improvements. In 2024-25 11 of our 13 school districts and 16 charter
schools were eligible for Differentiated Assistance (DA), with academics and suspensions as the primary drivers for this status. This is
true for our Charter Schools as well, but many of the Charters are also eligible for Graduation Rate and the College and Career
Indicator (CCI). We continue to see that Students with Disabilities, Foster/Homeless and English Learners are the most common areas
where LEAs need support. While many of these areas still hold true, there have been some improvements and some changing needs
based upon updates to our State’s accountability metrics. In 2023, seven districts were eligible for DA based upon Students with
Disabilities, but according to the 2024 CDE School Dashboard, only four of these districts remained eligible for this student group. The
same was true for our homeless population (i.e., district eligibility reduced from six in 2023 to four in 2024). Additionally, however, a
new student group was added - Long Term English Learners (LTELs). In 2024 seven districts were identified as eligible for DA based
upon this student group. These changing areas of focus have also changed how we work with LEAs. We currently have a deep focus
on how we align our work in DA to the efforts around Compliance Improvement Monitoring (CIM) to support students with disabilities.
We have also begun a new Improvement Network for LEAs focused on LTELs.
SCOE is committed to identifying, promoting, and collaborating with experts to provide educational solutions focused on improving
opportunities and access in service of high academic achievement for all students to our schools and districts. Utilizing strategies
from improvement science, implementation science, and compassionate systems awareness, our support of districts and schools to
close achievement and opportunity gaps, advance equity, and support the social-emotional needs of students and staff in
Sacramento County will continue to be of utmost importance in 2025-26.
The following plan to support districts in implementing Local Control Accountability Plans (LCAPs) and to strive for continuous
improvement adheres to the provisions/requirements of California Education Code (EC) 52066.

Actions the school district(s) will take independent of the COE

 Please provide a description of the actions the school district(s) will take independent of the COE to improve student outcomes.

SCOE will guide and coach the district/charter through the continuous improvement process and identify resources to support them in
identified areas of need. The LEA will have additional responsibilities: gather, organize and display data related to their area(s) of focus;
gather feedback from educational partners on root causes; gather input and prioritize change ideas; integrate change ideas into a
coherent system that aligns to LEA priorities; monitor and evaluate the effectiveness of their change ideas; and integrate actions and
funding into their LCAP and/or SPSAs. Additionally, SCOE directs LEAs to any professional development opportunities that align to
their current areas of focus, so they have the opportunity to increase content knowledge that might help them improve student
outcomes.
