The Sacramento County Board of Education (Board) authorizes independent study as an optional alternative
instructional strategy by which pupils may reach curriculum objectives and fulfill graduation requirements.
Independent study shall offer a means of individualizing the educational plan for pupils whose needs may be
best met through study outside the regular school program. A pupil's participation in independent study shall be
voluntary, and pupils participating in independent study shall have the right to return to in-person instruction at
any time.

When a request is received for independent study from a parent or guardian (or from a pupil who is an adult or
emancipated minor), the Superintendent, or designee, shall determine whether the prospective independent
study pupil understands and is prepared to meet the Sacramento County Office of Education's (SCOE)
requirements for independent study. SCOE's independent study program shall provide participating pupils with
standards-aligned content substantially equivalent in quality and quantity to in-person instruction. For high
school pupils, this shall include access to all courses offered by SCOE for graduation and approved by the
University of California or the California State University as creditable under the A-G admissions criteria. Pupils
in independent study shall have access to the same services, resources, rights and privileges that are
available to students participating in in-person instruction in a SCOE school, including access to dual
enrollment and career technical education courses available through SCOE.

Independent study for each pupil shall be under the general supervision of a SCOE employee who possesses
a valid certification document pursuant to Education Code section 44865 or an emergency credential pursuant
to Education Code section 44300. Pupils' independent study shall be coordinated, evaluated, and
documented, as prescribed by law and reflected in the accompanying administrative regulation.

The Superintendent or designee shall ensure that a written master independent study agreement exists for
each participating pupil, and is maintained in the pupil's file.

The written agreement shall specify the length of time in which each independent study assignment must be
completed. Because excessive leniency in the duration of independent study assignments may result in a
pupil falling behind peers and increase the risk of dropping out of school, the maximum length of time allowed
between the time an independent study assignment is given and the date by which the pupil must submit the
assigned work shall be no more than 25 school days for all SCOE programs and grade levels offering
independent study. However, when necessary based upon the specific circumstances of the pupil's approved
program, the Superintendent or designee may allow for a longer period of time between the date an
assignment is made and when it is due, not to extend beyond the termination date specified in the pupil's
written agreement.

When a participating pupil misses three assignments or is not making satisfactory educational progress, an
evaluation shall be conducted to determine whether it is in the pupil's best interest to remain in independent
study. Missing appointments with the supervising teacher without valid reasons may also trigger an evaluation
to determine whether the pupil should remain in independent study.

A pupil-parent-educator conference shall be held as appropriate, including when requested by a parent, as a
reengagement strategy, and prior to enrollment or disenrollment from independent study.

A nutritionally adequate breakfast and lunch shall be made available at no cost to any independent
study pupil scheduled for educational activities lasting two or more hours at a school site, resource
center, meeting space, or other satellite facility, who requests a meal.

The Board directs the Superintendent to develop Administrative Rules and Regulations to implement this Board
Policy. This policy will apply to independent study offered as of the 2024-2025 2022-2023 school year.
