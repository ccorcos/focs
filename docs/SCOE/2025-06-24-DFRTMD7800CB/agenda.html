Public Comment

 

Anyone may appear at the Board meeting to provide public comment regarding any item on the agenda or regarding any matter that is within the Board’s subject matter jurisdiction. However, the Board may not take action on any item not on the agenda except as authorized by Government Code section 54954.2. If possible, notify the Board President or Board Secretary in writing prior to the meeting if you wish to testify.

 

Individuals wishing to speak in person before the Board are requested to complete a public comment form. Speakers will be called in the order in which the cards are received. 

 

Public comment via Zoom teleconference may be made by using the raised hand feature in Zoom during the agenda item to be addressed. The speaker will be identified by their Display Name in Zoom when called upon to speak. Those joining the meeting via Zoom are requested to display their full name so there is no confusion when called upon to speak. Public comments will be received until the public comment period has elapsed.

 

Comments are limited to a maximum of two (2) minutes per speaker per agenda item, and individuals are limited to a single comment per agenda item.

 

Please contact boardcomments@scoe.net before the meeting if you have questions about the public comment process. 

 

Accommodating Individuals with Disabilities

 

The Sacramento County Office of Education encourages those with disabilities to participate fully in the public meeting process. If you need a disability-related modification or accommodation, including auxiliary aids or services, to participate in the public meeting, contact the Superintendent’s Office at (916) 228-2410 at least 48 hours before the scheduled Board meeting so that we may make every reasonable effort to accommodate you. [Government Code § 54953.2; Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990, § 202 (42 U.S.C. § 12132).]

BACKGROUND:

A public hearing will be held by the Sacramento County Board of Education (Board) to receive public comment and input from the community on the Miracle University Charter High School (MUCHS) Countywide Charter Petition. The public hearing will be held at the Sacramento County Office of Education located at 10474 Mather Boulevard in Mather, California during the regular meeting of the Board of Education which is scheduled to commence at 6:30 p.m. on June 24, 2025. At the conclusion of the public hearing, the Board may take action on MUCHS' charter petition.

On April 28, 2025, the Miracle University Charter High School (MUCHS) submitted a completed petition to establish a countywide charter school. The petition is available for viewing at the following link: Miracle University Charter High School Countywide Charter Petition

On June 24, 2025, the Board will conduct a public hearing and consider MUCHS’ Countywide Charter Petition. Following review of the MUCHS petition and the public hearing, the Board may either approve or deny the petition. If the County Board denies the countywide charter petition, MUCHS has no right of appeal. (Ed. Code, § 47605.6(k); Cal. Code Regs., tit. 5, § 11966.5(e).)

SUMMARY OF CHARTER PETITION ANALYSIS:

Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) staff with expertise in each relevant area have reviewed the MUCHS countywide charter petition and concluded: 

  • The petition satisfies the technical signature, school district notification, legal affirmations, and Educational Employment Relations Act declaration requirements. 

 

  • The petition seeks to unlawfully convert Miracle University’s private school into a public charter school in violation of Education Code section 47602(b).

 

  • MUCHS is demonstrably unlikely to successfully implement the program set forth in the petition because, among other things:
    • ​​the credentials of its lead petitioner were revoked for immoral, unprofessional, and/or unlawful conduct; 
    • the petition includes inaccuracies and misrepresentations, which undermine the credibility of the petition.
    • the budget is not viable and does not adequately fund the educational program promised by the petition, including adequate teachers, mentors, tutors, counselors, attendance specialists, special education services, and English language support; and 
    • the petition does not contain a sound operational or staffing plan or demonstrate the ability to operate an independent charter school, and instead seeks to make SCOE responsible for its special education staffing, services, and liabilities in its first year of operation. 

 

  • The petition does not describe a sound educational program for the pupils to be enrolled in the charter school.

 

  • The petition contains sufficient descriptions of certain elements but does not contain reasonably comprehensive descriptions of all the elements required by Education Code section 47605.6(b)(5)(A)-(P), including the educational program, measurable pupil outcomes, measurement of pupil progress, charter school facilities, governance structure, and staff qualifications.

 

  • The petition does not meet the legal requirements for approval as a countywide charter school.

 

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:

 

The Superintendent recommends that the Sacramento County Board of Education review and conduct a public hearing on the Miracle University Charter High School countywide charter petition, adopt the Sacramento County Office of Education's Recommendations and Findings, and deny the charter petition.

BACKGROUND:

 

The Every Student Succeeds Act (ESSA) requires schools receiving Title I funds and operating a school wide program, or schools identified for Comprehensive Support and Improvement (CSI), Targeted Support and Improvement (TSI), or Additional Targeted Support and Improvement (ATSI), to develop a school plan. Assembly Bill 716 revised California Education Code sections 64001-65001 to streamline and align state and federal planning processes and organized federal school planning requirements in ESSA.

 

The development of the School Plan for Student Achievement (SPSA) is required to include the following:

  • A comprehensive needs assessment (pursuant to ESSA)
    • Analysis of verifiable state data, consistent with state priorities, including state-determined long term goals
    • The plan may include local data
  • An identification of the process of evaluating and monitoring the implementation of the School Plan and progress towards accomplishing the goals

The Juvenile Court, Community School, and Special Education programs have prepared their school plans in accordance with school-wide program stipulations.

In accordance with California Education Code Section 52068(a)(4), a county office of education superintendent must review the Local Control and Accountability Plan (LCAP) and School Plans to ensure that the two plans are consistent. An integrated approach to planning ensures a consistent alignment between the LCAP and School Plans. As a result, the goals and relevant metrics outlined in the 2025-2026 Juvenile Court, Community School, and Special Education SPSAs are aligned to the 2025-2026 Sacramento County Office of Education LCAP.

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:

The Superintendent recommends that the Board approve each School Plan for Student Achievement in accordance with the requirements under Education Code and federal law.

BACKGROUND:

 

The 2025–2026 Course Catalog reflects our commitment to providing a rigorous, inclusive, and equitable set of learning pathways for all students. This catalog includes a wide range of academic and elective offerings that support college and career readiness. Key components of this year’s catalog include:

 

A-G Approved Courses
The catalog features updated University of California (UC)/California State University (CSU) A-G approved courses, ensuring students have access to the coursework necessary to meet entrance requirements for public universities in California. All subject areas (A–G) are represented.

 

Career Technical Education (CTE)
The catalog highlights CTE courses aligned to California's 15 industry sectors. Courses offer real-world skills, project-based learning, and industry certifications where applicable. These CTE offerings include Business (Entrepreneurship), Construction, Culinary, and Horticulture. Work-based learning opportunities are integrated when possible.

 

Dual Enrollment
In collaboration with Los Rios Community College, the catalog expands Dual Enrollment options for high school students. These courses provide both high school and college credit, supporting early college access and reducing the time and cost for postsecondary degree completion. Courses are aligned with student interests and workforce trends, including general education and career-focused pathways.

 

Online Learning - Edgenuity
To support flexible and personalized learning, the catalog appendix includes Edgenuity online course offerings. These courses meet California standards and offer credit recovery, grade improvement, and original credit opportunities. All Edgenuity courses are reviewed for rigor and alignment with district graduation and A-G requirements when applicable. Courses will be added once approved through UC Doorways and added to our Student Information System.

 

At the May 29, 2025 Curriculum Committee meeting, the 2025-2026 Course Catalog was reviewed, and the Committee recommended this catalog be sent forward for Board approval and adoption at the June 24, 2025 Board meeting.

 

Next Steps
Pending Board approval, the catalog will be published, translated into multiple languages, and shared with school counselors, administrators, and families to support course selection for the 2025–2026 academic year.

 

SUPERINTENDENTS RECOMMENDATION:

 

The Superintendent recommends that the Board approve the 2025-26 Course Catalog and associated courses and materials.

Program Description: 

The Every Student Succeeds Act (ESSA) appropriates funds to county offices of education for the purposes of supporting development and implementation of Comprehensive Support and Improvement (CSI) plans. SCOE's Planning and Improvement Department will provide plan development and implementation technical assistance and support to each local educational agency (LEA) in Sacramento County that serves schools that meet the criteria for CSI.

SCOE will use these funds to build LEA capacity to support their schools through meaningful engagement in the school improvement planning processes to effectively address disparities, including technical assistance activities related to:

  • Family and community engagement to inform and support continuous improvement.
  • Needs assessments and root cause analysis of persistently low student outcomes.
  • Selecting and implementing evidence-based actions and interventions.
  • Developing, implementing, monitoring, and evaluating data on the effectiveness of programs and services.
  • Cycles of inquiry with school leadership teams to advance equity for students with disabilities and other underserved student groups.
  • Identify resource inequities, which may include a review of LEA and school-level budgets.

Program Description: 

The Every Student Succeeds Act (ESSA) appropriates funds for county offices of education for the purposes of review and approval of Comprehensive Support and Improvement (CSI) plans through the CSI prompts in the local educational agency (LEA) Local Control and Accountability Plan (LCAP).

The Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) Planning and Improvement Department will review and approve the CSI prompts in the LEA LCAP Plan Summary, for each LEA in Sacramento County that serves schools that meet the criteria for CSI. In addition, SCOE will provide trainings to LEAs with CSI schools to support effective plan evaluations, comprehensive needs analyses, and effective family and community engagement to focus improvement efforts and resolve resource inequities.

The COE is required to review and approve CSI plans through the CSI prompts in 2024–2025 LEA LCAP for those LEAs in its county with schools eligible for CSI. Responses to these prompts constitute summaries of the LEA’s CSI plans and coupled with COE or CDE approval, as applicable, serve as the mechanism for SBE approval. The COE is working on behalf of the SBE to meet the ESSA SEA requirement to approve LEA CSI Plans.

Evaluation Component: 

Budget expenditures and services provided will be reported quarterly to the California Department of Education.

Funding Source: California Student Aid Commission

 

Amount Requested: $640,926

 

Fiscal Year(s): 2025-2026

 

Program Description: 

This grant application is for the Sacramento Cal-SOAP Consortium that SCOE has administered for the past 27 years. It requires delivery of advising and academic services geared toward 1) informing students of postsecondary options and the availability of financial aid, and 2) improving academic performance with the goal of achieving college eligibility and attendance. 

 

Sacramento Cal-SOAP provides college advising services via a series of workshops conducted by project staff and College Success Coaches to over 6,000 high school seniors and over 7,000 high school juniors in more than 200 classrooms in 13 high schools. Schools are selected based on college-going rates and percentage of students who meet the federal guidelines to receive free or reduced-priced meals. Individual and small group advising sessions for 1,500 low-income or first-generation students follow the classroom workshops. Additionally, the Sacramento Cal-SOAP plans to recruit, hire, and train 10 graduate students and 30 undergraduate college students as college success coaches and place them in over 20 schools.
