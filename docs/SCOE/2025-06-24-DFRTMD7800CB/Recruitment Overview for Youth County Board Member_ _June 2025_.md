                          Overview of Recruitment Activities
                        for Youth County Board Member Role

(1) Recruitment of SCOE Youth Fellows Who May Be Considered by the Board for
the Position of Youth County Board Member

The SCOE Youth Engagement Fellowship was launched to provide high school
graduates with lived experience with the opportunity to engage directly with SCOE
schools, students, and programs and amplify SCOE student voice, while having
structured employment through PRO Youth and Families. Fellows are guided and
mentored to build professional capacity and leadership skills through tailored training and
fieldwork aligned with SCOE’s mission.

To recruit Youth Fellows in 2024-2025—who may be considered for the Youth County
Board Member role—the following actions were taken:

   •   October 2024: First Recruitment Cycle
          o Position posted on Indeed and distributed to over 400 former SCOE
            students who had attended SCOE schools over the past four years.
          o 300+ applications received and reviewed by Pro Youth using a screening
            rubric focused on relevant skills and lived experience.
          o Pro Youth provided coaching and support to applicants unfamiliar with
            professional hiring processes.
          o 10% of applicants advanced to the next round (including all applicants
            identified as former SCOE students), received interview preparation
            support, and participated in first-round interviews.
          o Applicants were selected for interviews based on lived experience, SCOE
            background or alignment with SCOE initiatives, passion for the work, with
            emphasis on applicants who could benefit from and contribute to Fellows
            program.
          o Second-round interviews led to the hiring of one Youth Fellow in December
            2024.
   •   January 2025: Second Recruitment Cycle
          o Position re-posted and again distributed to over 400 former SCOE students.
          o 200+ applications reviewed using consistent selection criteria.
          o 20 first-round interviews held, including all applicants with SCOE
            experience.
          o 4 candidates advanced; two were hired as Fellows and also identified as
            strong Youth Board Member candidates based on their prior participation in
            SCOE schools and programs.
          o Interviews conducted by Superintendent Gordon, Deputy Superintendent
            Herota, and Board President Brown, Vice President Corona-Sabeniano,
            and Trustee Lefkovitz in April 2025.

Fellows are considered ideal candidates for the Youth County Board Member role due to
their ongoing engagement with SCOE students and ability to represent diverse student
voices. They are trained and supported through a customized professional development
program that enhances their skills, confidence, and knowledge of SCOE’s structure and
initiatives. Additionally, the Fellows position provides paid employment for 20-30 hours
per week to engage with SCOE schools and programs, and to prepare for and attend
Board Meetings.

(2) Informing and Recruiting Current SCOE Students for the Youth County Board
Member Role

In addition to recruiting former SCOE students as SCOE Youth Fellows, efforts have been
made to identify and recruit current SCOE students for consideration as Youth County
Board Members:

   •   In 2024-2025, SCOE staff proactively reached out to all current students in court
       and community schools to gauge interest in serving as the Youth Board Member,
       including targeted outreach to youth who participated in listening sessions.
   •   Despite outreach efforts, no current students expressed interest in the position
       during this cycle.

SCOE remains committed to providing both current students and recent graduates with
opportunities to engage in leadership and amplify the student voice. Future outreach
efforts may include:

   •   Enhanced in-school promotion of the Youth Board Member role.
   •   Peer-to-peer information sessions.
   •   Faculty and staff referrals to identify promising candidates.

Conclusion

The SCOE Board’s ability to select a Youth County Board Member from among Youth
Fellows or current students allows for flexible and inclusive representation. Recruiting
through the Fellowship program ensures that potential Youth County Board Members are
already immersed in SCOE environments, have undergone targeted training, and are
prepared to engage meaningfully with the Board and broader student community.
