BACKGROUND:

 

The Sacramento and Placer County Offices of Education received a completed petition to transfer territory from the Center Joint Unified School District (Center District) to the Roseville City School District and Roseville Joint Union High School District (Roseville Districts). The petition requests transfer of the Aspire Solaire neighborhood located in the Westpark area of Roseville, CA 95747, which is bordered by Daylight Drive, Solaire Drive, Santucci Boulevard and Pleasant Grove Boulevard in Roseville, CA within Placer County.

 

The Placer County Superintendent of Schools determined that the petition was legally sufficient, as it contained the signatures of at least 25% of the registered voters residing in the territory and sufficiently described the proposed territory to be transferred. Accordingly, on January 13, 2023, the Placer County Superintendent transmitted the petition to the Placer County Committee on School District Organization and the State Board of Education (SBE). On January 24, 2023, the Sacramento County Superintendent of Schools concurred in this sufficiency determination and transmitted the petition to the Sacramento County Committee on School District Organization (County Committee) and the SBE.

 

RATIONALE AND PROCEDURES:

 

The petition involves school districts within the jurisdictions of both the Sacramento and Placer County Committees. Therefore, both County Committees must consider the petition, make necessary findings and tentative conclusions, and exchange these findings and conclusions before the petition may be granted or denied. (Ed. Code, §§ 35520, 35524.)

 

As part of the petition consideration, public hearings must first be held in each of the affected school districts within 60 days of the County Committees' receipt of the validated petition. (Ed. Code, § 35705.) The Placer County Committee held its public hearing on the petition in the Roseville Districts on February 8, 2023. The Sacramento County Committee is holding this February 25, 2023 public hearing in the Center District.

 

At least 10 days before this hearing, the Sacramento County Committee public hearing notice and attached description of the petition was publicly posted and provided to the affected school districts and the Sacramento and Placer County Local Agency Formation Commissions. (Ed. Code, §§ 35700.5, 35705, 35705.5.) The public hearing notice may be viewed on the County Committee's website at https://www.scoe.net/divisions/board/cocom/.

 

Within 120 days of the initial public hearings or completion of any California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA) requirements, the Sacramento and Placer County Committees will both study the petition under applicable Education Code requirements, make and exchange findings regarding the petition, and grant or deny the petition. If both County Committees grant the petition, an election may be called on this matter. (Ed. Code, §§ 35520, 25524, 35706-35711, 35753.) 

 

SUPERINTENDENT’S RECOMMENDATION:

 

The Superintendent recommends that the County Committee hold a public hearing regarding the petition as required by Education Code section 35705 and that it receive comments from the Chief Petitioner, affected school districts, and members of the public.
