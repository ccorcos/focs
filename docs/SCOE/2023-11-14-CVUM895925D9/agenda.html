Public Comment

 

Anyone may appear at the Board meeting to provide public comment regarding any item on the agenda or regarding any matter that is within the Board’s subject matter jurisdiction. However, the Board may not take action on any item not on the agenda except as authorized by Government Code section 54954.2. If possible, notify the Board President or Board Secretary in writing prior to the meeting if you wish to testify.

 

Individuals wishing to speak in person before the Board are requested to complete a public comment form. Speakers will be called in the order in which the cards are received. 

 

Public comment via Zoom teleconference may be made by using the raised hand feature in Zoom during the agenda item to be addressed. The speaker will be identified by their Display Name in Zoom when called upon to speak. Those joining the meeting via Zoom are requested to display their full name so there is no confusion when called upon to speak. Public comments will be received until the public comment period has elapsed.

 

Comments are limited to a maximum of two (2) minutes per speaker per agenda item, and individuals are limited to a single comment per agenda item.

 

Please contact boardcomments@scoe.net before the meeting if you have questions about the public comment process. 

 

Accommodating Individuals with Disabilities

 

The Sacramento County Office of Education encourages those with disabilities to participate fully in the public meeting process. If you need a disability-related modification or accommodation, including auxiliary aids or services, to participate in the public meeting, contact the Superintendent’s Office at (916) 228-2410 at least 48 hours before the scheduled Board meeting so that we may make every reasonable effort to accommodate you. [Government Code § 54953.2; Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990, § 202 (42 U.S.C. § 12132).]

Alexis Gomez, Administrative Assistant with the Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion (DEI) Department, was nominated by Executive Director Kristin Wright. Kristin shared that, "since the day Alexis started with the DEI team, she has stepped up to not only do everything that has been asked of her by the members of the DEI team, but has gone above and beyond to help her colleagues across Prevention and Early Intervention (PEI) and System of Support (SOS). Alexis is not only competent and reliable, she has pitch-hit when other team members have been on leave and took each opportunity as a chance to learn and build her own skills. Alexis is responsible for coordinating numerous DEI events and trainings both internally and across Sacramento County. It has been both observed and reported by managers across the organization how much they appreciate Alexis' calm and helpful spirit and her willingness to "get the job done", whatever it is. She makes SCOE look and feel hospitable and warm. Her positive "I can" attitude and spirit make working with her easy." Alexis Gomez has been a SCOE employee since September 2021. 

 

CERTIFICATED

 

Ju Park, Alternative Education Teacher, was nominated by Principal Lisa Alcalá. Per Principal Alcalá, "Ms. Park joined our team at Nathaniel S. Colley, Sr. High School during its inaugural school year, 2022-2023. Before working at our community school, Ms. Park was a special education teacher at Bannon Creek Elementary Schools and a Behavior Management Technician at Leo A. Palmiter. What stands out when you enter Ms. Park's classroom is the supportive environment that she creates for her students. She wants them to succeed and makes it difficult for them to fail. In addition to learning about their academic subjects, math, science, health, and P.E., Ms. Park teaches students how to engage in the way that ensures they will leave her class being their own best self-advocate. Students learn how to ask questions, manage frustrations, and support each other in positive ways. Ms. Park is also quick to help her fellow teachers by sharing resources or brainstorming new ideas. Outside of school, you will find Ms. Park at the track or the soccer field supporting her son at one of his sporting competitions. We appreciate Ms. Park's dedication to our program and our students." Ju Park has been a SCOE employee since February 2007.

Department/Director: Educational Services/Brent Malicote

 

Funding Source: California Department of Education

 

Amount Requested: $452,549

 

Fiscal Year(s): 2023-2024

 

Program Description: 

The Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) will be awarded an Expanded Learning Opportunities – Program Technical Assistance (ELO-P) grant which will continue to require SCOE to operate as the Regional Lead for the Region 3 (Alpine, Colusa, El Dorado, Nevada, Placer, Sacramento, Sierra, Sutter, Yolo, and Yuba Counties) before, after, and intersession Expanded Learning programs to provide technical assistance for all ELO-P grantees. This support includes training and assistance related to accessing local, regional, and statewide resources. ELO-P Technical Assistance funds also allow the Regional Lead to provide high quality training to Expanded Learning practitioners that will help provide cohesion between the regular day with the California State Standards and Next Generation Science Standards. Additionally, SCOE will provide direct technical assistance support to grantees at the LEA grant manager/program provider and site levels.

 

The three main areas of focus for the ELO-P Technical Assistance grant are to:

  • Provide technical assistance to all grantees and sites to support the implementation of programs that fully address all ELO-P requirements.
  • Build capacity throughout the region they serve to develop, implement, and sustain support for effective before, after school, and intersession Expanded Learning programs.
  • Invite new Expanded Learning Opportunities Program grantees into current systems and structures that currently exist in the region to help build their Expanded Learning capacity.

 

New Positions: None

 

Subcontracts:

Pass through funding to the following county offices of education: Colusa, El Dorado, Sutter, and Yolo; they will provide technical assistance and support to the ELO-P grantees and programs in their respective counties.

 

Evaluation Component: 

Evaluation, assessment, and continuous improvement will be addressed in the mid-year and end-of-year reports submitted to the California Department of Education.

BACKGROUND:

 

The Sacramento County Superintendent of Schools is required to submit a report each November to the Sacramento County Board of Education describing the state of the schools in the county that have been designated as Williams Schools by the Superintendent of Public Instruction for the 2021-2024 school years. The list includes those schools 1) eligible for Comprehensive Support and Improvement under Every Student Succeeds Act (ESSA); 2) eligible for Additional Targeted Support and Improvement under ESSA; or 3) in which 15 percent or more of the school’s teachers do not possess a valid and clear or preliminary teaching credential. (Ed. Code, § 1240.) 

 

The enclosed report summarizes the results of Williams reviews conducted during the 2022-2023 school year. For additional details regarding the 2022-2023 Williams reviews, please refer to the Annual Williams reports provided by the County Superintendent in September 2023. Electronic copies are available upon request.

 

Currently, the Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) is undertaking visits to Williams Schools for the 2023-2024 school year, reviewing the sufficiency of instructional materials, and considering the condition of the school facilities. The results of these school visits will be included in the First Quarterly 2023-2024 Williams Review Reports, which will be completed in December 2023. These results will be shared with the Board as part of the 2023-2024 Annual Report.

 

AB 1078

 

SCOE will provide additional instructional material oversight in 2023-2024 in compliance with AB 1078, which was recently enacted into law. The law creates new repercussions for school districts that prohibit the use of inclusive textbooks, instructional materials, curriculum, or school library materials in violation of the California FAIR (Fair, Accurate, Inclusive, and Respectful Education) Act, enacted in 2011. A presentation on AB 1078 will occur at a future Board meeting.  

 

This item is provided for information and comment, and no action is required by the Board.
