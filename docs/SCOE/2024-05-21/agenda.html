Public Comment

 

Anyone may appear at the Board meeting to provide public comment regarding any item on the agenda or regarding any matter that is within the Board’s subject matter jurisdiction. However, the Board may not take action on any item not on the agenda except as authorized by Government Code section 54954.2.  If possible, notify the Board President or Board Secretary in writing prior to the meeting if you wish to testify.

 

Individuals wishing to speak in person before the Board are requested to complete a public comment form. Speakers will be called in the order in which the cards are received. 

 

Public comment via Zoom teleconference may be made by using the raised hand feature in Zoom during the agenda item to be addressed. The speaker will be identified by their Display Name in Zoom when called upon to speak. Those joining the meeting via Zoom are requested to display their full name so there is no confusion when called upon to speak. Public comments will be received until the public comment period has elapsed.

 

Comments are limited to a maximum of two (2) minutes per speaker per agenda item, and individuals are limited to a single comment per agenda item.

 

Please contact boardcomments@scoe.net before the meeting if you have questions about the public comment process. 

 

Accommodating Individuals with Disabilities

 

The Sacramento County Office of Education encourages those with disabilities to participate fully in the public meeting process. If you need a disability-related modification or accommodation, including auxiliary aids or services, to participate in the public meeting, contact the Superintendent’s Office at (916) 228-2410 at least 48 hours before the scheduled Board meeting so that we may make every reasonable effort to accommodate you. [Government Code § 54953.2; Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990, § 202 (42 U.S.C. § 12132).]

Department/Director: Educational Services/Dr. Channa Pitt

 

Funding Source: California Department of Food and Agriculture (CDFA)

 

Amount Requested: $200,000

 

Fiscal Year(s): 2024-2025, 2025-2026, 2026-2027

 

Program Description: SCOE's Early Head Start (EHS) Home Visiting Program, the Early Learning Department is applying for the California Farm to School Incubator Grant Program: Track 3. This grant through the California Department of Food and Agriculture (CDFA), will support projects that cultivate equity, nurture students, build climate resilience, and create scalable and sustainable change. The EHS Home Visiing Program provides expectant parents, children ages birth to 36 months, and their families, age and developmentally appropriate child-focused learning experiences in the family's home. This grant funding will enhance nutritional education and provide access to fresh, locally sourced produce for 77 EHS families and three Early Childhood Education sites. This multi-faceted approach encompasses the provision of local, fresh produce boxes delivered to families, immersive food education experiences, and community engagement activities. 

 

This grant opportunity will procure local California grown, whole or minimally processed foods for incorporation into meals/snacks and/or food boxes for young children, especially from local food producers that utilize climate-smart agricultural practices or production systems like certified organic or transitioning to certified organic, small to midsize food producers, veteran food producers, socially disadvantaged food producers, and/or limited-resource farm households in California. Ultimately, it will engage young children in hands-on food education opportunities that are Head Start standards-aligned and culturally relevant opportunities led by educators with a Child Development Teacher Permit. 

 

New Positions: None

 

Subcontracts: None

 

Evaluation Component: SCOE's Early Learning Department will support the collection of data for the California Farm to School Incubator Grant Program.

Department/Director: Center for Student Assessment & Program Accountability (C-SAPA)/Dr. Nancy Herota

 

Funding Source: California Department of Education (CDE)

 

Amount Requested: $6,795,823

 

Fiscal Year(s): 2024-2025, 2025-2026, 2026-2027

 

Program Description: 

The purpose of this contract is to provide independent, objective, technical advice, and consultation services on California’s statewide assessment system to ensure the highest technical quality, as well as the reliability and validity of its results to improve teaching and learning. In addition, this contract will provide a wide array of training, outreach, and professional development services to Local Education Agency’s (LEAs) to help ensure the full and successful implementation of the California statewide assessment system. The following tasks are included in this contract: 1) Coordinate and communicate with the CDE regarding the development of comprehensive training and outreach plans; 2) Provide support for high-quality, valid, and reliable statewide assessment and accountability systems; 3) Develop communication materials and videos for different target audiences (i.e., students, teachers, parents, school board members, etc.) based on the various statewide assessment and accountability systems; 4) Develop and implement a comprehensive and targeted engagement, outreach, and marketing plans to improve understanding, interpretation, and use of the statewide assessment system, including the California Assessment of Student Performance and Progress (CAASPP), English Language Proficiency Assessments for California (ELPAC), and others; and 5) Develop and implement comprehensive plans to provide training to educators from schools and LEAs throughout the state to support all aspects of the statewide assessment system, including the CAASPP and ELPAC.

 

New Positions: None

 

Subcontracts:

  • Captioning Services in the amount of $39,000 to create audio recording transcripts that are required for various projects and activities.
  • Conference Keynote in the amount of $35,000 will address the attendees of the trainings and conferences.
  • Event Registration System in the amount of $60,000 to provide online event management services for meetings, trainings, and conferences.
  • Audio-Visual Services in the amount of $30,000 to provide onsite technology support for conferences.
  • Translation Services in the amount of $110,000 to provide translations of written and web-based deliverables produced.
  • Technical Expert Services in the amount of $139,500 to support the development of a technically sound, valid, and reliable statewide assessment system via research, review, data analysis, and other expert consultative services. 

 

Evaluation Component: SCOE staff will prepare monthly progress reports on all activities in this contract and submit to the CDE per the contract requirements.
