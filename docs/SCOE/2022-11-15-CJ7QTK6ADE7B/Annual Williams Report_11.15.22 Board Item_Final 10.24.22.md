2021-2022 Annual Report of Sacramento County Williams Reviews
November 15, 2022

Williams Reviews

As a result of the Williams Settlement, Education Code section 1240 requires county
offices of education to visit certain schools identified in their county, review information
in the areas noted below, and report the results of the visits and reviews at a regularly
scheduled November meeting. The information below summarizes the results of
reviews conducted throughout the 2021-2022 school year.

To conduct these reviews during fiscal year 2021-2022, SCOE staff visited 107 schools
in 9 school districts within Sacramento County that ranked in deciles 1 to 3 of the 2012
Base Academic Performance Index (Williams Schools).

In August and September 2021, SCOE completed instructional material and facilities
reviews. Ten SCOE employees and 13 exempt temporary employees visited the 107
Williams schools. They reviewed the sufficiency of standards-aligned instructional
materials for 1,593 classrooms and the facility conditions in 2,578 areas within the
schools. The results for these visits were as follows:

Instructional Materials

All Williams schools had sufficient instructional materials in 2021-2022.

Facilities

As for the overall facilities conditions of the 107 schools visited:

    •    71 schools were found to be in exemplary condition,
    •    35 schools were found to be in good condition,
    •    1 school was found to be in fair condition,
    •    0 schools were found to be in poor condition, and
    •    All Williams schools in the Elk Grove, Elverta, Natomas, River Delta, and
         San Juan school districts were in exemplary condition.

School Accountability Report Cards

SCOE staff reviewed whether School Accountability Report Cards (SARCs) were
accurate as to the sufficiency of instructional materials, and the safety, cleanliness, and
adequacy of school facilities for the Williams schools. All SARCs were found to be
generally accurate with a few exceptions that were detailed in the Third Quarterly
Reports to the districts.

Teacher Vacancies and Misassignments

The California Commission on Teacher Credentialing (CCTC) provided final teacher
misassignment and vacancy data for the 2020-2021 schoolyear utilizing the new
monitoring system (CalSAAS). (Ed. Code, § 44258.9.) The monitoring window for
review and correction of 2020-2021 data concluded October 31, 2021.

A misassignment is when a certificated employee is placed in a teaching or services
position for which the employee does not hold a legally recognized certificate or
credential or is otherwise not authorized to hold the position. CCTC recognizes
“corrected misassignments” as teacher misassignments that are corrected prior to an
exception report being generated in CalSAAS at the beginning of the monitoring period.
While these are still considered misassignments, CCTC differentiates them in its
reporting. A vacancy is a position to which a single designated certificated employee
has not been assigned at the beginning of the year or semester. For the districts’
Williams schools there were a total of 152 teacher misassignments, 43 corrected
misassignments, and 14 teacher vacancies:

    •    Elk Grove Unified School District had 4 teacher misassignments, 3 corrected
         teacher misassignments, and 0 teacher vacancies.
    •    Elverta Joint School District had 0 teacher misassignments, 0 corrected teacher
         misassignments, and 0 teacher vacancies.
    •    Folsom. Cordova Unified School District had 0 teacher misassignments,
         1 corrected teacher misassignment, and 0 teacher vacancies.
    •    Natomas Unified School District had 17 teacher misassignments, 0 corrected
         teacher misassignments, and 0 teacher vacancies.
    •    River Delta Unified School District had 2 teacher misassignments, 1 corrected
         teacher misassignment, and 0 teacher vacancies.
    •    Robla School District had 4 teacher misassignments, 2 corrected teacher
         misassignments, and 2 teacher vacancies.
    •    SCUSD had 53 teacher misassignments, 11 corrected misassignments, and
         7 teacher vacancies.
    •    San Juan USD had 17 teacher misassignments, 3 corrected misassignments,
         and 0 teacher vacancies.
    •    Twin Rivers USD had 55 teacher misassignments, 22 corrected misassignments,
         and 5 teacher vacancies.

Uniform Complaints Procedures

Throughout the 2021-2022 school year, SCOE monitored uniform complaints filed in
each of the districts with Williams schools. Seven out of nine districts reported no filed
uniform complaints.

    •    FCUSD self-reported 1 UCP complaint regarding teacher misassignment, which
         was resolved.
    •    TRUSD self-reported 40 UCP complaints regarding facilities and a teacher
         vacancy. Thirty-six facilities issues were reported as resolved; 3 facilities issues
         were reported as unresolved; and 1 teacher vacancy was reported as unresolved
         due to being short staffed with limited substitutes available.
