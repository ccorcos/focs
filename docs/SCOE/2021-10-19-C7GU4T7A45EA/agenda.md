Public Comments\
Public comments may be made via the Zoom Q&A Tool. Once the meeting has
started, click the Q&A button at the bottom of the Zoom window. When the
Q&A window appears, enter your name and the agenda item you wish to
address in the input box, then choose \"Send.\" Be prepared to speak
when prompted by name during the public comments portion of the meeting.
Public Comments will be received until the public comment period has
elapsed. Comments are limited to a maximum of two (2) minutes per
speaker per agenda item, and individuals are limited to a single comment
per agenda item. You may contact boardcomments@scoe.net prior to the
meeting if you have questions about this process.\
\
Anyone may submit public comments to the County Committee on any item
that is within its subject matter jurisdiction. However, the County
Committee may not take action on any item not on the agenda except as
authorized by Government Code section 54954.2. Anyone may provide public
comments in support of or in opposition to any item being presented for
consideration.\

modification or accommodation, including auxiliary aids or services, to
participate in the public meeting, contact the Superintendent's Office
at (916) 228-2410 at least 48 hours before the scheduled meeting so that
we may make every reasonable effort to accommodate you. \[Government
Code § 54953.2; Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990, § 202 (42
U.S.C. § 12132).\]

[[**BACKGROUND:**]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[After each decennial federal census, county board of education trustee
areas must be assessed and potentially adjusted in order to meet legally
required population criteria. (Ed. Code, § 1002(b).) We have contracted
with a demographer, Cooperative Strategies, LLC, to assist the
Sacramento County Board of Education (Board), in its capacity as
Sacramento County Committee on School District Organization (County
Committee), to analyze 2020 census data and provide options for ensuring
the Board\'s trustee areas are compliant with state and federal legal
requirements.  ]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[By law, the difference in population between the smallest and largest
trustee area must be no more than 10%. Based upon assessment of the
Board\'s trustee area population balance using 2020 Census data, the
variance between the smallest and largest trustee areas is 8.3%, which
is within the 10% threshold. Therefore, the County Committee may choose
to maintain the Board\'s current trustee area boundaries, or
make adjustments to further
balance population.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[**NEXT
STEPS:**]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[At the October 19, 2021 meeting, Cooperative Strategies, LLC will
review redistricting standards and the current trustee areas maps,
provide options for potential readjustment scenarios, answer questions,
and seek County Committee input and direction on maintaining or
readjusting the Board\'s trustee area
boundaries.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[After receiving public comment and deliberating, the County
Committee may express its intention to readopt the Board\'s current map,
consider an alternative map, or seek additional options. Based upon the
County Committee\'s input and direction, potential map(s) will be
published and considered for adoption at a subsequent
meeting(s).]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[Once the County Committee adopts the Board\'s final trustee areas map,
the final map will be submitted to the County Registrar of Voters in
Sacramento, San Joaquin, Solano, Placer, and Yolo
counties. ]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[**SUPERINTENDENT\'S
RECOMMENDATION:**]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[The Superintendent recommends that the Sacramento County Committee on
School District Organization review and consider the information
provided by Cooperative Strategies, LLC and take action as it deems
appropriate.]{style="font-size:14px;"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}
