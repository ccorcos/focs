7. Superintendent’s Report

A. Superintendent Gordon reported on the following:

May 3-7, SCOE joined the Sacramento Metro Chamber’s Cap-to-Cap delegation in
Washington D.C. to advocate for critical investments in education. Thank you to our
Board President, Dr. Al Brown, and Trustees Caigoy and Lefkovitz for your participation.
Thank you to our Executive Director of Communications, Xanthi Soriano, who helped
co-lead this year’s Workforce and Education Committee with the Sacramento Metro
Chamber of Commerce. SCOE representatives met with over a dozen Congressional
leaders, including local representatives like Congresswoman Doris Matsui,
Congressman Kevin Kiley, and Congressman Ami Bera, as well as regional leaders.
There were meaningful conversations with national stakeholders including the U.S.
Department of Labor’s Office of Apprenticeships, the U.S. Chamber of Commerce, and
members of both the House and Senate Education and Workforce Committees, as well
Congresswoman Rosa DeLauro, Ranking Member of the House Appropriations
Committee.

On May 8, the Preliminary Administrative Services Credential Program participants,
faculty, and online principal coaches, and the School of Education leadership team,
convened at the home of Dr. L. Steven Winlock, for the end of year Meet and Greet.
Thank you, Trustee Caigoy for attending on behalf of our Board.

On May 8 and 9, two Foundations Academy Graduations were held at the SCOE
Conference Center, honoring students from high schools across the county and the
Jessie Baker Young Adult Program. Thank you, Trustee Keefer, for attending both days
and for showing our families the Board’s commitment to special education.

On May 10, the Sacramento County Superintendents Student Advisory Committee met
to discuss engaging K-8 students in civic education. This conversation will continue this
summer as part of a Summer Civic Academy, leading to planning and implementation of
selected strategies in the 25-26 school year.

On May 13, SCOE hosted the Legacy of Dedication event, honoring SCOE employees
with 30, 35, 40, and even 45 years of service.

On May 15, SCOE celebrated students at the School to World Awards at Leo A.
Palmiter Jr./Sr. High School. Thanks to our business partners and the WorkAbility team,
94 students were placed in paid work experiences—several of them earning direct hire
offers.

Also on May 15, SCOE welcomed 750 attendees to the SEL & Wellness Summit in

California counties. The summit emphasized the power of relationship-centered schools

On May 20, SCOE hosted an ice cream social to honor our Classified School

Preceding today’s Board meeting, SCOE held a powerful Art Talk on Searching for
Sanctuary featuring artist Milton 510 Bowens. Through his work and voice, we explored
what safety and sanctuary mean—particularly for Black Americans—and how art can be
a force for justice and healing.

This month, we recognized students from our construction programs at Nathaniel
Colley, Eleanor Hickey, and Leo A. Palmiter Jr./Sr. High School, who won the
Craftsmanship Award during the 40th annual Design Build Competition for Northern
California, hosted by the Construction Industry Education Foundation. Held at
Cosumnes River College, this hands-on competition gave students a chance to shine—
literally building their futures from the ground up. Instructor Mike Anckner summed it up
best: "We watched their confidence grow right before our eyes." A big congratulations to
all 16 students and their supporting staff. On May 7, El Centro students also
participated. This is their 4th year in the Sacramento Regional Builders Exchange
(SRBX) Design Build competition.

Senior Extension Graduation will be held on May 23 at 1 p.m. at the Scottish Rite
Masonic Center in Sacramento. Palmiter Graduation and the 8th Grade Promotion are
set for May 28 at 10 a.m.

Also on May 28, SCOE will gather for the Retiree Dinner in the Mather Room to wish
our colleagues a fond farewell and to celebrate their contributions. We’ll bring our entire
organization together for the All Employee In-Service and Collaboration Summit on May
30—a celebration of teamwork, impact, and a strong finish to the school year.

Coming up in June, SCOE will once again show our support for LGBTQIA2S+
communities and culture by participating in the annual Sacramento PRIDE March and
Parade on June 15th, 2025, at 11:00 a.m., organized by the Sacramento LGBT
Community Center.
