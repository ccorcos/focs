[[**Program

[[The Fresno County Superintendent of Schools (FCSS), Early Care and
Education Department, is facilitating the Count Play Explore
Professional Learning and Coaching (CPE PLC) Cohort, a statewide
initiative involving teams from County Offices of Education, School
Districts, non-profit organizations, and other entities that provide
technical assistance, training, coaching, and capacity-building support
for the early care and education (ECE) workforce from birth through
grade 3. Through this grant, four Sacramento County Office of Education
(SCOE) team members specializing in Early Learning, Math, Science, and
Computer Science will receive targeted training and coaching to
implement Count, Play, Explore with ECE educators across Sacramento
County Schools. In addition to building instructional expertise, the
team will strengthen their ability to identify and utilize community
resources that support high-quality math, science, and computer science
instruction in early learning
settings. ]{style="font-family:arial,helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[This grant complements and expands SCOE\'s existing efforts,
particularly aligning with the California Math, Science, Computer
Science (CalMSCS) Grant, which supports professional learning for grades
4-12. By focusing on birth through grade 3, the CPE grant ensures SCOE
provides a coherent continuum of support in these critical content areas
from birth through grade 12. In parallel, the Early Learning Department
is convening approximately 45 preschool educators through the California
Preschool Instructional Network (CPIN) to participate in Count, Play,
Explore training facilitated by FCSS. This initiative is further
strengthened by collaboration with SCOE\'s Curriculum and Instruction
and College and Career Readiness teams, which offer ongoing professional
learning aligned to the California Content Standards and Frameworks in
Math, Science, and Computer
Science. ]{style="font-family:arial,helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}
