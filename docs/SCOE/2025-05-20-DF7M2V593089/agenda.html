Public Comment

 

Anyone may appear at the Board meeting to provide public comment regarding any item on the agenda or regarding any matter that is within the Board’s subject matter jurisdiction. However, the Board may not take action on any item not on the agenda except as authorized by Government Code section 54954.2. If possible, notify the Board President or Board Secretary in writing prior to the meeting if you wish to testify.

 

Individuals wishing to speak in person before the Board are requested to complete a public comment form. Speakers will be called in the order in which the cards are received. 

 

Public comment via Zoom teleconference may be made by using the raised hand feature in Zoom during the agenda item to be addressed. The speaker will be identified by their Display Name in Zoom when called upon to speak. Those joining the meeting via Zoom are requested to display their full name so there is no confusion when called upon to speak. Public comments will be received until the public comment period has elapsed.

 

Comments are limited to a maximum of two (2) minutes per speaker per agenda item, and individuals are limited to a single comment per agenda item.

 

Please contact boardcomments@scoe.net before the meeting if you have questions about the public comment process. 

 

Accommodating Individuals with Disabilities

 

The Sacramento County Office of Education encourages those with disabilities to participate fully in the public meeting process. If you need a disability-related modification or accommodation, including auxiliary aids or services, to participate in the public meeting, contact the Superintendent’s Office at (916) 228-2410 at least 48 hours before the scheduled Board meeting so that we may make every reasonable effort to accommodate you. [Government Code § 54953.2; Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990, § 202 (42 U.S.C. § 12132).]

Department/Cabinet Member:  Educational Services/Dr. Channa Pitt

 

Funding Source: Fresno County Superintendent of Schools

 

Amount Requested: $350,000

 

Fiscal Years: 2024-2025, 2025-2026, 2026-2027, 2027-2028

 

Program Description: 

The Fresno County Superintendent of Schools (FCSS), Early Care and Education Department, is facilitating the Count Play Explore Professional Learning and Coaching (CPE PLC) Cohort, a statewide initiative involving teams from County Offices of Education, School Districts, non-profit organizations, and other entities that provide technical assistance, training, coaching, and capacity-building support for the early care and education (ECE) workforce from birth through grade 3. Through this grant, four Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) team members specializing in Early Learning, Math, Science, and Computer Science will receive targeted training and coaching to implement Count, Play, Explore with ECE educators across Sacramento County Schools. In addition to building instructional expertise, the team will strengthen their ability to identify and utilize community resources that support high-quality math, science, and computer science instruction in early learning settings. 

 

This grant complements and expands SCOE's existing efforts, particularly aligning with the California Math, Science, Computer Science (CalMSCS) Grant, which supports professional learning for grades 4-12. By focusing on birth through grade 3, the CPE grant ensures SCOE provides a coherent continuum of support in these critical content areas from birth through grade 12. In parallel, the Early Learning Department is convening approximately 45 preschool educators through the California Preschool Instructional Network (CPIN) to participate in Count, Play, Explore training facilitated by FCSS. This initiative is further strengthened by collaboration with SCOE's Curriculum and Instruction and College and Career Readiness teams, which offer ongoing professional learning aligned to the California Content Standards and Frameworks in Math, Science, and Computer Science. 

 

New Positions: None

 

Subcontracts: None

 

Evaluation Component: 

The implementation of the grant will be monitored by the FCSS through attendance at two virtual meetings per year with CPE leadership and participation in implementation logs, surveys, focus groups, and/or interviews with CPE's independent evaluator, WestED. A final report is due after the end of the grant period on June 30, 2027.
