[[Through this grant SCOE will operate as a Regional Science,
Technology, Engineering, Art, and Mathematics (STEAM) Hub to provide
Region 3 Expanded Learning practitioners with technical assistance to
support high-quality STEAM learning activities, skills, and strategies
during the expanded learning hours; including before, after, and during
intersession Expanded Learning programs. This support includes training
and assistance related to accessing local, regional, and statewide
resources to assist Expanded Learning programs that serve TK-12 grade
students. These funds allow the Region 3 STEAM Hub to facilitate a
community of practice to provide high-quality training to staff that
will help support cohesion with the Next Generation Science Standards,
Art, hands-on activities, project-based learning that engages the whole
child. Additionally, SCOE provides direct support to the STEAM Hub
participants at the site level and through regional events.
]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[The two main areas of focus for this grant period are
to:]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

[[1. Provide professional development and resources through the
Community of Practice model, to the expanded learning programs staff and
leadership with a STEAM focus.
]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

[[2. Provide opportunities and resources that help to engage all
expanded learning program students in high-quality STEAM
activities. ]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

[[[In accordance with the requirements of Education Code Section
60119(c), the Sacramento County Board of Education (Board) will hold a
public hearing during the August 13, 2024 Regular Board Meeting to
determine whether each pupil in the juvenile court schools has
sufficient textbooks or instructional materials, or both, in
English/language arts, including the English language development
component of an adopted program, mathematics, history/social science,
and science that are aligned to the state content standards adopted by
the State Board of Education (SBE).
]{style="line-height:107%"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

[[[As part of this determination, the Board also must determine if each
pupil who is actually enrolled in a foreign language or health course
has sufficient instructional materials that are consistent with the
content and cycles of curriculum frameworks adopted by the SBE. In
addition, the Board must determine if pupils enrolled in a laboratory
science course have adequate equipment.
]{style="line-height:107%"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

[[[Pupils enrolled in a health course will have sufficient materials.
The Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) does not offer foreign
language instruction in any grades, nor does SCOE offer science
laboratory courses in any of grades 9 through 12. Therefore, SCOE need
not maintain science laboratory equipment. Completion of a science
laboratory course is not a requirement for high school graduation,
though completion of a course in either foreign language or visual or
performing arts is a requirement (EC 51225.3). As necessary for
completion of this graduation requirement, SCOE students are provided a
course in fine arts.
]{style="line-height:107%"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

[[[Detail substantiating that sufficient instructional materials are
available to each student will be available for inspection by the Board
and public at the
hearing.]{style="line-height:107%"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

[[[The CCCA proposes to enroll international students starting this
year. The total number of international students will not exceed 5% of
enrollment. The CCCA is working with an independent agency, the Council
on International Educational Exchange, which will refer the students to
CCCA for enrollment. The CCCA cannot claim state funding for these
students nor can they charge tuition, however, they are requesting to
accept a fee from the Council on International Educational Exchange for
the students. The CCCA indicates that it can accommodate these students
without significant additional
expense.]{style="line-height:normal"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}

 

[[[The CCCA indicates that they are pursuing this program for the
benefit of local Sacramento students who may not otherwise have exposure

visitors will enhance class discussions and facilitate cross-cultural
understanding.]{style="line-height:normal"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}
