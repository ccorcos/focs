Public Comment

 

Anyone may appear at the Board meeting to provide public comment regarding any item on the agenda or regarding any matter that is within the Board’s subject matter jurisdiction. However, the Board may not take action on any item not on the agenda except as authorized by Government Code section 54954.2.  If possible, notify the Board President or Board Secretary in writing prior to the meeting if you wish to testify.

 

Individuals wishing to speak in person before the Board are requested to complete a public comment form. Speakers will be called in the order in which the cards are received. 

 

Public comment via Zoom teleconference may be made by using the raised hand feature in Zoom during the agenda item to be addressed. The speaker will be identified by their Display Name in Zoom when called upon to speak. Those joining the meeting via Zoom are requested to display their full name so there is no confusion when called upon to speak. Public comments will be received until the public comment period has elapsed.

 

Comments are limited to a maximum of two (2) minutes per speaker per agenda item, and individuals are limited to a single comment per agenda item.

 

Please contact boardcomments@scoe.net before the meeting if you have questions about the public comment process. 

 

Accommodating Individuals with Disabilities

 

The Sacramento County Office of Education encourages those with disabilities to participate fully in the public meeting process. If you need a disability-related modification or accommodation, including auxiliary aids or services, to participate in the public meeting, contact the Superintendent’s Office at (916) 228-2410 at least 48 hours before the scheduled Board meeting so that we may make every reasonable effort to accommodate you. [Government Code § 54953.2; Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990, § 202 (42 U.S.C. § 12132).]

BACKGROUND:

 

The California Department of Education Consolidated Application Part I allows school districts and county offices of education to apply for federal categorical program funding. This apportionment is made from federal funds provided to the state. This year the Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) will apply for four federally funded programs listed on the Consolidated Application for 2024-2025.

 

Federal Programs

 

  • Title I, Part A: Improving Basic Programs Operated by Local Educational Agencies - preliminary award amount is estimated $1,353,122 for the 2024-2025 school year.

 

  • Title I, Part D, Subpart 2: Prevention and Intervention Programs for Children and Youth Who Are Neglected, Delinquent or At-Risk - preliminary award amount is estimated $1,080,081 for the 2024-2025 school year.

 

  • Title II, Part A: Supporting Effective Instruction of the Elementary and Secondary Education Act of 1965 (ESEA), as amended by Every Student Succeeds Act (ESSA) (Public Law 114-95) - preliminary award amount is estimated $20,439 for the 2024-2025 school year.

 

  • Title IV, Part A: Student and School Support (ESSA Sec. 4101) - preliminary award amount is estimated $109,658 for the 2024-2025 school year.

 

Title I Programs

 

Title I, Part A funds are used to support effective, evidence-based educational strategies that close the achievement gap and enable students to meet the state's challenging academic standards.

 

Title I, Part D provides funds to improve educational services for formerly incarcerated youth or for neglected youth so that such youth have the opportunity to meet the same challenging state academic standards that all youth in the state are expected to meet. Additionally, Title ID funds may be used to provide such youth with the services needed to make a successful transition from incarceration to further schooling or employment.

 

Title II, Part A program funds are apportioned to local educational agencies (LEAs) to increase student academic achievement through strategies focused on preparing, training, and recruiting high-quality teachers, principals, or other school leaders.

 

Title IV, Part A: Student Support and Academic Enrichment is a federal program to increase the capacity of LEAs to meet the goals of the ESSA by providing all students with access to a well-rounded education, improving school conditions for student learning, and improving use of technology in order to improve the academic achievement and digital literacy of all students.

 

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:

 

The Superintendent recommends that the Board approve the submission of the Consolidated Application Part I and the acceptance of funds allocated for the 2024-2025 school year.

Program Description: 

The Teacher Residency Capacity grant supports a collaborative partnership between Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) and Pacific Oaks College to strengthen and support SCOE’s Teacher Residency Program. The Teacher Residency Capacity grant funding will support staffing for both SCOE and Pacific Oaks College, mentor development, and stipends for participants.

In accordance with the requirements of Education Code Section 60119(c), the Sacramento County Board of Education (Board) will hold a public hearing during the August 13, 2024 Regular Board Meeting to determine whether each pupil in the juvenile court schools has sufficient textbooks or instructional materials, or both, in English/language arts, including the English language development component of an adopted program, mathematics, history/social science, and science that are aligned to the state content standards adopted by the State Board of Education (SBE).

As part of this determination, the Board also must determine if each pupil who is actually enrolled in a foreign language or health course has sufficient instructional materials that are consistent with the content and cycles of curriculum frameworks adopted by the SBE. In addition, the Board must determine if pupils enrolled in a laboratory science course have adequate equipment.

Pupils enrolled in a health course will have sufficient materials. The Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) does not offer foreign language instruction in any grades, nor does SCOE offer science laboratory courses in any of grades 9 through 12. Therefore, SCOE need not maintain science laboratory equipment. Completion of a science laboratory course is not a requirement for high school graduation, though completion of a course in either foreign language or visual or performing arts is a requirement (EC 51225.3). As necessary for completion of this graduation requirement, SCOE students are provided a course in fine arts.

Detail substantiating that sufficient instructional materials are available to each student will be available for inspection by the Board and public at the hearing.

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:

 

The Superintendent recommends that the Board adopt Resolution No. 24-16 - Determination of Textbooks and/or Instructional Materials Sufficiency Pursuant to Education Code Section 60119 for Juvenile Court Schools.

BACKGROUND:

 

A public hearing will be held by the Board during the August 13, 2024 Regular Board Meeting to receive public comment and input from the community regarding the proposed material revision to the Capital College & Career Academy (CCCA) Charter School petition. 

 

The CCCA proposes to enroll international students starting this year. The total number of international students will not exceed 5% of enrollment. The CCCA is working with an independent agency, the Council on International Educational Exchange, which will refer the students to CCCA for enrollment. The CCCA cannot claim state funding for these students nor can they charge tuition, however, they are requesting to accept a fee from the Council on International Educational Exchange for the students. The CCCA indicates that it can accommodate these students without significant additional expense.

 

The CCCA indicates that they are pursuing this program for the benefit of local Sacramento students who may not otherwise have exposure to diverse global perspectives. They expect that international student visitors will enhance class discussions and facilitate cross-cultural understanding.

 

Revisions will be needed to CCCA’s charter and the Sacramento County Board of Education's Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) with the CCCA to reflect this program.

 

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:

 

The Superintendent recommends that the Board adopt Resolution No. 24-17 - Approve August 13, 2024 Material Revision to CCCA's Charter School Petition.
