Public Comment

 

Anyone may appear at the Board meeting to provide public comment regarding any item on the agenda or regarding any matter that is within the Board’s subject matter jurisdiction. However, the Board may not take action on any item not on the agenda except as authorized by Government Code section 54954.2. If possible, notify the Board President or Board Secretary in writing prior to the meeting if you wish to testify.

 

Individuals wishing to speak in person before the Board are requested to complete a public comment form. Speakers will be called in the order in which the cards are received. 

 

Public comment via Zoom teleconference may be made by using the raised hand feature in Zoom during the agenda item to be addressed. The speaker will be identified by their Display Name in Zoom when called upon to speak. Those joining the meeting via Zoom are requested to display their full name so there is no confusion when called upon to speak. Public comments will be received until the public comment period has elapsed.

 

Comments are limited to a maximum of two (2) minutes per speaker per agenda item, and individuals are limited to a single comment per agenda item.

 

Please contact contactscoe@scoe.net before the meeting if you have questions about the public comment process. 

 

Accommodating Individuals with Disabilities

 

The Sacramento County Office of Education encourages those with disabilities to participate fully in the public meeting process. If you need a disability-related modification or accommodation, including auxiliary aids or services, to participate in the public meeting, contact the Superintendent’s Office at (916) 228-2410 at least 48 hours before the scheduled Board meeting so that we may make every reasonable effort to accommodate you. [Government Code § 54953.2; Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990, § 202 (42 U.S.C. § 12132).]

BACKGROUND:

The County Superintendent of Schools is required to submit a report each November to the County Board of Education describing the state of the schools in the county that have been designated as Williams Schools by the State Superintendent of Public Instruction (SSPI). The list includes those schools 1) eligible for Comprehensive Support and Improvement under Every Student Succeeds Act (ESSA); 2) eligible for Additional Targeted Support and Improvement under ESSA; or 3) in which 15 percent or more of the school’s teachers do not possess a valid and clear or preliminary teaching credential. (Ed. Code, § 1240.) For the 2024-2025 school year, county superintendents used the 2021-2024 list of Williams schools, and the SSPI released a new list in April 2025 for 2025-2027. 

The attached report summarizes the results of Williams reviews conducted during the 2024-2025 school year. For additional details regarding these reviews, please refer to the Annual Williams reports provided by the County Superintendent in September 2025. Electronic copies are available upon request.

Currently, SCOE is undertaking visits to Williams Schools for the 2025-2026 school year, reviewing the sufficiency of instructional materials, and considering the condition of the school facilities. The results of these school visits will be included in the First Quarterly 2025-2026 Williams Review Reports, which will be completed in December 2025 and subsequently shared with the Board as part of the 2025-2026 Annual Report. There are 170 schools on SCOE's new Williams list, but SCOE staff will visit 195 sites this year due to schools having multiple campuses and year-round programs.

This item is provided for information and comment. No action is required by the Board.

A. Capital College & Career Academy Update on Financial Sustainability - Dr. Nancy Herota/Nicolas Schweizer

BACKGROUND:

 

At its September 16, 2025 Board meeting, the Sacramento County Board of Education adopted a motion to require CCCA to provide sufficient documentation to demonstrate that it can remain financially solvent throughout the 2025-2026 school year. The Board action required that this documentation be provided within 30 days (by October 16, 2025) so that it could be presented at the November 18, 2025 Board meeting.

 

AREAS OF CONCERN:

 

At the September 16, 2025 Board meeting, SCOE staff shared with the Board several areas of concern relating to CCCA’s fiscal sustainability, including:

 

  • Since its opening in 2023-2024 to the current school year, CCCA's actual enrollment has been 35–50% lower than projected in its charter petition.
  • These large differences in projected versus actual enrollment have resulted in significant shortfalls in revenues and heavy reliance on fundraising and one-time grants to cover operational costs.
  • In addition, CCCA has not maintained the required 5% reserve for economic uncertainties required by the MOU in any year of its operation and is at risk of running out of cash in a few months.
  • CCCA has little cash on hand and has needed to borrow and delay paying vendors to maintain its operations.  
  • At the time of the September Board meeting, CCCA did not have documentation to support the amount of one-time grants and donations it needed to support its operations. After repeated requests by SCOE, on October 26, 2025, CCCA provided additional information relating to the one-time grants and donations it needs to avoid running out of cash this year as well as updated budget and cashflow projections. 

 

Based upon the information provided to date, it appears that CCCA will have sufficient funds to continue operating throughout the 2025-2026 school year. However, it will still have a significant amount of outstanding debt and unpaid invoices.

 

If CCCA runs out of cash and becomes insolvent in the middle of a school year, students will be displaced and at risk of losing educational credit for any uncompleted semesters at CCCA. Even if CCCA can make payroll and remain open through the end of 2025-2026 notwithstanding its significant debt and shortage of cash, its financial condition in 2026-2027 will be even more precarious.

 

At the November 18, 2025 Board meeting, CCCA and SCOE staff will provide an update to the Board regarding this information and CCCA’s ongoing fiscal sustainability.

 

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:

 

The Superintendent recommends that the Board consider the CCCA's ongoing fiscal sustainability and determine whether any other next steps or actions are appropriate at this time.

BACKGROUND:

 

Sacramento County Board of Education (Board) seats in Trustee Areas I, II, and III are up for re-election in June 2026. The cost of this Trustee election will be substantially less expensive if it is consolidated with the statewide primary election held on June 2, 2026. To consolidate its Trustee election with the statewide election, the Board must adopt and file a resolution requesting consolidation.

 

Resolution No. 25-16 calls for the election of Board Trustees, and requests that it be consolidated with the primary election scheduled for June 2, 2026 for the following Trustee Areas and terms of office:

 

Trustee Area I

Said Term: 4 years

(07/01/2026 – 06/30/2030)

 

Trustee Area II

Said Term: 4 years

(07/01/2026 – 06/30/2030)

 

Trustee Area III

Said Term: 4 years

(07/01/2026 – 06/30/2030)

 

Trustee Areas 1 and 3 are fully within Sacramento County. Trustee Area 2 is primarily within Sacramento County, with a small portion extending into Placer County. 

 

The Resolution sets forth other guidelines for the election. To reduce election costs, the Resolution asks the Sacramento and Placer County Registrars to combine the required notice of election with other special districts. It also declares that individual candidate(s) shall be responsible for and prepay the publication cost of the voluntary candidate’s statement in the Sample Official Ballot Pamphlet, and it designates the number of words that may be in the candidate’s statement.

 

The Board will need to determine whether the candidate’s statement will be a maximum of either 200 or 400 words. The Sacramento County Elections Office estimates that a 400 word statement will cost approximately $5,650 - $6,150 per candidate for the Voter Information Guide Statement and $600 for the online-only statement, while a 200 word statement will cost approximately $5,350 - $5,850 per candidate for the Voter Information Guide Statement and $300 for the online only statement. The Placer County Elections Office estimates that a 400 word statement will cost $370, while a 200 word statement will cost $210.  

 

By law, Sacramento County Board of Education members are elected on the same date as the statewide direct primary election and take office on the first day of July. (Ed. Code, § 1007.) In 2026, and in years without a presidential election, the statewide direct primary is held on the first Tuesday after the first Monday in June in each even-numbered year. (Elec. Code, § 316.) In presidential election years, the statewide direct primary is held on the first Tuesday after the first Monday in March.

 

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:

 

The Superintendent recommends the Board determine the maximum number of words for the voluntary candidate’s statement and adopt Resolution No. 25-16 Calling for Election of Trustees to the Sacramento County Board of Education on June 2, 2026.

BACKGROUND:

 

David W. Gordon, Sacramento County Superintendent of Schools, recently announced his plan to retire as of June 30, 2027, to give the Sacramento County Board of Education (Board) ample time to search for a successor and ensure a smooth transition in leadership.

 

The Board may wish to begin the process of identifying potential executive search firms to assist with the recruitment of exceptional candidates for the County Superintendent role.
