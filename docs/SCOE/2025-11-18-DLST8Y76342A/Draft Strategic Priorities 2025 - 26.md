                Sacramento County Board of Education
                         Strategic Priorities
                             2025-2026


PRIORITY 1

Continue efforts to close the achievement gap and expand
opportunities for every student; increase access to mental health,
wellness, and social emotional support; and prepare students to
successfully transition to college, career, and community in SCOE
schools.

Strategies:
   ● Align individual student planning with assessment data and culturally
      relevant, research-based practices grounded in high expectations.
   ● Implement evidence-based instructional strategies and targeted
      interventions to accelerate literacy growth for all students.
   ● Implement recommendations from the Landscape Analysis:
      Understanding the School Experiences of Black Students in
      Sacramento County report.
   ● Use high-impact instructional practices to meet the needs of diverse
      learners, including neurodiverse students, students with disabilities,
      and multilingual learners.
   ● Ensure pathways to college and career readiness through work-
      based learning, CTE, workability, and dual enrollment opportunities.
   ● Utilize counselors to ensure student wellness through collaboration
      with SCOE staff—including mental health clinicians—and through
      partner agencies and referral systems.
   ● Build strong, authentic partnerships with community organizations to
      establish schools as community hubs and connect students to local
      resources and opportunities.

PRIORITY 2

Continue efforts to close the achievement gap, expand opportunities
for every student, and increase access to mental health, wellness, and
social emotional support in local educational agencies.

Strategies:
   ● Continue to expand Centers of Wellness throughout Sacramento
      County.
   ● Increase collaborative partnerships to expand career pipelines for
      teachers, administrators, and mental health practitioners in the
      region.
   ● Provide support and technical assistance for social emotional
      learning for districts and schools in Sacramento County.
   ● Continue to expand opportunities for funding and resources for
      behavioral health support in schools.
   ● Provide guidance and resources to champion the implementation of
      recommendations from the Landscape Analysis: Understanding the
      School Experiences of Black Students in Sacramento County report.
   ● Continue Sacramento READS and Dolly Parton Imagination Library,
      countywide literacy initiatives.
   ● Provide strategic and differentiated support throughout the math
      adoption process to ensure the selection of high-quality instructional
      materials.
   ● Expand networks focused on dropout prevention and continue to
      support districts to reduce chronic absenteeism.
   ● Provide data tools and training on data use to help Local Educational
      Agencies (LEAs) use data to analyze student strengths, areas of
      need, and to uplift student voice.
   ● Support LEAs through Improvement Networks to conduct systems
      analysis to understand student experiences/equity gaps.

PRIORITY 3

Expand opportunities for youth leadership, implement new civic
engagement initiatives, and enhance family and community
engagement.

Strategies:
   ● Continue to work with students to provide leadership opportunities for
      SCOE students, including the Youth Advisory Committee at Nathaniel
      S. Colley, Sr. High School and Elinor Lincoln Hickey Jr./Sr. High
      School/Leo A. Palmiter Jr./Sr. High School.
   ● Continue to provide opportunities for youth to work as SCOE Student
      Fellows, including the Youth County Board Member.
   ● Work with the Sacramento County Board of Education and
      Superintendent’s Youth Advisory Council that is comprised of youth
      representatives from districts, to promote civic awareness, leadership
      development, and engagement.
   ● Pilot a strong Civic Education emphasis in select elementary and
      middle schools, including SCOE schools.
   ● Continue to support and enhance the Family and Community
      Engagement initiative.
   ● Engage students to provide input and support the implementation of
      the recommendations from the Landscape Analysis: Understanding
      the School Experiences of Black Students in Sacramento County
      report.
