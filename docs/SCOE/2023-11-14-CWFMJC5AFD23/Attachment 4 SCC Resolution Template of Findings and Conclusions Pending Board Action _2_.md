SACRAMENTO COUNTY COMMITTEE ON SCHOOL DISTRICT ORGANIZATION

Findings and Tentative Conclusions Regarding Petition to Transfer Territory
From Center Joint Unified School District to Roseville City School District and
Roseville Joint Union High School District

Resolution No. CC-23-01

WHEREAS, in December 2022, the Sacramento and Placer County Offices of
Education each received a completed petition (Petition) to transfer territory presently
under the jurisdiction of the Center Joint Unified School District (Center Districts) and
the Sacramento County Superintendent of Schools, to the Roseville Joint Union High
School District and the Roseville City School District (Roseville Districts) under the
jurisdiction of the Placer County Superintendent of Schools; and

WHEREAS, the territory proposed to be transferred and its 213 parcels of residential
property, known as the “Aspire Solaire” neighborhood, are located in the City of
Roseville, County of Placer, and bounded by Pleasant Grove Boulevard, Solaire Drive,
Santucci Boulevard, and Daylight Drive; and

WHEREAS, in accordance with Education Code section 35521, in January 2023, both
County Superintendents determined the Petition to be legally sufficient and thereafter
transmitted the Petition to the State Board of Education, as well as their respective
County Committees on School District Organization; and

WHEREAS, on February 8, 2023, the Placer County Committee on School District
Organization (Placer County Committee) held a public hearing on the Petition within the
boundaries of the Roseville Districts; and

WHEREAS, on February 25, 2023, the Sacramento County Committee on School
District Organization (Sacramento County Committee) held a public hearing on the
Petition within the boundaries of Center District and, at least 10 days before the public
hearing, a description of the petition was provided to the public and the affected
governing boards; and

WHEREAS, on March 15, 2023, the Center District governing board adopted Resolution
No. 20/2022-23 declaring Center District’s opposition to the Petition and provided a
copy of this resolution to both County Committees; and

WHEREAS, on August 7, 2023, the Placer County Committee determined that the
proposed territory transfer would be exempt from the requirements of the California
Environmental Quality Act (CEQA); and

WHEREAS, following public hearing on August 15, 2023, the Sacramento County
Committee determined that the proposed territory transfer was categorically exempt
under CEQA without exception in accordance with Public Resources Code section
21084 and Cal. Code Regs., tit. 14, sections 15300.2 and 15314; and

WHEREAS, Education Code section 35710 provides that a county committee may
approve a legally sufficient petition to transfer territory between two or more school

WHEREAS, Education Code section 35524 specifies that when separate hearings are
conducted in each county, a decision to grant or deny the proposed reorganization shall
be made only after findings and tentative conclusions (Findings and Tentative
Conclusions) of the hearings in each county have been transmitted to the officer or
agency which conducted the hearings in each of the other counties; and

WHEREAS, on October 30, 2023, the Placer County Committee held a public hearing
wherein it (1) failed to either approve or disapprove the Petition and, (2) found the
statutory criteria set forth in Education Code section 35753 to be substantially met; and

WHEREAS, Sacramento County Committee has considered the GFS-JPA Report and
all written and oral comments submitted in support of and in opposition to the Petition.
