Public Comment

 

Anyone may appear at the County Committee meeting to provide public comment regarding any item on the agenda or regarding any matter that is within the Committee's subject matter jurisdiction. However, the Committee may not take action on any item not on the agenda except as authorized by Government Code section 54954.2. If possible, notify the Committee Chair in writing prior to the meeting if you wish to testify.

 

Individuals wishing to speak in person before the Committee are requested to complete a public comment form. Speakers will be called in the order in which the cards are received. 

 

Public comment via Zoom teleconference may be made by using the raised hand feature in Zoom during the agenda item to be addressed. The speaker will be identified by their Display Name in Zoom when called upon to speak. Those joining the meeting via Zoom are requested to display their full name so there is no confusion when called upon to speak. Public comments will be received until the public comment period has elapsed.

 

Comments are limited to a maximum of two (2) minutes per speaker per agenda item, and individuals are limited to a single comment per agenda item.

 

Please contact boardcomments@scoe.net before the meeting if you have questions about the public comment process. 

 

Accommodating Individuals with Disabilities

 

The Sacramento County Office of Education encourages those with disabilities to participate fully in the public meeting process. If you need a disability-related modification or accommodation, including auxiliary aids or services, to participate in the public meeting, contact the Superintendent’s Office at (916) 228-2410 at least 48 hours before the scheduled Board meeting so that we may make every reasonable effort to accommodate you. [Government Code § 54953.2; Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990, § 202 (42 U.S.C. § 12132).]

The Sacramento County Committee on School District Organization (County Committee) is in the process of evaluating a territory transfer petition to transfer certain inhabited territory from the Center Joint Unified School District to the Roseville City School District and the Roseville Joint Union High School District (Petition). The Petition requests transfer of the Aspire Solaire neighborhood located in the Westpark area of Roseville, California 95747, bordered by Daylight Drive, Solaire Drive, Santucci Boulevard, and Pleasant Grove Boulevard in Roseville, California within Placer County.

BACKGROUND ON THE TERRITORY TRANSFER PETITION:

 

A territory transfer petition (Petition) was submitted to the Sacramento and Placer County Offices of Education in December 2022. (Attachment 1) The territory proposed to be transferred is referred to as the "Aspire Solaire" neighborhood and is located in the City of Roseville, County of Placer. It is currently comprised of 144 developed homes and 66 vacant lots. Petitioners request that their neighborhood be transferred from Center Joint Unified School District (Center District) to the Roseville City School District and the Roseville Joint Union High School District (Roseville Districts). Center District has submitted a resolution opposing the territory transfer. (Attachment 2)

 

Because the petition involves school districts within the jurisdictions of the Sacramento County Committee on School District Organization (Sacramento County Committee) and the Placer County Committee on School District Organization (Placer County Committee), both County Committees must consider the Petition, comply with the California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA), and adhere to the requirements to approve or disapprove the petition. 

 

The County Committees previously held two public hearings in their respective districts regarding this Petition, including initial public hearings in February 2023 and public hearings in August 2023, in which both County Committees determined the proposed territory transfer to be categorically exempt from CEQA. Prior to making a final decision, each County Committee must transmit findings and tentative conclusions recommending approval or disapproval of the petition to the other County Committee.

 

REQUIRED CONDITIONS FOR REORGANIZATION:

 

Before approving a proposal to reorganize school districts, the Sacramento County Committee must first determine if the proposal substantially meets each of the conditions in Education Code section 35753. If the Sacramento County Committee finds that the conditions are substantially met, it may approve the Petition, but it has discretion to still disapprove the Petition. (Ed. Code, § 35710(a).) Guidance issued by the California Department of Education (CDE) advises a County Committee to disapprove a petition unless there is a compelling reason to approve, such as a local educational need or concern that the reorganization addresses.

 

The Placer County Superintendent of Schools and the Sacramento County Superintendent of Schools engaged Government Financial Services Joint Powers Authority (GFS) to evaluate whether the Petition satisfied the statutory criteria. GFS produced a written report (Attachment 3) concluding that all of the Education Code section 35753 conditions, listed below, are substantially met:

 

  1. The reorganized districts will be adequate in terms of the number of pupils enrolled.
  2. The school districts are each organized on the basis of a substantial community identity.
  3. The proposal will result in an equitable division of property and facilities of the original district or districts.
  4. The reorganization of the school districts will preserve each affected district’s ability to educate pupils in an integrated environment and will not promote racial or ethnic discrimination or segregation.
  5. Any increase in costs to the state as a result of the proposed reorganization will be insignificant and otherwise incidental to the reorganization.
  6. The proposed reorganization will continue to promote sound education performance and will not significantly disrupt the educational programs in the affected districts.
  7. Any increase in school facilities costs as a result of the proposed reorganization will be insignificant and otherwise incidental to the reorganization.
  8. The proposed reorganization is primarily designed for purposes other than to significantly increase property values.
  9. The proposed reorganization will continue to promote sound fiscal management and not cause a substantial negative effect on the fiscal status of the affected district.

 

The Placer County Committee held a public hearing on October 30, 2023, considered GFS’ report, heard all comments submitted in support of and in opposition to the Petition, and made a finding that the statutory conditions set forth in Education Code section 35753 are substantially met. The Placer County Committee was unable to form a tentative conclusion by majority vote to either approve or disapprove the Petition. Placer County Committee will issue its final decision after a public hearing set for December 4, 2023, after the Sacramento County Committee reaches its own findings and tentative decision and transmits them to Placer County Committee. The Sacramento County Committee will hold its second hearing on December 12, 2023, to reach its final decision.

The Superintendent recommends the Sacramento County Committee on School District Organization:

Upon making its findings and a tentative conclusion, the County Committee should direct staff to forward its findings and tentative conclusion to the Placer County Committee on School District Organization.
