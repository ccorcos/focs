[[[**[BACKGROUND ON THE TERRITORY TRANSFER
PETITION:]{style="border:none windowtext 1.0pt; color:black; padding:0in"}**]{style="background:white"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[[[A territory transfer petition (Petition) was submitted to the
Sacramento and Placer County Offices of Education in December 2022.
(Attachment 1) The territory proposed to be transferred is referred to
as the \"Aspire Solaire\" neighborhood and is located in the City of
Roseville, County of Placer. It is currently comprised of 144 developed
homes and 66 vacant lots. Petitioners request that their neighborhood be
transferred from Center Joint Unified School District (Center District)
to the Roseville City School District and the Roseville Joint Union High
School District (Roseville Districts). Center District has submitted a
resolution opposing the
]{style="border:none windowtext 1.0pt; color:black; padding:0in"}[territory
transfer. (Attachment
2)]{style="color:#333333"}]{style="background:white"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[[[Because the petition involves school districts within the
jurisdictions of the Sacramento County Committee on School District
Organization (Sacramento County Committee) and the Placer County
Committee on School District Organization (Placer County Committee),
both County Committees must consider the
P]{style="border:none windowtext 1.0pt; color:black; padding:0in"}[etition,
comply with the California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA),
]{style="border:none windowtext 1.0pt; color:black; padding:0in"}[and
adhere to the requirements to approve or disapprove the petition. 
]{style="color:#333333"}]{style="background:white"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[[[The County Committees previously held two public hearings in their
respective districts regarding this Petition, including initial public
hearings in February 2023 and public hearings in August 2023, in which
both County Committees determined the proposed territory transfer to be
categorically exempt from CEQA. Prior to making a final decision, each
County Committee must transmit findings and tentative conclusions
recommending approval or disapproval of the petition to the other County
Committee.]{style="border:none windowtext 1.0pt; color:black; padding:0in"}]{style="background:white"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}
