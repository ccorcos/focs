Public Comment

 

Anyone may appear at the Board meeting to provide public comment regarding any item on the agenda or regarding any matter that is within the Board’s subject matter jurisdiction. However, the Board may not take action on any item not on the agenda except as authorized by Government Code section 54954.2. If possible, notify the Board President or Board Secretary in writing prior to the meeting if you wish to testify.

 

Individuals wishing to speak in person before the Board are requested to complete a public comment form. Speakers will be called in the order in which the cards are received. 

 

Public comment via Zoom teleconference may be made by using the raised hand feature in Zoom during the agenda item to be addressed. The speaker will be identified by their Display Name in Zoom when called upon to speak. Those joining the meeting via Zoom are requested to display their full name so there is no confusion when called upon to speak. Public comments will be received until the public comment period has elapsed.

 

Comments are limited to a maximum of two (2) minutes per speaker per agenda item, and individuals are limited to a single comment per agenda item.

 

Please contact boardcomments@scoe.net before the meeting if you have questions about the public comment process. 

 

Accommodating Individuals with Disabilities

 

The Sacramento County Office of Education encourages those with disabilities to participate fully in the public meeting process. If you need a disability-related modification or accommodation, including auxiliary aids or services, to participate in the public meeting, contact the Superintendent’s Office at (916) 228-2410 at least 48 hours before the scheduled Board meeting so that we may make every reasonable effort to accommodate you. [Government Code § 54953.2; Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990, § 202 (42 U.S.C. § 12132).]

BACKGROUND:

 

A public hearing will be held by the Sacramento County Board of Education (Board) during the December 10, 2024 Regular Board Meeting to receive public comment and input from the community regarding the American River Collegiate Academy (ARCA) Charter School's request to amend its Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) to affirm that it will remain at its current school facility location and to adjust the term of its MOU to July 1, 2020 - June 30, 2028 to comport with Education Code section 47607.4.

 

School Facility

 

The Board has approved the ARCA Charter School to serve students in grades TK-5 within the San Juan Unified School District. The Board approved ARCA to open and operate its charter school at a temporary school site at 7755 Hazel Avenue in Orangevale, California while it sought a long-term facility within Citrus Heights.  

 

As explained in ARCA's attached request, ARCA has made multiple attempts to secure a long-term school facility in Citrus Heights. Unfortunately, none of these efforts have been successful. Therefore, ARCA is now planning to spend approximately $750,000 to improve and enhance its current school facility, including installing a modular building at its 7755 Hazel Avenue location and moving its playground. ARCA reports that more than half of its current students are from Citrus Heights, and its marketing efforts and outreach will continue to focus on historically underserved communities. Before making significant expenditures on its current school facility, ARCA has asked for the Board's approval to amend paragraph 13.1 of its MOU to clarify that ARCA may remain at its current location for the foreseeable future, as follows:

13.  Facilities

13.1.  It is understood and agreed that the County Board and/or SCOE have no obligation to provide facilities to TRA for the Charter School. The Charter School intends to begin operating in 2020-2021 at a temporary school site located at 7755 Hazel Avenue in Orangevale, California and to move to a permanent school site in Citrus Heights as soon as feasible. The Charter School will regularly update SCOE on the progress and plans for its permanent school facility in Citrus Heights, and will obtain County Board approval before opening and operating its permanent school facility or any other school facility.

 

Memorandum of Understanding Term

 

Consistent with governing law at the time it approved the ARCA charter in February 2020, the Board initially approved the ARCA charter and executed its MOU for a five-year term (July 1, 2020 - June 30, 2025). Subsequently, Education Code section 47607.4 extended the term of charter schools whose terms expire on June 30, 2025 by three years. Therefore, it is recommended that the term of ARCA's MOU be adjusted to July 1, 2020 - June 30, 2028 so that the MOU does not expire before the end of ARCAs' charter term.

2.  Term of the Memorandum of Understanding

2.1.   This Memorandum of Understanding (MOU), provided it is fully executed by all Parties, shall cover the term of the Charter five (5) fiscal years commencing on July 1, 2020, and ending on June 30, 20258 ("Term"), in accordance with Education Code section 47607.4.  The "effective date" of the Charter is July 1, 2020. This MOU will automatically expire upon the expiration or revocation of the Charter.

 

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:

 

The Superintendent recommends that the Board consider and decide whether to adopt Resolution No. 24-22 - Approving the American River Collegiate Academy's request to amend its Memorandum of Understanding regarding its current school facility location and to adjust the term of the MOU to comport with Education Code section 47607.4.

Department/Cabinet Member: College and Career Readiness/Jacqueline White

 

Funding Source: California Department of Education Grant Awarded to the San Joaquin County Office of Education 

 

Amount Requested: $404,520

 

Fiscal Years: 2024-2025, 2025-2026, 2026-2027

 

Program Description: 

CAL-MSCS is a comprehensive and collaborative program designed to establish a sustainable and impactful professional learning infrastructure for educators in mathematics, science, and computer science throughout California. By cultivating statewide communities of practice, providing guidance and support for local teams to build responsive, professional learning systems at the county or regional level, and facilitating coordination with other statewide grantees of TK-12 mathematics, science, and computer science educator professional learning initiatives, CAL-MSCS will:

  1. Expand existing statewide infrastructure and capacity to provide educator professional learning opportunities and coaching in mathematics, science, and computer science (MSCS), in grades 4-12, inclusive;
  2. Generate and disseminate professional learning opportunities for grades 4-12 educators designed to enable local implementation efforts of the MSCS standards; and 
  3. Support local efforts to improve family and community engagement in MSCS education, and engage families and communities in implementing the MSCS standards for students in grades 4-12.

 

This grant-funded contract will be used to assemble "Local Implementation Teams" and deliver relevant professional learning activities in mathematics, science, and computer science to educators in Sacramento County. 

 

New Positions: None

 

Subcontracts: None

 

Evaluation Component: 

SCOE staff will submit quarterly expenditure reports and biannual program reports describing the professional learning activities offered through this grant-funded contract.

BACKGROUND:

 

As authorized by Education Code section 1090(a)(2), Sacramento County Board of Education (Board) members who attend all meetings in a month receive compensation of $463.05 per month. On an annual basis, the Board may increase its members' present monthly compensation by up to 5 percent. Any increase becomes effective upon approval of the Board, unless rejected by voter referendum. (Ed. Code, § 1090(a)(2),(g); Board Bylaw 9260.) 

 

According to information gathered by Sacramento County Office of Education staff, county board of education members in similarly sized counties receive the following monthly sums as compensation for their services:

 

$546.02 - San Bernadino

$562.61 - Orange

$562.83 - San Diego

$649.00 - Fresno

$658.77 - Contra Costa

$683.85 - Riverside

$1,114.34 - Alameda

$1,170.12 - Santa Clara

 

In the event the Board would like to increase its monthly compensation by 5 percent (from $463.05 to $486.20 per month) as permitted by law, Resolution No. 24-21 is attached for the Board's consideration. If approved, this increase will be effective with the December 2024 payroll, which is payable on the first business day of January 2025.
