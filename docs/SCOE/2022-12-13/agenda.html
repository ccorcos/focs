Public Comment

 

Anyone may appear at the Board meeting to provide public comment regarding any item on the agenda or regarding any matter that is within the Board’s subject matter jurisdiction. However, the Board may not take action on any item not on the agenda except as authorized by Government Code section 54954.2.  If possible, notify the Board President or Board Secretary in writing prior to the meeting if you wish to testify.

 

Individuals wishing to speak in person before the Board are requested to complete a public comment form. Speakers will be called in the order in which the cards are received. 

 

Public comment via Zoom teleconference may be made by using the raised hand feature in Zoom during the agenda item to be addressed. The speaker will be identified by their Display Name in Zoom when called upon to speak. Those joining the meeting via Zoom are requested to display their full name so there is no confusion when called upon to speak. Public comments will be received until the public comment period has elapsed.

 

Comments are limited to a maximum of two (2) minutes per speaker per agenda item, and individuals are limited to a single comment per agenda item.

 

Please contact boardcomments@scoe.net before the meeting if you have questions about the public comment process. 

 

Accommodating Individuals with Disabilities

 

The Sacramento County Office of Education encourages those with disabilities to participate fully in the public meeting process. If you need a disability-related modification or accommodation, including auxiliary aids or services, to participate in the public meeting, contact the Superintendent’s Office at (916) 228-2410 at least 48 hours before the scheduled Board meeting so that we may make every reasonable effort to accommodate you. [Government Code § 54953.2; Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990, § 202 (42 U.S.C. § 12132).]

BACKGROUND:

 

The Every Student Succeeds Act (ESSA) requires schools receiving Title I funds and operating a school-wide program, or schools identified for Comprehensive Support and Improvement (CSI), Targeted Support and Improvement (TSI) or Additional Targeted Support and Improvement (ATSI), to develop a school plan. Assembly Bill 716 revised California Education Code sections 64001-65001 to streamline and align state and federal planning processes and organized federal school planning requirements in ESSA.

 

The development of the School Plan for Student Achievement (SPSA) is required to include the following:

  • A comprehensive needs assessment (pursuant to ESSA)
    • Analysis of verifiable state data, consistent with state priorities, including state-determined long term goals
    • The plan may include local data.
  • An identification of the process for evaluating and monitoring the implementation of the School Plan and progress towards accomplishing the goals

 

The Foundations Academy programs have prepared their school plan in accordance with school-wide program stipulations.

 

In accordance with California Education Code Section 52068(a)(4), a county office of education superintendent must first review the Local Control and Accountability Plan (LCAP) and School Plans to ensure that the two plans are consistent. An integrated approach to planning ensures a consistent alignment between the LCAP and School Plans. As a result, the goals and relevant metrics outlined in the 2022-2023 Foundations Academy SPSAs are aligned to the 2022-2023 Sacramento County Office of Education LCAP.

 

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:

 

The Superintendent recommends that the Board approve the School Plan for Student Achievement in accordance with the requirements under Education Code and federal law.

BACKGROUND:

 

Assembly Bill 288 (Holden) was enacted January 1, 2026, and added to the California Education Code section 76004. Assembly Bill 288 enables the governing board of a community college district to enter into a College and Career Access Pathways (CCAP) partnership with the governing board of a school district. For the first time in California's Education Code, the term "dual enrollment" is identified to define "special part-time" or "special full-time" students - that is, high school or other eligible special admit students enrolling in community college credit courses.

 

SCOE is pleased to be able to offer Dual Enrollment for all students in SCOE programs. Our current MOU with Lake Tahoe Community College (which will be ongoing) only provides for courses to be offered in our El Centro School programs.

 

This agreement will provide increased equity and access for students wishing to continue their post-secondary education which includes access for SCOE students to all four Los Rios Community Colleges.

 

 

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:

 

The Superintendent recommends that the Board of Education approve the College and Career Access Pathways (CCAP) MOU with the Los Rios Community College District.

Amount Requested: $3,750,000

 

Fiscal Year(s): 2022-2023, 2023-2024, 2024-2025, 2025-2026

 

Program Description: 

The Teacher Residency Implementation Grant is funded through the California Commission on Teacher Credentialing to implement a teacher residency pathway in an effort to positively impact teacher shortage in special education and transitional kindergarten within the Capitol Region. The overall goal of the Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) Teacher Residency Program is to create a new preliminary teacher credentialing pathway that recruits, develops, and retains a diverse teacher workforce. This Teacher Residency program is an alternative pathway to teaching which combines an accelerated course of study with a yearlong student teaching placement at a single school site. When a participant completes the program, they will be recommended for their preliminary teaching credential and be ready to join the teaching workforce. SCOE will serve as the lead LEA for this grant and under the parameters of the state's grant application. SCOE will partner with Pacific Oaks College as the Institution of Higher Education, who will provide the teaching credential coursework needed. SCOE’s consortium, including 15 local districts, requested 50 recipient slots from the California Commission on Teacher Credentialing for the grant project period of November 2022 through June 2026. The program offers fiscal support, including tuition, assessment fees, and a monthly stipend to support initial onboarding sessions. The grant will support 50 teachers to obtain credentials in special education and multiple subjects (with a TK authorization) per year, supporting 150 teachers over three years. 

 

New Positions:  Program Analyst

 

Subcontracts:  Pacific Oaks College

 

Evaluation Component: 

Progress reports on the status of teacher recruitment and number of credentialed teachers over the grant period.

BACKGROUND:

 

The purpose of the Sacramento County Local Child Care and Development Planning Council (LPC) is to provide a forum for the planning of childcare services that meet the diverse needs of families in Sacramento County, promote public participation in the planning process, advocate for the needs of families in the County with respect to childcare, and serve as a forum for discussion of community childcare issues. The LPC meets four times per year. Ad hoc subcommittees meet as needed. 

 

The LPC is comprised of 20 members with appointment duties split between the Board of Supervisors (10 members) and the County Board of Education (10 members). Each Board appoints two members in each of the following categories: 

 

Consumer - a parent or person who receives, or who has received within the past 36 months, childcare services.

Child Care Provider - a person who provides childcare services or represents persons who provide childcare services.

Public Agency Representative - a person who represents a city, county, city and county, or local education agency.

Community Representative - a person who represents an agency or business that provides private funding for childcare services, or who advocates for childcare services through participation in civic or community-based organizations but is not a childcare provider and does not represent an agency that contracts with the State Department of Education or the Department of Social Services to provide childcare and development services.

Board Discretion - a person appointed at the discretion of the Board. Candidates should have a combination of early learning education and diverse experiences that will contribute to fulfilling the LPC's responsibilities, including knowledge about childcare and early learning services in Sacramento County, and regional/statewide programs and trends that could impact early learning services.

Notice of these openings is disseminated by the Early Learning Department. After screening the applications, SCOE staff conducted interviews. The names of the following individuals (both re-appointees and new appointees) are submitted for appointment in the designated categories:

  • Board Discretion - Rachelle Davis (Re-appointee), Instructor of Record, CSUS -  Ms. Davis is an Instructor of Record for California State University, Sacramento (CSUS). She has served in a variety of positions in the Early Childhood Education field, including as a Family Child Care Home (FCCH) owner, a mentor teacher, and a Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion Consultant for Awareness, Nurturing, and Acceptance (ANA) Professional Development. 

 

  • Board Discretion - Abra Ruthenbeck (Re-appointee), Former Director, First Steps Child Care Center, operated by St. John's Shelter Program for Women & Children - Ms. Ruthenbeck was a center director and teacher with a strong commitment to the care and education of disadvantaged communities. She holds a doctorate in Educational Leadership and is a Licensed Clinical Social Worker. 

 

  • Public Agency Representative - Lisa Carr (Re-appointee), Head Start/Early Head Start Manager, Program Support Services, Sacramento and Employment and Training Agency - Ms. Carr is a program manager with oversight of family engagement, enrollment home base, and health services to over 2,500 children at Sacramento Employment and Training Agency (SETA) Head Start.

BACKGROUND:

 

Since the 2022-2023 County School Service Fund budget was adopted in June 2022, revisions have been made to keep the budget current with changing circumstances. The purpose of the Interim Financial Report is to project the total revenues and expenditures for the year, to compare the projected totals to the revised budget, to perform a summary review of the report according to the State criteria and standards, and to certify the financial condition of the Sacramento County Office of Education to the California Department of Education.

 

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:

 

The Superintendent recommends that the Board approve a positive certification that the Sacramento County Office of Education will be able to meet its financial obligations for the current fiscal year and subsequent two fiscal years.

BACKGROUND:

 

Attached are proposed revisions to Board Policy 5142 – Authorization to Administer Medication and for Parent Administration of Medicinal Cannabis.  All proposed revisions are indicated by strikeouts and bold underlined additions. 

 

The proposed revisions update the policy to reflect current law and practices regarding the availability and administration of medication, including epinephrine auto-injectors (EpiPens) and naloxone hydrochloride (Narcan) nasal spray.

 

The Policy Committee reviewed Board Policy 5142 – Authorization to Administer Medication and for Parent Administration of Medicinal Cannabis on November 15, 2022, and recommended that the revised policy be presented to the Board for First Reading.

 

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:

 

The Superintendent concurs with the recommendation of the Policy Committee that Board Policy 5142 – Authorization to Administer Medication and for Parent Administration of Medicinal Cannabis be submitted to the Board of Education for First Reading.
