2020-2021 Annual Report of Sacramento County Williams Reviews
November 16, 2021

Williams Reviews

Currently, there are 107 schools in 9 school districts within Sacramento County that
ranked in deciles 1 to 3 of the 2012 Base Academic Performance Index (Williams
schools). As a result of the Williams Settlement, Education Code section 1240 requires
that county offices of education visit the Williams schools in their county, review
information in the areas noted below, and report the results of the visits and reviews at a
regularly scheduled November meeting.

Senate Bill 820 waived the requirement for county offices of education to conduct
school site visits while students were in distance learning during 2020-2021. The
information below summarizes the results of reviews conducted remotely during the
2020-2021 school year.

Instructional Materials

Because SCOE was not able to visit schools in person, we relied upon the principals of
Williams schools to certify that all students had instructional materials and had equitable
access to the materials. Principals were asked to address whether students had access
to an electronic device and the internet as applicable. All Williams schools reported
sufficient instructional materials for 2020-2021.

Facilities

Each school district was responsible for performing its own facilities inspections in 2020-
2021. As for the overall facilities conditions of the 107 Williams schools, the districts
reported:

    •    49 schools were found to be in exemplary condition,
    •    51 schools were found to be in good condition,
    •    4 schools were found to be in fair condition,
    •    3 schools were found to be in poor condition.

School Accountability Report Cards

During the third quarter of the 2020-2021 school year, SCOE staff reviewed whether the
School Accountability Report Card (SARC) of each Williams school accurately reported
the sufficiency of instructional materials, and the safety, cleanliness, and adequacy of
school facilities. This year, the districts provided SCOE with their facility inspection
reports, and we used these reports to assess the accuracy of each Williams school’s
SARC. Most of the SARCs were clear and relatively complete.

Teacher Vacancies and Misassignments

The window for monitoring FY 2020-21 teacher misassignment and vacancy data in the
new system (CalSAAS) began August 1, 2021 and concludes October 31, 2021. We
will report the results of these reviews during our 2021-2022 second quarterly Williams

SCOE is required to monitor complaints filed under the Uniform Complaint Procedures
(UCP) in each of the school districts with Williams schools. No school district with
Williams schools reported any UCP complaints in 2020-2021.
