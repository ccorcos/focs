                             Since the enactment of AB 24 in 2017, the State Seal of Civic Engagement
                             has created pathways for students in high schools to become civically
                             engaged. In its first year, 5,369 seniors in the class of 2021 received
                             insignias. Since 2020, 28,030 Seals of Civic Engagement have been awarded
                             to 11th and 12th graders. While this is great progress, we must introduce
                             students early to civic education and systems of government to create clear
                             pathways toward a lifetime of civic engagement.

                             SB 584 begins to close the gaps in civic education between students in 1st
                             through 12th grades and encourages students to become active participants
                             in their communities and government. The Sacramento County Board of
                             Education is dedicated to preparing all students, beginning in elementary
                             grades, to be informed, responsible, engaged participants in civic life,
                             exemplifying the ideals and principles of our constitutional democracy.

                             For these reasons, the Sacramento County Board of Education is proud to
                             support SB 584.
