[[[Senator Monique]{style="color:#333333"} [Limón has introduced Senate
Bill (SB 584) which promotes civic learning in grades 1-8 to introduce
the opportunity of achieving a State Seal of Civic Engagement in grades
11 or 12. The Sacramento County Board of Education (Board) may review
and consider expressing its support for this education-related
legislation. A letter of support has been drafted and attached for the
Board\'s
consideration.]{style="color:#333333"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

[[[ ]{style="color:#333333"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

[[**[Senate Bill 584 (Limón) -- Civic Learning in Grades 1-8:  Creating
Pathways for the Seal of Civic
Engagement]{style="color:#333333"}**]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

[[**[ ]{style="color:#333333"}**]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

[[[Under California law, social sciences is a required course of study
for grades 1-6 and grades 7-12. Current ]{style="color:#333333"}law
requires instruction in social sciences to provide a foundation for
understanding, among other things, the history, resources, development,
and government of California and the United States of
America.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

[[ ]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

[[[If enacted, SB 584 will:
  ]{style="color:#333333"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

- [[[Require ]{style="color:#333333"}current instruction under social
  sciences curriculum for grades 1-6 and 7-12 to include civic
  engagement experiences with governmental
  institutions. ]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}
- [[Expand the criteria for local education agencies to apply for the
  existing California Serves Grant Program under the California
  Department of Education to include grades 1-8 for professional

better attendance, as student\'s will not need to return after the 4th
