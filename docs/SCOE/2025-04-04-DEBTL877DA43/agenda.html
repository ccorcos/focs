Public Comment

 

Anyone may appear at the Board meeting to provide public comment regarding any item on the agenda or regarding any matter that is within the Board’s subject matter jurisdiction. However, the Board may not take action on any item not on the agenda except as authorized by Government Code section 54954.2.  If possible, notify the Board President or Board Secretary in writing prior to the meeting if you wish to testify.

 

Individuals wishing to speak in person before the Board are requested to complete a public comment form. Speakers will be called in the order in which the cards are received. 

 

Public comment via Zoom teleconference may be made by using the raised hand feature in Zoom during the agenda item to be addressed. The speaker will be identified by their Display Name in Zoom when called upon to speak. Those joining the meeting via Zoom are requested to display their full name so there is no confusion when called upon to speak. Public comments will be received until the public comment period has elapsed.

 

Comments are limited to a maximum of two (2) minutes per speaker per agenda item, and individuals are limited to a single comment per agenda item.

 

Please contact boardcomments@scoe.net before the meeting if you have questions about the public comment process. 

 

Accommodating Individuals with Disabilities

 

The Sacramento County Office of Education encourages those with disabilities to participate fully in the public meeting process. If you need a disability-related modification or accommodation, including auxiliary aids or services, to participate in the public meeting, contact the Superintendent’s Office at (916) 228-2410 at least 48 hours before the scheduled Board meeting so that we may make every reasonable effort to accommodate you. [Government Code § 54953.2; Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990, § 202 (42 U.S.C. § 12132).]

CLASSIFIED 

 

Mark (Stephen) Benson, Transition Specialist at Elinor Lincoln Hickey Jr./Sr. High School, was nominated by Principal Lisa Alcala. Mr. Benson has an ability to connect with students in a way that makes them feel supported as he holds them accountable to being the best version of themselves. CERTIFICATED

 

Maureen Loft, Special Education Teacher at Leo A. Palmiter Jr./Sr. High School, was nominated by Principal Lauren Roth. When a teacher is absent, she assists with lessons and student support. She is patient, helpful, and positive, with a strong proficiency of using technology in the classroom. Students enjoy being in her class because she is engaging, patient, and supportive. She creates a welcoming and structured learning environment, adapts to diverse learning needs, and fosters an atmosphere that encourages student success. Maureen Loft has been a SCOE employee since July 2017.

BACKGROUND:

 

Senator Monique Limón has introduced Senate Bill (SB 584) which promotes civic learning in grades 1-8 to introduce the opportunity of achieving a State Seal of Civic Engagement in grades 11 or 12. The Sacramento County Board of Education (Board) may review and consider expressing its support for this education-related legislation. A letter of support has been drafted and attached for the Board's consideration.

 

Senate Bill 584 (Limón) – Civic Learning in Grades 1-8:  Creating Pathways for the Seal of Civic Engagement

 

Under California law, social sciences is a required course of study for grades 1-6 and grades 7-12. Current law requires instruction in social sciences to provide a foundation for understanding, among other things, the history, resources, development, and government of California and the United States of America.

 

If enacted, SB 584 will:   

  • Require current instruction under social sciences curriculum for grades 1-6 and 7-12 to include civic engagement experiences with governmental institutions. 
  • Expand the criteria for local education agencies to apply for the existing California Serves Grant Program under the California Department of Education to include grades 1-8 for professional learning and instruction materials on civic engagement.
  • Create a Civic Engagement Pathways Recognition Program to recognize demonstrated progress in civic education.

 

See attached fact sheet for more information. 

 

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:

 

The Superintendent recommends that the Board support SB 584 and approve the attached draft letter of support.

NOTICE OF PUBLIC HEARING SCHEDULED FOR FRIDAY, APRIL 4, 2025 AT 12:30PM OR THEREAFTER

 

Leo A. Palmiter Jr./Sr. High School

Multi-Purpose Room

2040 Ethan Way

Sacramento, CA 95825

 

BACKGROUND:

 

A public hearing will be held by the Board during the April 4, 2025 Board meeting to receive public comment and input from the community regarding the proposed schedule for the 2024/2025 SCOE Special Education Program's Extended School Year (ESY).

 

Per California Education Code 3043, Extended School Year should be a minimum of 20 days including holidays. To hold an ESY that is less than 20-days, a waiver can be filed with the California Department of Education and requires SCOE to provide public notice of the alternative schedule.  SCOE's Special Education Programs are proposing the 2024/2025 ESY operate 18 days with four-and-a-half hour days as opposed to the typical 20-day schedule with four hour days. This will promote better attendance, as student's will not need to return after the 4th of July Holiday, and will allow teachers to more closely follow their regular-year class routines. With this proposed schedule, the dates of operation would be Monday, June 9, 2025 through Thursday, July 3, 2025. 

 

 

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:

 

The Superintendent recommends the Board approve the proposed schedule for SCOE's Special Education's 2024/2025 ESY.
