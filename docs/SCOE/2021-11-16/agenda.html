CLASSIFIED 

 

Christi Goates, Re-Entry Transition Specialist, was nominated for her contributions to the Placer Re-Entry Program by Coordinator Christi Fee. Ms. Goates provides supportive services to youth and adults involved with the criminal justice system. She sees the potential in her clients before they do and inspires them to achieve their goals. During COVID-19, our clients struggled with extreme circumstances, often under immense stress. Ms. Goates navigated these difficult times with them and understood the importance of being consistently there for them. She provided remote services by phone and Zoom, to keep them engaged in the program as long as needed. The Placer Re-Entry Program would not be as successful as it is without the skill and dedication that Ms. Goates brings to her job every day. Christi Goates has been a Sacramento County Office of Education employee since May 2019.

 

CERTIFICATED

 

Joe Wilson, Career Technical Education Instructor/Nursery, Landscaping and Ground Maintenance, was nominated for his work in SCOE’s Re-Entry program by Director Chris Aland. Mr. Wilson helps re-entry clients at SCBC fulfill their community service requirements by leading projects in landscaping and bicycle repair. He is empathetic, patient, and personable with every client. Mr. Wilson didn’t miss a beat during COVID-19. He sought out projects to keep his clients on track and engaged. Mr. Wilson strives to create a positive culture for the SCBC Re-Entry program. Joe Wilson has been a Sacramento County Office of Education employee since July 2007.

Funding Source: California Department of Education

 

Amount Requested: $147,680

 

Fiscal Year(s): 2021-2022, 2022-2023

 

Program Description: 

The California Department of Education (CDE) has developed a comprehensive plan to provide technical assistance for continuous improvement to Local Education Agencies (LEAs). The Title III County Office of Education Regional English Learner Specialists Agreement Grant is funded through the Elementary and Secondary Education Act (ESEA). As part of the California Statewide System of Support, the CDE awarded grants to one county within each of the state’s eleven California County Superintendents Educational Services Association (CCSESA) regions. The Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) was selected in Region 3 as the Regional English Learner (EL)/Title III Specialist.

 

As the Region 3 EL Specialist, SCOE will provide technical assistance for continuous improvement to equip LEAs and COEs with resources and tools to support the development, implementation, and evaluation of strategies to ensure that English Learners have the resources they need to succeed. The scope of work includes guidance and support to LEAs and COEs in the implementation and improvement of programs as well as the development of Local Control and Accountability Plan (LCAP) Addenda. In addition, SCOE’s Curriculum Specialist for English Learner Programs and Accountability will organize, provide coaching for systemic improvement in data analysis, and data-based decision-making, high-quality instruction, connect resources for specialized programs, and facilitate quarterly Multilingual Education Leadership Network meetings to support LEAs.

BACKGROUND:

 

The Sacramento County Board of Education (Board), on its own and in its capacity as County Board and Sacramento County Committee on School District Organization, has held meetings via teleconference from April 2020 through September 2021 in compliance with Governors' Executive Orders, which relaxed certain legal requirements for Board meetings during the COVID-19 pandemic. The legal requirements for public meetings are set forth in Government Code section 54950, et. seq., which is known as the “Brown Act.” The Executive Orders expired on September 30, 2021. However, recently enacted Assembly Bill 361 amended elements of the Brown Act to permit local agencies to continue holding teleconferenced meetings using simplified procedures during a proclaimed state of emergency.  

 

Under Government Code section 54953(e), in order to continue holding teleconferenced Board meetings using simplified Brown Act procedures, local agencies, including school districts and county boards of education, must make the following findings by majority vote and readopt such findings every 30 days thereafter:

  1. The Board has reconsidered the circumstances of the state of emergency, AND
  2. The state of emergency continues to directly impact the ability of members to meet safely in person, OR state or local officials continue to impose or recommend measures to promote social distancing.

In the event the Board decides to continue to hold teleconferenced Board meetings using abbreviated Brown Act procedures, Resolution No. 21-15 is attached for the Board's consideration. 

 

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:

 

The Superintendent recommends that the Board consider and decide whether to adopt Resolution No. 21-15, which would permit it to continue holding teleconferenced Board meetings under abbreviated Brown Act procedures.

BACKGROUND:

 

The Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) will describe a draft plan for the expenditure of Educator Effectiveness Block Grant funds. SCOE has recently learned of the availability of Educator Effectiveness Block Grant funding from the California Department of Education (CDE). As a condition of receipt of the funds, SCOE must develop and present a plan for expenditure of the funds at a public meeting of its governing board and then adopt the plan at a subsequent public meeting on or before December 30, 2021. However, SCOE has not yet received an official notification from CDE with regards to funding amount or grant details. The CDE has scheduled a technical assistance webinar for November 9, 2021, at which SCOE staff will learn more details and will be developing SCOE's plan over the coming weeks.

 

The intent of the funds is to provide professional learning for teachers, administrators, paraprofessionals who work with pupils, and classified staff that interact with pupils, with a focus on any of the following areas: coaching and mentoring, programs that lead to effective standards-based instruction that improves literacy across all areas, practices and strategies to re-engage pupils and lead to accelerated learning, strategies to implement Social-Emotional Learning, strategies to create a positive school climate, strategies for effective language acquisition for English Learners, strategies to incorporate ethnic studies curriculum, and strategies for early childhood educators.

 

The final plan will be presented for adoption at the December 14, 2021 Board Meeting.

BACKGROUND:

 

The California Department of Education estimates that approximately 30% of the state's K-12 classrooms are at least 50 years old and 10% are 70 years old. In addition to health, safety, and normal wear and tear, schools need to be updated to meet 21st century educational needs and environmental efficiencies. Studies have found a positive relationship between the condition of school facilities and student achievement. There are significant student improvements in standardized test scores, attendance rates, and student effort following attendance at a new or modernized school facility.

 

California voters passed the last statewide School Bond (Proposition 51) in 2016, however, funds from this Bond are already fully obligated. As a result, the state Office of Public School Construction has created a ‘wait list’ which has over $3 billion worth of unfunded school facilities grants on it. Assembly Bill 75, scheduled for the ballot in 2022, another statewide school facility bond, worth $12 billion will fund projects on the ‘wait list’ and future needs. Furthermore, AB 75 will not raise individual local property taxes, but will reduce the need for additional local property taxes for school facilities.

 

SCOE is eligible to receive and has applied for $8.8 million in reimbursements from the state for the construction of Nathaniel S. Colley, Sr. High School. However, as indicated above, there currently is no state bond funding available to reimburse SCOE. The school facility bond to be placed on the ballot by AB 75 will fully fund the $8.8 million in reimbursements for SCOE.

 

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:

 

The Superintendent recommends that the Board adopt Resolution No. 21-14 - In Support of Kindergarten-Community Colleges Public Education Facilities Bond Act of 2022 (AB 75).
