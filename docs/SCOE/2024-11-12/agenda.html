Public Comment

 

Anyone may appear at the Board meeting to provide public comment regarding any item on the agenda or regarding any matter that is within the Board’s subject matter jurisdiction. However, the Board may not take action on any item not on the agenda except as authorized by Government Code section 54954.2. If possible, notify the Board President or Board Secretary in writing prior to the meeting if you wish to testify.

 

Individuals wishing to speak in person before the Board are requested to complete a public comment form. Speakers will be called in the order in which the cards are received. 

 

Public comment via Zoom teleconference may be made by using the raised hand feature in Zoom during the agenda item to be addressed. The speaker will be identified by their Display Name in Zoom when called upon to speak. Those joining the meeting via Zoom are requested to display their full name so there is no confusion when called upon to speak. Public comments will be received until the public comment period has elapsed.

 

Comments are limited to a maximum of two (2) minutes per speaker per agenda item, and individuals are limited to a single comment per agenda item.

 

Please contact boardcomments@scoe.net before the meeting if you have questions about the public comment process. 

 

Accommodating Individuals with Disabilities

 

The Sacramento County Office of Education encourages those with disabilities to participate fully in the public meeting process. If you need a disability-related modification or accommodation, including auxiliary aids or services, to participate in the public meeting, contact the Superintendent’s Office at (916) 228-2410 at least 48 hours before the scheduled Board meeting so that we may make every reasonable effort to accommodate you. [Government Code § 54953.2; Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990, § 202 (42 U.S.C. § 12132).]

CLASSIFIED 

 

Kayci Bath, Senior Personnel Technician with Personnel was nominated by Chief Administrator of Human Resources, Darrien Slider-Johnson. Since joining SCOE, Kayci advanced from Personnel Technician to Senior Personnel Technician within just four months. Kayci is always willing to help her colleagues, ask insightful questions, and seeks to understand the reasoning behind decisions. As a Senior Personnel Technician, Kayci has been instrumental in hiring and onboarding 40+ new Career Pipeline employees ranging from Family and Community Health System Navigators, Associate School-Based Mental Health and Wellness Clinicians, School-Based Mental Health and Wellness Clinicians, and School-Based Mental Health and Wellness Clinicians II to support SCOE's effort to transform each school in Sacramento County into a Center of Wellness. Kayci also plays a pivotal role in supporting our Exempt Temporary Employee workgroup and takes a proactive approach to solving problems, ensuring continuous support of our programs. Kayci Bath has been a SCOE employee since February 2023.

 

CERTIFICATED

 

Lisa Saenz, Teacher with Student Programs - CARE, was nominated by Director Marc Nigel. Lisa is an exceptional teacher who consistently strives to improve her practices for the benefit of her students. Recently, she visited her former students at Cordova High School to ensure they are receiving the support they need to succeed. Additionally, she initiated "Family Fridays", where parents can join their children for lunch in the CARE class, which fosters a sense of community. Lisa often reaches out to discuss ways to motivate her students, whether through pizza parties or other rewards to encourage school attendance and goal achievement. Her professionalism, work ethic, and positive spirit truly elevate our team, making us all better for having her on staff. Lisa Saenz has been a SCOE employee since March 2017.

Department/Cabinet Member: College & Career Readiness/Jacqueline White

 

Funding Source: California Community College Chancellor's Office (CCCOE)

 

Amount Requested: $824,839

 

Fiscal Year(s): 2024-2025, 2025-2026, 2026-2027

 

Program Description: 

The ICT Pathways aim to expand access to high-demand, high-wage career opportunities in the ICT sector for K-12 students in Sacramento County. This initiative will develop inquiry-based, equity-driven curricula focusing on emerging standards such as cybersecurity, artificial intelligence, and machine learning, while aligning these courses with relevant community college programs. Student-facing activities, including summer camps and competitions, will promote career readiness and increase engagement among underrepresented groups. Additionally, professional learning opportunities will equip educators with the tools to deliver cutting-edge ICT instruction. By fostering strong pathways from K-12 to postsecondary education, this project addresses labor market demands and ensures students are prepared for future ICT careers. 

 

New Positions: None

Evaluation Component: 

Evaluation of the Reimagining ICT Pathways will be provided to the CCCOE through Progress and Expenditure Reports and a final K12 Expenditure and Outcomes Report that will focus on student outcomes, such as course completion rates, postsecondary transitions, employment data, and demographic data to ensure equity gaps are being addressed.

BACKGROUND:

 

The County Superintendent of Schools is required to submit a report each November to the County Board of Education describing the state of the schools in the county that have been designated as Williams Schools by the Superintendent of Public Instruction for the 2021-2024 school years. The list includes those schools 1) eligible for Comprehensive Support and Improvement under Every Student Succeeds Act (ESSA); 2) eligible for Additional Targeted Support and Improvement under ESSA; or 3) in which 15 percent or more of the school’s teachers do not possess a valid and clear or preliminary teaching credential. (Ed. Code, § 1240.) The California Department of Education will produce a new list of schools eligible for Williams monitoring following the publication of the 2024 California School Dashboard by December 1, 2024.

 

The attached report summarizes the results of Williams reviews conducted during the 2023-2024 school year. For additional details regarding the 2023-2024 Williams reviews, please refer to the Annual Williams reports provided by the County Superintendent in September 2024. Electronic copies are available upon request.

 

Currently, SCOE is undertaking visits to Williams Schools for the 2024-2025 school year, reviewing the sufficiency of instructional materials, and considering the condition of the school facilities. The results of these school visits will be included in the First Quarterly 2024-2025 Williams Review Reports, which will be completed in December 2024 and subsequently shared with the Board as part of the 2024-2025 Annual Report.

 

This item is provided for information and comment. No action is required by the Board.
