Public Comment

 

Anyone may appear at the Board meeting to provide public comment regarding any item on the agenda or regarding any matter that is within the Board’s subject matter jurisdiction. However, the Board may not take action on any item not on the agenda except as authorized by Government Code section 54954.2.  If possible, notify the Board President or Board Secretary in writing prior to the meeting if you wish to testify.

 

Individuals wishing to speak in person before the Board are requested to complete a public comment form. Speakers will be called in the order in which the cards are received. 

 

Public comment via Zoom teleconference may be made by using the raised hand feature in Zoom during the agenda item to be addressed. The speaker will be identified by their Display Name in Zoom when called upon to speak. Those joining the meeting via Zoom are requested to display their full name so there is no confusion when called upon to speak. Public comments will be received until the public comment period has elapsed.

 

Comments are limited to a maximum of two (2) minutes per speaker per agenda item, and individuals are limited to a single comment per agenda item.

 

Please contact boardcomments@scoe.net before the meeting if you have questions about the public comment process. 

 

Accommodating Individuals with Disabilities

 

The Sacramento County Office of Education encourages those with disabilities to participate fully in the public meeting process. If you need a disability-related modification or accommodation, including auxiliary aids or services, to participate in the public meeting, contact the Superintendent’s Office at (916) 228-2410 at least 48 hours before the scheduled Board meeting so that we may make every reasonable effort to accommodate you. [Government Code § 54953.2; Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990, § 202 (42 U.S.C. § 12132).]

Department/Director: Technology Services/Jerry Jones

 

Funding Source: Department of Homeland Security

 

Amount Requested: $246,850

 

Fiscal Year(s): 2022-2023, 2023-2024

 

Program Description: 

To date, more than 17 million adults in the United States and around the world have used USA Learns (usalearns.org) to learn English and move towards U.S. citizenship. USA Learns is a free distance learning website created in 2008 by the Internet and Media Services Department.

 

As a part of their Innovations in Citizenship Education Grant, the Department of Homeland Security (DHS) is seeking creative approaches to expand the availability of high-quality citizenship preparation services for immigrants across the nation and to provide opportunities for immigrants to gain the knowledge and skills necessary to integrate into the fabric of American society.

 

Key activities of this project include making upgrades to the USA Learns Citizenship course, which is a premier free course that prepares immigrants for all aspects of the naturalization interview. New digital literacy content and more civic integration instruction will be added to align with the recent United States Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS) curriculum. Updates will also be made to reflect USCIS’ recent changes to technical processes related to applying to be a citizen and technical tools now used during the naturalization interview. New and innovative activities and citizenship instructions will also be added to the Access America course, which helps immigrants learn English and be successful in the U.S.    

 

New Positions: None

 

Subcontracts:

  • Lead Curriculum Developer: $20,000
  • Citizenship Subject Matter Expert: $10,000
  • Adult Education Citizenship Teacher: $2,000
  • Adult Education ESL Teacher: $5,000
  • Social Media Writer / ESL Teacher: $5,000
  • Social Media advertising to communicate with hard-to-reach learners: $30,000
  • Translations (translation services): $5,000

 

Evaluation Component: 

This project will be measured and evaluated by successful completion of deliverables as described in the contract, including:

  • Completion of critical milestones
  • Alpha and beta testing with learners and teachers
  • Report project status using Google Analytics

Department/Director: Center for Student Assessment & Program Accountability (C-SAPA)/Dr. Nancy Herota

 

Funding Source: California Department of Education (CDE)

 

Amount Requested: $543,000

 

Fiscal Year(s): 2022-2023, 2023-2024, 2024-2025

 

Program Description: 

All students in grades five, seven, and nine are expected to take the Physical Fitness Test (PFT), regardless of whether or not they are in a physical education class. The PFT results are reported on the CDE DataQuest Web page. The following tasks describe the work and deliverables involved in the grant:

Task 1 - Development of a comprehensive plan and schedule of events, activities, and regular meetings. 

Task 2 - Description of LEA deliverables designed to support PFT Coordinators and other educational community stakeholders, as well as communication activities. 

Task 3 - Outlines the operation and maintenance of the current PFT website and secure district portal for use in coordinator designation.

Task 4 - Provide customer service and technical assistance for LEAs that shall be provided to support their administration, scoring, and reporting of PFT data. 

Task 5 - Support for LEAs related to the submission of participation data to their School Accountability Report Card.

Task 6 - Research study and resulting reporting recommendations by SB820 relating to the purpose and administration of the PFT.

 

New Positions: None

 

Subcontracts:

  • San Joaquin County Office of Education (SJCOE) in the amount of $54,000 to provide staff to augment existing SCOE personnel resources in planning and producing contract activities and deliverables.
  • Panel of Experts in the amount of $10,000 will support the development of the research study via research, review, data analysis, and other expert consultative services.
  • Captioning in the amount of $1,800 will create transcripts that are required for webinars including transcripts for webcasts, videos, and related projects.

 

Evaluation Component: 

A report is submitted to the Governor and the Legislature every two years summarizing the results of the PFT.

Department/Director: Educational Services/Brent Malicote

 

Funding Source: California Department of Health Care Services (DHCS) 

 

Amount Requested: $65,000,000 

 

Fiscal Year(s): 2022-2023, 2023-2024 

 

Program Description: 

The Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) has submitted a proposal to the California Department of Health Care Services (DHCS) as a partner to extend the ongoing support of Social Emotional Learning (SEL) in a statewide Community of Practice (CoP). Cal-HOPE Student Support will enable California's schools to be leaders in supporting prevention and early interventions for students and families as we collectively respond to the mental health and wellness needs of students, families, and educators across California. Through a unique partnership with the California DHCS, California Public Education System, University of California, Berkeley, and an EdTech survey/data collection platform, California front-line educators will support families and children by responding to the trauma caused by the ongoing COVID-19 pandemic and systemic inequities.

 

New Positions:  

  • Social Emotional Learning (SEL) Coordinator
  • Mental Health Coordinator
  • Program Analyst 

 

Subcontracts:

  • County Offices of Education
  • University of California Berkeley Greater Good Science Center
  • An EdTech survey/data platform to support data collection and analysis which will contribute to continuous improvement practices and processes
  • Various organizations to be determined, to support the development of a SEL Conference that will be made available to all California educators

 

Evaluation Component: 

University of California Berkeley will evaluate the impact of this work for continuous quality improvement, which will contribute to the knowledge base of best practices and transformative change.
