Public Comment

 

Anyone may appear at the Board meeting to provide public comment regarding any item on the agenda or regarding any matter that is within the Board’s subject matter jurisdiction. However, the Board may not take action on any item not on the agenda except as authorized by Government Code section 54954.2.  If possible, notify the Board President or Board Secretary in writing prior to the meeting if you wish to testify.

 

Individuals wishing to speak in person before the Board are requested to complete a public comment form. Speakers will be called in the order in which the cards are received. 

 

Public comment via Zoom teleconference may be made by using the raised hand feature in Zoom during the agenda item to be addressed. The speaker will be identified by their Display Name in Zoom when called upon to speak. Those joining the meeting via Zoom are requested to display their full name so there is no confusion when called upon to speak. Public comments will be received until the public comment period has elapsed.

 

Comments are limited to a maximum of two (2) minutes per speaker per agenda item, and individuals are limited to a single comment per agenda item.

 

Please contact boardcomments@scoe.net before the meeting if you have questions about the public comment process. 

 

Accommodating Individuals with Disabilities

 

The Sacramento County Office of Education encourages those with disabilities to participate fully in the public meeting process. If you need a disability-related modification or accommodation, including auxiliary aids or services, to participate in the public meeting, contact the Superintendent’s Office at (916) 228-2410 at least 48 hours before the scheduled Board meeting so that we may make every reasonable effort to accommodate you. [Government Code § 54953.2; Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990, § 202 (42 U.S.C. § 12132).]

Department/Director: Court and Community Schools, College and Career Initiatives, Re-Entry/Jacqueline White

 

Funding Source: Placer County Board of Supervisors - Procurement Office AB 109 and Re-Alignment Funds

 

Amount Requested: $880,000

 

Fiscal Year(s): 2022-2023

 

Program Description: 

SCOE has provided Re-Entry services for Placer County since August 2015 as part of their AB 109 implementation plan. SCOE's team will continue to provide education and case management in the Auburn and Rocklin County jails, and out-of-custody programming at the Placer County Re-Entry Program (PREP) Centers located in Roseville, Auburn, and Kings Beach. Services provided will include cognitive, behavioral, and evidence-based programming, such as substance abuse education and anger management education, along with vocational training and employment services. 

 

Juvenile services will be provided at the Children's System of Care (Placer County Probation Juvenile Department) in Roseville, the Placer Juvenile Detention Facility in Auburn, and the PREP Center in Kings Beach. Juvenile services provided include individual case management and group level services in locations without adult clients.

 

New Position:  Project Specialist I

 

Subcontracts:

SCOE is in the process of selecting providers through an RFP process, which may result in new and/or additional providers. Current providers of this work are listed below:

  • Northern California Construction and Training: $63,000
  • Placer County Office of Education: $13,000
  • Affordable Counseling Education Services (ACES): $40,536
  • Affordable Counseling Education Services (ACES): $12,500
  • Granite Wellness Centers: $11,700
  • Granite Wellness Centers (First and Multiple Offenders): $25,000
  • Pacific Educational Services (Multiple Offenders): $25,000
  • Eaton Interpreting: $5,000

 

Evaluation Component: 

SCOE will report to the Placer County Board of Supervisors the number of clients served and the education and treatment hours completed by program participants. SCOE will also track clients that complete the program and those that separate from the program prior to completion. SCOE will provide an annual report of outcomes in writing and as part of related presentations.

BACKGROUND:

 

The Sacramento County Office of Education will be receiving $743,504 during the 2022-2023 and 2023-2024 fiscal years from the Sacramento Employment and Training Agency (SETA) to provide Early Head Start home-based services to 77 children and their families in the Sacramento region. This inclusive home visitation model will provide seamless, comprehensive services to children and their families through extensive collaboration between Early Head Start and SCOE's Infant Development Program. In addition, the transition from Early Head Start to Head Start will be enhanced through this partnership by facilitating the transition for children with disabilities or special needs into inclusive preschool settings. 

 

This grant abstract and budget were approved by the Board on June 14, 2022. The attached resolution must be signed and approved by the Board per the grantor (SETA). 

  

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:

 

The Superintendent recommends that the Sacramento Board of Education adopt Resolution No. 22-13 - Authorizing Execution of Provider Agreement with the Sacramento Employment and Training Agency.
