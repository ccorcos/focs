Public Comment

 

Anyone may appear at the Board meeting to provide public comment regarding any item on the agenda or regarding any matter that is within the Board’s subject matter jurisdiction. However, the Board may not take action on any item not on the agenda except as authorized by Government Code section 54954.2. If possible, notify the Board President or Board Secretary in writing prior to the meeting if you wish to testify.

 

Individuals wishing to speak in person before the Board are requested to complete a public comment form. Speakers will be called in the order in which the cards are received. 

 

Public comment via Zoom teleconference may be made by using the raised hand feature in Zoom during the agenda item to be addressed. The speaker will be identified by their Display Name in Zoom when called upon to speak. Those joining the meeting via Zoom are requested to display their full name so there is no confusion when called upon to speak. Public comments will be received until the public comment period has elapsed.

 

Comments are limited to a maximum of two (2) minutes per speaker per agenda item, and individuals are limited to a single comment per agenda item.

 

Please contact boardcomments@scoe.net before the meeting if you have questions about the public comment process. 

 

Accommodating Individuals with Disabilities

 

The Sacramento County Office of Education encourages those with disabilities to participate fully in the public meeting process. If you need a disability-related modification or accommodation, including auxiliary aids or services, to participate in the public meeting, contact the Superintendent’s Office at (916) 228-2410 at least 48 hours before the scheduled Board meeting so that we may make every reasonable effort to accommodate you. [Government Code § 54953.2; Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990, § 202 (42 U.S.C. § 12132).]

BACKGROUND:  

 

This is to inform you that the annual School Accountability Report Cards (SARCs) for the 2022-2023 school year have been updated. Since November 1988, state law has required all public schools receiving state funding to prepare and distribute a SARC. A similar requirement is also contained in the federal Every Student Succeeds Act (ESSA). The purpose of the report card is to provide parents/guardians and the community with information regarding each public school. The public may also use a SARC to evaluate and compare schools on a variety of indicators. California Department of Education (CDE) has not yet issued certain data points throughout the document. This is noted in each section where data is incomplete. This data will be added when CDE issues this information.

 

State law requires that the SARC contain information in the following categories:

  • School Mission, Goals, and Accomplishments
  • Enrollment and Demographics
  • School Safety and Climate for Learning
  • Teaching Staff
  • School Finances
  • Curriculum and Instruction
  • Student Performance
  • State and Federal Accountability
  • School Completion and Post-secondary Preparation Information

Program Description: The Lutheran Social Services (LSS) in collaboration with the Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE), California State University, Sacramento, American River College, Sacramento City College, and Cosumnes River College, developed a program designed to provide prevention, diversion, and intervention services for youth ages 24 and under, who are enrolled in one of the colleges and who are homeless parents. The collaboratively developed program is known as the College Initiative and is funded by the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD), specifically under the Youth Homelessness Demonstration Program who recently awarded Sacramento for the delivery of services that will aim to address all stages of homelessness for families and their children. Services will include limited-term youth support in the form of financial assistance, intensive case management, housing stabilization, and supportive services to youth who qualify under the HUD definition of homeless.

 

LSS, as the lead agency, will receive referrals from the colleges, assess their needs, and connect them to supportive services and community providers. SCOE will be a subcontractor under LSS and will receive referrals for college students experiencing homeless with children ages 0-5. SCOE will meet with these families to assess their children ages 0–5 for educational needs and/or childcare needs, connect older children to local education agency homeless liaisons, provide connections to resources, and follow up with families to track engagement in education programs.

 

New Positions: None

Evaluation Component: SCOE will be responsible for collecting required data about services provided for children ages 0-5 and will input all collected data into tracking forms as required by LSS to meet the College Initiative requirements.

BACKGROUND:

 

In response to the current need of additional office space to house SCOE program staff, the Board authorized staff to complete negotiations towards the purchase of 10520 Armstrong Avenue on October 17, 2023. SCOE has negotiated an offer to purchase the office building for $2,200,640.

 

The property consists of approximately .90 acres of land and approximately 9,765 square feet of office space. Part of the negotiated offer includes the option to rent back the space to the current owners for between 30 and 120 days after the close of escrow at a rate of $1.50 per square foot plus expenses and maintenance.

Once vacant, the office space will require upgrades and improvements to make it suitable for use by SCOE programs. Architectural and engineering services will be needed to assist in determining best options for the required upgrades, to develop construction documents, and to ensure construction documents are in adherence with the laws and codes required by federal, state, local, and fire district agencies.

Upon Board authorization, and completion of architectural drawings and specifications, staff will advertise and utilize a formal bid process for services. At a future Board meeting, staff will seek Board approval to award the contract for the construction work to the lowest responsible/responsive bidder.

SCOE has sufficient reserve funds to purchase the property and complete the necessary site improvements and believes it would be a prudent long-term investment for the agency. The building is currently being used as office space by an architecture firm and can be configured to meet our current office space needs. The proximity to SCOE's other facilities is ideal and will facilitate connecting technology and enable a more efficient use of space as staff could continue to utilize shared conference and meeting rooms.

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:

 

The Superintendent recommends that the Board approve the purchase of the property at 10520 Armstrong Avenue and authorize staff to conduct business as required to obtain bids for the necessary improvements, obtain all required federal, state, and local approvals, hiring of architects, and seeking public bids for the work.

BACKGROUND:

 

The Personnel Commission administers the Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) Merit System. The Commission is comprised of three members appointed for three-year terms with the term of one member expiring each year. One member is selected by the Sacramento County Board of Education, one member is selected by the classified employee organization (CSEA), and the third member is selected by the other two Commissioners. (Education Code 45245)

 

The Personnel Commission has three core responsibilities as defined in Education Code which are as follows:

 

  • Oversee a personnel management program based on merit principles that ensure the selection and promotion of employees is based solely on qualifications through competitive examination.
  • Develop and administer policies governing employment matters to ensure the fair and equitable treatment of employees within the classified service.
  • Conduct appeal hearings on matters involving employee disciplinary action, employment examinations, and personnel policies and procedures.

 

Personnel Commissioner Qualifications

 

Appointees to the Personnel Commission must meet the qualifications as per Education Code 45244 which include:

 

a) Applicant must be a registered voter and reside within the territorial jurisdiction of the county office/school district; and

 

b) Be a known adherent to the principle of the merit system (a person who, by virtue of prior public or private service, has given evidence of support to the merit system concept – i.e., the selection, retention, and promotion of individuals upon the basis of merit and fitness).

 

c) The candidate shall not be a member of the governing board or of any other district governing board or county board of education, nor shall he/she be an employee of the district/county office of education.

 

Personnel Commission Vacancy and Appointment

 

Personnel Commissioner Gail Mullen’s term ended November 30, 2023, leaving a vacancy for the CSEA-appointed commissioner. CSEA President Tara Degg has informed SCOE that CSEA has nominated Lee Miller to fill this vacancy, the term of which runs through November 30, 2026. Ms. Miller's bio (provided by CSEA) is attached.

 

Pursuant to Education Code section 45246, the Board is required to appoint the classified employees’ nominee unless the classified employees voluntarily withdraw the name of the nominee and submit the name of a new nominee, in which case the Board shall appoint the new nominee.
