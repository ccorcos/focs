[[**BACKGROUND:**]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[\"Operation Recognition\" is a program adopted by the Sacramento
County Board of Education and other organizations throughout the United
States to honor individuals whose secondary education was interrupted
due to circumstances of World War II, the Korean War, and the Vietnam
War. During the 1940s, and again in the 1950s and 1960s, thousands of
young men and women left high school to serve in the United States Armed
Forces. After these wars, many veterans were not able to finish high
school but led productive lives and helped build our communities over
their
lifetimes.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[Also during World War II, thousands of Japanese American citizens were
forced to leave their homes to be interned in relocation camps at
various locations in the United States. These citizens were denied the
freedom other Americans enjoyed, and many served with the U.S. Armed
Forces. Some were unable to complete high school, yet they, too, went on
to build productive lives and have greatly contributed to the American
way of
life.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[Through the \"Operation Recognition\" program, the Sacramento County
Office of Education will provide a high school diploma to qualifying
World War II, Korean War, and Vietnam War veterans, and to Japanese
American citizens who were interned in World War II relocation camps.
This is a gesture of our society\'s gratitude for the sacrifices they
made in the name of
freedom.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[The Sacramento County Office of Education has received applications
from individuals who qualify as recipients of high school diplomas
through the \"Operation Recognition\" program. The following individual
is considered to have met the requirements for high school
graduation:]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[Charles "Chuck" LeDoux (Vietnam

[[SCOE\'s Business Services Office is recommending that SCOE opt into
the California Uniform Construction Cost Account Act (CUPCCAA) to help
SCOE timely and efficiently complete small construction projects.
CUPCCAA will enable SCOE to use its own work force for small projects
and will provide more opportunities to contract with local businesses.
Opting into CUPCCAA requires the Board to adopt the new policy and to
adopt a resolution electing to become subject to the

[[[[***[What is
CUPCCAA?]{.underline}***]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}* *]{new=""
roman="" style="font-family:" times=""}]{style="font-size:12pt"}

 

[[[[[[[[[CUPCCAA was enacted in 1983 to help promote uniformity in
public entity construction projects in California. Almost 1,500 public
entities in California are using CUPCCAA, including 691 school districts
and 67 community college districts. Public entities that elect to become
subject to CUPCCAA must adopt a resolution and policy, file a copy of
the resolution with the State Controller's Office, and comply with the
]{style="user-select:text"}]{style="font-variant-ligatures:none !important"}]{contrast="auto"
style="user-select:text" lang="EN-US"}Uniform Public Construction Cost
Accounting Procedures. [[[CUPCCAA only governs bidding and cost
accounting procedures for construction projects. Other applicable public
contracting requirements will still apply, such as paying prevailing
wages.
]{style="user-select:text"}]{style="font-variant-ligatures:none !important"}]{contrast="auto"
style="user-select:text"
lang="EN-US"}]{style="font-kerning:none"}]{style="white-space:pre-wrap"}]{style="overflow-wrap:break-word"}]{style="user-select:text"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[***[Key Provisions of
CUPCCAA]{.underline}**** *]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[[[[[[[[CUPCCAA would allow SCOE to timely execute small construction
projects, expedite project delivery, and would provide greater access to
local businesses. SCOE could use its own workforce to complete
construction projects costing \$60,000 or less, or negotiate a contract
with a local business or other qualified contractor. SCOE could utilize
an informal bid process for projects costing \$200,000 or less, and
formal bidding would be required for projects costing more than
\$200,000. Currently, public projects costing \$15,000 and more require
formal
bidding.]{style="user-select:text"}]{style="font-variant-ligatures:none !important"}]{contrast="auto"
style="user-select:text"
lang="EN-US"}]{style="font-kerning:none"}]{style="white-space:pre-wrap"}]{style="overflow-wrap:break-word"}]{style="user-select:text"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[CUPCCAA would enable SCOE to timely complete small construction
projects and would allow for greater flexibility in the scheduling of
projects. While the formal bid process takes 3.5 to 4 months, the
contracting process under CUPCCAA would be much shorter for projects
less than \$200,000 (i.e., [[[within two weeks for projects less than
\$60,000, and
]{style="user-select:text"}]{style="font-variant-ligatures:none !important"}]{contrast="auto"
style="user-select:text" lang="EN-US"}[within three or four weeks for
projects less than \$200,000 using the informal bid
process).]{style="font-variant-ligatures:none"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[For the informal bid process, CUPCCAA requires SCOE to establish or
update a list of qualified contractors at least once per calendar year
by sending out a notice to designated trade journals inviting licensed
contractors to submit their names. SCOE would also post the notice with
local trade journals and with various groups to attract a diverse pool
of contractors. Qualified contractors must be placed on the list and
notified by SCOE of opportunities to bid on construction projects. The
lowest responsive bidder would be awarded the contract.
]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[Creating a list of qualified contractors will provide SCOE with
greater access to local businesses. SCOE must currently utilize CMAS and
other cooperative agreements for small, time-sensitive construction
projects. These lists enable SCOE to timely complete projects while
complying with public contracting requirements, but they do not have a
large selection of local
contractors.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[In the last six months, SCOE has completed twelve construction

including several that require structural and safety-related repairs

[[[**Joint Powers
Authorities**]{.underline}** **]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

[[[State law authorizes two or more public agencies to establish a JPA
for the purpose of jointly exercising common powers. Forming such
entities facilitates creative approaches to the provision of public
services and provides public agencies with the means to provide services
more efficiently and in a cost-effective manner. Local educational
agencies (LEAs) have formed JPAs to provide special education, school
transportation, school construction, financial information systems,
procurement services and insurance and risk management, among other
things.]{style="line-height:107%"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

[[[SCOE created the oldest JPA in California when it formed the Schools
Insurance Authority (SIA) in 1974. SIA currently provides property,
liability and workers\' compensation insurance to 35 LEA and JPA members
in California. The JPA structure allows SIA's members to take advantage
of prudent risk pooling techniques, effective prevention services, joint
purchasing of services and the development of necessary programs that
serve to protect their staff and their
assets.]{style="line-height:107%"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

[[[**[Government Financial Services
JPA]{style="line-height:107%"}**]{.underline}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

[[[The volume of debt issued by school districts in California has grown
significantly over the last few years and reached a new high of \$14.4
billion last year. As a result, there is  a growing need for specialized
expertise to support LEA debt financings that is guided by established
best practices and that is transparent, efficient and encourages public
trust with respect to taxpayer
funds.]{style="line-height:107%"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

[[[The purpose of the Government Financial Services JPA is to meet this
need by assisting LEAs with their financial matters including, but not
limited to, planning and implementing financings and financial
strategies best practices for the fiscal benefit of LEAs and the
taxpayers which support them. The JPA will provide SCOE with all the
services and support it currently receives from Government Financial
Strategies.]{style="line-height:107%"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

[[[SCOE will be a founding member of the JPA with a permanent seat on
its Board which will provide policy direction for the JPA, annually
adopt a budget, appoint a chief executive officer, approve new members
and set the annual membership fee. JPA members will have three
year-terms. At the end of each three-year term, SCOE will have the
option of discontinuing its membership or renewing it for another
three-year
term.]{style="line-height:107%"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

[[**Assembly Bill 2774 (Weber) - Adds Lowest-Performing Pupil Subgroup
to Supplemental and Concentration
Funds**]{style="font-size:14px;"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}

 

[[[California\'s public school financing system currently provides
funding for county superintendents of schools, school districts, and
charter schools (LEAs) as calculated under the Local Control Funding
Formula (LCFF). Under the LCFF, LEAs receive additional supplemental and
concentration funding based on their percentage of "unduplicated
pupils," which are defined as English learners, pupils eligible for free
or reduced-price meals, and foster
youth.]{style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51); font-style: normal; font-variant-ligatures: normal; font-variant-caps: normal; font-weight: 400; letter-spacing: normal; orphans: 2; text-align: justify; text-indent: 0px; text-transform: none; white-space: normal; widows: 2; word-spacing: 0px; -webkit-text-stroke-width: 0px; background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255); text-decoration-thickness: initial; text-decoration-style: initial; text-decoration-color: initial; float: none; display: inline !important;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}

 

[[[If enacted, AB 2774 would expand the definition of "unduplicated
pupils" to include pupils in the lowest performing subgroup(s) based on
the most recently available mathematics or language arts results on the
California Assessment of Student Performance and Progress (CAASPP). The
bill would require the State Superintendent of Public Instruction to
annually identify the lowest performing pupil subgroup(s) based upon the
most recently available CAASPP results, and to use 2019 CAASPP results
to identify this subgroup for the 2023-2024 school
year.]{style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51); font-style: normal; font-variant-ligatures: normal; font-variant-caps: normal; font-weight: 400; letter-spacing: normal; orphans: 2; text-align: justify; text-indent: 0px; text-transform: none; white-space: normal; widows: 2; word-spacing: 0px; -webkit-text-stroke-width: 0px; background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255); text-decoration-thickness: initial; text-decoration-style: initial; text-decoration-color: initial; float: none; display: inline !important;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}

 

[[[The lowest performing subgroup would not include those already
receiving LCFF supplemental and concentration funding under current law
(English learners, low-income pupils, and
foster ]{style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51); font-style: normal; font-variant-ligatures: normal; font-variant-caps: normal; font-weight: 400; letter-spacing: normal; orphans: 2; text-align: justify; text-indent: 0px; text-transform: none; white-space: normal; widows: 2; word-spacing: 0px; -webkit-text-stroke-width: 0px; background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255); text-decoration-thickness: initial; text-decoration-style: initial; text-decoration-color: initial; float: none; display: inline !important;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}[[[youth)

[[**Assembly Bill 2617 (Holden) and Governor\'s Budget Proposal on Dual
Enrollment**]{style="font-size:14px;"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}

 

[[Governor Newsom\'s budget has proposed \$500 million for school
districts, charter schools, and county offices of education (LEAs) to
strengthen and expand access for high school students to participate in
dual enrollment opportunities. Dual enrollment allows high school
students to take college courses and earn college
credit.]{style="font-size:14px;"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}

 

[[Existing law authorizes partnerships between community college
districts and LEAs to create dual enrollment opportunities with the goal
of developing seamless pathways for career technical education,
preparing students to transfer to college and to achieve college and
career readiness, and improving high school graduation
rates.]{style="font-size:14px;"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}

 

[[If enacted, AB 2617 would appropriate \$500 million for competitive
grants for LEAs to establish opportunities for pupils to obtain college
credit while enrolled in high school and provide dual enrollment. An LEA
could apply for (1) a one-time grant of up to \$500,000 to establish
outreach campaigns to encourage pupils to enroll in dual enrollment
programs and to couple robust pupil advising and success supports with
available dual enrollment and accelerated college credit opportunities;
(2) a one-time grant of up to \$250,000 to plan for and start up a
middle college or early college high school on its campus; and/or (3) a
one-time grant of up to \$100,000 to establish a College and Career
Access Pathways dual enrollment partnership agreement with a community
college to enable its pupils to access dual enrollment
opportunities.]{style="font-size:14px;"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}

 

[[The State Superintendent of Public Instruction would administer the
grant program and would be required to give priority for available grant
funds to LEAs with (1) enrollment of more than 50% unduplicated pupils;
(2) a higher than state average dropout rate; or (3) a higher than state
average rate of suspension and
expulsion.]{style="font-size:14px;"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}
