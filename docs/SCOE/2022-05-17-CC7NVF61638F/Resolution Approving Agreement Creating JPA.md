SACRAMENTO COUNTY BOARD OF EDUCATION
AOPTING AND APPROVING THE JOINT POWERS AUTHORITY AGREEMENT
CREATING GOVERNMENT FINANCIAL SERVICES JPA
Resolution No. 22-11

WHEREAS, Title I, Division 7, Chapter 5, Article I (Section 6500 et seq.) of the
Government Code of the State of California authorizes the joint exercise by two or more
public agencies of any power common to them ; and

WHEREAS, there is a need by public agencies for specialized expertise to support
financial operations including, but not limited to, planning and implementing financings,
developing financial strategies guided by established best practices, and providing
quantitative analysis, for the ultimate purpose of providing fiscal benefits to the
agencies and to the taxpayers which support them; and

WHEREAS, public agencies may utilize financing to accomplish the acquisition of real
property and real property interests and the development, improvement, and repair of
real property, and the leasing and/or purchasing of equipment; and

WHEREAS, financial analysis and implementation of financing transactions for such
periodic endeavors often requires the labor and expertise of external public finance
experts; and

WHEREAS, such financial activities can be guided by established best practices, with
the goals of being supported by appropriate quantitative analysis, and reflecting
transparency, efficiency, and the importance of the public trust with respect to taxpayer
funds; and

WHEREAS, each of the member agencies individually have the authority to perform
and procure the services contemplated by the Joint Powers Authority; and

WHEREAS, the Sacramento County Board of Education, has determined that entering
into a Joint Powers Agreement for making financial services available in the future to
assist it with financial activities through a joint effort with other public agencies is in its
best interests.

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Sacramento County Board of
Education hereby declares and formally approves its membership in the Government
Financial Services Joint Power Authority, a California Joint Powers Authority and
instructs the Sacramento County Superintendent of Schools to execute on behalf of the
Sacramento County Office of Education, the Joint Powers Authority Agreement and to
take any further actions necessary to carry out the purposes of this Resolution.

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Sacramento County
Board of Education hereby approves the payment of $5,000 as its initial membership
fee.
