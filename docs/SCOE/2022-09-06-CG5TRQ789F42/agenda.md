projects have involved obtaining all required federal, state, and local
approvals, hiring architects, and seeking public bids for the work to be
completed at various sites, including Sly Park Outdoor Education Center.

include a deck replacement at the administration office, roof
replacement of various buildings, HVAC modifications to increase
ventilation for improved indoor air quality, and installation of an
updated fire system. Staff sought construction bids, which closed on
July 28, 2022, and with Board approval, is ready to award the
construction contract

[[**BACKGROUND:**]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[[[[At a previous meeting, Sacramento County Board of Education (Board)
members asked that the Board consider whether to express support
for ]{style="background-color:#ffffff;"}]{style="color:#333333"}]{style="page:WordSection1"}[[California
Proposition 28, the Art and Music K-12 Education Funding Initiative,
which is on the November 8, 2022 California General Election ballot. If
the Board decides to support Proposition
28, ]{style="color:#333333"}]{arial=""}[[[Sacramento County Office of
Education staff will prepare a letter of support on behalf of the
Board.]{style="background-color:#ffffff;"}]{style="color:#333333"}]{style="page:WordSection1"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

::: WordSection1
 

[[[Beginning in 2023-2024, Proposition 28 would provide additional
funding to increase arts instruction and programs in public schools. The
amount provided each year would equal one percent of the share of the
Proposition 98 Guarantee (the constitutionally required amount of
funding for public schools and community colleges) that went to K-12
schools in the prior year. The Legislative Analyst\'s Office projects
this amount would be approximately \$1 billion next year. However, we
note that the Proposition 98 Guarantee is determined by state revenues
and other state economic factors and, as a result, can be volatile.
Furthermore, Proposition 28 would allow the Legislature to reduce the
funding it provides any year the Legislature suspends the Proposition 98
Guarantee.]{style="line-height:107%"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

[[[Proposition 28 would distribute the additional funding it provides to
public schools based on enrollment in preschool and K-12. Of the total
amount:]{style="line-height:107%"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

- [[[70 percent goes to schools based on their share of statewide
  enrollment.]{style="line-height:107%"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}
- [[[30 percent goes to schools based on their share of low-income
  students enrolled
  statewide.]{style="line-height:107%"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

[[[We estimate that SCOE would receive approximately \$600,000 and all
the local educational agencies (LEAs) in Sacramento County would
collectively receive approximately \$40 million for arts education
programs.]{style="line-height:107%"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

[[[The proposition would require local educational agencies (LEAs) to
distribute the funding to school sites based on their student enrollment
to supplement existing funding for arts education programs. Furthermore,
at least 80 percent of the funding must be used to hire staff, although
LEAs with fewer than 500 students would be exempt from this requirement.
The remaining funding can be used for training, supplies and materials,
and for arts educational partnership programs. In addition, LEAs may use
up to 1 percent of this new funding for administrative
expenses.]{style="line-height:107%"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

[[[Given the potential volatility of this funding and the requirements
that it supplement existing funding and that 80 percent be used to hire
staff, LEAs may have difficulty managing this funding. It may be
difficult to make year-to-year staffing adjustments necessary to align
with funding fluctuations. Furthermore, it may be difficult to reduce
funding for arts programs in the event of an economic downturn, which
could necessitate even greater reductions to other critical educational
programs. However, the proposition would allow the California Department
of Education to approve requests from schools to spend less on
staff.]{style="line-height:107%"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}
