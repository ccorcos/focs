[[**BACKGROUND: **]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[The Sacramento County Local Child Care and Development Planning
Council (LPC) is authorized by the California State Legislature, in
accordance with the Welfare and Institutions Code (WIC) Sections
10485-10487, to provide a forum for the identification of local
priorities for child care and the development of policies to meet the
needs identified within those priorities. Pursuant to Assembly Bill (AB)
131 (Chapter 116, Statues of 2021), signed by the Governor on July 23,
2021, all statues governing LPCs were transferred from the Education
Code (EC) to WIC, and are now found in WIC Sections 10480 and 10485
through
10487. ]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[The purpose of the LPC is to provide a forum for the planning of
childcare services that meet the diverse needs of families in Sacramento
County, promote public participation in the planning process, advocate
for the needs of families in the County with respect to childcare, and
serve as a forum for discussion of community childcare issues. The LPC
meets four times per year. Ad hoc subcommittees meet as
needed. ]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[The LPC is comprised of 20 members with appointment duties split
between the Board of Supervisors (10 members) and the County Board of
Education (10 members). Each Board appoints two members in each of the
following
categories:]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[**Consumer** - a parent or person who receives, or who has received
within the past 36 months, childcare
services. ]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[**Child Care Provider** - a person who provides childcare services or
represents persons who provide childcare
services.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[**Public Agency Representative** - a person who represents a city,
county, city and county, or local education
agency. ]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[**Community Representative** - a person who represents an agency or
business that provides private funding for childcare services, or who
advocates for childcare services through participation in civic or
community-based organizations but is not a childcare provider and does
not represent an agency that contracts with the State Department of
Education or the Department of Social Services to provide childcare and
development
services. ]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[**Board Discretion** - a person appointed at the discretion of the
Board. Candidates should have a combination of early learning education
and diverse experiences that will contribute to fulfilling the LPC\'s
responsibilities, including knowledge about childcare and early learning
services in Sacramento County, and regional/statewide programs and
trends that could impact early learning
services. ]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

disseminated by the Early Learning Department. After screening of
applications, SCOE staff conducted interviews. The name of the following
individual is submitted for appointment in the designated

  Appointment**), Early Childhood Education Coordinator, Twin Rivers
