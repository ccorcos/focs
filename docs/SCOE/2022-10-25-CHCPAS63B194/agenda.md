[[[The Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) submitted a grant
application to serve as a CA Community Schools Partnership Regional
Technical Assistance Center (RTAC). In partnership with Placer County
Office of Education (PCOE), SCOE will serve as a co-lead for Capitol
Area Community Schools Consortium (CACSC). CACSC consists of twelve
county offices of education (COEs) - Alpine, Colusa, El Dorado, Nevada,
Placer, Sacramento, San Joaquin, Sierra, Solano, Sutter, Yolo, and Yuba.
]{style="color:black"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[[SCOE and PCOE will [lead the consortium's strategic direction to
develop and implement a high-quality ]{style="background:white"}CCSPP
RTAC to support community schools across the Capitol Area region. The
consortium is partnering with three renowned and established non-profit
organizations: Community Schools Learning Exchange, Turnaround for
Children, and Learning Heroes. CACSC and its key partners will
collaborate to reach its goals and expected outcomes, by co-creating the
design of the regional technical assistance system and implementation of
the strategic plan to start up, sustain, and scale community schools.
Customized technical assistance and partnership will be provided via
consultation, training, coaching, and regional capacity building to
support high-quality systems improvement at the county, district, and
school
levels.]{style="color:black"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[The CCSPP provides Regional TAC funding for a contract period
beginning March 1, 2023 and running through June 30, 2028. This contract
period will be separated into two allocation periods; this initial RFA
is for the first contract period which runs through June 30, 2025.
]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}
