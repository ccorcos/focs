6. Superintendent’s Report

A. Superintendent Gordon reported on the following:

August has been an inspiring month filled with milestones, celebrations, and important
community partnerships. We began with a Back-to-School Backpack Drive during Team
Time. SchoolsFirst Federal Credit Union provided 400 backpacks filled with supplies for
students in need.

On August 21, 2025, El Centro Jr./Sr. High School held the first graduation of the year at
the El Centro campus, with four graduates honored. Thank you to Trustee Brown for
joining us to celebrate their success.

On August 22, 2025, at the Teachers of the Year Awards Banquet at the Hilton
Sacramento Arden West SCOE announced our 2026 Sacramento County Teachers of
the Year: Bethany Hancock, a third grade teacher at Elk Grove Elementary in the Elk
Grove Unified School District, and Derek Tahara, a third grade teacher at David Lubin
Elementary in the Sacramento City Unified School District. Thank you, Trustees Brown,
Caigoy, Davis, and our Youth County Board Member Dunbar for joining us and
representing the Board at this event. Thank you to our Communications Team and
volunteers from CNTS and Ed Services for making this such a memorable evening.

On August 27, 2025, we gathered at the Sacramento Central Library, with United Way
and state and local leaders to launch Dolly Parton’s Imagination Library in Sacramento
County. The event underscored the importance of introducing children to books early in

Leo A. Palmiter’s team will host a back-to-school night for students and families on
September 10, 2025. The evening begins at 5:30 p.m. with a light meal served in the
multipurpose room, followed by a brief program led by Principal Lauren Roth. The
evening concludes with classroom visits and a visit to the Horticulture program’s garden

On September 13, 2025, SCOE will participate in the Third Annual Sacramento Latino
Book and Family Festival at Valley High School in Sacramento. President Corona
Sabeniano will speak, following keynote speakers Grace Tiscareño-Sato, David Aguilar,
and Dolores Huerta.

Also on September 13, 2025, Underground Books and St. HOPE will present the Sac
BlackLit Book Fest in Oak Park. Xanthi Soriano will be representing SCOE and will take
part in several sessions, including Shop Talk with Shaka Senghor, a Conversation with
University Leaders, and the VIP Reception.

On September 20, 2025, SCOE will join the Literacy Corner at the One Natomas
Farmers Market at North Natomas Regional Park. Sacramento County READS and our
Early Learning Department will provide free books, Imagination Library sign-ups, early
literacy activities, and information about Help Me Grow and Early Head Start.

On September 24, 2025, SCOE will participate in the Shingle Springs Tribal TANF Back-
to-School Night at the Sacramento TANF Office. Our staff will share family resources,
information about CalSOAP and AVID, and connect directly with youth and families.

Also on September 24, 2025, at the SCOE Conference Center we kick off our next
series of Community of Practice sessions. The first session features Daniel Silberstein
from the California Department of Education’s Diagnostic Center of Northern California.
Daniel is a leading specialist in dyslexia identification and intervention, with deep
experience in screening protocols and assessment practices. He helped shape
California’s Reading Difficulties Risk Screener and has trained educators statewide
through the California Dyslexia Initiative. This session will focus on California’s new K–2
screeners and practical steps LEAs can take to implement them effectively.

On September 27, 2025, SCOE will be a sponsor of the Sacramento Literacy Festival at
the Oak Park Farmers Market. President Corona Sabeniano is scheduled to speak at
approximately 12:00 p.m. We will provide family resources, distribute free books, and
recruit for open staff positions. Early Learning will support Sacramento County READS
with Imagination Library sign-ups, early literacy activities, Help Me Grow, and Early
Head Start resources.

11. Board Reports, Comments, and Ideas

A. Board Member Suggestions on Future Agenda Items ("Idea Log")

Trustees Keefer and Lefkovitz requested a report on the Board’s role in negotiations.

Trustee Lefkovitz requested a report on strategies to improve attendance; address
transportation issues; identify number of kids that state transportation as a barrier to
attendance, identify subgroups with transportation challenges; and discuss a solution
for every kid to attend school every day. She also requested: that a site visit to North
Area School be added to schedule; a Board Report schedule be distributed; a
discussion about topics to be covered at future Board meetings be had, as well as a
discussion about Board priorities and how the Board plans to be more forward-
looking related to the changing landscape of education.

Trustee Caigoy requested that the dashboard presentation include the
intersectionality of our foster youth and our homeless youth as it relates to ELA and
math scores, etc. She asked if the 17 students that state transportation as a barrier
to attendance are part of a subgroup that might be lowest performing.

Trustee Brown requested to discuss ways to make schools a place of safety for
students.

Trustee Caigoy asked if we have any data on how students are being impacted by
trafficking as it relates to school safety and what can the Board do to educate our
staff and neighboring districts on ways to protect our students?

Trustee Fong requests that Miracle University present at a future SCSBA meeting as
well as more strategies for student safety as it relates to ICE on school campuses.
