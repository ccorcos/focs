property no longer needed or that is determined unsuitable for school
use.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[The Computer, Network, and Telecommunication Support Department (CNTS)
reviews all technology surplus equipment to ensure that any repairable,
non-obsolete equipment is re-used by SCOE programs. In the event the
equipment cannot be repaired, is no longer able to support the latest
security patches and updates (posing a network security risk) or is so
obsolete it can no longer be used for its intended purpose, the
equipment is deemed unsuitable for use and is recommended for
disposal. ]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[The technology equipment listed below has been determined to be
unsuitable for use and of insufficient value to defray the costs of
arranging a sale. It is in the SCOE's best interest to deem these items
obsolete and dispose of them. All storage devices (hard drives, solid
state drives, USB drives, etc.) are completely wiped of data and,
whenever possible, physically shredded to ensure the destruction of all
electronic data before
disposal.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

[[[In accordance with the requirements of Education Code Section 60119,
the Sacramento County Board of Education (Board) will hold a public
hearing during the September 2, 2025 Regular Board Meeting to determine
whether each pupil in SCOE community and special education schools has
sufficient textbooks or instructional materials, or both, in
English/language arts, including the English language development
component of an adopted program, mathematics, history/social science,
and science that are aligned to the state content standards adopted by
the State Board of Education
(SBE).]{style="font-style: inherit; font-variant-ligatures: inherit; font-variant-caps: inherit; font-weight: inherit;"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

[[[[[As part of this determination, the Board also must determine if
each pupil who is enrolled in a foreign language or health course has
sufficient instructional materials that are consistent with the content
and cycles of curriculum frameworks adopted by the SBE. In addition, the
Board must determine if pupils enrolled in a laboratory science course
have adequate
equipment.]{style="box-sizing: inherit; margin: 0px; padding: 0px; border: 0px; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; font-weight: inherit; font-stretch: inherit; line-height: 14.98px; font-optical-sizing: inherit; font-size-adjust: inherit; font-kerning: inherit; font-feature-settings: inherit; font-variation-settings: inherit;"}]{style="box-sizing: inherit; margin: 0px; padding: 0px; border: 0px; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; font-weight: inherit; font-stretch: inherit; line-height: inherit; font-optical-sizing: inherit; font-size-adjust: inherit; font-kerning: inherit; font-feature-settings: inherit; font-variation-settings: inherit;"}]{style="box-sizing: inherit; margin: 0px; padding: 0px; border: 0px; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; font-weight: inherit; font-stretch: inherit; line-height: inherit; font-optical-sizing: inherit; font-size-adjust: inherit; font-kerning: inherit; font-feature-settings: inherit; font-variation-settings: inherit;"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

[[[[[Pupils enrolled in a health course will have sufficient materials.
SCOE does not offer foreign language instruction in any grades, nor does
SCOE offer science laboratory courses in any of grades 9 through 12.
Therefore, SCOE need not maintain science laboratory equipment.
Completion of a science laboratory course is not a requirement for high
school graduation, though completion of a course in either foreign
language or visual or performing arts is a requirement (EC 51225.3). As
necessary for completion of this graduation requirement, SCOE students
are provided a course in fine
arts.]{style="box-sizing: inherit; margin: 0px; padding: 0px; border: 0px; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; font-weight: inherit; font-stretch: inherit; line-height: 14.98px; font-optical-sizing: inherit; font-size-adjust: inherit; font-kerning: inherit; font-feature-settings: inherit; font-variation-settings: inherit;"}]{style="box-sizing: inherit; margin: 0px; padding: 0px; border: 0px; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; font-weight: inherit; font-stretch: inherit; line-height: inherit; font-optical-sizing: inherit; font-size-adjust: inherit; font-kerning: inherit; font-feature-settings: inherit; font-variation-settings: inherit;"}]{style="box-sizing: inherit; margin: 0px; padding: 0px; border: 0px; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; font-weight: inherit; font-stretch: inherit; line-height: inherit; font-optical-sizing: inherit; font-size-adjust: inherit; font-kerning: inherit; font-feature-settings: inherit; font-variation-settings: inherit;"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

and public at the
hearing.]{style="box-sizing: inherit; margin: 0px; padding: 0px; border: 0px; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; font-weight: inherit; font-stretch: inherit; line-height: 14.98px; font-optical-sizing: inherit; font-size-adjust: inherit; font-kerning: inherit; font-feature-settings: inherit; font-variation-settings: inherit;"}]{style="box-sizing: inherit; margin: 0px; padding: 0px; border: 0px; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; font-weight: inherit; font-stretch: inherit; line-height: inherit; font-optical-sizing: inherit; font-size-adjust: inherit; font-kerning: inherit; font-feature-settings: inherit; font-variation-settings: inherit;"}]{style="box-sizing: inherit; margin: 0px; padding: 0px; border: 0px; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; font-weight: inherit; font-stretch: inherit; line-height: inherit; font-optical-sizing: inherit; font-size-adjust: inherit; font-kerning: inherit; font-feature-settings: inherit; font-variation-settings: inherit;"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}
