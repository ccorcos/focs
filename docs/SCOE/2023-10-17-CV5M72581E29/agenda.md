[[**BACKGROUND:**]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[The Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) has grown extensively
since it moved to the Mather location twenty years ago. New legislation,
grants, and contracts have resulted in SCOE taking on additional
responsibilities locally and statewide, creating many new programs.
Consequently, SCOE's current facilities are not sufficient to house
SCOE's staff, and there is a need to secure additional
space. ]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[SCOE has been exploring other facility options and after looking at
the cost of leasing a facility, it appears it would be more fiscally
responsible to purchase a property if we are able to find one with a
suitable location and price.
]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[SCOE has learned that the single-story office building adjacent to the
Conference Center may be available for purchase. The owners of 10520
Armstrong Avenue have indicated a potential interest in selling their
building, and they have quoted a price. SCOE staff will continue to
negotiate. ]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[SCOE has sufficient reserve funds to purchase the property and
believes it would be a prudent long-term investment for the agency. The
building is currently being used as office space by an architecture
firm, and it could be configured to meet our current office space needs.
Its proximity to SCOE's other facilities is ideal and would facilitate
connecting technology and enable a more efficient use of space as staff
could continue to utilize shared conference and meeting rooms. 
]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[This project will require SCOE to contract with consultants in the
following
areas:]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

[[1.
Appraisers;]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

[[2. Architects and a design
team; ]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

[[3. Experts for specialized studies and reports as
needed.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[For this project, staff requests authority to conduct research
regarding potential acquisition of the proposed Armstrong Avenue site
and enter negotiations. This grant of authority includes the ability to
seek proposals or bids as appropriate, engage all necessary contractors,
to conduct research, and negotiate. 
]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

RECOMMENDATION: **]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[The Superintendent recommends the Board authorize staff to complete
the business as required to hire necessary consultants, including an
architect and design team, and complete the research and negotiations
towards the purchase of the property located at 10520 Armstrong
Avenue.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}
