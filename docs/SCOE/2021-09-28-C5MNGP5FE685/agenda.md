[[**CHARTER
PETITION:**]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[On September 28, 2021, the Sacramento County Board of Education
(County Board) will conduct a public hearing and consider the Capital
Career & College Academy (CCCA) countywide charter petition. CCCA's
petition proposes a STEAM-focused, early college, countywide charter
high school, with a curriculum centered on linked learning and authentic
project-based experiences. CCCA's plan is to open with an initial 9th
grade cohort of 70 students in August 2022 and grow to serve 370
students in grades 9-12 by 2025-2026, while providing students with the
option to return for a fifth year of learning and
support.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[Following review of the CCCA petition and the public hearing, the
County Board must either approve or deny the petition. If the County
Board denies the countywide charter petition, CCCA has no right of
appeal. (Ed. Code, § 47605.6(k); Cal. Code Regs., tit. 5, §
11966.5(e).)]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[**BACKGROUND:**]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[From February 2019 through June 2021, Sacramento County Office of
Education (SCOE) staff met numerous times with CCCA representatives to
discuss its anticipated charter school petition, review the standards
required for approval of a countywide charter, and provide feedback
regarding major elements of CCCA's proposal that needed strengthening.
]{style="font-size:14px;"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}

 

[[CCCA submitted its petition for a countywide charter school on July 1,
2021, then supplemented its petition with signed affirmations on July 9,
2021, and with updated bylaws and board member descriptions on August
15,
2021.]{style="font-size:14px;"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}

 

[[[On August 4 and 6, 2021, SCOE met with CCCA to express concerns with
key elements of CCCA's petition, including the budget, description of
the educational program, and CCCA's readiness to successfully implement
its proposed program. CCCA elected to proceed with its petition and, on
September 2, 2021, submitted a revised budget and information on newly
appointed board members. Neither CCCA's original or supplemented
petition adequately address the major concerns raised by SCOE in its
multiple meetings with CCCA before and after the petition was
submitted.]{arial=""}]{style="font-size:14px;"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}

 

[[**SCOE CHARTER PETITION
ANALYSIS**]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[CCCA's vision is to prepare students for college and career by
providing opportunities for early college, project-based learning, and
practical workplace training, experience, and certifications. However,
CCCA's charter petition does not include sufficient justification for
authorization as a countywide charter school, nor does it present a
realistic, sustainable plan to successfully implement or accomplish its
vision.]{style="font-size:14px;"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}

 

[[While the petition contains adequate descriptions of certain elements,
it is deficient in the most important areas needed to operate and
sustain a school. It lacks a viable budget, a realistic staffing plan,
evidence of a sound educational program, a sufficient plan to support
students with special needs and English learners, and reasonably
comprehensive descriptions of all the legally required elements,
including the educational program, measurable pupil outcomes, charter
school facilities, staff qualifications, and retirement systems
coverage.]{style="font-size:14px;"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}

 

[[**SUPERINTENDENT\'S
RECOMMENDATION:**]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[The Superintendent recommends that the County Board adopt SCOE's
Recommended Findings of Fact and deny the Capital Career & College
Academy countywide charter
petition.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}
