Public Comment

 

Anyone may appear at the Board meeting to provide public comment regarding any item on the agenda or regarding any matter that is within the Board’s subject matter jurisdiction. However, the Board may not take action on any item not on the agenda except as authorized by Government Code section 54954.2.  If possible, notify the Board President or Board Secretary in writing prior to the meeting if you wish to testify.

 

Individuals wishing to speak in person before the Board are requested to complete a public comment form. Speakers will be called in the order in which the cards are received. 

 

Public comment via Zoom teleconference may be made by using the raised hand feature in Zoom during the agenda item to be addressed. The speaker will be identified by their Display Name in Zoom when called upon to speak. Those joining the meeting via Zoom are requested to display their full name so there is no confusion when called upon to speak. Public comments will be received until the public comment period has elapsed.

 

Comments are limited to a maximum of two (2) minutes per speaker per agenda item, and individuals are limited to a single comment per agenda item.

 

Please contact boardcomments@scoe.net before the meeting if you have questions about the public comment process. 

 

Accommodating Individuals with Disabilities

 

The Sacramento County Office of Education encourages those with disabilities to participate fully in the public meeting process. If you need a disability-related modification or accommodation, including auxiliary aids or services, to participate in the public meeting, contact the Superintendent’s Office at (916) 228-2410 at least 48 hours before the scheduled Board meeting so that we may make every reasonable effort to accommodate you. [Government Code § 54953.2; Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990, § 202 (42 U.S.C. § 12132).]

Department/Director: Educational Services/Brent Malicote

 

Funding Source: California Department of Education

 

Amount Requested: $144,760

 

Fiscal Year(s): 2023-2024

 

Program Description: 

Through this grant, the Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) will operate as Regional Science, Technology, Engineering. Art, and Mathematics (STEAM) Hub to provide Region 3 with technical assistance to support high-quality STEAM learning activities, skills, and strategies during the expanded learning hours; including before, after school, and during summer programs. This support includes training and assistance related to accessing local, regional, and statewide resources. These funds assist expanded learning programs that serve K-12 grade students. These funds allow the Region 3 STEAM Hub to facilitate a Community of Practice to provide high-quality training to staff that will help support the cohesion with the Next Generation Science Standards, Art, hands-on activities, project-based learning that engages the whole child. Additionally, SCOE provides direct support to the STEAM Hub participants at the site level and through regional events. The two main areas of focus for this STEAM Hub grant period are to:

  • Provide professional development and resources through the Community of Practice model, to the expanded learning programs staff and leadership with STEAM focus. 
  • Provide opportunities and resources that help to engage all expanded learning program students in high-quality STEAM activities.

BACKGROUND:

 

The Sacramento County Board of Education (Board) seats in Trustee Areas IV, V, VI, and VII are up for re-election in 2024. The cost of this Trustee election will be substantially less expensive if it is consolidated with the statewide primary election held on March 5, 2024. To consolidate its Trustee election with the statewide election, the Board must adopt and file a resolution requesting consolidation.

 

Resolution No. 23-12 calls for the election, and requests that it be consolidated with the primary election scheduled for March 5, 2024 for the following Trustee Areas and terms of office:

 

Trustee Area IV

Said Term: 4 years

(07/01/24-06/30/28)

 

Trustee Area V

Said Term: 4 years

(07/01/24-06/30/28)

 

Trustee Area VI

Said Term: 4 years

(07/01/24-06/30/28)

 

Trustee Area VII

Said Term: 4 years

(07/01/24-06/30/28)

 

Trustee Areas IV, V, and VII are in Sacramento County. Trustee Area VI is primarily in Sacramento County and extends into San Joaquin, Solano, and Yolo counties.

 

The resolution specifies other guidelines for the election. To reduce election costs, the resolution asks the County Registrar to combine the required notice of election with other special districts. It also declares that individual candidate(s) shall be responsible for and prepay the publication cost of the voluntary candidate's statement in the Sample Official Ballot Pamphlet, and it designates the number of words that may be in the candidate's statement.

 

The Board will need to determine whether the candidate's statement will be a maximum of 200 or 400 words in Sacramento County. The Sacramento County Registrar recommends a maximum of 200 words due to formatting criteria in the County Voter Information Guide.  It estimates a cost of approximately $5,050-$6,400 regardless of whether the word count is 200 or 400.

 

For Trustee Area VI, the Solano County Registrar estimates the candidate's statement cost at approximately $163.37-$213.37 for both a 200- and 400-word count. San Joaquin County Registrar estimates a cost of approximately $320 for a 200-word candidate's statement, and the cost for a 400-word statement will be billed post-election. The Yolo County Registrar estimates a cost of approximately $180 for a maximum 200-word statement.

 

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:

 

The Superintendent recommends the Board determine the maximum number of words for the voluntary candidate's statement and adopt Resolution No. 23-12 - Calling for the Election of Trustees to the Sacramento County Board of Education on March 5, 2024.

NOTICE OF PUBLIC HEARING SCHEDULED FOR TUESDAY, SEPTEMBER 19, 2023 AT 6:30 P.M. or thereafter

 

Sacramento County Office of Education

David P. Meaney Education Center

10474 Mather Boulevard

Mather, CA 95655

 

BACKGROUND:

 

A public hearing will be held by the Board during the September 19, 2023 Regular Board Meeting to receive public comment and input from the community regarding the proposed material revision to the American River Collegiate Academy Charter School petition. 

 

The Sacramento County Board of Education's (Board) Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) with the American River Collegiate Academy (ARCA) Charter School requires ARCA to seek a material revision of its charter petition when there are changes in enrollment that represent an increase or decrease of more than 25% in any grade level or 10% of total enrollment in any given year or when ARCA adds or deletes grade levels to be served in a given year or as a whole.

 

ARCA's actual enrollment has been significantly less than what was projected in the Material Revision the Board approved on August 9, 2022. Therefore instead of serving 180 students in grades TK-6, the charter school now intends to serve 130 students in grades TK-5 during its first five years of operation. Accordingly, ARCA is seeking a material revision to update its actual and projected enrollment and grade levels as specified in the attached.

 

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:

 

The Superintendent recommends that the Board adopt Resolution No. 23-13 - Approving the American River Collegiate Academy's September 19, 2023 Material Revision to the charter to update the actual and projected enrollment and grade levels to be served during the first five years of operation.
