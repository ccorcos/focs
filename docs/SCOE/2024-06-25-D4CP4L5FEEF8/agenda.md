**[[BACKGROUND:]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}**

 

[[The Every Student Succeeds Act (ESSA) requires schools receiving Title
I funds and operating a school wide program, or schools identified for
Comprehensive Support and Improvement (CSI), Targeted Support and
Improvement (TSI), or Additional Targeted Support and Improvement
(ATSI), to develop a school plan. Assembly Bill 716 revised California
Education Code sections 64001-65001 to streamline and align state and
federal planning processes and organized federal school planning
requirements in
ESSA.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[The development of the School Plan for Student Achievement (SPSA) is
required to include the
following:]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

- [[A comprehensive needs assessment (pursuant to
  ESSA)]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}
  - [[Analysis of verifiable state data, consistent with state
    priorities, including state-determined long term
    goals]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}
  - [[The plan may include local
    data]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}
- [[An identification of the process of evaluating and monitoring the

[[**BACKGROUND:  **]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[SCOE operates a diesel generator that provides power to the SCOE Data
Center in the event of an extended power outage. The generator ensures
SCOE can continue providing Internet connectivity and online services to
SCOE departments and school districts in the event of a power outage
that affects the DPMEC building. The existing generator is over 20 years
old and has reached the end of its useful lifespan. The Computer,
Network, and Telecommunication Support (CNTS) Department would like to
purchase a new generator model that can supply full power to the data
center while simultaneously providing live remote diagnostic and runtime
information regarding the generator status and power output, a feature
that is not available on the current outdated
generator.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

\
[[**DPMEC Generator Replacement**\
Project includes purchasing and installing a replacement generator. It
will include any required conduit, supply lines, cabling, parts, and
supplies to ensure the new generator is fully operational and able to
provide power to the SCOE Data Center as well as remote monitoring
capabilities.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[Architectural and engineering services will be engaged to determine
the best options for the upgrade, to develop construction and bid
documents, and to ensure construction documents are in adherence with
the laws and codes required by federal, state, local, and fire district
agencies. Staff will develop a list of qualified architectural firms
with successful school experience through a Request for Qualifications
process.  ]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[These projects will be undertaken after using a formal bid process.
Upon Board authorization, staff will advertise and seek bids. At a
future Board meeting, staff will provide a recommendation to award the
contract for the work to the lowest responsible/responsive
bidder.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

\
[[**SUPERINTENDENT\'S
RECOMMENDATION:**]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

\
[[The Superintendent recommends the Board authorize staff to conduct
business as required to replace the existing Data Center generator with
a new one, including obtaining all required federal, state, and local
approvals, hiring architects and/or other consultants as necessary, and
seeking public bids for the
work.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

[[The Sacramento County Local Child Care and Development Planning
Council (LPC) is authorized in accordance with state law. Pursuant to
Assembly Bill (AB) 131 (Chapter 116, Statues of 2021), signed by the
Governor on July 23, 2021, all statutes governing LPCs were transferred
from the Educational Code (EC) to the Welfare and Institutions Code
(WIC), and are now found in WIC Sections 10480 and 10485 through
10487.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[The purpose of the LPC is to provide a forum for the planning of
childcare services that meet the diverse needs of families in Sacramento
County, promote public participation in the planning process, advocate
for the needs of families in the County with respect to childcare, and
serve as a forum for discussion of community childcare issues. The LPC
meets four times per year; Ad hoc subcommittees meet as
needed. ]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[The LPC is comprised of 20 members with appointment duties split
between the Board of Supervisors (10 members) and the County Board of
Education (10 members). Each Board appoints two members in each of the
following
categories: ]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[**Consumer** -** **a parent or person who receives, or who has
received within the past 36 months, childcare
services.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[**Child Care Provider** -** **a person who provides childcare services
or represents persons who provide childcare
services.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[**Public Agency Representative** - a person who represents a city,
county, city and county, or local education
agency. ]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[**Community Representative** -** **a person who represents an agency
or business that provides private funding for childcare services, or who
advocates for childcare services through participation in civic or
community-based organizations but is not a childcare provider and does
not represent an agency that contracts with the State Department of
Education or the Department of Social Services to provide childcare and
development
services. ]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

trends that could impact early learning
