Public Comment

 

Anyone may appear at the Board meeting to provide public comment regarding any item on the agenda or regarding any matter that is within the Board’s subject matter jurisdiction. However, the Board may not take action on any item not on the agenda except as authorized by Government Code section 54954.2. If possible, notify the Board President or Board Secretary in writing prior to the meeting if you wish to testify.

 

Individuals wishing to speak in person before the Board are requested to complete a public comment form. Speakers will be called in the order in which the cards are received. 

 

Public comment via Zoom teleconference may be made by using the raised hand feature in Zoom during the agenda item to be addressed. The speaker will be identified by their Display Name in Zoom when called upon to speak. Those joining the meeting via Zoom are requested to display their full name so there is no confusion when called upon to speak. Public comments will be received until the public comment period has elapsed.

 

Comments are limited to a maximum of two (2) minutes per speaker per agenda item, and individuals are limited to a single comment per agenda item.

 

Please contact contactscoe@scoe.net before the meeting if you have questions about the public comment process. 

 

Accommodating Individuals with Disabilities

 

The Sacramento County Office of Education encourages those with disabilities to participate fully in the public meeting process. If you need a disability-related modification or accommodation, including auxiliary aids or services, to participate in the public meeting, contact the Superintendent’s Office at (916) 228-2410 at least 48 hours before the scheduled Board meeting so that we may make every reasonable effort to accommodate you. [Government Code § 54953.2; Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990, § 202 (42 U.S.C. § 12132).]

CLASSIFIED

 

Elke Uno, Registrar for Elinor Lincoln Hickey Jr./Sr. High School, was nominated by Vice Principal Jenn Kunkle for going above and beyond. To describe her merely as a great multi-tasker would not do justice to everything she accomplishes. Since starting in March 2017, as the registrar for the community schools and senior extension, she has been responsible for the accurate entry of all our students into PowerSchool. She also answers the phones for all our programs, helping to redirect calls of prospective students, and assisting families. Her patience with each phone call lets our families and students know that they are important; she never rushes, and she does her best to ensure that each conversation ends with a clear next step. She has the same patience with our staff as well, pausing her own work to help us whenever it is needed. We are so grateful to have Elke on our team!

 

CERTIFICATED

 

Francisco Marroquin, Teacher for SCOE’s CARE based out of Katherine Johnson Middle School in the San Jan Unified School District, was nominated by CARE Director Marc Nigel.  Francisco consistently goes above and beyond to support his students and colleagues. Francisco was hired in November 2018 and since that time, he has been a dedicated, student-centered educator who brings a positive, professional attitude to everything he does. Francisco embraces feedback as a tool for growth, showing a strong commitment to reflection and continuous improvement. In addition to his daily efforts in the classroom, Francisco has had tremendous success in his role as a CARE teacher at Katherine Johnson Middle School, where his ability to build strong, trusting relationships and provide consistent support has made a meaningful impact. His kindness, dedication, and unstoppable commitment to excellence make him a truly deserving candidate for this recognition.

Department/Cabinet Member: Educational Services/Navdeep Purewal

 

Funding Source: U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development

 

Amount Requested: $77,528

 

Fiscal Year(s): 2025-2026

 

Program Description:  

The Lutheran Social Services (LSS), in collaboration with SCOE, California State University, Sacramento, American River College, Sacramento City College, and Cosumnes River College, developed a program designed to provide prevention, diversion, and intervention services for youth aged 24 and under who are enrolled in one of the colleges and are homeless parents. This collaboratively developed program is known as the College Initiative and is funded by the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD), specifically under the Youth Homelessness Demonstration Program, who recently awarded Sacramento for the delivery of services addressing all stages of homelessness for families and their children. Services include short-term youth support in the form of financial assistance, intensive case management, housing stabilization, and supportive services for youth who qualify under HUD's definition of homelessness.

 

LSS, as the lead agency, will receive referrals from the colleges, assess their needs, and connect them to supportive services and community providers. SCOE will receive referrals for college students experiencing homelessness with children ages 0-5, and will meet the families, assess educational and/or childcare needs, connect older children to local education agency homeless liaisons, provide resource connections, and follow up with families to track engagement in education programs. 

 

Subcontracts: None

 

Evaluation Component:

SCOE will collect required data about services provided for children ages 0-5 and will input all collected data into tracking forms as required by LSS to meet the College Initiative requirements.

Department/Cabinet Member: Educational Services/Navdeep Purewal

 

Funding Source: California Department of Housing and Community Development

 

Amount Requested: $102,146

 

Fiscal Year(s): 2025-2026

 

Program Description:

SCOE will contract with Sacramento Steps Forward (SSF) to reduce barriers to education for families experiencing homelessness, as identified through Sacramento's Homeless Management Information System (HMIS). HMIS is the region's central database for prioritizing individuals and families experiencing homelessness for services within the homeless response system, also known as the Coordinated Access System (CAS). 

 

As a designated CAS assessor, SCOE will gain secure access to HMIS, enabling both data entry and visibility into client records. This role will allow SCOE to receive direct referrals from SSF and housing providers, including those identifying families as part of the newly defined subpopulation of Extremely Vulnerable Households. 

 

SCOE will also connect students and families with homeless liaisons within Sacramento's local education agencies (LEAs) to ensure educational rights are upheld and to promote enrollment and participation in education programs. Additionally, SCOE will be responsible for delivering training on the McKinney-Vento Act within the family shelter system and will actively participate in various meetings and feedback sessions throughout the CAS. 

 

Subcontracts: None

 

Evaluation Component:

SCOE will log all CAS assessor activities in HMIS and provide SSF with quarterly reports on non-HMIS activities, including training, the number of students supported and enrolled in school, and attendance and participation at stakeholder meetings.

Department/Cabinet Member: School of Education/Shanine Coats

 

Funding Source: California Department of Education

 

Amount Requested: $1,780,000  $1,766,625*

 

Fiscal Year(s): 2025-2026

 

Program Description: 

SCOE's School of Education Department serves as the 21st Century California School Leadership Regional Academy for the Placer-Sacramento Geographic Region supporting 14 counties. The   Academy provides high-quality professional development to support quality educational leadership which is offered to central office administrative leaders, school site administrative leaders, and   teacher leaders. Professional learning focuses on leadership development skills such as using data to inform decisions, understanding resources to support continuous improvement, building   teams to guide leadership, and building understanding of the impact of high-quality leadership to support continuous school improvement and equity. The underlying foundation is to support leaders’   capacity to lead for equity-driven continuous improvement.

 

SCOE's Regional Academy will continue to provide professional development by establishing five sub-regions, with a lead county office in each region, to directly support the leadership   development needs for each region. The lead county offices will assist in identifying the unique needs to support leadership development, and provide the ongoing coaching needed to assist with   the implementation of programs and practices for continuous school improvement.

 

New Positions: None

 

Subcontracts:

SCOE will enter into subcontracts, as necessary, to ensure the successful implementation of the grant. The subcontracts will support the completion of tasks outlined in the grant's scope of work   and will help fulfill all deliverables in alignment with the expectations and requirements set forth by the grantor.

 

 Evaluation Component:

 Participants will evaluate program components including, but not limited to, program delivery, level of knowledge gained, and degrees of concept implementation. School districts and county offices   will rate the effectiveness of the training and the level of continuous improvement to the region. The Regional Academy will be evaluated on an annual basis by the California Department of   Education on the effectiveness of programs provided in the region and alignment to grant requirements.

 

*The California Department of Education reduced the contract amount by $13,375 for 2025-2026.
