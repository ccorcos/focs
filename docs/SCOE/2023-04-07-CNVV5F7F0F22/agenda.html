Public Comment

 

Anyone may appear at the Board meeting to provide public comment regarding any item on the agenda or regarding any matter that is within the Board’s subject matter jurisdiction. However, the Board may not take action on any item not on the agenda except as authorized by Government Code section 54954.2. If possible, notify the Board President or Board Secretary in writing prior to the meeting if you wish to testify.

 

Individuals wishing to speak in person before the Board are requested to complete a public comment form. Speakers will be called in the order in which the cards are received. 

 

Public comment via Zoom teleconference may be made by using the raised hand feature in Zoom during the agenda item to be addressed. The speaker will be identified by their Display Name in Zoom when called upon to speak. Those joining the meeting via Zoom are requested to display their full name so there is no confusion when called upon to speak. Public comments will be received until the public comment period has elapsed.

 

Comments are limited to a maximum of two (2) minutes per speaker per agenda item, and individuals are limited to a single comment per agenda item.

 

Please contact boardcomments@scoe.net before the meeting if you have questions about the public comment process. 

 

Accommodating Individuals with Disabilities

 

The Sacramento County Office of Education encourages those with disabilities to participate fully in the public meeting process. If you need a disability-related modification or accommodation, including auxiliary aids or services, to participate in the public meeting, contact the Superintendent’s Office at (916) 228-2410 at least 48 hours before the scheduled Board meeting so that we may make every reasonable effort to accommodate you. [Government Code § 54953.2; Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990, § 202 (42 U.S.C. § 12132).]

Department/Director: School of Education/Dr. Nancy Herota

 

Funding Source: California Department of Education

 

Amount Requested: $4,005,000

 

Fiscal Year(s): 2023-2024

 

Program Description: 

The Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) School of Education serves as the 21st Century California School Leadership Regional Academy for the Placer-Sacramento Geographic Region. The Academy provides high-quality professional development to support quality educational leadership. This professional development will be provided to central office administrative leaders, school site administrative leaders, and teacher leaders. Professional learning will focus on leadership development skills such as using data to inform decisions, understanding resources to support continuous improvement, building teams to guide leadership, and building understanding of the impact of high-quality leadership to support continuous school improvement. The underlying foundation is to support leaders’ capacity to lead for equity-driven continuous improvement.

 

The SCOE Regional Academy will continue to provide professional development through establishing five sub-regions, with a lead county office in each region, to assist with directly supporting the leadership development needs for each region. The lead county offices will assist in identifying the unique needs to support leadership development, and provide the ongoing coaching needed to assist with the implementation of programs and practices for continuous school improvement.

 

New Positions:  

  • Coordinator

  • Administrative Assistant

 

Subcontracts:

  • Creative Leadership Solutions to provide professional development and coaching in the area of equity and continuous improvement.
  • Fire Horse Rising (Dr. Nancy Dome) to provide professional development in the area of equity and continuous improvement.
  • Various subcontracts with professional learning providers as identified through our program needs assessment.
  • Various inter-program contracts with Communications, Research and Evaluation, Printing Services, and the Leadership Institute.

 

Evaluation: 

Evaluation of the professional learning will be completed by participant evaluations of program components including, but not limited to, program delivery, level of knowledge gained, and degrees of concept implementation. School districts and county offices will rate the effectiveness of the training and the level of continuous improvement to the region.

 

The Regional Academy will be evaluated on an annual basis by the California Department of Education. This evaluation will include areas of level of effectiveness of program provided in the region and alignment to meeting grant requirements.

Funding Source: California Workforce Development Board

 

Amount Requested: $1,654,630

 

Fiscal Year(s): 2023-2024, 2024-2025

 

Program Description:

The High Road Training Partnerships (HRTP) are supported by the California Workforce Development Board (CWDB) in coordination with the Labor and Workforce Development Agency with various annual state fund allocations. The objectives of the HRTP initiative are to increase access to existing high-road jobs for underserved populations and create pathways for job growth for incumbent and new workers.

 

SCOE (Sacramento County Office of Education) has submitted an application for the Training and Implementation project type to deliver training programs to achieve employment, and career advancement outcomes for participants. This grant will support the expansion of the internship component of the School-Based Mental Health and Wellness Career pipeline program to add up to 60 Peer Specialists and 30 Graduate Clinical Interns over the two-year grant period, July 2023 – June 2025. These earn-and-learn internship opportunities provide paid training, support services, and professional development workshops enhancing their skills and abilities. Every intern is paired with a mentor and supported throughout their internship to continue their education and provide career guidance to apply for SCOE permanent employment opportunities or other agencies within the county.

Peer Specialists will be paid $17.50/hour for up to 500 hours during the school year and will receive Peer Specialist Certification training with support to obtain certification upon completion of a one-year internship. Graduate Clinical Interns will be paid a $7,000 or $10,000 stipend, and will receive a series of skill-based trainings in Cognitive Behavioral Intervention for Trauma in Schools. 

New Positions:  None

Subcontracts:

  • Cal Voices Wise U
  • Center for Safe & Resilient Schools and Workplaces
  • Supporting Transition Resilience in Newcomer Groups (STRONG)
  • The Sacramento Employment and Training Agency (SETA)
  • Additional or revised contracts as needed.

 

Evaluation Component: 

SCOE will submit quarterly reports to the California Workforce Development Board and a final year-end report summary of grant activities and measurable outcomes related to each program.

Department/Director: Educational Services/Brent Malicote

 

Funding Source: California Department of Education/ESSA Funds

 

Amount Requested: $248,991

 

Fiscal Year(s): 2023-2024

 

Program Description: 

Consistent with the California statewide system of support’s focus on increasing the capacity of local educational agencies (LEAs) to meet the needs of all students, the Budget Act of 2022 appropriated $5 million of the Every Student Succeeds Act (ESSA), Section 1003 funds to county offices of education. This will support development and implementation of the 2023-2024 Comprehensive Support and Improvement (CSI) plans in coordination with the statewide system of support for LEAs established in the California State Plan for the federal ESSA.

 

The Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) Planning and Improvement Department will provide plan development, implementation, technical assistance, and support to each LEA in Sacramento County that serves schools that meet the criteria for CSI. This is consistent with California’s System of Support to help LEAs, and their schools, meet the needs of each student they serve, with a focus on building local capacity to sustain improvement and to address disparities in opportunities and outcomes.

 

SCOE will use these funds to build LEA capacity to support their schools through meaningful engagement in the school improvement planning processes including technical assistance activities relating to:

  • Family and community engagement to inform and support continuous improvement.
  • Conducting needs assessments and root cause analysis of persistently low student outcomes.
  • Selecting and implementing evidence-based actions and interventions.
  • Using data to develop, implement, monitor, and evaluate the effectiveness of programs and services.
  • Creating cycles of inquiry with school leadership teams to advance equity for students with disabilities and other underserved student groups.
  • Identifying resource inequities, which may include a review of LEA and school-level budgets.

 

New Positions: None

 

Subcontracts: None

 

Evaluation Component: 

The ESSA Comprehensive Support and Improvement Plan Development Implementation and Support (CSI-D) County Office of Education budget expenditures and services provided will be reported quarterly to the California Department of Education.

Program Description: 

The Budget Act of 2022 appropriates $5 million of Every Student Succeeds Act (ESSA), Section 1003 funds for county offices of education (COEs), for the purposes of review and approval of 2023–2024 Comprehensive Support and Improvement (CSI) plans managed through the CSI prompts in the 2023–2024 local educational agency (LEA) Local Control and Accountability Plan (LCAP).

 

The Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) is required to review and approve 2023–2024 CSI plans through the CSI prompts in the 2023–2024 LEA LCAP for those LEAs within its county with schools eligible for CSI. SCOE Planning and Improvement Department will review and approve the CSI prompts in the LEA LCAP Plan Summary for each LEA in Sacramento County that serves schools that meet the criteria for CSI. In addition, SCOE will provide trainings to LEAs with CSI schools to support effective plan evaluations, comprehensive needs analyses, and effective family and community engagement to focus improvement efforts and resolve resource inequities.

Program Description: 

The Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) will be awarded the Expanded Learning Opportunities - Program Technical Assistance (ELO-P) grant to operate as the Regional Lead for Region 3 (Alpine, Colusa, El Dorado, Nevada, Placer, Sacramento, Sierra, Sutter, Yolo, and Yuba Counties) to provide technical assistance for all ELO-P grantees. This support includes training and assistance related to accessing local, regional, and statewide resources. ELO-P Technical Assistance funds also allow the Regional Lead to provide high quality training to Expanded Learning practitioners that will help provide cohesion with the CA State Standards and Next Generation Science Standards. Additionally, SCOE will provide direct technical assistance support to grantees at the LEA grant manager/program provider and site levels. The three main areas of focus for the ELO-P Technical Assistance Grant are:

  •     Provide technical assistance to all grantees and sites to support the implementation of programs that fully address all ELO-P requirements.
  •     Build capacity throughout the region they serve to develop, implement, and sustain support for effective before, after school, and                               intersession Expanded Learning programs.
  •     To invite new ELO-P grantees into systems and structures that currently exist in the region to help build their Expanded Learning capacity.

  •     Project Specialist I - Region 3 Expanded Learning Programs Site Coordinator Liaison
