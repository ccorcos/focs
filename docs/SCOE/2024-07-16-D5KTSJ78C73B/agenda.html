Public Comment

 

Anyone may appear at the Board meeting to provide public comment regarding any item on the agenda or regarding any matter that is within the Board’s subject matter jurisdiction. However, the Board may not take action on any item not on the agenda except as authorized by Government Code section 54954.2. If possible, notify the Board President or Board Secretary in writing prior to the meeting if you wish to testify.

 

Individuals wishing to speak in person before the Board are requested to complete a public comment form. Speakers will be called in the order in which the cards are received. 

 

Public comment via Zoom teleconference may be made by using the raised hand feature in Zoom during the agenda item to be addressed. The speaker will be identified by their Display Name in Zoom when called upon to speak. Those joining the meeting via Zoom are requested to display their full name so there is no confusion when called upon to speak. Public comments will be received until the public comment period has elapsed.

 

Comments are limited to a maximum of two (2) minutes per speaker per agenda item, and individuals are limited to a single comment per agenda item.

 

Please contact boardcomments@scoe.net before the meeting if you have questions about the public comment process. 

 

Accommodating Individuals with Disabilities

 

The Sacramento County Office of Education encourages those with disabilities to participate fully in the public meeting process. If you need a disability-related modification or accommodation, including auxiliary aids or services, to participate in the public meeting, contact the Superintendent’s Office at (916) 228-2410 at least 48 hours before the scheduled Board meeting so that we may make every reasonable effort to accommodate you. [Government Code § 54953.2; Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990, § 202 (42 U.S.C. § 12132).]

Program Description: 

The Sacramento County Division of Behavioral Health Services, Alcohol and Drug Services is renewing its contract with Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) to support the Friday Night Live (FNL), Club Live (CL), and Friday Night Live Mentoring (FNLM) programs. FNL and CL programs and services are available to high school and middle school students throughout Sacramento County. Program Description:

As part of the Inclusive Early Education Expansion Program, Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) will provide leadership and support for the implementation of goals related to increasing high quality inclusive early learning programs led by a consortium made up of partners from the following nine local education agencies: Arcohe Union School District, Center Joint Unified School District, Elk Grove Unified School District, Elverta Joint School District, Galt Joint Union Elementary School District, Sacramento City Unified School District, Sacramento County Office of Education, San Juan Unified School District, Robla School District, and Twin Rivers Unified School District. The consortium will also include the following three community-based state preschool providers: Beanstalk, Catalyst Kids, and Only Love Children's Centers. Through the consortium model, funding will be allocated to address local-level systems building, align practices with research and evidence-based strategies that support inclusive fiscal and programmatic education planning for successful outcomes for all children. Activities will include professional learning, adaptive equipment, and facility renovations to support consortium members with attention to inclusion, develop strategies to leverage funding for inclusive environments and strategic planning and implementation for instructional components.

  • Administrative Assistant

  • $75,000 for online professional learning/Teaching Pyramid licenses.
  • $300,000 with WestEd for training on Inclusive Practices. 
  • $79,500 with Brookes for county-wide training for the Ages and Stages Questionnaire (ASQ) and screening costs for the online system.
  • $225,000 for provider/family training to include but not limited to Be Strong Families for Training of Trainers, Strengthening Families and Brazelton and/or other specific inclusion professional development. 
  • $150,000 for expert facilitator, coaching support, and facilitation of professional development for countywide educators, administrative teams and support staff.

SCOE’s Prevention & Early Intervention Department collaborates with Safety Center, Inc., and other stakeholders, including local and state law enforcement agencies, to help middle school youth develop attitudes that support lifelong traffic safety practices, create and implement projects that promote pedestrian and bicycle safety among their peers and family members, and build a foundation for youth to make safe decisions when they become drivers. Funding Source: Sacramento County Department of Health Services

 

Amount Requested: $70,000

 

Fiscal Year(s): 2024-2025

 

Program Description: 

The Sacramento County Department of Health Services Behavioral Health Services Substance Use Prevention and Treatment Services will renew its contract with the Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) to implement and provide prevention services focused on tobacco education. SCOE will recruit, train, and engage at least 15 middle school and/or high school aged youth to enable them to effectively educate the community about tobacco control issues in target jurisdictions; facilitate youth-led coalition meetings and staff meetings to support and maintain a youth coalition each school year; conduct outreach recruitment activities for youth; participate in community events to reach target jurisdictions and/or priority populations; conduct trainings; develop coalition membership materials; maintain participation records; and conduct a youth coalition survey and provide a final report summarizing project goals, process, results and recommendations.

Department/Cabinet Member: Prevention and Early Intervention/Kristin Wright

 

Funding Source: Sacramento County Department of Health Services

 

Amount Requested: $522,500

 

Fiscal Year(s): 2024-2025

 

 Program Description: 

The Sacramento County Department of Health Services (DHS), with Mental Health Services Act (MHSA) Prevention and Early Intervention funds, will contract with the Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) to continue providing school-based bullying prevention and mental health wellness projects. In coordination with DHS, school districts, and the Student Mental Health and Wellness Collaborative, SCOE will:

  • Maintain and supplement bullying and cyberbullying prevention activities at established elementary demonstration schools
  • Conduct training and evaluation activities
  • Serve as liaison for community-based providers
  • Attend the Student Mental Health and Wellness Collaborative
  • Provide Youth Mental Health First Aid (YMHFA) trainings to districts and school staff, parents, youth-service law enforcement officers, and community-based organizational staff
  • Provide Question, Persuade, Refer (QPR) for Suicide Prevention trainings
  • Work collaboratively with designated school districts to conduct YMHFA and QPR trainings to district and/or site staff

 

New Positions: None

 

Subcontracts:

  • Agreements with the 13 school districts in Sacramento County ranging from $6,000 to $19,900 based on district student enrollment and scope of work from the Bullying Prevention Project.
  • Agreements with three selected school districts in Sacramento County ranging from $5,630 to $22,085 based on the number of trainings to be conducted for the YMHFA program.
  • Agreements with five selected school districts in Sacramento County for $3,000 each to support QPR training efforts.

 

Evaluation Component:

SCOE’s Prevention & Early Intervention Department will coordinate with SCOE’s Center for Student Assessment and Program Accountability (C-SAPA), district leads, demonstration sites, YMHFA and QPR trainers to measure program goals.

Funding Source: Educational Credit Management Corporation (A Non-Profit Company)

 

Amount Requested: $15,000

 

Fiscal Year(s): 2024-2025

 

Program Description:

This grant will be used to expand outreach efforts in Sacramento County to increase completion of the Free Application for Federal Student Aid Application (FAFSA) and to  expand the outreach and marketing of the September 3rd Cal Grant deadline for students attending a California Community College. According to the California Student Aid Committee, over 700 students in Sacramento County have started, but not completed, a FAFSA. Program staff will reach out to students and their families through phone calls, texts, and emails. Virtual and in-person workshops will be offered to help students and their families complete their FAFSA. Assistance will be provided in various languages, including Spanish and Hmong.

 

New Positions: None

 

Subcontracts: None

 

Evaluation Component:

Sacramento Cal-SOAP continues to focus on data collection and reporting on financial aid application completion and post-application work to secure aid.

Department/Cabinet Member: School of Education/Dr. Nancy Herota

 

Funding Source: California Department of Education

 

Amount Requested: $1,976,468

 

Fiscal Year(s): 2024-2025

 

Program Description: 

Sacramento County Office of Education's (SCOE) School of Education serves as the 21st Century California School Leadership Regional Academy for the Placer-Sacramento Geographic Region supporting 14 counties. The Academy provides high-quality professional development to support quality educational leadership which is provided to central office administrative leaders, school site administrative leaders, and teacher leaders. Professional learning will focus on leadership development skills such as using data to inform decisions, understanding resources to support continuous improvement, building teams to guide leadership, and building understanding of the impact of high-quality leadership to support continuous school improvement and equity. 

 

SCOE's Regional Academy will continue to provide professional development through the established five sub-regions, with a lead county office in each region supporting leadership development needs. The lead county offices will continue to assist in identifying the unique needs to support leadership development and provide the ongoing coaching needed to assist with the implementation of programs and practices for continuous school improvement.

 

New Positions:  Director 

 

Subcontracts:

  • Creative Leadership Solutions to provide professional development in the area of equity and continuous improvement.

  • Fire Horse Rising (Dr. Nancy Dome) to provide professional development in the area of equity and continuous improvement.

  • Solution Tree to provide professional development in the area of equity and continuous improvement.

  • Inform2Inspire to provide leadership training for professional learning communities.

  • National Center for Urban School Transformation to provide professional development in the area of equity and continuous improvement.

  • The Regents of the University of California, Davis, to provide professional development in the area of equity and continuous improvement, provide leadership training for professional learning communities, and provide conference center meeting space.

  • Learning RedesignED to provide professional development in the area of equity and continuous improvement and provide leadership training for professional learning communities.

  • Various subcontracts with professional learning providers as identified through our program needs assessment.

  • Various inter-program contracts with Communications, Research and Evaluation, Early Learning, the Leadership Institute, Printing Services and other departments as identified through program needs.

 

Evaluation Component: 

Participants will evaluate program components such as program delivery, level of knowledge gained, and degrees of concept implementation. School districts and county offices will rate the effectiveness of the training and the level of continuous improvement to the region. The CDE (California Department of Education) will evaluate on an annual basis on the level of effectiveness of the program provided in the region and alignment to meeting grant requirements.

BACKGROUND:

 

Per Sacramento County Board of Education Bylaw 9260 (attached), Board members may be paid for any meeting when absent if the Board, by resolution duly adopted and included in its minutes, finds that at the time of the meeting, he or she was performing services outside the meeting on behalf of the Board, he or she was ill, on jury duty, or the absence was due to a hardship deemed acceptable by the Board.

 

The attached resolution states that Trustee Harold Fong was absent on May 21, 2024 and June 11, 2024 due to bereavement leave and that the Board finds this to be an acceptable hardship.
