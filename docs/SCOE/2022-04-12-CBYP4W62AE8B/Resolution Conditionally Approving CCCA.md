SACRAMENTO COUNTY BOARD OF EDUCATION
Resolution Conditionally Approving the Capital College & Career Academy
Countywide Charter Petition
Resolution No. 22-07

WHEREAS, on February 25, 2022, the Capital College & Career Academy (Petitioner
or CCCA) submitted a petition for a countywide charter school to the Sacramento
County Board of Education (County Board) and submitted supplemental information
and documents on March 3 and 9, 2022;

WHEREAS, CCCA’s charter petition was analyzed by SCOE staff with expertise in
each of the petition areas (fiscal, educational programs, student assessments, special
education, English learners, human resources, facilities, and legal) and Sacramento
County Office of Education (SCOE) staff prepared written Recommendations and
Findings for consideration by the County Board;

WHEREAS, on March 28, 2022, SCOE’s written Recommendations and Findings
were published on SCOE’s public website and provided to CCCA and each of the
school districts in Sacramento County.

WHEREAS, on April 12, 2022, the County Board held a public hearing to consider the
level of public support for CCCA’s petition, hear testimony from CCCA, SCOE staff,
and the public, and CCCA representatives had equivalent time and procedures to
present evidence and testimony to respond to SCOE’s Recommendations and
Findings;

WHEREAS, on April 12, 2022, following review of the petition and the public hearing
noted above, the County Board considered and deliberated upon CCCA’s countywide
charter petition;

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that having reviewed CCCA’s petition,
conducted the public hearing, and considered and deliberated upon the petition, the
County Board adopts SCOE’s written Recommendations and Findings for the CCCA
countywide charter petition; and

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the County Board conditionally approves the
CCCA countywide charter petition, subject to satisfaction of the following conditions by
the deadlines identified:

    A. Before being authorized to open and operate the charter school, CCCA shall
       satisfy the following conditions through an acceptable Memorandum of
       Understanding between CCCA, the County Board, and SCOE, which shall
       include, but not be limited to the following terms:

         1. By May 1, 2023, CCCA shall provide verification that the school has parent or
            guardian enrollment commitments for the number of students equal to at least
            90% of the enrollment projected in its budget for the first year of operation.

         2. By May 1, 2023, CCCA shall provide verification that it has a beginning cash
            balance of at least $400,000 comprised of unrestricted grants and donations.

         3. By May 1, 2023, CCCA shall provide evidence that it has secured acceptable
            facilities for its main school site and sufficient learning center classrooms near
            student internship locations to serve students as proposed in its petition.

         4. CCCA shall revise its charter petition to describe a high quality CTE program
            for students that would qualify CCCA for competitive state and federal CTE
            grants to augment its attendance-based apportionment.

         5. Given the unique countywide program promised by CCCA, before opening
            and operating, CCCA shall demonstrate that it has sufficient certificated staff
            to provide the courses promised in the petition.

         6. Before opening and operating, CCCA shall be accepted as a member of a
            Special Education Local Plan Area.

    B. If any of the conditions are not met by the specific deadline for that condition,
       unless the deadline is extended by the County Board, approval of CCCA’s charter
       will be terminated and the charter will cease to operate.

    C. These conditions may be changed or supplemented by future County Board action.
