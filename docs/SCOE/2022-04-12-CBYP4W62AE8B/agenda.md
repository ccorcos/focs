[[**BACKGROUND:**]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[The Capital College & Career Academy (CCCA) submitted a new petition
for a countywide charter school on February 25, 2022 and submitted
supplemental information and documents on March 3 and 9,
2022.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[CCCA proposes a career technical education (CTE) based, STEAM-focused,
early college, countywide charter high school. It plans to open with an
initial 9th grade cohort of 80 students in August 2023 and grow to serve
375 students in grades 9-12 with the option for students to return for a
"plus one" year of support. During its first year, it intends to operate
a main school campus within the Twin Rivers Unified School District
(TRUSD) and a learning center site near anticipated internships outside
TRUSD, and to open additional learning center sites near internships
throughout the county in future
years.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[On April 12, 2022, the Sacramento County Board of Education (County
Board) will conduct a public hearing and consider CCCA's countywide
charter petition. Following review of the CCCA petition and the public
hearing, the County Board must either approve or deny the petition. If
the County Board denies the countywide charter petition, CCCA has no
right of appeal. (Ed. Code, § 47605.6(k); Cal. Code Regs., tit. 5, §
11966.5(e).)]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[**SUMMARY OF CHARTER PETITION
ANALYSIS:**]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) staff with expertise in
each relevant area have reviewed the CCCA countywide charter petition
and
concluded:]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

- [[The petition satisfies the technical signature, school district
  notification, legal affirmations, and Educational Employment Relations
  Act (EERA) declaration
  requirements.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

- [[CCCA's proposed program model meets the preliminary requirements
  that would allow it to be approved as a countywide charter
  school.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

- [[CCCA's budget will be balanced so long as it meets its enrollment
  and attendance projections, receives the donations pledged to it, and
  can sustain the \$9.5 million debt burden it plans to assume to pay
  for its main campus. Whether CCCA can successfully implement its
  proposed program will depend on its ability to remain financially
  solvent.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

- [[CCCA enrollment projections may be challenging based on declining
  enrollment across Sacramento County (e.g., since 2019-2020, enrollment
  declined 6.1% in TRUSD and 3.4% in Sacramento City Unified School
  District), and because of the CTE programs already offered in the nine
  districts operating high schools in Sacramento County (County). If
  students choose not to travel across the County to attend CCCA and its
  enrollment is 20% lower than projected, without changes in
  expenditures or other funding streams, CCCA would become insolvent
  before the end of its third year of
  operations.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

- [[CCCA's petition proposes a unique and logistically complex model of
  providing high school coursework, early college, internships, and
  other services to high school students across the County. While CCCA's
  current petition provides more detail than its previous petition, the
  logistical challenges of operating its program are not fully resolved.
  Successful implementation of CCCA's program will depend on its ability
  to address the heightened complexities of providing the array of
  promised educational programming and services to students with
  different learning levels and needs at different sites with limited
  staff, and to attract teachers with the credentials required for the
  courses and learning experiences promised in the
  petition.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

- [[[Although CTE is the central focus of the proposed charter school,
  the petition does not provide a clear enough description of a high
  quality CTE program that appears to meet the minimum eligibility
  requirements for the additional competitive state and federal CTE
  funding available to local school districts to enhance their CTE
  programs. While CCCA will be eligible for attendance-based
  apportionment funding, access to CTE enhancement funding is also
  important for enhancing and sustaining its
  program.]{style="vertical-align:top"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

- [[The petition contains reasonably comprehensive descriptions of most
  elements required by Education Code section 47605.6(b)(5)(A)-(P), with
  additional clarification needed in some areas, particularly the
  educational
  program.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[**SUPERINTENDENT\'S
RECOMMENDATION:**]{style="box-sizing: inherit; font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; margin: 0px; padding: 0px; border: 0px; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; font-weight: inherit; font-stretch: inherit; font-size: inherit; line-height: inherit;"}]{style="box-sizing: inherit; font-family: inherit; margin: 0px; padding: 0px; border: 0px; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; font-weight: inherit; font-stretch: inherit; font-size: 14px; line-height: inherit;"}

 

[[Based upon analysis of the petition and the applicable law, the County
Board may exercise its discretion to approve or deny CCCA's countywide
charter petition (see the attached resolutions for consideration).
However, if the County Board decides to approve the petition, the
Superintendent recommends that approval, opening, and operation of the
charter school be conditioned upon satisfaction of the following
conditions:]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

1.  [[CCCA shall enter an acceptable memorandum of understanding with

[[Consistent with the California statewide system of support's focus on
increasing the capacity of local educational agencies (LEAs) to meet the
needs of all students, the Budget Act of 2021 appropriated \$5 million
of the ESSA, Section 1003 funds to county offices of education (COEs)
for the purpose of supporting development and implementation of
the ]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}[[2022--2023
Comprehensive Support and Improvement (CSI) plans. These plans will be
coordinated with the statewide system of support for LEAs established in
the California State Plan for the federal
ESSA.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[The Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) Planning and
Improvement Department will provide plan development and implementation
via technical assistance and support to each LEA in Sacramento County
that serve schools that meet the CSI
criteria.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[SCOE will assist LEAs, and their schools, meet the needs of each
student they serve, with a focus on building local capacity to sustain
improvement, and to effectively address disparities in opportunities and
outcomes. SCOE will use these funds to build LEA capacity to support
schools through meaningful engagement in the school improvement planning
processes, including technical assistance activities related to the
following:]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

- [[Family and community engagement to inform and support continuous
  improvement.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}
- [[Conducting needs assessments and root cause analysis of persistently
  low student
  outcomes.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}
- [[Selecting and implementing evidence-based actions and
  interventions.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}
- [[Using data to develop, implement, monitor, and evaluate the
  effectiveness of programs and
  services.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}
- [[Cycles of inquiry with school leadership teams to advance equity for
  students with disabilities and other underserved student
  groups.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}
- [[Identify resource inequities, which may include a review of LEA and
  school-level
  budgets.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

[[The Budget Act of 2021 appropriated \$5 million of Every Student
Succeeds Act (ESSA) funds for county offices of education (COEs) for the
purposes of review and approval of 2022-2023 Comprehensive Support and
Improvement (CSI) plans through the CSI prompts in the 2022-2023 local
educational agency (LEA) Local Control and Accountability Plan
(LCAP).]{style="font-size:14px;"}]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}

 

[[The Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) Planning and
Improvement Department will review and approve the CSI prompts in the
LEA LCAP Plan Summaries for each LEA in Sacramento County that serve
schools that meet the CSI criteria. In addition, SCOE will provide
global trainings to LEAs to support effective plan evaluations,
comprehensive needs analysis, and effective family and community
engagement to focus improvement efforts and resolve resource
inequities.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[SCOE will review and approve 2022-2023 CSI plans, through the CSI
prompts, in 2022-2023 LEA LCAPs for those LEAs in its county with
schools eligible for CSI. The CSI prompt LCAP approval process was
approved by the SBE in January 2019, which authorizes COEs to assist the
SBE in meeting its statutory obligation to approve CSI Plan. Responses
to these prompts constitute summaries of the LEA's CSI plans and coupled
with COE or CDE approval, as applicable, serve as the mechanism for SBE
approval. The COE is working on behalf of the SBE to meet the ESSA
requirement to approve LEA CSI
Plans.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

[[**BACKGROUND:**]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[Assembly Bill 361 amended elements of the Brown Act (Government Code
section 54950, et seq.) to permit local agencies to continue holding
teleconferenced meetings using simplified procedures during a proclaimed
state of emergency. Under Government Code section 54953(e), in order to
continue holding teleconferenced board meetings using simplified Brown
Act procedures, local agencies, including school districts and county
boards of education, must make the following findings by majority vote
and readopt such findings every 30 days
thereafter:]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

1.  [[The board has reconsidered the circumstances of the state of
    emergency,
    AND]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}
2.  [[The state of emergency continues to directly impact the ability of
    members to meet safely in person, OR state or local officials
    continue to impose or recommend measures to promote social
    distancing.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[In the event the Board decides to continue to hold teleconferenced
Board meetings using simplified Brown Act procedures, Resolution No.
22-06 is attached for the Board\'s
consideration.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[**SUPERINTENDENT\'S
RECOMMENDATION:**]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[The Superintendent recommends that the Board consider and decide
whether to adopt Resolution No. 22-06 which would permit it to continue
holding teleconferenced Board meetings using simplified Brown Act
procedures.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}
