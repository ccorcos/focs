[[**BACKGROUND:**]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[The California Department of Education Consolidated Application Part I
allows school districts and county offices of education to apply for
available federal categorical program funding. This apportionment is
made from federal funds provided to the state. This year the Sacramento
County Office of Education (SCOE) will apply for four (4) federally
funded programs listed on the Consolidated Application for
2022-2023.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[**[Federal
Programs]{.underline}**]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

- [[Title I, Part A: Improving Basic Programs Operated by Local
  Educational Agencies - preliminary award amount is \$1,386,515 for the
  2022-2023 school
  year.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

- [[Title I, Part D, Subpart 2: Prevention and Intervention Programs for
  Children and Youth Who Are Neglected, Delinquent, or At-Risk - we
  received \$703,487 for the 2021-2022 school year. The award amount for
  the 2022-2023 school year has not been released by
  CDE.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

- [[Title II, Part A: Supporting Effective Instruction of the Elementary
  and Secondary Education Act of 1965 (ESEA), as amended by Every
  Student Succeeds Act (ESSA) (Public Law 114-95) - preliminary award
  amount is \$17,126 for the 2022-2023 school
  year.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

- [[Title IV, Part A: Student and School Support (ESSA Sec. 4101) -
  preliminary award amount is \$113,919 for the 2022-2023 school
  year.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[**[Title I
Programs]{.underline}**]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[Title I, Part A funds are used to support effective, evidence-based
educational strategies that close the achievement gap and enable
students to meet the state\'s challenging academic
standards.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[Title I, Part D provides funds to improve educational services for
formerly incarcerated youth or for neglected youth so that such youth
have the opportunity to meet the same challenging state academic
standards that all youth in the state are expected to meet.
Additionally, Title ID funds may be used to provide such youth with the
services needed to make a successful transition from incarceration to
further schooling or
employment.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[Title II, Part A program funds are apportioned to local educational
agencies (LEAs) to increase student academic achievement through
strategies focused on preparing, training, and recruiting high-quality
teachers, principals, or other school
leaders.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[Title I, Part A: Student Support and Academic Enrichment is a federal
program to increase the capacity of LEAs to meet the goals of the ESSA
by providing all students with access to a well-rounded education,
improving school conditions for student learning, and improving use of
technology in order to improve the academic achievement and digital
literacy of all
students.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}
