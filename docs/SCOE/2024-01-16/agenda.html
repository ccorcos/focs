Public Comment

 

Anyone may appear at the Board meeting to provide public comment regarding any item on the agenda or regarding any matter that is within the Board’s subject matter jurisdiction. However, the Board may not take action on any item not on the agenda except as authorized by Government Code section 54954.2.  If possible, notify the Board President or Board Secretary in writing prior to the meeting if you wish to testify.

 

Individuals wishing to speak in person before the Board are requested to complete a public comment form. Speakers will be called in the order in which the cards are received. 

 

Public comment via Zoom teleconference may be made by using the raised hand feature in Zoom during the agenda item to be addressed. The speaker will be identified by their Display Name in Zoom when called upon to speak. Those joining the meeting via Zoom are requested to display their full name so there is no confusion when called upon to speak. Public comments will be received until the public comment period has elapsed.

 

Comments are limited to a maximum of two (2) minutes per speaker per agenda item, and individuals are limited to a single comment per agenda item.

 

Please contact boardcomments@scoe.net before the meeting if you have questions about the public comment process. 

 

Accommodating Individuals with Disabilities

 

The Sacramento County Office of Education encourages those with disabilities to participate fully in the public meeting process. If you need a disability-related modification or accommodation, including auxiliary aids or services, to participate in the public meeting, contact the Superintendent’s Office at (916) 228-2410 at least 48 hours before the scheduled Board meeting so that we may make every reasonable effort to accommodate you. [Government Code § 54953.2; Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990, § 202 (42 U.S.C. § 12132).]

BACKGROUND:

 

Bill Garvey is the Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) 2024 Teacher of the Year. He is an instructor for the High School Equivalency Adult Re-Entry Program at the Sacramento Community Based Coalition (SCBC) and was nominated by Office Assistant, Essence Jeffery. Mr. Garvey is also a teacher with the Senior Extension Program and works among young adult students aged 17-21.

 

Mr. Garvey has been a SCOE employee since 2002. He has a Bachelor of Arts degree in history from California State University, Fullerton along with a Teaching Credential in Secondary Education from Chapman University and a Verification Process for Special Settings (VPSS) Education Certification from the University of California, Los Angeles. 

 

When selecting Mr. Garvey as the SCOE 2024 Teacher of the Year, the selection committee noted that he has been instrumental in helping the Re-Entry program in achieving its mission of helping students achieve their high school equivalency or GED. The SCBC program manager stated that Mr. Garvey's expertise and passion to create an educational track that is inclusive and a safe learning environment for all learners has been an asset to the department. He works with students until they complete their educational goals. 

 

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:

 

It is recommended that the Sacramento County Board of Education approve commendation of Bill Garvey, the Sacramento County Office of Education 2024 Teacher of the Year and that the Board present him with a plaque commemorating this honor.

Department/Director: Educational Services/Brent Malicote

 

Funding Source: State of California Department of Health Care Services

 

Amount Requested: $400,000,000

 

Fiscal Year(s): 2023-2024, 2024-2025

 

Program Description:

As a part of the Children and Youth Behavioral Health Initiative through the State of California Department of Health Care Services (DHCS), the Sacramento County Office of Education will administer $400 million in one-time grants to strengthen school-linked behavioral health services. SCOE will serve as the lead grant administrator and in partnership with Santa Clara County Office of Education (SCCOE) the technical assistance provider for County Offices of Education across the State. SCOE will manage grant application and follow-up, serve as fiscal intermediary, monitor and report on implementation, and deliver technical assistance. These grants will provide California public K-12 schools with resources to support institutional readiness for the statewide multi-payer school-linked fee schedule by providing resources to schools to expand its provider capacity, develop critical partnerships, and build the necessary infrastructure. This grant program is intended to provide schools with a starting point - building infrastructure, programs, and policies that can then be sustained through funding mechanisms such as the statewide, multi-payer school-linked fee schedule.

 

New Positions:  

  • Program Analyst (3 FTE)
  • Coordinator, Mental Health & Wellness (3 FTE)
  • Project Specialist I, Mental Health & Wellness (1 FTE)
  • Financial Analyst (1 FTE)
  • Staff Secretary (1 FTE)
  • Director, Health and Wellness, Strategic Initiatives (1FTE)

 

Subcontracts: 

  • SCCOE in the amount of $9,980,772 to provide evaluation and assessment, technical assistance to COEs and support in grant application and follow-up.
  • Glen Price Group (GPG) in the amount of $600,000 to provide advisory services and operational support.
  • Tim Taylor in the amount of $150,000 to provide consulting and technical assistance around small county and district support.
  • Deveau Burr Group, LLC in the amount of $300,000 to provide consulting and technical assistance around charter school support.

 

Evaluation Component: 

SCOE is sub-contracting with SCCOE to provide internal evaluation and assessment of the grant program. SCOE will submit semi-annual fiscal activity reports, annual narrative progress reports that include a description of accomplishments, challenges, identified resources, effective practices, and next steps to be developed, and annual program reports including data on the implementation of the goals and activities described in the proposed plan.

Department/Director: Administration/Dr. Nancy Herota

 

Funding Source: California Department of Education

 

Amount Requested: $100,000

 

Fiscal Year(s): 2023-2024, 2024-2025

 

Program Description: 

Under the direction of the State Board of Education (SBE), the Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) will receive $100,000 from the California Department of Education (CDE) to update the 2021 California Digital Learning Integration and Standards Guidance (DLISG) on mathematics to be in alignment with the SBE-adopted Mathematics Framework for California Public Schools: Kindergarten Through Grade Twelve (Mathematics Framework).

 

SCOE will fulfill five project tasks: (1) Research existing support practices and draft a 2024 DLISG project outline and budget; (2) Review and assess the current (2021) DLISG and identify inconsistencies, or insufficient guidance requiring revision or removal to align with the guidance in the approved Mathematics Framework; (3) Prepare draft amendments to Section B: Standards Guidance for Mathematics of the DLISG that will be reviewed by the SBE; (4) Present proposed amendments to the SBE; and (5) Edit, prepare, and submit the final accessible version of the SBE-adopted DLISG.

 

New Positions: None

Evaluation Component: None

BACKGROUND:

 

E-Rate is a federally funded program that provides discounts to assist schools with obtaining affordable broadband connectivity (network services) and network-related equipment. The CNTS Department applies for E-Rate discounts each year to reduce the cost of eligible services and equipment. Upon Board approval, the CNTS Department will award contracts for the services and equipment below if E-Rate discounts are provided for the 2024-2025 fiscal year. SCOE is under no obligation to purchase the services or equipment if SCOE's E-Rate applications are not funded. It is anticipated that SCOE will be eligible for a 53% discount on an Uninterruptible Power Supply (UPS) battery backup system for the data center and an 80% discount on wireless equipment.

 

As per the E-Rate guidelines, SCOE requested proposals in November 2023 for the following items:

 

  1. Uninterruptible Power Supply (UPS) for Data Center with extended warranty
  2. Wireless equipment and support

 

All RFPs were posted to SCOE's E-Rate website for over 28 days and advertised in the local newspaper twice so that any potential vendors could compete. Below are the vendors that were selected based on a scoring matrix that places the highest emphasis on the lowest total cost:
