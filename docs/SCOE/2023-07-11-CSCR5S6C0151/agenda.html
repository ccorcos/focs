Public Comment

 

Anyone may appear at the Board meeting to provide public comment regarding any item on the agenda or regarding any matter that is within the Board’s subject matter jurisdiction. However, the Board may not take action on any item not on the agenda except as authorized by Government Code section 54954.2.  If possible, notify the Board President or Board Secretary in writing prior to the meeting if you wish to testify.

 

Individuals wishing to speak in person before the Board are requested to complete a public comment form. Speakers will be called in the order in which the cards are received. 

 

Public comment via Zoom teleconference may be made by using the raised hand feature in Zoom during the agenda item to be addressed. The speaker will be identified by their Display Name in Zoom when called upon to speak. Those joining the meeting via Zoom are requested to display their full name so there is no confusion when called upon to speak. Public comments will be received until the public comment period has elapsed.

 

Comments are limited to a maximum of two (2) minutes per speaker per agenda item, and individuals are limited to a single comment per agenda item.

 

Please contact boardcomments@scoe.net before the meeting if you have questions about the public comment process. 

 

Accommodating Individuals with Disabilities

 

The Sacramento County Office of Education encourages those with disabilities to participate fully in the public meeting process. If you need a disability-related modification or accommodation, including auxiliary aids or services, to participate in the public meeting, contact the Superintendent’s Office at (916) 228-2410 at least 48 hours before the scheduled Board meeting so that we may make every reasonable effort to accommodate you. [Government Code § 54953.2; Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990, § 202 (42 U.S.C. § 12132).]

BACKGROUND:

 

Education Code sections 17545 and 17546 allow for the disposal of property no longer needed or that is determined unsuitable for school use.

 

The Computer, Network and Telecommunication Support Department (CNTS) reviews all technology surplus equipment to ensure that any repairable, non-obsolete equipment is re-used by SCOE programs. In the event the equipment cannot be repaired, is no longer able to support the latest security patches and updates (posing a network security risk) or is so obsolete it can no longer be used for its intended purpose, the equipment is deemed unsuitable for use and is recommended for disposal. 

 

The technology equipment listed below has been determined to be unsuitable for use and of insufficient value to defray the costs of arranging a sale. It is in the Sacramento County of Education’s best interest to deem these items obsolete and dispose of them. All storage devices (hard drives, solid state drives, USB drives, etc.) are completely wiped of data and, whenever possible, physically shredded to ensure the destruction of all electronic data before disposal.

Program Description: 

First 5 IMPACT is designed to improve the quality of private and public early learning centers, family child care homes, and alternative settings; including family, friends, and neighbors. First 5 IMPACT is aligned with Quality Counts California (QCC) and supports the implementation of the California Quality Rating and Improvement System (QRIS) Framework and early learning quality improvement efforts in Sacramento County through Raising Quality Together (ROT), Sacramento County's QRIS. SCOE staff will support, improve, and assess program quality, strengthen partnerships, and monitor program goals. Funds will be used to support and engage providers with quality improvement activities and resources including incentive funds to assist non-state-funded programs with quality improvement goals identified in Quality Improvement Plans that are aligned with the core elements of the California QRIS Framework. Child Action, Inc. will conduct outreach, technical assistance, ongoing support, and assessments and coordinate with SCOE. 

 

Evaluation Component: 

SCOE prepares an annual report that summarizes the participation and ratings, if applicable, of early learning programs who are part of RQT. Tier ratings are based on the Quality Continuum Framework Rating Matrix in the areas of Child Development and School Readiness, Teachers and Teaching, and Program and Evaluation. SCOE will submit quarterly expenditure reports to First 5 California.

Department/Director: Educational Services/Brent Malicote

 

Funding Source: Sacramento County Department of Health

 

Amount Requested: $522,500

 

Fiscal Year(s): 2023 - 2024

 

Program Description: 

The Sacramento County Department of Health Services (DHS), with Mental Health Services Act (MHSA) Prevention and Early Intervention funds, will contract with the Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) to continue providing school-based bullying prevention and mental health wellness projects. In coordination with DHS, school districts, and the Student Mental Health and Wellness Collaborative, SCOE will:

  • Maintain and supplement bullying and cyberbullying prevention activities at established elementary demonstration schools.
  • Conduct training and and evaluation activities.
  • Serve as liaison for community-based providers.
  • Attend the Student Mental Health and Wellness Collaborative. 
  • Provide Youth Mental Health First Aid (YMHFA) trainings to districts and school staff, parents, youth-serving law enforcement officers, and community-based organizational staff. 
  • Provide Question, Persuade, Refer (QPR) for Suicide Prevention trainings.
  • Work collaboratively with designated school districts to conduct YMHFA and QPR trainings to district and/or site staff. 

 

New Positions:  None

 

Subcontracts:

  • Agreements with each of the thirteen school districts in Sacramento County ranging from $6,000 to $19,900 based on district student enrollment and scope of work from the Bullying Prevention Project.
  • Agreements with three selected school districts in Sacramento County ranging from $5,630 to $22,085 based on the number of trainings to be conducted for the YMHFA program.
  • Agreements with five selected school districts in Sacramento County for $3,000 each to support QPR training efforts. 

 

Evaluation Component: 

SCOE will coordinate with SCOE’s Center for Student Assessment and Program Accountability, district leads, demonstration sites, YMHFA and QPR trainers to measure the following goals: 

 

Bullying Prevention Program

  • Increase awareness among school staff of the negative effects of bullying.
  • Increase ability by school personnel to learn specific techniques to intervene early.
  • Increase shared collaboration among school districts, SCOE, Behavioral Health Services (BHS), community partners and family members.
  • Develop best practices and policies regarding bullying and other educational efforts as part of a comprehensive school improvement and safety plan.
  • Improve students' perception of school safety.

 

Youth Mental Health First Aid and QPR

  • Increase participant recognition of the signs that a young person may be dealing with a mental health crisis or addiction challenge or crisis. 
  • Increase participant ability to reach out to a young person who may be dealing with a mental health crisis or addiction challenge or crisis. 
  • Increase participant awareness of biased views and feeling about mental health problems and disorders.
  • Increase participant knowledge of mental health supports and treatment resources.

BACKGROUND:

 

On May 16, 2023, the Sacramento County Board of Education (Board) received a report on the pre-opening status of the Capital College and Career Academy Countywide Charter (CCCA). The report indicated that while CCCA had made excellent progress in many areas, CCCA had failed to meet its May 2023 enrollment goal of 72 students, and that several areas of concern had occurred after the review of the charter budget. As a result, the Board approved a motion to allow CCCA to proceed with its efforts to open the charter school in 2023-2024, subject to the following conditions:

  1. By July 30, 2023, CCCA shall submit its confirmed enrollment, along with an updated budget and 3-year cash flow projections to the Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) for approval.
  2. CCCA’s budget and revenues shall be based upon confirmed revenues and donations and shall include supporting documents or other information requested by SCOE.
  3. The Board shall approve CCCA’s revised budget before the charter school may open and operate for the 2023-2024 school year.

 

Sue Stickel, who provides the oversight and monitoring of CCCA, has continued to meet with CCCA. CCCA has complied with the submission of its confirmed enrollment, an updated budget, and 3-year cash flow projections to SCOE. CCCA submitted these documents on June 14, 2023 because the school is scheduled to open at the beginning of August 2023. SCOE has analyzed the budget and cash flow projections.

As of June 23, 2023, 72 students were enrolled at CCCA for the 2023-2024 school year. The 72 students are 90% of the 80 students that the CCCA budget is based on. The demographics of the enrolled students are as follows:

SCOE completed its fiscal analysis on June 22, 2023. The newly revised CCCA budget projects a budget surplus of $107,357.00 and an ending fund balance of $530,572.00 for 2023-2024. SCOE staff are also concerned about CCCA’s enrollment. While its enrollment has grown to 72 students, CCCA has not met the target of the 80 students that its budget is based on. SCOE also continues to be concerned that CCCA’s budget includes considerable risk primarily related to its fundraising revenue projections and that CCCA has few options other than increased borrowing to accommodate any unforeseen costs or revenue losses if the fundraising revenues do not materialize. SCOE’s complete fiscal analysis is provided in Attachment 1.

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:

The Superintendent recommends that the County Board approve CCCA's revised budget on the condition that CCCA takes further action to limit its reliance on fundraising and borrowing to support ongoing operational expenses.
