[[**BACKGROUND:**]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[Education Code sections 17545 and 17546 allow for the disposal of
property no longer needed or that is determined unsuitable for school
use.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[The Computer, Network and Telecommunication Support Department (CNTS)
reviews all technology surplus equipment to ensure that any repairable,
non-obsolete equipment is re-used by SCOE programs. In the event the
equipment cannot be repaired, is no longer able to support the latest
security patches and updates (posing a network security risk) or is so
obsolete it can no longer be used for its intended purpose, the
equipment is deemed unsuitable for use and is recommended for
disposal. ]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[The technology equipment listed below has been determined to be
unsuitable for use and of insufficient value to defray the costs of
arranging a sale. It is in the Sacramento County of Education's best
interest to deem these items obsolete and dispose of them. All storage
devices (hard drives, solid state drives, USB drives, etc.) are
completely wiped of data and, whenever possible, physically shredded to
ensure the destruction of all electronic data before
disposal.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

[[The Seeds of Partnership contract is intended to assist the California
Department of Education (CDE), Special Education Division (SED) in
supporting the State Performance Plan (SPP) and Annual Performance
Report (APR), required under the Individuals with Disabilities Education
Act (IDEA) through statewide coordinated technical assistance related to
Family Outcomes, Preschool, Parent Involvement, and to the Family
Empowerment and Disability Council
(FEDC).]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[The project will accomplish this through the
following:]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

- [[Gathering meaningful and specific feedback through surveys and
  interviews from families of children with disabilities and
  collaborating with agencies who serve them to inform the CDE, Local
  Educational Agencies (LEA), and Special Education Local Plan Areas
  (SELPAs) on family perceptions and experiences related to the
  education of their
  children.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}
- [[Researching, identifying, and gathering information related to
  family engagement and Preschool Least Restrictive Environment (LRE)
  directly from LEAs about their current practices and areas of need,
  and collaborating with agencies who serve
  them.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}
- [[Providing the CDE with guidance and expertise to increase the
  quality and quantity of information collected from families of
  children with disabilities to assist the CDE in monitoring and
  statewide activities related to family engagement.
  ]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}
- [[Creating, establishing, maintaining, and updating the SEEDS web
  site, and enhancing technical assistance materials to provide guidance
  to the CDE, LEAs, and SELPAs to support family engagement between the
  LEAs and families and promote evidence-based and recommended practices
  for Preschool
  LRE.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}
- [[Facilitating the coordination of youth leadership activities and
  opportunities.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}
- [[Providing statewide support and coordinated technical assistance to
  the
  FEDC.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

[[SCOE completed its fiscal analysis on June 22,^ ^2023. The newly
revised CCCA budget projects a budget surplus of \$107,357.00 and an
ending fund balance of \$530,572.00 for 2023-2024. SCOE staff are also
concerned about CCCA's enrollment. While its enrollment has grown to 72
students, CCCA has not met the target of the 80 students that its budget
is based on. SCOE also continues to be concerned that CCCA's budget
includes considerable risk primarily related to its fundraising revenue
projections and that CCCA has few options other than increased borrowing
to accommodate any unforeseen costs or revenue losses if the fundraising
revenues do not materialize. SCOE's complete fiscal analysis is provided
in Attachment
1.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}
