Public Comment

 

Anyone may appear at the Board meeting to provide public comment regarding any item on the agenda or regarding any matter that is within the Board’s subject matter jurisdiction. However, the Board may not take action on any item not on the agenda except as authorized by Government Code section 54954.2.  If possible, notify the Board President or Board Secretary in writing prior to the meeting if you wish to testify.

 

Individuals wishing to speak in person before the Board are requested to complete a public comment form. Speakers will be called in the order in which the cards are received. 

 

Public comment via Zoom teleconference may be made by using the raised hand feature in Zoom during the agenda item to be addressed. The speaker will be identified by their Display Name in Zoom when called upon to speak. Those joining the meeting via Zoom are requested to display their full name so there is no confusion when called upon to speak. Public comments will be received until the public comment period has elapsed.

 

Comments are limited to a maximum of two (2) minutes per speaker per agenda item, and individuals are limited to a single comment per agenda item.

 

Please contact boardcomments@scoe.net before the meeting if you have questions about the public comment process. 

 

Accommodating Individuals with Disabilities

 

The Sacramento County Office of Education encourages those with disabilities to participate fully in the public meeting process. If you need a disability-related modification or accommodation, including auxiliary aids or services, to participate in the public meeting, contact the Superintendent’s Office at (916) 228-2410 at least 48 hours before the scheduled Board meeting so that we may make every reasonable effort to accommodate you. [Government Code § 54953.2; Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990, § 202 (42 U.S.C. § 12132).]

CLASSIFIED 

 

Lanitra O’Dell, Program Analyst/Early Learning, was nominated by Early Learning (EL) Executive Director Julie Montali, Ph.D., and Prevention and Early Intervention (PEI) Executive Director Kristin Wright. Lanitra O’Dell is a dedicated member of the EL team with a focus on managing multiple complex budgets and contracts including workforce stipends. With her attention to detail, Lanitra quickly and accurately completes all tasks, often related to critical financial matters. She is an integral part of the office culture, helping to set a tone of connectivity, support, and positivity as leader of the EL team’s social and wellness committee. When the PEI Department faced a need for staffing due to unforeseen circumstances, Lanitra kept contracts and purchase orders moving along. She continues to go above and beyond in support of both the EL and PEI departments. Lanitra O’Dell has been a SCOE employee since October 2014.

 

CERTIFICATED

 

Jayme Massey, Alternative Education Teacher/Folsom Middle School, was nominated by Director Marc Nigel for her contributions to SCOE’s CARE Program. Ms. Massey teaches Math, English, and a curriculum support class for 20 students in eighth grade. She is phenomenal when it comes to building relationships with students. Ms. Massey makes sure she knows every student’s story and passion in life to help motivate them in the classroom. Her classroom walls are rich with student work and engaging bulletin boards. Ms. Massey takes the time to work with each family, helping them navigate the school process each day. She is engaged with Folsom Middle School staff and runs the "Club Live" program at the school. Additionally, Ms. Massey has provided professional development around curriculum support for the school staff. Jayme Massey has been a SCOE employee since August 2018.

BACKGROUND:

 

Education Code sections 17545 and 17546 allow for the disposal of property no longer needed or that is determined unsuitable for school use. 

 

The Computer, Network, and Telecommunication Support Department (CNTS) reviews all technology surplus equipment to ensure that any repairable, non-obsolete equipment is re-used by SCOE programs. In the event the equipment cannot be repaired, is no longer able to support the latest security patches and updates (posing a network security risk), or is so obsolete it can no longer be used for its intended purpose, the equipment is deemed unsuitable for use and is recommended for disposal. 

 

The technology equipment listed below has been determined to be unsuitable for use and of insufficient value to defray the costs of arranging a sale. It is in the Sacramento County of Education’s best interest to deem these items obsolete and dispose of them. All storage devices (hard drives, solid state drives, USB drives, etc.) are completely wiped of data and, whenever possible, physically shredded to ensure the destruction of all electronic data before disposal.

  

SUPERINTENDENT’S RECOMMENDATION:

 

The Superintendent recommends that the Board declare this equipment unsuitable for school use and of insufficient value to defray the costs of arranging a sale, and authorizes the Procurement Department to dispose of this equipment as authorized under Education Code sections 17545 and 17546.

BACKGROUND:

 

Attached are proposed revisions to Board Policy 5142 - Authorization to Administer Medication and for Parent Administration of Medicinal Cannabis. All proposed revisions are indicated by strikeouts and bold underlined additions.

 

The proposed revisions update the policy to reflect current law and practices regarding the availability and administration of medication, including epinephrine auto-injectors (EpiPens) and naloxone hydrochloride (Narcan) nasal spray.

 

The Policy Committee reviewed Board Policy 5142 - Authorization to Administer Medication and for Parent Administration of Medicinal Cannabis on November 15, 2022, and recommended that the revised policy be presented to the Board for First Reading. The First Reading of the proposed revisions to the Policy occurred at the December 13, 2022 Board meeting.

 

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:

 

The Superintendent recommends that the Board of Education hear the Second Reading and adopt the proposed revisions to Board Policy 5142 - Authorization to Administer Medication and for Parent Administration of Medicinal Cannabis.
