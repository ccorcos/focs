SACRAMENTO COUNTY BOARD OF EDUCATION
RESOLUTION TO RECOGNIZE AND CELEBRATE THE ASSETS AND STRENGTHS
OF BLACK STUDENTS AND TO SUPPORT AN IMPROVED EDUCATION SYSTEM
THAT BETTER SERVES BLACK CHILDREN AND YOUTH

RESOLUTION NO. 25-07

WHEREAS, Black students bring extraordinary intellect, talents, creativity, resilience,
and diverse cultural contributions that enrich schools and communities; and

WHEREAS, high academic expectations for all students, coupled with culturally
affirming, sustaining, and supportive learning environments, are essential for
educational equity and success; and

WHEREAS, the Sacramento County Board of Education recognizes the alarming
disparities in educational outcomes for Black students as highlighted in the California
Assessment of Student Performance and Progress (CAASPP) scores and the California
State Dashboard; and

WHEREAS, Black students in Sacramento County are suspended at more than twice
the rate of all students in the county, highlighting systemic issues that disproportionately
impact their educational experiences; and

WHEREAS, Black students have both the lowest graduation rates and the highest
absenteeism rates across the districts within Sacramento County which further
exacerbates educational inequities and the likelihood of student success; and

WHEREAS, Black students in Sacramento County are over-represented in high-need
student groups, including foster youth, homeless students, justice-involved and students
with disabilities; and

WHEREAS, a landscape analysis completed in 2025 provided critical insights into the
barriers and systemic conditions impacting Black student achievement and offered
evidence-based recommendations for improving educational equity and access; and

WHEREAS, this Board encourages the implementation of the recommendations of the
landscape analysis in order to close the opportunity and learning gap and improve
outcomes for all learners in districts and countywide; and

WHEREAS, creating an improved education system that prioritizes equity, inclusion,
and cultural responsiveness benefits all students and strengthens the broader
community; and

WHEREAS, fostering safe and inclusive school environments free from discrimination,
harassment, and inequitable disciplinary practices is essential for the well-being and
success of Black students and all students; and

WHEREAS, families and caregivers play a vital role in the academic and social
development of children, and partnerships between schools, families, and communities
are critical partners for fostering positive outcomes; and

WHEREAS, centering and elevating the voices of Black students and families in
shaping educational policies and practices fosters a more inclusive and responsive
education system; and

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Sacramento County Board of
Education will work with district and community leaders to prioritize the success of Black
students in the following ways:

   1. Acknowledge and Celebrate the Strengths of Black Students
      Recognizes the brilliance, creativity, and resilience of Black students and their
      invaluable contributions to our schools and communities.
   2. Uplift Black Student Leadership
      Engages Black students in shaping educational policies, practices, and
      initiatives, ensuring their perspectives and needs are integral to building an
      inclusive and responsive education system.
   3. Support Targeted Interventions
      Support evidence-based interventions tailored to the unique needs of Black
      students, including community mentorship programs, academic support, and
      culturally relevant curricula and teaching pedagogy that reflect their experiences
      and histories, and that will benefit all students.
   4. Ensure Measurable Goals
      Support to Local Education Agencies (LEAs) to establish measurable goals and
      timelines for improving CAASPP scores and graduation rates and reducing
      suspension and absenteeism rates for Black students.
   5. Engage Educational Partners and Youth
      Foster partnerships with community organizations, families, and partners to
      develop initiatives and interventions aimed at supporting Black students and
      addressing the specific challenges they face in education. Engage student voice

      success.
   6. Seek Equitable Allocation of Resources
      Provide support to LEAs and schools to specifically ensure Black student
      success.
   7. Ensure SCOE Leadership and Resource Commitment
      Apply these principles in SCOE schools and coordinate alignment with other
      SCOE initiatives and ensure the effectiveness of this work.
   8. Commit to Continuous Improvement
      Encourages the use of data to identify and address disparities, ensuring
      accountability for progress in creating equitable educational opportunities for
      Black students.

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Sacramento County Board of Education will
identify and advocate for policy changes at the local, state, and federal levels that
address the systemic inequities faced by Black students, striving to create a more
inclusive and equitable educational system for all.

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Sacramento County Board of Education will
encourage and promote policy changes within the 13 districts in our county to address
systemic inequities faced by Black students and foster a more inclusive and equitable
educational system for all.
