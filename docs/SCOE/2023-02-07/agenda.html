Public Comment

 

Anyone may appear at the Board meeting to provide public comment regarding any item on the agenda or regarding any matter that is within the Board’s subject matter jurisdiction. However, the Board may not take action on any item not on the agenda except as authorized by Government Code section 54954.2.  If possible, notify the Board President or Board Secretary in writing prior to the meeting if you wish to testify.

 

Individuals wishing to speak in person before the Board are requested to complete a public comment form. Speakers will be called in the order in which the cards are received. 

 

Public comment via Zoom teleconference may be made by using the raised hand feature in Zoom during the agenda item to be addressed. The speaker will be identified by their Display Name in Zoom when called upon to speak. Those joining the meeting via Zoom are requested to display their full name so there is no confusion when called upon to speak. Public comments will be received until the public comment period has elapsed.

 

Comments are limited to a maximum of two (2) minutes per speaker per agenda item, and individuals are limited to a single comment per agenda item.

 

Please contact boardcomments@scoe.net before the meeting if you have questions about the public comment process. 

 

Accommodating Individuals with Disabilities

 

The Sacramento County Office of Education encourages those with disabilities to participate fully in the public meeting process. If you need a disability-related modification or accommodation, including auxiliary aids or services, to participate in the public meeting, contact the Superintendent’s Office at (916) 228-2410 at least 48 hours before the scheduled Board meeting so that we may make every reasonable effort to accommodate you. [Government Code § 54953.2; Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990, § 202 (42 U.S.C. § 12132).]

CLASSIFIED 

 

Sis Hendrix, Administrative Assistant/Court and Community Schools, was nominated by Director Marc Nigel for her contributions to the Sacramento County Office of Education. Ms. Hendrix supports SCOE's Court Schools, Community Schools, and CARE (Community Action for Responsive Education) programs in all areas of operation, including providing clerical aid, scheduling, budget entry and monitoring, and communications. She has an amazing positive attitude and always has a smile on her face. Ms. Hendrix sees the big picture as well as the intricate details of her team's work and is an excellent problem solver. Her efficiency and reliability have allowed more SCOE students to be served. Sis Hendrix has been a SCOE employee since March 2021. 

 

CERTIFICATED

 

Edith West, Vision Specialist, was nominated by Area Principal Guy Holman for her contributions to the Special Education Department. As a teacher of the visually impaired, Ms. West works diligently to ensure students have the resources and support needed to engage in their classroom activities and to access their curriculum. She readily shares her knowledge and experience with classroom teams and families in a professional and friendly manner. Through the IEP process, Ms. West is thorough in her report writing and in helping families understand their child's vision diagnosis, as well as how it will impact their learning, while also suggesting supports and strategies for mediating the challenges and empowering the student. Ms. West goes the extra mile in every aspect of her position and has been an invaluable addition to the SCOE Vision Team. Edith West has been a SCOE employee since August 2021.

Department/Director: Educational Services/Brent Malicote

 

Funding Source: California Department of Education 

 

Amount Requested: $148,121

 

Fiscal Year(s): 2022-2023, 2023-2024

 

Program Description: 

The California Department of Education (CDE) developed a comprehensive plan to provide technical assistance for continuous improvement to Local Education Agencies (LEAs). The Title III County Office of Education Regional English Learner (EL) Specialists Agreement grant is funded through the Elementary and Secondary Education Act (ESEA). As part of the California Statewide System of Support, the CDE awarded grants to one county within each of the state's 11 California County Superintendents regions. The Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) was selected as the Regional English Learner EL/Title III Specialist for Region 3. 

 

As the Region 3 EL Specialist, SCOE will provide technical assistance for continuous improvement to equip LEAs and COEs with resources and tools to support the development, implementation, and evaluation of strategies to ensure that English Learners have the resources they need to succeed. The scope of work includes guidance and support to LEAs and COEs in implementing and improving programs and developing their Local Control and Accountability Plan (LCAP) Addenda. In addition, the SCOE's Curriculum Specialist for English Learner Programs and Accountability will organize and provide coaching for systemic improvement in data analysis and data-based decision-making, high-quality instruction, connect resources for specialized programs, and facilitate quarterly Multilingual Education Leadership Network meetings to support LEAs. 

 

New Positions: None

 

Subcontracts: None

 

Evaluation Component: 

The Title III County Office of Education Regional English Learner Specialist will report on services provided. Data for these annual reports includes qualitative data that includes survey results based on the support provided to LEAs. Quantitative data is captured on the number of consultations provided to LEAs annually.

BACKGROUND:

 

E-Rate is a federally funded program that provides discounts to assist schools with obtaining affordable broadband connectivity (network services) and network equipment. The CNTS Department applies for E-Rate discounts each year to reduce the cost of providing broadband connectivity to our school sites. Upon Board approval, the CNTS Department will award contracts for the services and equipment below if E-Rate discounts are provided for the 2023-2024 fiscal year. SCOE is under no obligation to purchase the services or equipment if SCOE's E-Rate applications are not funded. It is anticipated that SCOE will be eligible for an 80% discount on these services.

 

As per the E-Rate guidelines, SCOE requested quotes in November 2022 for the following items:

 

  1. Replacement batteries for battery backup systems located at 8 SCOE school sites.
  2. Leased dark fiber service to provide broadband connectivity to 5 SCOE sites.

 

All quote requests were posted to SCOE's E-Rate website for over 28 days and advertised in the local newspaper twice so that any potential vendors could compete. Below are the vendors that were selected based on a scoring matrix that places the highest emphasis on the lowest total cost:
