[[**Teagan Hackbarth**, Transition Specialist with Court and Community
Schools, was nominated by Barbara Modlin, Principal at El Centro Jr./Sr.
High. Teagan\'s commitment to meeting the needs of our students makes a
huge impact on their successful transitions when they leave us. Teagan
genuinely cares about each student she interacts with, ensuring that
their diverse needs are met. Her creative and innovative approach to
problem-solving engages students and inspires them to envision their
future possibilities. One of Teagan\'s standout contributions is her
role in helping organize our Annual Career Fair, an event that allows
students to explore various career paths. Additionally, her leadership
in the Budget Cents program through SAFE Credit Union empowers students
with financial literacy skills and helps prepare them for independent
living. Another important contribution is Teagan\'s work connecting
students with the Department of Rehabilitation so they have access to
paid job training in areas such as barbering, truck driving, machine
operations, or medical assisting when they are released. Teagan
consistently demonstrates a remarkable work ethic and a heartfelt
dedication to our students\' success. She makes a positive impact on our
school community, and is truly deserving of this recognition. Teagan
Hackbarth has been a SCOE Employee since June
2022. ]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[**[CERTIFICATED]{.underline}**]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[**Joe Linehan**, Program Specialist of Assistive Technology with
Special Education, was nominated by Michael Kast, Assistant
Superintendent of Student Programs. Joe\'s ability to work with
classroom staff and ensure they are well trained in using the equipment
is what truly makes him stand out. His passion for improving the
classroom experience through technology is contagious, and it allows our
classrooms and staff to continue adapting along with the needs of our
students. Recently, Joe took on the enormous task of implementing Smart
Boards in every Special Education classroom. This required communication
with outside vendors and other SCOE departments, as well as trainings
for each classroom site to ensure all staff felt comfortable using the
equipment.  The feedback from staff who attended these trainings was
overwhelmingly positive and many could not wait to start using this new
resource with their students. Joe\'s patience, enthusiasm, and
commitment to bettering the school experience for our students are what
make him a vital part of our Special Education team. Joe Linehan has
been a SCOE employee since August
2016. ]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}
