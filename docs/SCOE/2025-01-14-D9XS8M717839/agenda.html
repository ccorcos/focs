Public Comment

 

Anyone may appear at the Board meeting to provide public comment regarding any item on the agenda or regarding any matter that is within the Board’s subject matter jurisdiction. However, the Board may not take action on any item not on the agenda except as authorized by Government Code section 54954.2. If possible, notify the Board President or Board Secretary in writing prior to the meeting if you wish to testify.

 

Individuals wishing to speak in person before the Board are requested to complete a public comment form. Speakers will be called in the order in which the cards are received. 

 

Public comment via Zoom teleconference may be made by using the raised hand feature in Zoom during the agenda item to be addressed. The speaker will be identified by their Display Name in Zoom when called upon to speak. Those joining the meeting via Zoom are requested to display their full name so there is no confusion when called upon to speak. Public comments will be received until the public comment period has elapsed.

 

Comments are limited to a maximum of two (2) minutes per speaker per agenda item, and individuals are limited to a single comment per agenda item.

 

Please contact boardcomments@scoe.net before the meeting if you have questions about the public comment process. 

 

Accommodating Individuals with Disabilities

 

The Sacramento County Office of Education encourages those with disabilities to participate fully in the public meeting process. If you need a disability-related modification or accommodation, including auxiliary aids or services, to participate in the public meeting, contact the Superintendent’s Office at (916) 228-2410 at least 48 hours before the scheduled Board meeting so that we may make every reasonable effort to accommodate you. [Government Code § 54953.2; Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990, § 202 (42 U.S.C. § 12132).]

CLASSIFIED 

 

Teagan Hackbarth, Transition Specialist with Court and Community Schools, was nominated by Barbara Modlin, Principal at El Centro Jr./Sr. High. Teagan's commitment to meeting the needs of our students makes a huge impact on their successful transitions when they leave us. Teagan genuinely cares about each student she interacts with, ensuring that their diverse needs are met. Her creative and innovative approach to problem-solving engages students and inspires them to envision their future possibilities. One of Teagan's standout contributions is her role in helping organize our Annual Career Fair, an event that allows students to explore various career paths. Additionally, her leadership in the Budget Cents program through SAFE Credit Union empowers students with financial literacy skills and helps prepare them for independent living. Another important contribution is Teagan's work connecting students with the Department of Rehabilitation so they have access to paid job training in areas such as barbering, truck driving, machine operations, or medical assisting when they are released. Teagan consistently demonstrates a remarkable work ethic and a heartfelt dedication to our students' success. She makes a positive impact on our school community, and is truly deserving of this recognition. Teagan Hackbarth has been a SCOE Employee since June 2022. 

 

CERTIFICATED

 

Joe Linehan, Program Specialist of Assistive Technology with Special Education, was nominated by Michael Kast, Assistant Superintendent of Student Programs. Joe's ability to work with classroom staff and ensure they are well trained in using the equipment is what truly makes him stand out. His passion for improving the classroom experience through technology is contagious, and it allows our classrooms and staff to continue adapting along with the needs of our students. Recently, Joe took on the enormous task of implementing Smart Boards in every Special Education classroom. This required communication with outside vendors and other SCOE departments, as well as trainings for each classroom site to ensure all staff felt comfortable using the equipment.  The feedback from staff who attended these trainings was overwhelmingly positive and many could not wait to start using this new resource with their students. Joe's patience, enthusiasm, and commitment to bettering the school experience for our students are what make him a vital part of our Special Education team. Joe Linehan has been a SCOE employee since August 2016. 

 

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION: 

 

The Superintendent recommends that the Board approve commendation of the individuals named as Sacramento County Office of Education Classified and Certificated Employees of the Month for February 2025 and that the Board present Certificates of Recognition to these employees.

Funding Source: California Workforce Development Board

 

Amount Requested: $3,000,000

 

Fiscal Year(s): 2025-2026, 2026-2027

 

Program Description: 

As part of the High Road Training Partnership - Health Care (HRTP-HC) Initiative, SCOE has submitted an application for the Training Implementation project to deliver training programs aimed at achieving employment and career advancement outcomes for participants. This grant will support the internship component of the School-Based Mental Health and Wellness Career Pipeline program for up to 20 Peer Specialists and 40 Graduate Clinical Interns over the two-year grant period. These earn-and-learn internship opportunities provide paid training, support services, and professional development workshops to enhance participants' skills and abilities. Every intern is paired with a mentor and supported throughout their internship to continue their education and receive career guidance, with the goal of applying for SCOE permanent employment opportunities or positions at other agencies within the county.

These funds will also support the expansion of SCOE's Career Pipeline summer workforce development program for up to 260 high school students over the two-year grant period. Students enrolled in this program will be exposed to peer mentoring, mental health, careers in education and health/mental health, resume writing, and interview skills, and will be connected to job placement and referrals through the Sacramento Employment and Training Agency (SETA). 

HRTP-HC funding will help increase the number of diverse and representative mental and behavioral health professionals employed in the county and connect aspiring and upward-bound practitioners to a broader network of employers in Sacramento County.

Department/Cabinet Member: Prevention and Early Intervention/Kristin Wright

 

Funding Source: California Department of Education/Special Education Division

 

Amount Requested: $1,000,000

 

Fiscal Year(s): 2024-2025

 

Program Description: 

SCOE will partner with Think College and Los Angeles County Office of Education, who lead the California Center for Inclusive College (CCIC) work, to expand inclusive college programs (ICP) for students with intellectual and developmental disabilities (IDD). The CCIC will develop and curate resources, create meaningful opportunities for ICPs to network and collaborate with each other and PK–12 partners, and convene an advisory workgroup of ICP leaders to share distinct program features and learnings for statewide benefit. Additionally, the CCIC will intentionally elevate the voices of youth with IDD through a Youth Ambassadors Program while working with partners through California’s State System of Support, including Supporting Inclusive Practices, SEEDS of Partnership, High-Quality IEPs, the California Transition Alliance, and others. The CCIC will support a collective goal of promoting a culture of high expectations and presumed competence for every student. CCIC will also partner with the California Departments of Developmental Services, Rehabilitation, and Education to ensure systems alignment and equitable opportunities for all students to attend ICPs.

 

New Positions:

  • Program Analyst

Evaluation Component: 

Think Data will be collected at each participating California ICP and will measure accessibility and equity, while also being comparable to other publicly available data sources on the same topics.Student exit outcomes will be collected and made available in a student-level report. Participating ICP data results will inform recommendations to expand evolving best practices and will be shared annually via the public CCIC website. The statewide data will be publicly available via the CCIC website portal and via the Think College data dashboard.

BACKGROUND:

 

The Association of California County Boards of Education (ACCBE) established a non-profit public benefit organization in October 2024 for the purpose of providing a strong independent voice for California county boards of education to advocate for the needs of the students and communities they serve. This new group is currently in formation and is soliciting all county offices of education to consider joining the orgainzation.

  

If the Sacramento Board of Education decides to become a member of ACCBE, its 2025 membership dues will be $5,584. These ACCBE dues are based on 2023-2024 dues for the former California County Boards of Education organization (CCBE), which was a subdivision of the California School Boards Association (CSBA) for many years.

 

Attached are ACCBE's adopted Bylaws (Attachment 1), a description of the ACCBE from ACCBE's website (Attachment 2), and a proposed Board Resolution approving membership in ACCBE (Attachment 3) for the Board's consideration.
