SACRAMENTO COUNTY BOARD OF EDUCATION
Resolution Approving Membership in the Association of California County
Boards of Education
Resolution No. 25-01

WHEREAS, county boards of education were established by the California Constitution,
and as such are constitutional offices. The 58 county boards have played an integral
role in education since their creation in 1860.

WHEREAS, in addition to the appellate role that they play, county boards of education
have led important, educational reform, promoted innovative measures for county
offices of education, insured fiscal integrity of county offices, and promoted policy
changes to benefit California’s students, including the most vulnerable, at promise,
children.

WHEREAS, public education in California can be strengthened and supported when
county board of education members have a strong independent voice to advocate for the
needs of students, and communities they serve. County offices and boards of education
play uniquely vital roles, impacting schools and students across California. Their
responsibilities encompass supporting students and families facing significant
challenges and needing a statewide representative voice, including those in programs
for students with disabilities, early childhood education, foster and homeless youth, and
court and community schools.

WHEREAS, joining and supporting the Association of California County Boards of
Education (ACCBE) strengthens our ability to advocate for the needs of county offices of
education and provides additional support for public education statewide. ACCBE was
established as a statewide organization to bring a unified, strong voice representing the
unique interests of county boards of education on issues impacting county offices of
education and to provide a vehicle to advocate for the needs of students we serve,
ensuring these needs are not overlooked in the legislative process. As a dedicated
advocate for students, this organization will join other educational organizations to
champion public schools and adequate funding.

WHEREAS, the primary objectives and purposes of ACCBE are to improve public
education by supporting to advocate the work of California county boards of education
and their members and advocating on their behalf.

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, that the Sacramento County Board of
Education will join the membership of the Association of California County Boards of
Education.

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, the Sacramento County Board of Education will
encourage its Board members to support the objectives and purposes of the
Association of California County Boards of Education.
