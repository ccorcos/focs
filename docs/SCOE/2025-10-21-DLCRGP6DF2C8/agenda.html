Public Comment

 

Anyone may appear at the Board meeting to provide public comment regarding any item on the agenda or regarding any matter that is within the Board’s subject matter jurisdiction. However, the Board may not take action on any item not on the agenda except as authorized by Government Code section 54954.2. If possible, notify the Board President or Board Secretary in writing prior to the meeting if you wish to testify.

 

Individuals wishing to speak in person before the Board are requested to complete a public comment form. Speakers will be called in the order in which the cards are received. 

 

Public comment via Zoom teleconference may be made by using the raised hand feature in Zoom during the agenda item to be addressed. The speaker will be identified by their Display Name in Zoom when called upon to speak. Those joining the meeting via Zoom are requested to display their full name so there is no confusion when called upon to speak. Public comments will be received until the public comment period has elapsed.

 

Comments are limited to a maximum of two (2) minutes per speaker per agenda item, and individuals are limited to a single comment per agenda item.

 

Please contact contactscoe@scoe.net before the meeting if you have questions about the public comment process. 

 

Accommodating Individuals with Disabilities

 

The Sacramento County Office of Education encourages those with disabilities to participate fully in the public meeting process. If you need a disability-related modification or accommodation, including auxiliary aids or services, to participate in the public meeting, contact the Superintendent’s Office at (916) 228-2410 at least 48 hours before the scheduled Board meeting so that we may make every reasonable effort to accommodate you. [Government Code § 54953.2; Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990, § 202 (42 U.S.C. § 12132).]

BACKGROUND:

 

Jessie Storrs is the Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) 2026 Teacher of the Year. She is a Teacher Librarian at El Centro Jr./Sr. High School. Known by students as “Ms. Storries,” Jessie has made consistent and meaningful contributions to El Centro, particularly in the areas of Educational Technology and Social Emotional Learning. She helps create a learning environment that is inclusive, innovative, and student-centered. Growing up in East Sacramento, attending Sacramento High School, and learning with limited resources in Title 1 schools, Jessie knew she wanted to be a teacher who provides those resources and a space, especially for young people who often go unheard or overlooked. Jessie wanted to be what these teachers were to her. Nationally, Jessie has been honored as a School Librarian of the Year Finalist by School Library Journal and has used that platform to uplift the voices of incarcerated youth and the educators who serve them. Most recently, she has consulted with various facilities and California libraries as they plan to expand libraries in Juvenile Justice spaces — an opportunity to pay forward what she’s learned. Jessie takes time and effort to reach students at El Centro using data, student interest, prior knowledge, background, and culture to affirm her students and to provide pathways for student achievement. She is the epitome of a life-long learner and a true champion for all students. Jessie is making a difference in her students’ lives that will sustain them for years to come.

 

SUPERINTENDENT’S RECOMMENDATION:

 

It is recommended that the Sacramento County Board of Education recognize Jessie Storrs, the Sacramento County Office of Education 2026 Teacher of the Year, and that the Board present her with a plaque commemorating this honor.

BACKGROUND:

 

Education Code sections 17545 and 17546 allow for the disposal of property no longer needed or that is determined unsuitable for school use. 

 

The Computer, Network, and Telecommunication Support Department (CNTS) reviews all technology surplus equipment to ensure that any repairable, non-obsolete equipment is re-used by  SCOE programs. In the event the equipment cannot be repaired, is no longer able to support the latest security patches and updates (posing a network security risk) or is so obsolete it can no  longer be used for its intended purpose, the equipment is deemed unsuitable for use and is recommended for disposal. 

 

The technology equipment listed below has been determined to be unsuitable for use and of insufficient value to defray the costs of arranging a sale. It is in the Sacramento County of  Education’s best interest to deem these items obsolete and dispose of them.  All storage devices (hard drives, solid state drives, USB drives, etc.) are completely wiped of data and, whenever   possible, physically shredded to ensure the destruction of all electronic data before disposal.

Program Description:

This funding will support SCOE in advancing the development of high-quality Career Technical Education (CTE) programs for court and community school students, fostering pathways to

future college and career success. CTEIG funding will help expand SCOE CTE programs by:

 

  • Including additional alternative leadership and work-based learning opportunities for students throughout the course curricula
  • Securing dual enrollment agreements for construction and horticulture programs
  • Increasing business/industry member engagement
  • Researching and including additional stackable credentials/certifications for SCOE students
  • Improving data collection systems and applying continuous improvement principles to all CTE pathways

Subcontracts:

SCOE will enter into subcontracts, as necessary, to ensure the successful implementation of the grant. The subcontracts will support the completion of tasks outlined in the grant's scope of work and will help fulfill all deliverables in alignment with the expectations and requirements set forth by the grantor.

BACKGROUND:

 

A public hearing will be held by the Board during the October 21, 2025 Board meeting to receive public comment and input from the community in establishing the appropriations limit for each fiscal year.

 

Proposition 4 of 1979 and Proposition 111 of 1990, limit the growth in appropriations made by the State of California, school agencies, and local governments. Under these propositions, the County Board of Education is required to adopt a resolution establishing the appropriations limit for each fiscal year and to provide public notice of the action. Notice to the public has been given by making available the attached resolution and appropriations limit calculations in the reception area of the Sacramento County Office of Education. 

 

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:

 

The Superintendent recommends the Board adopt Resolution No. 25-15 Establishing the 2025-2026 Appropriations Limit.
