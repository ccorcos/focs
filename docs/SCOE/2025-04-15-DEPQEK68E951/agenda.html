Public Comment

 

Anyone may appear at the Board meeting to provide public comment regarding any item on the agenda or regarding any matter that is within the Board’s subject matter jurisdiction. However, the Board may not take action on any item not on the agenda except as authorized by Government Code section 54954.2. If possible, notify the Board President or Board Secretary in writing prior to the meeting if you wish to testify.

 

Individuals wishing to speak in person before the Board are requested to complete a public comment form. Speakers will be called in the order in which the cards are received. 

 

Public comment via Zoom teleconference may be made by using the raised hand feature in Zoom during the agenda item to be addressed. The speaker will be identified by their Display Name in Zoom when called upon to speak. Those joining the meeting via Zoom are requested to display their full name so there is no confusion when called upon to speak. Public comments will be received until the public comment period has elapsed.

 

Comments are limited to a maximum of two (2) minutes per speaker per agenda item, and individuals are limited to a single comment per agenda item.

 

Please contact boardcomments@scoe.net before the meeting if you have questions about the public comment process. 

 

Accommodating Individuals with Disabilities

 

The Sacramento County Office of Education encourages those with disabilities to participate fully in the public meeting process. If you need a disability-related modification or accommodation, including auxiliary aids or services, to participate in the public meeting, contact the Superintendent’s Office at (916) 228-2410 at least 48 hours before the scheduled Board meeting so that we may make every reasonable effort to accommodate you. [Government Code § 54953.2; Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990, § 202 (42 U.S.C. § 12132).]

BACKGROUND:

 

Heather Morehouse-Jack is the Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) 2025 Teacher of the Year. She is a Deaf and Hard of Hearing Specialist and has worked in the Infant Development Program at SCOE since 2011. Ms. Morehouse-Jack’s role is critical as she helps provide specialized instruction and support to children with hearing impairments.

 

Growing up, Ms. Morehouse-Jack experienced both challenges and triumphs as she navigated the hearing-focused world around her. By learning American Sign Language and working to find successful deaf adults to serve as examples of determination and acceptance, her mother unknowingly drew up a blueprint for a career as a trusted advocate for children and their families.

 

Ms. Morehouse-Jack and the Infant Development team provide vital resources and support to families with young children (birth to three years old) who are identified as deaf or hard of hearing. She collaborates closely with families during home visits to identify strengths and opportunities for their children’s development. She also understands the fundamental importance of early language acquisition and is dedicated to ensuring the children in her program have the best possible start in life.

 

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:

 

It is recommended that the Sacramento County Board of Education recognize Heather Morehouse-Jack, the Sacramento County Office of Education 2025 Teacher of the Year and that the Board present her with a plaque commemorating this honor.

A. The Implementation Partnership, Phase I: Planning Grant for Mathematics - Dr. Channa Pitt
Meeting
Apr 15, 2025 - Sacramento County Board of Education Meeting Agenda - 6:30 p.m.
Category
9. New Business - Authorization to Submit Grant Applications/Service Contracts and Accept Funding if Awarded; and Approval of Contracts, Positions, and Other Expenditures Associated with the Grants as Outlined in the Proposed Budget – David W. Gordon
Type

Recommended Action
Motion to Approve.

Department/Cabinet Member: Educational Services/Dr. Channa Pitt

 

Funding Source: Walton Investment Foundation

 

Amount Requested: $130,000 

 

Fiscal Year: 2024-2025

 

Program Description:

This project aims to strengthen professional learning support for educators across Sacramento County, equipping them with the knowledge, resources, and strategies to foster meaningful math experiences that build student confidence, problem-solving skills, and deep conceptual understanding. Through professional learning and coaching, educators will create inclusive environments that inspire curiosity, perseverance, and a lifelong appreciation for mathematics. To achieve this, SCOE and PowerMyLearning (PML) will develop a plan to dentify key shifts in instructional practice and establish data points to evaluate professional learning effectiveness. To develop the plan, we will collect and review relevant policies, regulations, and data on math instruction and professional development, and analyze this information to map current practices and establish a baseline for instructional improvement. Within 90 days, we will finalize process mapping and develop a high-impact professional learning and implementation plan for Sacramento County that is aligned with the California Math Framework. The goal of this grant is to create a comprehensive three-year strategy, ensuring sustainable professional learning support that directly enhances student outcomes. 

 

New Positions: None

 

Subcontracts: None

 

Evaluation Component:

Data collection efforts will focus on gathering relevant documents, policies, and longitudinal summative math data to support the development of a three-year implementation plan for Sacramento County.

Per Sacramento County Board of Education Bylaw 9260 (attached), Board members may be paid for any meeting when absent if the Board, by resolution duly adopted and included in its minutes, finds that at the time of the meeting, he or she was performing services outside the meeting on behalf of the Board, he or she was ill, on jury duty, or the absence was due to a hardship deemed acceptable by the Board.
