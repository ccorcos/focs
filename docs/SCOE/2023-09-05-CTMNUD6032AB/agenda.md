**[[BACKGROUND:]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}**

 

[[The Sacramento County Board of Education will hold a public hearing
during the September 5, 2023 Regular Board Meeting to determine whether
each pupil in SCOE\'s community and special education schools has
sufficient textbooks or instructional materials, or both, in
English/language arts, including the English language development
component of an adopted program, mathematics, history/social science,
and science that are aligned to the state content standards adopted by
the State Board of Education (SBE). As part of this determination, the
Board also must determine if each pupil who is actually enrolled in a
foreign language or health course has sufficient instructional materials
that are consistent with the content and cycles of curriculum frameworks
adopted by the SBE. In addition, the County Board must determine if
pupils enrolled in a laboratory science course have adequate equipment.
Detail substantiating that sufficient instructional materials are
available to each student will be available for inspection by the County
Board and public at the
hearing.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[In accordance with the sufficiency requirements of Education Code
Section 60119, every pupil in community and special education schools
will have sufficient textbooks or instructional materials, or both, in
English/language arts, including the English language development
component of an adopted program, mathematics, science and history/social
science that are aligned to the state content standards adopted by the
SBE. Pupils enrolled in a health course will have sufficient materials.
SCOE does not offer foreign language instruction in any grades, nor does
SCOE offer science laboratory courses in any of grades 9 through 12.
Therefore, SCOE need not maintain science laboratory equipment.
Completion of a science laboratory course is not a requirement for high
school graduation, though completion of a course in either foreign
language or visual or performing arts is a requirement (EC 51225.3). As
necessary for completion of this graduation requirement, SCOE students
are provided a course in fine arts.
]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

[[**BACKGROUND:  **]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[Several network infrastructure modernization projects are scheduled
for the 2023-2024 through 2024-2025 school years.  These projects
include replacing/upgrading outdated campus fiber optic cabling,
installing new Category 6 cabling in classrooms to support wireless
access points and computer equipment, installing SCOE-provided wireless
access points, replacing outdoor speakers to support campus
announcements, installing new intermediate distribution frame (IDF)
cabinets, installing supporting electrical outlets, and providing all
conduits and related materials required to support the installation of
the network
cabling.  ]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[The sites are as
follows:]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

\
[[**Leo A. Palmiter Jr./Sr. High School, Elinor Lincoln Hickey, and
North Area Community Schools**\
Project will include replacement/upgrade of campus fiber optic cable,
outdoor speakers, and the installation of Category 6 cabling in
classrooms to support SCOE-provided wireless access
points.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[**El Centro Jr./Sr. High
School**]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

[[Project will include replacement/upgrade of campus fiber optic cable,
the installation of Category 6 cabling to support SCOE-provided wireless
access points in classrooms and designated areas, the installation of
Category 6 cabling to support connectivity in the library and other
designated locations, and the installation of supporting electrical
circuits, as required.\
\
**Sly Park Outdoor Education Center**\
Project will include replacement/upgrade of campus fiber optic cable,
the installation of fiber optic cable to the multipurpose room and
cabins, the installation of Category 6 cabling to support SCOE-provided
wireless access points, the creation of new IDFs in the multipurpose
room and cabins to support network connectivity at these locations, and
the installation of supporting electrical circuits, as
required.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[Architectural and engineering services will be needed to assist in
determining best options for the required upgrades, to develop
construction documents, and to ensure construction documents are in
adherence with the laws and codes required by federal, state, local, and
fire district agencies. Staff have developed a list of qualified
architectural firms with successful school experience through a Request
for Qualifications
process.  ]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

 

[[These projects will be undertaken after using a formal bid process.
Upon Board authorization, staff will advertise and seek bids. At a
future Board meeting, staff will provide a recommendation to award the
contract for the work to the lowest responsible/responsive
bidder.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}

and local approvals, hiring architects, and seeking public bids for the
work.]{style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"}]{style="font-size:14px;"}
