BACKGROUND:

 

The governing board of the Natomas Unified School District (NUSD) has adopted and submitted a proposal to change the method of electing NUSD governing board members. (Ed. Code, § 5019(a), (c).) Currently, the five NUSD board members are elected under an "at-large” system, where each trustee is elected by the registered voters of the entire school district. NUSD proposes, effective for the November 2022 and 2024 election cycles, to change its election method so one board member residing within each trustee area will be elected by the registered voters residing in that trustee area (“by-trustee area” elections).

NUSD board members are elected in even-numbered years and serve staggered, four-year terms. The next election for two NUSD board members is scheduled for November 2022, and the election for the remaining three board members is scheduled for November 2024. NUSD proposes to sequence its elections so that two trustee areas are assigned for election in 2022 and three trustee areas are assigned for election in 2024.

By law, the Sacramento County Committee on School District Organization (County Committee) must call and conduct a hearing on NUSD’s proposal and, at the conclusion of its hearing, approve or disapprove NUSD’s proposal. (Ed. Code, § 5019(c)(2).)

PUBLIC HEARING PROCESS:

NUSD’s proposal and public website and other publicly available information indicate the following history, public hearings, outreach, and other events related to NUSD’s proposed change of election method:

  • January 20, 2021 – NUSD adopted Resolution No. 21-03 of intent to initiate transition to a by-trustee area election system to be implemented with the November 2022 and 2024 governing board election cycles. At that time, NUSD expressed its intention to develop its trustee area boundaries based on the most updated federal decennial census data following its release in 2021. 
  • July 14, 2021 – NUSD launched a survey to the Natomas community to gather initial input on the proposed transition.
  • July 21, 2021 – NUSD presented a timeline for transition to by-trustee-area elections.  
  • July 26, 2021 – NUSD launched a second community survey to gather input about the possibility of NUSD seeking a waiver of the election requirement for implementing the by-trustee-area elections from the State Board of Education.
  • August 4, 2021 and September 1, 2021 (continued to September 14, 2021) – NUSD held public hearings to consider community input and comment regarding the potential composition of trustee areas prior to draft maps being drawn.
  • August 9, 2021 – NUSD launched a dedicated webpage and a dedicated email address specific to the transition to by-trustee area elections, both of which were widely communicated through various platforms.
  • August 5, 2021 to August 31, 2021 – NUSD met with stakeholders, including the California School Employees Association, Natomas Teachers Association, District English Learner Advisory Committee ("DELAC"), Community Advisory Council ("CAC"), Superintendent's Parent Advisory Council, Natomas Chamber of Commerce, and the Natomas Schools Foundation.
  • September 23, 2021 – NUSD launched a third community survey to gather additional input prior to the map hearings.
  • October 13, 2021 – NUSD presented information on stakeholder engagement and provided an update on census data at a regular Board meeting.
  • Week of October 18, 2021 – NUSD published population density maps.
  • October 20, 2021 – NUSD published five draft trustee-area map scenarios (scenarios A-E) for public review and input.
  • October 27, 2021 – NUSD held public hearing to gather public input on the proposed trustee-area map scenarios and sequence of elections.
  • November 1, 2021 – NUSD published sixth draft trustee-area map scenario (scenario F) for public review and input.
  • November 4, 2021 – NUSD launched a fourth community survey to gather input on six draft map scenarios (maps A–F), available in English, Spanish, and Punjabi.
  • NUSD communicated that its draft map scenarios were available for viewing through multiple platforms, including: announcements on the main NUSD webpage and a dedicated webpage for the transition to by-trustee area elections, direct emails and phone calls home to families, community newsletters, and making the content available in multiple languages.
  • November 8, 2021 – NUSD held public hearing during a special board meeting focused on the by-trustee-area transition, reviewed the six draft map scenarios (maps A-F), and directed staff to consider Map Scenarios C, E, and F for final consideration at NUSD's regular meeting on November 17, 2021.
  • November 17, 2021 – NUSD held public hearing and adopted Resolution No. 21-44 proposing to transition to “by-trustee area” elections using the NUSD trustee area boundaries map and sequence of elections identified as Map Scenario F.
  • January 11, 2022 – County Committee to hear and consider NUSD’s proposal to change to “by-trustee area” elections, adopt a trustee areas map, and establish the sequence of elections.

(See Attachments 1, 2, and 3.) Additional information regarding the NUSD process, along with copies of the alternative trustee area maps, an interactive trustee area map viewer, NUSD resolutions and presentations are posted on the NUSD website at https://natomasunified.org/board-of-trustees/transition-to-by-trustee-area-elections/.

 

COUNTY COMMITTEE ROLE:

 

The County Committee must call and conduct a hearing and, at its conclusion, either approve or disapprove NUSD’s proposal. (Ed. Code, § 5019(c)(2).)

NUSD Resolution No. 21-44 (Attachment 1) requests County Committee approval of NUSD's proposal to:

  1. Adopt by-trustee area elections, implementing NUSD’s trustee area boundaries map identified as Map Scenario F.
  2. Assign the sequence of elections for NUSD’s five trustee areas as follows:
    1. Trustee Areas 1 and 4 scheduled for election in 2022; and
    2. Trustee Areas 2, 3, and 5 scheduled for election in 2024.

The relevant statutes provide no guidelines or standards by which the County Committee must review a school district’s proposal. Therefore, the County Committee has discretion to consider NUSD’s proposal in light of the local area concerned. Absent specific standards, government entities often give deference to the decisions of other governmental entities with more localized knowledge of the issue at hand, unless the local decision was arbitrary, capricious, or entirely lacking in evidentiary support. Thus, if the record reflects rational reasons for NUSD’s decision, the reasons are supported by evidence, and the decision is not in violation of the law, the County Committee may properly approve NUSD’s proposal.

In the event that the County Committee approves NUSD’s proposal after the public hearing, a proposed resolution (County Committee Resolution No. CC-22-01) is included for the County Committee’s consideration. If the County Committee denies NUSD’s proposal, a resolution is not needed. (See Attachment 4.)

Approval by County Committee

As of January 1, 2022, school districts may transition to by-trustee-area elections upon approval by the County Committee, without voter approval and without a State Board of Education waiver of the election requirement so long as the County Committee's Resolution includes a declaration that the change in election method is in furtherance of the purposes of the California Voting Rights Act of 2001 (CVRA). (Ed. Code, § 5020 (a)(2).) NUSD's proposal and supporting materials demonstrate that NUSD initiated its change to by-trustee-area elections in furtherance of the CVRA. Therefore, if the County Committee approves NUSD’s proposal to change to “by-trustee area” elections and adopts Resolution No. CC-22-01, NUSD will change to by-trustee area elections beginning with the next NUSD governing board election on November 8, 2022. 

 

Denial by County Committee

If the County Committee denies NUSD’s current proposal, NUSD could conduct further public hearing(s) to consider and adopt an alternative plan and submit a revised proposal or take no further action and maintain the status quo.

 

SUPERINTENDENT'S RECOMMENDATION:

 

The Sacramento County Superintendent of Schools recommends that the Sacramento County Committee on School District Organization hold a public hearing and approve or deny NUSD’s proposal.
