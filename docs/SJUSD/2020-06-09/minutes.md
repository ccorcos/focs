Deputy Superintendent of Schools and Student Support Melissa Bassanelli explained that, in compliance with California
Governor Gavin Newsom’s recently issued Executive Order N-56-20, a COVID-19 operations written report is required in

Bassanelli provided a COVID-19 operations update which included information related to the following key areas: changes
to program offerings, meeting the needs of targeted populations, delivering high-quality distance learning, providing school
meals and supervision of students. Ms. Bassanelli also shared the results of the district family survey and discussed some
of the strategic priorities related to planning for the next school year.

Public Hearing: Adoption of 2020-2021 Budget (I-4)
Deputy Superintendent Kent Stephens presented the proposed 2020-2021 budget and the revisions to the 2019-2020 budget.
Mr. Stephens noted that, due to the current economic situation and many unknown allocations and expenses, there is not
sufficient information to prepare an accurate budget at this time, and a revised budget will likely be presented in the fall.
Mr. Stephens first reviewed the current-year budget revisions, including some of the major revenue and expense
adjustments, stating that the current projection for 2019-2020 is a $4 million deficit. Mr. Stephens then reviewed the
proposed 2020-2021 budget, sharing that the district will receive about $10.7 million in phase 2 of the federal stimulus
legislation, which will be formally budgeted in a later budget revision, and that a scale of revenue scenarios ranging from
a -2% Cost of Living Adjustment (COLA) to a -10% COLA reduction from current-year funding is being presented. Mr.
Stephens also noted that Governor Newsom has an additional $4.4 billion in one-time stimulus funds available for
distribution, which is still being contemplated by state officials. Mr. Stephens explained, currently the projection for 2020-
2021 is a $24 million deficit.

Ms. Villescaz declared the presentation of the 2020-2021 budget a public hearing and invited the public to speak.

Visitor Comment: [via Zoom]
Gabreal inquired about the increase in medical insurance premiums, which Mr. Stephens explained is a projected figure
provided by consultants.

There being no further comments or questions from the public, Ms. Villescaz declared the public hearing closed and invited
board members to speak. Staff answered questions from the board related to the spending down of reserves, allowable
FEMA reimbursements, appropriations limits, budgeted amounts for law enforcement, student enrollment and average daily
attendance (ADA).

It was moved by Dr. McKibbin, seconded by Mr. Hernandez, to adopt the 2020-2021 budget, including staffing standards,
the use of the Education Protection Account funds, the substantiation of excess reserves (required by Senate Bill 858), and
to approve the revisions to the 2019-2020 budget. MOTION CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY [Villescaz, McKibbin, Creason,
Costa, Hernandez].
