Katherine Johnson Middle School Transition Update (H-1)
Assistant Superintendent of Secondary Education and Programs Kristan Schnepp, Chief Operations Officer Frank Camarda,
Director of Communication Raj Rai, and Principal Damon Smith gave a presentation on the collaborative steps taken to
support the midyear move of Katherine Johnson Middle School campus to its new location at 2641 Kent Drive, Sacramento.
Ms. Schnepp spoke about key milestones including the groundbreaking, the announcement of construction delays and the
campus opening. Mr. Camarda expressed thanks to the community and shared information about the project, which resulted
in a new, dedicated middle school facility that honors Katherine Johnson’s legacy. Ms. Rai discussed the collaborative team
created to support communication and outreach with students and families, staff and future neighbors, focusing on the
timing and the logistics of the move, communication strategies, and the impacts a midyear move might have. Ms. Rai
explained that the team engaged students and families through surveys, translated materials, and weekly updates, along
with regular outreach and communication with nearby neighbors. Ms. Schnepp spoke about the districtwide collaborative
efforts to support staff during the midyear move, which included support from technology services and facilities. Mr. Smith
shared information about community partnerships, noting the contributions of JP Aerospace, the Aerospace Museum of
California, the Assistance League and Sacramento Area Bicycle Advocates. Mr. Smith also discussed next steps, which
include monitoring academic and attendance data, gathering community feedback, and refining logistics and procedures
based on post-move experiences. Board members made comments and posed questions, which staff addressed. Ms. Creason
expressed pride in separating the campus from Encina High School, the partnerships with other community leaders and the
energy at the ribbon cutting ceremony, and she inquired whether Katherine Johnson’s family has been notified of this honor.
Ms. Costa spoke about the pride of families and students, the bicycle partnership and the communication plan. Ms.
Kravchuk acknowledged the campus design, the communication efforts and the community partnerships, and she also
inquired about feedback from neighbors. Mr. Avey inquired about attendance and the easement related to the nature area,
and he complimented staff on their engagement with the community and neighbors.

Maintenance and Operations Update (H-2)
Chief Operations Officer Frank Camarda introduced Director of Maintenance and Operations Colin Bross who gave a
presentation regarding the work and structure of the Maintenance and Operations Department. Mr. Bross shared the
department’s mission and spoke about staffing, skilled trades, the dispatch center, custodial staffing and the number of
properties supported. Mr. Bross also discussed the new digital work order system, sharing processes and statistics. Lastly,
Mr. Bross spoke about recent and planned deferred maintenance projects, capital improvement projects and self-help
projects. Board members made comments and posed questions, which Mr. Bross and Mr. Camarda answered. Mr. Bloise
expressed appreciation for the team and commented on self-help projects. Ms. Kravchuk inquired about custodial staffing
standards, cell tower funds and opportunities for trades staff to mentor students. Ms. Creason thanked the team for their
responsiveness to work orders. Mr. Avey shared that he recently viewed the weight room improvements at Del Campo High
School, inquired about the digitization of work orders and any resulting metrics, and noted the importance of shade projects
at school sites.
