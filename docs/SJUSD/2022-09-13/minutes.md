Cultivating Leaders Pilot Program (I-1)
Assistant Superintendent of Educational Services Debra Calvin, Ed.D., Katherine Johnson Middle School Principal Damon
Smith, Dyer-Kelly Elementary School Principal Jamal Hicks and San Juan Teachers Association Executive Director
Shannan Brown gave a presentation on the Cultivating Leaders Seminar Series and Program, which was recently developed
collaboratively by the San Juan Unified School District, San Juan Professional Educators Coalition and San Juan Teachers
Association. Dr. Calvin discussed the purpose of the program, the four pathways, the program components and the monthly
seminars. Mr. Smith and Mr. Hicks provided an overview of the mentor program and shared their personal experiences
about being a mentor and a mentee. Ms. Brown shared information about the recent meet and greet event and today’s first
seminar, which focused on unconscious bias. Ms. Brown also spoke about the communication and feedback loops and
guiding tools. After Dr. Calvin discussed next steps and future networking opportunities, she answered questions from the
board. Board members expressed support for the program and asked clarifying questions related to caseload numbers,
frequency of meetings and future plans to expand the program in order to accommodate more participants, which Dr. Calvin
addressed. Chief of Staff Mr. Allen stated that the communication team appreciates the opportunity to work with the group,
and on behalf of Superintendent Kern, he thanked everyone for their work.

Proposed Revisions to LCAP PAC Bylaws (I-2)
Chief of Staff Trent Allen presented the proposed revisions to the Local Control and Accountability Plan Parent Advisory
Committee (LCAP PAC) bylaws, noting the changes that were made since the item was originally presented at the last
board meeting. Mr. Hernandez pointed out that the new bylaws limit the composition of the committee to “parents or legal
guardians of pupils enrolled in the district”, noting that this could prevent a qualified community member from being
eligible to serve on the committee. Dr. McKibbin shared similar thoughts, and he suggested revisiting the clause in the
future once its effects are known. Ms. Creason expressed support for representation from parents/guardians on the
committee, stating that if that if recruitment becomes too much of a challenge then a change should be considered. Ms.
Creason also thanked Mr. Hernandez and Ms. Costa for their work on the bylaw revisions. It was moved by Mr. Hernandez,
seconded by Ms. Costa, to approve the proposed revisions to the LCAP PAC bylaws. MOTION CARRIED
UNANIMOUSLY [McKibbin, Creason, Costa, Hernandez, Villescaz].

2021-2022 Unaudited Actuals and 2022-2023 Revised Budget (I-3)
Chief Financial Officer Jennifer Stahlheber presented the 2021-2022 unaudited actuals and 2022-2023 revised budget. Ms.
Stahlheber read the core commitments and reviewed the significant changes to the 2022-2023 budget since it was adopted
last June, explaining that there has been a large increase in revenues from Local Control Funding Formula (LCFF) revenues,
one-time state discretionary block grant funds and Expanded Learning Opportunities Program funds. Ms. Stahlheber
explained next steps and then answered questions from the board. It was moved by Ms. Villescaz, seconded by Ms. Creason,
to accept the unaudited actuals for 2021-2022 and approve the revisions to the 2022-2023 budget. MOTION CARRIED
UNANIMOUSLY [McKibbin, Creason, Costa, Hernandez, Villescaz].
