New High School Courses for 2026-2027 (J-1)
Deputy Superintendent of Schools and Student Support Timothy Chip Dale, Ed.D., presented the topic and then introduced
Director of Professional Learning and Curriculum Innovation Nicole Kukral. Ms. Kukral explained the new course request
process, discussed community feedback themes and reviewed the list of newly proposed high school courses for 2026-2027
which include the following: Arts: Commercial Music/Modern Band, Jazz Choir, Mariachi Ensemble, Musical Theater, and
Percussion Ensemble; Electives: Personal Finance (a new California graduation requirement, effective for the class of 2030-
2031, which must be offered by 2027-2028), Philosophy, and Project Management.

Board members made comments. Mr. Avey expressed support for the proposed courses, highlighting Project Management
and Personal Finance. Mr. Avey noted that as more courses are added, it becomes increasingly challenging for students to
fit everything into their schedules, raising questions about block versus traditional schedules and ensuring students still
have access to a range of elective options. Ms. Creason also supported the proposed courses, emphasizing the Personal
Finance course and the importance of connecting coursework to careers. Ms. Creason also encouraged a focus on starting
with job pathways and then helping students understand transferable skills and career relevance. Ms. Costa reported that
the C&S committee voted unanimously to recommend the approval of the proposed courses, noting the thoughtful
discussion at the committee meeting and sharing that teachers and administrators expressed enthusiasm for the courses.
Action was scheduled for March 24.
