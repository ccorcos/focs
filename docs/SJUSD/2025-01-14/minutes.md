Multilingual Learner Program Update (I-1)
Assistant Superintendent of Educational Services Harvey Oaxaca, Ed.D., Director of the Office of Student Learning
Assistance Gwyn Dellinger and English Learner Program Manager Genoveva Mendoza Navarrete gave a presentation on
the district’s multilingual learner program. Dr. Oaxaca introduced the topic, while Ms. Dellinger spoke about the California
English Learner Roadmap, enrollment numbers, language diversity and categories of English learners. Ms. Navarrete shared
information on reclassification, the California Dashboard English Learner Progress Indicator (ELPI), systems of supports,
designated and integrated English Language Development (ELD), professional learning opportunities, the E.L. Achieve
Systematic ELD program, and the State Seal of Biliteracy. Newcomer Support Team Supervisor Aref Asimi shared his
experiences and provided information about the work of the newcomer support team to help families feel welcome and
supported. Dr. Oaxaca outlined next steps. Board members thanked the team for their continued work, making comments
and posing questions which staff addressed. Ms. Kravchuk inquired about the expansion of professional development at
secondary sites, Assembly Bill 714, the English learner master plan and transportation. Mr. Stanekzai inquired about
bilingual instructional assistants, high school graduation requirements and college enrollment. Ms. Creason spoke about the
statewide cradle-to-career data system and the complexities of navigating the American education system. Ms. Costa

The Ralph M. Brown Act Presentation (I-2)
General Counsel Fhanysha Clark Gaddis gave a detailed presentation on The Ralph M. Brown Act. Topics discussed
included: the intent of the Brown Act, board role and conduct, meetings, teleconference laws, communications, agendas,
closed session, social media, meeting disruption/interruptions/threats and consequences for Brown Act violations. Board
members made comments and posed questions which Ms. Gaddis addressed.

Annual Policy Review (I-3)
Ms. Gaddis explained that, in accordance with Board Bylaw 9311 and with state and local law, the board is required to
conduct an annual review of the following board policies (BPs): BP 3430 Investing; BP 5116.1 Intradistrict Open
Enrollment; BP 6020 Parent Involvement; and BP 6145 Extracurricular and Cocurricular Activities. Ms. Gaddis noted that
staff has reviewed the board policies, and two revisions are recommended at this time to BP 3430 and BP 6020 and no
revisions to BP 5116.1 and BP 6145. Mr. Avey commented on the board policies. Action was scheduled for January 28.

Williams Complaint Report (I-4)
Ms. Gaddis explained that the district must report on the status of Williams-type complaints filed with the district per
Education Code section 35186(d), which requires each school district to publicly report, on a quarterly basis, summarized
data on the nature and resolution of all Williams-type complaints. Ms. Gaddis stated that, during the time period from
October 1, 2024, through December 31, 2024, there were no Williams-type complaints filed.

Resolution No. 4174: Certifying Results of the November 5, 2024, General Obligation Bond Election (I-5)
Chief Operations Officer Frank Camarda presented the item, thanking the community and everyone who contributed to the
work during the bond election. The bond measure was approved by more than 55 percent of the votes cast at the bond
election. Ms. Costa described this as a celebration and thanked everyone who made this possible, noting that it was a
collective effort. It was moved by Ms. Costa, seconded by Ms. Creason, to adopt Resolution No. 4174, certifying to the
Board of Supervisors of Sacramento County all proceedings in the November 5, 2024, general obligation bond election.
MOTION CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY [Avey, Kravchuk, Perez, Creason, Bloise, Stanekzai, Costa].
