Revisions to Governance Handbook (J-9)
Chief of Staff Trent Allen presented the proposed revisions to the Governance Handbook. Board members engaged in a
discussion regarding the professional development language that was proposed at the February 25, 2025, board meeting,
specifically the expectation that board members complete the California School Boards Association (CSBA) Masters in
Governance (MIG) program in order to move into a leadership position on the board. Board members agreed to modify the
language to read: “Prior to serving in an officer role, a member is expected to be enrolled in, and actively working to
complete, CSBA’s Masters in Governance program.” Mr. Avey also inquired about the section of the handbook related to
assigning work to staff. Superintendent Bassanelli explained that board member information requests need to go through
her in order to ensure responsiveness and transparency. Ms. Creason noted that this process is a normal business protocol
and is not unique to the district. It was moved by Mr. Perez, seconded by Ms. Creason, to approve the proposed revisions
to the Governance Handbook as amended. MOTION CARRIED 6-0-1 [AYES: Avey, Kravchuk, Perez, Creason, Bloise,
Stanekzai; NOES: None: ABSENT: Costa].
