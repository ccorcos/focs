Resolution No. 4178: Commit Fund Balance (H-1)
Chief Financial Officer Joel Ryan presented the resolution for consideration, which includes two new commitments: (1.) a
one-year extension of identified Learning Recovery Emergency Block Grant (LREBG) actions and (2.) a one-time
commitment of funds for support for newcomer students. Mr. Ryan explained these one-time commitments are possible
due to the current strength and growth of the ending fund balance.

Public Comments:
Angela Luna encouraged the board to vote yes in support of the resolution.

Naima Reddick expressed support for extending the funding for instructional assistants.

After public comment, board members engaged in a lengthy discussion. Ms. Costa expressed support for the resolution,
noting the large input from the community and the value of extending funding for instructional assistants and intervention
teachers. Mr. Bloise also expressed support for the resolution, emphasizing the importance of instructional assistants and
closing the COVID gap before middle school, and he shared that he sees literacy as one hope for the additional newcomer
funding. Ms. Creason expressed support for the resolution, reaffirming her commitment to minimizing disruption within
the system and protecting jobs, and she also expressed support for the additional funding for newcomers. Ms. Kravchuk
spoke about creating high-leverage solutions and investing in resources for all students, and she recommended pausing this
item to allow for further conversation and time to conduct a needs assessment to evaluate if the current solutions are
effective. Ms. Costa raised concerns about the timeline due to the approaching PKS deadlines and the potential impacts of
layoffs on the system. Ms. Creason acknowledged the need to re-evaluate supports and services but agreed with moving
the item forward tonight and having additional future discussions. Mr. Avey expressed support for the resolution, making
comments regarding the state budget, the use of one-time funds and future discussions regarding the LCAP and budget,
while advocating not to delay the decision tonight. Ms. Kravchuk noted this additional funding presents an opportunity to
possibly incorporate new strategies, reiterating her desire for high-leverage solutions over maintaining the status quo. Mr.
Bloise asked clarifying questions about the potential impacts of delaying a decision, which staff addressed, and he concluded
by stating his support for deciding now in order to retain staff. Ms. Costa expressed support for continued conversations

2024-2025 Local Control and Accountability Plan (LCAP) Mid-Year Update (H-2)
Interim Deputy Superintendent of Schools and Student Support Amy Slavensky, Ph.D., provided a mid-year update on the
LCAP, which included an overview of the LCAP, community engagement, metrics and data, and budget. Dr. Slavensky
began by providing background information about the development of the 2024-2027 LCAP, which is currently in year one
of the three-year cycle, including details about the Local Control Funding Formula (LCFF), the eight state priority areas,
LCAP goals and continuous improvement. Dr. Slavensky also spoke about the educational partnership engagement strategy
that took place with the community to help inform the LCAP. Assistant Director of Assessment, Evaluation and Planning
Christopher Smith provided an update on LCAP actions, expenditures, metrics and data. Dr. Slavensky concluded by
providing a budget overview and outlining next steps. Board members made comments and posed questions, which staff
addressed. Ms. Kravchuk commented on the varying levels of support for English learners, depending on the location of
the school site, and she also inquired about funding for technology and individual school site budget allocations. Mr. Avey
made comments about how students are moving into the district. Mr. Avey asked how the board could engage in the LCAP
process before June. Superintendent Bassanelli responded that she could connect individually with board members to collect
feedback on specific LCAP actions.

Choices Charter School 2024-2025 Local Control and Accountability Plan (LCAP) Mid-Year Update (H-3)
Assistant Superintendent of Educational Services Harvey Oaxaca, Ed.D., introduced Choices Charter School Director Brett
Givens who gave a mid-year update on the Choices Charter School LCAP, which included an overview of the LCAP, 2024-
2025 mid-year data and a budget overview of funding sources. Mr. Givens also highlighted areas of success and growth for
Choices Charter School. Board members made comments and posed questions, which Mr. Givens addressed.

Revisions to Board Policy 6145.6 International Exchange (H-4)
Dr. Oaxaca presented the proposed revisions to Board Policy 6145.6 International Exchange for discussion. Dr. Oaxaca
explained that the proposed revisions would allow the option for exchange students to participate in one semester rather
than an entire school year, which aligns with other school districts in the state.

Public Comment:
Rachele DeBoice, area manager from EF (Education First) Exchange, expressed support for the proposed revisions.
