Arts Education and Proposition 28 Update (J-1)
Interim Deputy Superintendent of Schools and Student Support Amy Slavensky, Ph.D., introduced the topic and Visual and
Performing Arts Program Specialist Michael Dittmer, who gave a presentation on the district’s arts education programs and
the related implementation of Proposition 28, which provides additional arts funding to schools. Mr. Dittmer highlighted
the importance of arts education and spoke about the arts opportunities available to students in all grade levels as a result
of the Proposition 28 funding. Mr. Dittmer also spoke about the Arts, Music and Instructional Materials (AMIM)
Discretionary Block Grant and next steps.

Board members made comments and posed questions, which Mr. Dittmer and Director of Professional Learning and
Curriculum Innovation Nicole Kukral addressed. Mr. Stanekzai acknowledged the music opportunities for his daughter at
Greer Elementary School. Ms. Kravchuk expressed support for arts education, encouraged an increase in programs at middle
schools and inquired about opportunities to address staffing shortages. Mr. Perez shared the importance of high school band
classes to his son. Mr. Avey inquired about the career technical education (CTE) theater pathway at Mesa Verde High
School and expressed support for the growth of the smaller arts programs.

Resolution Reducing or Discontinuing Particular Kinds of Services – TK-12 Certificated (J-3)
Chief of Human Resources Rebecca Toto, Ed.D., provided a detailed explanation of the process and that a resolution is
required by the California Education Code to provide advanced notice to TK-12 certificated employees by March 15 for
reducing or discontinuing particular kinds of services (PKS). Dr. Toto further explained that an additional resolution
establishes criteria to apply to break a tie in seniority for certificated staff affected by program reductions and who have the
same date of hire.

Resolution Reducing or Discontinuing Certain Classified Services (J-4)

Revisions to Governance Handbook (J-7)
Chief of Staff Trent Allen presented the proposed revisions to the Governance Handbook for discussion, which resulted
from the recent board workshop, the incorporation of protocols for disruptions during board meetings, and updated
references to the strategic plan. Mr. Avey proposed adding language to the handbook that codifies the expectation that board
members complete the California School Boards Association (CSBA) Masters in Governance (MIG) program in order to
move into a leadership position on the board. There was general consensus from the board to add this language. Mr. Allen
will incorporate the request into the handbook and bring the item back for action.
