Closed Session/Expulsion Actions (F-6)
Mr. Avey shared that there were two closed session actions to report.

First, Mr. Avey read the following statement: “The Board of Education recently concluded its review of an independent
investigation concerning complaints regarding agendized item named Board of Trustees- 25-26_001. The investigation is
related to Trustee Nick Bloise. The investigation concludes substantiated allegations against the Trustee that did not meet
the Board’s standards for professional conduct and governance; as such, the Board of Education unanimously voted, in
closed session, to formally reprimand Trustee Nick Bloise. The Board takes its responsibility to model respectful, ethical,
and lawful behavior seriously. Accordingly, the Board has directed Trustee Bloise to complete targeted professional
development including: (1.) Harassment prevention and respectful workplace training (to be completed through in-person,
one-on-one sessions with a law firm within 90 days); (2.) Board governance, conduct, and ethical leadership training (a
minimum four-hour, in-person course to be completed within 90-days); and (3.) Compliance with all site visit
recommendations. Trustee Bloise is reminded of the Board’s governance standards and the expectation that all members
uphold the highest level of professionalism in interactions with staff, fellow Trustees, and the community. Trustee Bloise
is responsible for following all guidelines and requirements. The Board will continue to monitor conduct and will take
further actions as permitted by law should any additional violations occur. This action reflects the Board’s commitment to
fostering a safe, respectful, and effective environment for students, staff, and the community we serve.”

Second, Mr. Avey also reported that, in closed session, the board voted unanimously to accept a hearing panel’s
recommendation of one expulsion in case number S-01.

Summer Learning Update (J-1)
Assistant Superintendent of Elementary Education and Programs, Amberlee Townsend-Snider; Assistant Superintendent
of Secondary Education and Programs, Kristan Schnepp; Director of Career Technical Education (CTE), K-12 Counseling
and College/Career Readiness, Brett Wolfe; and Director of Equity and Student Achievement, Crista Koch, gave a
presentation on student learning that took place during the summer of 2025. Ms. Townsend-Snider introduced the topic,
shared enrollment data and spoke about the special education Extended School Year (ESY) and Expanded Learning
Programs. Ms. Koch discussed the Office of Student Learning Assistance (OSLA) summer programs including Camp
Invention, K1 Boost, Camp Kinder, Newcomer Middle Grades, Native Wisdom and the summer intern program. Mr. Wolfe
provided an overview of the middle school and high school summer programs, shared information about Camp Winthers
and outlined next steps for summer 2026. Board members made comments and posed questions, which staff addressed. Ms.
Creason expressed appreciation for students having the opportunity to explore potential career paths. Ms. Kravchuk
acknowledged the high school internship program and the Jump Start Math with Prisms Virtual Reality program, inquired
about high school credit recovery data and expressed appreciation for the enrollment date change. Mr. Avey commented
on students sampling CTE offerings, noted the credit recovery data and supported the earlier enrollment window.

2024-2025 Unaudited Actuals and 2025-2026 Budget Revisions (J-4)
Chief Financial Officer, Joel Ryan, presented the 2024-2025 unaudited actuals and 2025-2026 revised budget. Mr. Ryan
reviewed the 2024-2025 unaudited actuals, highlighting three main points: the unrestricted fund balance increased, the
restricted fund balance decreased, and the district ended the year in good financial standing. Mr. Ryan also explained the
significant changes to the 2025-2026 budget since its adoption last June, which includes an increase in unrestricted funds
due to higher revenue for transitional kindergarten, aimed at lowering student-to-teacher ratios, and an increase in restricted
funds resulting from a change in the Expanded Learning Opportunities Program (ELO-P) grant funding and partial
restoration of Learning Recovery Emergency Block Grant (LREBG). Mr. Ryan also discussed next steps. Board members
made comments and posed questions, which Mr. Ryan addressed. Ms. Kravchuk inquired about Rate 2 vs Rate 1 funding.
Mr. Avey expressed appreciation for the fiscally sound approach taken with the budget. It was moved by Ms. Creason,
seconded by Mr. Bloise, to accept the unaudited actuals for 2024-2025 and approve the revisions to the 2025-2026 budget.
MOTION CARRIED 6-0-1 [AYES: Avey, Kravchuk, Creason, Bloise, Stanekzai, Costa; NOES: None; ABSENT: Perez].
