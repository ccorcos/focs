Budget Financial Status Report/2024-2025 Second Interim Report (I-1)
Chief Financial Officer Joel Ryan presented the 2024-2025 Second Interim Report, certifying the Second Interim Report
as positive, with the understanding of the fiduciary responsibility to maintain fiscal solvency for the current and subsequent
two fiscal years. The Second Interim Report reflects actual revenue and expenditures through January 31, 2025, forecasts
the remainder of the 2024-2025 fiscal year, and provides a multi-year budget projection for years 2025-2026 and 2026-
2027.

It was moved by Mr. Bloise, seconded by Ms. Costa, to approve the 2024-2025 Second Interim Report with the positive
certification of the district’s ability to meet its financial obligations for the current fiscal year and subsequent two fiscal
years. MOTION CARRIED 6-0-1 [AYES: Avey, Kravchuk, Perez, Creason, Bloise, Costa; NOES: None; ABSTAIN:
Stanekzai].

Measure P (2016) and Measure P (2024) Update (I-2)
Mr. Ryan presented the topic and introduced Isom Advisors Principal Jason List and Raymond James Financial Inc.
Managing Director Brett Lee who gave a tax rate presentation on Measure P (2016) and Measure P (2024). Mr. List and
Mr. Lee discussed the district’s tax base, historical assessed values, Measure P plan of finance, tax rate estimates and
historical and projected tax rates as well as a timeline of steps to a potential bond sale later this year. Board members
thanked the bond team for their efforts and made comments regarding fiscal responsibility.

Facilities Master Plan (I-3)
Chief Operations Officer Frank Camarda introduced the item and thanked the team, explaining that the updated Facilities
Master Plan serves as a long-term roadmap and vision for the district’s facility needs. Mr. Camarda discussed the extensive
community outreach that took place. Derek Labrecque from JKAE Design shared that the plan includes comprehensive
assessments of the physical conditions and educational environments, as well as cost analyses of each campus and support
sites. Mr. Labrecque further explained that the plan incorporates educational goals, updated site and building plan concepts,
useful life data of major building systems, demographic analysis, state funding opportunities, capacity, utilization, deferred
maintenance and a scoring rubric to assist in making data-driven decisions for project implementation. Mr. Camarda also
reviewed some of the additional project implementation planning factors that may need to be considered, such as state
mandates, security initiatives, evolving demographics, educational programs, large capital assets, compliance issues or
emergencies. Jason Villanueva from VM3 Consulting and Cherie Chenoweth, San Juan Unified Facilities Business and
Compliance Coordinator, demonstrated the interactive website and public-facing dashboard, explaining that the platform
will support proactive planning for project development and show updates as projects progress. Mr. Camarda explained
next steps, which includes the completion of the master plan and the launch of the public-facing interactive tool in June
2025.

Board members expressed support for the plan and posed questions, which staff addressed. Ms. Creason spoke about the
Facilities Committee, the connection to LCAP actions, the community engagement process, the rollout process and
variables that may impact project priority recommendations. Ms. Kravchuk expressed appreciation for the transparency and
interactive nature of the dashboard, and she inquired about the visibility of historical updates and the rubric. Mr. Bloise
inquired if principals had seen the vision plans. Mr. Avey shared that he believes the rubric will dictate which projects need
to be prioritized 95 percent of the time; however, he proposed adding language to the motion to allow the superintendent
flexibility in addressing the remaining 5 percent. There was general consensus among board members to add the language.

It was moved by Ms. Creason, seconded by Mr. Perez, to approve the Facilities Master Plan rubric for future project
development and implementation while authorizing the superintendent to modify the plan to meet district needs. MOTION
CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY [Avey, Kravchuk, Perez, Creason, Bloise, Stanekzai, Costa].
