College and Career Readiness Update – Part 2 (J-1)
Assistant Superintendent of Secondary Education and Programs, Kristan Schnepp, introduced the topic, explaining that the
update would focus on the College Career Indicator (CCI), one of the seven indicators on the California School Dashboard.
The CCI represents the percentage of high school graduates who leave high school prepared for college or a career. Director
of College and Career Readiness, CTE and K-12 Counseling, Brett Wolfe, discussed the various ways students can achieve
the definition of prepared, including A-G completion and Advanced Placement (AP). K-12 Counseling and College/Career
Readiness Program Specialist Tracie Locke spoke about Naviance, a college and career readiness planning platform, and
also shared information about the college and career readiness initiatives taking place at Lichen K-8 School, Sylvan Middle
School and Mesa Verde High School. Ms. Schnepp then explained next steps. Board members made comments and posed
questions, which staff addressed.

New High School Courses for 2025-2026 (J-2)
Interim Deputy Superintendent of Schools and Student Support Amy Slavensky, Ph.D., presented the topic and introduced
Director of Professional Learning and Curriculum Innovation Nicole Kukral who provided an update with additional
context and clarity regarding the newly proposed ethnic studies courses. Board members engaged in a robust and lengthy
discussion, offering comments and posing questions, which staff addressed. Ms. Kravchuk made comments and asked
clarifying questions regarding core curriculum and electives, and she recommended approval of all proposed courses except
for the six ethnic studies courses, citing the current lack of a funded mandate from the state. Ms. Creason expressed support
for all the proposed courses, noting the unanimous support of the C&S committee. Ms. Costa also voiced support for the
full list of proposed courses, acknowledging the work of the teachers and the C&S committee, and she inquired about the
potential impact on the International Baccalaureate (IB) program. Mr. Bloise asked about the course approval and budget
timelines. Ms. Kravchuk reiterated her position against approving the ethnic studies courses until the graduation
requirement becomes a funded mandate. Ms. Kukral clarified that three of the ethnic studies courses are core curriculum
areas and three are electives. Mr. Bloise inquired about the possibility of delaying the vote until June. Ms. Creason expressed
concerns about setting a precedent by not approving curriculum that is unmandated. Mr. Avey expressed support for the
courses, noting the community feedback, the recommendation of the C&S committee and the value of offering students
additional course options. Various motions on the topic were considered.

Ms. Kravchuk presented an initial motion. It was moved by Ms. Kravchuk to approve the new proposed high school courses
except for the six ethnic studies courses – English 9: Ethnic Studies; Middle Years Programme (MYP) 4 Language and
Literature: Ethnic Studies; Middle Years Programme (MYP) 5 United States History and Ethnic Studies; Chicano Studies;
Music and Ethnic Studies, advanced Placement (AP) African American Studies. There being no second, the motion was
not considered.

Instructional Materials Adoption (J-3)
Dr. Slavensky presented the topic and then introduced Ms. Kukral who presented the 14 instructional materials
recommendations for high school which includes: Advanced Placement (AP) United States History; AP World History;
American Government; AP Macroeconomics; AP United States Government and Politics; Cultural Anthropology;
Constitutional Law; Political Science: Criminal and Civil Law; You & the Law; English 3: Ethnic Studies; Japanese 1-4,
including Middle Years Programme (MYP) Japanese 1-4 and International Baccalaureate (IB) Japanese 1-2; AP Japanese
Language and Culture; Financial Math; and Mathematical Modeling. Ms. Kukral explained the small adoptions process and
shared the details of the adoption selection process, including the selection criteria and stages of the process, noting the
opportunities for feedback from staff, students and families. Ms. Kukral then shared information regarding the cost of the
adoptions and professional learning opportunities for teachers. Action was scheduled for April 22.
