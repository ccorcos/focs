Revisions to Board Policy 6146.1 High School Graduation Requirements (I-2)
Assistant Superintendent of Secondary Education and Programs, Kristan Schnepp gave a presentation on the proposed
revisions to Board Policy 6146.1 High School Graduation Requirements, which are necessary to align with recent updates
to Education Code 51225.3 and Assembly Bill (AB) 181, which created an alternate pathway for students with significant
cognitive disabilities to receive a high school diploma, including an exemption from local graduation requirements beyond
the state minimum. Ms. Schnepp also explained that AB 2251 further clarified that school districts can, at their discretion,
adopt a policy to exempt students from additional coursework requirements beyond the state minimum for graduation. Ms.
Schnepp reviewed the history of the district’s graduation requirements and discussed the Class of 2025 pilot waiver process,
providing the breakdown of credit waivers requested. Ms. Schnepp shared that the proposed revisions to the board policy
align with current district practice, provide a clear process for graduating seniors who qualify to receive a diploma, establish
a waiver process for seniors who are credit-deficient but meet state minimum requirements, and ensure that all high schools
use a consistent, districtwide process when reviewing graduation requirements and applying waivers. Ms. Schnepp also
discussed next steps, which include refining the course of study options for students with exceptional needs; developing
four-year plans starting in the eighth grade, with additional course of study options for students with exceptional needs;

Board members made comments and posed questions, which staff addressed. Ms. Kravchuk requested clarification on the
next steps and encouraged more frequent updates regarding the board policy. Mr. Avey acknowledged the district’s
commitment to continuing discussions with parents and the Community Advisory Committee (CAC) concerning alternative
pathways and graduation requirements for special education students, noting that this presents an opportunity to address
some long-standing issues. Ms. Schnepp confirmed that the proposed revisions align with the most recent language of AB
181, and that the district is continuing to collaborate with the Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) to determine
the best course of action moving forward. Rebecca Foster, director II of special education, elaborated on the work completed
thus far to help inform the creation of a clear alternative pathway. Superintendent Bassanelli confirmed that the item could
return at a future board meeting for a progress update. Ms. Costa emphasized the importance of communicating not only
with the CAC but also with parents of students who have an IEP. Action was scheduled for September 9.
