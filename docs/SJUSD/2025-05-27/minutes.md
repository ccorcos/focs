Public Hearing: 2025-2026 Special Education Local Plan Area Annual Update (J-1)
Interim Deputy Superintendent of Schools and Student Support Amy Slavensky Ph.D. presented the item and introduced
Director II of Special Education Rebecca Foster who shared information about the Special Education Local Plan Area
(SELPA) and related updates on steps currently in progress to respond to feedback and strengthen services and supports for
special education students. Mr. Avey declared the topic of the 2025-2026 SELPA annual update a public hearing and invited
the public to speak. There being no comments from the public, Mr. Avey declared the public hearing closed. Mr. Bloise
asked clarifying questions about the CAC’s involvement with the SELPA, assistive technology devices and services, and
the SELPA budget template, which Ms. Foster addressed. Mr. Bloise also inquired about the inclusion of settlement
agreements in the SELPA, which General Counsel Fhanysha Gaddis responded that no such legal obligation exists. It was
moved by Ms. Kravchuk, seconded by Mr. Perez, to approve the 2025-2026 Special Education Local Plan Area (SELPA).
MOTION CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY [Avey, Kravchuk, Perez, Creason, Bloise, Stanekzai, Costa].

Student Discipline/Restorative Practices (J-3)
Assistant Superintendent of Educational Services Harvey Oaxaca, Ed.D., Director of Student Support Services Dominic
Covello and Multi-Tiered System of Supports Administrator on Special Assignment Katie Knifton gave a presentation
about student discipline, alternatives to suspension, restorative practices and expulsions. Dr. Oaxaca introduced the topic
and reviewed the three objectives for the 2024-25 school year. Mr. Covello discussed the outcomes of each objective,
sharing information about new state regulations and the calibration of discipline data reporting processes. Mr. Covello
presented the year-to-date data for home suspensions and in-school suspensions as well as alternatives to suspension data.
Ms. Knifton spoke about Navigate360 which provides on-demand digital lessons for K-12 students and was piloted at 29
school sites this year.

Board members made comments and posed questions, which staff addressed. Ms. Kravchuk asked clarifying questions
regarding defiance and disruptive behavior, staffing for in-school suspensions, Navigate360, and how schools reconcile
restorative practices with the consistent enforcement of behavior expectations. Ms. Kravchuk also suggested the need to
proactively educate the community about new discipline strategies to reduce confusion surrounding the significant shift in
approach. Ms. Costa inquired about the specific reading requirements of Navigate360 for elementary students and the
impact of the legislative changes on classroom dynamics and student achievement. Ms. Creason applauded the reduction
in suspensions, requested insights from school staff about the highlights and challenges of implementing the changes, and
asked if there is an intentional plan to include families in behavior discussions. Mr. Bloise inquired about the impact of
having Instructional Assistants (IAs) in classrooms. Mr. Stanekzai requested suspension data by home language, and he
advocated for presentations that promote peace and unity among students.

New Board Policy 5131.8 Mobile Communication Devices (J-4)
Chief of Staff Trent Allen presented the item for action. The proposed policy prohibits the use of smartphones or other
mobile communication devices during instructional time unless directed to do so by a staff member or are subject to the
listed exemptions.

Public Comment:
BJ Combs expressed support for a consistent, districtwide policy.

Board members made comments and posed questions, which staff addressed. Ms. Kravchuk expressed support for a
consistent districtwide policy of no cellphone use from bell to bell, with exceptions for Career Technical Education
programs and emergencies, she suggested refining the definition of mobile communication devices and she acknowledged
the potential for initial resistance but eventual normalization of a more restrictive policy. Mr. Stanekzai also expressed
support for a bell-to-bell policy. Ms. Creason expressed support for a middle-ground approach, commenting on policy
interpretation, existing school policies and the importance of community input. Mr. Perez also expressed support for a
middle ground approach, noting staffing considerations. Mr. Bloise expressed support for a bell-to-bell policy with
appropriate exceptions. Mr. Avey acknowledged both perspectives but currently supports the policy as is, commenting
about site-level implementation and future opportunities for observation and adjustments. Ms. Costa expressed support for
starting with a middle-ground approach, she spoke about staff time regarding enforcement of strict no-use rules and she
emphasized the value of input from the community and workgroups.
