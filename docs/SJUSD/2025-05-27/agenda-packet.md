                                    San Juan Unified Land Acknowledgement
The San Juan Unified School District acknowledges that our schools are located on the ancestral lands of the
Nisenan and Miwok peoples, who continue to care for this land as they have since time immemorial. We deeply
respect their ongoing connection to this land and recognize their resilience in preserving and revitalizing their
cultures and traditions despite historical and ongoing challenges.

We recognize the profound injustices endured by their ancestors, including genocide, forced assimilation, and
displacement, and we remain aware of the ongoing challenges and injustices they face today. As we advance in our
commitment to equity and inclusion, we pledge to strengthen our connections by fostering a culture of
understanding, respect, and active collaboration with their communities.

New Board Policy 5131.8 Mobile Communication Devices (J-2)
Chief of Staff Trent Allen presented the item explaining that Assembly Bill (AB) 3216 requires that all local education
agencies engage their community to develop a policy restricting student cellphone use by July 1, 2026. Mr. Allen shared
information about research findings and the requirements of AB 3216. Mr. Allen then spoke about the engagement strategies
and steps that took place to gather input from students, parents/guardians, staff and others around the topic, in order to
inform a draft policy, which was also made available for review and feedback. Mr. Allen shared that the results showed
mixed support for and against restrictions that extended beyond instructional time and emphasized the need for access to
devices for emergencies, health needs and other specific situations. Mr. Allen explained that based on the feedback, the
draft policy was refined, and the recommended board policy would restrict student use of mobile devices during
instructional time only and maintain availability of devices to students as required by law. Mr. Allen explained next steps
and the timeline.

Board members made comments and posed questions, which Mr. Allen addressed. Mr. Bloise noted that certain
instructional programs require students to use cellphones. Mr. Perez supported the idea of putting cellphones away during
class but opposed locking them up, citing the need for accessibility in emergencies. Ms. Creason asked clarifying questions
about the proposed policy and its implementation. Ms. Kravchuk expressed support for a more restrictive approach and
commented on the language regarding exceptions and the use of devices for translation during instructional time. Ms. Costa
expressed support for the proposed policy, noting the effective enforcement of designated zones at some school sites. Mr.
Stanekzai inquired about the possibility of having different policies for elementary, middle and high schools. Mr. Perez
raised concerns about liability for stolen phones. Ms. Creason said she might agree with stricter policy, but emphasized that
compromise often leads to greater success, also noting that pouches have not been an issue for liability. Mr. Bloise
mentioned that alternatives to pouches exist, such as secured boxes. Mr. Avey shared that while he prefers a stricter policy,
he supports the proposed policy as a first step, hoping that school sites will help inform future versions. Ms. Kravchuk
proposed reviewing the policy more frequently than the required five-year interval, which the majority of the board
supported. Superintendent Bassanelli stated that tools and a timeline could be developed to allow time for data collection
and feedback. Action was scheduled for May 27.

the core concerns raised by the district, and he asserted that a five-year term is the only term that should be considered. Mr.
Avey declared the topic of the Golden Valley Orchard School charter school renewal petition a public hearing and invited
the public to speak.

SUBJECT: Board Policy 5131.8: Mobile Communication Devices (Allen) - 8:15 p.m.

DEPARTMENT: Administration

ACTION REQUESTED:
Action: The superintendent is recommending that the board adopt Board Policy 5131.8: Mobile Communication
Devices.

RATIONALE/BACKGROUND:
In response to interest by the Board of Education and recent legislation, staff have undertaken a review of
cellphone restriction policies and practices. AB 3216 was signed by Governor Newsom in September 2024. It
requires that all local education agencies engage their community to develop a policy restricting student cellphone
use by July 1, 2026.

Research on the benefits of limiting cellphone access finds mixed results when looking at improvements to
academic outcomes with indications that the impacts may vary between different groups of students. Both research
and surveys of practitioners find that restrictions have the potential to positively impact both classroom behaviors,
mental health, safety and overall well-being.

Following a board presentation and discussion in October 2024, staff gathered voice from students,
parents/guardians, staff and others around the topic. A ThoughtExchange with more than 4,000 participants
surfaced a shared interest to implement reasonable restrictions that take into account emergency situations and
needs.

A small workgroup reviewed the input collected to inform a draft policy. That draft policy was made available for
review and feedback, receiving more than 700 responses. Feedback showed mixed support for and against
restrictions that extended beyond instructional time and again emphasized the need for access to devices for
emergencies, health needs and other specific situations. Based on the feedback, the draft policy was refined. The
policy presented to the board in this item would restrict student use of mobile devices during instructional time
only and maintain availability of devices to students as required by law.

If the policy is adopted, an implementation plan would require that devices be accessible to students in an
emergency situation, provide for progressive response to violations, produce a communication and rollout toolkit
for sites and see staff monitor and revise practice during 2025-26 including further exploration of strategies to
address concerns during non-instructional time.

Policy 5131.8: Mobile Communication Devices                                        Status: DRAFT

Original Adopted Date: TBD| Last Revised Date: TBD | Last Reviewed Date: TBD



The Governing Board recognizes that student use of smartphones and other mobile
communication devices on campus may be beneficial to student learning but and could be harmful
and disruptive of the instructional program and detrimental to student mental health and wellbeing
in some circumstances.

Students may use mobile communication devices only as permitted under this policy.

Students shall not use smartphones or other mobile communication devices during instructional
time unless directed to do so by a staff member or are subject to an exemption listed below.

This policy shall not prohibit a student from possessing or limited use of a mobile communication
device under any of the following circumstances: (Education Code 48901.5, 48901.7)

   1. In the case of an emergency, or in response to a perceived threat of danger

   2. When a teacher or administrator grants permission to the student to possess or use a
      mobile communication device, subject to any reasonable limitation imposed by that teacher
      or administrator

   3. When a licensed physician or surgeon determines that the possession or use is necessary
      for the student's health and well-being

The Superintendent or designee may undertake measures or strategies in accordance with law, to
limit student access to smartphones and other mobile communication devices on campus.
(Education Code 48901.7)

Smartphones and other mobile communication devices shall not be used in any manner which
infringes on the privacy rights of any other person.

A student's personal electronic device shall not be searched without the consent of the student or
the student’s parent/guardian, except pursuant to a lawfully issued warrant, or when a school
official, in good faith, believes that an emergency involving danger of death or serious physical
injury to the student or others requires access to the electronic device information, or when the
search is otherwise permitted pursuant to Penal Code 1546.1.

When a student uses a mobile communication device in an unauthorized manner while at a school
site or under the supervision of a district employee, the student may be disciplined and the district
employee may confiscate the device. The employee shall store the device securely until it is
returned to the student or parent/guardian or turned over to the principal or designee, as
appropriate. A student may be banned from bringing a device on campus after multiple violations.

A student may also be subject to discipline, in accordance with law, Board policy, or administrative
regulation, for off-campus use of a mobile communication device which poses a threat or danger
to the safety of students, staff, or district property or substantially disrupts school activities
                                                                                                        134
including but not limited to acts of harassment or cyberbullying.

The district will not be responsible or liable for a student's mobile communication device which is
brought on campus or to a school activity and is lost, stolen, or damaged.

The Board shall review and, as necessary, update this policy at least once every five years. Any
such review or update shall include significant educational partner participation to ensure that the
policy is responsive to the unique needs and desires of the school community.
