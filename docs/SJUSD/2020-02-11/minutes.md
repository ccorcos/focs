Continuous Improvement Series: English Language Learner Update (I-1)
Assistant Superintendent of Educational Services Debra Calvin, Ed.D., introduced Director of the Office of Student
Learning Assistance Roxanne Mitchell and Director of Assessment, Evaluation and Planning Susan Green who presented
an update on the English language learner program. Ms. Mitchell highlighted some of the comprehensive services for
English learners (EL), which include classroom, family and extended supports. Ms. Green provided an overview of the
measurable data related to demographics and EL performance. El Camino Fundamental High School Principal Randy
Holcomb explained the services that are currently available to EL students at El Camino, discussed the results from recent


                                                                                                                       2219
EL student and parent listening circles and described the next steps for the school. Ms. Mitchell explained the next steps
for the EL department. Staff answered questions from the board.

Mr. Hernandez asked questions regarding the services at El Camino, which Mr. Holcomb clarified. Ms. Creason asked how
teacher feedback is included in the stakeholder process. Ms. Mitchell stated that English Language Development (ELD)
teachers attend a monthly meeting. Ms. Costa inquired if the case management system only applies to high schools. Ms.
Mitchell explained that elementary and middle schools are included as part of the feeder school pattern. Mr. Hernandez
asked if the number of ELs at El Camino are typical of other schools. Ms. Green provided additional data. Superintendent
Kern stated that there are other schools in the district with higher EL populations than El Camino, but the high school was
selected for this presentation due to the increase in its EL population in recent years, which is similar to what other schools
on the west side have experienced. Dr. McKibbin posed questions related to the after-school programs at El Camino,
elementary students not meeting the growth criteria and high school dropouts. Mr. Holcomb explained the after-school club
is still in the development process, Ms. Leslie Wriston explained the three types of tests that are administered at the
elementary level and Ms. Green stated that an in-depth analysis of the high school dropout data is currently in process. Ms.
Villescaz inquired about the fifth-year cohort, the EL services El Camino is most proud of and plans for next steps. Ms.
Green clarified the data for the fifth-year cohort, Mr. Holcomb stated he is most proud of the work of the El Camino teachers
and counselors who know each EL student personally and Ms. Mitchell explained the progression of high school ELD
courses and stated that the new case management system will help EL students have access to the resources they need in
order to make progress.

Oral Recommendation and Addendum to Superintendent’s Employment Contract (I-2)
General Counsel Linda Simlick stated that pursuant to SB 1436, the district is required to provide a verbal summary of the
proposed compensation and fringe benefits of a local agency executive to the public during open session, and based on a
satisfactory evaluation of Superintendent Kern in August 2019, the recommendation is that the board accept the third
addendum to the superintendent’s employment agreement which includes: (1) the term of the superintendent’s agreement
shall be extended by one year to June 30, 2022; (2) effective July 1, 2020, the parties agree that the superintendent's work
year shall be 222 days of full and regular service to the district annually during the period covered by this agreement, and
the superintendent shall not be entitled to paid vacation days, but shall be allowed to take the legal and local holidays as
defined in the Education Code; and (3) effective August 13, 2019, the superintendent’s annual base salary shall be
$294,991.00 based on the positive evaluation received for the 2018-19 school year.

It was moved by Ms. Costa, seconded by Dr. McKibbin, that the third addendum to the superintendent’s employment
agreement be approved. MOTION CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY [Villescaz, McKibbin, Creason, Costa, Hernandez].
