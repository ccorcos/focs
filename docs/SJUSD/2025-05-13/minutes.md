New Board Policy 5131.8 Mobile Communication Devices (J-2)
Chief of Staff Trent Allen presented the item explaining that Assembly Bill (AB) 3216 requires that all local education
agencies engage their community to develop a policy restricting student cellphone use by July 1, 2026. Mr. Allen shared
information about research findings and the requirements of AB 3216. Mr. Allen then spoke about the engagement strategies
and steps that took place to gather input from students, parents/guardians, staff and others around the topic, in order to
inform a draft policy, which was also made available for review and feedback. Mr. Allen shared that the results showed
mixed support for and against restrictions that extended beyond instructional time and emphasized the need for access to
devices for emergencies, health needs and other specific situations. Mr. Allen explained that based on the feedback, the
draft policy was refined, and the recommended board policy would restrict student use of mobile devices during
instructional time only and maintain availability of devices to students as required by law. Mr. Allen explained next steps
and the timeline.

Board members made comments and posed questions, which Mr. Allen addressed. Mr. Bloise noted that certain
instructional programs require students to use cellphones. Mr. Perez supported the idea of putting cellphones away during
class but opposed locking them up, citing the need for accessibility in emergencies. Ms. Creason asked clarifying questions
about the proposed policy and its implementation. Ms. Kravchuk expressed support for a more restrictive approach and
commented on the language regarding exceptions and the use of devices for translation during instructional time. Ms. Costa
expressed support for the proposed policy, noting the effective enforcement of designated zones at some school sites. Mr.
Stanekzai inquired about the possibility of having different policies for elementary, middle and high schools. Mr. Perez
raised concerns about liability for stolen phones. Ms. Creason said she might agree with stricter policy, but emphasized that
compromise often leads to greater success, also noting that pouches have not been an issue for liability. Mr. Bloise
mentioned that alternatives to pouches exist, such as secured boxes. Mr. Avey shared that while he prefers a stricter policy,
he supports the proposed policy as a first step, hoping that school sites will help inform future versions. Ms. Kravchuk
proposed reviewing the policy more frequently than the required five-year interval, which the majority of the board
supported. Superintendent Bassanelli stated that tools and a timeline could be developed to allow time for data collection
and feedback. Action was scheduled for May 27.

Public Hearing No. 2: Golden Valley Orchard School Charter School Renewal Petition (J-3)
Dr. Oaxaca presented the item, sharing that the district’s charter review team recommends conditional approval of the
Golden Valley Orchard School (GVOS) charter school renewal petition, contingent upon strict adherence to the terms and
conditions set forth in the findings of fact and recommendations, for a reduced term of three years (from July 1, 2025,
through June 30, 2028), in accordance with Education Code. Dr. Oaxaca shared the findings of fact and recommendations,
which identified required corrective actions related to the GVOS charter school renewal petition. Dr. Oaxaca noted that
fiscal oversight and special education are areas of concern that informed the recommendation for a reduced three-year term,
rather than a five-year term. GVOS Executive Director Caleb Buckley addressed the board, stating that GVOS has addressed
the core concerns raised by the district, and he asserted that a five-year term is the only term that should be considered. Mr.
Avey declared the topic of the Golden Valley Orchard School charter school renewal petition a public hearing and invited
the public to speak.

Public Comments:
Marcus Neal expressed concerns about special education at Golden Valley Orchard School.

Janice Oliver-Iraci expressed support for Golden Valley Orchard School.

There being no further comments, the public hearing was closed.

Ms. Costa expressed concerns about the 46 items marked as “not met” by the charter review team, she noted the lack of
timely responses from GVOS during the review process and stated that she is not willing to support more than a three-year

Special Education: Finding of Noncompliance (J-4)
Interim Deputy Superintendent of Schools and Student Support Amy Slavensky, Ph.D., presented the item and introduced
Director II of Special Education Rebecca Foster who provided the board with information regarding a special education
finding of noncompliance in one case by the California Department of Education (CDE) as well as the required corrective
actions. Ms. Foster explained the Special Education Department is actively implementing the corrective actions and will
ensure that all CDE requirements are met by the specified deadlines. Ms. Foster also shared that the department is
undertaking ongoing work and providing professional learning to mitigate complaints and challenges in the future.
