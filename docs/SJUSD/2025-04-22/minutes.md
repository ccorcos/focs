Reading Difficulties Screener (J-2)
Interim Deputy Superintendent of Schools and Student Support Amy Slavensky, Ph.D., introduced English/Language Arts
and History-Social Science Program Specialist Heather Brandt who presented the topic, explaining that Senate Bill 114
requires school districts to assess risk of reading difficulties, including dyslexia, by annually screening students in
kindergarten through second grade beginning in Fall 2025. Ms. Brandt discussed the legislative requirements, and the
process the review committee used in consideration of the state board of education-approved reading difficulties screeners
and the selection criteria. Ms. Brandt shared that the recommended screener is mCLASS (Amplify) with Dynamic Indicators
of Basic Early Literacy Skills (DIBELS) Eighth Edition and mCLASS Lectura. Lastly, Ms. Brandt provided information
about the recommended screener, including the associated costs and implementation plan. Board members made comments
and posed questions, which staff addressed. Ms. Kravchuk asked about DIBELS and funding sources. Ms. Costa inquired
about teacher assessment time, the availability of remedial programs and materials, and teacher training. Mr. Avey made
comments about the makeup of the review committee, and he inquired about feedback from the Community Advisory
Committee (CAC) and the District English Learner Advisory Committee (DELAC). Action was scheduled for May 13.

Instructional Technology (J-3)
Dr. Slavensky presented the topic and introduced Instructional Technology Program Specialist Nicole Naditz, who gave a
presentation on the district’s efforts to support the effective use of instructional technology to ensure that students are
prepared for a rapidly evolving world. Ms. Naditz noted that since 2020, efforts to support instructional technology have
accelerated, as students and staff have gained increased access to technological tools. Ms. Naditz provided an overview of
the district’s vision and the evolution of instructional technology, current data on its use and impact on both students and
staff, and information on how instructional technology supports student learning. Ms. Naditz also discussed current
initiatives related to generative artificial intelligence (AI), and she outlined next steps including the development of a draft
instructional technology plan and the anticipated rollout of MagicSchool to students in the fall. Mr. Avey inquired about
the feedback loop for the instructional technology plan and made comments regarding AI, which Ms. Naditz addressed.
Superintendent Bassanelli thanked Ms. Naditz and the team for their advanced work and regional leadership in AI.

Williams Complaint Report (J-4)
General Counsel Fhanysha Clark Gaddis explained that the district must report on the status of Williams-type complaints
filed with the district per Education Code section 35186(d), which requires each school district to publicly report, on a
quarterly basis, summarized data on the nature and resolution of all Williams-type complaints. Ms. Gaddis stated that,
during the time period from January 1, 2025, through March 31, 2025, there were no Williams-type complaints filed.
