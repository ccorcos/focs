Public Hearing: Local Control and Accountability Plan (LCAP) (J-1)
Interim Deputy Superintendent of Schools and Student Support Amy Slavensky, Ph.D., introduced Director of Continuous
Improvement and LCAP Gianfranco Tornatore, Ed.D., who gave a detailed presentation on the district’s Local Control and
Accountability Plan (LCAP) which is in year two of the three-year cycle. Dr. Tornatore provided an overview of the LCAP
including an update on engagement strategies, a budget overview of revenue and expenditures, notable changes to the goals,
actions and metrics, and information regarding engagement of the LCAP Parent Advisory Committee.

Mr. Avey declared the topic of the Local Control and Accountability Plan (LCAP) a public hearing and invited the public
to speak.

Public Comment:
Corey Carskaddon commented about transportation, personnel and bullying.

There being no further comments from the public, Mr. Avey declared the public hearing closed.

Board members made comments and posed questions, which staff addressed. Mr. Bloise asked a clarifying question
regarding English language arts actions. Ms. Costa expressed appreciation for the growth in partnerships and acknowledged
the lack of quorum at LCAP PAC meetings. Ms. Creason expressed her appreciation for the work and encouraged staff to
identify creative strategies to help communicate the complex information. Mr. Avey made comments about the relationship
between the LCAP and the School Plan for Student Achievement (SPSAs). Action was scheduled for June 24.

Public Hearing: San Juan Choices Charter School Local Control and Accountability Plan (LCAP) (J-2)
Assistant Superintendent of Educational Services Harvey Oaxaca, Ed.D., introduced Director of Choices Charter School
Brent Givens who gave a presentation on the LCAP for Choices Charter School. Mr. Givens discussed some of the school’s
successes and identified areas of need, spoke about the engagement of educational partners, and provided a budget overview
of revenues and expenditures. Mr. Avey declared the topic of the Choices Charter School Local Control and Accountability
Plan (LCAP) a public hearing and invited the public to speak. There being no comments from the public, Mr. Avey declared
the public hearing closed. Board members asked questions, which Mr. Givens answered. Ms. Creason inquired about the
number of students enrolled, and Mr. Stanekzai asked about the teacher-to-student ratio. Action was scheduled for June 24.

Public Hearing: Presentation of 2025-2026 Budget (J-3)
Chief Financial Officer Joel Ryan presented the proposed 2025-2026 budget, including staffing standards, the use of
Education Protection Account (EPA) funds, and the substantiation of excess reserves (required by Senate Bill 858) for
discussion. Mr. Ryan shared information about the district’s general fund revenues, including Local Control Funding
Formula (LCFF) funds and federal/state/local funds, noting that the one-time pandemic relief funding has ended. Mr. Ryan
also discussed the district’s general fund expenditures, noting an increase in certificated positions and a potential upcoming
rise in benefit costs. Mr. Ryan reviewed the multi-year projections and confirmed that the district meets the 2 percent reserve
requirement. Mr. Ryan also addressed possible adjustments related to the upcoming state budget 45-day revision, including
the expansion of ELO-P, potential one-time discretionary funds and increased funding for transitional kindergarten. Mr.
Avey declared the topic of the 2025-2026 budget a public hearing and invited the public to speak. There being no comments
from the public, Mr. Avey declared the public hearing closed.

Board members made comments and posed questions, which Mr. Ryan addressed. Ms. Creason spoke about the uncertainty
of the federal budget and expressed support for the district’s fiscally prudent approach. Mr. Stanekzai inquired about the
impacts of the upcoming closure of Highlands Community Charter School later this month. Mr. Avey expressed
appreciation for a financially responsible budget, noting the strategic investments and the intentional decrease in fund
balance over next three years. Action was scheduled for June 24.
