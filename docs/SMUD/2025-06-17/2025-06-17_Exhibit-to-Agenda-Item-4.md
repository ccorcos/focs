Traffic Control Scope of Work
  • Required by law any time work impedes
    vehicle, bicycle, or pedestrian traffic

  • Provides space and protection for our
    crews to work safely on, or near, the
    roadway

  • Provides public safety

  • Traffic control is utilized in pre-arranged
    work and emergency work

  • Serves as a major component in storm
    response

Traffic Control: Principles and Practices
 • Traffic control must be performed by trained
   individuals whose sole purpose is to:
       • Build and maintain road/highway work zone
         buffer (cones, lighting, and signage) per traffic
         control plan
       • Direct traffic, if needed
       • Assist pedestrians
       • Provide crew and public safety


 • SMUD requires contractors to have a C-31 license
   (Construction Zone Traffic Control Contractor) to be
   able to perform work in any capacity.
