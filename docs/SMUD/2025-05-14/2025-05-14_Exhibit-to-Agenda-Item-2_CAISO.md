The ISO conducted a probabilistic assessment to evaluate the sufficiency of
the anticipated 2025 summer fleet to meet the 1-in-10 loss of load
expectation planning target.




    Loss of load expectation (LOLE) is a measure of the number of days per year for which the available generation capacity is insufficient to serve the demand and maintain required reserves
    at least once during that day. 0.1 LOLE or 1-day-in-10 LOLE equates to “1 day with an event in 10 years”.

    PLEXOS is an energy market simulation engine.
                                                                                 CAISO 2025 - Public                                                                                       Page 9
The ISO’s probabilistic assessment concludes that the portfolio meets
planning performance targets, yielding a surplus of 1,451 MW.
             This assessment evaluates the likelihood of needing
            emergency measures to balance supply and demand.




    This assessment takes into account reasonable historical trends and data, but does not consider
    coincident extreme or emergency events.

                                                 CAISO 2025 - Public                                  Page 10
The ISO’s multi-hour stack analysis also indicates a reasonable margin
above the planning reserve margin required to achieve a 0.1 loss of load
expectation.




A planning reserve margin of 22.67 percent is required to meet a 0.1 LOLE, calculated by first subtracting the surplus capacity of 1,451 MW (as determined in the probabilistic study) from
all available resources.
The load-weighted average planning reserve margin across all load serving entities for the 2025 resource adequacy year, which reflects Local Regulatory Authority established
requirements, is 16.7 percent.
