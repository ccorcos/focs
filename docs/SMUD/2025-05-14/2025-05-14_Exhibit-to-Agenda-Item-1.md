Background
• In 2024, SMUD received a competitive offer from DESRI, the parent
  company of Dry Creek Energy Storage, LLC, to construct and operate a
  battery storage project at SMUD’s Rancho Seco property (Dry Creek
  Project).

Context

•     SMUD will purchase the capacity and ancillary services.

•     The Dry Creek Project will be co-located with Rancho Seco Solar II and
      share the existing Point of Interconnection to SMUD’s transmission system.

•     The Project will be owned and operated by DESRI with SMUD having full
      controllability and acting as the scheduling coordinator.

•     SMUD will be the Lead Agency responsible for the CEQA Addendum.
