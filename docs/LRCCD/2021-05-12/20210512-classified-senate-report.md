COSUMNES RIVER COLLEGE
Julie Olson, Classified Senate President
Cosumnes River College held the final session for the Classified Equity Institute, the fourth session focused on,
Understanding Native American Tribes, History, and Students within Higher Education, a Student Panel, and Key
Takeaways from Part I,II, and III. The feedback regarding the final session from the Classified are the following…
            o Have a better understanding of the history of Native American Tribes and how to serve Native
                American students
            o Have a better understanding of the challenges students face
            o Have a better understanding of the strategies designed to support students
            o Have a better understanding of the of the impact of equity-minded practices
            o The student panel received the highest satisfaction rating
   • For future planning, respondents shared an interest in continuing equity professional development and
       understanding how to put this learning into action, including concrete examples of equity, diversity, and
       inclusive work.

To conclude this school year, the Classified Leadership will be transitioning into a new Leadership/membership of
the Classified Senate, come July 1st, (elections are currently going on). Classified Appreciation with be May 13 th
via zoom, with a robust fun and exciting agenda. Finally, Open forums with the College President and his
Executive team will continue during the summer months.
