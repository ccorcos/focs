            LOS RIOS COMMUNITY COLLEGE DISTRICT CLASSIFIED SENATE
                     Board of Trustees Report – November 2020


AMERICAN RIVER COLLEGE
Olga Prizhbilov, Classified Senate President
ARC classified professionals participated in Impression Groups for the candidates for the
ARC President and ARC Vice President of Instruction positions. It was rewarding to be able
to have our voice heard and participate in the process. We are excited to welcome Melanie
Dixon as the next ARC President and we are eager to hear the results of the VPI search.
Stacey Gershon was appointed to fill a vacancy to the ARC Classified Senate as the Senator
representing Operations. Pam Whipple from the ARC Health and Wellness Center presented
to the Classified Senate on resources available to students and staff in the remote
environment. The ARC Classified Senate is actively working on quite a few projects that we
are excited to present to you once they are realized.

COSUMNES RIVER COLLEGE
Julie Olson, Classified Senate President
Cosumnes River College Classified Senate continues to host monthly open forums with
President Bush and his executive counsel. The forums are for classified personnel only to
have a space to ask questions and receive any college updates from the executive team.
The Classified Senate hosted a Tae Kwon Do stress break, where Classified members were
able to take a break from their busy workplace and kick out some stress. Planning for the
Second Annual Classified Equity Institute is coming along, with a “Kick off” in January, this
year we will have monthly sessions for all Classified to attend, focused on Equity. Many
members are working hard on Accreditation teams, Participatory Governance, and providing
excellent service to our students, faculty, and management.

FOLSOM LAKE COLLEGE
Lindsey Campbell, Classified Senate President
The “We Are All Educators” training for classified staff wrapped up its four-part workshop
series on Thursday, October 29. The six hours of equity training, with content and curriculum
by FLC Student Services, covered topics such as: what is racism and how to talk about it,
color blindness, implicit bias, privilege, and more. Thank you to all the participants who
approached these difficult conversations with respect, and a willingness to grow. Thanks to
the Falcons who assisted with the training, including FLC Administrators and Classified
Professionals as facilitators. The Classified Senate is also beginning plans for a virtual
Classified Retreat this upcoming spring.
