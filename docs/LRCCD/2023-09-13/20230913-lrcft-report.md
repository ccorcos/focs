LRCFT Report to the Los Rios Board of Trustees      Sept. 13, 2023
The LRCFT sponsored and attended the SCLC annual Labor
Day picnic last week. It included over 2,000 union members and
their families. Many thanks to the trustees who were able to
attend.
The issue of homelessness in our community is at a crisis point.
The LRCFT recently sponsored the Second Annual Community
Summit on Homelessness held by the Sacramento Homeless
Organizing Committee. Student homelessness was one of the
topics discussed.
We are very pleased to see that all 13 of the CFT sponsored and
cosponsored bills were passed out of the appropriations
committee of either the state senate or assembly, including AB
811, the repeatability bill.
As part of LRCFT’s efforts at the state capitol, our LRCFT
PAFC will interview several candidates for potential
endorsement next week for AD 6 and the mayor’s race.
The LRCFT will take part in a Job Fair in October sponsored by
the Central Labor Council. We have informed our students and
colleges of this event. The LRCFT collaborated with LRCCD at
the last job fair in June and look forward to a future relationship
in this endeavor.
The LRCFT is expanding our stewards network and membership
meetings on each of the campuses as a way to get our members
more involved.
The LRCFT will take part in the American Federation of
Teacher’s Human, Civil, and Women’s Rights conference in
Houston, Texas, next month.
As part of membership appreciation, this semester we are
encouraging our faculty membership to attend a local theatrical
performance at the B St. theater. “Papi, Me, and Cesar Chavez.”
We purchased fifty tickets for the membership. The play runs
the month of October.
We are also conducting a membership drive this year and will
begin by contacting the 52 Long Term temporary faculty in the
district.
At a recent CFT Exec. Council meeting, our statewide
leadership reminded us that the Part Time faculty health care
agreement with Los Rios is viewed as THE model to follow for
other CFT local unions statewide.
Regarding the recent closure of Davies Hall at ARC, the LRCFT
has made clear in recent meetings with LRCCD that we would
like to hear about vital safety information sooner rather than
later in the process.
In closing, we look forward to the Chancellor’s cabinet
orientation next week.
Thank you!
Jason Newman LRCFT President
