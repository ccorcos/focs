LRCFT Report to the LRCCD Board of Trustees        Dec. 12, 2024
As we wrap up this semester, our faculty union is making plans to
participate in several public events in Spring that support students
and faculty in our region.
Our board voted to provide funds and participate in the two major
MLK Day celebrations and marches held in mid-January.
We are also sponsoring a group of students and faculty to attend
FACCC’s Advocacy and Policy conference held in Sacramento in
late February.
Along with students and faculty from across the state, the LRCFT
will participate next semester in CFT’s March in March to protect
higher education for students. The event is scheduled for March 4
and will include 2,000 students from across the state marching
from Southside Park in Sacramento to the state Capitol for a rally
with elected officials.
The LRCFT continues to actively assist undocumented students,
colleagues, and community members by disseminating immigration
information and signing on to a compact with LRCCD to protect
undocumented students. We are also providing funds to immigrant
rights organizations such as Nor Cal Resist.
The LRCFT recently concluded an election for campus
representatives, LRCFT Treasurer, and LRCFT President. The
outcome of the election includes Stephanie Rowe continuing as
Treasurer for another two-year term. I will also continue serving as
LRCFT President. I want to congratulate Jacob Traugott for winning
their election for Part Time representative at SCC.
We look forward tomorrow to attending the post-election holiday
celebration hosted by our new Los Rios Trustee, Dr. Collette Harris
Mathews. We are proud to have provided Dr. Harris-Mathews with
an early endorsement in her election campaign. We look forward to
working with our new trustee in the coming years.
Thank you!
Jason Newman
LRCFT President
