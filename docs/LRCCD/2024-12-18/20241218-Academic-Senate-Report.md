   Academic Senates’ Reports to the LRCCD Board of Trustees
                       December 2024

District Academic Senate (DAS) President, Paula Cardwell, Reports:
   ● The District Academic Senate voted to approve the Noncredit Implementation
       Task Force, which is tasked with exploring feasibility from the Admissions &
       Records/Student Services perspective as well as the Budget and Union
       perspective. Once we receive recommendations from these teams, we will
       determine if the Curriculum/10+1 feasibility piece should move forward.
   ● In response to board concerns regarding the exclusion of American Institutions
       as a local degree requirement:
          ○ Title 5 changes raised the minimum number of GE units from current 18 to
              21 units (6 units in English Composition, Oral Communication and Critical
              Thinking; and 3 units each in Math/Quantitative Reasoning, Arts &
              Humanities, Social & Behavioral Sciences, Natural Sciences, and Ethnic
              Studies)
          ○ Los Rios currently requires two additional GE courses beyond Title 5: 3-
              units of Living Skills coursework and two courses in Social & Behavioral
              Sciences, one being an American Institutions course.This brings our
              current local GE requirement to 24 units.
          ○ Our faculty had difficult decisions to make regarding our recommendation
              to the Board: to a) require only the 21 units specified in new Title 5
              regulations; b) retain both Living Skills AND American Institutions, bringing
              our local GE total units to 27; c) require either Living Skills OR American
              Institutions to keep our GE requirements at 24 units; d) to specify only
              Living Skills or e) to specify only American Institutions.
          ○ Discussion and debate was extensive, thorough, and thoughtful.
          ○ The interest in keeping the prescribed number of units equal to or lower
              than our current GE requirements was strong, as flexibility in course
              choices is related to degree completion.
          ○ Arguments for retaining living skills only included:
                  ■ Many of our Dual Enrollment high school partners request Living
                      Skills courses
                  ■ Work Experience falls under Living Skills; many local degree
                      earners are focused upon vocational/occupational skills.

Cosumnes River College Senate President, Jacob Velasquez, Reports:
  ● After careful consideration and much deliberation, the CRC Academic Senate
     voted overwhelmingly in support of adopting a proposal related to academic
     integrity. The proposal has three main components:
         1. to inform faculty of best practices to promote a robust culture of academic
             integrity;
         2. to amend CRC’s student conduct referral form to improve faculty
             communication to the Student Conduct Officer; and
         3. to make use of a follow-up letter to improve communication about how the
             academic integrity process is being resolved.
     The Senate recommends these measures be taken at CRC to promote a robust
     culture of academic integrity.
  ● The CRC Academic Senate regards the American Institutions requirement as an
     important part of our students’ education, especially in the context of the
     implementation of CalGETC. Being required to take an ethnic studies course,
     students will learn much about the history of social justice issues in the United
     States. The American Institutions requirement would benefit our students by
     ensuring they are exposed to information about our institutions and how those
     institutions can be used to create change and make progress toward a more just
     society.
