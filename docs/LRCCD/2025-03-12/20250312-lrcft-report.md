LRCFT Report to the LRCCD Board of Trustees                  March 12, 2025

The first weeks of the Trump administration have witnessed a slew of
executive actions targeting higher education and our students. In response,
several groups, including the American Federation of Teachers, have filed
lawsuits to stop the executive overreach. The LRCFT is proud that our
national affiliate is taking a strong stand for students and faculty.
On behalf of the LRCFT, I want to thank the Los Rios students and faculty
who attended the recent March in March to protect students and higher
education. Over 1,000 students and CFT members from across the state
took part in the Day of Action in Sacramento. Our students and faculty
marched and rallied for dignity and respect for all members of the higher
education community. The Sacramento Bee covered the event.
March 4 was also a national day of action by AFT and other organizations,
with a focus on protecting kids and fighting back against the Trump
administration. The day of action included over 2,000 AFT local
events throughout the country. AFT actions in CA. included a massive
turnout of school site actions by the Salinas Valley Federation of Teachers,
a rally with parents and community allies hosted by the Paso Robles Public
educators, a joint rally over the 101 Freeway by the San Rafael Federation
of Teachers and Novato Federation of Teachers, and many other protests.
Other recent LRCFT actions include partnering with FACCC at its recent
Advocacy and Policy Conference to lobby statewide elected officials to
support sponsored legislation. The LRCFT is also taking a leading role in
financially supporting LACLAA’s upcoming Cesar Chavez March, the
largest annual labor rally in Sacramento.
A recent LRCFT meet and greet event held at our faculty union for Los
Rios Part Time faculty was well attended with over 50 participants.
This weekend the LRCFT is sending 16 faculty delegates to the CFT
biennial convention in southern California. The theme of this year’s
convention is Union Power for All, a call to action to expand our power and
defend our schools, students, and communities from outside attack.
In closing, I want to congratulate ARC ESL professor                  Oranit
