   Academic Senates’ Reports to the LRCCD Board of Trustees
                         March 2025

District Academic Senate (DAS) President, Paula Cardwell, Reports:

   ●​ At our most recent meeting, the District Academic Senate approved two
      proposed regulation changes:
          ○​ R-7151, International Education, which changed to recognize the two
              major types of International Education programs: multi-partner consortium
              programs, which include students from other community college districts
              and usually host semester-long programs abroad, and college-based
              programs, which are typically smaller, Los Rios-based programs of shorter
              duration. The District Academic Senate thanks our International
              Education Committee liaison, Professor Richard Mowrer, for his hard work
              on this!
          ○​ R-7421, Work Experience Education, which changed to be consistent
              with Title 5 changes. Our students can now earn up to 14 units of Work
              Experience Education. The changes to Title 5 significantly expand
              opportunities for students to engage in hands-on, credit-bearing work
              experiences across a wider range of instructional programs. The District
              Academic Senate proudly recognizes and thanks Vivian Dillon, Faculty
              Coordinator of Work Experience Education at ARC for her work on this
              change.
   ●​ We forwarded a recommendation from our District Instructional Accessibility
      Committee that our district partner with the state Chancellor’s Office Accessibility
      Center and adopt the Accessibility Capability Maturity Model, a transformative
      framework designed to guide colleges and districts in advancing digital
      accessibility. This model “breaks down broad concepts of digital accessibility into
      clear, achievable milestones, ensuring that institutions can proactively improve
      accessibility at a pace that aligns with their resources.” We thank the District
      Instructional Accessibility and chair Beki Mendell for their hard work on this.
   ●​ The District Academic Senate has concerns regarding our recent space
      utilization studies with Applied Research + Consulting. We appreciate the
      efforts taken to inform our Facilities Master Plan. Faculty are excited about
