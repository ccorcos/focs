   Academic Senates’ Reports to the LRCCD Board of Trustees
                          May 2025

District Academic Senate (DAS) President, Paula Cardwell, Reports:
   ●​ Three of our four colleges are welcoming new Senate presidents at this time:
          ○​ ARC: Veronica Lopez
          ○​ CRC: Lauren Wagner
          ○​ FLC: Wayne Jenson
          ○​ SCC: Amy Strimling will be continuing
          ○​ DAS: Paula Cardwell will be continuing
   ●​ The District Academic Senate recently approved a task force to update our
       Faculty Hiring Manual. We look forward to working with Vice Chancellor Bray
       and managers throughout Los Rios on this important and much-needed update.
   ●​ We accepted the recommendations of the Class Size Class Force, convened four
       years ago, to create a taxonomy of courses with optimal size ranges.
   ●​ We will also continue our exploration into noncredit feasibility, focusing on
       curriculum and 10+1 matters, including potential impacts upon our counselors
   ●​ The District Equity & Student Success Subcommittee of the DAS will also
       convene an AI Task Force. An amazing team of people across the district are
       coming together to create ethical frameworks for AI that address syllabus
       language related to AI use that aligns with district policies, evaluating AI
       detection tools and providing recommendations on their ethical, equitable, and
       responsible use, and proposing criteria for equitable access to AI resources for
       both students and faculty, among other important things.

American River College Senate President, Brian Knirk, Reports that ARC:
  ●​ Has elected Veronica Lopez as the next ARC Academic Senate President.
  ●​ Would like to voice concern regarding the district’s plan to include “inclusive
     access materials” in the Bookstore RFP. While beneficial to the bookstore, there
     are concerns regarding additional costs to students. We look forward to further
     discussion on this topic.

Folsom Lake College Senate President, Eric Wada, Reports:
   ●​ We thank Admissions and Records Supervisor, Christine Wurzer for her decades
      of service to FLC and Los Rios and passed a resolution in her honor.
   ●​ The FLC Academic Senate also reviewed and passed a resolution in support of
      bringing a Puente program to FLC.
   ●​ The FLC Academic Senate continues to advocate for sustained support for
      faculty coordination of professional development, new faculty academy, and SLO
      coordination. We are thankful for the collaborative spirit of our college
      administration, and hope to continue to work towards institutionalizing the
      sustained support so our growing college can meet faculty and student needs
      while also focusing attention on other issues instead.

Sacramento City College Senate President, Amy Strimling, Reports:
