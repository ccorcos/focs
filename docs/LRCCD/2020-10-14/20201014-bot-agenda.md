ARC: ARC student Marya Wozniuck is a winner of the Sherwin-Williams 2020 Student Design
Challenge, receiving a $2,500 cash prize as a result of her award-winning work. An Interior Design
major at ARC, Marya received the 1st place award in residential design with her eclectic New York
penthouse. She was competing against top students from collegiate programs across the country.
ARC's Interior Design program also received a $1,000 contribution as a result of Marya's success.

CRC: CRC will kick off celebrations for its 50th anniversary next Monday, Sept. 14, which was the
opening day of the college back in 1970. This year’s celebrations will be via videos, including an
opening statement by president Ed Bush and the opening of a time capsule, which was buried on
the 25th anniversary. Interviews of former presidents, administrators, faculty and students were
conducted over the last year to prepare for these celebrations. The CRC Theatre Department
recently took home a number of Elly Awards for the productions, "Servant of Two Masters," and
"Getting Out." The annual Elly Awards honor outstanding achievements of community theaters
and artists in the Sacramento area. Elly Awards are named for the late Eleanor McClatchy, who
was a devoted patron of the local arts. The most notable is the Outstanding Achievement Award
for Overall Production of “Getting Out.” Other Outstanding Achievement awards were given for
costume design, direction, leading and supporting female and others.

FLC: Folsom Lake College’s Innovation Center continues to be recognized as a leader in the Maker
movement and was recently accepted into two prestigious national programs. “Making Spaces:
Expanding Maker Education Across the Nation” aims to sustainably integrate Making into
educational institutions and build a national resource network. As a member of the program’s
new cohort, FLC’s makerspace will lead a regional partnership with local schools, libraries,
community organizations, and other educational institutions to help these new Maker sites
launch and maintain programs. In addition, the Innovation Center has been chosen as a
placement location for the “Makers + Mentors Network: Maker Fellows” program that will
connect FLC students with underrepresented and underserved youth to serve as mentors for
Maker-centered learning experiences that will help K-12 students build the academic and social
skills key to their success in school, postsecondary enrollment, and workforce placement. These
unique opportunities will allow the Innovation Center to continue and expand its efforts to bring
“Making” deeper into the community that we serve.

SCC: Sacramento City College has been awarded a five-year, $2.9 million Developing Hispanic
Serving Institutions (DHSI) grant to support our work as an HSI. The federal grant will support an
early college program focused on introducing local Latinx and low-income high school students
to college through participation in dual enrollment. Dual enrollment allows high school students
to enroll in college-level courses and earn college units and high school credit simultaneously.
With dual enrollment programs, some students can earn an Associate Degree or Certificate and
be prepared for employment or transfer to a four-year institution upon graduating from high
school. This shortens a student’s time to degree completion and ultimately allows them to enter
their careers sooner.

Course Deletions


American River College

   1. ECE 356 Programs for the School-Age Child (3.00 units)

      Justification: As part of a comprehensive effort to streamline our course offerings and provide
      direct pathways to employment, this course is being deleted. It is not required for employment
      and many of the topics are covered in other courses. We are beginning work on new courses and
      pathways that will provide the coursework required for those interested in transfer and
      Transitional Kindergarten-6th grade teaching.

   2. ECE 358 Activities for the School-Age Child (3.00 units)

      Justification: In response to changing workforce demands, we are revising our School Age
      Certificate and removing this course. Several course topics are being incorporated in to ECE
      356: Programs for the School-Age Child.

   3. ECE 407 Curriculum and Strategies for Children with Special Needs (4.00 units)

      Justification: This course no longer meets the needs of our students and program.

   4. ECE 455 Environment Rating Scales in Early Childhood Programs (1.00 unit)

      Justification: This course was originally developed and offered through a grant on a rotating

      working in a residential care facility for the elderly (RCFE). These non-medical assisted living

      course does not appear on the Degree/Certificate list, which makes it difficult for students to
      find. 2) Because it is not currently set up as a program the district does not track annual
      certificate completions for this successful career education course like they do for all other
      GERON certificate completions.

Retiree Health Benefits Trust (Trust):

standards issued by the Governmental Accounting Standards Board (GASB), specifically GASB 43
and 45. Wells Fargo Bank, N.A. was appointed trustee and investment manager of the Trust. As
the sponsor and sole contributor of monies to the Trust, the District has primary responsibility
for the management of the Trust for the benefit of its current and future retirees. Under review
by the Oversight Committee, established by the Board, and within investment guidelines and
restrictions of Board Policy P-8252, the Wells Fargo Bank investment manager has invested Trust
monies in various mutual and index funds. As of June 30, 2020, approximately 70% of the $143.0
million portfolio was invested in bond funds and 30.0% in equity funds. The Committee meets
quarterly with the investment manager to review the portfolio. As is good practice, returns are
evaluated against benchmarks for similar investments.

BACKGROUND:

The 2020-21 General fund tentative budget, adopted by the Board of Trustees in June, has been
revised to reflect the enacted State budget. The Board is required to hold a public hearing on the
proposed budget for the fiscal year on or before the 15th day of September. For 2020-21, this
date has been extended by one month. Budget revisions will be brought to the Board as needed,
typically at least twice during the year.

The Governor’s May Revise proposal for California’s Community Colleges was the basis for the
tentative budget. The enacted State budget is substantially similar to the May Revise, but it defers
$1.5 billion in CCC funding to 2021-22. Of the $1.5 billion deferral, up to $791 million would be
rescinded if Congress approves a fourth stimulus package with sufficient funding by October 15,
2020. These deferrals we’re in exchange for the massive cuts proposed for the Student Centered
Funding Formula and categorical programs in the May Revision.

The enacted budget increases the Tentative Budget by approximately $10.6 million under the
Student Centered Funding Formula, which is the hold harmless level. All three budgets project
the formula would yield the same funding as 2019-20. The X budget assumes a 10% decrease in
full-time equivalent students, the Y budget assumes a 7.5% decrease in full-time equivalent
students, and the Z budget assumes a 5% decrease in full-time equivalent students served. While
the decline will not impact our overall apportionment from the state, it will impact the level of
funding we believe will be on-going and will force us to ensure our on-going expenditures match
our projections of on-going revenues.

The District’s fiscal health is strong. The budget is balanced and reserves are in line with board
policy. The District is strategically using funds provided by the federal government (CARES Act)
and state government (COVID-19 Block Grant) to mitigate and offset impacts caused by the
disruption. Additionally, there are sufficient resources in the Capital Outlay Projects Fund that
could be accessed to support operational needs if needed. Further, the District continues to fund
and refine projections to mitigate the impact of increasing employer contributions required by
CalPERS and CalSTRS.
