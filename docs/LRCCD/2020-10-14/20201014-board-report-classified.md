COSUMNES RIVER COLLEGE
Julie Olson, Classified Senate President
Cosumnes River Classified members are knee deep in supporting students, faculty, and
management during this unprecedented Fall semester. Currently, Classified leadership is
hosting open forms with President Bush along with his executive team. The open forms are
only for Classified members and are held every month. It gives the Classified members an
opportunity to address any questions they have for the Executive team.

Classified Equity Institute, phase II is currently in the planning stages for a fully online
experience, unlike the previous Classified Equity Institute held in January where the campus
was closed for all Classified to attend. Phase II of Classified Equity Institute will focus on the
We Won't Fall Campaign How to be an Anti-Racist Institution.

Classified leadership is currently exploring different workshops focusing on self-care during
these troubling times. And lastly, Classified are busy working on Accreditation.
FOLSOM LAKE COLLEGE
Lindsey Campbell, Classified Senate President
Folsom Lake College virtually welcomed back Dr. Frank Harris on Thursday, October 1st and
Friday, October 2nd as a follow up to his Spring Convocation visit. This workshop on Thursday
was focused on Classified professionals and the effects of racial microaggressions that occur
outside the classroom on belonging and success for students of color. The workshop was
well attended by over 60 participants. Thursday, October 8th was the first workshop in a four
week mandatory racial equity and anti-racism training for all FLC Classified. The workshop
series covers topics such as how to talk about race, the different forms of racism, tips on how
to respond to racism, privilege, and intersectionality, with opportunities for open discussion
and dialogue throughout. The planning for the training series was a collaborative process
including Administration, Student Services, Classified Senate and the Classified Professional
Development committee.
