      ARC Academic Senate President, Carina Hoffpauir, Reports:

      officers for 2023-2024 includes President, Carina Hoffpauir; Vice President, Brian Knirk;
      and Secretary, Veronica Lopez. Alisa Shubb has agreed to serve another year as our Past
      President. We appreciate our officers and all our senators for their service to our college
      and our profession.

      Zarate have been selected to join the Center for Teaching & Learning as professional
      development trainers specializing in STEM.
  •   Recent reports and discussions at our Senate have focused on proposed LRCCD policies
      and regulations updates, Areas of Interest realignment with HomeBases, faculty critical
      hire and LTT requests, professional development offerings for next year, Student

    CRC Academic Senate President, Scott Crosier, Reports: (updated Mon
    May 5th)
•   Through the extraordinary work of our faculty and staff, CRC now offers over 50% of our
    courses as Zero Textbook Cost. This has saved the Hawks $2 million this Fall semester
    alone.
•   Through our efforts in the We Won’t Fall campaign as well as our Opt Out Scheduling,
    we have closed several achievement gaps for our African American and Latinx students.
    These efforts will continue and expand.
•   This will be my final Board of Trustee meeting, as Dr. Velasquez will be taking the lead as
    our new Academic Senate President. Be nice to him, and try to keep the meetings short.


    FLC Academic Senate President, Eric Wada, Reports:
•   The FLC Academic Senate has been discussing ways to bring learning communities such
    as Umoja, Puente, and MESA to our college. The Senate views these programs as ways
    to encourage students of color to study in a supportive environment at FLC.
•   Our Senate By-laws are being reviewed and revised this semester.
•   We are welcoming a new Academic Senate Vice President, Wayne Jensen (professor of
    mathematics). We wish to thank Dr. Danielle Beck for serving as Vice President for the
    past year and a half.
•   We are preparing for next year’s Senate retreat where we are looking forward to
    working with other college governance groups and meeting our college administration
    (75% of our top administrators are new in their regular roles this year).

    SCC Academic Senate President, Sandra Guzmán, Reports:

•   The SCC Academic Senate wishes to express our congratulations to the nineteen Faculty
    colleagues who have earned Tenure!
•   Our senate has supported the formation of a Task Group to address the use of Artificial
    Intelligence in the community college classroom and academic integrity.
•   We congratulate all of our students who are earning Certificates, degrees and/or
    transferring to four-year institutions.
•   On behalf of the SCC Academic Senate, we congratulate all faculty who are retiring and
    wish them well in the next stage of their lives.
•   Our summer enrollments show a 19% increase from last year. SCC Senate is excited to
    see students returning to our college and look forward to serving them in the next
    terms.
•   We support the continued investigation of collegial consultation within LRCCD and hope
    to participate in critical dialogue necessary to move us forward.
