LRCFT Report to the Los Rios Board of Trustees   Oct. 18, 2023
The LRCFT is pleased to see that Governor Newsom signed 6 of
the 13 CFT-sponsored and cosponsored bills passed by the
legislature.
At a recent PAFC meeting, the LRCFT endorsed Flojaune Cofer
for mayor, Paula Villescaz for AD 6, and Porsche Middleton for
AD 7. We will take up the Sacramento City Council races next.
Several LRCFT board members attended the CFT State Council
meeting in San Jose last week. The candidates for the US Senate
for California attended and spoke to the membership.
The LRCFT took part in a Job Fair last week sponsored by the
Central Labor Council and Sacramento City Council members
Mai Vang and Caity Maple. We met many current and former
Los Rios students in South Sacramento and answered questions
about education and social services.
The LRCFT at each campus continues to hold membership
meetings to discern the needs of our members.
The LRCFT will take part in the American Federation of
Teacher’s Human, Civil, and Women’s Rights conference in
Houston, Texas, next week.
As part of membership appreciation, we are encouraging our
faculty membership to attend a local theatrical performance on
Oct. 22 at the B St. theater. “Papi, Me, and Cesar Chavez.” We
purchased fifty tickets for the membership.
We are also conducting a membership drive this year and have
signed up 40 new faculty as LRCFT members.
The LRCFT recently held a faculty only town hall meeting to
discuss the recent closure of Davies Hall at American River
College. 70 faculty attended.
The LRCFT will hold elections later this semester for the
college president position.
The LRCFT supports the district moratorium on the use of
human remains in Los Rios and continues to meet with the
district to negotiate the workload impacts.
Thank you!
Jason Newman LRCFT President
