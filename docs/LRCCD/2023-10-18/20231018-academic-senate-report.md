                       Academic Senates’ Reports to the LRCCD Board of Trustees
                                            October 2023



District Academic Senate (DAS) President, Alisa Shubb, Reports:
  •   DAS voted to "Support and honor our Tribal Nations' request to the Moratorium on the
      Use of Human Remains, Native American Cultural Items, Images, and Reproductions of
      Native American Human Remains and Cultural Items under the condition that LRCCD
      engage Academic Senate appointed faculty from each college in any policy or procedure
      impacting curriculum and further, that LRCCD commit necessary resources in an
      expedited manner to provide teaching materials that ensure curriculum can be
      honored."
         o Adoption of the moratorium as written by the Tribal Leaders was scheduled to
              be approved at the September 18, 2023 Chancellor’s Cabinet to go into effect on
              Sept 28th, without consultation with Academic Senate. At the Chancellor's
              Cabinet, DAS leadership requested the opportunity to expedite our processes in
              order to be able to bring back a recommendation.
         o The DAS President would like to acknowledge and thank faculty and Senate
              colleagues for engaging in the thoughtful and difficult discussions necessary to
              support the moratorium language which requires, in some cases, prioritizing
              doing right by our Tribal Nations over Academic Freedom.
         o A district-level Moratorium on Use of Human Remains Advisory Task Force
              charter is being worked on at DAS to ensure the recommendations of faculty
              discipline experts and the DAS are being relied upon in the implementation of
              the moratorium and drafting of new policy/regulations related to the use of such
              items and instructional materials.
  •   DAS is responding to the District Education Technology Committee (DETC)
      recommendation that implementation of student Multifactor Authentication (MFA)
      begin in Jan 2024 with 1) a request that Student Senate be consulted, and 2) concerns
      about the barriers MFA is likely to create for students, particularly when accessing their
      courses and course materials on Canvas. DAS has yet to hear a compelling argument for
      why MFA is needed to access Canvas.
  •   DAS is currently soliciting faculty feedback on proposed revisions to Policy & Regulation
      2222 (attendance).

    FLC Academic Senate President, Eric Wada, Reports:

•   The FLC Academic Senate first acknowledges the success stories we heard from the
    speakers who completed Utility Line Clearance Arborist Training (ULCAT) and pre-
    inspector training. We also acknowledge that the development of these programs
    predates the hiring of our current president and the dean who presented the program
    today. We wish to alert the Board that none of the participatory governance
    committees: the FLC academic senate, curriculum committee, or the district program
    placement council was aware of the ULCAT program’s development. ULCAT is an
    administratively-developed and maintained educational program associated with FLC
    and the LRCCD. We have been told that as a not-for-credit course/program, there is no
    need for faculty involvement; however, we respectfully disagree. Collegial consultation
    on academic and professional matters should have occurred in the program’s
    development, maintenance, and review. Specifically, faculty must be involved in
    curriculum development, degree/certificate requirements, and program review. In
    addition, faculty hiring and evaluation processes are in the purview of the academic
    senate. We raise this lapse in collegial consultation with the Board because this episode
    challenges both the law and accreditation standards. Senate-led processes exist to
    attest to the quality and currency of curriculum, pedagogical practices, and program
    requirements. They also exist to ensure that curriculum, pedagogy, and programs are
    relevant to student goals and are rooted in equity. While we are now past the point to
    authentically engage faculty in the development of the ULCAT program, we want to
    point out that this program exemplifies a pattern of bypassing collegial consultation in
    the district and we want to emphasize the need for collegial consultation in future
    educational program development at FLC.

    SCC Academic Senate President, Sandra Guzmán, Reports:

•    SCC Senate is piloting a new hiring prioritization process with a focus on equity, impact
    to students and demand for courses. We hope the needs of our student community will
    be reflected in our hiring allocation.
•   SCC Senate supported the LRCCD Moratorium and honors the requests of Tribal Nations.
    We look forward to working with LRCCD and DAS appointed Faculty on a task force to
    establish process and priorities.
•   We reviewed the demographics of the seventy-seven Long Term Temporary Faculty
    hired between 2020-2023 and are alarmed at the clear lack of diversity and
    representation. SCC has an interest in reviewing the LTT Faculty guidelines and/or
    processes that yield such results and working to intentionally disrupt the Institutional
    bias/racism embedded in the existing process.
