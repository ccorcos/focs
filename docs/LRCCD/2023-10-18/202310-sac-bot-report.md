                    LRCCD Board of Trustees Student Report

                                     October Report


Report provided by: Mollie Nielson - Student Senate President of Sacramento City College


Student-led activities (i.e. March in March, campus events, fundraisers):

   ● ARC held an event on Constitution Day to educate students about the United
     States Constitution.
   ● FLC hosted a student movie night.
   ● ARC put on two supplementary events: a de-stress event with therapy dogs for
     students and a picnic day in the quad.
   ● CRC and SCC are celebrating the student body with Fall Festivals; CRC provided
     opportunities for clubs to fundraise.
   ● SCC and ARC hosted club rushes, where they significantly increased participation
     in student clubs.

Senate and Clubs & Events Board Accomplishments (i.e. surveys, engagement in campus
and regional governance):

   ● CRC got involved in the Universal Transit Pass election. They spread awareness
      about universal transit by presenting in classes, providing flyers around campus,
      and posting on social media. SCC and ARC also got involved in marketing the
      election.
   ● CRC tabling has brought the number of chartered clubs to 18.
   ● SCC and FLC purchased uniforms for their associated student bodies.
   ● SCC is working on a recruitment event to increase student engagement in district
      committees.
   ● SCC and FLC are working hard to compile topics and prepare for lobby day.
Leadership Development Accomplishments (i.e. conferences, trainings, workshops, guest
speak
   ● All four colleges are preparing for the California Community College Student Affairs
      Association conference.
   ● CRC’s student senate is collaborating with the office of the president to host a
      safety event in the Spring.
   ● CRC and ARC are holding training events for their Associated Student Bodies.
   ● The VP of FLC attended and spoke at one of their senate meetings. The associated
