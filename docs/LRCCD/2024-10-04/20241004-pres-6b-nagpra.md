NAGPRA / CalNAGPRA
Los Rios is subject to federal NAGPRA and CalNAGPRA
because we have received state or federal funds in the past
which means we are legally designated a ‘museum.’
The Los Rios audit of FLC, CRC, ARC, and SCC uncovered
human remains, unassociated funerary objects, and objects of

Why does Los Rios have remains of Native
Americans, funerary objects, and cultural objects?
    Source 1: From the 1930’s to the 1950’s J.B. Lillard initiated an
    excavation program throughout much of the Central Valley to train
    students in archaeology, poor documentation of individuals and
    cultural objects means information about what was excavated was
    lost and they became alienated from their origins.
    Source 2: Before the passage of NAGPRA in 1990 several people
    donated cultural items and some Native American ancestors of
    unclear origin to SCC because they felt a college would be a better
    place for stewardship than private ownership.
    No Native American ancestors or objects were collected by current
    faculty at any Los Rios institution.

There are two major steps towards repatriation
 1. Completing ‘summaries.’ Summaries are for cultural items,
 such as unassociated funerary objects and objects of cultural
 patrimony. Several tribes through consultation have made
 requests for repatriation and we are working towards that goal.
 2. Completing ‘inventories.’ Inventories are for human remains
 and associated funerary objects. Some inventories have been
 published in the federal register (which describe the context
 where individuals are from), and we are working towards
 complete repatriation of human remains.
 These requirements exist so Native American tribes and lineal
 descendants can be made aware we may have ancestors and
 cultural items.

 NAGPRA compliance, where are we?
We are actively consulting with the Sacramento Tribes working group to
expedite the repatriation of Native American ancestors and cultural items
to their Indigenous nations.
Los Rios will be compliant with all ‘summary’ requirements by the end of
the year 2024 and continue to work towards consultation and repatriation
of cultural items in its control.
To date we have repatriated 4 Native American Ancestors from ARC and
SCC and are finalizing the repatriation of 9 funerary objects. This process
will speed up over the next year.
We have also returned 6 cultural items subject to NAGPRA that were on
loan from California State parks.
We are on track to be fully compliant with NAGPRA requirements by
January 2026 and will work towards repatriation within legally defined
timelines.
