LRCFT report to the LRCCD Board of Trustees            Dec. 17, 2025
The LRCFT continues to prepare for negotiations with the district for
early next year. In the past month, we held 8 in person and virtual
negotiations forums with over 500 faculty. We submitted our
sunshine list to the District and look forward to starting formal
negotiations on January 23.
The LRCFT recently held our annual holiday party for all faculty
union members. Over one hundred faculty and family members
attended the event.
The LRCFT held internal elections for college union pres. at ARC. I
want to congratulate EOPS counselor Art Jenkins who will continue
as president for another two-year term. Rebecca Goodchild,
librarian, will also start as union president at SCC. They join Teresa
Aldredge (CRC) and Kalinda Jones (FLC) to begin two-year terms.
The LRCFT on MLK Jr. Day will participate in two marches along with
students, faculty, and community members. We are also funding 20
students to attend an MLK Day breakfast celebration.
In addition to ﬁnancial contributions provided last month to the Los
Rios student food pantries, the LRCFT contributed to the SCLC’s
Operation X mas basket for local union members in ﬁnancial need.
The LRCFT continues to assist faculty with information regarding
public loan forgiveness and part-time faculty unemployment. Both
the CFT and AFT provide programs in these areas to help faculty.
The LRCFT continues to work with CFT and community college
unions statewide to plan the student March in March on March 12 at
the Capitol. Local elected oZicials and student and union leaders
will speak at the event which promises to be one the largest student
marches in recent memory.
The LRCFT continues to plan for CFT’s statewide One Faculty Week
scheduled for April to showcase the common employment interests
of full and part time faculty.
The LRCFT and CFT are collaborating with other unions and FACCC
in the next few months to gather 1.3 million signatures to renew and
make permanent the current Millionaire’s Tax that funds public
education. This measure passed as Prop. 30 in 2012 and was
renewed as Prop. 55 in 2016.

traZic control program. Recently, the program was featured

I would also like to congratulate Napa Valley College
Superintendent/Pres. Dr. Torence Powell on his new role as LRCCD
Chancellor eZective July 1. We look forward to working with Dr.

In closing, I would like to acknowledge the unfortunate passing on
December 1 of long-time Cosumnes River College nurse and LRCFT
exec. board member Reona James. Reona worked as a nurse at CRC
for thirty years and provided a guiding inﬂuence for our union during
its early development. She was personally inﬂuential to many of us
and convinced me to run for union president at CRC in 2010. Our
exec. board has passed a resolution in her honor and I have
submitted the resolution as part of my report. Reona will be dearly
missed.

                                Resolution Honoring Reona L. James

WHEREAS, Reona L. James dedicated her professional life to serving students, faculty, and staff within
the Los Rios Community College District with compassion, integrity, and commitment; and

WHEREAS, Reona joined the Los Rios College Federation of Teachers (LRCFT) Executive Board
in Fall 1989 and served the union for decades as a respected leader and advocate; and

WHEREAS, in her role as Grievance Chair, Reona was known for her fairness, sound judgment, and
steadfast dedication to protecting faculty rights and upholding due process; and

WHEREAS, Reona also served for 30 years as a faculty member and college nurse at Cosumnes
River College, caring for the campus community until her retirement in 2010; and

WHEREAS, Reona L. James passed away on December 1, 2025, leaving a lasting legacy of service,
leadership, and compassion;

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, that the Los Rios College Federation of Teachers honors
and remembers Reona L. James with deep gratitude for her many years of service to the LRCFT,
Cosumnes River College, and the Los Rios community; and

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that this resolution be entered into the official record and shared with
her family as a lasting expression of respect and appreciation.

Jason Newman, President,
Los Rios College Federation of Teachers

Teresa Aldredge, Campus President
