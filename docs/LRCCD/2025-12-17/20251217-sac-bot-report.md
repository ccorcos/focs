                                 Student Advisory Committee

              Student Senates’ Reports to the LRCCD Board of Trustees

                                           December 2025

Report presented by Soraya Amin, American River College Student Senate President

 Student-Led Activities:
    •   American River College (ARC) hosted its Winter Wonderland Club Fundraiser and a two-day Cookie
        Decorating event on December 9-10 and passed Bill F25-14 “Warming Your Winter,” a student-
        driven initiative aimed at increasing participation in shared governance at the college and across
        the district.
    •   Cosumnes River College (CRC) collaborated with APIDA Hawks on Hmong cultural events and is
        preparing Stress Relief Days in December and the Giving Tree gift-wrapping service project on
        December 12.
    •   Folsom Lake College (FLC) held Movie Night on December 4 in partnership with clubs and hosted
        multiple Town Halls to gather student concerns.
    •   Sacramento City College (SCC) hosted major events such as a Toy Drive that collected over 130 toys
        for CalWORKs and basic need families (October 13-November 14), the Gratitude Gobble
        community-building event on November 19, and “A Very Hughie Holiday” on December 3.

 Senate and Clubs & Events Board Accomplishments:
    •   ARC created a Technology Committee to collaborate with campus IT and partnered with a Pacific
        Dining on a food accessibility survey addressing vending, food options, and dietary concerns. The
        Senate met with Financial Aid regarding impacts of their proposed stipends resolution and
        presented the launch of a new student government newsletter. ARC also fully funded Model UN
        Club’s participation in the 2026 Model UN Far West Conference (April 17-21, 2026) and hosted a
        Chancellor Candidate Forum watch party on December 8.
    •   CRC now has 16 students serving on campus and district participatory governance committees and
        has chartered 25 clubs. The Student Senate President continues regular coordination with the
        College President.
    •   FLC conducted a collegewide survey and gathered feedback to guide Senate priorities. All three
        Senate presidents served as judges for the first annual Innovation Center Cookie Competition on
        December 10, where student clubs won two of the three categories.
    •   SCC continued rebuilding its student government infrastructure, appointing new Senators and a
        Historian, reestablishing a fully formed Accountability Panel for the first time in five years, and
        creating both a Student Affairs Committee and a Resolution Committee. The Joint Budget
        Committee approved two $600 scholarships supporting undocumented students, and CAEB
        finalized club incentives and began preparing a spring Distress Fest.
 Leadership Development Accomplishments:
     •   ARC passed its Governmental Stipends Resolution, funded participation in the SSCCC General
         Assembly (March 27-29, 2026), and held a resolution-writing workshop led by its SSCCC delegate
         on December 5.
     •   CRC will hold a joint Senate/CAEB retreat on December 19, featuring training from parliamentarian
         Tami Dunning, and received a presentation from Vitalant on December 5 about future blood drive
         partnerships.
     •   FLC sent students to the SSCCC Advocacy Academy in Riverside (December 5-6), received findings
         from the Hotep Consultants’ Student Equity Study, and heard updates from the Equity Center on
         affinity program changes.
     •   ARC, CRC, and SCC each hosted Delta College leaders for inter-campus leadership tours, and SCC's
         Secretary of Legislative Affairs attended the SSCCC Advocacy Academy.
