                     Academic Senates’ Reports to the LRCCD Board of Trustees, July 2022



        District Academic Senate (DAS) President, Alisa Shubb, Reports:

    •   The District Ethnic Studies Council has been hard at work addressing serious inequities at the
        CCCCO level with respect to curricula recently submitted for Area F approval. At Council
        recommendation, ETHNS 300, 320, 330, 340 and 350 curricula were developed together - word
        for word the same across all 4 Los Rios colleges - and submitted in response to the CSU's
        reviewer's recommendations. Based on this uniform practice, approval and/or denial should be
        consistent, however, ARC's 300 was approved and SCC's 320, 330, 340 and 350 were approved
        while the same courses were denied at CRC and FLC. ARC was also denied 320, 330, 340 and
        350. The Council is in the process of asking the CSU Chancellor's Office to reconsider all denials
        of our courses prior to resubmitting the revisions. Without such reconsideration the result for
        Fall 2022 will be:
             o CRC will have no Ethnic Studies classes that will count towards Area F.
             o SCC won't have it's primary ETHNS 300 class approved for Area F but will have 4 other
                courses.
             o ARC and FLC will have the ETHNS 300 class approved but no others.

    •   PREP (Prison Reentry Education Program) - the District Academic Senate’s newest standing
        committee has been hard at work 1) addressing the program’s need for faculty coordinators,
        and 2) creating a job description for PREP Library Lead to address the CRC Rising Scholars Grant.
        This position is open to all Los Rios Librarians and supports the Los Rios 2016-2021 Strategic Plan
        Goals, specifically, Goal 1: Establish effective pathways that optimize student access and
        success, and Goal 2: Ensure equitable academic achievement across all racial, ethnic,
        socioeconomic, and gender groups.
