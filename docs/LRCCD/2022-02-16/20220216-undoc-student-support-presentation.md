                     Mission & Goals
LRCCD Dreamer Centers
The mission of the Dreamer Centers is to assist undocumented students and
students from mixed-status families achieve their academic goals. The Dreamer
Centers provide services, resources and support to address the unique barriers
students face in their pursuit of higher education.
Goals
● Increase successful enrollment, persistence, graduation and transfer rates of
  undocumented students and students of mixed-status families.
● Cultivate a campus culture that acknowledges, and integrates the nuanced
  experiences of undocumented students and students of mixed-status families
  within their department and processes.

                 Student Support
Undocumented Student Staff Provide Students:
1. Assistance with:
    a.    AB540 Form (California Nonresident Tuition Exemption Form)
    b.    Application Processing (to obtain LRCCD ID)
    c.    California Dream Act Application (CADAA)
    d.    LRCCD Application
    e.    LRCCD program & counselor referrals
     f.   Help with how to become a student at LRCCD campuses
    g.    Residency
    h.    Connection to Campus Resources
     i.   Researching scholarships and financial aid
2. Information on Immigration legal services resources and referrals
3. Information about Local Resources for students and their families

• Love of our communities, students, and
  colleagues
• Protecting the rights of students, staff, and
  faculty
• Investing in changing campus climates that
  are unwelcoming and at times hostile to
  undocumented communities

Don’t be a dream crusher, be a hope
dealer
• Too often we tell our undocumented
  students what they ”can’t do” or “should
  not do”
• We fail to communicate the possibilities or
  work for the creation of opportunities
• Undocumented communities are resilient
  and resourceful.
