                    Academic Senates’ Reports to the LRCCD Board of Trustees
                                        November 2022



District Academic Senate (DAS) President, Alisa Shubb, Reports:
  •   DAS is discussing strategies for operationalizing the mandatory equity training LRCFT is
      currently working to negotiate into our contract - we have agreed to refer to this
      training as Equity-Minded Professional Learning.
  •   DAS is reviewing a proposal to charter a faculty-led workgroup charged with exploring
      potential benefits, drawbacks, and barriers to creating and offering noncredit courses
      and programs in Los Rios using a student-centered, equity minded framework.
  •   DAS is examining possible improvements to our Equivalency Committee processes and
      Regulations.
  •   DAS leadership attended ASCCC Fall Plenary “Centering Authentic Voices and Lived
      Experiences in the 10+1”. Some of the topics addressed were:
          o AB 928 - requiring the establishment of a single lower division GE pathway for
              CCC, CSU & UC. ASCCC voted to endorse the CalGETC pattern and urge that CSU
              change the “Lifelong Learning & Self-Development” area to serve as a CSU
              graduation requirement (instead of restricting it to a CSU upper division GE
              requirement. Discussions about the loss of Lifelong Learning in the proposed
              CalGETC focus on the ramifications for students and programs.
          o Strategies to ensure that a focus on all students, both “academic and
              vocational” remains as per the California Community College mission (section
              66010.4 of the California Education Code) even thoughState policies since 2010
              have mostly focused resources on transforming transfer in California community
              colleges.
          o ASCCC is strongly urging local Senates to review their policies on Academic
              Freedom. We are gratified to work in a District with strong Board policy (P-7142)
              that specifically endorses Academic Freedom, including such strong statements
              as it is the “responsibility of the college instructor to present and encourage
              discussion of controversial material where appropriate to the class subject
              matter”.



      ARC Academic Senate President, Carina Hoffpauir, Reports:
  •   Recent topics at ARC’s Academic Senate have included discussion of our Student Equity
      and Achievement Plan for 2022-2025, additional expansion of our Open Educational
      Resources (OER) program, and proposed revisions to the regulations regarding Dual
      Enrollment. We are also actively working with our VPI on a collaborative process for
      assessing critical hire requests for faculty hiring.

    CRC Academic Senate President, Scott Crosier, Reports:
•   CRC continues to take great leaps into providing learning materials to students as a
    reduced or Zero-Textbook cost. Nearly half of all full-time faculty are participating and
    we are working to support our part time faculty in the same efforts.
•   We are continually concerned that the materials provided to our students meet or
    exceed accessibility requirements. We are working on strategies to ensure this through
    faculty workshops as well as acquiring the needed support.
•   One item missing this year from our traditional Fall calendar was our faculty hiring
    prioritization. After discussions with the district leadership suggesting that each campus
    would be hiring zero, or up to one faculty member, we did not conduct the campus-
    wide prioritization process.


    FLC Academic Senate President, Eric Wada, Reports:
•   Implementing dual enrollment with fidelity to equity, student success, and academic
    freedom remain interests of the FLC Academic Senate.
•   We are reviewing our SEA plan in advance of the Nov 30 submission deadline.
•   We are continuing discussions on district equivalency processes, and note the
    importance of discipline faculty expertise primacy in equivalency discussions.
•   We are discussing the Los Rios Ethnic Studies Faculty Council’s request for increased full-
    time faculty to support student needs for Ethnic Studies curriculum.
•   District-level decision making on full-time faculty, long-term temporary, and critical
    hiring allocations is an ongoing discussion item that has generated questions from the
    Senate.
•   We are thankful for the collaboration with local college administration, facilities
    management, IT, and AV to help host a couple of ASCCC sponsored or co-sponsored
    events at FLC including the Intersegmental Committee of Academic Senates back in
    October, and the upcoming OERI LibreTexts training on November 18th.

        SCC Academic Senate President, Sandra Guzmán, Reports:

    •   Our Senate met with Chancellor King regarding the SCC White Paper. This provided an
        opportunity for Faculty to inquire further about the LRCCD response and actions.
    •   Our Senate completed a Hiring Prioritization process for full-time faculty positions that
        meet the district criteria of needed for accreditation and/or for program viability. There
        were seven positions sent forward and ranked by Senators. The rankings are aligned
        with the district’s guidance.
    •   Senate leadership attended the ASCCC Fall Plenary with other District Senate leaders.
    •   The Student Equity Plan was reviewed in Senate meetings with robust conversations
        and contributions.
    •   SCC wishes President Melanie Dixon well and expresses sincere gratitude for her
        contributions as SCC’s interim Vice President of Student Services. Under her leadership,
        student programs and centers were created. These include the ASHE and Raza Center
        that intentionally serves our black and brown student community. We appreciate her
        support and thank her on behalf of our students as well.
