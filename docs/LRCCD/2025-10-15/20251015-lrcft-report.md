LRCFT report to the LRCCD Board of Trustees            October 15, 2025

The LRCFT this semester is conducting internal officer elections for
campus union president at the four Los Rios colleges.
Recently at a statewide CFT conference, our membership listened in
person and via webinar to candidate interviews for CA. State
Superintendent of Public Instruction. We will participate in a similar
CFT event on Oct. 23 for the state gubernatorial candidates.
Meanwhile, we continue along with 90 other unions in Sacramento
to canvas locally and statewide in support of Proposition 50.
This Saturday, we will continue our public activism locally by
participating in the No Kings rally in Sacramento.
Our on-going LRCFT membership drive continues next week with a
focus on FLC. We will reach out to faculty on all 4 of the campuses
this semester.
Also happening next week, the LRCFT will hold a One Faculty Party
at the CFT union hall in Sacramento to call attention to the shared
interests of both full and part time faculty.
On Thanksgiving day, the LRCFT will field a union team of 25
members to participate in the Run to Feed Hungry Race.
In closing, this past Friday, the LRCFT held a two-hour information
workshop to help faculty understand their options for insurance
plans in Los Rios. Faculty continue to express concerns to us about
the rising cost of health care in our district. The LRCFT looks forward
to working with the district to find more creative solutions to what is
becoming a major crisis statewide for education workers.
