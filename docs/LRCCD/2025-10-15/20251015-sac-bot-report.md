                                  Student Advisory Committee

              Student Senates’ Reports to the LRCCD Board of Trustees

                                            October 2025

Report presented by Keira Moeckly, Cosumnes River College Student Senate Designee

Student-Led Activities:
    •   Student-led events were held across the district to promote engagement and community building:
            o American River College hosted the Karaoke Midterm Mic Drop on October 23 and is
               preparing for the Campus Life Tea Party Workshop in November.
            o Cosumnes River College assisted with Voter Registration Day and is organizing Fall Club
               Rush at the end of October.
            o Folsom Lake College hosted Movie Night: K-Pop Demon Hunters and initiated new
               collaborations with the Innovation and Welcome Center.
            o Sacramento City College held Club Day, Constitution Day, and Voter Registration Day
               celebrations, along with Raza Bienvenida and HSI Tardeada events, engaging more than
               230 participants.

Senate and Clubs & Events Board Accomplishments:
   • Students across all colleges were appointed to district and campus committees, including faculty
        hiring committees, supporting shared governance and student representation.
    •   Club development continues to grow districtwide:
             o American River College hosted its first Club Day with 45 active clubs and is planning an
                 event for Homecoming on October 28.
             o Cosumnes River College chartered 19 clubs and finalized top advocacy topics for Lobby
                 Day.
             o Folsom Lake College hosted Club Rush and established subcommittees for Lobby Day
                 preparation, student grievances, and Town Halls.
    •   Leadership and advocacy initiatives include:
             o Folsom Lake College worked with campus administration on water filter replacement and
                 passed a resolution supporting extended library hours.
             o Cosumnes River College’s Student Senate President serves as the SSCCC Region II delegate,
                 and Sacramento City College hosted a guest speaker from SSCCC Region II to provide an
                 overview of student advocacy work.

Leadership Development Accomplishments:
   • Colleges held retreats, workshops, and training to onboard and develop student leaders.
   • Students from all four colleges will attend the CCCSAA Student Leadership Conference on October
        17-19 in Santa Clara, California.
     •   American River College hosted presentations on Homebase updates and library services.

         Dean of Institutional Effectiveness Research and Planning.
     •   Sacramento City College conducted Super Tuesday and Fifth Tuesday leadership training covering
         governance, planning, and student engagement practices.
