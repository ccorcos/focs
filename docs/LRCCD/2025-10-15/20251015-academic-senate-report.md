   Academic Senates’ Reports to the LRCCD Board of Trustees
                      October 15, 2025

District Academic Senate (DAS) President, Paula Cardwell, Reports:
   ●​ We recommended changes to Board Policy 7421 relating to Work Experience
       Education; these proposed changes would update policy to match the recently
       changed regulation.
   ●​ Our AI Task Force has drafted a framework document. That document is being
       shared with campus AI groups, where available, and will likely be returning to
       District Academic Senate for vetting with the colleges and possible adoption.
   ●​ Our Hiring Manual Revision Task Force is meeting regularly and often and
       making great progress on our revision of the hiring manual.
   ●​ Academic Probation Language and Noncredit task forces are nearly fully
       appointed and getting underway.
   ●​ We are researching possible changes/recommendations for attendance policy
       R-2222, as well as our counseling to student ratio.
   ●​ The Los Rios AI Summit is taking place Friday, Nov. 14 from 9-1 at FLC; Sadie
       St. Lawrence of the Human-Machine Collaboration Initiative, and founder of
       Women in Data, will present our keynote address. Our own amazing students,
       faculty and several industry partners, including Google, Intel, and Nectir AI, will
       be presenting workshops at the Summit. We hope that representatives from our
       Board will be able to attend.

American River College Senate President, Veronica Lopez, Reports:
  ●​ Faculty working diligently preparing for our faculty prioritization process.
  ●​ Faculty are also carefully analyzing the hiring data that was presented at the
     October Board Retreat.
  ●​ Academic Senate is currently considering whether to pilot Cluster Hiring for
     another year.
  ●​ Academic Senate recently approved the 2025-2028 Student Equity Plan (SEP)
     application with modifications.
  ●​ Faculty continue to diligently work on removing barriers for students trying to
     enroll in courses with linked support courses. A solution will require a
     collaborative approach with District IT.
