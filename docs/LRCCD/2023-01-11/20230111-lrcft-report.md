LRCFT Report to the Board of Trustees                January 11, 2023
The LRCFT is looking forward to entering formal negotiations with the
District this semester. The sunshine documents submitted by District and
LRCFT indicate an appropriate full range of interests to negotiate in our IBA
process.
After a full semester of partnering with LRCCD, the LRCFT is very excited
to see part time faculty in our District receive health care on the same
terms as full-time faculty. Once again, we thank VC Mario Rodriguez and
AVC Carrie Bray for making this happen.
We also want to congratulate both faculty and District regarding the Folsom
Lake College prison reentry program receiving a million-dollar grant from
Congress.
The LRCFT begins this semester with five new representatives and a new
treasurer elected by our members. We have also hired Michael Henderson
as our new Executive Director. He was a history lecturer at CSU while
serving as a union staffer for the California Faculty Association for several
years. He replaces Robert Perrone, who served as our ED for the past 32
years.
We are happy to see that the UC academic worker strike has been
resolved. We anticipate that a similar scenario may arise for the CSU
faculty later this year.
The LRCFT has formed a PTFIC to assist with part time faculty items to
negotiate this semester.
The LRCFT will be holding a two-day retreat in late January for our new
board.
We are also exploring this semester how our union can further assist
transgender faculty.
In closing, we want to congratulate Art Pimentel in his new position as
President of FLC.
