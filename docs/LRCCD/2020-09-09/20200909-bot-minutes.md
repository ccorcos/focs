Chancellor’s Report:

ARC: ARC student Marya Wozniuck is a winner of the Sherwin-Williams 2020 Student Design
Challenge, receiving a $2,500 cash prize as a result of her award-winning work. An Interior Design
major at ARC, Marya received the 1st place award in residential design with her eclectic New York
penthouse. She was competing against top students from collegiate programs across the country.
ARC's Interior Design program also received a $1,000 contribution as a result of Marya's success.
The Sherwin-Williams Student Design Challenge is a professional competition that celebrates

info at http://www.swstudentdesign.com/2020/eclectic-nyc-penthouse/.

CRC: CRC will kick off celebrations for its 50th anniversary next Monday, Sept. 14, which was the
opening day of the college back in 1970. This year’s celebrations will be via videos, including an
opening statement by president Ed Bush and the opening of a time capsule, which was buried on
the 25th anniversary. Interviews of former presidents, administrators, faculty and students were
conducted over the last year to prepare for these celebrations. The CRC Theatre Department
recently took home a number of Elly Awards for the productions, "Servant of Two Masters," and
"Getting Out." The annual Elly Awards honor outstanding achievements of community theaters
and artists in the Sacramento area. Elly Awards are named for the late Eleanor McClatchy, who
was a devoted patron of the local arts. The most notable is the Outstanding Achievement Award
for Overall Production of “Getting Out.” Other Outstanding Achievement awards were given for
costume design, direction, leading and supporting female and others.

FLC: Folsom Lake College’s Innovation Center continues to be recognized as a leader in the Maker
movement and was recently accepted into two prestigious national programs. “Making Spaces:
Expanding Maker Education Across the Nation” aims to sustainably integrate Making into
educational institutions and build a national resource network. As a member of the program’s
new cohort, FLC’s makerspace will lead a regional partnership with local schools, libraries,
community organizations, and other educational institutions to help these new Maker sites
launch and maintain programs. In addition, the Innovation Center has been chosen as a
placement location for the “Makers + Mentors Network: Maker Fellows” program that will
connect FLC students with underrepresented and underserved youth to serve as mentors for
Maker-centered learning experiences that will help K-12 students build the academic and social
skills key to their success in school, postsecondary enrollment, and workforce placement. These
unique opportunities will allow the Innovation Center to continue and expand its efforts to bring
“Making” deeper into the community that we serve.

SCC: Sacramento City College has been awarded a five-year, $2.9 million Developing Hispanic
Serving Institutions (DHSI) grant to support our work as an HSI. The federal grant will support an
early college program focused on introducing local Latinx and low-income high school students
to college through participation in dual enrollment. Dual enrollment allows high school students
to enroll in college-level courses and earn college units and high school credit simultaneously.
With dual enrollment programs, some students can earn an Associate Degree or Certificate and
be prepared for employment or transfer to a four-year institution upon graduating from high
school. This shortens a student’s time to degree completion and ultimately allows them to enter
their careers sooner.
