Chancellor’s Report:

ARC: ARC alum Sean Groark has been honored with the 2020 Coast Guard Aeronautical Engineering Officer
of the Year Award. This annual award, presented by the Assistant Commandant for Engineering and
Logistics and sponsored by the Coast Guard Aviation Association, recognizes individual accomplishments
and achievements within the Aeronautical Engineering Program. Sean, who is a Lieutenant Commander
with the Coast Guard, is serving as the Air Station Clearwater Assistant Aeronautical Engineering Officer.
During Hurricane Dorian he established an international base of operations supporting Coast Guard
response. Stepping up to serve as the acting Engineering Officer for 109 days, he faced unprecedented
challenges of the COVID-19 pandemic. Due to Department of Defense COVID-19 policy, Sean rapidly
coordinated the evacuation and relocation of the Atlantic Undersea Test and Evaluation Center to Exuma
Island, enabling continuity of operations and sustaining international partnerships. Sean was an ARC
student athlete 2001-03 (swimming); his senior year at Sac State he returned to ARC to serve as an
assistant coach.

CRC: The CRC Music Department found a unique way to serve students during the fall semester, which
proved very successful. So much so, that they will continue it this spring semester. The music department
created the “In the Studio,” online weekly series, which featured innovative artists pushing the boundaries
in their fields. The series also highlighted student voices through a series of open mics and student
ensemble performances. Performers from New York to Philadelphia to Vancouver to Sacramento
participated in the series, bringing world-class artists to the CRC Community to talk about their craft. The
unique series got the attention of local media as well and two of CRC’s music professors were interviewed
on Good Morning Sacramento and KCRA. The music department plans to do another series this spring
semester.

FLC: Folsom Lake College’s Emergency Medical Technology (EMT) program recently received approval as
an official EMT Training Program by the El Dorado County Office of Emergency Medical Services. This
college-community collaboration brings several benefits, including enhanced utilization of the Los Rios
eLearning opportunities, patient simulation assets, and use of the region's Mobile Sim Lab. Thank you to

SCC: Former Panther Roshaun Davis was honored as one of Sacramento Business Journal’s 40 Under 40
for his work as the founder and CEO of Unseen Heroes, an event marketing and management company.
While an emcee for hip-hop group Righteous Movement, Roshaun realized that there weren’t any artist
management agencies representing or hiring people of color, like him, so he learned how to do it himself.
As he delved into the industry he realized this was not unique to the Sacramento music industry and in
2008 launched Unseen Heroes to be able to create unforgettable moments by bringing together local
businesses, entrepreneurs, designers, artists, makers, farmers, and chefs.
