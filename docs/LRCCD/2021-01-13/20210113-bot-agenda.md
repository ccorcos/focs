ARC: ARC students Natalie Loo (track and field) and Duquan Broadnax (football) were recently
honored by the California Community College Counselors/Advisors Academic Association for
Athletics as recipients of the Student Athlete Achievement Award. This is the Association’s most
prestigious award, recognizing student athletes who have overcome significant personal and/or
family challenges and have succeeded in spite of all obstacles. Here are inspiring comments from
both students: Natalie: "I walked through a valley that was filled with challenges, but I am no
longer fearful. I am climbing a mountain, I am going to stumble, but I now have nothing to fear."
Duquan: "For me, life is more about what you put into it rather than what you take out of it. My
life will fully consist of trying to make this world a better place."

CRC: A big shout out to the five Health Information Tech students who passed their RHIT exam
for this reporting period! Program Director Kristen Borth said they have been working very hard
to make sure HIT students are getting the very best information to be able to pass their national
exam, and move onto a successful career. The faculty have revamped the program recently,
updating and improving course content.

FLC: Folsom Lake College student Shreya Gowda has been selected to participate in the NASA
Community College Aerospace Scholars program. This national program for community college
students who have an interest and aptitude for mathematics, science, engineering, or computer
science seeks to develop students’ talents, interests, and passions to become future STEM
professionals. Shreya, a computer science major, will complete a five-week interactive online
course, including virtually attending a NASA speaker series, and completing a final project. Top
performers will then be invited to a NASA on-site experience, a four-day engineering design
workshop at a NASA center. Shreya is the second FLC student selected for this prestigious
program, following Abraham Iniguez in 2019.

SCC: President Gutierrez presented Sacramento City College’s Caring Campus work to more than
450 participants at an IEBC webinar on Dec. 10. SCC’s VPA and more than 50 classified
professionals worked together on the Caring Campus initiative during the summer of 2020 to
develop action-based behavioral commitments to improve student experience and engagement.
This work will reinforce and support the college’s City Ways efforts.
