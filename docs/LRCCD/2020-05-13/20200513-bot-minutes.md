Chancellor’s Report:

ARC: ARC Funeral Service Education students Ally Pawelczyk and Benjamin Cardinelli are
volunteering in New York City, where more than 10,000 people have been killed by COVID-19.
Ally and Benjamin will be spending two weeks there helping funeral homes flooded by the surge
in deaths. The two learned of a call for assistance from the National Funeral Directors Association
and they quickly stepped forward. After volunteering for two weeks, Ally and Benjamin will have
to quarantine there for another two weeks. Then, participants are given the option to volunteer
again — and Ally and Benjamin are considering that opportunity.

CRC: Former CRC MESA students Riana Menezes was very recently offered a full-time position as
an Aerospace Structural Analysis Engineer with Kratos Defense in Roseville. She has a BS in
Aerospace Structural Engineering from UC San Diego. Her identical twin sister, Tiana, has been

working as an Associate CAD (Computer Aided Design) Engineer with Lyft since early March and
has been working on autonomous vehicle technology. She also has a BS in Aerospace Structural
Engineering from UCSD.

FLC: Folsom Lake College IT Systems/Database Administration Analyst II Jim Cosentino is
commended for his solution-based approach to supporting online learning in his role as the
NETLAB+ Administrator for the North/Far North Regional Consortium. As part of California’s
Strong Workforce Program, North/Far North provides leadership in the creation and
implementation of career education programs in the region’s fifteen community colleges.
NETLABs allow students and instructors to access specialized networking equipment, server
operating systems, and software from off-campus. Jim is responsible for the installation and
maintenance of the complex system which contains more than 1,400 virtual computers. To
increase opportunities for continuing student learning while campuses are closed due to the
pandemic, Jim and the Consortium are making its NETLAB Virtual Networking Environment
available to college professors, middle and high school teachers, and their students throughout
the region.

SCC: SCC recently received $4,545 from the Foundation for California Community Colleges’ First
Response Fund to support our nursing students with emergency grants to help support the
people who will be our future medical workforce, particularly during the pandemic. By combining
the funding with our Hulda Mae Stone endowment ($7,350), we were able to award $350 each
to all 34 RN and VN final semester students who applied, to help them pay for their testing and
license registration (or other emergency needs, if they have more pressing priorities). We are
pleased to be able to support our students and our community by helping to add vital medical
personnel to the workforce!
