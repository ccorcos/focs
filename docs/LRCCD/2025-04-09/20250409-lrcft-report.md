LRCFT Report to the LRCCD Board of Trustees:                  April 8, 2025

During our recent spring break, 15 LRCFT union members attended the
annual CFT convention in San Diego. One highlight of the convention
included a massive protest by over 1,000 teachers in front of a downtown
federal courthouse. We also attended several workshops that focused on
immigration policy, building our membership base, and negotiating
contracts with our District partners.
The LRCFT also took part in the Cesar Chavez March in Sacramento on
March 29th. Hundreds of students and community members gathered and
marched to the Capitol to celebrate the Chavez legacy.
The CFT held its annual Legislative Lobby day at the Capitol today, April 9.
We met with many local Assemblymembers and Senators to provide an
overview of key legislation to improve the educational experience of
community college students and the working conditions of faculty and
classified professionals statewide. CFT sponsored legislation includes
several bills to accomplish a range of improvements. These include bills to
expand the Part Time Faculty Office Hour program to provide more office
hours for student access; a bill to expand the College Promise grant
program to include part time students; a bill to enable undocumented
students to work on college campuses; and a bill to establish due process
protections for part time faculty.
The LRCFT picnic this year will take place on Thursday, May 1 but in a
different format and venue than in previous years. We have rented Oak
Park Brewery in Sacramento from 4 to 8 pm with food and drinks provided
by the brewery. We have also invited the classified professional unions---
LRCEA and SEIU 1021---to join us for a joint union celebration of the hard
work by our collective membership in serving and teaching students in Los
Rios.
In May, a dozen members of our Executive Board will engage in
negotiations training in preparation for negotiations with the District in early
2026. In June we will also send another contingent of new board members
to CFT’s Union Summer school training program in Los Angeles.
The LRCFT is in the planning stages for a Faculty Health and insurance
benefits conference scheduled for late August. In the past, we have
partnered with FACCC in providing this conference and will do so again this
year.
The LRCFT continues to support the efforts of faculty on the campuses to
expand student success by funding Math Dept. Integration Bees and
student affinity-based graduations at the end of this semester.
We will also circulate in April another issue of the LRCFT Union News that
showcases faculty accomplishments on the four campuses.
The LRCFT’s Social Justice Caucus (SJC) is donating $3000 in gift cards
to support students in the Re-emerging Scholars Program at each campus.
Funds for this donation come from a special fundraiser hosted by the
Caucus in support of students. Each campus's Re-emerging Scholars
Program faculty coordinator will receive $750 in visa gift cards to distribute
to students. We trust that our faculty know their students best and will
make sure that the funds go to students who can use a little extra support.
Thank you!
Jason Newman
