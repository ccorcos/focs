   Academic Senates’ Reports to the LRCCD Board of Trustees
                         April 2025

District Academic Senate (DAS) President, Paula Cardwell, Reports:

   ●​ The District Academic Senate is forwarding the recommendation that R-3412 and
      R-7241 be revised to strike out language related to Writing, Reading, and
      Mathematics Competency Committees for consistency with current Title 5
      regulations.
   ●​ We also recommend additional changes to R-7241 that will allow students
      earning local AA/AS degrees to use any GE pattern to meet local degree
      requirements.
   ●​ We have received initial feedback from one of our tribal partners regarding our
      draft NAGPRA policy and we look forward to receiving additional feedback from
      other partners.
   ●​ We reviewed and suggested modifications - which were accepted - to the
      Institutional Effectiveness Partnership Initiative plan which is integral to the IEPI
      grant process relating to the district’s proposal to bring back IBA.
   ●​ We have proposed a Faculty Hiring Manual Revision task force and are
      collaborating with Vice Chancellor Carrie Bray in this process.
   ●​ Several DAS members will be heading to Irvine, California for spring plenary later
      this month. This is the largest number of in-person attendees we’ve had since
      COVID!

American River College Senate President, Brian Knirk, Reports that ARC:

   ●​ Has approved 2 critical hire coordinator positions for the Public Safety Training
      Center with the understanding that this valuable program supports not just
      American River College, but all of Los Rios, and a failure to hire additional
      coordinators would likely jeopardize the center’s accreditation status.
   ●​ Is working with administration to review coordinator job descriptions for Fall
   ●​ Is discussion ways in which to better support our most vulnerable populations
