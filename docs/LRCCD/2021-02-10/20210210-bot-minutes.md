Chancellor’s Report:

ARC: ARC welcomes photographer Matika Wilber on Thursday, February 18, at 7:00 pm as she
presents "Project 562: Changing the Way We See Native America" to the Los Rios community via
Zoom. Project 562 is a multi-year national photography project dedicated to photographing over
562 federally recognized tribes in the United States in an effort to create an unprecedented
repository of imagery and oral histories that accurately portray contemporary Native Americans.

This special event is held in conjunction with the ARC Photography Area, ARC Native American
Resource Center, and Viewpoint Photographic Art Center Student Program and is made possible
by an Innovation Grant from the ARC Philanthropy Office.

CRC: 195 student athletes at CRC recorded a 3.109 cumulative grade-point-average while
attempting 13.22 units for the fall 2020 semester.

Eight CRC programs recorded a team GPA of 3.0 or higher, including a 3.453 mark from CRC
Women's Basketball. Also achieving a 3.0 team GPA or higher was Women's Swimming (3.413),
Women's Tennis (3.397), Women's Volleyball (3.337), Baseball (3.286), Softball (3.103), Men's
Tennis (3.027), and Women's Soccer (3.006).

An astounding total of 23 student-athletes recorded a perfect 4.0 GPA for the fall semester while
attempting 12 or more units, with 9 different teams having at least one 4.0 student-athlete with
12 more units on their roster. Baseball had 7 team members with a 4.0, followed by Women's
Soccer with 4, and Women's Volleyball, Men's Soccer, and Women's Swimming who each had
three.

A program record 95 student-athletes were recognized as scholar-athletes for recording a 3.00
or higher GPA while attempting 12 or more units.

FLC: Last fall, as part of Folsom Lake College’s expansive “equity in action” efforts, the college
launched a campus-wide book read of Ibram X. Kendi’s How to Be an Antiracist, which studied
systemic racism in America and proposed how we can individually and collectively work towards
an antiracist society. This spring, the self-reflection work continues using the text Me and White
Supremacy: Combat Racism, Change the World, and Become a Good Ancestor by Layla F. Saad.
Starting this week, Saad's book will guide FLC Book Circle participants through a step-by-step
process of journaling and small group discussion, encouraging those with white privilege to
examine their conscious and unconscious biases, and then do the necessary and vital work that
can ultimately lead to improving race relations benefiting our greater college community. For
more information on FLC’s equity efforts across the institution, go to:
inside.flc.losrios.edu/equityinaction.

SCC: Across the district, and here at SCC we are celebrating Black History Month with events all
month long, featuring spotlights on Black art, literature, music, wellness, finances and
community. The districtwide Welcome Black Event on Zoom was held last Friday, Feb. 5, allowing
students to mix and mingle with each other and hear from Black college presidents, vice
president, administration and staff. SCC also has a series of events planned, including “Melanated
Music Monday,” “Creative Writing Circle,” “Wisdom, Nutrition and Wellness,” and “Black
Jeopardy.”
