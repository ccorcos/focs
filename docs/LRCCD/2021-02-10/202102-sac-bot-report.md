Report provided by ASO Student Senate President: ARC Student Senate President, Naomi Dasari

Below is a list of highlighted activities, accomplishments and events at each of the colleges within the Los Rios
District. This report is to be used as a guide for the Student Advisory Committee representative and the
Student Trustee in providing the monthly LRCCD student report at the LRCCD Board of Trustee (BOT)
meetings. Only the information on this report will be used. Not all areas need to be completed; rather, this is a
guide on topics to consider. Approved/signed college reports are due to the Student Trustee one week prior to
each scheduled BOT meeting. The Student Trustee in consultation with the SAC Advisor will compile these
submissions for one comprehensive report which is due to District Advisor Dr. Rosario, the designated student
speaker, and SAC Advisor by noon the Friday before the BOT meeting. Late submissions/unauthorized reports
will not be presented at the BOT meeting.


Student led-activities (i.e. March in March, campus events, fundraisers):
   ● ARC: ARC has continued work on virtual club days with the website BoothCentral. In addition,
        internal presentations were given to leaders to provide more information on this year’s Los Rios
        Lobby Day topic.
    ●   CRC: CRC hosted a virtual Hawk Week in late January.
    ●   FLC: FLC’s Joint Budget Committee was assigned a Canvas Shell project that included having a
        Youtube Channel. In addition, Student Senate Secretary Allison Schiffmaier formed a GA Resolution
        for the statewide Student Senate General Assembly.
    ●   SCC: Student Senate has been preparing for Lobby Day and General Assembly.

Senate and CAEB Accomplishments (i.e. surveys, engagement in campus and regional governance):
   ● ARC: ARC held their first Senate and CAEB meetings for the semester and appointed more students
       to the ASB Basic Needs Committee.
    ●   CRC: CRC confirmed the appointment of Sabehaa Baqui as Student Senate Treasurer. In addition,
        CRC is working on changes to the ASCRC Constitution/Bylaws, creating a Transcript Notation
        Resolution, and creating a Canvas course to further outreach.
    ●   FLC: ASFLC, played a role in naming the new PEAC2E program- Peer Engagement for Achievement,
        Culture, Connection, and Excellence (PEAC²E). This program is geared towards enhancing the
        educational experience for Black/African-American students at Folsom Lake College. In addition, a
