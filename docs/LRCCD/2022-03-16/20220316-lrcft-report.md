LRCFT report to LRCCD Board of Trustees                 March 16, 2022


The LRCFT continues to assist hundreds of faculty make the transition
back to on ground teaching and serving students.
The LRCFT also extends our ongoing support to ARC President Melanie
Dixon regarding racist hate speech that she unfortunately experienced.
The LRCFT continues to help plan the Cesar Chavez March which takes
place on March 26 in Sacramento.
The LRCFT recently took part over two full days in the Sacramento
Central Labor Council’s interviews of candidates seeking Sacramento
City council, Sacramento Board of Supervisors, and state Assembly
political office.
The LRCFT PAFC will also interview candidates for the Sacramento City
council election to be held this June.
Our exec. team this week will be attending the California Federation of
Teachers three-day conference in San Francisco.
The LRCFT will be hosting trustee Dustin Johnson at our next meeting
and will reach out to trustee John Knight to attend one of our board
meetings this semester as well.
The LRCFT will contribute to support the Los Rios student graduations
this May.
The LRCFT faculty picnic will be held on May 6 at the Sierra 2 center in
Sacramento.
In closing, our late president Dean Murakami will be honored along
with other labor leaders at the upcoming Salute to labor dinner on May
26 in Sacramento.
