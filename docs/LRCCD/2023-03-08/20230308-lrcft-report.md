LRCFT Report to the Board of Trustees                 March 8, 2023
The LRCFT is now halfway through the process of formal negotiations with
the District this semester. Our small and large teams continue to meet
productively, and we are on schedule to complete the process by late April.
The new PT faculty health care agreement with Los Rios continues to
generate statewide praise. We have been asked by numerous other higher
education unions to explain our successful agreement to their exec.
boards.
The LRCFT exec. board will attend our annual CFT conference next week
in SF. The CFT has created a new statewide award in the name of Dean
Murakami, our former esteemed LRCFT President.
The LRCFT is sponsoring faculty to attend the upcoming FACCC Advocacy
and Policy Conference held in Sacramento.
The LRCFT will fund some of the cost for the five Los Rios student
graduations to be held in May.
The LRCFT Social Justice Caucus is sponsoring a book reading and
speaker event for faculty this semester focused on The Costs of
Completion: Student Success in Community College.
In closing, the LRCFT would like to acknowledge the loss of two faculty
members who recently passed away:

On February 1st, we lost Cicely Young to cancer. Cicely was an adjunct
professor of English at Cosumnes River College. During her 48 years, she
was the epitome of strength, perseverance, and determination. Her brother
said, "Cicely never complained about her limits. Instead, she challenged
them...On her journey in life, Cicely was blessed to, not only succeed, but
exceed... She lived life as it should be – fully and without fear. She was the
reason my family and I never had a reason to give up, continuing to strive
forward. I hope the faculty and staff who knew Cicely experienced this as
well." Cicely is survived by her son Amari Young.

On January 6th, we lost Dr. Travis Silcox -- a retired professor of English at
Sacramento City College. Travis retired in 2019 after working in our District
for 28 years. Travis was a social justice warrior who worked tirelessly to
help build stronger communities. She was a beloved professor who
empowered her students to take ownership of their education and to find
joy, motivation, or sometimes questions and anger in the materials they
read and talked about. She worked on behalf of our undocumented
students before colleges and universities even thought about providing
services. Her husband notes that during her brief hospital stay, one of her
nurses, a former student, said, "Dr. Silcox, you have no idea how many
lives you touched and what a difference you made to your
students." Travis is survived by her husband, Preston Rudy.

Thank you!       Jason Newman, LRCFT President
