LRCFT report to LRCCD Board of Trustees                         August 10, 2022


The LRCFT thanks the trustees for approving our recently negotiated agreement
with LRCCD for a one-time 10 percent retroactive payment and a 4 percent COLA
applied to the salary schedule. We also want to thank VC Mario Rodriguez and
AVC Carrie Bray for their diligence in finalizing the process.
The LRCFT is working with the Academic Senate and LRCCD to finalize our new
mandatory equity training component of our contract to be in place during fall
semester.
LRCFT continues to meet with state leadership and local legislators to improve PT
faculty access to health care, job security, and pay parity. The CFT is holding a
regional meeting of local CFT unions to discuss organizing strategies.
The LRCFT is gearing back up to assist our local candidates endorsed by the LRCFT
for the November election. One of our first events is a fundraiser on August 15 for
Dave Jones who is running for a state senate seat in district 8.
The LRCFT is sponsoring three of our board members to attend the Los Rios trip to
Ghana in September.
Eight LRCFT exec. board members attended our national American Federation of
Teachers conference in Boston last month.
The LRCFT will send a delegation of board members to Oaxaca, Mexico, on
October 7 for a three-day Trinational Labor Coalition conference.
The LRCFT is proud to have supported the Naturalization and DACA workshop
conducted by the Coalition for Humane Immigrant Rights (CHIRLA) this past
August 6. CHIRLA provided free legal consultations, workshops on Naturalization
for those students and faculty and classified staff who are permanent residents
and wish to become US Citizens, as well as those who wish to renew their DACA
forms. CHIRLA will help with the costs for both applications for qualifying
individuals.
The LRCFT is a major sponsor of the Sacramento Central Labor Council’s Labor Day
Picnic that will take place on September 5.
The LRCFT is also one of the sponsors of the Poor People’s Campaign march from
Delano to the Capitol by farmworkers seeking voting rights in union elections
under bill AB 2183. The 300-mile march coincides with California Farmworker Day
on August 26.


Thanks,
