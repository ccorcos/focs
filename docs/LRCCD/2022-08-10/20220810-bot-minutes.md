Chancellor’s Report:

ARC: ARC’s Culinary Arts & Hospitality Management program will be featured at the September
1 groundbreaking for the Sacramento Food Literacy Center’s new cooking school. The Food
Literacy Center teaches children in low-income elementary schools cooking, nutrition, gardening,
and active play to improve their health, environment, and economy. ARC culinary students will
volunteer at the September 1 event to provide support for the center’s work.

CRC: Congratulations to CRC student athlete TJ Czyz, who was drafted by the Oakland A’s. On the
third day, Czyz was selected in the 12th round of the Major League Draft of amateur baseball
players.

Czyz, a right-handed pitcher, started 13 games for CRC Coach Don Mico and the Hawks and was
7-3 with a 2.96 ERA. In 85 innings pitched, he struck out 113 batters while walking just 33. Czyz
gets a lot of spin rate on his curveball (he was clocked at 2912 rpm in the Draft League last
summer). He’s been pitching this summer for the Reno Astros.

FLC: The Folsom Lake College/UC Davis Mentor Research internship program at UC Davis’
Agricultural Chemistry Lab returned this summer following a two-year hiatus due to the
pandemic and recruited five FLC students - Taylor Orsel, Kalyn Amezcua, Avigna Vasanthan, Jesse
Skratt, and Nadia Gonzalez - who had the unique opportunity to be directly involved with real
scientific research related to food and agriculture under the guidance of UC Davis Professor Dr.
Susan Ebeler. At UC Davis' Mondavi Institute on Thursday, July 21, the students presented their
capstone summer research projects to UC Davis and FLC faculty. This internship partnership was
first established in 2015 by FLC Biology professor Linda Abraham and Chemistry professor Christa
Oberth.

SCC: The Sacramento City College Mechanical Electrical Technology Program (MET) in
partnership with the Refugee Career Pathway Program collaborated on an event to introduce
recent refugees to the great career opportunities in MET. Attendees talked about how supported
they felt by the college and were excited to learn about the MET program. Several students even
enrolled before leaving the event! The MET and Refugee Career Pathway team is looking forward
to hosting another event in the fall.
