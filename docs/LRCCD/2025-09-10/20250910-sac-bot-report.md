                                Student Advisory Committee

              Student Senates’ Reports to the LRCCD Board of Trustees

                                          September 2025

Report provided by Nathaniel Petueli, Sacramento City College Student Senate President

 Student-Led Activities:
    ●​ Welcome Week events were held across the district, including Hawk Days at Cosumnes River
       College, Week of Welcome at American River College, and interactive welcome tables at Folsom
       Lake College.
    ●​ Sacramento City College is planning a combined event on September 17 to recognize Club Day, U.S.
       Constitution Day, Voter Registration Day, and Welcome Back activities.
    ●​ American River College hosted Arcade Day on August 28, fostering student engagement and
       community building.
    ●​ Folsom Lake College is organizing a recruitment event focused on connecting students with shared
       governance committees.

 Senate and Clubs & Events Board Accomplishments:
    ●​ Students have been appointed to shared governance committees across the district, ensuring
       student representation in campus decision-making.
    ●​ Folsom Lake College established strategic priorities with assigned leads to guide Senate and Clubs &
       Events Board work.
    ●​ American River College’s Student Senate formed an ad hoc committee focused on food accessibility.
    ●​ Sacramento City College began its Fall 2025 Senate session on August 26 and acquired a new
       mascot costume (Huey 3.0) to enhance school spirit.
    ●​ Club development continues to expand districtwide:
           o​ American River College’s Club and Events Board has chartered 23 clubs and is planning Club
               Day on September 16, in collaboration with El Centro’s Bienvenida 2025.
           o​ Folsom Lake College is preparing for Club Rush from September 8-11.

 Leadership Development Accomplishments:
    ●​ All four colleges have held or planned retreats and orientations to onboard new student leaders.
       American River College hosted a two-day ASB Orientation in July, and Folsom Lake College held an
       August retreat covering governance structures and leadership skills.
    ●​ Sacramento City College conducted two summer training days and continues weekly leadership
       development sessions through its Super Tuesday program.
    ●​ Cosumnes River College’s ASCRC Advisor will lead a Lobby Day workshop on September 5.
    ●​ Funding has been approved across multiple campuses to support attendance at the 2025 CCCSAA
       Student Leadership Conference in October.
