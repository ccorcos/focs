LRCFT report to the LRCCD Board of Trustees            September 10, 2025

During the recent flex week in August, the LRCFT partnered with
FACCC, SEIU 1021, LRCEA, CalSTRS, CalPERS, and our District in
presenting a retirement and health benefits conference for faculty
and classified employees. Over 600 attended the virtual event. We
thank the District for helping us make the informational event a
great success. We will repeat the event next year.
I want to thank VC Carrie Bray and Senate President Paula Cardwell
for organizing the new full time faculty welcome events during flex
week. It was inspirational to meet the 80 new full-time faculty.
The LRCFT also held a union membership drive during flex week. We
staffed tables at each of the campuses and signed up many new
members, both full and part time. The membership drive is ongoing.
On August 23, the LRCFT Exec. Board joined the Community College
Council division of the California Federation of Teachers for a one-
day organizing workshop in San Diego. The focus was electing
trustees to the governing boards of California community colleges.
On Labor Day, the LRCFT participated in the Sacramento Central
Labor Council’s annual labor day celebration at Fairytale Town. I
want to thank trustee Deborah Ortiz for attending.
The LRCFT PAFC meets later this month to discuss endorsements
for the office of State Superintendent for Public Instruction.
Other LRCFT events this semester include a membership social for
Part Time faculty in our district. The LRCFT will also field a race team
in November for the Run to Feed Hungry Race in Sacramento.
In closing, as you heard earlier this evening in board public
comment, the LRCFT continues to express major concerns with the
District’s lack of meaningful support of faculty within the Prison and
Reentry Education Program, also known as PREP. We recently
experienced an incident where we thought our faculty member
could have been more closely supported in their attempt to clear
their name regarding an unfounded accusation by CDCR. We hope
in the future that our faculty will be better supported in this program
because PREP is vital to our students, faculty, and District.
Thank you,
Jason Newman, LRCFT President
