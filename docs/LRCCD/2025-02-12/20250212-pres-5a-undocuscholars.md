  Protecting Students and Employees
• Los Rios Police Department (LRPD) is NOT in the business of immigration enforcement
• If any Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) or any other deferral immigration
  law enforcement agency comes onto a Los Rios facility, we have instructed
  employees or students to contact LRPD immediately.
• Launched Districtwide Awareness Campaign, including:
      • Written and Video messages to college community, Signage around Los Rios
        facilities (QR code below links to video)
      • Social media
      • Webinar on 2/13/25
• LRPD personnel trained on federal immigration officers’
  statutory authorities and limitations
      • 8 USC § 1357 and 8 CFR § 287
• Los Rios Public Safety Advisory Committee
• Trainings for manager/supervisors, faculty, & key front line staff (e.g. dream center
  and learning resource center, admissions and FA staff)




                                                                                 8
              Protecting Student Data
• Training on Handling Confidential Student Information, with added
  safeguards for undocumented students
• Review and assessment of data collected, ie, CCCApply, CADAA,
  Electronic Health Record (EHR)
• Access restricted to key personnel in A&R, Financial Aid, Dream Centers
  and other areas
• Limited data that identifies legal status
• Secure servers, with encryption
• Dream Centers serve broader population of students (undocumented,
  mixed status, AB 540)- legal status not identifiable from WID#
• Dream/UndocuScholar Center procedures & best practices in handling
  confidential student information with cultural sensitivity

              Admissions Support for
              Undocumented Students
●   A&R does not require students to disclose status for AB540

●   When a student chooses to disclose status, A&R does not record the disclosure in the student
    record

●   Collaboration between A&R and campus Dream Center staff

●   AB 540 training for Dream Center staff

●   Guidance/resource documents on how to apply latest title 5 regs and Ed Code changes related to
    undocumented students

●   UndocuAlly training for all A&R staff

●   Streamlined, consistent process for addressing CCCApply applications submitted with no SSN’s &
    welcome message with information about available resources (Dream Center, CADAA…)

The UndocuScholar Resource Center empowers students,
staff, faculty, and community members who are
undocumented, of mixed-status families, or allies by
supporting their academic, personal, and professional goals.

The UndocuScholar Resource Center is committed to
providing individualized support and resources to help
students achieve their academic pathway.

2025. I’m so grateful for this program, and the people supporting it—it’s been
life-changing."
