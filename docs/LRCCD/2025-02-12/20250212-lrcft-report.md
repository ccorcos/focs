LRCFT report to the LRCCD Board of Trustees            February 12, 2025

The LRCFT is proud to have partnered in the past two months with LRCCD
and the CFT in assisting with the creation and implementation of policies to
assist our immigrant students. I want to thank our chief negotiator Belinda
Lum for being the point person for us in these efforts.
The LRCFT has recently assisted our sibling unions in Los Angeles with a
donation to CFT’s Disaster Relief Fund to help dozens of members recover
from the devastating fires last month.
To help keep higher education affordable and accessible for students, the
LRCFT is helping to sponsor CFT’s The March in March. Over 1,000
community college students from throughout the state will meet in
Sacramento on the afternoon of March 4 to march to the Capitol for a rally
with elected officials. Our collective goal is to call attention to the need to
protect higher education.
Our faculty union continues to hold events for all faculty. These include Part
Time faculty membership meetings at our union hall, social gatherings near
the campuses, an American River cleanup project, and a joint picnic with
SEIU and LRCEA planned for May. We are also sponsoring a dozen faculty
to attend the Advocacy and Policy Conference hosted by the Faculty
Association of California Community Colleges in late February.
The Sacramento Central Labor Council has recently honored the LRCFT and
our past president, the late Dean Murakami, by naming a new library in his
honor at the SCLC headquarters on K St. The new Dean Murakami Labor
Library is now open and accepting donations.
The LRCFT is also taking a leading role in supporting LACLAA’s Cesar
Chavez March, the largest annual labor march in Sacramento. We are also
helping to fund the five affinity-based Los Rios student graduations in May.
In closing, I want to welcome faculty union members Erica Tyler (FLC),
Robert Unzueta (SCC), Ivan Zaigralin (CRC), and Bill Zangeneh-Lester
(ARC) as new LRCFT board members. We have also recently appointed a
new LRCFT Communications Coordinator, LaQuisha Beckum, psychology
professor at American River College.
Thank you!
Jason Newman, LRCFT President
