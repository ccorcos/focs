   Academic Senates’ Reports to the LRCCD Board of Trustees
                       February 2025

District Academic Senate (DAS) President, Paula Cardwell, Reports:

   ●​ Our Moratorium Task Force is finishing up our draft NAGPRA policy
      proposal and will have it to the District Academic Senate very soon. Once
      on our agenda, this document will be public and we will share the draft with
      our college and tribal communities for input and feedback. I am extremely
      proud of the incredible and arduous work this task force has devoted to the
      creation of this policy proposal. We look forward to its thorough vetting
      and our forthcoming formal recommendation to the Board of Trustees.
   ●​ We are in the process of vetting two regulation change proposals with our
      local senates regarding the removal of math, reading, and writing
      competency committees as required under the old Title 5, as well as a
      proposal to allow continuing students to use any general education pattern
      to satisfy GE requirements for our local degrees.
   ●​ The District Academic Senate is representing faculty on the newly formed
      Public Safety Advisory Task Force. Concerted efforts are being made to
      better inform faculty and the campus communities to be best prepared
      should U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement agents visit our
      campuses. We appreciate and respect the efforts of our Chief Strategy and
      Communications Officer Gabe Ross and Los Rios Policy Chief Harvey Woo.

American River College Senate President, Brian Knirk, Reports:

   ●​ ARC Senate is working to approve a number of regulation revisions to
      move forward to DAS
   ●​ ARC Senate is supporting its numerous faculty working on nearly 30 hiring
      committees. For context that’s over 100 faculty serving on the hiring
      committees this semester. We are pleased to have this opportunity to do
      this important work.

Folsom Lake College Senate President, Eric Wada, Reports:

   ●​ The FLC Academic Senate passed a resolution by acclamation,
      enthusiastically supporting bringing an Umoja program to FLC. FLC is
      recognized as a Hispanic Serving Institution (HSI), Asian American, Native
      Hawaiian, Pacific Islander (AANHPI) serving institution, and a grantee of
      Native American Student Support and Success Program (NASSSP), while
      we currently have college-based programs such as Peer Engagement for
      Achievement, Connection, Culture, and Engagement (PEAC2E) and Black
      African Student Edu-culture (BASE), the FLC Academic Senate recognizes
      the importance of building statewide and national connections with
      established learning communities like Umoja.
   ●​ The FLC Academic Senate is also beginning our nominations process for
      the next group of Senators and Officers.


Sacramento City College Senate President, Amy Strimling, Reports:

   ●​ SCC is excited to welcome seven new adjunct faculty members to our
      Academic Senate this semester. Fair and appropriate compensation for our
