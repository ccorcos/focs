DATE:            October 1, 2021

TO:              Members, Los Rios Board of Trustees

FROM: Brian King, Chancellor

SUBJECT:         Board Self-Evaluation Process


As you know, the Los Rios Board of Trustees self-evaluation process includes three major components
detailed below.

      1.     Annual Outcomes and Achievements: Along with the Chancellor, the Board annually sets
             goals, monitors progress toward those goals at mid-year, and at year-end formally assesses
             their achievements for the year.

      2.     Twice Annual Board Retreats: In retreat twice annually (in October and March) the Board
             discusses Future Directions.

      3.     Board Self-evaluation Instrument: The Board also completes a self-evaluation instrument,
             discusses the results of the survey among themselves, and makes modifications in their
             working as a Board as a result of the conversation.

This ongoing process of self-evaluation, planning and modifications based on that evaluation has
produced significant innovation and continuous improvement within Los Rios over the years. It is again
time to complete the enclosed Board self-evaluation instrument, which will be administered by a third-
party consultant.


      A link to the electronic Board Evaluation survey will be sent to the Board of Trustees by
                      October 1 to be completed no later than October 8, 2021.


Thank you for your attention to this very important process.

AGENDA ITEM 4.A                                    TYPE OF BOARD CONSIDERATION:
                                                   Board Action


BACKGROUND
The Los Rios Board of Trustees directed staff to develop and implement a plan to require all
faculty and staff and all students attending on-ground classes or utilizing on-ground services to
have received at least a first dose of vaccine for COVID-19 by October 1, 2021. Since Los Rios had
not previously captured medical information from students and had captured only limited
medical information from students before the vaccination requirement was approved by the
Board, implementing the vaccination requirement in less than sixty days has been one of the
most ambitious, difficult, and challenging projects in the history of Los Rios Community College
District. In many ways, October 1, 2021, is more of a starting line for the project than a finish line.
Because of the complexity of the project and the many unknowns concerning COVID-19 and the
Delta Variant, it is likely that a vaccine requirement will be in place for the Spring and Fall
semesters of 2022 at a minimum. Staff will provide an update on the work completed and under
way to implement the vaccine requirement.

RECOMMENDATION
This item is presented for the Board of Trustees information and discussion.
