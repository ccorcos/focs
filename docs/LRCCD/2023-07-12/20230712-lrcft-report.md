LRCFT Report to the Board of Trustees                   July 12, 2023


Now that our contract has been approved, our executive board is in the
process of meeting internally to decide how to allocate the COLA and retro
payment. We will then meet with the district to arrive at a final agreement.
In the fall semester, we will engage in a union membership drive and hold
meeting sessions to help faculty understand our new contract.
As part of our ongoing efforts at member appreciation, this Sunday we are
sponsoring our members to attend the last of three River Cats games that
we purchased.
Just this morning, the LRCFT and CFT testified at the Capitol in favor of AB
811, the repeatability bill. This bill increases the ability of students to retake
courses so that they can transfer more easily.
Along with the CFT, the LRCFT PAFC will hold interviews for candidates in
the AD 6 race which covers the city of Sacramento, a vital seat for Los Rios
and the LRCFT. The races for AD 7, Sacramento city mayor, and
Sacramento city council will keep us busy in fall semester.
We heard back from one of the producers of PBS and its Inside CA
Education documentary series about a potential future episode on our
DACA students and program in Los Rios.
Once again this year, the LRCFT will sponsor the SCLC’s annual Labor
Day picnic. Hopefully it won’t be 115 degrees like last year.
In closing, the LRCFT and DAS will meet in August with our new FT faculty
hires and take them out to dinner.


Thank you!        Jason Newman, LRCFT President
