LRCFT report to the LRCCD Board of Trustees                September 14, 2022
The LRCFT looks forward to opening full negotiations with the district next
semester. We are currently putting together our negotiating team.
The LRCFT during convocation participated on each campus by holding various
events in person and online to assist faculty.
LRCFT met in August with state CFT leadership and faculty from Yuba college
district in a joint meeting to improve PT faculty access to health care, job security,
and pay parity, which is part of the $200 million augmentation provided by the
legislature.
The LRCFT PAFC is meeting with our local endorsed candidates to provide any last
minute assistance for the November general election. The races include the
Sacramento City Council, Sacramento Board of Supervisors, State Senate, and
State Assembly races
The LRCFT is sponsoring three of our board members to attend the Los Rios trip
this week to the All-African Diaspora Educators Summit at the University of Cape
Coast in Ghana.
The LRCFT took part in the Sacramento Central Labor Council’s Labor Day Picnic
on September 5.
The LRCFT participated in the recent UFW farmworker march to support voting
rights for farmworkers in union elections under AB 2183.
This semester the LRCFT is in the process of searching for a new building for our
headquarters and we are also hiring a new executive director as our long-term
current director Robert Perrone is retiring effective January 1, 2023.
This semester in November the LRCFT is also holding internal elections for campus
representative, Secretary/Treasurer, and LRCFT President positions.
The LRCFT has met with the SCTA to help us finalize the dual enrollment
agreements with LRCCD and the local high schools.
The LRCFT worked diligently over the summer lobbying legislators to pass AB 1856
which would provide Part Time faculty with a load increase up to 85%. This bill
awaits the governor’s signature.
