                         Academic Senates’ Reports to the LRCCD Board of Trustees
                                             December 2022



District Academic Senate (DAS) President, Alisa Shubb, Reports:
  •    Highlights of the DAS work this semester have been:
           o Collaborating on draft Regulation -2212 (Special Admit & Dual Enrollment
               Students).
           o Chartering a faculty-led task group to recommend criteria for establishing
               optimal class sizes through an equity practitioner framework.
           o Chartering a faculty-led task group to explore noncredit in Los Rios.
           o Providing feedback on the District Strategic Planning process.
           o Working with LRCFT to incorporate equity-minded professional learning as a
               required portion of college service hours.
  •    In 2019, the DAS passed a resolution Creating Accountability for Collegial Consultation
       on Academic and Professional Matters which, in the 3rd resolved, urged continued work
       between the Chancellor, Vice Chancellor of Education & Technology (now Deputy
       Chancellor) and DAS to develop and implement a system of mutual accountability with
       clear and measurable criteria to ensure consistent collegial consultation on academic &
       professional matters. The final resolved recommends that the DAS Executive Council
       (the 5 Academic Senate Presidents) provide a report at least once a semester
       documenting the status of collegial consultation between the Chancellor's Office & DAS
       using the mutually agreed upon criteria. Recognizing that mutually agreed upon criteria
       have not yet been established, the DAS Executive Council prepared the following
       assessment report:

    ARC Academic Senate President, Carina Hoffpauir, Reports:
•   Recent topics at ARC’s Academic Senate have included reaffirmation of our Strategic
    Goals, reports from our ARC Bias Response and Employee Onboarding project teams,
    implementation of Guided Pathways through HomeBases, and academic and
    professional impacts related to COVID protocols.

    CRC Academic Senate President, Scott Crosier, Reports:
•   Despite the terrorist threat and subsequent campus closure, the faculty and staff at CRC
    continue to provide our highest support in helping our students complete another
    semester at CRC.
•   The Academic Senate Exec Team recognizes and appreciates Dr. Bush’s efforts to
    include the senate leadership in some of the decision making processes regarding the
    campus closure and reopening.
•   CRC Academic Senate has recently passed a resolution addressing the need for
    additional resources and support as we provide accessible learning environments for
    our students.


    FLC Academic Senate President, Eric Wada, Reports:
•   The FLC Academic Senate will be nominating Dr. Tami Cheshire for the ASCCC Hayward
    Award for Excellence in Education for her demonstrated equity-minded leadership at
    FLC, in Los Rios, and at the state level. We have been reviewing the district dual
    enrollment regulations with the interests of equity, student success, and academic
    freedom in mind. We recently reviewed our college's SEAP plan. We recently made a
    recommendation that our college finds funds to continue to use our SLO and
    department planning/program review software platform.

•   Many members of the Academic Senate and other faculty participated in the
    impressions sessions for our next college president. We look forward to working with
    Dr. Art Pimentel. We thank Dr. Rachel Rosenthal for her service as Interim President this
    busy semester.

•   We wish to extend our best wishes to Melanie Dixon who many of us at FLC fondly
    remember when she started in Los Rios as Dean of Student Success. Many of our equity-
    focused programs and structures that exist today are credited to Melanie's skills and
    knowledge as an effective and collaborative leader.
    SCC Academic Senate President, Sandra Guzmán, Reports:

•   Senate and LRCFT leadership met with our Professional Development and Equity leads
    to discuss Equity Minded Faculty Learning, assess what we are currently offering and
    areas for growth or continued improvement. We will include affinity group input, such
    as the Black Faculty and Staff Association (BFSA) and Comunidad in our PD planning and
    offerings.
•   The college hosted a luncheon celebration honoring our recently-hired tenure-track and
    recently-tenured faculty, Fall 2020 through Fall 2022. Honorees included faculty from
    every division at the college, including our New Faculty Academy alums.
•   Congratulations to Kim Goff (classified staff) and Tracey Gomez (Faculty Coordinator)
    who are being recognized by the College Futures Foundation, for their adaptive and
    equity-focused holistic student support.
•   Our Senate has voted for action to be taken in support of Women’s reproductive rights.
    We ask Chancellor King to issue a statement in support of the CCC State Chancellor's
    and our statements regarding the Dobbs decision. We ask Chancellor King to forward
    the statement from the California Community College State Chancellor's office to each
