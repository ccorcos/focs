LRCFT report to LRCCD Board of Trustees                                           August 11, 2021



The LRCFT has successfully negotiated a 6% retroactive payment to faculty. We thank our District
negotiating partners for their role in the process.

The LRCFT continues to receive a heavy volume of questions regarding mandatory vaccination policy.

The LRCFT continues to be involved in helping DACA students and DACA eligible students navigate the
challenging terrain of new federal and state policy.

The LRCFT in conjunction with the DAS will host a training session for faculty during flex week to
understand the Equity Reflection statement that is now part of our contract.

The LRCFT exec. board continues to conduct an Equity Audit of existing LRCFT board and union practices.

The LRCFT PAFC is taking an active role against the recall of Governor Newsom.

The LRCFT PAFC recently endorsed Dawnte Early for the West Sacramento City Council race.

The LRCFT PAFC is beginning the process of interviewing candidates for the California legislature 6th
Senate District election which will take place either late this year or early next year.

The LRCFT will continue to invite LRCCD trustees to our meetings over the next six months.

The LRCFT will take part in the annual Labor Day picnic September 6 which takes place outside
at Fairytale Town in William Land Park.
The LRCFT continues to negotiate with LRCCD regarding the safety parameters for fall
reopening, the impacts of mandatory vaccination policy, the impacts of centralization of district
resources upon faculty working conditions, remote work options for faculty, and faculty
compensation.
Our first LRCFT executive board meeting is September 1, from 3-5 pm.
In closing, we unfortunately lost one of our faculty recently, Tanya Rodriguez, philosophy
professor at SCC. The LRCFT would like to acknowledge her excellence in teaching and her
generosity to students and faculty. She will be missed.


Jason Newman
LRCFT President
