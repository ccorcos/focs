LRCFT report to the LRCCD Board of Trustees                              November 10, 2025

The LRCFT continues to prepare for negotiations with the district in early 2026. We recently
sent a survey to all faculty to help build our sunshine list of negotiation topics. We are also
holding all-faculty negotiation forums in person over the next month at the four colleges.

At one of our recent LRCFT board meetings, the faculty voted to contribute funds to the
student food pantries at the four Los Rios Colleges.

The LRCFT recently held a One Faculty Party to bring full and part time faculty together to
discuss shared interest as employees of Los Rios. Over 80 faculty attended.

As part of the LRCFT’s ongoing membership drive, we visited FLC a couple weeks ago and
met with faculty and students.

The LRCFT is holding internal elections this semester for college union president at
American River College where two candidates are vying for the position.

The LRCFT is fielding a team for the Run to Feed the Hungry which takes place on
Thanksgiving Day near SCC.

The LRCFT and CFT are ecstatic that California voters passed Prop. 50 which has energized
the labor movement statewide and nationally as we gear up for the congressional midterm
elections in 2026.

Recently, the LRCFT took part in a public loan forgiveness webinar hosted by the American
Federation of Teachers. I want to commend Jacob Traugott, LRCFT PAFC chair and SCC
philosophy professor, for coordinating the event for our faculty.

In closing, the CFT continues to plan for the student March in March for affordable and
accessible higher education. This year’s march takes place March 12 at the capitol and
promises to be the largest student protest in early 2026 north of Los Angeles.

Thank you,

Jason Newman, LRCFT President
