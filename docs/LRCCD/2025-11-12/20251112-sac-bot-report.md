                                Student Advisory Committee

              Student Senates’ Reports to the LRCCD Board of Trustees

                                          November 2025

Report presented by Tina Khuc, Folsom Lake College Student Senate Vice President

 Student-Led Activities:
    •   American River College hosted Karaoke Night, Homecoming Week, “I Stand with Immigrants” in
        partnership with the UndocuScholar Resource Center and is planning the Campus Life Tea Party
        Event on November 12-13.
    •   Cosumnes River College held its Fall Club Rush.
    •   Folsom Lake College organized a Halloween Dance, Fall Festival, and community wetlands cleanup,
        with upcoming Town Halls and student grievance visits.
    •   Sacramento City College hosted Ballot Bowl 2025 to promote voter engagement and Proposition
        50 awareness, organized a campus-wide Halloween celebration, and launched a Fall Toy Drive to
        support local families.

 Senate and Clubs & Events Board Accomplishments:
    •   American River College reviewed the Study Equity Plan, advanced discussions on ASB stipends,
        updated Clubs and Events Board bylaws, expanded to 50 active clubs, and addressed dietary
        restrictions and cross-contamination concerns in campus dining through the Accessible Food
        Committee.
    •   Cosumnes River College held its first Legislative Affairs Committee meeting and engaged with
        district administration on the campus’s Facilities Master Plan.
    •   Folsom Lake College approved a resolution supporting extended library hours, created surveys to
        improve outreach, and collaborated with the Curriculum Committee to provide student input on
        course prerequisites.
    •   Sacramento City College approved funding for key initiatives including outreach equipment and
        upcoming cultural and community events.

 Leadership Development Accomplishments:
    •   Student leaders from all four colleges attended the CCCSAA Student Leadership Conference in
        Santa Clara.
    •   American River College hosted presentations by the Vice President of Student Services and the
        Dean of Outreach on equity and the HomeBase Early Alert System.
    •   Folsom Lake College gained feedback from the Dean of Student Services on student services and
        emergency housing and participated in SSCCC sessions on delegate responsibilities.
    •   Sacramento City College conducted an Advocacy Crash Course and began planning participation in
        the upcoming Federal Advocacy Academy in Washington, D.C.
