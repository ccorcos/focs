    Academic Senates’ Reports to the LRCCD Board of Trustees

District Academic Senate (DAS) President, Paula Cardwell, Reports:
   ●​ The District Academic Senate passed a proposal to update several board
       policies and regulations that would change the terms “Academic Probation” to
       “Academic Notice” and “Academic Dismissal” to “Academic Pause.” The task
       force that made this recommendation to the senate was organized well in
       advance of the Title 5 changes made more recently, as our faculty are committed
       to eliminating deficit-minded, punitive-sounding language wherever possible. We
       wish to thank the District Equity and Student Success Committee administrative
       co-chair, Sonia Ortiz-Mercado, for her assistance with this proposal, as well as
       Jason Ralphs and General Counsel Aly Bivins for the quick turn-arounds with the

Folsom Lake College Senate President, Wayne Jensen, Reports:
   ●​ Our Senate is working on revising our Faculty Hiring Prioritization Process.
   ●​ We are also in the process of working with representatives from all constituent
      groups to update our Enrollment Management Plan.
   ●​ Our Senate supported a critical hire request for a Public Service Librarian.
   ●​ We are excited as a college as FLC has been selected to be a Puente Project
      Site. This was a collegewide effort and are grateful for all the faculty, classified
      staff, and administrators who advocated for the Puente program at FLC.
   ●​ FLC had a successful Partnership Resource Team visit for the IEPI grant to
      revamp SLO assessments. The team recognized the “significant positive
      changes that have been achieved cultivating a culture of assessment at FLC.”
      They also recognized that we are “well positioned” to keep the momentum going.
      Many thanks to faculty who developed and are carrying out the plan and for the
      strong support we have received from all of our FLC administrative team.
   ●​ Faculty will be participating in follow up meetings with CCC Accessibility Center
      representatives regarding the Assessability Capability Maturity Model (ACMM) in
      early March. Recognizing the reality that the heavy lift of what needs to be done
      at each college to be at a compliant level of accessibility, faculty feel strongly that
      we need to maintain a fully funded UDA Coordinator at each college and
      encourage the District administration to identify an ongoing funding source for
      these needed positions.
