2. FY 2025-26 Wholesale and Retail Budget (W & R)

  President Costa opened hearing at 6:24 p.m.

  Ms. Grant, SJWD Board Secretary, confirmed that the hearing was properly noticed
  as required by law. GM Helliker reported that there were no formal written or verbal
  comments received.

  Ms. Silva reviewed the staff report which was provided in the Board packet. She
  provided a recap of the budget process, indicating that the assumptions that were
  discussed at the April Board meeting were used to develop the draft FY 2025-26
  Wholesale and Retail budget, which was reviewed in detail at the May Board
  meeting.

  Ms. Silva informed the Board that there have been some changes made to the
  budget since the May Board meeting, which are detailed in her written staff report.
  She informed the Board that these changes resulted in no monetary changes to the
  budget.

  In response to Director Zamorano’s question, Ms. Silva informed the Board that the
  $5.15 million for Groundwater Production Facilities is included in the Retail Financial
  Plan and the proposed rates. In addition, she explained that the proposed budget
  includes both a cost and a revenue for this item, which makes it a net neutral item.

  Director Zamorano moved to remove the $5.15 million for the Groundwater
  Production Facilities from the proposed FY 2025-26 budget. Director Tobin
  seconded the motion.

  President Costa requested an amendment to the motion to sunset the $5.15 million
  until after staff finalize negotiations with the various agencies regarding groundwater
  partnerships. Director Zamorano stood firm on his motion and added that once a
  project is defined then it can be added to the budget. Ms. Silva explained that the
  item is a placeholder in the budget, and the actual debt financing would have to be
  approved by the Board. GM Helliker noted that if the item is taken out of the budget,

then he would continue discussions/negotiations, in good faith, regarding
groundwater partnerships, and staff would have to obtain Board approval for any
upfront payment, once defined. In response to Director Zamorano’s question, Ms.
Silva explained that inclusion of this item in the budget has no impact on the
proposed rates. GM Helliker further explained that the Retail Financial Rate Plan
does include this item as potential debt with revenue from debt financing.

3. FY 2025-26 Liens for Delinquent Charges (R)

   President Costa opened hearing at 6:56 p.m.

   Ms. Grant, SJWD Board Secretary, confirmed that the hearing was properly noticed
   as required by law. GM Helliker reported that no comment letters were received.

   Mr. Barrett reviewed the staff report which was included in the Board packet. He
   explained that staff remain committed to working collaboratively with customers to
   bring accounts current and avoid placement of delinquent charges on the Property
   Tax Roll. In addition, he stated that staff will work to resolve as many delinquencies
   as possible prior to the first week of September, which is the deadline to remove
   accounts from the county tax rolls. Mr. Barrett is seeking the approval of Resolutions
   25-07 and 25-08 to ensure the collection of outstanding revenue.

   In response to President Costa’s question, Mr. Barrett explained that the District no
   longer disconnects water and instead uses the Property Tax Roll as the mechanism
   to collect from the property owner. In response to Vice President McRae’s question,
   Mr. Barrett explained that the property owner will see the delinquent water charge
   on their property tax bill which they pay in two installments and there is no effect on
   their credit rating. In response to Director Zamorano’s question, Mr. Barrett explained
   that the property owner and the renter, if it is a rental property, received the notice.
   Ms. Silva explained that, per District Ordinances, the owner receives the water bill
   and is ultimately responsible for unpaid water bills.

   Mr. Barrett confirmed that the exhibits to the resolutions are correct and if the account
   is paid prior to the submission to the county then that account will be removed from
   the exhibit list.

   President Costa opened the floor for public comment and there were no public
   comments made.

VI.   OLD BUSINESS

      1. Acceptance of the Retail Financial Plan and Water Rate Update Study and
         Adoption of Four-Year Schedule of Retail Water Rates (R)
         GM Helliker reported that there have been 14 meetings regarding this topic and the
         rate plan was developed to meet the Board’s direction. He added that workshops
         and a public hearing were conducted where some public comments were received.
         In addition, he reiterated the comments that the rate consultant (Mr. Mark
         Hildebrand), Ms. Silva and himself made that some of the public comments will be
         addressed during the retail rate structure project that will be launched in July. He
         explained that the project will look at different rate structures and the weighted
         average of the hydraulic factor. He explained that the District’s current rate structure
         includes the same meter charge for a 5/8”, a 3/4" and a 1” meter, which are used for
         single family residential customers, and noted that state law requires that the District
         charge similarly situated customers the same rate; therefore, staff will not be
         recommending that this change, especially since the District will be changing all
         residential customers to 1” meters. At Director Tobin’s request, Legal Counsel Jones
         will provide the Board with the legal reference regarding similarly situated customers.

         Ms. Silva reported that there were no additional protest letters received by the close
         of the public hearing on the rates; therefore, a total of seven (7) protest letters were
         received, which is below the 5,553 number that is required to prevent the Board from
         adopting the proposed rate increase. She reminded the Board that the proposed rate
         schedule in the Prop. 218 notice are the maximum rates that the Board could adopt
         without completing another Prop. 218 hearing process. She stated that staff
         recommends that the Board adopt the proposed rate schedule as presented, then
         have staff work with the rate consultant to provide a new rate schedule with the
         removal of the Retail Groundwater Production Facilities Project. She explained that
         the Board could vote for the January 1, 2026, rate schedule and forward to be
         reduced by the amount of that project. She recommends approving the rate schedule
         and directing staff to return in July or August with more information on how the
         removal of the project would affect the rates, as the Board could reduce the proposed
         rate schedule if needed.

In response to Director Tobin’s comment about never reducing rates, GM Helliker
explained that removal of the project would result in a lower revenue requirement
which would result in a lower rate. Ms. Silva commented that the consultant would
also need to analyyze and incorporate an amount into the rates to cover anticipated
costs of any groundwater partnership agreement that may occur.

Director Rich moved to adopt Resolution No. 25-10 adopting a four-year
schedule of retail water rates. President Costa seconded the motion.

Director Tobin voiced concern regarding the District’s rate setting process as it
relates to fairness, transparency, and compliance with the Prop. 218 requirements.
She pointed out a member of the public’s comments regarding the current rate
structure and encourages the Board to hold off adopting the proposed rates until a
rate structure analysis is completed.

Director Tobin moved for a substitute motion to delay adopting Resolution No.
25-10, which adopts a four-year schedule of retail water rates, until after a retail
rate schedule analysis is performed. Vice President McRae seconded the
motion.

Ms. Deshmane addressed the Board and stated that she received information from
GM Helliker which showed eleven different agencies that have different rates for ¾”,
5/8” and 1” meters so if that was against the law then how can they have those rates.
GM Helliker responded that he never said that those rates were against the law, and
instead had explained that the District has 95% single family residential customers
who are similarly situated and it is arbitrary whether they have a ¾” or a 1” meter,
and they are receiving the same service. GM Helliker pointed out that the rate
consultant structured the rates in a way that is legally supportable.

In response to Mr. Larsen’s question, Legal Counsel Jones informed the Board that
if any changes are made that increase any rate, then the Prop. 218 process will have
to be implemented again. Ms. Silva added that the current rate structure is legal,
allowed and reasonable according to our rate consultant. She commented that Ms.
Deshmane’s proposal regarding the weighted average of actual meter sizes, based
on the data that she was provided (which is not accurate at this time), would result
in a higher factor which would also be acceptable. However, the ramification of
utilizing the factor that Ms. Deshmane proposed would result in an approximate rate
increase of 3.5% for 97% of the District’s customers, while there would be a rate
decrease of 7-10% for the other customers. Ms. Silva informed the Board that an
additional Prop. 218 notice would need to be issued at a cost of approximately
$10,000 and it would be very confusing for the District’s customer base. Ms. Silva
pointed out that Ms. Deshmane’s methodology is not incorrect and it is logically
sound; however, the methodology that the rate consultant has done is legal and
defensible.

GM Helliker pointed out that the rate structure will be reviewed as part of the rate
review and the rate consultant did note that the amount of water that is being used
                                                               June 12, 2025, Board Minutes
                                                                                   Page 10


by customers with a 1” meter is similar to water use of those with a ¾” meter, so the
38 GPM is reflective of the use with a 1” meter, which will be verified in the rate
structure analysis. In addition, he informed the Board that the rates need to be put in
place in time to generate revenue to pay for the projects in the Retail Master Plan,
and it will take quite some time to complete the structure analysis process.

Vice President McRae voiced concern and does not agree with the wording in
Resolution 25-10 under the 4th Whereas, the 5th Whereas, the 8th Whereas, Item 2,
and Item 4, specifically as related to services provided in a gravity zone versus a
pump zone. He would like to have the Board delay any changes in rates until after a
rate structure study is completed.

Director Zamorano commented that Ms. Deshmane raised these issues for months
and questioned why it isn’t reflected. GM Helliker noted that Ms. Deshmane initially
discussed concerns regarding the fixed versus variable rate charges, then on May
20th she presented her calculations on weighted average. GM Helliker pointed out
that Prop. 218 does not require the District to charge a specific cost for every
individual property, it merely requires the District to develop a fair and equitable rate
structure. Ms. Deshmane pointed out that she has talked about fixed costs since the
beginning and the weighted average is a part of the fixed costs, and she believes
that the weighted average is a reason the fixed costs are high.

Ms. Deshmane stated that the methodology that the District uses is not
recommended by AWWA. GM Helliker commented that there is no recommended
methodology in the AWWA manual. He pointed out that Ms. Deshmane’s initial
complaint regarding fixed charges was that there was too much of the bill associated
with fixed charges. However, he previously had a discussion with Ms. Deshmane
and reviewed the Cost of Service Analysis which showed which costs are allocated
to the fixed charges. He explained that the retail rate structure analysis will review
the weighted average, the flow rate, and the equity associated with the different sizes
of meters.

In response to Mr. Barela and Director Zamorano’s question on the impact of
delaying the rate increase, President Costa responded that the rates will not go into
effect, will have to re-do the rate study, and will have to re-do the Prop. 218 hearing
process at an additional cost. President Costa commented that it makes sense to
put the rates in place then work on the rate structure; however, if adopted at this time
at a 2% rate increase, then that amount could be lowered without completing an
additional Prop. 218 hearing. GM Helliker explained that the process being proposed
is to look at the issues raised tonight and bring back a recommendation to the Board,
and if rate increases are recommended, then another Prop. 218 hearing process will
need to be implemented. GM Helliker commented that the current rate structure is
legally sound and has a proposed 2% rate increase for the next four years with the
caveat to remove the retail Groundwater Production Facilities Project. In addition,
GM Helliker pointed out that if the proposed rate increase is not adopted for July
2025, then the District will be facing a 4% rate increase in January in order to
generate the revenue required by the Retail Financial Plan.
