         Re:        SJWD Board Meeting 2/19/25, Agenda Item 3.1

         Dear President Costa and Members of the SJWD Board:

         Fair Oaks Water District (FOWD) notes with interest Item 3.1 of the San Juan Water District (SJWD) Board
         meeting agenda scheduled for February 19, 2025. It appears the SJWD Board intends to consider sending
         a letter to FOWD’s Board requesting a joint board meeting related to water transfers and water banking.
         We anticipate that placement of this item on the agenda may have arisen from direction provided by
         President Costa and the SJWD Board of Directors at its February 4 Board meeting.

         While the FOWD Board appreciates the need for family and regional coordination on a variety of matters,
         including potential water banking, the view of the FOWD Board at this juncture is that the suite of issues
         should be handled as part of confidential settlement discussions related to the legal challenge FOWD and
         Citrus Heights Water District brought against the SJWD wholesale rates. In our view, water banking and
         other issues between our respective agencies are bound up with the financial issues that drive the
         Proposition 26 litigation. For example, will SJWD treat its water banking staff and capital costs as
         wholesale or retail expenses? What if only certain family agencies desire to be involved in banking
         operations—how will SJWD costs in these efforts be fairly allocated among the family? How will any
         banking-related costs affect wholesale rates going forward? How will participating family members derive
         financial benefits from banking? Only with a comprehensive agreement regarding financial issues will we
         be able to move forward together on the numerous issues that are affected by the SJWD wholesale rate
         structure.

         In addition, both of our boards have already given policy direction that we each favor water banking
         between the SJWD Wholesale Agencies, provided financial, technical and other issues can be worked out.
         Thus, there is little need to go over the policy issues again. The FOWD Board is also concerned that holding
         a joint public meeting will necessarily lead to discussions that could interfere with the confidential nature
         of the coming litigation settlement talks which, we understand, could create a host of legal and other
         issues.
