STAFF REPORT
To:             Board of Directors
From:           Paul Helliker, General Manager

Date:           May 31, 2023
Subject:        Proposed amendments to ACWA Policy GO-2.9


RECOMMENDED ACTION
Staff request that the Board of Directors approve Resolution 23-07, opposing certain
revisions to ACWA Policy GO-2.9 and direct staff to distribute Resolution 23-07 to the
ACWA Region 4 Board and the ACWA Board of Directors.

BACKGROUND
The Association of California Water Agencies (ACWA) currently has a “code of conduct”
policy, to define expected behavior by attendees in ACWA events (conferences and
other meetings organized by ACWA). That policy currently defines unacceptable
behavior to be the following:

         Verbal abuse, such as the use of derogatory remarks, insults, and epithets or
         Verbal or physical conduct that a reasonable person would find threatening,
          intimidating, or humiliating.

This policy was used recently to sanction an ACWA Board member and a participant in
one of ACWA’s committee meetings, for comments that could reasonably be construed
as potentially constituting “sexual harassment” within the meaning of the law. Because
that terminology was not explicitly used in the ACWA policy, there were some questions
raised about whether or not the sanctions were supported by the ACWA policy. Some of
the proposed revisions appear to be intended to address this lack of clarity, e.g., the
revisions and additions to the definitions of disrespectful or abusive conduct, contained
on p. 2 of the policy – specifically items 1-4. Such prohibitions against behavior that may
reasonably be construed as constituting “sexual harassment” are typical of anti-
harassment policies, and are included in San Juan’s personnel manual.

Other proposed revisions to this policy are problematic, however. For example, the
reference to the code of conduct relating to “guid<ing> everyone who participates in
communications, <or> interactions related to the work of ACWA” is vague,
unnecessarily broad and could apply to any communication by anyone that happens to
reference a topic “related to the work of ACWA”. Such language would be tantamount to
censorship by ACWA of any communication that the Executive Director might deem
objectionable for any reason. Similar problems plague items 5 and 6 in the definitions of
disrespectful or abusive conduct. In item 5, “sharing materials without authorization from
the presenters”, “or other misuse of ACWA member resources” are similarly vague and
potentially illegal, if the proposal is intended to limit the distribution of public documents

(as a presentation made at an ACWA event would likely be). Item 6 is a catch-all
prohibition of “any other conduct not covered above deemed inappropriate”, including
conduct that might “jeopardize…ACWA<’s> reputation and goodwill”. This would open
to sanction any behavior that the ACWA Executive Director decided was somehow
inappropriate for any reason whatsoever, or which could be construed to jeopardize
ACWA’s “reputation” – a rather nebulous term. Such vague language could allow the
Executive Director to sanction anyone who made any comment or sent any email that
he/she did not appreciate or concur with, and then deemed to be inappropriate or
potentially damaging to ACWA’s reputation. Such language in an ACWA policy is
unacceptably broad and subjective and could lead to attempts to censor
communications by anyone. Items 5 and 6 should be deleted in their entirety from the
policy.

The policy also suffers from a lack of due process – the Executive Director would be
allowed to make decisions to bar people permanently from ACWA events without any
oversight or possibility of appeal. At a minimum, an appeals process needs to be
included in the policy.

For all of these reasons, these revisions need to be tabled. If they are to be
reconsidered, it must be with significantly more input from the ACWA Board and
membership. Staff recommend that the San Juan Board adopt Resolution 23-07, to
articulate its opposition to the proposed revisions and convey that position to the Region
4 Board and the entire ACWA Board of Directors.

              RESOLUTION OF THE BOARD OF DIRECTORS
                  OF THE SAN JUAN WATER DISTRICT
      OPPOSING REVISIONS TO POLICY GO-2.9 OF THE ASSOCIATION OF
                    CALIFORNIA WATER AGENCIES


      WHEREAS, the San Juan Water District is a member district of the Association
of California Water Agencies (ACWA);

      WHEREAS, the ACWA board of Directors adopted Policy GO-2.9 on February 3,
2023, to establish a code of conduct for participants in ACWA events;

      WHEREAS, ACWA staff have proposed revising this policy via a draft set of
revisions to Policy GO-2.9, to be considered by the ACWA Board of Directors at its June
2, 2023 meeting;

      WHEREAS, the proposed revisions include references to their being applicable
to “communications, and any other interactions related to the work of ACWA”, and

      WHEREAS, the proposed revisions include restrictions on sharing public
documents, such as presentations from ACWA conferences and other “member
resources”;

      WHEREAS, the proposed revisions include a reference to constraints on “any
other conduct not covered above deemed inappropriate” and any conduct that may
“jeopardize… ACWA reputation and goodwill” (sic);

      WHEREAS, the policy allows the ACWA Executive Director to unilaterally decide
what conduct is unallowable pursuant to the policy, and provides no mechanism for
review or appeal of the decision; and

      NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Board of Directors of the San
Juan Water District finds that the proposed revisions to Policy GO-2.9 are vague,
arbitrary and apply inappropriately to behavior or communications beyond those
occurring at ACWA events; and
       BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that, for the reasons stated above, the San Juan
Board of Directors opposes the proposed revisions in Policy GO-2.9.
