VI.    DISCUSSION AND ACTION ITEMS

       1. Long Term Warren Act Contract (LTWAC) (W)
          Mr. Greg Zlotnick reviewed a written staff report which was included in the Board
          packet. He explained that the new long term Warren Act Contract is required for the
          District to receive Placer County Water Agency water delivered by the Bureau of
          Reclamation through Folsom Dam, as the current contract expires February 28,
          2026.

          Mr. Zlotnick reported that a negotiation session was held last week, resulting in a
          draft contract that is substantially similar to the current agreement. He noted that the
          contract and associated environmental documentation have been posted for a 30-
                                                              January 21, 2026, Board Minutes
                                                                                      Page 3


   day public comment period which will conclude after the February Board meeting.
   Staff is therefore requesting the Board to authorize the General Manager to execute
   the contract on behalf of the District when it’s available to do so. He also explained
   that the District will be required to file a Notice of Exemption (NOE) to comply with
   CEQA requirements, and that the Board is being requested to authorize the General
   Manager to sign those documents as well.

   Mr. Zlotnick further advised that it was still possible the environmental documentation
   and/or contract may require review by the Assistant Secretary for Water and Science
   in Washington, D.C., which had not been required previously. If such review occurs,
   a one-year extension of the current contract would be necessary to continue
   deliveries of PCWA water until the new contract is available to sign. He explained
   that was why the Board was also being asked to authorize the General Manager to
   sign the extension and its associated NOE if it became necessary to do so.

   Vice President Tobin moved to approve a CEQA Notice of Exemption related
   to taking action to authorize the General Manager to sign a LTWAC with the
   United States Bureau of Reclamation (Reclamation), for which Reclamation
   has prepared an Environmental Assessment pursuant to the National
   Environmental Policy Act. Director Zamorano seconded the motion, and it
   carried unanimously.

   Director Zamorano moved to approve Resolution 26-02 authorizing the
   General Manager to sign the new LTWAC with Reclamation so long as it is
   substantially similar to the District’s current Warren Act Contract. Director
   Machado seconded the motion, and it carried unanimously.

   Vice President Tobin moved to approve a CEQA Notice of Exemption related
   to taking action to authorize the General Manager to sign a 1-year extension of
   the District’s current Warren Act Contract with Reclamation. Director Machado
   seconded the motion, and it carried unanimously.

   Director McRae moved to approve Resolution 26-03 authorizing the General
   Manager to sign a 1-year extension of the District’s current Warren Act
   Contract with Reclamation. Director Machado seconded the motion, and it
   carried unanimously.

       7. Conjunctive Use and Groundwater Banking Activities Update (W & R)
          Mr. Zlotnick reported that he attended the regional groundwater bank program
          committee meeting and advised that WIIN Act funding has been approved and is
          expected to be received shortly. He noted, however, that a funding shortfall remains
          and staff will work with consultants to reduce the gap. He also explained that funding
          for federal acknowledgment of the water bank is currently unfunded and poses a
          significant gap, expressing concern that CVP contractor agencies should not bear a
          disproportionate share of the cost since all participants contributed to the overall
          program.

          In response to President Costa’s question about attending the Cap-To-Cap event in
          Washington, D.C., Mr. Zlotnick stated that he does not believe the event justifies the
          expense or time commitment and recommended that the District instead coordinate
          Washington, D.C. meetings through its federal lobbyist.

4. DIRECTOR OF OPERATIONS’ REPORT
   4.1 Miscellaneous District Issues and Correspondence
        Mr. Barela reported that negotiations with the Bureau concerning the Hinkle 2
        project are currently on hold while staff work to secure the Parcel C lease.
        Once discussions regarding Parcel C are finalized, negotiations for Hinkle 2
        are expected to resume. He explained that the Hinkle 2 project involves
        securing USBR-owned property south of the reservoir for construction of a
        second reservoir, which would provide emergency storage, allow continued
        operations when Hinkle Reservoir is offline, and support future cover and liner
        replacement efforts.

         Mr. Barela reported that PCWA completed their study on the raw water pipeline
         that will potentially deliver water to the City of Roseville, which was originally
         designed for a 30-inch pipeline. He explained that San Juan is inquiring if a 36
         or 42-inch pipeline could be installed to allow up to an additional 20 MGD that
         could be delivered to San Juan. He explained that there are some alignment
         issues and they will be discussing this at a meeting on Friday.
5. DIRECTOR OF ENGINEERING SERVICES’ REPORT
   5.1 Miscellaneous District Issues and Correspondence

        site and provide professional input regarding the feasibility of a percolation

        4. OTHER REPORTS, CORRESPONDENCE, COMMENTS, IDEAS AND SUGGESTIONS
           President Costa commented on Mr. Zlotnick’s groundwater banking update, noting
           that the District may receive credit for water released from Baldwin Reservoir into
           the creek. Mr. Zlotnick responded that while the concept is viable, several factors
           must be evaluated, including property ownership along the creek (City of Folsom),
           required technical analysis, resource availability, and environmental considerations.
           He added that such a project could also require assessing the feasibility of
           maintaining environmental benefits during periods without water flow and confirmed
           that Mr. Barela and Mr. Pierson are working on the matter.
