Water Budget Rate Structures
Water Budgets are a type of inclining block rate with block widths that are individualized to each
account, typically based on indoor and outdoor needs.
Water Budget rates are designed to address the following weaknesses associated with standard
inclining block rates:
    • Equity concerns for large families / lots
    • Revenue stability during droughts
    • Targeted conservation signal


Water Budget Rates disadvantages:
    • Large implementation costs
         • Can be significant depending on utility’s current capabilities
    • Higher on-going administrative effort and cost
    • Particularly difficult to implement for commercial accounts

       Benefits:                                        Benefits:
       - Revenue stability                              - Affordability
       - Alignment with cost of service                 - Conservation signal




California courts have given water utilities latitude in how much revenue to recover
through variable rates.
Historically, CA water utilities have targeted at least 70% variable revenue due to BMPs
published by the CUWCC.
More recently, driven by revenue volatility during droughts, the balance has shifted
towards more fixed revenue.
