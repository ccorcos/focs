      2. Water Supply Agreement with Carmichael Water District (W)
         GM Helliker informed the Board that there will be three items for the Board to discuss
         regarding an agreement to deliver water to Carmichael Water District (CWD). The other
         two items are on the regular Board meeting agenda. A staff report was provided in the
         Board packet which will be attached to the meeting minutes.

         Director Costa moved to make a finding that the up to 1,500 acre-feet of pre-14 water
         rights water contemplated to be potentially sold to Carmichael Water District is
         surplus to the supplies available to the District to meet Wholesale Customer Agency
         demands. Director Hanneman seconded the motion.

         In response to Director Costa’s question, GM Helliker explained that this agreement is for
         the potential sale of water to CWD since at this time they are uncertain if their water rights
         will be curtailed. In addition, GM Helliker explained that should CWD request water then
         CWD would cover the cost that the District would incur for purchasing water from Placer

STAFF REPORT
To:       Board of Directors
From:     Greg Zlotnick
          Water Resources Manager

Date:     May 26, 2021
Subject: Agreement to sell Pre-14 Water Rights Water to Carmichael Water District if its
         water rights are curtailed


Recommended Actions

Make a finding that the up to 1,500 acre-feet (AF) of pre-14 water rights water contemplated to
be potentially sold to Carmichael Water District is surplus to the supplies available to the District
to meet Wholesale Customer Agency (WCA) demands;

Background

With this water year being one of the driest on record, the State Water Resources Control
Board has indicated it will be moving to curtail all post-14 water rights in the coming months.
Should this occur, Carmichael Water District (CWD) will lose access to its water rights water,
which is a significant part of its supply.
On the agenda for the May 26 Regular Board meeting are two related items – approval of a
Notice of Exemption for a potential delivery of up to 1,500 AF to CWD, and a water supply
agreement with CWD for up to 1,500 AF of water to be delivered from the District during the
June-September window, to provide mutual aid to CWD should CWD request such supplies,
which is within the District’s historic place of use.
Staff consulted with the WCAs earlier this year, to determine what their projected demands for
water supply would be this year, and the Board previously determined that the transfer of up to
6,000 AF of pre-1914 water right water to the Sacramento Suburban Water District (SSWD)
would be surplus to WCA needs. Staff has similarly determined that the potential 1,500 acre-
feet of pre-1914 water to potentially be sold to CWD is surplus to the supplies available to the
District to meet the demands of the WCAs.
At this time, after transferring less than 2,000 AF to SSWD, that transfer is being halted to try
to preserve surface water in the basin. Consequently, the Board’s prior surplus determination
for the SSWD transfer would more than cover the up to 1,500 AF in emergency, mutual aid
water contemplated to be potentially sold to CWD should they request it. Even if the SSWD
transfer was not halted, staff is confident, based on expected WCA demands and available
supplies, that the 1,500 AF would still be available from surplus supplies above those needed
to meet WCA demands.
Action by Carmichael Water District
The Carmichael Water District Board of Directors approved the agreement at its meeting on
May 18th.
