                                                   TOPIC

       Adopt Ordinance 2025-01, designating Fire Hazard Severity Zones, pursuant to
       Government Code Section 51179, whereas local agencies are required to adopt, by
       ordinance, the fire hazard severity zone (FHSZ) designations within 120 days of
       receiving the recommendations from the State Fire Marshal. The effective date of
       Ordinance 2025-01 would be June 12, 2025, or as soon as possible thereafter.


                                    BACKGROUND-OR-SUMMARY

       Government Code Section 51178 requires the California State Fire Marshal to identify ,
       and recommend areas within the state as moderate, high, or very high fire hazard
       severity zones based on consistent statewide criteria. On March 10, 2025, the District
       received the 2025 Fire Hazard Severity Zone (FHSZ) Map from the State Fire Marshal,
       identifying designated zones within District's jurisdiction.

       On May 22, 2025, the Board held a public hearing and introduced Ordinance 2025-01
       adopting the FHSZ Map.


                                                DISCUSSION

       The District has not reduced or altered the fire hazard severity designations established
       by the State Fire Marshal, and this adoption is consistent with the model ordinance
       requirements by the State Fire Marshal. The District finds that adoption of the map is in
       the interest of public safety, supports fire prevention and mitigation efforts, and ensures
       compliance with applicable state law.

       No fiscal impact.

     FROM:         Anthony Peck, Assistant Chief - Wellness, Health and Safety

     SUBJECT:      Request for Exception to the Formal Solicitation Process -
                   Professional Services Contract with Concern for Employee
                   Assistance Program Services


                                                 TOPIC
     Staff requests the Board of Director's adopt the Resolution approving a one-year contract
     with Concern Employee Assistance Program, doing business as Concern, for Employee
     Assistance Program (EAP) services effective July 1, 2025 without a competitive solicitation
     process.

                                            BACKGROUND
     The Sacramento Metropolitan Fire District (District) values the health and wellbeing of its
     members and is committed to providing support to members who may be experiencing
     personal, health, or work-related issues. Providing EAP services is one way in which the
     District may assist members cope with a variety of life-events, including events unique to
     the first-responder culture. As a benefit to our employees, the District currently offers EAP
     services through Concern. This program provides employees and eligible family members
     referrals to licensed, professional therapists/counselors. Included in this benefit are eight
     free counseling sessions per person, per issue, per 12-month period for each employee and

                                                DISCUSSION

         Over the last several years, the District's Peer Support Team has worked to educate
         members on the services available through our EAP and have connected members with
         these essential resources when they need them most. In 2023, the District's prior EAP
         provider, Managed Health Network (MHN), announced that they were leaving the EAP
         market, thus causing the District to unexpectedly find a replacement EAP provider. To
         ensure members had continuous access to EAP services, the District entered into a one-
         year agreement with Concern and elected to enter into a subsequent one-year agreement
         in 2024 as opposed to issuing a Request for Proposals (RFP) as members were already
         faced with learning to navigate a new EAP the year prior.

         The Peer Support Team has been very pleased with Concern's management of the
         District's contract, level of service provided and quality of clinicians as they have a first
         responder specialty panel of counselors to assist with the unique needs of our members
         and their families. For these reasons and to prevent members who are currently receiving
         treatment from experiencing possible disruptions in care, the Peer Support Team preferred
         to seek out an existing contract between Concern and another governmental agency to
         piggy-back off of as the District's Purchasing and Contracting Policy permits exceptions to
         competitive bidding when services are obtainable from suppliers which have a current
         contract in force with another governmental agency. Unfortunately, an appropriate contract
         was not available for piggy-back use.

         With efforts to utilize a piggy-backable contract being unsuccessful and the Peer Support
         Team's desire to offer members continuous EAP services provided by Concern and
         Concern's clinicians, the District wishes to enter into one more one-year agreement. The
         District's Purchasing and Contracting policy states that purchases may be allowed without
         competitive bidding under circumstances when the branch Deputy Chief and Purchasing
         Agent determine that making of a purchase without competitive proposals is reasonably
         necessary for the conduct of District business.

                                               FISCAL IMPACT

         The estimated cost of a 1-year contract with Concern is $202,602.00. The cost is $25.62
         per first responder employee per month and $5.46 per non-first responders per month. This
         cost is budgeted as part of the benefits package that the District offers to its employees.


                                            RECOMMENDATION

         Staff recommends that the Board of Directors adopt the Resolution approving an exception
         to the formal solicitation process and authorize the Fire Chief or his designee to negotiate,
         execute, and administer a one-year agreement, which is materially similar to the attached
         agreement, with Concern for the provision of EAP services. This agreement will be effective
         July 1, 2025.

                                                        TOPIC

   Request to adopt a resolution to authorize the Fire Chief or his designee to execute and administer
   the Agreement Between the County of Sacramento and the Sacramento Metropolitan Fire District
   for Hazardous Materials Response Teams . .

                                                   BACKGROUND

   The Sacramento Metropolitan Fire District (District) provides hazardous materials (HazMat)
   response services throughout Sacramento County under a June 20, 2023, agreement with the
   County. The agreement outlines the scope of services to be provided by the District and the
   associated compensation for such services. Initially set to expire June 30, 2024, the agreement
   was extended through June 30, 2025, to allow time for good faith negotiations for a revised fee
   methodology. As the changes required approval from all benefitting agencies, the one-year
   extension served as an interim agreement while negotiations continued on a multi-year contract.

                                                    DISCUSSION

   The County has expressed interest in continuing its contractual relationship with the District for
   HazMat response services. The proposed new agreement is materially similar to the existing
   agreement, with the following notable revisions:

         •      The Sacramento Area Sewer District and Sacramento Regional County Sanitation District
                have been removed as they are no longer participating agencies.
         •      The agreement will span a three-year term, from July 1, 2025, through June 30, 2028.
         •      There is an option for one additional two-year extension upon mutual agreement

                                                  FISCAL IMPACT

   Under the proposed agreement, the District will continue to receive 33.34% of the total amount
   collected by the County from the participating agencies. Estimated compensation for FY 2025/26
   is $225,629, which has been included in the District's proposed Preliminary Budget.

                                                RECOMMENDATION

   Staff recommends that the Board authorize the Fire Chief or designee to execute and administer
   the Agreement between the County of Sacramento and the Sacramento Metropolitan Fire District
   for Hazardous Materials Response Teams.

  DATE:          June 12, 2025

  TO:            Board of Directors

  SUBJECT:       Revisions to Fiscal Year (FY) 2024/25 Budget



                                                TOPIC

  Staff is requesting revisions to the FY 2024/25 Midyear Budget to address unanticipated
  expenditures in excess of budgeted amounts.


                                           BACKGROUND

  The Sacramento Metropolitan Fire District (District) adopted the FY 2024/25 Midyear
  Budget on March 13, 2025. Since adoption, there have been unanticipated expenses in
  excess of budgeted amounts that now require fund transfers to align expenditures with
  budget savings and updated revenues.

                                            DISCUSSION
                                                              '
  The amendments to the FY 2024/25 Midyear Budget will result to a net increase in
  expenditures of $2.26 million which include:
      •   $300,000 in unanticipated charges from Sacramento County related to a new
          property tax administration system. All subordinate special districts and cities must
          fund this IT project at the amounts and timing specified by Sacramento County.
      •   $1 .7 million in labor expenses above budget, mainly attributable to higher vacation
          and sick leave payouts for separating employees, and fewer transitions of Metro
          Medic Program personnel to grant-funded firefighter positions.
      •   $1.1 million in savings from the FY 2024/25 services and supplies budget.
      •   $1 .32 million transfer to the Capital Facilities Fund for capital equipment that will
          be subsequently fully reimbursed in FY 2025/26 by the Measure O Building Fund.

  The amendments to the FY 2024/25 Midyear Budget will result to an increase in revenues
  of $936,000, including:
      •   $317,000 in federal reimbursements for District costs incurred in 2021 for COVID-
          19-related emergency services.
      •   $618,000 in unanticipated revenues associated with EMS transports in FY
          2024/25.


                              Serving Sacramento and Placer Counties
                                                                                                         P106
                                  FISCAL IMPACT

The proposed amendment will increase spending by $2,260,027, offset by an increase in
revenues of $936,000 and reimbursements from Measure O for approved projects The
net result is a temporary General Fund Balance decrease of $1,324,027 million at year-
end, which will be offset by reimbursements from the proceeds of Measure O bond sale
that will be reflected in the FY2025/26 Final Budget.

Staff recommends the approval of the attached resolutions.
