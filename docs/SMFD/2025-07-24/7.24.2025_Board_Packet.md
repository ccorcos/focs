                                                 TOPIC

     Staff is seeking Board of Directors' approval to renew the Sacramento Metropolitan Fire
     District's (District) contract with lmageTrend for an additional three years without a
     competitive solicitation process.

                                            BACKGROUND

     The District has maintained a contract with lmageTrend since September 2014, which
     began with the implementation of the District's Electronic Patient Care (ePCR) system
     as a result of a formal solicitation process. In 2020, the District's National Fire Incident
     Reporting System (NFIRS), arson investigations, and data analytics systems were
     upgraded to be technologically in line with the lmageTrend ePCR system. In 2022, a
     test site was added to limit the impact of interface updates for end users.


                                             DISCUSSION

     Since the implementation of the ePCR system, the District has experienced numerous
     advances in technology and the Emergency Medical Services (EMS) system itself.
     lmageTrend is already integrated with CAD, local hospitals, neighboring agencies, and
     Lifepak 15 monitors. District EMS System Technicians have r~ceived specialized
     training in the lmageTrend program, and regularly work with a specially-assigned
     ImageTrend account advisor to maintain system optimization.

     lmageTrend allows for the production of fire reports, provides data collection, and the
     built-in tools needed for budgeting, grants, training, and more. The Investigations
     module permits fire investigators to document all aspects of their investigation, including
     witness details, evidence grids, and weather-related information. The Continuum
     module provides active data monitoring to enhance patient care and the statistics used
     in operational and administrative decision making.

         District staff has specialized training and extensive expertise utilizing lmageTrend
         software. Transitioning to an alternative software solution would require extensive
         retraining across multiple departments, incurring substantial costs in both time and
         funding. These costs include but are not limited to: procurement of new software,
         onboarding and implementation, downtime during the transition period, and staff hours
         allocated to training rather than core responsibilities. Maintaining the current contract
         ensures continuity, preserves operational efficiency, and avoids the risks and costs
         associated with disruption and knowledge loss. Continuing our contract with
         lmageTrend will provide data and reporting continuity and benefit the District as a
         whole.


                                              FISCAL IMPACT

         The estimated total cost for three 1-year subscription terms is $704,405.78. The cost for
         the first year is $227.896.66, which has been included in the FY 25/26 preliminary
         budget. Each year following the first year, the subscription fee may increase, but shall
         not exceed 3% of the then-current price in any given year.


                                           RECOMMENDATION

         Staff recommends the Board of Directors approve an exception to the formal solicitation
         process and authorize the Fire Chief or his designee to negotiate, execute, and
         administer the renewal of the lmageTrend subscription agreement. The term of this
         agreement includes three 1-year subscription terms.

                                                TOPIC

  Authority to execute Memorandum of Understating (MOU) to carry out enhanced services
  to the City of Rancho Cordova


                                           BACKGROUND

  On May 13, 2025 the City of Rancho Cordova (City) allocated $825,000 in its Fiscal Year
  (FY) 2025/26 budget to secure enhanced fire protection and emergency medical services
  from the Sacramento Metropolitan Fire District (District). On July 7, 2025, the City
  approved a memorandum of understanding (MOU) to direct expenditures and reporting
  associated with these enhanced services, which include:

      •    Squad 61, assigned to Station 61 , will be principally assigned to mitigate
           emergency incidents in its first due area and occasionally respond to incidents
           within the District's jurisdiction,
      •    Squad 61 will consist of 6 firefighters working two-day shifts, providing
           uninterrupted services to City, and
      •    Squad 61 will be in a Type V engine.

  The MOU must be approved by the District to be fully executed.


                                            DISCUSSION

  The proposed MOU includes robust reporting and performance measurement
  requirements to ensure the funds are properly accounted for and spent on related
  projects.

  Progress reports will begin three months after the effective date of the agreement and
  include a narrative of work accomplished during the reporting period and a detailed
  expenditure report of payrol! costs by pay period for Squad 61 personnel. Additionally,

                              Serving Sacramento and Placer Counties

the progress reports will include a comparison of actual accomplishments to the
objectives indicated in the MOU, including measurement against agreed-upon metrics
and outcomes. Metrics used to gauge performance will include response times, call
volume and distribution, nonstructural fire response metrics, Squad 61 operations, trends
and analysis, and impact assessments.


                                   FISCAL IMPACT

Execution of the contract will enable the District to secure up to $825,000 in
reimbursement funding, an amount approximately equivalent to one-half of the staffing
cost to operate Squad 61. City funds will be paid quarterly upon submission and approval
of the District's progress reports.

Staff recommends the Board of Directors authorize the Fire Chief to sign the MOU to
provide enhanced services to the City and facilitate the acceptance of up to $825,000 in
payment for those services in FY 2025/26.

    •   Project Description. Sacramento Metropolitan Fire District will provide the following

            jurisdiction. See Station 61 's first due map attached. ·

                benefit the citizens of Rancho Cordova. It is believed that Station 61, already the
                busiest fire station in Rancho Cordova, will benefit significantly from the
                continuation of this resource. The squad unit serves as the first responder to
                medical calls, enabling the fire engine to remain available for other emergencies,

                increases the department's ability to provide timely and efficient services to the

                equipped to handle emergency demands while improving overall job satisfaction
                and firefighter safety within Rancho Cordova.
