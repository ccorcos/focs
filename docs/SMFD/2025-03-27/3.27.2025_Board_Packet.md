                                            TOPIC

Staff requests that the Sacramento Metropolitan Fire District (District) Board of
Directors (Board) approve the California Employers' Pension Prefunding Trust
(CEPPT) agreement and election with the California Public Employees' Retirement
System (CalPERS), and authorize the disbursement of deposited funds by approving
the Delegation of Authority to Request Disbursements form.

                                      BACKGROUND

On March 13, 2025, the Board approved the FY 2024/25 Midyear Budget containing
an allocation of $1 million to establish a pension trust fund with CalPERS. The CEPPT
is a tax-exempt trust organized under Section 115 of the Internal Revenue Code to
facilitate employer contributions to offset long-term pension obligations. The CEPPT
started in 2019 and now has more than 90 local agency members.

                                        DISCUSSION

The CEPPT will enable the District to invest funds set aside for pension expenses,
reduce the District's pension liability, improve the overall level of pension cost funding,
and permit the Board to use the trust funds on pension expenses at a time of their
choosing. The amount and frequency of deposits and withdrawals to and from the
CEPPT are at the discretion of the District.

CEPPT funds are invested with other local agency funds and will generate interest
earnings on the invested funds. The net annual return since plan inception five years
ago is approximately 4. 7 percent.

The attached resolution establishes the contractual relationship with CalPERS for
CEPPT investments. It includes CalPERS' obligations to report to their local agency
investors and the Board, restrictions on use of deposits, information on reports and
statements, disbursement requirements, fee requirements·, and other general
provisions.

                                             TOPIC

Staff requests that the Sacramento Metropolitan Fire District (District) Board of
Directors (Board) approve a contract award to Stradling Yocca Carlson & Rauth, LLP
(Stradling, LLP) for the services specified in Request for Proposals (RFP) 25-02: Bond
and Disclosure Counsel.

                                       BACKGROUND

On November 5, 2024, District voters approved Measure 0, authorizing the District to
issue up to $415 million in General Obligation (GO) bonds for capital projects, which
the District plans to issue and commit over the next 1O years. The proceeds from the
issuance and sale of GO bonds may only be used for the construction, expansion,
reconstruction, rehabilitation, replacement, furnishing, and equipping of fire facilities;
the acquisition or lease of real property for fire facilities; and the acquisition of fire and
emergency medical vehicles, apparatus, and capital equipment.

To ensure compliance with legal, regulatory, and reporting responsibilities, the District
determined that the services of a bond and disclosure counsel is needed.

                                         DISCUSSION

On March 3, 2025, the District issued RFP 25-02 to solicit proposals from qualified law
firms to provide bond counsel and disclosure counsel services. The District has not
previously issued GO bonds or used the services of bond and disclosure counsel for
that purpose.

The District received a total of three proposals in response to RFP 25-02. The
proposals were independently evaluated and scored by an evaluation committee using
the following criteria:

      •   Experience and Qualifications
      •   Approach and Work Plan
      •   Past Performance
      •   Fee Proposal



                             Serving Sacramento and Placer Counties


                                                                                                              P33
Page 2


         Stradling, LLP was the highest scoring proposer. The firm has been ranked one of the
         top bond counsel firms in the state based on bond size and issuances, and has
         completed more than 870 transactions as bond counsel in the last five years alone.

                                              FISCAL IMPACT

         The cost of the contract will be approximately $100,000 for the first bond issuance, and
         subsequent transaction fees will cost approximately $90,000. (Staff estimates up to three
         subsequent issuances.) Special services outside of the scope of the bond and disclosure
         activities identified in RFP 25-02 would be billed at a per-hour rate. The District anticipates
         that all costs would be paid from Measure O bond proceeds.               '

                                            RECOMMENDATION

         Staff recommends that the Board adopt a resolution to approve the award of RFP 25-
         02: Bond and Disclosure Counsel to Stradling, LLP, and authorize the Fire Chief or
         his designee to negotiate, execute, and administer a professional service agreement
         materially similar to the attached agreement for the provision of bond and disclosure
         counsel services for a five-year period, with the option to extend for up to five additional
         one-year periods.

     Request to adopt a pre-qualification process for general contractors that wish to bid on the Fire

     The Sacramento Metropolitan Fire District's (District) FY2022/23 Capital Improvement Plan,
     adopted by the Board of Directors (Board) on June 9, 2022, authorized the implementation of

     consideration.

     There is no fiscal impact related to adopting the proposed pre-qualification system.

SECTION 02- PROJECT INFORMATION

A. PROJECT BACKGROUND
Metro Fire is an independent special district formed in 2000 with a mission to provide professional and
compassionate protection, education, and service to the community. Metro Fire is the largest
fire/Emergency Medical Services (EMS) agency in Sacramento County, serving a population of over
720,000 in 23 distinct communities over its 359 square mile jurisdiction, which includes large portions of
unincorporated Sacramento County, the cities of Citrus Heights and Ranchd Cordova, and a small area of
Placer County.

In 2003, Metro Fire's Board of Directors authorized the purchase of real property located at 8101
Bradshaw Road in Sacramento as the intended site for a future fire station. In 2013, the District's Growth
Analysis identified the1number and location of new fire stations that will be required to serve greenfield
development areas. The analysis anticipated that the Project will serve portions of the Vineyard Springs,
Florin-Vineyard Gap, and North Vineyard Station plan areas in southern _Sacramento County. In 2022, the
District determined that it was necessary to move forward with the development of the Project in order
to meet adopted standards and service level objectives.

B.   PROJECT DESCRIPTION
The Project includes construction of a 10,148 SF one-story fire station with three (3) apparatus bays and
eight (8) dormitories, a storage/truck wash building, emergency standby generator, civil and utility
sitework, and other related items, developed on a 4.77-acre site.

The construction of the P_roject will be completed under a public works contract administered by the
District with assistance from the District's Architect of Record (COAR Design Group) and Construction
Manager (Kitchell CEM) in accordance with a strict schedule whereby time is of the essence.

The bid solicitation process for the project is anticipated for Summer 2025. Construction is anticipated to
begin in Fall 2025 and last for 14 months.

The estimated construction cost is $12,900,000.

C.   PROJECT REQUIREMENTS
The Project is considered a public works project and is subject to compliance monitoring and enforcement
by the Department of Industrial Relations (DIR).

No Contractor or subcontractor may be listed on a bid proposal for a public works project unless registered
with the DIR pursuant to Labor Code section 1725.5 [with limited exceptions from this requirement for
bid purposes only under Labor Code section 1771.l(a)]. No Contractor or subcontractor may be awarded
a contract for public work on a public works project, unless registered with the DIR pursuant to Labor
Code section 17'25.5.

The Project is also subject to the requirements of Section 1720 and 1770 et seq. of the California Labor
Code requiring the payment of prevailing wages, the employment and training of apprentices and
compliance with other applicable requirements. Contractors and all subcontractors who perform work on
the Project are required to comply with these requirements.

The project is not subject to a Project Labor Agreement (PLA).

                                                     INSTRUCTIONS

The pre-qualification questionnaire must be completed in its entirety by Contractors seeking pre-qualification
and submitted in accordance with the instructions outlined in the RFQ. Any required supplemental
information must be submitted as a separate attachment in Adobe Acrobat PDF format.

PARTS I and II are not scored, but are required to provide general information about the Contractor.

PART Ill is scored on a pass/fail basis, based on the Contractor's ability to meet minimum requirements.
Contractors who fail to meet minimum requirements will not be eligible for pre-qualification.

Contractors are required to have a passing score for both PART IV and PART V to be considered for pre-
qualification.

For PART IV, Contractors must pass with a score of 84 or higher. Scores will be assigned as shown in PART
IV, beginning on page 9.

For PART V, Contractors must pass with a score of 32 or higher. Scores will be assigned as shown in PART
V, beginning on page 13.

PART VI must be completed and signed by an individual who has the legal authority to bind the
Contractor on whose behalf that individual is signing.
