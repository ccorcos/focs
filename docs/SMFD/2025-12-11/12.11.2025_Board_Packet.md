       SUBJECT:        Amendment to FY2025/26 Capital Improvement Program Plan

                                                TOPIC
       Request to approve an amendment to the FY2025/26 Capital Improvement Program Plan.

                                                  BACKGROUND
       For the past several years, the District's Capital Improvement Program Plan has identified a
       need to replace the District's inventory of 800 MHz and VHF portable radios as current
       equipment is beyond its useful life and no longer supported by the manufacturer. To date, this
       need has been deferred due to funding limitations. In an attempt to secure alternative funding
       for the project, the District participated alongside six other local fire agencies in a regional grant
       request through the FY2024 Assistance to Firefighters Grant (AFG) Program that was submitted
       by Sacramento Fire Department for the replacement of portable radios throughout the region,
       however this request was unsuccessful.

                                                 DISCUSSION
       On September 11, 2025, the Sacramento Metropolitan Fire District's (District) Board of Directors
       adopted Resolution 2025-061 to adopt the FY2025/26 - FY2029/30 Capital Improvement Plan
       (GIP), which did not include funding for Project 26-44 Portable Radio Replacement, since there
       was a chance that the project would be funded through a grant. Now that grant funding is not
       available, an amendment to the adopted FY2025/26 GIP is required in order to move forward
       with the project and allocate funding. The requested amendment would authorize the purchase
       of 310 portable radios and necessary components to replace obsolete equipment.

                                                FISCAL IMPACT
       The total cost for the project is $3,135,902. $3,004,049 is considered capital equipment eligible
       for funding with bond proceeds, and will be added to the budget through a budget amendment
       to the Measure O Building Fund (0 Fund). $131,853 for licensing and warranty costs will be
       funded via a budget transfer within the General Fund (A Fund) and will not require an
       amendment to the budget.

                                        RECOMMENDATION
       Staff recommends the Board adopt the attached resolutions amending the FY2025/26 Capital
       Improvement Program Plan and amending the budget for the Measure O Building Fund 2120.

     SUBJECT: . Deferred Compensation Plan Committee Charter



                                                 TOPIC

     Consider adopting a Resolution approving the Sacramento Metropolitan Fire District
     Deferred Compensation Plan Committee Charter.

                                            BACKGROUND

     The Sacramento Metropolitan Fire District established a Deferred Compensation Plan on
     August 8, 2001 through Resolution No. 43-01, pursuant to Section 457 of the Internal
     Revenue Code. The Deferred Compensation Plan provides eligible employees with an
     opportunity to defer a portion of their compensation for retirement savings.

     To ensure effective oversight and compliance with federal regulations, the Board previously
     appointed a Deferred Compensation Committee to administer the Plan on behalf of the ,
     District. The Committee oversees investments options, vendor management, participant
     education, and compliance matters related to the Plan.

     The proposed Deferred Compensation Plan Committee Charter establishes a
     comprehensive governance framework for the Committee. The Charter defines the
     Committee's ·structure, responsibilities, and authority to manage the administration of the
     District's 457 Plan. It also clarifies appointment procedures, meeting requirements, fiduciary
     responsibilities, and investment oversight provisions.

     Key elements of the Charter include:

        •   Composition of nine voting members, appointed by the Board and serving without
            compensation.
        •   Requirement for the Committee to meet at least quarterly to review plan
            performance, participant demographics, investment options, and participant
            education.
        •   Establishment of roles such as Chairperson, Vice Chair, Treasurer, and Education
            Officer. -
        •   Clear standards for fiduciary responsibility and compliance with Internal Revenue
            Code Section 457 and regulations thereunder.



                                  Serving Sacramento and Placer Counties


                                                                                                            P12
Page 2


            •   Authority for the Committee to recommend plan service providers and to maintain all
                necessary plan records.
            •   Review of the Committee Charter at least every three years to ensure the policies
                continue to effectively meet the needs of the Committee, the Plan, and participants.

         Adoption of the new Charter ensures the District's 457 Plan is administered in accordance
         with current best practices, federal compliance standards, and the District's fiduciary
         obligations.

                                                 DISCUSSION

         The Deferred Compensation Plan Committee Charter provides an updated and transparent
         framework for administering the District's Deferred Compensation Plan. It aligns with current
         legal standards and establishes clear roles and accountability for committee members. The
         Deferred Compensation Plan Committee Charter underwent a legal review with the
         District's General Counsel after its initial presentation to the Sacramento Metropolitan Fire
         District's Board of Directors at the November 13, 2025 board meeting. The final version ,
         inclusive of counsel's edits, is included as Attachment 2 of this document.

         By adopting this charter through Resolution, the Board affirms its oversight responsibility
         and formally delegates administrative authority to the Committee under a defined
         governance structure. The Charter supersedes any prior documents related to the
         Committee's operation.

                                               FISCAL IMPACT

         There is no direct fiscal impact associated with adoption of the Charter. Committee
         members serve without compensation, and any incidental expenses are reimbursed in
         accordance with District policy or paid from plan assets.

                                             RECOMMENDATION

         Staff recommends the Board of Directors adopt a Resolution approving the Sacramento
         Metropolitan Fire District Deferred Compensation Plan Committee Charter.

                                COMMITTEE CHARTER

I.     INTRODUCTION

This Charter sets forth the rights, powers, responsibilities, and obligations of the Deferred
Compensation Plan Committee {the "Committee") for the Sacramento Metropolitan Fire District
Deferred Compensation Plan (the "Plan"). The Committee is charged under this Charter with
carrying out its duties and advising the Administrator for the Plan as originally established
pursuant to authorization contained in the Sacramento Metropolitan Fire District's Resolution 43-
01 and applicable provisions of the Plan Document.

II.    MEMBERSHIP OF THE COMMITTEE

The Committee shall be comprised of nine (9) voting members. The appointment prc;,cess for
these nine (9) voting members will consist of a qualification review and interview by at least a
quorum of the Committee. Once completed, the Committee will make a recommendation to the
Sacramento Metropolitan Fire District Board of Director for final appointment of the newly selected
Committee member(s) by Board Resolution. Members shall serve without compensation. All
reasonable out-of-pocket expenses of the Committee shall be paid by the District in accordance
with its standard reimbursement policies unless paid from Plan assets.

III.   COMMITTEE ACTION

The Committee shall meet at least quarterly to review Plan features, participant demographics,
investment performance, investment fund characteristics, and legal and regulatory updates. The
Committee Chairperson shall have authority to place items on the agenda. Committee Members
may propose items for future meeting of the Committee. The Committee will also make changes
to the investment offerings as provided under the Investment Policy Statement as warranted. The
committee shall elect a Committee Chairperson who will retain signatory authority for any
document or documents on behalf of the Committee for all Plan related functions. The Plan
Administrator will be selected by the Committee after a formal Request for Proposal process is
completed. The Secretary shall prepare the agenda for each meeting and shall keep minutes of
the Committee's proceedings and forward to the Committee. The Committee will also select a
Vice Chair, Education Officer, and Treasurer. The District, through this Committee, shall be
responsible for the data, records, and documents pertaining to the Committee's duties associated
with the Plan. With respect to any matter brought to the Committee for a vote, each voting member
shall be entitled to one vote. The Committee shall act by a majority of its members and such
action may be taken either by a vote at a meeting with at least five members present physically
or through teleconferencing.

The Committee Chairperson or the Vice Chair in his/her absence, shall preside at all Committee
meetings, will exercise the powers and functions as specified herein, and ensure that meetings
are conducted in accordance with applicable public meeting laws, including the Ralph M. Brown
Act.

not be limited to, the following:

      A.    To modify, amend or alter the provisions of the Plan in whole or in part to remain

      8.    To determine the investment options offered to participants of the Plan pursuant to

            custodian(s), insurance company(ies), regulated investment company(ies) and/or other

      H.    To review the Committee Charter at least every three years to ensure the policies

responsibilities under the Plan. In the event that a responsibility is allocated to a Committee

as may otherwise be required by law. If a responsibility is delegated to a persorv other than a
Committee member, the Committee shall not be responsible or liable for any act or omission of
such person in carrying out such responsibility except as may otherwise be required by law.

SUBJECT:        Purchase Approval - Purchase of Motorola Portable Radios

                                                TOPIC

Staff requests Board approval for the purchase of APX Next Series portable radios from
Motorola using County of Sacramento Open Item Contract# WA00034181.

                                           DISCUSSION

The Sacramento Metropolitan Fire District (District) is a subscriber to Sacramento
County's Motorola 800 MHz radio system and utilizes Motorola 800 MHz radios for two-
way voice communication between field units and dispatch. To ensure optimal
interoperability with Sacramento County's radio system and to meet public safety and
mutual aid demands, utilizing compatible products is necessary. The District's current
portable radios are no longer supported, and parts are no longer available for repairs.
Since the current radios cannot be repaired, the District would like to purchase 310
radios and necessary accessories. These radios will be assigned to members.

The County of Sacramento's Open Item Contract with Motorola (contract
#WA00034181) will be utilized for this purchase. Per the County's contract, all agencies
using the Sacramento Regional Radio Commu'nications System (SRRCS) may utilize
this contract for purchases. Motorola has confirmed that this contract provides the
District with tier-quantity discounts that are not offered on its other cooperative
contracts. The quantity of radios needed by the District alone under this purchase
provides a 33% discount. The Sacramento County Sheriffs Department (Sheriff) will
also be purchasing radios under this contract this month. By placing the order along
with the Sheriff, the District will receive a 40% discount, resulting in a savings of
$365,853.97.

                                         FISCAL IMPACT

Funding for the portable radios was not included in the FY 2025/26 Budget, and this
unanticipated expense would incur a cost of $3,135,900.54; approximately $3,004,049
from Measure O Funds and $131 ,852 from the A Fund.

                                      RECOMMENDATION

Staff recommends that the Board of Director's allow the District to procure the APX Next
Series Portable Radios from Motorola using the County of Sacramento Open Item
Contract# WA00034181.

     Request to approve a contract award to Kelstin Group, Inc., dba Pacific Credit Services

                                            BACKGROUND
     To assure high-quality care and service are maintained, the Sacramento Metropolitan Fire
     District (District) imposes assessments and fees for various services provided by the
     District. A third-party billing company screens, prepares, and submits claims to payors.
     Once the District's third-party billing company has exhausted applicable options for
     collecting payment on an account, delinquent accounts are then sent to · collections. The
     District presently uses a third-party contractor for debt collection services, whose contract
     will be expiring in December.

                                              DISCUSSION
     In preparation for the expiration of the present debt collection services contract, the District
     issued a Request for Proposals (RFP) on September 3, 2025, soliciting proposals from
     qualified firms to provide debt collection services for the District. Firms are required to
     provide full-service debt collection services for delinquent accounts. Such accounts include,
     but are not limited to, ambulance billing, false alarm fees, and fire inspection fees. The
     District received seven proposals through the District's e-procurement portal, OpenGov,
     from the following companies:                                          •

        •    Cedars Business Services, LLC

RE:    First Responder Fee Exceptions



Dear Board Members,


I write to you as both a taxpaying resident of Fair Oaks and a Metro Fire engine captain with
a request to amend our use of first responder fees. I understand we are undergoing a fee
study but I do not believe that study fully encapsulates the humanity of this problem.

Cost recovery is a real issue but the $502 treatment on scene fee we are charging citizens is
not being covered by most major insurers. To the best of my knowledge after speaking with
EMS it is only currently covered by Kaiser and Medical. This leaves a fully insured citizen
footing a large unexpected bill- and one that will dramatically increase following the
conclusion of this fee study.
I spoke recently with a citizen in 105's who had called for chest pain. Although his
symptoms had completely dissipated prior to our arrival, we performed an ALS assessment
due to cardiac history. He explained that he would need to be transported because last
time he initiated a refusal of transport his insurance company (Anthem Blue Cross) denied
coverage of his treatment on scene fee. They told him they did not have a code for this fee
and so they could not cover it. We do not have control over the insurance companies, but
we have control over our own actions and how they affect the best interests of our citizenry.
These patients are doing the right thing for the EMS system by not taking an ambulance that
is not needed and we are penalizing them for that decision.

We are in a period of great economic uncertainty, with healthcare premiums skyrocketing
and food insecurity at an all-time high due to a combination of record prices and absent or
uncertain SNAP benefits. A significant unexpected bill, often for calls where we did noth ing
more than ask a few questions and t ake a blood pressure, can be the difference between
having food in the fridge this month or not. Families will be forced to choose between food
and healthcare. Should a young mother, faced with a BRUE that she believes to be life-
threatening, hesitate to call 911 because it might deprive the household of food, gas, or




                                                                                                P79
rent for the month? Should our only outlet be the possibility of appeal hidden on our
website and likely inaccessible to the elderly population?
When citizens receive this out-of-pocket bill they view it as a violation of trust by their fire
department. I know this because it came up regularly while I canvassed my own
neighborhood for Measure 0. We asked our citizens for $415 million dollars and they
generously gave us their trust by a large majority margin. Do we honor that trust by not only
charging this fee knowing it will not be reimbursed, but by raising it? We can tell them their
taxes only pay for fire suppression but being billed beyond insurance the first time they ever
call 911 erodes their faith in their fire department instantaneously.


Some possible solutions
1. Do not bill a first responder/treatment on scene fee for the first:twQ EMS calls per
   person in a calendar year. The third call and onwards can be billed ("free before
   three"), and the count will reset for that person on January 1. We can notify a citizen
   after their second EMS assessment that they will be billed for future assessments in the
   calendar year. I think most people can agree that high user-scan contribute more, and
   this gives some leeway for those who pay their taxes and do not call 911 frequently. It
   allows young parents to call without fear of crushing economic burden. It allows
   bystanders to call for others in distress without creating a sudden bitrfor them. It allows
   us to perform assessments for liability purposes when called by law enforcement
   without severely penalizing the person they called for.


2. Do not bill first responder/treatment on scene fees for patients <18 or ~65 years of
   age. This allows parents or schools to call for minors without the unexpected bill. It
   allows elderly people on a fixed income to call for minor issues that often can be
   underlying signs of something more significant without putting them into poverty.



3. Charge more for ambulance transports, which are covered by insurance, and then
   do not charge the first responder fee or only charge after multiple uses in a
   calendar year. This distributes the burden and recognizes that insured citizens should
   not be paying out of pocket for their medical assessments.



4. Bill care facilities that call us for lift assists to offset costs.


5. Suspend our first responder/treatment on scene fees and work with insurance
   companies to make this a covered event. Only resume once we have achieved
   success with major insurers. This would be a winning item for political offices and I'm
   confident we would have allies in this push.              •

   • Recommendation for Targeted Adjustments to First Responder Fees

Dear Members of the Board,

I am writing to respectfully express concerns regarding the District's current First Responder
Fee program, particularly the application of fees to patients who receive care but ultimately
decline transport. While I understand the fiscal challenges of maintaining a high-quality fire and
EMS system, the present structure has produced significant unintended consequences for both
our community and personnel.

My primary concern is that the current fee model has led to hesitation among residents to call
9-1-1, inconsistent public messaging, and a growing perception of double taxation. These
effects disproportionately impact seniors, low-income households, and families with limited
insurance coverage. I urge the District to implement safeguards, compassionate billing policies,
and well-defined exemptions that protect vulnerable populations.

Our crews are the face of the First Responder Fee. They interact directly with patients and
families, managing confusion, frustration, and sometimes anger tied to these charges. A truly
effective EMS system does not require every patient to be transported or every encounter to
generate a fee to sustain operations. The public readily understands that fees are appropriate
when necessary treatment or transport is provided. However, when crews are responding to
minor issues or simply providing reassurance, attaching fees can feel punitive and undermine
public trust, w.hile placing our staff in the difficult position of explaining charges they had no role
in creating.

]n today's EMS system, with. high call volumes, frequent transports, and increasingly impacted

jA.dvocating for patients does not mean that every patient requires ambulance transport. For
 instance, during a recent call, we assisted an adult female with a lower-leg injury safely into her
 own vehicle without requiring transport. This kept an ambulance in service and supported the
 patient's needs and her family. Situations like this highlight that first responder services often
 provide essential support without hospital transport, and applying fees in such cases may
 impose unnecessary financial burdens while discouraging appropriate use of EMS services in
the future.

Another example, E41 responded to a domestic violence call at the request of the Sacramento
County Sheriff, our crew encountered a female patient standing at her front door with a large
hematoma on her forehead. Upon arrival, she yelled, "Don't you touch me, I don't want you
