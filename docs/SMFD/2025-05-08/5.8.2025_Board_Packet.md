     SUBJECT:         Surplus Real Property- 9·116 Vancouver Drive (APN 075-0064-005)

                                                    TOPIC

     Request to adopt a resolution to declc;1re surplus real property for APN 075-0064-005 and
     authorize the Fire Chief or his designee to begin the disposition process.

                                               BACKGROUND

     The Sacramento Metropolitan Fire District (Metro Fire), or its predecessor agency, has owned a
     0.18-acre parcel located at 9116 Vancouver Drive in Sacramento (Site) since 1970. The Site,
     previously known as Station 64, is currently vacant, and is considered a permanent closure. Metro
     Fire has assessed the operational impact of the station closure and has determined that the Site
     is not operationally relevant due to overlapping coverage and facility constraints.

                                                DISCUSSION

     District Policy #106.1 requires the Board of Directors (Board) to designate property as surplus
     prior to disposing of the asset. Since the Site is not considered operationally relevant to the
     District's service delivery, the District no longer has use for the Site. If the Board designates the
     Site as surplus, the District must comply with California Surplus Land Act disposal procedures,
     which requires a Notice of Availability be sent to public agencies and affordable housing
     developers. If no qualified offers are received within 60 days, the District may then market the
     Site for sale. The final disposition of the Site will require subsequent Board action.

                                               FISCAL IMPACT

      The fair market value for the Property is estimated between $410,000-$470,000.

                                            RECOMMENDATION

      Staff recommends that the Board declare the vacant 0.18-acre Site as surplus and authorize the
      Fire Chief or his designee to begin the disposition process.

  SUBJECT:        Contract Award Recommendation - Janitorial Cleaning and Maintenance Services

                                                    TOPIC
  Staff requests the Board of Directors approve a contract award to City Wide Property Services,
  Inc. for janitorial cleaning and maintenance services.

                                               BACKGROUND

  To ensure clean and sanitary conditions are maintained at the Sacramento Metropolitan Fire
  District (District or Metro Fire) administrative facilities, the District requires contracted janitorial
  cleaning and maintenance services. The provision of such services is also required per the
  contracts held between the District and the District's two lessees that occupy District owned
  facilities.
                                                DISCUSSION

  On March 20, 2025, the District's Purchasing Division issued Request for Proposals (RFP) 25-01
  to solicit proposals from qualified and experienced firms to provide the highest level of janitorial
  cleaning and maintenance services for the following locations:

  Metro Fire Headquarters, 1st and 2nd Floors               10545 Armstrong Ave., Mather, CA
  Metro Fire Logistics Division                             3012 Gold Canal Dr., Rancho Cordova, CA
  Metro Fire Training Division                              3121 Gold Canal Dr., Rancho Cordova, CA
  Sacramento County Sheriff's Department
  Metro Fire Communications Division
                                                            2101 Hurley Way, Sacramento, CA
                                                            4427 Dudley Blvd., McClellan Park, CA
                                                                                                                     r
  Metro Fire Fleet Division                                 4425 Dudley Blvd, McClellan Park, CA

  Interested firms were required to attend a mandatory pre-submittal job-walk on April 8, 2025 to
                                                                                                                     I
  familiarize themselves with each facility in need of the janitorial services described within the RFP.
  Firms with attendees that did not attend the job-walk in its entirety were not permitted to submit a
  proposal. Representatives from 12 janitorial firms attended the job-walk, and all representatives
  attended the job-walk in its entirety.

  Metro Fire received 10 proposals through the OpenGov e-procurement portal from the following
  firms:

      •    Chavez Clean Machine, LLC

                                                  TOPIC
   Staff recommends adoption of the attached reimbursement resolution to reimburse the
   Sacramento Metropolitan Fire District ("District") for funds that have been or may be
   expended prior to issuing tax-exempt debt. The recommended action is in anticipation of
   the District selling Measure O general obligation bonds to fund the purchase of authorized
   projects.

                                              DISCUSSION
   In order to legally reimburse expenditures made prior to the issuance of tax-exempt debt,
   a reimbursement resolution must be in place pursuant to U.S. Treasury regulation
   Section 1.150-2. Adoption of a reimbursement resolution does not bind the District to
   issuing future debt. Instead, a reimbursement resolution allows the District to use its own
   funds for capital expenditures and later repay itself from the financing proceeds.

   Purchases made after the reimbursement resolution adoption and those made no more
   than 60 days prior to adoption are eligible for reimbursement. Staff anticipates financing
   approximately $1.33 million of capital purchases consisting of:

         •   Four squad vehicles (approximately $938,000)
         •   Two ambulance remounts (approximately $336,000)
         •   Three automatic chest compression devices (approximately $49,000)

   These expenditures were originally to be financed with direct borrowing from banks and
   budgeted in the Capital Facilities Fund (212D Fund) in the fiscal year 2024/25 Midyear
   Budget. These expenses were subsequently identified to be eligible expenditures under
   Measure 0.

   The 2120 Fund will have to utilize available reserves and may potentially require a budget
   amendment in the General Fund (212A Fund) for the 212A fund to temporarily advance
   money through an operating transfer to the 212D Fund. Both funds will eventually be
   reimbursed with proceeds from the sale of Measure O bonds expected to occur in July
   2025.

SUBJECT:        Measure 0- General Obligation Bond Structuring Options


                                            TOPIC

The District's municipal advisor for Measure O financing, KNN Public Finance, LLC
will present four different scenarios for structuring the issuance of Measure O General
Obligation (GO) bond debt.

                                       BACKGROUND

On November 5, 2024, District voters approved Measure 0, authorizing the District to
issue up to $415 million in GO bonds for capital projects, which the District plans to
issue and commit over the next 10 years. The District anticipates three GO bond
issuances; the first early in fiscal year (FY) 2025/26, followed by bond issuances
anticipated in FY 2028/29 and FY 2031/32.

The proceeds from the issuance and sale of GO bonds may only be used for the
construction, expansion, reconstruction, rehabilitation, replacement, furnishing, and
equipping of fire facilities; the acquisition or lease of real property for fire facilities; and
the acquisition of fire and emergency medical vehicles, apparatus, and capital
equipment.

The Board of Directors (Board) will be presented a bond issuance plan on May 22,
2025 that will include staff's recommendation for structing the first issuance of
Measure O bonds.

                                        DISCUSSION

The District must weigh several factors when considering how to structure its first
general obligation bond issuance. Principal factors are the number and size of debt
issuances, property tax rate, property assessed value growth, interest rates, bond
type, repayment term, bond type, and debt service structure.

The Board will be presented four scenarios incorporating the factors named above.

    1. "Aggregate Level:" This scenario maximizes the tax rate immediately,
       gradually easing down the tax rate over the life of the bond repayment period.

   2. "Ramping:" A modification to Aggregate Level, this scenario also begins with a
      high tax rate and eases the rate more gradually, providing a more consistent
      tax rate level.
                            Serving Sacramento and Placer Counties


                                                                                                             P53
Page 2




            3. "Level by Series:" This scenario 'increases the tax rate substantially with each
               new issuance over the first six years, with a declining tax rate over the
               remaining payoff period.

            4 . "Level Principal:" A modification to Level by Series, this scenario ramps up the
                tax rate more steeply before declining more rapidly than the Level by Series
                scenario. This scenario has the lowest total tax levy over the life of the bonds.

                                            FISCAL IMPACT

         There is no fiscal impact associated with this discussion. Once a bond structure has been
         selected at a subsequent Board meeting, costs associated with the issuance of Measure
         0 bonds will be paid for out of bond proceeds.

                                          RECOMMENDATION

         This is a presentation item only and staff has no recommendation at this time. A
         recommendation will be provided as part of the Measure O financing plan presented
         on May 22, 2025.

DUTIES

The County Board of Supervisors created the Treasmy Oversight Committee (the "Committee") on February 27,

quarterly Pooled Investment Fund reports, monitoring and reviewing the County Treasurer's annual Investment
Policy, and causing an annual audit of the Pooled Investment Fund portfolio. The Committee is advismy and does
not direct individual investment decisions, select investment advisors, brokers, or dealers, or impinge on the day-
to-day operations of the county treasmy.
