# Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) -- 12-Month Summary

**Question:** Summarize what happened at SCOE over the past 12 months, including noteworthy events, big budget items, and topics the community might want to explore further.
**Date:** 2026-03-25
**Organizations:** SCOE

---

## Overview

The Sacramento County Office of Education (SCOE) held regular board meetings roughly twice per month from April 2025 through December 2025 (the most recent data available). Led by Superintendent David W. Gordon and a seven-member board (President Mariana Corona Sabeniano), SCOE oversees 13 school districts, 52 charter schools, and over 250,000 students in Sacramento County. SCOE also directly operates Court and Community Schools serving approximately 932 students -- expelled, incarcerated, and at-risk youth -- across six school sites.

The past year was marked by a massive budget adjustment as one-time pandemic-era and behavioral health grants expired, an ambitious equity agenda focused on Black student outcomes, new legislative engagement on civic education and charter school accountability, labor tensions with classified staff, and significant oversight activity around a struggling charter school.

## Budget and Finances

### 2025-2026 Adopted Budget

SCOE's proposed 2025-2026 budget projects **$192.4 million in total revenue** and approximately **$209 million in total expenditures** across its General Fund. The budget was presented at a public hearing on June 10, 2025, and adopted on June 24, 2025.

Key revenue sources:
- **LCFF (Local Control Funding Formula):** $38.2 million, including ~$25.8 million for the County Operations Grant, ~$3.5 million for the Alternative Education Grant, and ~$4.7 million for Differentiated Assistance through the System of Support
- **Other State Funds:** $90.8 million
- **Local Funds:** $54.1 million
- **Federal Funds:** $9.3 million

### Dramatic Revenue Decline from Prior Year

The most significant budget story is the dramatic decrease in revenue and expenditures from 2024-2025 to 2025-2026, driven by the expiration of large one-time grants:

- **Children and Youth Behavioral Health Initiative (CYBHI):** Down $112.6 million as the School-Linked Partnership and Capacity Grant program wound down
- **Project CalHOPE Student Support:** Down $35.6 million (grant ended)
- **ESSER (pandemic relief):** Down $3.3 million (fully spent)
- **Educator Workforce Investment Grant (Computer Science):** Down $8.4 million
- **Student Mental Health Career Pipeline:** Down $5 million
- **Literacy Coaches & Reading Specialist grants:** Down $1.6 million

Total Services and Other Operating Expenses dropped by approximately $165.9 million, largely because SCOE had been passing through CYBHI and CalHOPE funds to other county offices of education statewide.

### Bright Spots

- LCFF revenue increased by $1.4 million due to higher Differentiated Assistance funding
- ESEA Title I funding for Court and Community Schools increased by $1.3 million
- California Community Schools Partnership Program grants increased by $1.5 million
- Sly Park Environmental Education Center fee revenue increased by $508,000

### Budget Revision No. 1 (December 2025)

By December, Budget Revision No. 1 revised the General Fund budget to show **$178.1 million in revenues** and **$205.7 million in expenditures**, with a projected ending fund balance of **$117.9 million** (beginning balance was $132.5 million). The revision added significant new state revenues (+$20.3 million) and other local revenues (+$5.2 million) while reducing LCFF sources slightly. Services and Other Operating Expenses jumped by $32 million in the revision.

### First Interim Report (December 2025)

SCOE received a **Positive Certification** at its First Interim Report, meaning it expects to meet its financial obligations for the current fiscal year and subsequent two years. However, several criteria were "Not Met" -- notably ADA projections, Other Revenues, Other Expenditures, and Deficit Spending -- indicating significant variance from budget adoption. Temporary interfund borrowings were also flagged, and both certificated and classified salary negotiations remained unsettled as of First Interim.

### 2024-2025 Unaudited Actuals

The 2024-2025 fiscal year ended with total revenue of approximately $187.3 million and expenditures of $203.1 million in the General Fund, with an ending fund balance of approximately $132.5 million. The County Operations Grant ADA was 236,020.

## Major Policy and Equity Initiatives

### Black Student Landscape Analysis

One of the most significant policy documents presented during this period was the **"Landscape Analysis: Understanding the School Experiences of Black Students in Sacramento County,"** presented at the May 20, 2025, board meeting. Key findings:

- Black students make up just 10% of enrollment but account for nearly 25% of all suspensions
- Black students are the lowest-performing racial group on statewide assessments
- The report identifies four themes: the need for culturally responsive education, rebuilding trust between schools and Black communities, having honest conversations about race and equity, and aligning systems to support Black students
- Recommendations call for systemic changes across all Sacramento County districts

This report was incorporated into SCOE's 2025-2026 Strategic Priorities and is being used to guide professional development and program design across all SCOE schools.

### 2025-2026 Strategic Priorities (November 2025)

The Board reviewed draft Strategic Priorities organized around three areas:

1. **Closing the achievement gap in SCOE schools** -- accelerating literacy, implementing Black Student Landscape Analysis recommendations, expanding dual enrollment and CTE pathways, mental health support, and community schools partnerships
2. **Closing the achievement gap in local districts** -- expanding Centers of Wellness, supporting the Sacramento READS/Dolly Parton Imagination Library literacy initiative, math adoption support, chronic absenteeism reduction, Long-Term English Learner improvement networks
3. **Youth leadership and civic engagement** -- Youth Advisory Committee, Youth County Board Member, civic education pilots, family and community engagement

### Immigration Safe Haven Resolution (July 2025)

Resolution No. 25-11, "In Support of Creating a Safe and Positive Learning Environment," was a comprehensive resolution affirming that SCOE will:
- Not report undocumented students or families to ICE
- Not enter agreements with ICE or Border Patrol
- Not inquire about immigration status during enrollment
- Not collect Social Security numbers unless legally required
- Refer all immigration enforcement requests to the Superintendent and legal counsel

This was adopted during a period of heightened federal immigration enforcement activity.

### LGBTQIA+ Pride Resolution (June 2025)

Resolution No. 25-10 recognized Pride Month and Transgender Visibility Week, reaffirming SCOE's commitment to safe, inclusive school environments for LGBTQIA+ students, families, and staff.

## Legislative Engagement

SCOE actively engaged with state legislation during this period:

- **SB 584 (Civic Learning in Grades 1-8):** SCOE co-sponsored this bill with Los Angeles COE to promote civic education pathways for younger students, extending the State Seal of Civic Engagement concept down to elementary and middle school
- **AB 84 (School Accountability/Education Inspector General):** Addressed charter school authorization, oversight, financial audits, and the creation of an Office of the Education Inspector General -- relevant to SCOE's role as a charter authorizer
- **SB 249 (County Board Elections):** Would require county board of education elections to be consolidated with the statewide general election starting January 2026
- **SB 414:** Also reviewed at the July 2025 meeting alongside AB 84

## Charter School Oversight: Capital College & Career Academy

A major oversight issue involved **Capital College & Career Academy (CCCA)**, a charter school authorized by the SCOE board in 2022:

- CCCA originally projected 375 students by year five (2027-2028) but revised this down to 230 students
- Low enrollment directly impacted its financial solvency; CCCA failed to maintain minimum reserves
- **September 3, 2025:** SCOE issued a notice of concern requesting revised budgets and a plan to remain solvent
- **September 12, 2025:** CCCA submitted a revised budget that SCOE determined was insufficient to demonstrate financial solvency
- **September 16, 2025:** Board held a public hearing on CCCA's material revision request (Resolution 25-13), conditionally approving enrollment and course changes but withholding approval of revised enrollment projections until CCCA provides documentation proving solvency within 30 days

CCCA also changed its course offerings (Mandarin instead of Spanish, Video Game Design instead of construction-focused arts), expanded enrollment to include 11th graders, and added "Career Experience" graduation requirements totaling 30 additional units.

## Youth Engagement

SCOE made notable progress in youth engagement:

- **Youth County Board Member:** Nairobi Dunbar was introduced as SCOE Youth County Board Member at the August 12, 2025, meeting
- **Youth Engagement Fellowship:** The program received 300+ applications in October 2024 and 200+ in January 2025, hiring three fellows who work 20-30 hours/week with SCOE schools through PRO Youth and Families
- **Judith Marquez** was recognized as the first SCOE Appointed Board Member Representing Students (August 2025)
- Despite outreach to 400+ former SCOE students, no current court/community school students expressed interest in the Youth Board Member position

## Board Election (June 2026)

Resolution No. 25-16, adopted November 18, 2025, called for an election on **June 2, 2026**, to fill three seats on the Sacramento County Board of Education:
- Trustee Area I (4-year term)
- Trustee Area II (4-year term, spans Sacramento and Placer counties)
- Trustee Area III (4-year term)

## Williams Reviews and Teacher Staffing

The November 2025 Annual Williams Report revealed:
- SCOE staff monitored **119 schools in 9 districts** identified as Williams schools
- **109 schools** were in exemplary condition; **12** in good condition; none in fair or poor condition
- All Williams schools had sufficient instructional materials
- Concerning teacher staffing data: **236 misassignments, 81 corrected misassignments, and 186 vacancies** across Williams schools
- Sacramento City USD had 70 misassignments and 55 vacancies; San Juan USD had 60 misassignments and 58 vacancies; Twin Rivers USD had 31 misassignments and 58 vacancies

SCOE's own schools showed **only 52.5% of teaching assignments as "clear"** (vs. 86.2% countywide and 84.4% statewide), with 29.1% out-of-field. This is a significant staffing challenge for SCOE's court and community schools.

## Labor Relations

At the August 12, 2025, board meeting, nine members of the public and one union representative (Wendi Beatty, President of SCOE CSEA 480) made comments about contract negotiations for classified employees. As of the December 2025 First Interim Report, both certificated and classified salary negotiations remained unsettled.

## Topics for Further Community Exploration

1. **Black Student Achievement Gap:** The Landscape Analysis found Black students account for 25% of suspensions despite being 10% of enrollment. How are districts responding to the recommendations? What measurable progress has been made?

2. **Teacher Staffing Crisis in Court/Community Schools:** Only 52.5% of SCOE teaching assignments are "clear" (fully credentialed), compared to 86.2% countywide. Why is staffing so challenging for these schools serving the most vulnerable students, and what is being done?

3. **Post-Pandemic Funding Cliff:** SCOE lost over $160 million in pass-through funding as CYBHI, CalHOPE, and ESSER grants expired. What programs that were supported by these funds are now at risk? How are mental health and student support services being sustained?

4. **Capital College & Career Academy Solvency:** This charter school authorized by SCOE is at risk of running out of cash. What happened? What are the implications for the students currently enrolled? How does SCOE's oversight process work?

5. **Classified Employee Contract Negotiations:** Multiple public comments at the August meeting and unsettled negotiations as of December suggest tension. What are the key issues? How do classified employee conditions affect school operations?

6. **Differentiated Assistance for 11 of 13 Districts:** In 2024-2025, nearly all Sacramento County districts qualified for Differentiated Assistance, with academics and suspensions as primary drivers. What does this say about the state of education in the county?

7. **Dolly Parton's Imagination Library and Sacramento READS:** This ambitious new initiative aims to enroll 65,000 children aged 0-5 for free monthly books. How is enrollment progressing? What impact is expected on kindergarten readiness?

8. **Immigration Enforcement and Schools:** The July 2025 safe haven resolution responded to federal enforcement concerns. Have there been any incidents? How are families responding?

9. **June 2026 Board Election:** Three of seven board seats are up for election. Who is running? What are the key issues candidates are focused on?

10. **Long-Term English Learner Crisis:** Seven districts were newly identified for Differentiated Assistance based on Long-Term English Learner outcomes. What specific interventions are being tried, and what does the new LTEL Improvement Network look like?
