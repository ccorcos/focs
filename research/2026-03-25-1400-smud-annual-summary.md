# SMUD: Past 12 Months Summary and Topics for the Community

**Question:** Summarize what happened in the past 12 months for Sacramento Municipal Utility District (SMUD). Include anything new and noteworthy, explain big budget items, and suggest interesting topics for the community to explore further.
**Date:** 2026-03-25
**Organizations:** SMUD

---

## Overview

SMUD, the nation's sixth-largest community-owned electric utility serving 1.5 million people in Sacramento County, had an eventful year. The period from roughly April 2025 through March 2026 included a $2.3 billion budget authorization for 2025, a significant rate action process culminating in approved 3% rate increases for 2026 and 2027, major progress on the 2030 Zero Carbon Plan, new solar and wind power purchase agreements, wildfire mitigation plan updates, a structural reorganization transferring power plant assets from SFA to SMUD, and ongoing community impact programs.

## 2025 Budget: $2.3 Billion

SMUD's 2025 budget of $2,281 million was presented by CEO Paul Lau in November 2024 and represented a $138 million increase over 2024. Here is the breakdown:

| Category | 2024 ($M) | 2025 ($M) | Change ($M) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Commodity | $572 | $610 | +$38 |
| Other O&M | $746 | $786 | +$40 |
| Public Good | $68 | $68 | $0 |
| Debt Service | $202 | $205 | +$3 |
| Capital | $555 | $612 | +$57 |
| **Total** | **$2,143** | **$2,281** | **+$138** |

**Key budget drivers:**
- **Commodity costs (+$38M):** Higher system peak loads requiring more resource adequacy purchases, which have increased significantly in cost. Continued commodity hedging for price volatility.
- **Capital spending (+$57M):** Major investments include the Country Acres solar+storage project (344 MW solar / 172 MW battery), rebuilding the Elverta (El Rio) Substation, designing the new Folsom Administrative Operations Building, deploying 200,000 smart meters through the Connected Clean PowerCity (CCPC) grant, and upgrading the SAP ERP system to S/4 HANA.
- **O&M increases (+$40M):** Community Impact Plan, transportation and building electrification incentives, load flexibility programs, vegetation management ($46M for wildfire mitigation), cybersecurity, and insurance costs.
- **Staffing:** 2,295 full-time positions authorized, an increase of 45, primarily for field operations and apprentice classes.

## 2024 Financial Results

SMUD's 2024 financials were strong, reported at the March 18, 2025 Finance & Audit Committee meeting:

- **Operating revenues:** $1,782 million, $58M (3.4%) above budget, driven by higher residential and commercial customer usage.
- **Net position ("Net Income"):** $225 million favorable to budget.
- **Commodity costs:** $599 million, $36M (5.7%) under budget due to lower fuel costs from reduced thermal generation and lower market prices.
- **Notable one-time items:** $89M received from Cosumnes Power Plant business interruption insurance; $7.5M from Substation A fire insurance; $6M from Rancho Seco spent fuel settlement.
- **Debt management:** $650M in revenue refunding debt issued, generating $24M in cash flow savings through 2036. $690M in commodity supply revenue refunding debt issued, generating $34M in savings through August 2030.
- **Rate stabilization fund:** $60M created for commodity rate stabilization; $41M set aside for one-time future expenditures; $20M for Community Impact Plan expenditures through 2028.

## Rate Increases: 3% in 2026 and 2027

A major action item was the 2025 rate process, which unfolded over several months:

- **January 14, 2025:** Rate proposal preview
- **March 18, 2025:** Finance & Audit Committee discussion
- **March 20, 2025:** CEO & GM Report released
- **April 30 and May 13, 2025:** Public workshops
- **June 4, 2025:** Public hearing (special board meeting)
- **June 19, 2025:** Board vote on rate proposal

**What was approved:**
- **3.0% rate increase for all customers effective January 1, 2026**
- **3.0% rate increase for all customers effective January 1, 2027**
- **New optional residential rate** ("Time-of-Day Low Use") for customers with small electrical panels (125 amps or less) -- lower monthly fixed charge of $17 with higher per-kWh energy prices, designed to be revenue neutral. Benefits customers using 270 kWh/month or less.

**Bill impacts for average residential customer (750 kWh/month):**
- Current average monthly bill: $144.92
- 2026 increase: +$4.35/month
- 2027 increase: +$4.48/month
- Total impact: +$8.83/month

**Rate increase drivers:**
- Commodity costs increasing by $34M over two years for reliability and new state Renewables Portfolio Standard (RPS) requirements
- $280M for new generation and storage projects for RPS compliance
- $251M for substation and line capacity projects
- $90M for the Folsom Administrative Operations Building
- $22M increase in wildfire mitigation costs and inflation
- SMUD continues to have among the lowest rates in California -- system average rate of $0.1755/kWh vs. PG&E at $0.3830/kWh (more than 50% lower)

## Zero Carbon Plan Progress

SMUD's 2030 Zero Carbon Plan targets zero carbon in its energy supply by 2030. An update was presented at the April 16, 2025 ERCS Committee.

**Committed renewable and storage projects (~1,520 MW, 44% of ~3,400 MW goal):**

| Project | Type | Capacity | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| Drew Solar | Solar | 100 MW | Online |
| Geysers | Geothermal | 100 MW | Online |

| Hedge Battery | Battery | 4 MW | Online |

**New local projects announced:**
- **Oveja Ranch (NEW):** 75 MW solar + 37 MW battery storage, Sacramento County
- **Curry Creek (NEW, formerly Country Acres II):** 156 MW solar + 156 MW battery storage, Placer County

**Pipeline projects (timing TBD):** ~1,575 MW additional across local solar (510 MW), local battery (390 MW), regional wind (95 MW), regional solar (165 MW), geothermal (100 MW), and carbon capture & sequestration (315 MW).

**Carbon Capture & Sequestration:** Calpine's Sutter project, awarded $270M from DOE, aims to capture up to 1.7 million metric tons of GHG annually. The demonstration at Los Medanos showed >95% capture rates. SMUD would potentially purchase clean energy from this facility under a PPA.

**CAISO Extended Day-Ahead Market (EDAM):** SMUD is upgrading technology infrastructure to participate in EDAM by 2027, expected to yield $5M+ in annual benefits by optimizing hydroelectric generation.

## Wildfire Mitigation

The 2025 update to SMUD's 2023-2025 Wildfire Mitigation Plan was adopted at the June 19, 2025 board meeting. Key elements:

- $46 million budgeted for vegetation management and wildfire risk reduction
- Updated timelines for project completions and customer communication
- Tabletop exercise conducted May 27, 2025, simulating a wildfire in the Upper American River Project (UARP)
- Pole Clearing Area (PCA) recloser installation with SCADA-enabled devices on 12kV feeders
- Plan submitted to the Wildfire Safety Advisory Board by July 1, 2025

## Reliability Performance (2024)

The SD-4 Reliability monitoring report (June 11, 2025) showed mixed results:

- **Transmission system availability:** 100%
- **Customer energy supply:** Met 100% of the time
- **Distribution reliability (excluding major events):** COMPLIANT
  - SAIFI (outage frequency): 0.75 (limit: 1.14)
  - SAIDI (outage duration): 44.4 minutes (limit: 68.7 minutes)
- **Distribution reliability (including major events):** EXCEEDED TARGETS
  - SAIFI: 1.43 (limit: 1.33)
  - SAIDI: 228.0 minutes (limit: 93.3 minutes)
- 98.7% of all 773 distribution circuits met reliability criteria
- Major events (likely storms) significantly impacted the with-major-event metrics

## Building Electrification and Energy Efficiency

Presented at the June 10, 2025 Strategic Development Committee:

- **102,395 metric tons of carbon saved in 2023** from energy efficiency and building electrification programs
- **69,652 equivalent all-electric homes** converted to date (cumulative through Q1 2025), against a Zero Carbon Plan goal of 154,000
- Investment has shifted dramatically from EE to building electrification: In 2024, $51M total spent -- $9.2M on EE, $41.8M on BE (up from $24.5M total in 2017, all on EE)
- Customers switching to heat pumps can save up to $500/year today and up to $900/year by 2030

## Clean Transportation

Presented at the October 8, 2024 Strategic Development Committee:

- SMUD anticipates a 6x increase in light-duty EVs and 30x increase in medium/heavy-duty EVs by 2030
- EV fleet charging could add 528 MW in managed-peak load by 2035
- LCFS (Low Carbon Fuel Standard) credit values at historic lows (~$50), with proposed CARB changes that could benefit SMUD
- FAST grant deploying DC fast chargers at Sacramento International Airport, Sac Valley Amtrak Station, and Sac State
- REACH 2.0 installing 400+ Level 2 EV charging stations at 26-41 multifamily housing sites

## Grant Funding

At the March 11, 2025 Strategic Development Committee, SMUD reviewed its grant portfolio:

- **Federal awards:** $142M in project costs ($57M grant share, $85M SMUD share)
- **State awards:** $130M in project costs ($45M grant share, $84M SMUD share)
- **Key grants in implementation:** CCPC ($50M federal award for smart meters/grid intelligence), Meadowview Neighborhood Electrification ($3M)
- **Key grants in contracting:** CERRI ($25M for network protectors, transformers, underground cabling), CFI ($6M for 90 EV charging handles)
- **Federal policy headwinds:** Removal of DEI and community benefit plans, NEVI charging infrastructure funding paused, reduced federal workforce causing delays, risk of audits and funding elimination

## Organizational Changes

**SFA Asset Transfer:** At the June 17, 2025 Finance & Audit Committee, SMUD moved to consolidate the Sacramento Municipal Utility District Financing Authority (SFA) back into SMUD. All SFA debt was defeased as of May 2025, allowing the transfer of five power plants (Cosumnes, Carson, Procter & Gamble, Campbell, McClellan) to SMUD for administrative efficiency and cost savings (eliminating duplicate audits, financial reporting, and budgeting).

**Community Energy Services:** SMUD's consulting arm (formed 2017) now serves 7 utility clients, 2.7 million+ customers, with 100% client satisfaction. A new contract with Sonoma Clean Power was approved in October 2024 for data management, billing, and contact center services.

## Enterprise Risk Management

November 2024 update showed top risks for the utility industry:
- Cyber threats (increasing)
- Regulatory changes and scrutiny (increasing)
- Catastrophic natural disasters and weather (increasing)
- Climate change policies and regulations (increasing)
- Legacy IT infrastructure (new risk area)

SMUD had 5 high-risk items, 41 medium-risk, and 39 low-risk across strategic, operational, financial, and external categories. KPMG completed a maturity assessment and SMUD moved to quarterly board reporting on risk.

## Other Notable Items

- **Next-generation radio system:** $19.7M contract with Motorola Solutions for P25 digital radio system upgrade in the UARP, replacing a 2012 analog system nearing end of support
- **Gas pipeline operations:** SMUD owns 76 miles of high-pressure gas pipelines in Solano and Sacramento Counties. Advanced leak detection includes satellite methane plume detection, AI-based pressure monitoring, and laser detection systems
- **Methane emissions panel:** November 2024 strategic development committee featured presentations from SMUD, RMI, EPRI, and SMAQMD on managing methane from gas pipelines
- **Underground line work:** 3.1 miles of overhead-to-underground conversion projects in Citrus Heights (Auburn Blvd), Elk Grove (Elk Grove Blvd), and Sacramento County (Greenback Lane)
- **Folsom Administrative Operations Building:** Multi-year project replacing the existing Energy Management Center, with design and site work beginning in 2025. Budgeted at $90M
- **Barclays Letter of Credit:** Three-year renewal supporting $150M in outstanding Commercial Paper Series L

## Topics for Further Community Exploration

1. **Rate increases and affordability:** With 3% rate hikes in 2026 and 2027, how does SMUD's "lowest in California" positioning hold up? What are the cumulative effects of consecutive years of increases (2.0% in 2023, 5.6% in 2024, 5.6% in 2025, 3.0% in 2026, 3.0% in 2027)? How does the new optional low-use rate actually benefit Fair Oaks residents with smaller homes?

2. **Zero Carbon Plan reality check:** SMUD is at 44% of its ~3,400 MW goal with committed projects, and the Coyote Creek project is delayed. With ~1,575 MW still in the pipeline with "timing TBD," can SMUD realistically hit its 2030 zero-carbon target? What are the cost implications if it falls short?

3. **Wildfire risk and the UARP:** SMUD budgets $46M for vegetation management and wildfire mitigation. With the Upper American River Project in the Sierra foothills, what are the wildfire risks to SMUD's hydroelectric infrastructure? The May 2025 tabletop exercise focused specifically on a UARP wildfire scenario.

4. **Federal grant vulnerability:** SMUD has $142M in federal grant-funded projects. With the changing federal policy landscape (paused NEVI funding, removed DEI requirements, audit risks), how exposed is SMUD? The CFI grant for EV chargers was flagged as "high risk to lose funds."

5. **Reliability after major events:** While SMUD's day-to-day reliability is excellent (44 minutes average outage excluding major events), the 228-minute average duration including major events far exceeds the 93.3-minute target. What drove these major events, and what is SMUD doing to improve storm resilience?

6. **EV charging and grid impact:** SMUD projects EV fleet charging could add up to 528 MW by 2035. How is the grid being prepared? What does managed vs. unmanaged charging mean for neighborhoods? What about multifamily housing, where only 2% of the CEC's charging goal has been met?

7. **Building electrification push:** SMUD spent $41.8M on building electrification in 2024, up from zero in 2017. With 69,652 equivalent all-electric homes against a 154,000 goal, what incentives are available for Fair Oaks homeowners? What is the real cost-benefit of switching from gas to heat pumps?

8. **Folsom Administrative Operations Building ($90M):** This is a significant capital project to replace the existing Energy Management Center. What exactly is being built and why is it needed? How does a $90M admin building factor into keeping rates low?

9. **SFA dissolution and thermal plant future:** Five natural gas power plants are being transferred from SFA back to SMUD. As SMUD pursues zero carbon, what is the long-term plan for these thermal assets? The Cosumnes Power Plant is getting a thermal transition turn-down project to reduce emissions by 27%.

10. **Community Energy Services as a business line:** SMUD now serves 7 external utility clients including Sonoma Clean Power. Is this consulting business generating meaningful revenue? How does it benefit Sacramento ratepayers?

11. **Carbon capture and sequestration:** The Calpine Sutter project ($270M DOE award) promises to capture 1.7 million metric tons of GHG annually. SMUD is also supporting legislation to classify CO2 pipelines under pipeline safety law. What are the risks and community implications of CCS technology and CO2 pipelines in the region?

12. **Smart meter deployment (CCPC):** 200,000 new Grid Edge intelligent meters are being deployed through a $50M federal grant. What new capabilities do these bring, and what are the privacy/data implications for customers?
