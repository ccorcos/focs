# San Juan Unified School District: 12-Month Summary (March 2025 - March 2026)

**Question:** Summarize what happened in the past 12 months for SJUSD, including noteworthy items, big budget items, and interesting topics for further community exploration.
**Date:** 2026-03-25
**Organizations:** SJUSD

---

## Board Leadership Changes

The board underwent its annual reorganization in December 2025. Tanya Kravchuk was elected president (succeeding Ben Avey), Manuel Perez became vice president, and Nick Bloise was elected clerk. Board member remuneration was set at $787.50 per month.

## Superintendent Medical Leave and Acting Superintendent

Superintendent Melissa Bassanelli went on approved medical leave beginning October 13, 2025. The board appointed Chief of Staff Trent Allen as Acting Superintendent during her absence, approving the first amendment to his employment agreement with compensation adjusted to the superintendent's salary rate. Bassanelli resumed her duties on February 2, 2026. Her annual evaluation for 2024-2025 was rated "satisfactory" by unanimous vote (June 2025).

## Trustee Bloise Investigations

Two separate investigations involving Trustee Nick Bloise drew significant board attention:

1. **Conduct Investigation (25-26_001):** An independent investigation substantiated allegations that Bloise did not meet the board's standards for professional conduct and governance. In September 2025, the board unanimously voted to formally reprimand him and directed him to complete harassment prevention training, board governance and ethics training, and comply with all site visit recommendations.

2. **Residency Investigation (25-26_002):** An outside law firm reviewed complaints regarding Bloise's residency in Trustee Area 4. The review, completed October 2025, affirmed that Bloise meets the residency requirement to serve in his elected role.

## Big Budget Items

### 2025-2026 Budget Adoption (June 2025)
The board adopted the 2025-2026 budget, including the Education Protection Account spending plans and excess reserves. One-time pandemic relief funding had ended, but the budget increased certificated positions and noted potential benefit cost rises. The district meets the 2% reserve requirement. The multi-year projection showed an intentional decrease in fund balance over three years with strategic investments.

### General Obligation Bonds -- Measure P (2016)
- **Series 2025 (May 2025):** The board authorized the issuance and sale of GO bonds (election of 2016, Series 2025) in an aggregate principal amount not to exceed $205,080,000.
- **Series 2026 (March 2026 agenda):** The board is considering the issuance of Series 2026 bonds not to exceed $100,080,000, plus $105,000,000 in refunding bonds to refinance outstanding GO debt.
- A tax rate presentation (March 2025) covered the district's tax base, historical assessed values, and projected rates for both Measure P (2016) and Measure P (2024).

### OPEB Liability
The district approved other post-employment benefits (OPEB) liability of $117,036,258 for financial statements for fiscal year ending June 30, 2025.

### Fiscal Status Reports
- **Second Interim 2024-2025 (March 2025):** Positive certification. Revenue and expenditures through January 31, 2025.
- **Unaudited Actuals 2024-2025 (September 2025):** Unrestricted fund balance increased, restricted fund balance decreased, and the district ended the year in good financial standing. Budget revisions included increased unrestricted funds from higher TK revenue and increased restricted funds from ELO-P and partial LREBG restoration.
- **First Interim 2025-2026 (December 2025):** Positive certification. Board member Avey noted emerging warning signals around deficit spending, though the district remains on a positive path. Uncertainty at federal and state levels noted.
- **Second Interim 2025-2026 (March 2026):** Positive certification again.
- **2024-2025 Audit (December 2025):** Clean, unmodified audit opinion from Crowe LLP, with one minor state compliance finding related to attendance reporting.

### Staff Reductions
Both in March 2025 and February-March 2026, the board adopted multiple resolutions reducing or discontinuing classified and certificated services across TK-12, early childhood education, and adult education due to lack of work and/or lack of funds. Public comment from instructional assistants and counselors raised concerns about the impacts. In March 2025, multiple school counselors spoke against reductions, expressing concern about student-to-counselor ratios.

### Legislative Priorities (November 2025)
The board adopted six legislative principles and priorities for 2026-2027, adding a new sixth item -- "Provide stability in services and programs" -- to address uncertainty at the federal level.

## Major Facilities and Construction

### Facilities Master Plan (March 2025)
The board approved a new Facilities Master Plan with a rubric for future project development. It includes assessments of physical conditions, educational environments, and cost analyses for each campus, supported by a public-facing interactive dashboard. The superintendent was authorized to modify the plan to meet district needs.

### Bond Project Updates
Major projects funded by Measure P bonds include:
- **Encina High School:** Full site renovation (multiple lease amendments approved throughout the year)
- **Arcade Middle School:** New construction (multiple lease amendments, up to the 10th amendment)
- **Katherine Johnson Middle School:** New construction (9th amendment); easement conveyed to Sacramento Area Sewer District
- **San Juan High School:** Science wing modernization project (notice of award to Core West, Inc.)
- **Mariemont Elementary School:** Modernization project (6th amendment)
- **Earl LeGette Elementary School:** Project (6th amendment)
- **Gateway International School (Winterstein Site):** Portables, field modification, TK/K wing and shade structure

### School Security Projects (Spring 2025)
Multiple student safety projects approved via public hearings and CEQA exemptions at: Bella Vista High School, Andrew Carnegie Middle School, Mary Deterding Elementary, Pasadena Avenue Elementary, Pershing Elementary, Whitney Avenue Elementary. In 2026, additional security projects at James Cowan Fundamental Elementary and Green Oaks Fundamental Elementary.

### Mira Loma High School
Locker rooms improvement project CEQA approved (March 2026). Community members advocated for additional funding including a third vice principal, improved camera systems, and interpreter funding.

### Other Facilities
- Rio Americano High School: Public safety cell tower project approved
- Howe Avenue Elementary: Emergency HVAC BACTalk Retrofit project
- Multiple early childhood education sites (Kingswood K-8, Encina, Choices Charter, Sunrise Tech) received playground, canopy, and hardscape replacements
- Multiple elementary schools approved for modular classroom CEQA exemptions (Cottage, Dyer-Kelly, Greer, Howe Avenue, Starr King K-8, Whitney Avenue)
- Easements conveyed at Arcade Middle School (to County of Sacramento) and Del Paso Manor Elementary (to SMUD)

## Curriculum and Instruction

### Ethnic Studies Controversy (March-April 2025)
A heated and divisive multi-meeting debate occurred over new ethnic studies courses for 2025-2026. The board was sharply divided. Kravchuk opposed three core ethnic studies courses, arguing the state had not yet provided a funded mandate. After multiple failed motions (3-3-1 ties), the board ultimately approved all proposed courses except three ethnic studies courses: English 9 Ethnic Studies, MYP 4 Language and Literature: Ethnic Studies, and MYP 5 United States History and Ethnic Studies. The other ethnic studies electives (Chicano Studies, Music and Ethnic Studies, AP African American Studies) were approved. Community members spoke passionately on both sides.

### New High School Courses for 2026-2027 (March 2026)
The second round of new courses was less controversial. Proposed courses include: Commercial Music/Modern Band, Jazz Choir, Mariachi Ensemble, Musical Theater, Percussion Ensemble, Personal Finance (a new California graduation requirement for the class of 2030-2031), Philosophy, and Project Management.

### TK-8 Math Curriculum Adoption
The district is in the process of adopting a new TK-8 math curriculum to be implemented in fall 2026. Board members expressed concern over flat or declining math scores and pushed for consistent, districtwide instructional practices including the Building Thinking Classroom (BTC) model.

### Instructional Materials Adoption (April 2025)
14 new high school instructional materials adoptions approved, including AP courses, Japanese language courses, ethnic studies, financial math, and mathematical modeling.

### Reading Difficulties Screener (April-May 2025)
Per Senate Bill 114, the district selected mCLASS (Amplify) with DIBELS Eighth Edition as the screener for students in K-2 starting Fall 2025.

### Literacy Resolution (September 2025)
The board adopted Resolution No. 4222, affirming the district's commitment to evidence-based literacy practices aligned to the Science of Reading.

## Student Wellbeing and Policies

### Mobile Communication Devices / Cell Phone Policy (May 2025 - January 2026)
One of the most closely watched items. AB 3216 required a policy by July 2026. After extensive community engagement, the board adopted Board Policy 5131.8 (May 2025) restricting student cellphone use during instructional time only, rejecting a stricter "bell-to-bell" amendment (which failed 3-4). By January 2026, all schools had implemented the policy. A December 2025 survey showed the majority of respondents believe the policy improved school climates, but concerns remain about inconsistent enforcement. Several board members, including Kravchuk and Avey, indicated growing support for a stricter districtwide policy with all-day restrictions.

### E-Transportation Devices & Student Safety (March 2026)
The board is discussing student use of e-transportation devices (scooters, e-bikes) on school grounds, following a request by Kravchuk.

### Attendance Improvement (September 2025)
The district presented its tiered attendance improvement model. Chronic absenteeism continues to decline, though at a slower pace. The Attendance Academy and Attendance Recovery Program (from CDE) were highlighted.

### Student Discipline and Restorative Practices (May 2025)
Updates on alternatives to suspension and restorative practices. Navigate360 digital lessons piloted at 29 school sites. Home suspensions decreased, and in-school suspension programs expanded.

### Comprehensive School Safety Plans (February 2026)
The board approved school safety plans for the first time (previously approved by staff). New Board Policy 0450 shifts approval responsibility to the board.

### Immigration Enforcement Policies (February 2026)
New Board Policy 1445 "Response to Immigration Enforcement" adopted, replacing retired BP 5145.13, in response to AB 495, SB 98, and AB 49 (2025).

## Student Achievement Data

### Elementary Schools (November 2025)
Overall: small gains in ELA, decline in math, improvement in chronic absenteeism and suspension rates. Board members expressed concern about math.

### Middle and High Schools (October 2025)
ELA scores showed gains, math performance remained flat, chronic absenteeism continued to decline, and suspensions continued to decrease.

### College and Career Readiness (October 2025, April 2025)
Improved graduation rates. Growth in CTE pathways, dual enrollment, and AP participation. The aviation CTE pathway at Mira Loma High School was highlighted as a "crown jewel." New Naviance college/career readiness platform deployed.

### English Learner Data (January 2026)
ELPAC data reviewed; district-funded initiatives include Project GLAD, EL Achieve, and a new ELD IV Journalism class for long-term English learners. The board approved $1 million in one-time supplemental funds for multilingual learner supports.

- **Golden Valley Orchard School:** Conditionally approved for a reduced three-year term (July 2025 - June 2028) due to 46 unmet items, fiscal oversight concerns, and special education compliance issues
- **Marconi Learning Academy:** Approved for five-year renewal (July 2026 - June 2031)
- **Aspire Alexander Twilight Secondary Academy:** Approved for five-year renewal (July 2026 - June 2031)

- **Sunshine Charter School:** Public hearing held March 2026; action anticipated May 2026
- **Highlands Community Charter School:** Noted as closing (June 2025)
- Board Policy 0420.4 Charter School Authorization was revised to incorporate education code updates

## Technology and AI

### AI Policy Updates (August 2025)
Revisions to Board Policies on Employee Use of Technology (BP 4040), Student Use of Technology (BP 6163.4), and Academic Honesty (BP 5131.9) all incorporated language regarding artificial intelligence. MagicSchool AI rollout to students planned for fall 2025.

### Technology Update (March 2025)
Overview of Technology Services including E-Rate program, cybersecurity threats and protection, and AI initiatives.

### Social Media (March 2026)
The board added a future agenda item to explore developing an educational program on the effects of social media. The board has also been conferencing with legal counsel regarding anticipated social media litigation in closed sessions throughout the year.

## Special Education

- A finding of noncompliance from the California Department of Education was disclosed (May 2025) with corrective actions being implemented.
- The Community Advisory Committee (CAC) was active, with public commenters raising concerns about special education services, graduation requirements, and IEP processes.
- The board discussed alternative graduation pathways for students with significant cognitive disabilities per AB 181 and AB 2251.
- The 2025-2026 SELPA annual update was approved.

## Other Notable Items

- **San Juan Education Foundation:** Reached $1 million in total grants since inception in 2006. The Propel teacher grant program was revitalized.
- **Teacher Residency Program:** Update on the district's "grow your own" teacher pipeline.
- **San Juan Youth Voice Advocates (SJYVA):** Student leadership program held listening sessions and presented districtwide recommendations to the board (May 2025).
- **Citrus Heights Schools:** Ongoing partnership with the City of Citrus Heights to strengthen enrollment and community engagement at Citrus Heights area schools.
- **Joint Resolution with Folsom Cordova USD (October 2025):** Formalized longstanding interdistrict transfer agreements for families in American River Canyon South area.
- **Labor Negotiations:** The board adopted initial bargaining proposals for the San Juan Teachers Association successor contract, CSEA Chapter 127, and SJPEC for 2025-2026.
- **Minimum Wage Increase:** January 2026 increase to $16.90/hour for certain short-term, temporary classifications.
- **Board Election (November 2026):** Resolution certified the election order for four trustee areas (3, 5, 6, 7).

## Topics for Further Community Exploration

1. **The Cell Phone Policy Debate -- Is a Stricter Policy Coming?** The current policy only restricts phones during instruction, but several board members now support all-day restrictions. Survey data shows mixed results. This is a topic that directly affects every student family and could see action in the coming months.

2. **Bond Spending and Tax Rates:** With over $300 million in new bond issuances in the past year alone (Series 2025 at $205M, Series 2026 at $100M, plus $105M in refunding), community members may want to understand what projects are being funded, how tax rates are projected to change, and how the new Facilities Master Plan rubric prioritizes projects.

3. **Ethnic Studies Curriculum -- Unfinished Business?** The 3-3 board deadlock on core ethnic studies courses in 2025 left three courses unapproved. With a new board configuration and the state graduation requirement approaching (class of 2029-2030), this issue is likely to resurface.

4. **Math Achievement Crisis:** Math scores are flat or declining at both elementary and secondary levels. A new TK-8 math curriculum adoption is coming in fall 2026. The community may be interested in what the new curriculum will look like and whether the Building Thinking Classroom model will be adopted districtwide.

5. **Staff Reductions vs. Service Quality:** Annual resolutions reducing classified and certificated positions raise questions about impacts on class sizes, counselor ratios, and instructional aide support. Multiple public commenters have expressed concerns.

6. **Social Media Litigation and Education:** The board has been conferencing with legal counsel in closed session about anticipated social media litigation throughout the year, and they added an educational program on social media effects to the future agenda. This may signal the district joining the growing wave of school districts taking legal action against social media companies.

7. **Special Education Accountability:** A CDE noncompliance finding, community concerns about IEP processes, and ongoing CAC engagement suggest this is an area where parents and community members should stay engaged.

8. **School Safety and Security Investments:** Dozens of site security projects funded by bond dollars. The new Comprehensive School Safety Plan process shifts approval to the board, increasing transparency.

9. **Superintendent Stability:** Bassanelli's medical leave and Trent Allen's temporary appointment as Acting Superintendent (October 2025 - February 2026) is worth monitoring given the importance of leadership continuity during budget pressures and federal uncertainty.

10. **Charter School Landscape:** With one charter closing (Highlands), one on a short leash (Golden Valley Orchard, 3-year conditional), one low-performing (Aspire College Prep, 2-year renewal), and a new charter opening (Sunshine petition pending), the charter school ecosystem is shifting. Community members may want to understand how these schools serve students differently and what accountability mechanisms exist.
