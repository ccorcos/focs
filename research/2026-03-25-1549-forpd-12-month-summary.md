# Fair Oaks Recreation and Park District (FORPD) -- 12-Month Summary

**Question:** Summarize what happened at FORPD in the past 12 months. Include anything new and noteworthy, explain big budget items, and suggest interesting topics for further community exploration.
**Date:** 2026-03-25
**Organizations:** FORPD

---

## Overview

The past 12 months (March 2025 through March 2026) have been a transformative period for FORPD, defined by the completion and grand opening of the Village Park Renovation Project (including the new Fair Oaks Performing Arts Center), a leadership transition, a foundation relaunch, and continued growth in programming and community events. The Board held 13 regular meetings across this period.

---

## Major Developments

### 1. Village Park Renovation and the Fair Oaks Performing Arts Center (FOPAC)

The single biggest story of the year is the completion and grand opening of the Village Park Renovation Project, funded by **Measure J** (a general obligation bond approved by Fair Oaks voters in 2018). The Measure J Bond Fund for FY 2025-2026 totaled **$4,910,690**, all allocated to structures and improvements for the Village Park Project.

Key milestones:
- **Spring 2025:** Ongoing construction, with change orders for amphitheater hanger doors ($77,455 -- March 2025), road paving around Village Park, and a construction project allowance increase (April 2025). Multiple change orders were processed monthly through the spring.
- **May 2025:** The Board approved a logo for the Fair Oaks Performing Arts Center.
- **July-August 2025:** The Board discussed and approved a dedication plaque for the facility, honoring the 2025 Board (Ralph Carhart, Raymond James Irwin, Darren Mounts, John O'Farrell, Delinda Tamagni), past Board members from 2018, and District Administrator Michael J. Aho.
- **September 2025:** Grand Opening presentation at the Board meeting. The September meeting was notable for being the first at which the Village Park grand opening was formally presented to the Board.
- **October 2025:** Significant grand opening expenditures visible in vendor reports -- catering ($3,635), decorators ($9,304), entertainment ($4,900+), floral ($2,038), marketing, and promotional items. The grand opening was a major community event.

The FOPAC now hosts a "Black Box" theater, concessions, and a variety of arts and entertainment programming. Vendor reports show ongoing FOPAC-related spending on social media management ($11,000/two months via Greydot Media), scheduling software, promotional items, and events.

### 2. End of the Fair Oaks Theatre Festival

At the September 2025 meeting, the Board adopted a resolution recognizing the **Fair Oaks Theatre Festival (FOTF)** for over 40 years of service to the community. The Festival, founded by Wendell and Lois Frazier, had concluded its final season. The resolution honored its legacy of Shakespearean productions, community theater, and educational programs at the Veterans Memorial Amphitheater. This marks the end of a long-running cultural institution in Fair Oaks.

### 3. District Administrator Transition

A significant leadership change unfolded across the fall and winter:
- **October 2025:** Closed session for District Administrator performance evaluation.
- **November 2025:** The Board approved the District Administrator job description, signaling the beginning of a recruitment process.
- **December 2025:** The Board approved an agreement with CPS-HR for District Administrator recruitment.
- **January 2026:** The Board approved the District Administrator salary range and the recruitment process/timeline. A board vacancy appointment process was also initiated.
- **February 2026:** Closed session regarding anticipated litigation.
- **March 2026:** The mid-year budget report noted that the District Administrator's termination payout (estimated at approximately $39,000) was not budgeted and would likely require a one-time allocation from the Unrestricted Fund Balance. CPS-HR recruitment services appear in vendor reports ($10,000 in February 2026).

Michael J. Aho has served as District Administrator throughout this period but appears to be departing. This is a major transition for the District.

### 4. Fair Oaks Recreation and Arts Foundation Relaunch

The District's foundation has undergone a significant reorganization:
- **October 2025:** The Foundation voted to update its name (from Fair Oaks Recreation Foundation to Fair Oaks Recreation and Arts Foundation).
- **November 2025:** Updated Foundation by-laws were reviewed and approved.
- **March 2026:** The Board moved to appoint the initial member of the newly restructured Foundation Board of Directors. Three candidates were considered: Cassie Kendall, Michael Cole, and Allison Hoppe. The Foundation's mission is to support fundraising, community partnerships, sponsorship, and philanthropic support for recreation, arts, cultural programming, and park improvements.

All allocated to the Village Park Project (structures and improvements).

### Assessment Districts:
- Parks Maintenance and Recreation Improvement District (FOLL): $772,000 (revenues ~$649,919/year from assessments with CPI increases up to 3%)
- Phoenix Field Landscape and Lighting: $61,614 ($154.58/single family equivalent, no CPI increase)
- Gum Ranch Landscape and Lighting: $121,703 (CPI increases up to 3%)

### Notable Budget Items:
- **3.1% COLA** for employees, per MOU, costing approximately $47,000
- **Health care costs** increased (single: $1,256/mo, family: $3,264/mo)
- **Landscape maintenance** (Cagwin & Dorward): ~$33,417/month across all parks
- **Water bills**: Significant, especially Phoenix Park (~$5,000-9,400/month)
- **Insurance liability**: $154,370
- **Electricity**: $62,000 budgeted, expected to increase significantly due to new FOPAC facilities and EV charging stations
- **Security cameras**: $12,000 addition at La Vista
- **Holiday lights**: $33,000 (leased, not purchased)

### Mid-Year Budget Update (March 2026)

At mid-year (through January 2026), the District is operating within the adopted budget:
- **$198,500 in anticipated savings** in Services and Supplies, primarily from a **$165,000 savings** due to a planned Broadway show that did not happen, plus $28K in reimbursements from Garden Gala and Grand Opening events
- **Legal services** overspent by ~$20K due to Foundation by-law work and bond-related legal costs; contingency may be used to cover
- **Parks electricity** running higher than budgeted due to new facilities and EV charging; offset by $25K in other parks savings and ~$3,500 in EV charging revenue
- **Facility rentals** exceeding projections by about 20%
- **Cell tower lease revenue** shortfall (anticipated co-locator revenue has not materialized)
- **Property tax collections** on track; next year's assessed valuation increase is 3.2%
- **Contingency**: $6,500 used for locksmithing equipment, leaving $43,500
- **DA termination payout**: ~$39K unbudgeted, will likely require allocation from Unrestricted Fund Balance

## Topics Worth Exploring Further

1. **The District Administrator Transition** -- Who will replace Mike Aho? What is the salary range? What direction will the new DA take the District? The recruitment is being handled by CPS-HR and the timeline was approved in January 2026. The ~$39K termination payout and any changes in leadership philosophy could significantly affect the District going forward.

2. **FOPAC Financial Sustainability** -- The new Performing Arts Center is a major new asset but also a major new cost center. The budgeted Broadway show did not happen, creating a $165K revenue gap (offset by savings). How will the arts programming revenue model stabilize? What shows and events are being planned? The A&E department has a $921,445 budget -- is it self-sustaining through ticket sales and rentals?

3. **Measure J Bond Accountability** -- With $4.9M in the bond fund and the Village Park project winding down, what is the remaining balance? What future projects might be funded? The Bond Oversight Committee presented its annual report in May 2025. Community members may want to understand total Measure J spending to date vs. original bond authorization.

4. **Disc Golf at Bannister Park** -- A feasibility study was presented in May 2025. There has been community interest in both installing a course at Bannister Park and returning baskets to Miller Park. What happened after the feasibility study? Is the project moving forward?

5. **The Foundation Relaunch** -- The newly renamed Fair Oaks Recreation and Arts Foundation is just getting started with its first Board appointments. What fundraising goals has it set? How will it interact with the District? Who ultimately was appointed?

6. **Water and Electricity Costs** -- The District spends heavily on water (Phoenix Park alone costs $5,000-$9,400/month) and electricity is rising with the new FOPAC facilities. Are there conservation efforts? What is the EV charging station usage and revenue trend?

7. **Concessions Model** -- The District shifted from a contractor to staff-led concessions at FOPAC, which required unbudgeted part-time hours. How is this working? Is it financially sustainable compared to contracting out?

8. **End of the Fair Oaks Theatre Festival** -- After 40+ years, what fills the cultural gap? The new FOPAC was partly designed to serve this purpose, but the Theatre Festival was a volunteer-driven community institution. Is the Take Note Troupe or Shakespeare San Francisco stepping in? How does the community feel about the transition?

9. **Assessment District Levy Increases** -- The Parks Maintenance and Recreation Improvement District and Gum Ranch assessments can increase up to 3% per year. What are the actual increases proposed for FY 2026-27? The March 2026 meeting directed preparation of engineer's reports for the next fiscal year.

10. **Board Vacancy** -- A board vacancy appointment process and timeline was presented in January 2026. Who fills the seat and how does it change Board dynamics?
