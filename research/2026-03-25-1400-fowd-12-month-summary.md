# Fair Oaks Water District: Past 12 Months Summary

**Question:** Summarize what happened in the past 12 months for Fair Oaks Water District (FOWD). Include anything new and noteworthy, explain big budget items, and suggest interesting topics for further community exploration.
**Date:** 2026-03-25
**Organizations:** FOWD

---

## Board Leadership

- **Through 2025:** Misha Sarkovich served as Board President and Randy Marx as Vice President.
- **December 2025:** The Board unanimously elected Randy Marx as Board President and Mark Dolby as Vice President for 2026.
- **Full Board:** Misha Sarkovich, Randy Marx, Mark Dolby, Chris Petersen, George Babcock.
- **General Manager:** Tom R. Gray continues as GM, but announced a transition plan toward retirement at the December 2025 meeting. He stated the Board plans to initiate recruitment for a new GM approximately six months before the end of his contract, allowing overlap for the successor to work with him.
- **Assistant General Manager (AGM):** The district has been actively recruiting for an AGM. By September 2025, GM Gray was pursuing a candidate and putting together an offer. The position had been restarted after a prior failed attempt.

## 2026 Budget -- Zero Rate Increase

The 2026 budget was the centerpiece of the October 20, 2025 public workshop and was formally adopted at the November 17, 2025 meeting. Key highlights:

### Board Direction for the 2026 Budget
1. **Zero rate increase in 2026.** After 10% increases in 2023 and 2024 and a 5% increase in 2025, FOWD held rates flat. FOWD water rates remain among the lowest in the Sacramento region ($166.58 bi-monthly for a 1-inch meter at 40 CCF, compared to a regional average of $198.25).
2. **No new debt.** FOWD enters 2026 with zero existing debt and zero debt payments.
3. **No new capital projects** beyond those already approved and deemed necessary.
4. **Focus on completing work-in-progress projects.**
5. **Meet reserve policy targets.**
6. **90% surface water / 10% groundwater supply target.**
7. **3% COLA** included in staff calculations.
8. **Commitment to developing groundwater supply.**

### Budget Numbers
- **2026 Projected Revenue:** $14,236,200
- **2026 Operating Budget:** $9,861,000 (a 12.5% increase over 2025's estimated year-end of $8,764,500)
- **2026 Projected Net Income (before capital):** $4,375,200
- **Total 2026 Capital Projects:** $8,196,600
- **Cash Reserves at end of 2025:** $7,831,500
- **Projected 2026 Year-End Undesignated Reserve:** $3,251,200 (just below the $3,287,000 policy target)

### Key Drivers of the 12.5% Operating Increase
- Labor & benefits: +$682,700 (13.9%) -- driven largely by filling open positions and rising benefit costs
- Professional services: +$203,900 (36.3%)
- SJWD purchased water cost: +$114,600 (4.3%)
- Other net increases: $93,300

### Water Rate Comparisons
FOWD highlighted that its rates are nearly the lowest in the Sacramento region, significantly below districts like Carmichael Water ($230.46), Sacramento Suburban Water ($215.26), and Orangevale Municipal Water District ($214.52).

## Emergency Water Supply Milestone

At the August 2025 meeting, GM Gray reported a major milestone: **By the end of 2026, FOWD will have met its goal of developing enough groundwater capacity to meet emergency water demand for the entire system.** The district will have 10,700 gallons per minute of capacity from wells built between 2007 and 2026. Even taking the largest well offline, FOWD would still have 8,400 GPM against peak single-day demand of 7,500 GPM. Every well was grant-funded through SGA. By the end of 2026, FOWD will own four additional properties for water supply infrastructure.

## Water Supply Strategy: Why 90% Surface Water?

An ongoing discussion addressed why FOWD uses 90% surface water and only 10% groundwater, even though it has significant well capacity. The answer lies in SJWD's wholesale rate structure: FOWD essentially must pay for surface water whether it takes delivery or not. Purchasing 8,972 AF from SJWD costs an estimated $2,455,455, while purchasing zero AF would still cost approximately $1,931,580 in fixed charges. The financial disincentive for pumping groundwater instead is only about $524,000 -- not enough to justify the additional cost of running wells. The Board directed GM Gray to continue minimizing groundwater use until a plan demonstrating financial and operational advantages could be presented.

## Litigation

FOWD has been in closed session on multiple active legal matters throughout the year:

1. **Citrus Heights Water District & Fair Oaks Water District v. San Juan Water District** (Sacramento Superior Court Case No. 23WM000064) -- Discussed in closed session at nearly every meeting. This ongoing lawsuit between the retail agencies and their wholesale supplier SJWD is a significant matter.

2. **Corcos v. Fair Oaks Water District** (Sacramento Superior Court Case No. 25WM000050) -- Appeared starting April 2025. At the April meeting, the Board authorized hiring Theodore S. Wolter of Klinedinst Sacramento to represent FOWD.

3. **Corcos & FOVEC v. Fair Oaks Water District** (Sacramento Superior Court Case No. 26VVM000022) -- A newer case appearing on the March 2026 agenda, apparently involving the Fair Oaks Village Enhancement Committee (FOVEC).

## Water Forum 2050 Agreement

The Sacramento Water Forum 2050 Agreement was a major discussion topic:
- **April-June 2025:** Draft agreement discussed. GM Gray noted FOWD will now pay its portion directly (approximately $55,000-$60,000/year) rather than through the San Juan family of agencies, after Sacramento County backed out of some funding.
- **January 2026:** Board unanimously approved Resolution No. 26-01 adopting the Water Forum 2050 Agreement and the related Interagency Agreement. Director Babcock raised concerns about equal treatment of water districts versus other entities and lack of protections during drought. GM Gray explained it is a trust-based framework that has historically prevented litigation and enabled grant funding for the region.

## Relationship with San Juan Water District (SJWD)

Several notable developments in the FOWD-SJWD relationship:
- **May 2025:** SJWD leadership (GM Helliker, Board President Costa, Vice President McRae) attended the FOWD board meeting to discuss the Water Forum Agreement.
- **June 2025:** GM Gray reported that SJWD's planned significant retail rate increase was voted down after a customer (an engineer) found errors in the Prop 218 documents and SJWD staff could not answer questions. SJWD was required to do an updated rate study.
- **August 2025:** SJWD General Manager Helliker's employment was fully terminated. Adam Larsen was announced as the new SJWD GM.
- **September 2025:** SJWD Director Rich resigned, creating a vacancy in Division 1.
- **October 2025:** FOWD sent a letter to SJWD (October 14, 2025) and issued a public information release (October 15, 2025).
- **FOWD-SJWD 2x2 Ad-Hoc Committee** (Directors Petersen and Marx) existed throughout the period but rarely met.

## Sacramento Regional Water Bank

At the January 2026 meeting, RWA staff presented on the Sacramento Regional Water Bank. Director Marx reported from SGA meetings that initial balances for the Water Bank were being decided. GM Gray noted meetings with RWA and the San Juan Family regarding the Water Bank. From the March 2026 minutes, groundwater levels in the region are generally going up, except in the Orange Vale Water Company service area.

## Capital Budget Discipline Discussion

A noteworthy exchange at the May 2025 meeting: Director Petersen pointed out that FOWD had a $9.7 million approved capital budget for 2025 but had only spent $800,000 by May -- a pattern he said had persisted for his four years on the Board, with FOWD typically spending only about 25% of its annual capital allocation. He pushed for more realistic budgeting tied to projects the district actually plans to execute in a given year, rather than placeholder budgets. Vice President Marx agreed. Staff responded that many delays are outside FOWD's control (weather, permitting). GM Gray committed to reformatting the capital projects status report for greater transparency.

## Other Notable Actions

- **Cross-Connection & Backflow Prevention Policy** (June 2025): Board updated Policy 6030 in compliance with new California code requiring surveys of every property for potential cross-connection issues. About 1,247 backflow devices currently exist in the system (less than 10% of connections).
- **Employee Leave Balances** (June 2025): Board reinstated all leave limits from the Employee Handbook.
- **2026 Salary Ranges** (December 2025): Board approved a 3% COLA for all positions.
- **GM Employment Agreement** (December 2025): Board approved an amended and restated employment agreement for GM Gray.
- **Employee Compensation Policy** (January 2026): Updated Policy 4070 to restore a Board-approved line that had been deleted in error, related to retention incentives adopted during COVID.
- **Urban Water Management Plan** (February 2026): Board authorized up to $64,000 for Verdantas to prepare the FOWD 2025 Urban Water Management Plan.
- **2026 Election** (November 2025): Sacramento County estimated election administration costs at $32,441 for FOWD.
- **2025 Annual Financial Audit** (December 2025): Richardson & Company, LLP approved as independent auditor.
- **SMUD Rate Increases** (April 2025): Board discussed impacts of proposed SMUD rate increases and "time of day" surcharges on FOWD operations.
- **RWA Executive Committee** (January 2026): FOWD approved the executive committee with the condition that a sub-committee examine differences between intended and current committee makeup. FOWD was appointed to the sub-committee.
- **Water Currents Newsletter** (October 2025): Board directed staff to distribute a semi-annual newsletter as an insert with customer bills.

## Topics for Further Community Exploration

1. **GM Succession and Leadership Transition:** Tom Gray's upcoming retirement after a long tenure is a major turning point for FOWD. Who will lead the district next? What happens to institutional knowledge? The AGM hire is a crucial stepping stone. Community members may want to attend meetings during the recruitment process.

2. **The FOWD vs. SJWD Lawsuit:** The ongoing litigation between Fair Oaks (and Citrus Heights) against their wholesale water supplier San Juan Water District has been a closed-session item at nearly every meeting for years. What are the stakes? How could the outcome affect water rates for Fair Oaks customers?

3. **Corcos/FOVEC Lawsuits Against FOWD:** Two separate cases have been filed against the district. Who is bringing these suits and why? What are the potential financial implications for ratepayers?

4. **The "90/10" Water Supply Question:** FOWD has significant groundwater capacity but pays for surface water whether it uses it or not. Is this arrangement fair to FOWD customers? Could renegotiating the wholesale rate structure with SJWD save money? This ties directly to the lawsuit.

5. **Capital Budget Execution Gap:** Director Petersen raised valid points about the district consistently spending only 25% of its capital budget. Are projects being adequately planned and executed? What does this mean for infrastructure maintenance?

6. **The Field Services Center (Corporate Yard) Saga:** This project has been in planning for years, still waiting on County permits. The salvage value of the old lumber in the existing building was discussed. When will construction actually begin?

7. **Emergency Water Supply Achievement:** By end of 2026, FOWD will have full emergency groundwater capacity -- a 20-year goal. This is a major accomplishment that has largely flown under the radar. Community members might be interested in learning more about what this means for water reliability.

8. **Water Forum 2050:** FOWD signed a 25-year agreement with dozens of regional entities. What are the implications? Director Babcock's concerns about drought protections and litigation risk deserve public attention.

9. **Regional Water Bank:** This emerging initiative could affect how water is stored and shared across the Sacramento region. How will FOWD participate and benefit?

10. **Rate Trajectory:** After 10%, 10%, 5%, and now 0% increases, what comes next? The budget shows reserves narrowing to just below policy minimums. Will rate increases be necessary in 2027 or beyond?
