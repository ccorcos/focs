# Los Rios Community College District: 12-Month Summary (March 2025 -- March 2026)

**Question:** Summarize what happened over the past 12 months for LRCCD, including new and noteworthy developments, big budget items, and ideas for topics the community might want to explore further.
**Date:** 2026-03-25
**Organizations:** LRCCD

---

## Overview

Los Rios Community College District -- the second-largest community college district in California, serving over 67,000 students across American River College (ARC), Cosumnes River College (CRC), Folsom Lake College (FLC), and Sacramento City College (SCC) -- had a very active year. The district adopted a large budget under challenging state fiscal conditions, advanced major capital projects, launched new academic programs, negotiated labor contracts, and began planning for a potential future bond measure.

## Budget and Fiscal Picture

### 2025-26 Adopted Budget (September 10, 2025)

The Board adopted the 2025-26 budget at its September meeting. Key figures from the General Fund:

- **Total General Fund revenue:** $703.9 million (adopted budget)
- **Total General Fund expenditures/appropriations:** $828.2 million
- **Beginning fund balance:** $209.1 million
- **Ending fund balance:** $84.8 million
- **Academic salaries:** $229.3 million (the largest single expense)
- **Classified salaries:** $149.3 million
- **Employee benefits:** $194.3 million
- **Financial aid pass-through:** nearly $158.7 million projected for students
- **Foundation assets:** $28.7 million projected balance, virtually all committed to college purposes

The budget incorporated a **2.3% Cost-of-Living Adjustment (COLA)** from the state and enrollment growth funding. The state closed a projected **$12 billion deficit** using reserves, deferrals, and targeted reductions. Notably, the state **fully depleted the Proposition 98 Rainy Day Fund**, leaving fewer tools to buffer future downturns.

### Unfunded Growth -- A Major Structural Issue

As of the February 2026 board retreat, Los Rios has the **largest unfunded enrollment growth in the state by dollar value**: approximately **$21.8 million representing over 4,000 FTES** (full-time equivalent students). This is effectively the enrollment load of a small college going without full ongoing funding. Even if the proposed additional state growth funding is included in the 2026-27 final budget, it would only cover about 75% of the gap. Growth funding remains one of Los Rios' top advocacy priorities.

### Enrollment Recovery and Growth

Enrollment is up about **5% year-over-year** in 2025-26, on top of a fully restored base from the prior year. The district expects low single-digit growth in 2026-27. Growth drivers include main campus efforts, dual enrollment expansion, and employer partnerships.

### State Budget Context for 2026-27

At the February 2026 retreat, the administration noted that the Governor's Budget projects a stronger near-term revenue outlook, but the Legislative Analyst's Office (LAO) is more cautious. A $5.6 billion Proposition 98 settle-up remains delayed due to a lawsuit. There is a risk of a Proposition 98 "suspension" if revenues track closer to LAO estimates.

### Bond Issuance

In April 2025, the Board approved the issuance of **$10 million in Series F general obligation bonds** (from the 2008 Measure M authorization) and **$45 million in 2025 Refunding General Obligation Bonds** to refinance existing debt at lower rates.

### OPEB Trust

The district's Other Post-Employment Benefits (OPEB) trust ended 2024-25 with **$174.5 million**, well in excess of the $140.7 million total OPEB liability -- a sign of strong fiscal health and foresight.

## Major Capital Projects and Facilities

### Davies Hall at American River College -- $56.7 Million State Investment

The single biggest facilities win this year: the state enacted budget included **$56.7 million for a new Davies Hall** at American River College. The building had been deemed unsafe by the Department of State Architects over a year ago, and this state funding moves the project forward to provide a safe, modern facility.

### Facilities Master Plans (December 2025)

In December 2025, the Board received updated **Facilities Master Plans for all four colleges** (ARC, CRC, FLC, SCC). The plans included space utilization studies, facility condition assessments, identification of structures needing replacement, and conceptual designs for campus improvements including new entry plazas, central plazas, pedestrian spines, and quad redesigns at ARC.

### 5-Year Capital Outlay Plan (FY2027-2031)

A comprehensive 5-year capital outlay plan was submitted to the state Chancellor's Office in May 2025, covering the district's nearly 600 acres of land across all campuses and centers.

### Possible Future Bond Measure

At the October 2025 retreat, the Board discussed capital needs and bond possibilities. By February 2026, a **facilities planning timeline** was presented showing:
- A public opinion survey was completed in August 2025
- School Facility Improvement District (SFID) boundaries had their first reading in February 2026
- SFID boundary action planned for March-April 2026
- A public awareness campaign (50+ speaking engagements) planned for March-June 2026
- A tracking survey in June to re-assess viability
- **Possible Board action in July 2026** to place a measure on the November 2026 ballot

## New Programs and Academic Initiatives

### Folsom Lake College AI Department (February 2026)

FLC established the **second stand-alone Artificial Intelligence department in California** in 2024. The department offers an AI and Machine Learning Certificate, with an Applied AI AS Degree, Deep Learning Certificate, Generative AI Certificate, Natural Language Processing Certificate, and Computer Vision Certificate all in development. An **AI Summer Bootcamp** was piloted in summer 2025 with 31 local high school students. Partnerships include Sac State (AI concentration in business), a $5 million NSF S-STEM grant proposal with UC Davis, and the city of Rancho Cordova's initiative to become an "AI City."

### Sacramento City College Air Traffic Control Program (March 2026)

SCC's Enhanced Air Traffic Control (AT-CTI) program received **FAA approval on December 22, 2025**, making it one of only 30 approved colleges in the U.S. SCC was the **second program nationally to be dual-certified in both Tower and En Route specialties**. The program addresses a national shortage of approximately 3,800 controllers (25% of the needed workforce). Graduates can earn starting salaries of $60,000-$130,000 with a median annual salary of $144,580. Cohorts are 16 students (8 tower, 8 en route) over 3 semesters.

### Zero Textbook Cost / Open Educational Resources (March 2025)

The Board received a detailed report on ZTC/OER progress. At ARC alone, spring 2025 saw **31% of sections designated ZTC/OER**, up from 14% in spring 2023. ARC has 5 fully developed ZTC pathways with additional ones in development. ZTC/OER participation correlates with higher student success rates and disproportionately benefits BIPOC and lower-income students.

### Workforce Readiness and CTE (April 2025)

A comprehensive report detailed career technical education across all four colleges, including 84 degrees and certificates at ARC alone across eight meta majors. Highlighted programs include Geographic Information Systems (award-winning), Respiratory Care (nationally recognized), and Funeral Service Education (rare and distinguished).

### Healthcare Career Pathways at FLC (February 2026)

FLC presented on healthcare career development, noting a staggering **16:1 workforce gap** (7,560 annual job openings vs. 460 annual program completions). Current programs include CNA, EMT, Medical Lab Technician, Radiology Technician, and Advanced Imaging Technician, with Medical Assistant and Surgical Technician under consideration. Major philanthropic investments include **$2.5 million from Sutter Health** and $100,000 from Dignity Health.

### Dual Enrollment and Adult Learners (May 2025)

Dual enrollment students (high schoolers taking college courses) average a 3.3 GPA with 84% course success rates (vs. 74% overall). Adult learners (age 25+) represent **45.5% of the Los Rios student body** and average a 3.1 GPA with 75% course success.

### Embedded Tutoring at ARC (February 2026)

Launched in Fall 2024 with 11 sections, by Spring 2026 the program expanded to **73 sections with 90 tutors**. Students who participated had a **75% course success rate compared to 58%** for non-participants -- a 17-percentage-point advantage. First-generation, low-income, and students of color are overrepresented in participation and thriving.

## K-16 Collaborative and Regional Partnerships

### K-16 Collaborative Grant ($18.6 million)

Los Rios serves as fiscal agent for the Sacramento K-16 Collaborative, funded by an $18.6 million state grant. The multi-county partnership spans eight counties and includes UC Davis, Sac State, Sierra College, K-12 districts, and employers like SMUD, Kaiser Permanente, Accenture, and Bosch. Achievements include:
- On track for HSI (Hispanic Serving Institution) status for every regional college and university
- 2,000+ degrees/certificates awarded to adult learners and "comebackers"
- 1,300 additional young men of color enrolled in UC Davis and Sac State
- A regional data sharing network with 15+ partners

### Formation of K-16 Joint Powers Authority (November 2025)

As the state grant sunsets, the Board approved forming a **PK-16 Collaborative Joint Powers Authority** to sustain the work. Los Rios was the first agency to approve the JPA, with no financial commitment required. The JPA aims to raise $1.5 million by June 2026, with 11 grant opportunities being pursued.

## Labor Relations and Governance

### Collective Bargaining Agreements

- **LRSA (Supervisors):** New contract approved December 2025, running January 2026 through December 2028
- **LRCEA (Classified Employees):** New 2026-2028 contract ratified in February 2026
- **LRCFT (Faculty):** Initial bargaining proposals presented in December 2025
- **SEIU:** Negotiations ongoing (closed session at December meeting)

The district's "Bucket System" for employee compensation allocates 80% of defined new revenues to compensation improvements and 20% to operational costs.

### New Chief Counsel

At the October 2025 retreat, the Board approved hiring **Alyssa (Aly) Rutsch Bivins** as Chief Counsel (7-0 vote).

### Chancellor Succession

The December 2025 agenda included closed session items for Public Employee Appointment and labor negotiations for the Chancellor position, suggesting planning for future leadership.

## Student Support and Sustainability

### Student Health and Wellness Centers (January 2026)

The annual report showed **12,102 points of connection**, **10,356 services provided**, and **256 health promotion outreach events** in 2024-25. Mental health services served 1,176 students with 3,203 appointments. Notably, 3 of 4 students seeking mental health services reported significant stress, 50% experienced depression, 60% experienced anxiety, and **1 in 4 had thoughts of suicide**. Physical health services served 1,691 students with 3,080 services.

### Livestreaming Board Meetings (November 2025)

The Board discussed livestreaming meetings for the first time. The estimated cost is **$160,000-$185,000** to equip the board room, plus ~$16,000 in annual ongoing costs. This remains under consideration.

### Fleet Electrification and EV Charging (May 2025)

The district is working toward Board of Governors sustainability benchmarks: 50% of new fleet vehicles zero-emission by 2030, 100% by 2035. Currently, 109 EV charging stations are installed or under construction district-wide, with plans to replace 45 existing chargers with networked units and add more.

## Topics for Further Community Exploration

1. **The potential November 2026 bond measure:** The district is actively planning for a possible general obligation bond on the ballot. Community members should watch the July 2026 Board meeting for a potential vote. What projects would it fund? How would it affect property taxes?

2. **The $21.8 million unfunded growth gap:** Los Rios has the largest unfunded enrollment growth in the state. What does it mean when 4,000+ students are essentially being educated without state funding? How does this affect class availability, faculty hiring, and student services?

3. **FLC's AI Department and Rancho Cordova's "AI City" initiative:** As the second stand-alone AI department in California's community colleges, this is a local first. How does this connect with the city of Rancho Cordova's ambitions? What workforce opportunities does this create?

4. **SCC's Air Traffic Control program:** One of only 30 FAA-approved programs nationwide, with high earning potential for graduates. How are students being recruited? Is the program capacity sufficient given the national controller shortage?

5. **The healthcare workforce gap (16:1):** With only 460 annual completions against 7,560 annual job openings in the region, what can be done to expand healthcare training? How do the Sutter Health and Dignity Health partnerships help?

6. **Student mental health crisis:** With 1 in 4 students seeking mental health services reporting suicidal thoughts, what resources are available? How does funding for these centers compare to need?

7. **Chancellor succession:** Closed session items suggest planning for a chancellor transition. Dr. Brian King has led since 2013 -- who will be next and what direction will the district take?

8. **The K-16 JPA as a model for regional collaboration:** This public entity for cross-institutional data sharing and career pathway development is novel. How will it sustain itself after the state grant ends? What does it mean for K-12 students in the region?

9. **Board meeting transparency:** The discussion about livestreaming (currently audio-only recordings) raises questions about public access. Many comparable districts already livestream. Will Los Rios follow?

10. **Dual enrollment growth and its impact on K-12 partners:** With high school students achieving 84% course success rates, how is this changing the high school experience? What does the expansion mean for local school districts?
